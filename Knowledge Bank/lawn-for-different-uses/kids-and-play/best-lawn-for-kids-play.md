# What's the best lawn for kids' play?

Wear tolerance is the main requirement. Plus safety considerations (chemistry timing, surface conditions).

Species selection:
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing stoloniferous) handles wear and recovers from damage.
- NZLA Perennial Rye or DPR as a base ryegrass.
- Blend with tall fescue for drought tolerance.
- Avoid fine fescue alone (low wear tolerance).

Cultural management:
- Mowing height 40 to 50 mm (taller cushions impact, more leaf for recovery).
- Strong fertility programme (NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ three to four times a year).
- Regular watering through summer.
- Annual overseed in autumn to maintain density.

Common kids' lawn issues:

Trampoline area:
- Concentrated shade and wear underneath.
- Bare patches develop within months.
- Move trampoline periodically to spread wear.
- Or accept the bare patch and mulch underneath.

Sandpit and paddling pool:
- Smother the lawn underneath.
- Move regularly during summer.
- Reseed any dead patches in autumn.

Football, soccer, cricket use:
- Concentrated wear in specific areas (centre of football play, cricket bowling crease).
- Overseed worn areas in autumn.
- Heavier fertility supports recovery.

Bike tracks:
- Repeated tyre tracks compact and wear the lawn.
- Designated bike track (mulch or hard surface) saves the lawn.
- Or accept bare tracks as part of family life.

Safety considerations:

Chemistry timing:
- Keep kids off the lawn for at least 24 hours after chemical applications.
- Read product labels for re-entry intervals.
- Schedule chemistry for times when kids won't be playing.
- Wash kids' hands and feet after lawn play if recent chemistry.

Slip hazards:
- Wet lawn is slippery for running and ball play.
- Clover-rich lawn is more slippery than pure grass.
- Avoid mowing wet for safer surface afterwards.

Bee stings:
- Flowering clover and lawn weeds attract bees.
- Bare feet on lawn with bees is a bee sting waiting to happen.
- Keep weeds and clover under control if kids run barefoot.
- For severe bee allergies, consider full broadleaf control programme.

Slip on freshly mown clippings:
- Heavy clippings underfoot can be slippery.
- Mulch mow rather than catch to reduce surface clippings.

Pet and child interaction:
- Same lawn management considerations as dog lawn (urine spots, chemistry timing).

For high-use family lawns:
- Accept that the lawn will show wear and tear.
- Maintenance focused on regular reseeding and fertility programme.
- Designated play areas (trampoline pad, sand pit, paddling pool spot) protect the main lawn.

Cultural expectations:
- A perfect lawn and an active family don't coexist easily.
- Manage for resilience and recovery rather than premium aesthetics.
- The kids' enjoyment is the point - some lawn damage is the cost.

For a kids' play lawn with reasonable expectations, NZLA RSR Ryegrass plus standard fertility programme plus seasonal overseed handles most situations well. Premium aesthetic standards aren't realistic for genuinely played-on lawns.
