# Lawn for Events and Real Estate

Whether you're listing the property or hosting something in the garden, the goal is the same: make the lawn look as good as possible within the available time. The approach is different depending on how many weeks you've got.

## Pre-Listing Lawn Preparation

The lawn features in listing photos and open home first impressions. Buyers notice colour, edges, and visible  before they notice anything else. A tired yellow lawn says "neglected" even if the house is immaculate. A few weeks of focused work changes that.

**8 weeks out:**
- Soil test if the lawn has been neglected and you're not sure what's going on underneath.
- Address any major problems — severe visible large bare patches.
- Apply NZLA All Seasons granular for a steady feeding programme going into the final push.
- Reseed any bare patches with NZLA Starter and the matching seed blend. You need this many weeks for seedlings to establish properly before photos.

**6 weeks out:**
- NZLA Liquid Boost foliar application for a growth and density push.
- Check for and address any . Active  in photos undermines everything else.
- Get into a regular mowing rhythm with a sharp blade at the right height.
- Clean up edges along paths and beds.

**4 weeks out:**
- Monitor. Maintain what you've established.
- Spot-treat any remaining  with NZLA BWC (allow 4 to 6 weeks for treated  to die and clear — this is the last window to use a broadleaf spray without dead yellow patches in the photos).

**2 weeks out:**
- NZLA Iron+ application for colour. Iron gives a deep green response within 5 to 7 days that holds for 2 to 3 weeks — well-timed, this lines up with listing photography.
- Final fertiliser application if needed.
- Last overseed window if any spots remain that need coverage.

**Listing day and photography:**
- Mow within 24 hours of photos. Not the morning of — clippings are still visible and the cut edge hasn't settled.
- Clean up clippings, leaves, and garden debris.
- Sharp edges along paths and beds.
- Water deeply 24 to 48 hours before photos. A well-watered lawn photographs greener and looks cared for. Don't water the morning of — wet lawn looks patchy and reflects light awkwardly.

## Quick Fixes When Time Is Short

Two weeks or less before listing:
- Sharp mow at the right height, clean edges.
- NZLA Iron+ for colour pop — the single biggest visual improvement per dollar spent.
- Hand-weed any particularly obvious  near paths and the front entrance.
- Accept that structural problems (large bare patches, thin lawn) won't be solved.

One week or less:
- Cleanup mowing and edging.
- Iron+ if you haven't already.
- Fresh bark mulch in adjacent garden beds does more for photos than most lawn work you can do in a week.

Don't apply ready-lawn sod in the week before listing. New turf looks like new turf, and buyers notice the seam lines. It can actually undermine the "established and well-maintained" impression you're going for.

## Common Problems and Their Fixes

**Pale or yellow lawn:** NZLA Iron+ one week before photos. If you have more time, Iron+ plus Liquid Boost combined gives both colour and density response.

**Patchy or thin areas:** NZLA Iron+ masks minor thinning in photos by deepening the overall colour. For genuine thin patches with 4 to 6 weeks lead time, overseed with NZLA Starter.

**Visible :** NZLA BWC applied 6 weeks before listing deals with broadleaf  and leaves time for the affected area to recover. Don't spray within 4 weeks of listing photography — dead yellow  look worse in photos than live ones.

**Bare spots:** Reseed if you have 6+ weeks. For shorter timelines, a light top dressing of dark compost over bare areas improves the photo more than bare soil.

## Pre-Event Lawn Setup

For garden parties, weddings, or open days, the preparation logic is similar but the timeline is usually compressed.

**2 to 4 weeks out:**
- NZLA All Seasons or Liquid Boost for a green-up and density push.
- Weed control if  are visible (BWC if broadleaves; allow adequate time for plants to die back).
- Aerate if the lawn has been soft and wet — helps drainage-and-soil-moisture for the event day.

**1 week out:**
- NZLA Iron+ for colour.
- Mow to a consistent height. Consider a slightly lower cut than usual for the event look.
- Edge everything — along paths, beds, paving.

**Day before:**
- Final mow if needed (not the day of — clippings).
- Water if weather has been dry.
- Blow or rake any debris.

**Event day:**
- Don't mow. Don't water. Let the lawn be what it is.

For events with heavy foot traffic on the lawn (marquee, chairs, dancing), accept that the turf will show wear afterwards. NZLA RSR Ryegrass and a post-event fertility application support recovery. Don't try to prevent it with event-day management — it won't work.

## What Actually Shows in Photos

Real estate photographers and buyers respond to: colour intensity, edge definition, and visible  or bare patches. Fine turf perfection isn't what they're assessing. A dense green lawn with clean edges and no obvious  reads as "well-maintained property" — which is all you need.

NZLA Iron+ for colour, clean edges with the trimmer, and basic debris removal handles most pre-listing situations. The 8-week programme is for lawns that genuinely need the work. For a lawn that's already reasonable, 2 to 3 weeks is enough.
