# What's the best lawn for dogs?

Two main issues dogs cause: wear from running and digging, plus urine spots from concentrated nitrogen.

Species selection:

For wear:
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing stoloniferous) recovers from wear faster than standard ryegrass.
- Elfin Tall Fescue (rhizomatous) has underground self-repair.
- Couch and kikuyu have strong wear recovery in upper NI.

For urine tolerance:
- No grass species is fully urine-proof.
- Tall fescue tolerates urine slightly better than ryegrass.
- Couch handles dog urine in upper NI better than cool-season options.

Cultural management:

For wear:
- Higher mowing height (40 to 50 mm) gives more leaf for recovery.
- Heavier fertility programme to support faster recovery.
- Overseeding worn areas in autumn.

For urine:
- Encourage dogs to use a designated spot (mulch or stone area).
- Water urine spots within a few hours to dilute.
- Higher cutting height handles small urine spots better (less concentrated damage).

Designated dog areas:
- Setting aside a section of the property as the dog's spot saves the main lawn.
- Mulch, gravel, or artificial turf in the dog area.
- Treat the remaining lawn as the family/garden area.

Common dog damage patterns:
- Running tracks along fence lines (perimeter patrol).
- Concentrated wear in favourite digging spots.
- Urine spots in regular toileting areas.
- Damage near gate/entry points.

Fixes for specific damage:

Running track fence line:
- Reseed with NZLA RSR Ryegrass for self-repair.
- Or accept the bare track and mulch it.

Concentrated urine area:
- Reseed periodically (every 6 to 12 months).
- Train dog to use a different area.
- Flush with water after urination if possible.

Digging spots:
- Reseed and repair after stopping the digging behaviour.
- Sometimes need physical barrier (rocks, plants) to discourage.

Realistic expectations:
- Dogs and premium lawn don't coexist easily.
- A lawn with dogs will show wear and urine spots regardless of management.
- The right species and cultural management minimise damage but don't eliminate it.
- Accept some damage as part of having dogs.

Chemistry caveats with dogs:
- Some herbicides and fungicides have re-entry intervals for pets.
- Read NZLA product labels for pet safety.
- NZLA Grub+ (insecticide) - keep dogs off treated lawn until watered in and dried.
- Slug pellets containing metaldehyde are toxic to dogs - use iron-based slug bait instead.

For owners genuinely wanting both dogs and a good lawn, the right species choice (RSR Ryegrass) plus a designated dog area for serious traffic and urine handles most situations. Heavy use areas may need periodic re-establishment.
