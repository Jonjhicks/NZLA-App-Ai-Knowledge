# How do I repair dog urine spots in my lawn?

The repair process is the same as any patch repair, but the cause is concentrated nitrogen and salts from urine that have damaged the grass and the soil.

Why urine spots happen:
- Concentrated nitrogen burns the grass at the centre of the spot.
- Salts in urine accumulate in the soil.
- Higher nitrogen at the edge of the spot fertilises a ring of dark green grass.
- Result: dead centre with green ring.

Process for repair:

1. Flush with water:
- Pour 10 to 20 L of water on the spot to dilute the urine salts.
- This should be done within a few hours of urination ideally.
- Less effective after weeks but still helps.

2. Wait 2 to 4 weeks:
- Let the soil chemistry settle.
- Some grass may recover from the edges without further intervention if damage was light.

3. Rake out dead material:
- Remove dead grass and any thatch in the spot.
- Light cultivation of the surface (rake or fork) opens the soil for new seed.

4. Apply NZLA Starter:
- Small amount of NZLA Starter granular sprinkled on the bare area.
- Rake in lightly.

5. Sow seed:
- Match the lawn species (NZLA Perennial Rye or DPR for most lawns).
- Sow at heavier rate than full establishment (40 to 50 g/m²) for fast cover.
- Cover lightly with quality topsoil or compost.

6. Water:
- Light frequent watering until germination (5 to 10 days).
- Once seedlings are 30 to 40 mm, reduce to standard watering.

7. Push recovery:
- NZLA Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 days post-germination, again at 17 to 20 days.
- First mow when seedlings are 70 to 80 mm.

Prevention going forward:
- Designate a specific dog toileting area away from the main lawn.
- Train dogs to use that area.
- Water spots immediately after dog urinates if you catch it.
- Consider supplementing dog's water intake to dilute urine.

Multiple spots:
- For lawns with frequent dog use and many spots, repeated spot repair becomes tiresome.
- Periodic overseeding of high-use areas (every 6 to 12 months) maintains coverage.
- Or accept the pattern and focus on overall lawn health rather than individual spots.

Variations by dog:
- Female dogs typically cause more damage than males (squat urination concentrates the load).
- Larger dogs cause more damage than smaller dogs (more urine per event).
- Older dogs and dogs on medication may produce more concentrated urine.

For the spot's appearance:
- Dead patch with green ring around it is the classic dog urine pattern.
- Distinguish from disease (defined round patch with dead centre but no green ring).
- Diagnosis matters - disease needs chemistry, urine spots need patch repair.

Realistic expectations:
- A lawn with regular dog use will have some urine spots no matter how well managed.
- Patch repair is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time fix.
- Heavy use areas may need to be redesigned (move the dog's spot) rather than continually repaired.
