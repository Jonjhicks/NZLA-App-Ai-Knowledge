# Quick Lawn Improvement for Property Sale

Preparing a lawn for a property sale is a specific job. You're not building a showpiece — you're making it look good in photos and at open homes, on a timeline that might be 4 to 8 weeks. The returns are real: real estate agents consistently cite lawn and garden presentation as a top factor in buyer first impressions.

The game here is maximising visible improvement in the available time, not doing things that take a season to show.

---

## Quick wins (Week 1 to 2)

**Colour first.** Iron and nitrogen are the fastest cosmetic improvement available. NZLA Iron+ at 100 mL/100m² plus Charger at 150 mL/100m², applied together, will deepen colour noticeably within 5 to 7 days. A second application 10 to 14 days later sustains it. This is the single highest-return action for presentation.

**Mow correctly.** A lot of residential lawns are mown too high, too infrequently, and with a blunt blade. Drop the height gradually (never more than 30% at once), sharpen or replace the blade, and mow more frequently in the weeks before listing. A consistently mown, short-ish lawn photographs better than a tall one.

**Edge the boundaries.** Crisp edges at driveway, paths, and garden beds make a lawn look much larger and neater than it is. Takes an hour. High visual return.

**Granular feed.** All Seasons at 2.5 kg/100m² gives sustained colour and growth through the sales period. Apply early in the process — granular takes 7 to 10 days to show.

---

## Weed control (Week 1 to 3)

Visible  are the second-biggest detractor after bare patches. Address them early so the lawn looks clean by listing date.

**Broadleaf ** (clover, dandelion, plantain, oxalis): NZLA Gold (+ Sticker) is the standard treatment. Weeds will die and brown off over 14 to 21 days — plan application far enough ahead that the dead  are gone before the first open home. 3 to 4 weeks before listing is ideal.

**If  are dead but gaps remain** after herbicide: light oversow into the gaps at 15 to 20 g/m² with NZLA Perennial Rye. In warm conditions this fills in within 2 to 3 weeks.

Don't apply herbicide in the week before listing — a lawn covered in brown dying  is worse for presentation than a lawn with some live  in it.

---

## Bare patches (Week 1 to 3)

Bare patches stand out badly in photos. Reseed early in the campaign.

Scarify or rough up the bare soil lightly to create a seedbed. Oversow at 25 to 35 g/m² for full coverage. Water daily for 14 days — consistent moisture is everything for germination. In warm conditions (soil temp above 14°C), ryegrass germinates in 7 to 10 days and shows visible cover within 2 weeks.

NZLA Liquid Starter at sowing (100 to 200 mL/100m²) drives establishment.

---

## Realistic expectations by timeline

**4 to 5 weeks:** Colour improvement, weed dieback complete, bare patches with early germination showing. Very achievable with Iron+/Charger immediately and herbicide Week 1.

**3 weeks:** Colour improvement definite, weed dieback likely still showing. Bare patch reseeding in early stages. Borderline — depends on weather and existing lawn condition.

**Less than 3 weeks:** Focus on mowing quality and iron/nitrogen only. Don't start herbicide this late. Reseed only if bare patches are large and visible.

---

## What not to do

**Don't paint or dye the lawn.** Lawn dye products exist. They look terrible in person even if they photograph acceptably, and a buyer who notices will be immediately suspicious of everything else.

**Don't apply herbicide the week of photos.** Timing matters — see above.

**Don't mow scalp.** Stressed, scalped lawn goes brown within a week of scalping in dry conditions. Maintain the 1/3 rule throughout.

**Don't overseed over a weed-dense lawn.** Seed sown into heavy weed cover will be outcompeted. Kill the  first, then reseed into the gaps 2 to 3 weeks later.
