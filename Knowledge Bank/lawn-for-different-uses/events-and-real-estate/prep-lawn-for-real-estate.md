# How do I prep my lawn for selling the house?

The lawn is one of the first things buyers see. A bit of investment in the lawn pays back in first impressions and price.

Timeline:

8 weeks before listing:
- Soil test if you're concerned about underlying issues.
- Address any major problems (severe weeds, disease, bare patches).
- NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular feed.
- Reseed bare patches with NZLA Starter and Liquid Starter.

6 weeks before listing:
- NZLA Liquid Boost foliar feed for growth and density push.
- Address any disease showing up.
- Cleanup mowing (sharp blade, sensible height).
- Edging clean and tidy.

4 weeks before listing:
- Monitor and maintain.
- Spot treat any remaining weeds.
- Continue regular mowing.

2 weeks before listing:
- NZLA Iron+ application for deep green colour pop.
- Last full fertiliser application if needed.
- Final overseed or patch repair if any spots remain.

Day of listing/photography:
- Mow within 24 hours.
- Clean up clippings, leaves, garden debris.
- Edge along paths and beds.
- Water deeply 24 hours before photos (lawn looks better just after irrigation).

What buyers notice:
- Overall colour and appearance from street view.
- Edges clean or unkempt.
- Visible weeds or bare patches.
- Lawn density.
- General tidiness.

Common problems and quick fixes:

Pale yellow lawn:
- Iron+ application 1 week before photos gives 2 to 3 weeks of strong colour.
- Liquid Boost for added growth response.

Patchy thinning:
- Overseed if 4 to 6 weeks lead time.
- Iron+ application masks minor thinning visually.

Weeds visible:
- Spray with NZLA BWC 4 to 6 weeks before listing.
- Allow time for affected weeds to die and clear.

Bare spots:
- Reseed if 6+ weeks lead time.
- Top dressing of compost or topsoil over bare spots for cosmetic improvement.
- For shorter timeline, ready lawn patches if available.

If timeline is tight (2 weeks or less):
- Cleanup mowing, edging.
- Iron+ for colour pop.
- Hand-weeding visible weeds.
- Accept that major issues won't be fixed.

What not to do:
- Don't apply heavy chemistry the week of photos.
- Don't mow the morning of photos (clippings visible).
- Don't water the morning of photos (wet appearance).
- Don't lay ready lawn 1 week before listing (looks like new turf - which it is).

For premium listings:
- More extensive pre-listing programme.
- Consider professional lawn care 4 to 8 weeks before listing.
- Mature lawn looks more established than fresh repair.

For practical mid-range listings:
- Standard preparation works.
- A "tidy and cared-for" lawn beats "perfect" for most buyers.

The lawn at listing time should look intentional and maintained. Doesn't need to be a showcase, just needs to suggest the property has been looked after.
