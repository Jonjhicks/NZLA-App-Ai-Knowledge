# How do I manage a lawn around a pool?

Pool-adjacent lawn has specific issues: chlorine or salt from pool water, concentrated foot traffic, splash zone wear, sun exposure on concrete surrounds.

Chlorine pool issues:
- Splashed pool water adds chlorine to the lawn at the splash zone.
- Low-level chlorine is fine for grass - it breaks down in soil within hours.
- High concentration or frequent splash can damage adjacent lawn over time.
- Drain pool water onto lawn? Generally no - the volume is too much and chlorinated.

Salt pool issues:
- More damaging long-term than chlorine.
- Salt accumulates in soil at splash zone.
- Periodic freshwater flushing helps.
- Salt-tolerant grass species help.

Splash zone management:
- Hose down the immediate poolside area weekly to dilute chemistry.
- Consider non-lawn surface (paving, decking) for immediate poolside.
- Set lawn back 1 to 2 metres from pool edge if possible.

Foot traffic patterns:
- Concentrated wear from people walking between house and pool.
- Path or stepping stones in the traffic pattern saves the lawn.
- Or accept the worn track and reseed periodically.

Sun exposure:
- Concrete or paving around pool reflects heat onto adjacent lawn.
- Higher water requirements for poolside lawn than open lawn.
- Drought-tolerant species help.

Species selection:
- Tall fescue for general use.
- Salt-tolerant species (couch in upper NI) near salt pools.
- Self-repairing species (RSR Ryegrass, Elfin Tall Fescue) for the wear pattern.

Specific issues:

Algae growth on lawn from pool water:
- Constant moisture from splash zone can encourage algae.
- Better drainage and air movement help.
- Periodic checks for algae and slime mould.

Lawn over old pool excavation:
- Subsidence over years can leave low spots.
- Top dressing and levelling over time corrects.

Pool chemistry impact:
- Modern pool chemistry (low-chlorine, salt systems) less damaging than older heavy chlorine.
- Hydrogen-peroxide pools have minimal lawn impact.
- Older heavily chlorinated pools more damaging.

Practical setup:
- Pool surround of paving or composite decking (1 to 2 metres).
- Lawn beyond that, with consideration for splash zone.
- Path from house to pool (paving or stepping stones).
- Main lawn area away from the pool environment.

For pool removal site:
- Old pool excavation often has poor soil mixed in.
- Soil prep and import of quality topsoil before establishing lawn.
- See lawn-after-construction Q&A for the process.

For new pool installation:
- Plan lawn boundaries with the pool installation.
- Consider drainage from pool surround onto lawn.
- Future maintenance access for both pool and lawn.

For pool-side lawn with realistic expectations:
- Some chemistry exposure is inevitable.
- Some wear is inevitable.
- Setting back from the pool edge protects the main lawn.
- Regular maintenance keeps the area looking good.

Premium-quality lawn isn't realistic right next to a pool. Setting up the design with non-lawn surfaces close to the water and proper lawn further back gets the best of both.
