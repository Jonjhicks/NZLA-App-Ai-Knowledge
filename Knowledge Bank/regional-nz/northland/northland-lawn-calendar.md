# What's the lawn care calendar for Northland?

Northland climate is subtropical-influenced with mild winters and warm humid summers. Both cool-season and warm-season grasses viable, with warm-season (kikuyu, couch) often preferred for summer performance.

January (peak summer):
- Hot humid conditions. Disease pressure (brown patch, pythium) at peak.
- NZLA Azoxy preventative on susceptible lawns.
- Watch for fall armyworm (established in Northland since 2022).
- Wetter or Wetter 3W for moisture management.
- Mow at 45 to 50 mm for cool-season, 30 to 40 mm for warm-season.

February:
- Continued peak summer conditions.
- NZLA Grub+ for grub control.
- Heavy disease pressure window continues.

March (late summer/autumn transition):
- Best fertiliser application of the year for cool-season lawns.
- Begin renovation planning.
- Warm-season turf still growing well.

April:
- Prime renovation window for cool-season turf.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- BWC herbicide application.
- NZLA Blockade autumn pre-emergent on warm-season turf.

May:
- Continued autumn renovation.
- Last big fertiliser application.
- Cool-season pre-emergent if needed.
- Watch for fusarium patch.

June (mild winter):
- Mild conditions year-round. Cool-season lawns still growing.
- Warm-season turf semi-dormant but not fully brown like cooler regions.
- NZLA Green pigment can mask warm-season winter colour loss.
- Some mowing still needed.

July:
- Coldest month but still mild compared to South Island.
- Cool-season lawns grow slowly.
- Plan spring work.

August (late winter):
- Spring pre-emergent (Blockade for warm-season turf) timing.
- First fertiliser as growth picks up.

September (early spring):
- Spring growth strong on warm-season turf.
- Cool-season grasses also active.
- BWC herbicide window.
- Pre-summer wetter agent.

October:
- Heavy spring growth.
- Last big fertiliser before summer.
- PGR application for high-input lawns.

November:
- Late spring building heat.
- Reduce nitrogen.
- Disease prevention strategy in place.

December:
- Early summer. Disease prevention active.
- Deep watering programme.
- PGR application on kikuyu to manage growth.

Northland-specific notes:
- Warm-season turf (NZLA Kikuyu, NZLA Couch) thrives.
- Cool-season turf works but suffers in peak summer.
- Disease pressure high year-round.
- Subtropical pests (fall armyworm, more black beetle activity).
- Coastal salt exposure on many properties.
- High UV exposure in summer.

Species recommendations:
- Kikuyu for low-maintenance high-traffic family lawns.
- Couch for finer textured warm-season lawn.
- Tall fescue blend for cool-season preference with some heat tolerance.
- Avoid pure ryegrass - struggles in summer heat.

Cultural management:
- Watering programme matters less for warm-season turf, more for cool-season.
- PGR essential for kikuyu management.
- Frequent mowing through summer.
- Edging discipline (kikuyu invades fast).

Soil considerations:
- Clay common.
- Some volcanic soils.
- pH often slightly acidic.
- High organic matter possible on long-established sites.

For Northland home lawns, the choice between cool-season and warm-season approach defines the whole programme. Warm-season turf is generally the easier and better-looking choice for summer; cool-season requires more management to survive the peak heat.
