# What's the lawn care calendar for Otago and Southland?

Otago and Southland climate is cold-dominated with cool summers and very cold winters. Cool-season grasses only. Frost and snow possible in some areas.

January (peak summer, mild):
- Summers cooler than rest of NZ.
- Less drought stress than Canterbury or upper NI.
- Standard cool-season programme.
- Mow at 40 to 50 mm.

February:
- Late summer continuing.
- Watch for occasional dry spells.

March (late summer/autumn):
- Best fertiliser of the year. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+.
- Begin renovation planning.
- Cool conditions favour establishment.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- Second autumn fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide if weeds present.
- Disease pressure shifts to fusarium.

May (late autumn):
- Last fertiliser before winter.
- Pre-winter fungicide (Azoxy or Fungus Pro) on susceptible lawns.
- Final renovation finishing.

June (winter):
- Winter dormancy on cool-season grasses.
- Frost protection considerations.
- Minimal intervention.
- Snow possible in inland and southern Southland.

July, August (deep winter):
- Very cold. Snow possible.
- No lawn intervention.
- Equipment maintenance time.

September (early spring):
- Soil temps slowly rising.
- Frost still common.
- Plan but don't push spring action yet.
- First fertiliser late in month if conditions allow.

October (spring):
- Growth resuming.
- First fertiliser application if not done in September.
- BWC herbicide window opens.
- Heavier weed pressure than warmer regions due to weed seed bank.

November:
- Strong spring growth.
- Last big fertiliser push.
- Wetting agent programme starts (Wetter or Wetter 3W).

December (summer):
- Early summer, mild.
- Continued lawn maintenance.
- Lower heat stress than rest of NZ.

Otago-Southland specific notes:
- Cool-season grasses only - kikuyu and couch not viable.
- Tall fescue (NZLA Ultimate or Elfin) excellent for drought tolerance.
- Ryegrass (NZLA Perennial, DPR, MDR, RSR) good general choice.
- Fine fescue for shaded areas works well.
- Winter is the dominant climate driver.
- Snow possible inland (Wanaka, Queenstown, Te Anau) and in Southland generally.
- High UV in summer despite cooler temperatures.

Frost management:
- More frost than rest of NZ.
- Don't apply heavy nitrogen late in autumn (vulnerable soft growth).
- Maintain higher mowing height through autumn.
- Frost cloth practical for high-value small areas.

Snow management:
- Snow on lawn for extended periods can damage turf.
- Pink snow mould (cold-condition fusarium) possible.
- Don't walk on snow-covered lawn (compresses crowns).

Disease pressure:
- Brown patch unusual due to cool summers.
- Fusarium patch / snow mould significant.
- Rust possible in shoulder seasons.

Soil considerations:
- Variable across region.
- Clay in many lowland areas.
- Sandy soils in some coastal Southland.
- pH often acidic.

Wind:
- Inland Otago and Southland exposed to strong winds.
- Coastal Southland deals with salt spray.

For Otago and Southland home lawns, the cool-season programme works well with allowances for the shorter growing season and longer winter dormancy. Spring renovation and fertilising timing slightly later than warmer regions. Disease management focused on cool-season pathogens (fusarium/snow mould).
