# What's the lawn care calendar for Bay of Plenty?

Bay of Plenty climate is warm-temperate with humid summers. Similar to Auckland but slightly warmer summer. Both cool-season and warm-season grasses work.

January (peak summer):
- Hot humid. Disease pressure peak.
- NZLA Azoxy preventative on susceptible lawns.
- Fall armyworm risk (first NZ detection was in BOP).
- Wetter or Wetter 3W programme.
- Mow at higher heights.

February:
- Continued heat and humidity.
- NZLA Grub+ application.
- Disease window continuing.

March (late summer/autumn):
- Best fertiliser application of the year.
- Begin renovation programme.
- Watch for spring dead spot showing in autumn on warm-season turf.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- NZLA Blockade autumn pre-emergent on warm-season turf.
- Second fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide if weeds present.

May:
- Continued renovation finishing.
- Last fertiliser before winter.
- Watch for fusarium patch.

June (mild winter):
- Mild conditions. Cool-season lawns growing slowly.
- Warm-season turf semi-dormant.
- Some mowing still needed.

July:
- Coldest month but mild compared to South Island.
- Slow growth.

August (late winter):
- Spring pre-emergent timing for warm-season turf.
- First fertiliser as growth picks up.

September (early spring):
- Strong spring growth.
- BWC herbicide window.
- Pre-summer wetter agent.

October:
- Heavy growth. Mowing frequency up.
- Last big spring fertiliser.

November:
- Heat building. Reduce nitrogen.
- Disease prevention strategy.

December:
- Early summer. Deep watering programme.

Bay of Plenty specific notes:
- Pumice soils in many areas (parts of Rotorua, Taupo, inland BOP) - sandy with low CEC, leach nutrients fast.
- Mount Maunganui and coastal areas deal with salt spray.
- Tauranga and surrounding areas warm-temperate.
- Inland BOP cooler in winter.
- Fall armyworm first detected here in 2022.
- Documented clopyralid-resistant onehunga in Mount Maunganui area.

Species options:
- Tall fescue blend excellent for the climate.
- Kikuyu and couch viable on coastal lower elevations.
- Ryegrass works but suffers in peak summer.

Soil considerations:
- Pumice and volcanic soils need different management than typical clay or loam.
- More frequent fertiliser at lower rates.
- Wetting agents particularly important (sandy soils prone to hydrophobic).
- Organic matter building over years important.

Disease pressure:
- High due to humidity.
- Brown patch in summer.
- Fusarium patch in autumn-winter.
- Spring dead spot on warm-season turf.

For BOP home lawns, the warm humid summer is the main challenge. Disease prevention, wetter agent use on sandy soils, and species suited to humidity all matter. Coastal and inland areas have different microclimates requiring slight adjustments.
