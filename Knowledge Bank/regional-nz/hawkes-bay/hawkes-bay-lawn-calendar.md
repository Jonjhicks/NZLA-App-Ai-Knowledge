# What's the lawn care calendar for Hawke's Bay?

Hawke's Bay climate is hot dry summers and cool winters. Mediterranean-style climate. Drought is the main lawn challenge. Cool-season grasses dominate but warm-season works too.

January (peak summer):
- Hot and very dry. Drought stress significant.
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W essential for water management.
- Deep watering programme critical.
- Mow at high cutting height (50 to 60 mm).
- Iron+ for colour maintenance.

February:
- Continued hot dry conditions.
- Watch for sustained drought stress.
- Cricket and grub damage possible.

March (late summer):
- Best fertiliser application of the year.
- Begin renovation planning.
- NZLA Grub+ for any grub issues.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window. Cooler temperatures arrive.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- Second autumn fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide if weed pressure built up over summer.

May:
- Continued renovation finishing.
- Last fertiliser before winter.
- Watch for fusarium patch.

June (winter):
- Cool dry winter. Frost possible.
- Slow lawn growth.
- Minimal intervention.

July, August (deep winter):
- Cold but rarely severe.
- Plan spring programme.
- Limited active lawn work.

September (early spring):
- Soil temps rising.
- First fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide window opens.

October:
- Spring growth strong.
- Heavy mowing frequency.
- Pre-summer wetter agent (Wetter 3W).

November:
- Late spring building heat.
- Last big fertiliser push.
- PGR application option.

December:
- Early summer building. Drought already a consideration.
- Deep watering programme in place.

Hawke's Bay specific notes:
- Drought is the dominant climate challenge.
- Cyclone Gabrielle in 2023 caused major damage to lawns across the region - some are still recovering.
- Tall fescue blend (NZLA Ultimate or Elfin) is the smart species choice for drought tolerance.
- Warm-season turf (couch, kikuyu) viable on lower elevations.
- Coastal areas (Napier, Hastings city) milder than inland.
- Inland (Central Hawke's Bay, ranges) more extreme.

Species options:
- Tall fescue for cool-season drought tolerance.
- NZLA Couch for warm-season option in suitable locations.
- Ryegrass works but needs irrigation through summer.
- Mixed blends for combined characteristics.

Soil considerations:
- Variable. Heavy clay in some areas, lighter alluvial soils in others.
- River plains have rich deep soils.
- Hillside lawns often have shallower soils.

Drought management:
- Wetting agent programme essential.
- Deep watering when allowed.
- Drought-tolerant species selection.
- Higher mowing height through summer.
- Realistic expectations during water restrictions.

Disease pressure:
- Lower than humid regions.
- Fusarium patch in autumn-winter.
- Brown patch occasional in humid summer spells.

For Hawke's Bay home lawns, the dry summer is the main challenge. Species selection plus wetting agent programme plus consistent irrigation gets a good lawn. Without irrigation infrastructure, the lawn will brown off in summer and recover in autumn - this is normal for the region.

Post-Gabrielle recovery is still relevant for many properties in 2025-2026. See recovering-from-cyclone Q&A for the process.
