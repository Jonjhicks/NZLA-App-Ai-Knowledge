# What's the lawn care calendar for Wellington?

Wellington climate is mild-temperate with strong wind year-round. Less extreme heat than Auckland but cool damp winters. Cool-season grasses dominate.

January (peak summer):
- Less extreme heat than Auckland or Canterbury.
- Wind can dry lawns despite reasonable temperatures.
- NZLA Wetter for water retention.
- Continue regular watering.
- Mow at 45 to 50 mm.

February:
- Late summer dry spells possible.
- Monitor for disease and dry patch.

March (late summer):
- Best fertiliser of the year. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+.
- Begin renovation planning.
- Apply NZLA Grub+ for any grub issues.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window. Soil still warm, conditions becoming favourable.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- Second fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide if weeds present.

May:
- Cool damp conditions starting.
- Watch for fusarium patch.
- Preventative fungicide if patch history.
- Last fertiliser before winter.

June (winter):
- Cool damp conditions.
- Slow growth but not dormant.
- Some mowing still needed (every 3 to 4 weeks).
- Watch for winter disease.

July, August (deep winter):
- Coldest months but rarely severe frost in central Wellington.
- Hutt Valley and inland areas colder.
- Limited intervention.

September (early spring):
- Soil temps rising.
- First spring fertiliser later in month.
- BWC herbicide window opens.

October:
- Strong spring growth.
- Heavy mowing frequency.
- Foliar feeding.
- Pre-summer wetter agent application.

November:
- Last big fertiliser push before summer.
- PGR application option.

December:
- Early summer. Build up watering programme.

Wellington-specific notes:
- Wind is the constant. Even on calm summer days, exposed sites dry out faster than sheltered.
- Coastal Wellington deals with salt spray.
- Inland (Karori, Khandallah, hills) has its own microclimates.
- Hutt Valley colder than central Wellington in winter.
- Kapiti and Wairarapa different climate again (more like central NI).

Cool-season grasses thrive:
- Ryegrass (NZLA Perennial Rye, DPR, MDR) excellent.
- Tall fescue good for drought tolerance in exposed sites.
- Fine fescue good for shaded areas.
- Warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) generally not suited.

Wind management:
- Windbreaks transform exposed lawn microclimates.
- Drought-tolerant species help with wind desiccation.
- Higher mowing height for some protection.

Disease pressure:
- Moderate. Fusarium patch in cool damp shoulder seasons.
- Less brown patch than warmer regions due to milder summers.

Soil considerations:
- Variable across the region.
- Clay common in Hutt Valley.
- Sandy soils in some coastal areas.

For Wellington home lawns, the year-round cool-season programme works well. Wind management is the additional consideration that matters more than in most NZ regions.
