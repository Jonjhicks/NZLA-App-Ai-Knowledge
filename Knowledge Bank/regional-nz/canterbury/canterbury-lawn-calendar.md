# What's the lawn care calendar for Canterbury?

Canterbury climate is dry hot summers and cold winters. Coastal Christchurch milder than inland (Mackenzie, central Canterbury). Drought is the main challenge. Cool-season grasses dominate.

January (peak summer):
- Hot dry conditions. Lawn often brown without irrigation.
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W critical for water management.
- Deep infrequent watering (25 mm twice a week).
- Mow at 50 to 60 mm for soil protection.
- Avoid heavy chemistry on stressed turf.

February:
- Continued heat and drought stress.
- Nor'wester wind events dry the lawn fast.
- Watch for grass grub damage starting.

March (late summer):
- Best fertiliser application of the year. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+.
- Begin renovation programme as temperatures cool.
- Apply NZLA Grub+ for grub control in late summer.
- Hydrate the lawn before any chemistry.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window. Soil temps 12 to 18 °C for ryegrass seeding.
- Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- Second fertiliser application of autumn.
- BWC herbicide if weed control needed.
- Tall fescue overseeding for drought tolerance going forward.

May:
- Last autumn opportunity for renovation.
- Final fertiliser before winter.
- Watch for fusarium patch as conditions cool.

June (winter):
- Winter dormancy begins for cool-season grasses.
- Frost protection considerations (frost cloth for high-value areas).
- Minimal mowing.

July, August (deep winter):
- Cold and often frosty.
- No fertilising.
- Plan spring renovation work.

September (early spring):
- Soil temps rising slowly. Frost still possible.
- First spring fertiliser application later in month.
- Spring weeds emerging.

October:
- Spring growth resumes.
- BWC herbicide window opens.
- NZLA Wetter 3W in late October to set up summer.

November:
- Heavy spring growth. Last big fertiliser before summer.
- PGR application for high-input lawns.
- Increase mowing frequency.

December (early summer):
- Heat building. Reduce nitrogen.
- Begin deep watering programme.
- Watch for early summer drought stress.

Canterbury-specific notes:
- Drought is the dominant challenge - irrigation infrastructure important.
- Tall fescue blend (NZLA Ultimate or Elfin) is the smart species choice for drought tolerance.
- Hot dry nor'westers dry lawns fast in summer.
- Cold winters with frost - frost-tolerant species important.
- Cool-season grasses only - warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) marginal at best.
- Inland Canterbury (Methven, Geraldine) colder than Christchurch.
- High UV in summer adds stress.

Soil considerations:
- Heavy clay in many areas - aeration important.
- Some areas have light shingle soils - water and fertiliser leach fast.
- Soil pH often acidic - periodic lime may be needed.

Irrigation strategy:
- Deep watering twice a week through summer.
- NZLA Wetter 3W programme for sandy areas.
- Smart controllers worth the investment for water savings.

Disease pressure:
- Lower than humid regions due to dry summers.
- Fusarium patch in autumn-winter.
- Rust in cool wet shoulder seasons.

For Canterbury home lawns, the right species choice plus a wetter agent programme plus reliable irrigation gets a good-looking lawn through the worst summers. Without those, lawns brown off through summer and recover in autumn.
