# Hawke's Bay Lawn Calendar

Hawke's Bay has a Mediterranean-style climate: hot dry summers and cool wet winters. It's one of the driest regions in New Zealand through the summer months, which is the single biggest factor in lawn management here.

Disease pressure is lower than humid regions in summer, but fusarium patch in autumn and winter and the occasional brown patch event in a humid summer spell are still real. The drought challenge outweighs the  challenge for most lawns.

## Species Choice

Tall fescue is the right choice for Hawke's Bay. NZLA Elfin Tall Fescue or Ultimate blend handles extended dry periods significantly better than ryegrass. A Hawke's Bay lawn on pure ryegrass without reliable irrigation-general goes dormant by January and recovers slowly in autumn. The same lawn on tall fescue manages through the same dry summer with far less stress.

Couch is viable in the lower-elevation areas around Napier and Hastings and handles drought well once established. Worth considering for lawns where low summer maintenance input is the priority.

RSR Ryegrass as a component in a mixed blend adds self-repairing wear tolerance useful for family lawns.

## The Annual Calendar

**January — hot and dry.** Peak drought stress for most lawns. NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W is important for maximising irrigation-general efficiency. Deep infrequent watering — 25 mm twice a week — is more effective than frequent light watering. Mow at 50 to 60 mm for soil protection and to reduce water demand. Iron+ maintains colour during dry periods. Hold off on heavy nitrogen on drought-stressed turf.

**February.** Continued dry conditions. Grub damage or cricket damage may be appearing. Monitor for signs — brown patches that lift like carpet (grass grubs) or irregular surface feeding.

**March — best fertiliser timing.** Heat easing, ground still warm. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+. Begin renovation planning. NZLA Grub+ if grub numbers are present. Hydrate the lawn before any chemistry application.

**April — renovation window.** Soil temperatures 14 to 18°C — good ryegrass germination conditions. Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed with NZLA Starter. Second autumn fertiliser. BWC for broadleaf . Fusarium patch watch begins as nights cool.

**May.** Continued autumn renovation. Last fertiliser before winter. Preventative NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro on lawns with fusarium history.

**June — cooler and wetter.** Growth slowing. Wet winters bring fusarium risk. Mowing frequency reduces. No nitrogen fertiliser.

**July–August — winter.** Cool and often wet. Inland areas (Central Hawke's Bay) can be frosty. Limited intervention. Plan spring programme.

**September — early spring.** Warming up. First spring fertiliser. BWC herbicide window opens.

**October.** Strong spring growth. Weekly mowing. NZLA Wetter 3W applied late October — getting ahead of the coming dry season early is smart in Hawke's Bay.

**November.** Last big fertiliser push before summer. PGR for high-input lawns.

**December — early summer.** Drought pressure already building. Deep watering programme in place.

## Hawke's Bay-Specific Notes

**Cyclone Gabrielle (February 2023)** caused widespread lawn damage across the region from flood inundation, silt deposition, and debris. Many Hawke's Bay lawns are still in recovery or were fully re-established after Gabrielle. If you're dealing with a post-flood lawn, the priority order is: drainage-and-soil-moisture and surface restoration first, core-aeration relief (core aeration), then soil rehabilitation before re-establishing lawn. NZLA Restore, NZLA Starter, and the appropriate seed blend are the tools for re-establishment.

**Drought is the dominant challenge.** Irrigation infrastructure makes a significant difference to what you can achieve year-round. Without irrigation-general, the approach is autumn-focused: do the work in autumn when the conditions are right, maintain through winter, accept summer dormancy, repeat. With reliable irrigation-general and tall fescue as the base, a good-looking year-round lawn is achievable.

**Inland Hawke's Bay** (Central Hawke's Bay, hill country, Elsthorpe area) is colder and drier than coastal Napier/Hastings. Frost timing is earlier and spring warming is later. Species choice matters more here — tall fescue's cold tolerance as well as its drought tolerance makes it the right call.

**Water restrictions** are a regular summer feature in Hawke's Bay. A pre-established wetting agent programme (NZLA Wetter 3W from October) and a drought-tolerant species blend reduce how hard those restrictions hit. A lawn on tall fescue under water restrictions will look substantially better than the same management approach on pure ryegrass.
