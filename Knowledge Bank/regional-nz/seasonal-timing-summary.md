# NZ Regional Seasonal Timing Summary

New Zealand runs four distinct lawn care climatic zones. Timing in Northland is 4 to 6 weeks ahead of Canterbury for the same seasonal actions. Otago and Southland run a further 2 to 4 weeks behind Canterbury.

---

## Key seasonal windows by region

| Action | Northland/Auckland | Waikato/BOP/Hawke's Bay | Wellington/Nelson/Marlborough | Canterbury | Otago/Southland |
|--------|---|---|---|---|---|
| First spring granular fertiliser | Late Aug | Early Sep | Mid Sep | Late Sep | Early Oct |
| Pre-emergent poa/weed application | Late Jul | Early Aug | Mid Aug | Late Aug | Sep |
| Main weed herbicide (spring) | Sep | Sep-Oct | Oct | Oct-Nov | Nov |
| Grass grub treatment (Grub+) | Sep–Oct & Mar | Sep–Oct & Mar | Sep–Oct & Mar | Sep–Oct & Mar | Sep–Oct & Mar |
| Armyworm/porina watch begins | Jan | Jan-Feb | Feb | Feb-Mar | Mar |
| Last granular before winter | Apr | Apr-May | May | May | May-Jun |
| Aeration (autumn, primary window) | Apr-May | Apr-May | May | May-Jun | May-Jun |
| Oversowing/renovation (autumn) | Mar-Apr | Apr | Apr-May | Apr-May | May |
| Oversowing/renovation (spring) | Sep | Sep-Oct | Oct | Oct | Oct-Nov |
| PGR (Primo) last autumn application | Apr | Apr-May | May | Late Apr | Mid-Apr |
| Fusarium watch window | May-Aug | Jun-Aug | May-Sep | May-Oct | Apr-Oct |
| Dollar spot watch window | Dec-Apr | Dec-Apr | Jan-Apr | Jan-Mar | Jan-Mar |

---

## Cool-season vs warm-season

**Cool-season turf** (ryegrass, fescue): actively grows in spring and autumn, slows in summer heat and winter cold. Main fertilising and renovation windows are Mar-May and Sep-Oct.

**Warm-season turf** (kikuyu, couch): actively grows Oct-Apr, dormant or very slow May-Sep. Main growing season work happens Nov-Mar. Renovations in spring (Oct-Nov) after last frost risk.

## Soil temperature as a guide

More reliable than calendar dates for many operations.

| Soil temperature | What to do / watch for |
|---|---|
| Below 8°C | Minimal root activity. No granular fertiliser. Foliar only if needed. |
| 8-12°C | Slow growth resuming. Careful foliar feeding. |
| 12-15°C | Active spring growth beginning. First granular application. Start PGR programme. |
| 15-20°C | Main growing season. Full programme. |
| 20-26°C | Peak summer. Disease pressure high. Liquid N caution above 26°C. |
| Above 26°C | Avoid liquid nitrogen. Reduce mowing height stress. |

NZ soil temperatures by region are tracked by NIWA. The NZLA App GDD tracker uses soil temperature data to calculate biological timing.

---

## Annual rhythm by season

**Spring (Aug-Nov by region):** Fertilise, pre-emergent applications, first mow of year, PGR programme start, renovation/oversowing, aeration if not done in autumn.

**Summer (Nov-Mar):** Disease pressure management, irrigation-general, reduced granular nitrogen in heat, PGR maintenance, pest watch (armyworm, grass grub).

**Autumn (Mar-Jun):** Primary renovation window, aeration, oversowing, pre-emergent reapplication, last granular, transition to foliar programme.

**Winter (Jun-Aug):** Foliar feeding only, fungal monitoring (fusarium), minimal disturbance, no herbicide in cold wet conditions, don't mow wet grass.
