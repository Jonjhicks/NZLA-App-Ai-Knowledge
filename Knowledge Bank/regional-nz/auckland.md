# Auckland Lawn Calendar

Auckland's warm humid climate is good for grass and good for . Both need planning for.

The dominant challenges are summer  pressure (particularly brown patch and pythium in January and February) and humidity-driven moisture management. Cool-season ryegrass and fescue handle the climate but struggle in peak summer heat. Warm-season kikuyu and couch are viable and often easier to manage year-round.

## Species Choice

Most Auckland home lawns run cool-season ryegrass or a tall fescue blend. These look good through autumn, winter, and spring but can look tired in February. NZLA RSR Ryegrass is the better ryegrass choice for Auckland — the self-repairing stolon system copes with the recovery cycles better than standard ryegrass.

Kikuyu and couch handle Auckland's summer well and are worth considering on properties where summer performance matters more than winter colour. Both go semi-dormant in the cooler months and benefit from NZLA Green for cosmetic reasons if needed.

Tall fescue blends (NZLA Ultimate, Elfin) offer a middle ground — cool-season preference with better summer tolerance than pure ryegrass.

## The Annual Calendar

**January — peak heat and humidity.** Disease pressure at its highest. Brown patch, pythium, and dollar spot all active. Apply NZLA Azoxy preventatively on any lawn with  history. NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W combats dry patch on water-repellent areas. Mow at 45 to 50 mm. Hold off on heavy nitrogen fertiliser on heat-stressed lawns — it promotes the soft growth  loves. Iron+ for colour if the lawn looks flat.

**February — continued peak conditions.** Fall armyworm pressure in upper Auckland (established pest since 2022). Check for surface-feeding caterpillar damage — brown patches overnight with moth activity visible at dusk. NZLA Grub+ for fall armyworm; apply in the evening and water in lightly. Continue  management. Deep watering twice weekly where possible.

**March — the most important month.** The heat is easing and the ground is still warm. This is the year's best fertiliser timing. Apply NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ now. Start the renovation programme: scarify thatch, overseed bare or thin areas. NZLA Etho applied now begins the autumn poa control programme if needed.

**April — renovation window.** Soil temperatures still 15 to 18°C — ryegrass seed germinates in 7 to 10 days. Core aerate, top dress, overseed. Second autumn fertiliser application. NZLA Blockade autumn pre-emergent on warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) to suppress annual grass. BWC for broadleaf . Disease pressure shifting from summer pathogens toward fusarium — watch for it as nights cool.

**May.** Last solid fertiliser opportunity before winter. Final timing for ryegrass seeding-and-over-seeding into warm enough soil. Preventative NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro on any lawn that's had fusarium history.

**June — winter.** Mowing drops to every 2 to 3 weeks. No nitrogen fertiliser. Watch for fusarium patch in cool damp conditions — circular patches 50 to 200 mm across with a water-soaked edge are the tell. NZLA Fungus Pro or Azoxy curative if it appears.

**July — minimum intervention.** Slow growth. Disease management continues if pressure is high. This is the month to service equipment and plan the spring programme.

**August.** Soil temperatures beginning to creep up. Spring pre-emergent window opens for Blockade on warm-season turf. Start watching for spring weed flush.

**September — spring starts.** First spring fertiliser application: NZLA All Seasons. Resume regular mowing as growth picks up. BWC herbicide window opens for broadleaf . Warm-season turf breaking dormancy.

**October.** Strong growth. Mowing frequency up to weekly or more for vigorous lawns. Foliar feed with NZLA Liquid Boost and Iron+ for a spring push. NZLA Wetter 3W pre-summer application to set up water management for summer.

**November.** Last big fertiliser before summer. Disease pressure building as temperatures climb — preventative Azoxy on susceptible lawns. PGR application for high-input lawns wanting controlled growth and density. Kikuyu edging becoming a weekly job.

**December — early summer.** Deep watering programme established. Disease prevention strategy in place. Mow at higher heights as summer heat builds. Watch for first signs of brown patch.

## Auckland-Specific Notes

Coastal areas — west coast Auckland, Hauraki Gulf islands, eastern suburbs — deal with salt spray affecting lawns near the sea. More frequent shallow watering and salt-tolerant species help. RSR Ryegrass and tall fescue handle it reasonably well.

Disease pressure in Auckland is higher than anywhere south of it. If you're running a cool-season lawn and not running any  prevention, you'll eventually have a summer  event. NZLA Azoxy on a preventative programme from November through March is the sensible approach for lawns that have had issues before.

Fall armyworm is established in Northland and increasingly regular in Auckland. Learn to identify the early signs: irregular brown patches that appear fast (overnight to within a few days), visible caterpillars when you pull back the turf, moth activity at dusk. NZLA Grub+ applied in the evening with a light irrigation-general after is the treatment.
