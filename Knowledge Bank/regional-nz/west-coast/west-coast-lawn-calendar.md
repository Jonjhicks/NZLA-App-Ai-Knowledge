# What's the lawn care calendar for the West Coast?

West Coast South Island climate is very wet year-round with mild temperatures. Waterlogging and disease are the main challenges. Cool-season grasses dominate.

January (summer):
- Mild summers. Rainfall still significant.
- NZLA Penetrate moves surface water down through profile.
- Standard cool-season programme.
- Disease pressure higher than dry regions.

February:
- Continued mild wet conditions.
- Some periods of drier weather.
- Watch for moss build-up where canopy stays wet.

March (autumn):
- Best fertiliser application of the year.
- Begin renovation planning.
- Continued wet conditions need addressing.

April (autumn):
- Prime renovation window where conditions allow.
- Scarify (avoid heavy scarifying in wet conditions).
- Core aerate to improve drainage.
- Second fertiliser application.

May (late autumn):
- Final renovation timing.
- Last fertiliser before winter.
- Disease pressure (fusarium) building.
- Preventative fungicide consideration.

June, July (winter):
- Very wet. Frequent rainfall.
- Disease pressure high.
- NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro for fusarium control.
- Don't walk on saturated lawn.

August:
- Late winter. Continue disease vigilance.

September (early spring):
- Soil temps rising. Growth picking up.
- First fertiliser application.
- BWC herbicide if conditions allow.

October:
- Strong spring growth.
- Continue disease management.

November:
- Heavy growth. Last big spring fertiliser.

December:
- Early summer. Mild conditions.

West Coast specific notes:
- High rainfall is the dominant climate factor (some areas exceed 5000 mm/year).
- Waterlogging issues common.
- Drainage matters enormously.
- Disease pressure year-round.
- Cool-season grasses only.
- Mild temperatures support year-round growth.

Soil considerations:
- Heavy clay common.
- Drainage installation often necessary for lawn quality.
- Organic matter accumulates faster than drier regions.
- pH often acidic.

Species selection:
- Tall fescue blend handles wet conditions reasonably well.
- Ryegrass works but susceptible to disease in wet conditions.
- Fine fescue in shaded areas (lots of bush cover in West Coast properties).

Drainage management:
- Surface and subsurface drainage often necessary.
- NZLA Aerate and Penetrate programme through wet seasons.
- Higher mowing height for soil protection.
- Reduce traffic on wet lawn.

Disease management:
- Year-round watchfulness.
- Azoxy and Fungus Pro on rotation.
- Cultural management (improve drainage, reduce nitrogen, address shade).

Fertility:
- High rainfall leaches nutrients.
- More frequent applications at lower rates.
- Slow-release granulars more efficient than fast-release.

Wind exposure:
- Some West Coast lawns also wind-exposed (coastal locations).
- Combined wet plus wind is challenging.

For West Coast home lawns, drainage and disease management dominate the programme. Standard cool-season approach with extra attention to surface and subsurface water management. Lawn appearance can be excellent in summer but requires year-round vigilance.

For some West Coast properties, lawn isn't the right answer - the conditions favour native bush integration or designed wet-tolerant gardens rather than fighting the climate.
