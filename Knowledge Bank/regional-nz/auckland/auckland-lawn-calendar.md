# What's the lawn care calendar for Auckland?

Auckland climate is warm-temperate, with mild winters and warm humid summers. Cool-season ryegrass and fescue dominate but warm-season kikuyu and couch also viable.

January (mid-summer):
- Peak heat and humidity. Disease pressure (brown patch, pythium) at maximum.
- NZLA Azoxy preventative on susceptible lawns.
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W for dry patch.
- Continue regular deep watering.
- Mow at the higher end of normal heights (45 to 50 mm).
- Skip fertiliser on heat-stressed lawns.

February:
- Continued heat. Watch for fall armyworm in upper Auckland areas (established pest since 2022).
- Apply NZLA Grub+ if grub damage showing.
- NZLA Iron+ for colour as lawns can look tired.

March (late summer/early autumn):
- Most important fertiliser application of the year. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+.
- Best time to start renovation programme.
- Begin overseeding worn or thin areas.
- NZLA Etho if poa control programme starts.

April:
- Peak renovation window. Scarify, core aerate, top dress, overseed.
- Cool soil temperatures still high enough for ryegrass germination (10 to 15 days).
- Second autumn fertiliser application.
- NZLA Blockade autumn pre-emergent on warm-season turf (registered for kikuyu and couch).
- Disease pressure shifts toward fusarium - watch for it.

May:
- Last decent fertiliser opportunity before winter.
- Final renovation timing for ryegrass overseed.
- Autumn fungicide preventative (Azoxy or Fungus Pro) on susceptible lawns.

June (winter starts):
- Reduced mowing (every 2 to 3 weeks).
- Watch for fusarium patch in cool damp spells.
- No fertiliser through deep winter.

July:
- Low growth period.
- Disease management continues if pressure is high.
- Minimal lawn intervention.

August:
- Late winter, plan for spring.
- Soil temps starting to rise (10 to 12 °C ground).
- Time to apply spring pre-emergent (Blockade on warm-season turf).

September (spring):
- First spring fertiliser application (NZLA All Seasons).
- Resume regular mowing as growth picks up.
- BWC herbicide application if needed.
- Soil temps reaching ryegrass active range.

October:
- Heavy growth period. Mowing frequency increases.
- Foliar feeding (Liquid Boost, Iron+) for spring push.
- Pre-summer wetter agent (Wetter 3W) application.

November:
- Late spring, getting hot. Last big fertiliser push before summer.
- Disease pressure starting to build.
- PGR application (NZLA PGR) for high-input lawns.

December:
- Early summer. Deep watering programme.
- Disease prevention strategy in place.
- Mow at higher height as heat builds.

Auckland-specific notes:
- Annual rainfall reasonable, summer dry spells common but not severe.
- Disease pressure higher than southern regions due to humidity.
- Warm-season turf viable - kikuyu and couch grow well.
- Coastal areas (Auckland west coast, Hauraki Gulf) deal with salt spray.
- Cool-season ryegrass and tall fescue still the dominant choice for most home lawns.
