# How do I identify black beetle in my lawn?

The adult is a shiny black beetle about 12 to 15 mm long, hard-shelled, oval-shaped. You'll see them at the soil surface or flying around on warm evenings, particularly late spring and through summer. They're not the lawn killer though, the larvae are.

The larvae (white grubs) live in the top 50 to 100 mm of soil. White curl-shaped grub, brown head capsule, three pairs of legs near the head, body curls into a C when you pick it up. About 10 to 15 mm long when mature. They look very similar to grass grub larvae but black beetle grubs are usually a bit slimmer.

The damage shows up as yellowing patches that pull up like loose carpet. The grubs are chewing the roots off underneath. If you can lift a square of turf by hand and see grubs in the soil below, that's your confirmation.

In NZ, black beetle is more of an upper North Island problem (Auckland up). Heavy clay paddocks and warm-season turf get hit hardest, but kikuyu and ryegrass lawns can both suffer.

Treat with NZLA Grub+ when larvae are small (late summer through autumn). The smaller they are, the easier they're killed.
