# Are the adult black beetles themselves harmful to my lawn?

Not really. The adults chew a bit of grass at the base and they fly around at night, but the meaningful damage is from the larvae underneath the surface eating the roots. You can have plenty of adult beetles around without major turf damage, then six weeks later the larvae from those eggs start ruining the lawn.

So when you see adults active in late spring and early summer, that's your warning. Eggs are being laid into the lawn. Six to eight weeks after peak adult activity, the larvae will be small and feeding, which is the right window to apply NZLA Grub+. Hit them while they're small and shallow. Once they're full-size grubs feeding hard in autumn, the damage is already done and control gets harder.

Squashing the odd adult you see at the back door isn't going to save the lawn. It's the timing of the larval treatment that matters.
