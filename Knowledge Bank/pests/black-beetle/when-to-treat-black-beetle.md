# When should I treat for black beetle?

Target the larvae when they're small. In most of the upper North Island, that's late January through to late March. You're applying after peak adult flight (December into January) once eggs have hatched and the new larvae are feeding near the surface.

Apply NZLA Grub+ to a well-watered lawn and water it in straight after. The active ingredient needs to move down through the thatch into the top of the soil where the grubs are feeding. Dry application sitting on the surface isn't going to do much.

If you wait until autumn when grubs are big and damage is visible, treatment still helps but recovery is slower because the roots are already chewed and the turf has less to work with going into winter.

One application timed right is usually all you need for the season. Repeat treatments aren't recommended, label rates are set for a reason.
