# What is eating the grass tips at night?

Most likely a surface-feeding caterpillar. Three candidates in NZ:

**Sod webworms.** Small grey-brown caterpillars, about 20 to 25 mm at maturity, living in silk-lined tunnels at the surface. Feed at night by chewing grass blades off near the base. Damage shows up as small irregular brown patches. Peak pressure late summer through autumn.

**Lawn armyworm (or other armyworm species).** Larger caterpillars (40 to 50 mm), green-brown with dark stripes. Feed in groups and can do significant damage fast. The damage looks like the lawn has been "shaved" — even cutting off tips across a wider area. Worst in summer through early autumn.

**Porina (in colder months).** Brown to grey caterpillars up to 60 mm, in vertical burrows in the soil. Feed at night on grass at the burrow entrance. Damage looks like patches with visible small holes (5 to 8 mm wide). Pressure builds autumn into winter.

How to confirm which: soapy water flush. Mix dish soap with water in a watering can and pour over the damaged area. All three caterpillar types come to the surface within minutes. The size, colour and behaviour tell you which.

Treatment: NZLA Grub+ (chlorantraniliprole 200 g/L) is on label for all three. Up to 6 months control. Maximum 1 application per year.

For surface-feeders (sod webworm, armyworm), delay watering and mowing 24 hours after application so the chemical stays on the leaf where the caterpillars feed.

For porina (which retreat into burrows), the irrigation rule is mixed — they feed at the surface but burrow into soil. Light irrigation after application is fine.

Cultural side: stressed lawns (drought, low fertility, poor health) are more vulnerable. Healthy thick turf shrugs off light pressure without intervention.
