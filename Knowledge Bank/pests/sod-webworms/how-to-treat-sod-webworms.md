# How do I treat sod webworms?

Sod webworms are caterpillars (around 15 to 20 mm long when mature) that live in silk-lined tunnels in the thatch and chew grass blades at night. You'll see patchy thinning, sometimes a chewed-down appearance, and if you part the grass on a still morning you can spot the fine silk webbing at the soil surface.

In NZ they're less common than grass grub or porina but do show up, particularly in warm-season turf and the upper North Island.

NZLA Grub+ controls them. Apply in the evening, water in lightly, the active needs to contact the caterpillars when they're feeding above the soil at night.

The adults are small tan-coloured moths that fly low over the lawn at dusk. If you're seeing a lot of those moths through summer evenings, that's a heads-up that you might have webworm pressure building.

Cultural prevention is the same as for other surface-feeding pests: dense well-watered turf with adequate fertility tolerates damage better than thin, stressed turf.
