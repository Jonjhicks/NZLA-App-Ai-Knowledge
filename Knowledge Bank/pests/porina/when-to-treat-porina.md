# When should I treat for porina?

Autumn, when the caterpillars are small. March through May depending on region. The smaller they are, the easier to control. Once they're full-grown and burrowing deep in late winter, control becomes much harder.

Apply NZLA Grub+ in the evening or late afternoon to a moist lawn, then water in lightly. The active needs to be there when the caterpillars come up at night to feed. A heavy watering immediately after can wash too much down the burrows past the surface where they're feeding, so a light irrigation rather than a soaking is the better approach.

The adult moths fly in spring and summer and lay eggs into pasture and lawn. Eggs hatch into tiny caterpillars in early autumn. That's the window. By mid-winter the caterpillars are 30 to 50 mm long, deep in burrows, and far less susceptible to surface-applied chemistry.

If you've had porina before, treat as a preventative each autumn rather than waiting to see the damage.
