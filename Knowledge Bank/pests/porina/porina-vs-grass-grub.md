# What's the difference between porina and grass grub?

Both are NZ native lawn pests but they're different animals causing different damage.

Porina is the larva of a moth (Wiseana species). Caterpillar shape, grey-brown, soft-bodied, up to 50 mm long. Lives in vertical burrows in the soil and comes up at night to chew grass blades. Damage shows in autumn and winter. Small burrow holes (5 to 10 mm) visible at the soil surface are the dead giveaway.

Grass grub is the larva of a beetle (Costelytra giveni). C-shaped curl, white body with a tan head, three pairs of legs near the head, up to 15 to 20 mm long. Lives in the soil and feeds on grass roots. Damage shows as yellowing patches that lift easily because the roots are chewed off. Peak damage is autumn into early winter. No surface holes, the grubs stay underground.

If you see a curl-shaped white grub when you dig down, it's grass grub. If you see a long grey caterpillar coming up at night from a burrow, it's porina.
