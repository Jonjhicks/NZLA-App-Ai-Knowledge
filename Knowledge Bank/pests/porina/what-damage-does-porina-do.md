# What damage does porina do to lawns?

Porina caterpillars live in vertical burrows in the soil and come up at night to feed on grass blades. You'll see thinning patches with a chewed-down appearance, and if you look closely there will be small (5 to 10 mm) holes in the soil surface, which are the burrow entrances.

The caterpillars themselves are grey-brown, soft-bodied, up to 50 mm long when mature. Distinct from grass grub which is curl-shaped and white.

Damage is usually visible in autumn and winter, peaking from May through August in most of the country. South Island, Manawatu, Wairarapa, lower Waikato and Taranaki tend to have the worst problems.

Heavy infestations can thin a lawn out significantly over a single autumn. Light pressure causes more cosmetic issues, a few burrow holes and minor thinning.

NZLA Grub+ controls porina if applied when the caterpillars are small (autumn). Once they're big and well-established in their burrows, control gets much harder, the active ingredient needs to make contact with them above ground at night.
