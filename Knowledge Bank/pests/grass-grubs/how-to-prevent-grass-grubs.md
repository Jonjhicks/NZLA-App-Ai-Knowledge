# How do I prevent grass grubs in my lawn?

Apply NZLA Grub+ in late summer to early autumn (February through March in most regions) before damage shows. Targeting small larvae is the most effective option. Water the lawn well before applying, then water in straight after so the active moves down to where the grubs are feeding.

Beyond chemistry, a healthy dense sward with good root depth tolerates a bit of grub pressure better than a thin stressed lawn. Adequate watering through summer, balanced fertiliser, and good mowing height all help. Stressed lawns get smashed by grubs, vigorous ones cope.

If you've had a grub problem before, treat annually as part of a programme. Once they're established in an area, the moths keep coming back. One year of treatment doesn't clear them permanently.

There's no realistic biological control product for home lawns in NZ. The pasture-focused Bt-based products work in farming but aren't practical at home-lawn scale.
