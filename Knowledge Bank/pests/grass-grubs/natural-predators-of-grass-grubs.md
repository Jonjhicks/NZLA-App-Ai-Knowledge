# Do any natural predators eat grass grubs?

Yes. Magpies, starlings and blackbirds will tear a lawn apart to get at grass grubs. If you see birds working a section of lawn intensively in autumn, especially repeatedly returning to the same patches, there's a fair chance you've got grubs underneath.

The birds aren't going to clear an infestation. They'll get some but they're also pulling the lawn around in the process, which causes its own damage on top of the grub problem. The lawn is essentially being attacked from both directions.

Some research has looked at predatory nematodes and fungal pathogens as biological controls. None are commercially viable for home lawn use in NZ at present.

Hedgehogs eat grubs and other soil invertebrates too, but populations are nowhere near high enough to make a dent in a serious infestation.

If birds are working your lawn hard, that's a signal to test for grubs and consider a Grub+ application before next season.
