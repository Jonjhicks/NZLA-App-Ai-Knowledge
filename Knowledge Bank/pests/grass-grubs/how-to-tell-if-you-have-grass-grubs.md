# How do I know if I have grass grubs?

Three signs to look for.

First, irregular yellow or brown patches in the lawn, especially appearing in spring (October through December) when the grubs are most active feeding on roots. The patches do not follow any pattern — they show up wherever the grubs are.

Second, the turf in affected areas lifts like a carpet. The grubs eat through the roots, so the grass loses its anchor. If you can grab a handful and pull it up easily without it tearing, that is grub damage.

Third, birds tearing the lawn up. Blackbirds, magpies, even starlings will work over a lawn pulling grubs out of damaged areas. If you have got birds making a mess of your lawn, they are telling you grubs are there.

To confirm, lift a square spade-full of turf in an affected area and count what you find. Cream coloured C-shaped grubs about 15 to 20 mm long are the typical grass grub larvae. More than about 15 per 0.1m² (a square the size of a dinner plate) and treatment is worth doing.
