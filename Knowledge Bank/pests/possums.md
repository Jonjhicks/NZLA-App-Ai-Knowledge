# Possum Damage to Lawns

Possums digging up a lawn are almost always doing it for grass grubs. Suburban possums (Trichosurus vulpecula) have worked out that lawns in autumn are a reliable food source. They're not eating the grass — they're eating the fat, full-grown grubs in the soil below it.

## What possum damage looks like

Patches of torn-up turf appearing overnight. Soil disturbed and turned over in chunks 200 to 500 mm across. Damage is worst in autumn (April to June) when third-instar grass grubs are near the soil surface and at their biggest. The same patches get hit repeatedly. Birds (blackbirds, starlings, magpies) often work the same areas during the day for the same reason.

The possum is the visible problem. The grass grubs underneath are the actual problem.

## Fix the grubs first

Repairing torn-up turf before treating for grubs is wasted effort. The possums will keep coming back as long as there's a food reward. And there will be, because grass grubs in an untreated lawn build year on year.

NZLA Grub+ (chlorantraniliprole 200 g/L) applied at the right time — late summer to early autumn (February to April) targeting small larvae — knocks back the grub population before the grubs reach the size that attracts possums. Once grub numbers drop, possum visits decrease. They'll find easier feeding elsewhere.

Apply to a well-watered lawn and irrigate 6 mm immediately after. Maximum 1 application per year. Up to 6 months control from one application.

After the grub treatment is in place, repair the torn-up areas with NZLA Starter fertiliser and oversow bare patches with NZLA Perennial Rye.

## Direct possum deterrents

The grub treatment is the main intervention, but direct deterrents can reduce damage while the grub programme takes effect.

Motion-activated sprinklers startle possums and deter repeat visits. Outdoor lights on motion sensors reduce nocturnal activity in the affected area. Removing tree branches and fence access points that give possums easy entry to the property reduces visits generally.

## Trapping and baiting

Cage traps baited with apple or peanut butter are effective. Trapped possums must be dispatched — it's illegal to relocate them in NZ, they're a declared pest. Hardware stores sell cage traps; councils sometimes loan them.

Bait stations with possum-specific baits (cyanide-based or anticoagulant-based) are highly effective but require careful handling, correct placement away from pets and children, and strict label compliance. Some councils coordinate possum control programmes in suburban areas — check what's available through your local council.

NZLA range doesn't include possum control products. For broader property-level possum management, a pest control contractor or your regional council pest team are the right contacts.

## Long-term

Possum populations in suburban NZ are well established and won't be eliminated from a single property. Ongoing grub management each season reduces the food reward and keeps possum damage to a minimum. Coordinating with neighbours facing the same problem makes the grub treatment more effective across a wider area.
