# How do I control slugs and snails on a new lawn?

NZLA Tea Saponin or Worm Pellets handle most home lawn situations. The camellia tea seed saponins irritate molluscs and reduce feeding on seedlings.

For a new lawn or patch repair:
- Apply NZLA Tea Saponin (liquid) at label rate to the seeded area starting at day 5 to 7 after sowing.
- Or apply NZLA Worm Pellets (granular) for slower-release protection.
- Repeat as needed through the first 3 to 4 weeks of establishment.

For heavy slug pressure:
- Iron-based slug baits (ferric phosphate, available at garden centres) are reliable and pet-safe.
- Metaldehyde-based slug pellets work but are toxic to pets and birds, less suited for home lawns with kids and animals.
- Beer traps (shallow dish of beer at soil level) work for backyard slugs but only catch a small percentage of the population.

Reduce habitat around the seeding area:
- Clear leaf litter and garden debris within 2 metres of the new lawn.
- Trim back damp ground covers that harbour slugs.
- Don't apply heavy mulch over the seedbed (gives slugs daytime shelter).

Conditions that increase slug pressure:
- Cool damp autumn establishment (March to May).
- Cool damp spring establishment (September to November).
- Shaded sites with poor air movement.
- Heavy clay soil that stays damp.

Conditions that reduce slug pressure:
- Summer establishment (most of NZ - too hot and dry for slugs to be at peak activity).
- Sandy soils that dry out between rains.
- Open sunny sites with good air movement.

For typical home lawn establishment, a single Tea Saponin or Worm Pellet application at the right time handles most slug pressure. Severe infestations are uncommon and usually point to surrounding garden habitat that needs addressing.

Once seedlings are mature (4 to 6 weeks established, several mows in), slug damage stops being an issue. The grass is too tough for them to bother with.
