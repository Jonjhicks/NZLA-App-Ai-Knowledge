# What is Argentine stem weevil?

A small weevil (Listronotus bonariensis) that's a major pest of pasture ryegrass in NZ and crosses into turf situations, particularly newly established lawns and ryegrass-dominant turf in upper NI.

The damage is caused by the larvae, not the adults. Adult weevils lay eggs into the leaf sheath of ryegrass. The larvae tunnel inside the stem, hollowing out the growing point. The grass plant stops growing and dies from the centre outward.

What it looks like:
- Patches of yellowing or thinning turf, often with the centre of tillers dying back.
- Pull a tiller apart and you may see the small cream-coloured larva inside (2 to 5 mm).
- Damage spreads through a ryegrass lawn over weeks.
- More common on new lawns than established ones, but established ryegrass lawns can suffer.

What drives it:
- Warm temperatures (over 15 °C soil).
- Pure ryegrass swards more than mixed lawns.
- Spring and summer activity.
- New lawn establishment - weevils move in from surrounding pasture.

NZ relevance:
- Major pasture pest, well-studied at AgResearch and Lincoln.
- Insecticide resistance has been documented (organophosphate resistance widespread).
- Lawn relevance is increasing as ryegrass home lawns and new lawn establishment continue.

NZLA Grub+ may provide some control - check the current label for registered target list. The chemistry may or may not cover stem weevil specifically. If you've identified stem weevil damage, contact NZLA support to confirm what's appropriate from the current range.

The biological control approach (parasitoid wasp Microctonus hyperodae) has been used in NZ pasture systems for decades and is the primary management approach in pasture. Whether it provides meaningful control on home lawns depends on the surrounding landscape.

Endophyte-containing ryegrass (AR1, AR37 endophyte cultivars) provides natural protection against stem weevil. Worth checking if seed used contains endophyte.
