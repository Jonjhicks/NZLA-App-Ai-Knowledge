# How do I treat Argentine stem weevil?

Several angles, depending on the situation.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Grub+ may provide some control of stem weevil - the active is registered for several turf insects. Check the current Grub+ label for the registered pest list before assuming it covers stem weevil specifically.
- Apply at label rate to a well-watered lawn, water in lightly.
- Pasture-grade insecticides used by farmers have documented organophosphate resistance issues, so even if you can access those products, they may not work effectively.

Endophyte seed for new lawns:
- Endophyte (a beneficial fungus that lives inside the grass plant) produces chemistry that's toxic to stem weevil and other insect pests.
- AR1, AR37 endophyte cultivars provide strong natural protection.
- Most NZLA premium ryegrass varieties (MDR, DPR, RSR, Perennial Rye) may or may not contain endophyte - check the specific seed bag for endophyte status.
- If stem weevil is a recurring issue, look for endophyte-containing seed for new establishment.

Biological control:
- A parasitoid wasp (Microctonus hyperodae) parasitises stem weevil adults and has been used in NZ pasture for decades.
- Naturally established across most of NZ now. The wasp keeps weevil populations down in surrounding pasture, which reduces pressure on home lawns.
- Not a direct control measure you apply, just background biological pressure.

Cultural management:
- Dense well-fertilised turf tolerates some stem weevil pressure better than thin stressed turf.
- Mixed-species lawn (ryegrass plus tall fescue) is less attractive than pure ryegrass.
- Maintaining lawn fertility through spring and summer reduces susceptibility.

For a damaged lawn:
- Overseed with endophyte-containing seed where possible.
- Push recovery with NZLA Liquid Boost and Iron+.
- Address any underlying stress that made the lawn vulnerable.

Honest framing: stem weevil isn't a standard NZLA Grub+ target in the same way grass grub and porina are. If you've got confirmed stem weevil damage, contact NZLA support for current advice on what's available from the range, and consider endophyte-containing seed for any re-establishment.
