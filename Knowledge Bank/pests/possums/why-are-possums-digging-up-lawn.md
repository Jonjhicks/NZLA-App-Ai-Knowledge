# Why are possums digging up my lawn?

They're looking for grass grubs and other insect larvae in the soil. Possums in NZ suburban areas have learned that lawns hold a reliable food supply through autumn and winter when other food sources are scarce.

Signs:
- Patches of disturbed soil and torn-up turf, often in small clumps.
- Damage appears overnight - possums are nocturnal.
- Grass torn up rather than chewed cleanly off.
- Most common in autumn (April to June) when grub larvae are full-size and feeding shallow.
- Sometimes in patches that line up with where the heaviest grub populations are.

What it's telling you:
- You probably have a grass grub problem. The possum is the visible symptom of an underlying pest issue.
- Birds (particularly magpies, starlings, blackbirds) often work the same lawns for the same reason.
- Where there are grubs, possums and birds find them.

The two-part fix:

First, deal with the grubs. NZLA Grub+ applied at the right time (late summer to early autumn for grass grub) controls the larvae before they grow big enough to attract possums and birds. Once the grub population drops, the possums move on to look elsewhere.

Second, address the surface damage. Repair torn-up patches with NZLA Starter and seed once the grub treatment is in place. Don't reseed before treating the grubs - the possums will dig up the new seed too.

Possum management options:
- Trapping (legal and effective, traps available from hardware stores and councils).
- Bait stations with possum-specific baits (cyanide or anticoagulant - read the label and follow safety requirements carefully).
- DOC (Department of Conservation) and regional councils sometimes run possum control programmes in affected areas.

Honest framing for the customer:
- Repairing the lawn without addressing the grub problem is wasted effort.
- Possums are persistent - they'll keep coming back as long as there's a food reward.
- A clean grub programme reduces the possum incentive significantly.

For typical NZ suburban lawns with possum activity, the Grub+ treatment is the most cost-effective intervention. Possum control is a separate decision based on the wider pest pressure in your area.
