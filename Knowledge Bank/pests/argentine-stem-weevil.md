# Argentine Stem Weevil

Argentine stem weevil (Listronotus bonariensis) is a serious ryegrass pest, well-established in NZ pasture and increasingly relevant to home and sports turf. The adults are small grey-brown weevils about 3 to 4 mm long. The damage comes entirely from the larvae.

## How it damages the lawn

Adults lay eggs into the leaf sheath of ryegrass tillers. The larvae hatch and tunnel inside the stem, hollowing out the growing point. The tiller dies from the centre outward. Affected grass yellows and thins as tillers die off across the lawn.

Pull a yellowing tiller apart lengthways and look for a small cream-coloured larva (2 to 5 mm) inside the stem. That's the confirmation.

Damage spreads through a ryegrass-dominant lawn over weeks. Worse in spring and summer when weevils are active and soil temperatures are above 15°C. New establishment lawns are especially vulnerable because adult weevils move in from surrounding pasture and the fresh ryegrass is ideal egg-laying territory.

## Identifying it correctly

Argentine stem weevil is commonly confused with other problems because the yellowing and thinning look similar to drought stress, grass grub damage, or . The key difference: with stem weevil, when you pull the dying tiller apart, you'll find the larva inside. With grass grub, the roots are chewed off and the turf lifts easily. With drought, there's no larva and the roots are intact.

It's primarily a ryegrass problem. Mixed lawns with tall fescue or other species are less attractive.

## Treatment

Check the current NZLA Grub+ label for Argentine stem weevil on the registered target list before applying. The active ingredient (chlorantraniliprole) has activity on various turf insects. Apply at label rate to a well-watered lawn.

One important note on the NZ pasture context: organophosphate resistance in Argentine stem weevil is well-documented in NZ. Products from the pasture chemical shelf may not work effectively because of this. Chlorantraniliprole has a different mode of action, which is one reason it's the better starting point.

## Endophyte seed

Endophyte is a fungus that lives inside ryegrass plants and produces chemistry toxic to stem weevil and related insects. Endophyte-containing ryegrass varieties (AR1 and AR37 endophyte strains) provide significant natural protection. If stem weevil is a recurring problem on your lawn, look for endophyte-containing seed when re-establishing or seeding-and-over-seeding.

NZLA seed varieties — check the current product information for endophyte status on each variety, as this varies.

## Cultural management

Dense, well-fertilised turf tolerates stem weevil pressure better than thin or stressed lawns. Mixed-species lawns that include tall fescue alongside ryegrass are less attractive to weevils than pure ryegrass swards. Maintaining fertility through spring and summer with NZLA Liquid Boost and the All Seasons programme keeps turf in the best shape to handle some pressure.

Where a lawn has been damaged, push recovery with Liquid Boost and Iron+ once the active pest pressure is under control.

## Biological control background

A parasitoid wasp (Microctonus hyperodae) was deliberately released in NZ pasture in the 1990s to control Argentine stem weevil. It's now naturalised across most of the country and provides background population suppression in pasture landscapes. Whether it provides meaningful protection on a home lawn depends on the surrounding area.
