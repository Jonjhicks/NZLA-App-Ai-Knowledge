# Should I encourage worms in my lawn?

Yes. Worms are one of the better indicators of soil health. They process organic matter, aerate the soil through their burrowing, and move nutrients around the profile. A lawn with good worm populations is generally well-set-up for healthy turf.

To support them, get organic matter into the soil. Mulching grass clippings rather than catching them is the simplest thing you can do. Top dressing with compost or a quality screened topsoil every couple of years also helps.

NZLA Humic+ and Amino+ both feed soil biology. Humic acids stimulate microbial activity, amino acids are food for the whole soil food web (which includes worms). Regular use over a season noticeably improves soil biology in most lawns.

Avoid heavy use of products that hammer soil biology. Insecticides like Grub+ are necessary for pest control but won't help worm populations, use them only when needed for actual pest issues rather than as a routine preventative.

Keeping the soil moist (not waterlogged) and not letting the lawn get bone-dry through summer also helps worms stay active near the surface.
