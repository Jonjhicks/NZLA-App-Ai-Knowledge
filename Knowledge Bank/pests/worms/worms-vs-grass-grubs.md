# How do I tell worms apart from grass grubs?

They look quite different once you have them in front of you.

Earthworms are long, slim, pink to red-brown, segmented along their whole length, no legs, no distinct head capsule. They wriggle and stretch when handled. Useful for the lawn.

Grass grubs are short, C-shaped, white-bodied, with a hard tan head capsule and three pairs of small legs at the front. They curl up tightly when you pick them up. Damaging to the lawn.

If you dig down 50 mm into a patch of yellowing damaged lawn and find lots of curl-shaped white grubs, that's a grass grub problem worth treating. If you dig down and find worms but no grubs, the worms aren't the cause of the damage, look for another reason (drought stress, disease, mowing damage, dog urine).

A heavy worm population doesn't damage a lawn. A heavy grub population does. Worth understanding the difference before reaching for any treatment.
