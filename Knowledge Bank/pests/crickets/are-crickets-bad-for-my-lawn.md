# Are crickets bad for my lawn?

Mostly cosmetic, but in big populations they do real damage. Crickets chew grass blades and stems at night, and the larger species (black field cricket in particular) cut grass off near the crown. A heavy infestation in a dry summer can thin a lawn out fast, especially in warm-season turf like kikuyu.

You'll often hear them before you see the damage. Chirping at night, especially through summer. Then patchy thinning across the lawn and small holes (their burrows) at the soil surface.

Light populations don't justify treatment. A few crickets aren't a problem and they're food for birds. It's the explosive populations after a warm dry spring that cause grief, particularly in Hawke's Bay, Northland, Auckland, Waikato and Bay of Plenty.
