# When are crickets most active in NZ lawns?

Late spring through summer and into early autumn is when they're feeding and laying eggs. Peak chirp and peak damage is January and February in most regions. They drop off through April as cooler weather arrives.

Eggs are laid in soil through summer and the nymphs emerge later in the season. A warm dry spring leads into a heavy population through summer. Wet cool springs knock them back naturally.

In Auckland, Northland and Bay of Plenty, you can hear crickets calling well into March. South Island infestations are rare, populations don't build to damaging levels in the cooler climate.

If you're going to treat, do it as numbers are building rather than waiting for the damage. Late November into December gives Grub+ the best chance to keep populations down through the worst of summer.
