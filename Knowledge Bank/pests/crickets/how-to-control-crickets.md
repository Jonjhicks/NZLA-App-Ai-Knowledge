# How do I control crickets in my lawn?

NZLA Grub+ knocks crickets back at the same rate that controls grubs. Apply to a well-watered lawn, water in straight after. The active works on the crickets as they contact the treated turf and soil surface.

Best results come from treating when populations are building, not after damage is widespread. If you've had a cricket problem last summer, treat in early summer the following year as a preventative.

Outside chemistry, keeping the lawn well-watered and dense reduces cricket damage. They prefer thinning, dry, stressed turf. A vigorous lawn with good density isn't a free buffet.

If you've got a major infestation, a follow-up treatment four to six weeks later can help, but most home lawns won't need more than one application a year.
