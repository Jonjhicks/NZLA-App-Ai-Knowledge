# Hedgehog Lawn Damage

Hedgehogs (Erinaceus europaeus) are common in NZ suburban gardens. They forage at night for slugs, beetles, worms, and grubs, and they leave small scrapes and soil disturbances as they work. For most home lawns, hedgehog damage is minor and tolerable.

## What it looks like

Small scrapes 50 to 100 mm across, soil pushed aside rather than torn up. Often in soft moist areas where worms and slugs concentrate. Damage is scattered across the lawn rather than concentrated in one spot. Look for the distinctive droppings (small dark cylindrical pellets) to confirm it's hedgehogs rather than possums or birds.

Compared to possum damage (larger torn-up patches, 200 to 500 mm, turf actually lifted and flipped) or bird damage (many small holes from pecking), hedgehog disturbance is smaller and less dramatic.

## Distinguishing from other causes

If you're not sure what's making the marks, a cheap motion-activated trail camera set up overnight will confirm it quickly. Check at dusk or dawn if you'd rather look directly. Hedgehogs are visible when they're active.

Other possibilities for small soil disturbances: cats digging in favourite spots, birds (fantails, thrushes) flipping leaf litter, or minor grass grub activity near the surface. Large-scale damage that looks similar to hedgehog work but is more severe usually points to possums or a significant grub population attracting them both.

## The underlying cause

When hedgehogs are concentrating in one area and returning repeatedly, they're usually following a food source. A high grub or worm population in a particular patch is what keeps them coming back. The hedgehog isn't the problem — it's a symptom of what's underneath.

NZLA Grub+ to manage grass grub populations takes away the food reward. Once grub numbers drop in that patch, hedgehogs spread their foraging elsewhere and the concentrated damage stops.

## No action needed for typical activity

A hedgehog moving through a lawn occasionally is not a management issue. It's eating slugs and snails (useful), beetles (mildly useful), and worms (less useful, but not a disaster). The soil disturbance heals over quickly.

If hedgehog activity is genuinely creating surface damage you want to address: motion-activated sprinklers work well as a deterrent. Reducing food around the property (cat food left out overnight, garden vegetable scraps, mulch piles full of insects) reduces their interest. Removing tree branches that give them access to specific areas of the garden can redirect where they forage.

## Conservation context

Hedgehogs are considered a pest by DOC because they eat native invertebrates, lizards, and bird eggs. If you're in or near native bush areas, hedgehog control may align with broader pest management for ground-nesting birds or lizard recovery. Regional councils and DOC sometimes coordinate programmes in sensitive areas.
