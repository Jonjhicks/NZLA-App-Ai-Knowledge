# How do I treat fall armyworm?

Speed matters. Fall armyworm populations build fast and can do significant damage in a few days.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Grub+ targets surface-feeding caterpillars (sod webworm, porina) and likely provides some control of fall armyworm too. Check the current Grub+ label for fall armyworm specifically. If it's not on the label, contact NZLA support for current advice.
- Apply in the evening when fall armyworm caterpillars come up to feed at night.
- Water in lightly so the active stays near the surface where the caterpillars are active.
- Don't apply a heavy watering after treatment - that washes the chemistry away from the surface where the pest is.

Monitoring:
- Check the lawn at dusk or early morning with a torch to see caterpillars feeding.
- Look for fresh chewing damage on leaf edges.
- A pheromone trap (specific to fall armyworm) can confirm presence and population level.

For confirmed heavy infestations:
- A second application 7 to 10 days after the first can catch the next wave of newly emerged caterpillars.
- Don't over-apply - resistance can develop with repeated use of single-mode-of-action chemistry.

If chemistry isn't getting full control:
- Multiple modes of action may be needed. Pasture-grade insecticides with different modes of action can be sourced through agricultural suppliers if home lawn products aren't doing the job.
- Contact MPI biosecurity or AgResearch for current best-practice advice as this pest evolves in NZ.

Cultural side:
- Healthy fertilised turf recovers from light damage better than stressed thin turf.
- Push recovery with NZLA Liquid Boost once the active infestation is controlled.
- Reseed bare patches once the threat has passed.

Reporting:
- This is a new biosecurity pest in NZ. Report confirmed sightings to MPI (0800 80 99 66) so the spread can be tracked.
- Photos and location details help confirm identification and spread mapping.

Honest framing: fall armyworm is a recent arrival and the NZ lawn industry is still figuring out best practice. NZLA Grub+ is the most likely candidate from the current range. Contact NZLA support before treating to confirm current label status and any updated guidance.
