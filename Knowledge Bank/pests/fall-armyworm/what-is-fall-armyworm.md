# What is fall armyworm?

A new lawn and crop pest in NZ. Fall armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) is a moth species native to the Americas that's spread globally over the past decade. First detected in NZ in 2022 (Tauranga area), now established in upper North Island.

The damage is from caterpillars feeding on grass leaves at night. Heavy infestations can strip a lawn rapidly.

What it looks like:
- Caterpillars 30 to 40 mm long when mature, brown-grey with distinctive markings (dark stripes along the back, an inverted Y mark on the head).
- Feeds on grass leaves at night, hides in thatch and soil during the day.
- Damage shows as patches of chewed-down grass, ragged leaf edges, in severe cases the lawn looks like it's been mowed too short randomly.
- Heavy infestations move across a lawn quickly (the "army worm" name from how they advance).

Hosts:
- Wide host range - ryegrass, fescue, kikuyu, couch, paspalum, maize, sweetcorn, sorghum, many grain crops.
- Particularly damaging on lush warm-season turf in summer.

NZ context:
- Established in upper NI since 2022.
- MPI biosecurity has documented spread south and inland from initial Bay of Plenty detection.
- Still a relatively new pest in lawn management terms - knowledge base on home lawn treatment is developing.

NZLA Grub+ - check the current Grub+ label for fall armyworm specifically. Grub+ targets sod webworm and other surface-feeding caterpillars, so the chemistry likely covers fall armyworm but the label may or may not list it explicitly. Contact NZLA support to confirm before treating for fall armyworm specifically.

If unsure: collect a sample (caterpillar in a small container) and contact MPI biosecurity for identification. Reporting helps track the spread of this new pest.

Cultural management is limited for fall armyworm. Healthy turf tolerates light pressure, but heavy infestations need chemistry to control before significant damage. Catch them early.
