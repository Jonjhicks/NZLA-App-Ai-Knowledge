# My lawn has small soil disturbances - is this hedgehogs?

Possibly. Distinguishing between hedgehog, possum and bird damage helps work out what to do (if anything).

Hedgehog damage:
- Small scrapes 50 to 100 mm across.
- Soil pushed aside rather than torn up.
- Often in soft moist areas where slugs and worms live.
- Repeated across the lawn rather than concentrated in one spot.
- Hedgehog droppings nearby (small dark cylinders).

Possum damage:
- Larger torn-up patches 200 to 500 mm.
- Turf actually lifted and flipped over.
- Often in autumn searching for grass grubs.
- Concentrated where grub populations are heavy.
- Damage more dramatic than hedgehog activity.

Bird damage (magpie, starling, blackbird):
- Many small holes and pecks.
- Lawn looks aerated by holes but not severely torn.
- Birds visible working the lawn during daylight.
- Often follows in the same patches as possum damage (both after grubs).

Pukeko damage (rural and semi-rural):
- Larger pulls and tears than hedgehogs.
- Often near water sources or wet patches.
- Pukeko visible during the day.

Cat or dog damage:
- Localised digging in specific spots (one cat's "spot").
- Often around favourite hiding places or scent marks.

To confirm hedgehogs:
- Set up a wildlife camera (cheap motion-activated trail cams) to record overnight activity.
- Check at dusk or dawn for hedgehogs visible foraging.
- Look for hedgehog droppings specifically.

If it is hedgehogs and the damage bothers you:
- Reduce food sources (cat food left out, garden vegetable scraps, mulch piles).
- Motion-activated sprinklers startle them and deter return visits.
- Lighting changes in active areas.

If hedgehog damage is genuinely a problem on your lawn (rare), the underlying issue is usually heavy insect activity - slugs, worms, beetles. Address the insect side rather than trying to evict hedgehogs.

For most NZ home lawns, hedgehogs cause occasional minor disturbance that resolves on its own. Not worth chemistry or fencing for typical levels of activity.
