# Are hedgehogs bad for my lawn?

Less destructive than possums but still cause some lawn disturbance. Hedgehogs forage at night for insects, slugs, snails and grubs. The damage to lawns is from snuffling and pushing soil aside while searching for food.

Signs of hedgehog activity:
- Small scrapes and indentations in the soil surface.
- Lifted patches of soil, smaller and less destructive than possum damage.
- Hedgehog droppings (small dark cylindrical pellets, distinctive shape) in feeding areas.
- Visible at dusk or dawn if you're out at the right time.

NZ context:
- European hedgehogs (Erinaceus europaeus) are an introduced species in NZ, common in suburban areas.
- Now considered a pest by conservation groups because they eat native invertebrates and lizards.
- Lawn damage is usually mild compared to possums.

What hedgehogs eat:
- Slugs and snails (helpful from a garden perspective).
- Beetles, including grass grub adults.
- Worms (less helpful - worms are good for lawns).
- Grass grub larvae (helpful for lawn pest management).
- Bird eggs and chicks (concern for native bird populations).

The trade-off:
- Hedgehogs eating grubs and slugs in your lawn is mildly helpful.
- The soil disturbance is usually minor and tolerable.
- Conservation concerns about native species are more significant than lawn damage concerns.

For typical home lawn:
- No action usually needed.
- If hedgehogs are concentrating in one spot and causing visible damage, check whether there's a heavy grub population - that's what's attracting them.
- NZLA Grub+ to control the grub population reduces the food reward and hedgehogs spread their foraging.

Conservation note:
- If you're in or near native bush areas, hedgehog control may be coordinated with broader pest management for kiwi, weka, lizard recovery.
- Council and DOC sometimes coordinate hedgehog trapping in sensitive areas.

For most NZ home lawns, hedgehogs are an occasional visitor causing minor disturbance, not a real management issue. If you want to discourage them, motion-activated sprinklers and reducing easy food (cat food left outside, garden vegetable scraps) help.
