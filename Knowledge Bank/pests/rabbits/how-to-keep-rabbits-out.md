# How do I keep rabbits off my lawn?

Physical exclusion is the reliable answer. Other methods help but won't fully keep rabbits out.

Fencing:
- Rabbit-proof fence: 30 mm mesh, 900 mm above ground, 300 mm buried below ground (rabbits dig).
- Permanent option for serious rabbit problems on rural lawns and lifestyle blocks.
- Cost can be significant for larger areas.

Temporary exclusion:
- Rabbit netting on temporary stakes around new seeding areas.
- Effective for the establishment window (first 4 to 6 weeks of a new lawn).
- Once the lawn matures, rabbits cause less damage and the netting can come down.

Reduce cover:
- Trim back hedges and shrubs that rabbits use for daytime cover.
- Clear long grass and weeds along fence lines.
- Open ground is less attractive to rabbits than overgrown edges.

Repellents:
- Commercial rabbit repellent sprays (capsaicin-based, ammonia-based, blood-meal-based) work for short periods.
- Need regular reapplication, especially after rain.
- Limited effectiveness compared to physical exclusion.

Trapping and shooting:
- Effective for rural property owners with the skills and legal framework.
- Live cage traps work but need to be checked regularly.
- Lethal control through shooting is standard rural practice.
- Recreation shooting permits sometimes available through regional councils.

Biocontrol:
- Rabbit haemorrhagic disease virus (RHDV) releases have been used to reduce NZ populations.
- Regional councils manage releases in affected areas.
- Not a property-level decision but reduces overall pressure where deployed.

For typical NZ home lawn rabbit issues:
- Suburban lawn with occasional rabbit visits: clear edges, check fence integrity, accept occasional damage.
- Lifestyle block with regular rabbit pressure: rabbit-proof fencing on the main lawn area.
- Rural lawn with heavy rabbit population: coordinated property-level pest management plus permanent fencing.

NZLA range doesn't have rabbit control products - this is wildlife management not chemistry. For lifestyle block owners dealing with rabbits as a wider property issue, regional council pest teams are the right contact for advice and sometimes assistance.
