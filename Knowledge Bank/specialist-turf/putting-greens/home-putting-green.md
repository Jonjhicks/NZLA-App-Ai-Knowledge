# Can I build a putting green at home?

Yes, with significant investment in construction, equipment, and ongoing management. Home putting greens are specialised installations very different from general lawn care.

Construction options:

Built to specifications (premium):
- USGA spec construction: sand-based root zone over gravel drainage layer.
- Hundreds of mm of specific material in layered profile.
- Professional installation typically.
- Cost: $200 to $400 per square metre for a quality home green.

Modified construction (mid-range):
- Sand-modified existing soil rather than full root zone replacement.
- Better drainage than standard lawn soil but not USGA standard.
- DIY-possible with effort.
- Cost: $50 to $150 per square metre.

Standard soil construction (entry level):
- Standard soil base with appropriate species.
- Functional putting surface, not premium quality.
- Cost: $20 to $50 per square metre.

Species selection:
- Browntop (Agrostis capillaris) for cool-season home greens.
- Selected fine fescue blends.
- Creeping bent for the most premium home greens.
- Tropical bent or seashore paspalum for upper NI in some cases.

Equipment:
- Reel mower essential. Pedestrian reel mower for small greens, ride-on greens mower for larger.
- Mowing at 3 to 8 mm depending on standard sought.
- Roller for surface firming.
- Spreader and sprayer setup.

Maintenance intensity:
- Daily or near-daily mowing in season.
- Frequent light foliar feeding (NZLA Charger, Iron+).
- NZLA Mini Prill granular for occasional applications (small prill drops through dense canopy).
- PGR programme (NZLA PGR / paclobutrazol) for surface density.
- Wetting agents (NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W) critical.
- Disease management (Azoxy, Fungus Pro on rotation).

Realistic time commitment:
- 30 to 60 minutes daily during season for a small home green.
- More for larger greens.
- This is hobby-level commitment, not casual lawn care.

Is it worth it?
- For golf enthusiasts who play regularly: probably yes, the practice value is real.
- For casual interest: a modest practice strip costs much less than a premium green.
- Maintenance cost is ongoing - not just construction.

NZ resources:
- NZ STI is the technical authority for greens management.
- Local golf clubs sometimes have greenkeepers who consult.
- USGA technical references for construction standards.

For most NZ home owners interested in putting practice, a small practice area at lower management intensity makes more sense than a premium home green. The premium option is for genuine enthusiasts willing to invest both money and time.
