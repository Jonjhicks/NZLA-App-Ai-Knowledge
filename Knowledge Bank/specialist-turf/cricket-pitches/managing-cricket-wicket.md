# How do I manage a cricket pitch?

Cricket wickets are specialist surfaces with very specific requirements. Different to outfield turf and different to other sports surfaces.

Wicket basics:
- Clay-based soil (specific cricket soils, often imported or specifically blended).
- Mown short (5 to 8 mm during play preparation).
- Heavily rolled to firm the surface.
- Grass cover (couch in upper NI, ryegrass in cool regions) provides binding.

Key management:

Pre-season preparation:
- Top up wicket soils as needed (specific cricket clay).
- Heavy rolling programme to firm the surface.
- Targeted overseed if grass cover is thin.
- Pre-season feed to push growth before mowing down for play.

Match preparation:
- Mowing down to playing height in the week before.
- Rolling sequence to produce hard playing surface.
- Watering and drying cycles to produce the right pitch behaviour.
- Each pitch type (slow, fast, bouncy) has its own preparation.

Post-match recovery:
- Repair scratches and wear.
- Light irrigation and feeding to push grass recovery.
- Roll out divot marks where possible.
- Reseed worn areas.

Off-season renovation:
- Major scarification, possibly to clay depth.
- Top dressing with cricket-specific clay material.
- Reseeding with appropriate grass species.
- Programme-based to be ready for the next season.

For club-level cricket wickets:
- Experienced wicket prep is a specialist skill.
- New Zealand Cricket and provincial associations have technical resources.
- NZ STI publishes some cricket surface guidance.

Fertility:
- NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ as the standard base.
- Iron+ for colour and grass quality.
- Avoid heavy nitrogen which produces soft growth unsuitable for the playing surface.
- Avoid late nitrogen applications before matches.

Disease pressure:
- Cricket pitches under intensive management face significant disease pressure.
- NZLA Azoxy for general disease coverage.
- Fungus Pro for rust and rotation partner.
- Specific issues vary by region and season.

For home cricket pitches:
- Backyard cricket strips are simpler than club pitches but use similar principles.
- Clay-rich soil, short mowing, regular rolling.
- Less specialist than club-level but still differs from general lawn care.

Cricket pitch management is specialist work. Local cricket associations and groundsmen networks are the practical knowledge source. NZ STI provides technical research depth. Standard home lawn programmes don't translate directly to cricket wickets.
