# How is sports field turf managed?

Different from home lawns in several ways. Heavy wear, recovery between use, and specific playability requirements all shape management.

Key differences from home lawns:

Wear and recovery cycles:
- Sports use is concentrated (matches, training sessions) followed by recovery periods.
- Schedule manages around match days.
- Off-season is for major renovation and recovery work.

Species selection:
- Wear-tolerant species essential. NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing stoloniferous) is well-suited.
- Tall fescue for drought tolerance on inland fields.
- Couch and kikuyu for warm-season fields in upper NI.
- Often mixed species for combined wear and aesthetics.

Mowing:
- Typically 25 to 35 mm for rugby and soccer.
- Higher (50 mm) for cricket outfield.
- Striping patterns for visual quality (TV broadcast quality).

Fertility:
- Heavy granular base programme (NZLA All Seasons, All Seasons).
- Foliar applications (Liquid Boost, Iron+) between granular feeds.
- Push growth before play, recover after.

Recovery management:
- After matches, immediate divot repair, recovery feeds.
- NZLA Liquid Boost pushes regrowth between matches.
- Overseeding wear patches between fixtures.
- Coring and top dressing during off-season.

PGR use:
- NZLA PGR (trinexapac) common on club-level sports turf.
- Reduces mowing frequency, improves density.

Disease and pest pressure:
- Constant management cycle.
- Azoxy and Fungus Pro on rotation.
- Insecticide programmes for grub and surface pest seasons.

Wear-tolerance optimisation:
- High organic matter and good rooting depth for resilience.
- Aeration programmes to prevent compaction.
- Top dressing to maintain surface levels.

Different sports, different requirements:
- Rugby: high wear in scrum and ruck areas, durable surface needed.
- Soccer: even surface preferred, less concentrated wear than rugby.
- Cricket: outfield (managed as field) vs wicket (specialist clay surface).
- Hockey: artificial turf has largely replaced grass at competitive levels.

For school and community sports fields:
- Lower budget than professional fields.
- NZLA standard products work but at lower intensity than professional management.
- Goals: durable functional surface within budget.

For home owners with sports field aspirations:
- Backyard rugby, soccer, cricket all use turf principles from sports field management.
- Less intensive than professional fields.
- Species selection (RSR Ryegrass for wear recovery, tall fescue blend for drought) makes a difference.

Sports field management is professional work for high-level fields. The principles scale down for home and community use, but the equipment and time investment increase with the quality sought.
