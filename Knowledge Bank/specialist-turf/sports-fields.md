# Sports Field Turf Management

Sports field management operates on a different logic from home lawn care. The schedule is driven by the fixture list, not the season. Recovery windows are fixed by when the next game is. And the demands on the turf — concentrated wear, core-aeration from boots and machinery, high traffic in specific zones — are more intense than anything a home lawn experiences.

## What Makes Sports Turf Different

**Wear patterns are concentrated and predictable.** Scrum and ruck zones in rugby. Goal area and centre circle in soccer. The batting and bowling ends in cricket. These areas take 80% of the wear while the rest of the field takes 20%. Management is directed at the high-wear zones.

**The fixture list drives the schedule.** You can't decide to let a field rest for three weeks if there's a home game in seven days. Recovery work happens in the windows the schedule creates. Post-match work starts immediately after the final whistle, not when it's convenient.

**Playing quality has to hold.** A home lawn that's a bit thin in February is a cosmetic issue. A playing surface with bare hard ground or uneven divots is a safety issue and an injury risk.

## Species Selection

**NZLA RSR Ryegrass** is the primary species for NZ sports fields. Stoloniferous self-repair fills worn areas faster than standard perennial ryegrass. The above-ground spreading runners actively colonise bare spots between fixtures rather than waiting to be reseeded. For any field that takes heavy use, RSR Ryegrass is the right base.

**Tall fescue** (NZLA Elfin, Ultimate) in the blend for drought tolerance on fields without reliable irrigation-general — Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, and inland sites where summer dry conditions affect the growing season. Deeper root system holds up under dry conditions.

**Couch and kikuyu** in the upper North Island for fields where summer playing continues through the warm months. Warm-season turf handles the summer heat and recovers faster in warm conditions but goes dormant in cooler months — a factor for fields used year-round.

## Fertility Programme

**Granular base:** NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ three to four times a year. The base programme keeps nutrition consistent through the season.

**Foliar push:** NZLA Liquid Boost applied in the 48 to 72 hours following a match accelerates turf recovery in the window before the next use. This is one of the highest-return applications in sports turf — the recovery from each game is faster, and the turf arrives at the next fixture in better condition.

**Iron+:** regular foliar Iron+ maintains colour and quality of presentation, particularly through periods when heavy granular feeding isn't appropriate (pre-match).

**Pre-match timing:** avoid heavy nitrogen applications in the 3 to 5 days before a match. The rapid growth from high nitrogen produces soft growth that compacts badly under play. Solid granular applications done 7+ days before a match give time for uptake without pre-match softening.

## Mowing

Typical playing heights:
- Rugby and soccer: 25 to 35 mm.
- Cricket outfield: 40 to 50 mm.
- Presentation cut for marking day: sharp clean cut 24 hours before the game.

Sharp blades matter more on a sports field than most turf applications because mowing at slightly higher heights can hide dullness on a home lawn, but a sports field needs a clean cut for presentation and surface health.

Striping for visual quality on broadcast-quality fields: alternate mowing directions to produce the light/dark stripe pattern. Rollers on the rear of the mower achieve this on a home lawn mower; dedicated marking equipment on professional fields.

## Post-Match Recovery

The 24 to 48 hours after a match are the most productive recovery window. Start immediately:

- Divot repair in the heavily worn areas. Replace lifted turf, firm soil contact.
- NZLA Liquid Boost foliar application to push growth in damaged zones.
- Light irrigation-general to settle disturbed soil.
- Overseed bare or thin areas with NZLA RSR Ryegrass and NZLA Liquid Starter where recovery time allows (6+ weeks of growth before next heavy use is the minimum).

For heavily worn patches that need reseeding: NZLA Starter granular into the prepared area plus seed at 40 to 50 g/m², light topdress of fine material, keep moist. Even with 4 to 6 weeks of growing time, RSR Ryegrass establishes and provides some coverage.

## Aeration Programme

Compaction accumulates through the season. Boots, marking machines, maintenance equipment, and concentrated traffic all compress the root zone. Core aeration relieves core-aeration and is typically done once or twice per year, often in the main renovation period and once mid-season if the fixture list allows.

NZLA Aerate applied after aeration helps water and air movement through the profile between mechanical aeration events. For fields with intensive year-round use, this is a routine part of the maintenance programme rather than an occasional application.

## PGR Use

NZLA PGR (trinexapac) on club and regional-level fields reduces mowing frequency, improves turf density, and produces a firmer sward. On a field with limited maintenance resource, PGR reduces the mowing burden while maintaining surface quality.

## Disease and Pest Management

Fusarium patch and dollar spot are the main  concerns. NZLA Azoxy and Fungus Pro on rotation through the season as preventative applications on fields with history. Post-match conditions — disturbed wet turf — can accelerate  development.

Grass grubs are a significant pest on sports fields, particularly in the upper North Island and Canterbury. Black beetle larvae and grass grub damage shows as turf lifting like carpet, with birds and animals probing the surface. NZLA Grub+ applied in the correct seasonal window (late February to March for grass grubs, following label guidance for fall armyworm) addresses the pest population. Water in at 6 mm after application.

## Off-Season Renovation

The end of the playing season is the main renovation window. Major core aeration or solid-tine aeration, significant scarification, top dressing, heavy seeding-and-over-seeding of worn ends and high-wear zones, fertility programme to push establishment through autumn.

For clubs managing on limited budgets, prioritising renovation resources at the highest-wear areas — the scrum channels, the goal areas — and maintaining the lower-wear field areas through standard mowing and fertility delivers the best return.

## Home Backyard Sports Lawns

The same principles apply at home scale. NZLA RSR Ryegrass as the base species for wear recovery. NZLA All Seasons three to four times a year for the fertility programme. Autumn overseed of worn areas. The input is lighter than a club field but the direction is the same: prioritise the worn areas, push recovery after heavy use, reseed in autumn.
