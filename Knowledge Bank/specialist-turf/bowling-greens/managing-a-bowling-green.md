# How do I manage a bowling green?

Bowling greens are specialist fine turf with specific requirements. Different from home lawns in mowing height, fertility, species, and management intensity. NZ has two traditional species systems.

Cotula-based greens (traditional NZ system):
- Selected cotula (Leptinella squalida and L. dioica varieties) - prostrate native daisies grown as fine-textured turf surface.
- Mown at 3 to 5 mm.
- Roll-friendly surface for traditional NZ bowling.
- NZ STI is the standard-setting body, publishes the Bowls Chemical Guide and other technical references.

Browntop-based greens:
- Browntop (Agrostis capillaris/tenuis) selected varieties.
- More common internationally.
- Mown at 5 to 7 mm.

Mowing:
- Daily or near-daily mowing during the playing season.
- Reel mower required (rotary mowers tear at these heights).
- Bedknife adjustment and sharp blades critical.

Fertility:
- Light frequent foliar feeding rather than heavy granular applications.
- NZLA Mini Prill (SGN 150 small prill) for granular applications, drops through dense canopy to soil.
- NZLA Charger or Iron+ for foliar programme.
- Low rates frequently rather than heavy intermittent.

Wetting agents:
- Critical on bowling greens. Hydrophobic conditions destroy surface uniformity.
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W as standard summer programme.
- NZLA Wetter GR (granular) for clubs with no irrigation system.

PGR:
- NZLA PGR (trinexapac) reduces mowing frequency and improves surface density.
- Paclobutrazol for poa management on bowling greens.
- Programme-based application across the season.

Disease pressure:
- High on intensively managed fine turf.
- Fusarium patch in autumn-winter.
- Dollar spot in summer.
- Brown patch / yellow patch.
- NZLA Azoxy and Fungus Pro on resistance management rotation.

Renovation:
- Annual scarification.
- Core aeration and top dressing.
- Specific timing tied to playing season.

For greens management, the NZ Sports Turf Institute (NZSTI) publishes detailed guides including the Bowls Chemical Guide. NZSTI is the authoritative NZ source for greens management standards.

Home green vs club green:
- Home bowling greens for backyard practice: less intensive management, similar principles.
- Club greens managed by greenkeepers: detailed programmes informed by NZSTI guidance.

For home owners maintaining a small bowling green or putting green, the principles scale down but the specialist nature remains. Standard home lawn programmes don't transfer directly - fine turf needs different chemistry, equipment, and timing.
