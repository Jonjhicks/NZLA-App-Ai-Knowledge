# What is a cotula green?

A bowling green surface made from selected cotula species - prostrate native NZ daisies grown as fine-textured turf for bowling. This is a NZ tradition with no real equivalent overseas.

Species used:
- Leptinella squalida (selected varieties for green use).
- Leptinella dioica.
- Specific cultivars developed in NZ for green performance.

Why cotula:
- Prostrate growth habit (lies flat, no upright stems).
- Tolerates very low mowing (3 to 5 mm) better than most grass species.
- Dense mat-forming growth provides uniform surface.
- Native to NZ, evolved for our conditions.
- Distinct from grass species - actually a flowering plant in the daisy family.

Management is specialised:
- Different to grass-based greens in many ways.
- Doesn't tolerate the same herbicide range as grass greens.
- Has specific susceptibility to particular diseases.
- Fertility requirements differ from grass turf.

NZ STI publishes specific guidance for cotula green management. The Bowls Chemical Guide covers what's safe to use on cotula and what isn't.

Why cotula is uniquely NZ:
- Tradition developed in NZ from the 1880s onwards.
- Selected varieties bred specifically for green use here.
- Many older NZ bowling clubs still maintain cotula greens.
- Internationally, browntop and bent grass greens dominate.

Trends:
- Some clubs have converted cotula greens to grass-based (browntop or bent) for various reasons (consistency, easier management, international standards).
- Other clubs maintain cotula as part of heritage and local playing tradition.
- Both approaches have merits.

For someone managing a cotula green:
- NZ STI is the primary technical reference.
- The Bowls Chemical Guide (NZ STI) is essential.
- Specialised chemistry that's safe on cotula but doesn't help on grass greens, and vice versa.
- NZLA range may have specific products suited to cotula management - check the current product range and NZSTI references for which products to use.

For someone considering a cotula lawn at home:
- Unusual choice but possible.
- The species is native and adapted to NZ conditions.
- Grows much flatter than grass and looks different.
- Won't tolerate the same management as a grass lawn.

Cotula isn't a standard home lawn species. It's specialist turf primarily for bowling greens. The knowledge to manage it well sits with experienced NZ greenkeepers and NZSTI publications rather than typical home lawn references.
