# PPE for Lawn Chemistry

Minimum for any NZLA product application: long sleeves and trousers, closed shoes, nitrile gloves ($5 to $10 per pair), and safety glasses. That baseline covers most routine foliar fertiliser and standard herbicide work.

## Match PPE to the product

**Low-risk products** (Iron+, Charger, Liquid Boost, Amino+, Humic+, Liquid N, Wetter): standard PPE is sufficient. These are low-toxicity formulations at home lawn rates.

**Herbicides** (BWC, Gold, Halo, Etho, Meso, Sedge Pro): standard PPE plus careful handling at the mixing step. Concentrated product at the mixing stage is the highest exposure point. Use a measuring jug, pour carefully, avoid splashing. Wash skin thoroughly after.

**Fungicides** (Azoxy, Fungus Pro): standard PPE. Fungus Pro contains thiram which is irritating to eyes — don't skip eye protection.

**Grub+** (insecticide): standard PPE, avoid skin contact, wash thoroughly after.

**Sticker** (organosilicone adjuvant): this is the product requiring the most caution. Toxic if inhaled. Use a P2/P3 respirator mask, sealed goggles (not just safety glasses), and avoid any skin contact. Handle in a well-ventilated area.

**NZLA Moss** (benzalkonium chloride): chemical-resistant gloves are essential, eye protection is essential, avoid inhaling the mist. DANGER classification — treat it accordingly.

## Mixing is the highest-risk step

Concentrated product going into the sprayer tank before dilution is where exposure risk is highest. Mix outdoors or in a well-ventilated area. Never add product to a dry empty tank — add to one-third water first, then top up. Use a dedicated measuring jug that stays with the sprayer kit. Never eat, drink, or smoke during application.

## After application

Wash exposed skin with soap and water. Wash spray clothing separately from regular laundry. Wash gloves before removing them. Clean the measuring jug and any equipment that contacted concentrate.

## Approved Handlers

Some products in NZ require Approved Handler certification for commercial use. Check the label — home users are generally exempt but contractors must hold the relevant certification for restricted products.

Read the SDS (Safety Data Sheet) for any new product before first use. It lists the actual hazards and required PPE specific to that formulation.
