# Reading SDS and Product Labels

## The label vs the SDS

The product label tells you what to do and how to do it safely. The SDS (Safety Data Sheet, formerly MSDS) is the detailed safety reference document behind it.

For routine use, the label is sufficient. For first use of a new product, for an exposure incident, or for mixing decisions, the SDS is the reference.

## Reading a product label

**Signal word**: DANGER (high hazard), WARNING (moderate), CAUTION (low). Affects the care you take with handling and PPE.

**Active ingredient and concentration**: tells you the chemistry. Azoxy = azoxystrobin. Fungus Pro = thiram. Grub+ = chlorantraniliprole. Knowing the active is useful for understanding mode of action, resistance groups, and restrictions.

**Registered uses**: the specific turf species and target  or applications the product is registered for in NZ. Use outside these is off-label.

**Application rates**: rate per 100 m² for most NZLA products. Some older-format labels give rates in L/ha or kg/ha — divide by 100 to get per 100 m² equivalents.

**Timing restrictions**: water-in requirements, re-entry intervals, mowing restrictions before or after.

**Buffer zones**: distance from waterways, drains, or sensitive areas.

**Mix instructions**: order of addition, water volume, compatibility with other products.

## Reading an SDS

An SDS has 16 standardised sections. The most useful for home lawn users:

**Section 2 — Hazards Identification**: GHS hazard pictograms, signal word, H-statements (what the hazard is), P-statements (what precautions to take). Read this before using a new product.

**Section 4 — First Aid**: what to do for eye contact, skin contact, ingestion, inhalation. Keep this handy if anything goes wrong.

**Section 7 — Handling and Storage**: safe handling and storage conditions.

**Section 8 — PPE and Exposure Controls**: required PPE for safe use.

## GHS hazard symbols

**Skull and crossbones**: acute toxicity (poisonous if swallowed, inhaled, or skin-absorbed).

**Exclamation mark**: health hazard — skin/eye irritation, allergic response, less severe toxicity.

**Health hazard** (silhouette with starburst): serious long-term health effects.

**Environment** (dead tree and fish): aquatic or environmental toxicity.

**Corrosion**: skin or eye corrosion.

**Flame**: flammable.

Most NZLA home lawn products show an exclamation mark at most — indicating moderate hazards manageable with standard precautions. NZLA Sticker and NZLA Moss are the exceptions within the range, carrying higher hazard classifications.

## Where to find SDS

NZLA website for all NZLA products. For other products, the manufacturer's website or ask the supplier. Suppliers are required to provide SDS on request.
