# What does ACVM registration mean for NZ lawn chemicals?

ACVM is the Agricultural Compounds and Veterinary Medicines Act. It governs registration and use of all agricultural chemicals in NZ, including lawn herbicides, fungicides, insecticides, and some fertilisers.

What ACVM does:
- Registers each product for specific uses.
- Sets label requirements.
- Approves what chemicals can be sold in NZ.
- Sets re-evaluation cycles for ongoing registration.
- Enforces compliance.

Administered by MPI (Ministry for Primary Industries).

What ACVM registration means for a product:
- The product has been assessed for safety to humans, animals, and the environment.
- The registered uses (specific crops, target pests, application rates) are what's been tested.
- The label is approved as part of registration.
- Use beyond the registered label is "off-label" and operator's risk.

NZ vs international labels:
- Each country has its own registration system.
- Australian APVMA, US EPA, EU EFSA all have different processes.
- Product registered overseas isn't automatically registered in NZ.
- NZLA Blockade is a clear example: registered in NZ for warm-season turf only, but elsewhere registered for cool-season too.

What this means for users:
- Check that the product you're using is registered in NZ for your intended use.
- Off-label use is technically a legal issue and a personal risk issue.
- For home users, off-label use risk is mostly turf damage and personal liability.
- For commercial users, off-label use can have regulatory consequences.

Re-evaluation:
- ACVM periodically re-evaluates registered chemicals.
- Some chemicals lose registration over time as new safety information emerges.
- Glyphosate, neonicotinoids, others have been under re-evaluation in recent years.

How to check current registration:
- ACVM register (online at MPI website).
- Search by product name or active ingredient.
- Shows current registration status, registered uses, label.

For NZ home lawn users:
- Stick to label uses. The label tells you what's been registered.
- If unsure, check ACVM register.
- Don't follow overseas advice without verifying NZ registration.

Reading an NZ-registered product label:
- Active ingredient and concentration.
- Registered uses (specific crops/turf, target pests, application rates).
- Application instructions.
- Restrictions (don't apply near waterways, buffer zones, re-entry intervals).
- Safety information.
- Approved Handler requirements.

Off-label use concerns:
- May damage turf.
- May not work effectively.
- Resistance management implications.
- Personal liability if something goes wrong.
- Insurance implications.

For NZLA products:
- All actively-marketed NZLA products are ACVM-registered.
- Labels reflect current NZ registration.
- Use according to label for safe and effective performance.

ACVM is one of several NZ chemical regulations:
- ACVM: agricultural and veterinary chemicals (MPI).
- HSNO: Hazardous Substances and New Organisms (EPA).
- WorkSafe NZ: workplace use.
- Regional councils: environmental discharge.

For home lawn use, the main consideration is that the chemistry you use is NZ-registered for the use you're applying it to. Check labels carefully and don't assume international advice transfers to NZ regulatory framework.
