# Chemical Storage and Disposal

## Storage

Keep all lawn chemicals in their original labelled containers. The label is the safety reference — removing a product from its original container creates a risk of misidentification later.

**Location**: garage shelf or shed, out of reach of children and pets. Locked cabinet for higher-risk products. Not inside the house. Not near food or drink storage.

**Temperature**: most lawn chemicals are stable between 5 and 35°C. Avoid freezing (degrades many formulations, particularly biologicals) and avoid high heat — a garage in full summer sun can hit 40°C+. Don't leave product in a vehicle on a hot day.

**Separation**: store in a way that contains any leaks (shelf with a lip or a tray underneath). Keep oxidisers away from combustibles. Most NZLA products are compatible stored together, but check the SDS for any specific requirements.

**Biological products** (Amino+, Humic+): more sensitive than synthetic products. Use within 12 to 18 months of opening and store in cool conditions.

**Granular fertilisers**: store in a dry location. Moisture causes caking and can affect spreader calibration. If the bag is opened, transfer to a sealed container or use a tight snap-clip on the bag.

Inspect stored chemicals annually. Check for damaged containers, leaking caps, and degrading labels. Dispose of anything expired or damaged.

## Disposal

**Don't** pour chemicals down sinks, toilets, or drains. Don't pour onto garden, lawn, driveway, or paving. Don't put chemical containers in general rubbish (even rinsed). Don't bury or burn.

**Unused product**: try to use it for its intended purpose at label rate before treating it as waste. If genuinely surplus, take it to council hazardous waste collection.

**Empty containers**: triple-rinse before disposal. Fill to 25% with water, replace cap, shake, pour the rinse water into your next spray batch (not down the drain). Repeat twice more. After three rinses, the container is generally safe for recycling — check local council guidelines, as some require rinsed chemical containers in hazardous waste.

**Council hazardous waste**: most NZ councils run collection days 1 to 4 times a year, some have permanent drop-off points. Check your council website for dates and locations. Auckland Council, Wellington, Christchurch, and most other main centres have established programmes.

**Spray rinsate** (water used to clean the sprayer): spray it over the same treated area at low rate. Don't pour it on the driveway or into drains.

## Product-specific notes

**NZLA Gold** (picloram + triclopyr): the rinse water contains residual picloram that damages broadleaf garden plants. Direct rinse water onto the treatment area only, not driveways or paths.

**NZLA Sticker** (organosilicone): higher-risk concentrate. Handle carefully.

**NZLA Moss** (benzalkonium chloride): toxic to aquatic life. Keep all rinse water and product away from drains, waterways, and streams.

The general principle: chemicals applied to your lawn should stay in your lawn ecosystem or go through proper hazardous waste channels. They don't belong in drains, waterways, or other environments.
