# How should I store lawn chemicals at home?

Cool, dry, dark, secure. Original labelled containers always.

Storage location:
- Garage shelf or shed is fine.
- Out of reach of children and pets.
- Locked cabinet ideal for higher-risk products.
- Not in the house (kitchen, bathroom, laundry).
- Not next to food or drink storage.

Temperature:
- Most lawn chemicals are stable from 5 to 35 °C.
- Avoid freezing (degrades many formulations, particularly biological products).
- Avoid high heat (over 40 °C - back of garage in summer can hit this).
- Don't leave product in vehicle in summer heat.

Original containers:
- Always keep product in its original labelled container.
- The label is the safety reference.
- Don't decant into other bottles for convenience - dangerous if mistaken.
- Keep child-resistant caps tight.

Separation:
- Don't store strong oxidisers near combustibles.
- Don't mix incompatible products in the same shelf (acids and bases, oxidisers and organics).
- Most NZLA products are reasonably compatible to store together but read SDS for any specifics.

Organisation:
- Keep an inventory of what you have and when you bought it.
- Note opening date on the bottle when first used.
- Old product loses potency - mostly safety issue but also performance issue.

Stock rotation:
- Use older product first.
- Don't accumulate large quantities of seasonal products year over year.
- Buy what you'll use in 1 to 2 seasons.

Containment:
- Store on a shelf with a lip or in a tray to catch any leaks.
- Avoid storing liquid chemicals directly above anything sensitive.
- A small bunded tray (commercial chemical storage) is ideal for liquid concentrates.

Inspection:
- Check stored chemicals periodically (annually).
- Look for damaged containers, leaking caps, deteriorating labels.
- Replace anything that's damaged.

For shared properties (rental, retirement village, etc.):
- Make sure other occupants know what's stored and where.
- Lockable storage if needed.

Disposal of expired or no-longer-needed chemicals:
- Don't pour down drains or onto the garden.
- Council hazardous waste collection days handle home chemicals.
- Some councils accept all chemical waste, some only specific types.
- Check with your council before assuming.
- See chemical-disposal Q&A for detail.

For NZLA-specific products:
- Most NZLA liquid concentrates have 2 to 5 year shelf life when stored properly.
- Biological products (Nurture, Amino+) are more sensitive - 12 to 18 months after opening.
- Granular fertilisers store indefinitely if kept dry, but moisture causes caking.
- Open bag of granular: transfer to sealed container or use a snap-clip on the bag.

Sensible storage avoids most safety issues with home lawn chemistry. The bigger risks are from carelessness during use, not from properly stored products.
