# What PPE should I wear when spraying lawn chemistry?

Minimum kit for typical NZLA home lawn applications:

Always wear:
- Long sleeves and long trousers (no shorts).
- Closed shoes (not jandals or open sandals).
- Chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile gloves, $5 to $10 per pair).
- Eye protection - safety glasses or goggles.

Add for higher-risk applications:
- Respirator or P2/P3 mask for products with inhalation hazards (NZLA Sticker, some fungicides).
- Apron or chemical-resistant suit for full-coverage protection.
- Boots and waterproof overalls for heavy applications.

Match PPE to the product:

NZLA Iron+, Charger, Liquid Boost, Amino+, Humic+, Liquid N, Wetter:
- Standard PPE (long sleeves, gloves, eye protection).
- Low-toxicity products, basic precautions sufficient.

NZLA BWC, Gold, Halo, Etho, Meso, Sedge Pro (herbicides):
- Standard PPE plus careful handling at mixing.
- Avoid drift to skin and eyes.
- Wash hands and exposed skin after application.

NZLA Azoxy, Fungus Pro (fungicides):
- Standard PPE.
- Avoid eye contact (Fungus Pro contains thiram which is irritating).

NZLA Grub+ (insecticide):
- Standard PPE.
- Avoid skin contact, wash thoroughly after handling.

NZLA Sticker (organosilicone adjuvant):
- Higher-risk product. Toxic if inhaled.
- P2/P3 respirator mask essential.
- Sealed eye protection (goggles, not just glasses).
- Avoid skin contact.

NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride):
- Wear chemical-resistant gloves.
- Eye protection essential.
- Avoid inhalation of mist.

After application:
- Wash exposed skin thoroughly with soap and water.
- Wash PPE before next use.
- Wash spray clothes separately from regular laundry.
- Don't eat, drink or smoke during application.

For mixing chemicals (the highest-exposure step):
- Mix in a well-ventilated area, ideally outdoors.
- Use the same PPE plus extra care for the concentrated product.
- Pour carefully, avoid splashing.
- Use a measuring jug rather than pouring directly from the bottle.

Equipment safety:
- Knapsack sprayer in good condition (no leaks).
- Triple-rinse empty containers before disposal.
- Don't reuse empty chemical containers for anything else.

For commercial application (lawn care contractors):
- Approved Handler certification required for many products.
- Additional PPE requirements for some chemicals.
- Council compliance for residential spraying.

Read the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for each specific product before first use. SDS lists the actual hazards and required PPE for that product.

Most NZLA products at home lawn rates have low-to-moderate toxicity. Standard PPE precautions are adequate for typical use. Don't skip the basics because the products seem mild - regular exposure adds up over time.
