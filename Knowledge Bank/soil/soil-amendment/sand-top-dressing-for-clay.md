# Will sand top dressing improve heavy clay soil?

Yes, gradually, over multiple seasons. Sand top dressing is a slow but effective way to improve heavy clay lawn soil. Don't expect a one-off application to do much.

How it works:
- Sand applied to the surface gets worked into the top layer of soil by aeration, worm activity, rain, and gravity.
- Over years of repeated applications, the proportion of sand in the surface 100 mm of soil increases.
- The mixed sand-clay layer drains better than pure clay and develops better structure.

Realistic timeline:
- Year 1 to 2: minimal visible improvement, but the process is underway.
- Year 3 to 5: noticeably better drainage, less surface pooling.
- Year 5 to 10: surface soil substantially improved over what it was at the start.

Practical programme:
- Light top dress every autumn or spring (3 to 5 mm of sandy loam or screened topsoil).
- Pair with core aeration just before the top dressing (so the sand falls into the cores).
- Use sandy loam rather than pure builders' sand. Pure sand sits on top of clay rather than mixing in.
- Avoid heavy single applications (over 10 mm) which can smother the lawn.

Material specifications:
- Sandy loam (60 to 80% sand, 20 to 40% loam) is the standard top dressing for clay improvement.
- Pure sand only works on already-sandy soils (for sports field maintenance) or on very thick thatched lawns where you're flushing thatch.
- Don't use builders' sand or beach sand. Look for screened lawn top dressing from a landscape supply.

Cost over 5 years for a 200 m² lawn at 5 mm/year is about $400 to $700 in materials.

For deeper clay improvement (more than the surface 100 mm), you need cultivation rather than top dressing. Top dressing only works on the surface and above. For a brand-new lawn over heavy clay, mixing 100 to 200 mm of sandy loam into the surface before sowing is faster than years of top dressing.
