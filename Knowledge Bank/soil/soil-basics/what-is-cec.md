# What is CEC and why does it matter for my lawn?

CEC is cation exchange capacity. It's the soil's ability to hold and release positively charged nutrients (calcium, magnesium, potassium, ammonium and others).

Why it matters:
- High CEC soils hold more nutrients. Fertiliser applications stay available for longer, lawn responds steadily.
- Low CEC soils lose nutrients to leaching faster. Fertiliser washes through after rainfall, lawn needs more frequent feeding.
- CEC informs how aggressive your fertiliser programme needs to be.

Typical NZ soil CEC ranges (me/100g):
- Sandy soils: 5 to 15. Low CEC. Apply fertiliser more often at lighter rates.
- Loam soils: 15 to 25. Moderate CEC. Standard fertiliser programmes work well.
- Clay soils: 25 plus. High CEC. Hold nutrients well, but also hold problems (compaction, drainage).
- High organic matter soils: 25 to 40 plus. Highest CEC, behave like clay nutritionally but with better structure.

What changes CEC:
- Soil texture (sand to clay continuum). Doesn't change quickly.
- Organic matter content. The main thing you can influence over time.
- Clay mineral type. Doesn't change.

How to influence CEC on your lawn:
- Build organic matter. Mulch mowing (return clippings to the lawn) adds organic matter slowly. Top dressing with compost adds it faster. Worm-active soil cycles organic matter into stable humus.
- NZLA Humic+ supplies humic acid which contributes to CEC over time.
- Don't expect quick changes. CEC moves over years, not seasons.

For lawn programme decisions:
- Sandy soil with low CEC: more frequent lighter feeds. Slow-release granular base (All Seasons or All Seasons N+) plus monthly foliar Charger or Liquid Boost.
- Loam soil with moderate CEC: standard programme. Three to four granular applications per year.
- Clay soil with high CEC: granular feeds last longer, focus more on structure and drainage than feeding frequency.

CEC is one of those numbers on a soil test that informs your approach without dictating a specific action. Knowing your CEC range tells you what kind of programme suits the lawn.
