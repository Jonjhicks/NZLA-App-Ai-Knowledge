# Why does organic matter matter in lawn soil?

Organic matter is the foundation of healthy lawn soil. Several things it does:

Holds water. Soil with 5% organic matter holds twice the water of soil with 1%. Drought-tolerance starts in the soil chemistry, not the grass species.

Holds nutrients. Organic matter has high CEC, holds positively charged nutrients (potassium, calcium, ammonium nitrogen) where they're plant-available.

Feeds soil biology. Worms, fungi, bacteria all need organic matter as food. Without it, biology drops off and the soil becomes biologically dead.

Improves structure. Organic matter binds soil particles into aggregates, improving drainage on clay and water-holding on sand.

Buffers pH. Resists rapid swings in pH from fertiliser applications and rainfall.

Stores carbon. Healthy soil sequesters carbon, contributing to the climate side of sustainable lawn management.

What organic matter levels look like:
- Native bush soil: 8 to 15% (very high, accumulated over centuries).
- Mature pasture: 5 to 8%.
- Established home lawn that's mulch-mowed and top dressed: 4 to 6%.
- Standard home lawn that catches clippings, occasional top dress: 2 to 4%.
- New build over subsoil or scraped fill: under 1% (very low).

How to build organic matter on a lawn:
- Mulch mowing rather than catching clippings. Single biggest contributor.
- Top dressing with compost every 2 to 3 years.
- Encouraging worms and soil biology with NZLA Humic+, Amino+ and Nurture.
- Avoiding heavy chemical use that kills soil biology.
- Letting the lawn grow to a sensible height (40 to 50 mm) so the root system contributes more biomass to soil over time.

Realistic timeline:
- Building organic matter takes years. Going from 2% to 4% takes 5 to 10 years of consistent good practice.
- Losing organic matter is much faster. Heavy chemistry use, scraping topsoil, or constant catching with no return can drop organic matter measurably in a single season.

For a new lawn from scratch on poor subsoil, importing topsoil with 3 to 4% organic matter is the fast way to start. Building organic matter from below 1% takes a long time.
