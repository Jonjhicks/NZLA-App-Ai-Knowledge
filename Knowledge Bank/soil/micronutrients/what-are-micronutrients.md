# What are micronutrients and does my lawn need them?

Micronutrients are the nutrients lawns need in small amounts (compared to nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium). They include iron, manganese, zinc, copper, boron, molybdenum and chlorine.

For most NZ home lawns, micronutrients aren't the limiting factor. The soil supplies enough of most of them, and standard granular fertilisers (NZLA All Seasons has 5% iron, All Seasons has 6% iron) cover what's typically needed.

When micronutrients matter:
- Iron deficiency (chlorosis): the most common micronutrient issue on NZ lawns. Pale yellow-green colour despite adequate nitrogen. NZLA Iron+ chelated iron is the foliar fix.
- Manganese deficiency: less common, more likely on alkaline soils. Pale interveinal chlorosis on younger leaves.
- Sulphur deficiency: occasional on ex-pasture and lifestyle blocks. Uniform pale colour, looks like nitrogen deficiency.
- Zinc deficiency: rare on lawns, more common on cropping ground.
- Boron deficiency: very rare on lawns, mostly a horticulture issue.

What drives micronutrient issues:
- Soil pH outside 6.0 to 6.5 reduces availability of iron, manganese, zinc.
- Sandy soils with low organic matter hold fewer micronutrients.
- Very high phosphorus levels can interfere with iron and zinc uptake.
- Long-term fertilising with NPK only and no trace elements can deplete soil over decades.

For most home lawns the simple programme covers it:
- Granular base (NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+) supplies iron plus minor amounts of other trace elements.
- Foliar Iron+ for additional colour and to address iron deficiency on alkaline soils.
- Soil test if symptoms suggest something beyond the standard micronutrient picture.

Don't chase micronutrient supplementation as a routine practice. Most NZ lawns don't need more than what's in the standard fertiliser range. Specific deficiencies show specific symptoms and are addressed with specific products.
