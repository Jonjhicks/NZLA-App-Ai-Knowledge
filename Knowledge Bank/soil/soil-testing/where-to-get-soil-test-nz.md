# Where do I get a soil test done in NZ?

Several options at different price and detail levels.

Commercial labs:
- Hill Laboratories (hill-laboratories.com), Hamilton-based, broad service. The standard basic soil test gives pH, Olsen P (phosphorus), exchangeable potassium, calcium, magnesium, sodium, plus base saturation. Most home users get more than they need from the basic test.
- Eurofins NZ - similar service offering.
- Analytical Research Laboratories (ARL) - longer-standing testing service.

Most labs are set up for farm and commercial customers but accept home lawn samples through the same process. Pricing for a basic lawn soil test is typically $50 to $100 depending on the lab and what's included.

DIY test kits from garden centres:
- Quick pH tests (paper strips or simple chemical kits) cost $10 to $20.
- Less accurate than lab tests but adequate for confirming whether your soil is acidic or alkaline.
- Doesn't tell you about nutrient levels or CEC.

What you actually need:
- For a problem-solving test (lawn isn't responding to fertiliser, suspect pH issue): basic lab test is worth the money.
- For a programme baseline: lab test once every 3 to 5 years to track changes.
- For quick pH check: DIY kit is fine.

When ordering, tell the lab it's for lawn turf. They'll send the right sampling instructions and might suggest additional tests (sulphur, trace elements) depending on your symptoms.

For most NZ home lawns, the basic test ($60 to $80 range) is enough. The premium tests with full micronutrient panels are useful if you've got a specific problem the basic test didn't explain.
