# My lawn has brown patches - what's the cause?

Brown is the next stage past yellow. The grass has stopped functioning, the question is why.

Round well-defined brown patches that appeared in summer humidity: brown patch disease. NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro for treatment, plus cutting back on irrigation if it's been overdone.

Patches that lift like loose carpet when you tug at the grass: grass grub or black beetle larvae chewing the roots underneath. Pull up a small section and check for white curl-shaped grubs in the soil. NZLA Grub+ is the treatment side.

Patches that won't take water no matter how much you irrigate: hydrophobic soil or dry patch. The water beads off the surface. NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W breaks the surface tension and rehydrates the patch.

Brown patches in high-use areas (pathways, kids' play zones, dog runs): wear damage and compaction. Aeration plus reseeding fixes it. NZLA RSR Ryegrass is the species that recovers fastest from wear if you're overseeding.

Brown patches in low spots after heavy rain: waterlogging. The roots have drowned. Improve drainage with aeration, NZLA Aerate to move water through compacted soil, or physical drainage installation in serious cases.

Brown patches with a yellowing ring around them: fusarium patch progressed from the early stage.

Whole sections of the lawn brown but not patchy: drought stress, heat stress, or in upper NI in winter, warm-season turf going dormant (kikuyu, couch). Warm-season dormancy isn't damage, the lawn greens back up in spring.

If it's none of these, post a photo with details on the NZLA app forum.
