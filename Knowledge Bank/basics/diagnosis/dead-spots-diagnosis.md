# My lawn has dead spots - what caused them?

Dead is past brown. The grass is gone, the question is what killed it and how to repair.

Round dead spots with a darker green ring around the outside: dog urine, particularly female dogs. The concentrated nitrogen and salt burn the centre and the perimeter is fertilised. Repair by flushing with water (dilute the salt), then reseeding with NZLA Starter and Liquid Starter.

Dead patches in lines or stripes: chemical spillage, leaking sprayer or spreader, petrol or oil from mower maintenance.

Dead patches that pull up like loose carpet: grub damage. Roots eaten underneath. NZLA Grub+ treats the next round of grubs but the current dead patches need reseeding.

Dead patches with no obvious cause and irregular shape: previous disease that wasn't treated and progressed past recovery. The fungus moved on but the dead grass remains.

Dead patches under recently removed trees: stump or root decomposition tying up nitrogen. Time and a strong fertiliser programme bring it back.

Dead patches where a paddling pool or trampoline sat for weeks in summer: suffocation. The lawn was blocked from light and trapped moisture. Reseed.

Dead patches over buried debris (old concrete, pipe trench, gravel layer): shallow soil over an obstruction. The grass can't get roots deep. Either dig out and replace soil or accept the patch will struggle.

Dead patches from scalping (uneven ground hit by mower): mowing damage. Level the high spots, reseed the scalped patches.

For most dead spot situations, the repair process is the same: rake out the dead material, sprinkle NZLA Starter, sow seed, water lightly and frequently. Then address the underlying cause so it doesn't happen again.
