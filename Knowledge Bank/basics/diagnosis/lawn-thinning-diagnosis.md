# Why is my lawn thinning out?

Thinning means the lawn is losing density, you can see soil between the tillers. Various causes, often combined.

Under-fertilised. The most common reason. Grass needs nitrogen, potassium and trace elements to maintain density. Without enough, the lawn slowly thins out and weeds move into the gaps. NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ three to four times a year is the base programme. Add foliar Liquid Boost or Liquid N between granular feeds for sharper response.

Mowing too short. Cutting under 25 mm on most cool-season home lawns puts the lawn under continuous stress, density drops, weeds move in. Raise the cutting height to 40 to 50 mm.

Compacted soil. Roots can't develop in dense soil, density drops above ground. Core aeration once or twice a year plus NZLA Aerate through summer helps. Lawn over heavy clay is particularly prone.

Wear and traffic. High-use areas thin from foot pressure. Overseeding with NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing stoloniferous cultivar) helps the lawn recover from wear faster.

Shade. Heavy shade thins any lawn regardless of feed. NZLA Fine Fescue is the most shade-tolerant option. If shade is heavy, accept the thinner lawn or change to a ground cover.

Drought stress. Repeated unwatered summers thin the lawn out. Either irrigate consistently or shift to more drought-tolerant species (tall fescue, kikuyu in upper NI).

Disease damage left to recover on its own. Repeated mild disease over years thins the lawn. Treat disease when it appears, then push recovery with Liquid Boost.

Iron deficiency. The lawn doesn't thin much but looks pale and thin together. NZLA Iron+ for colour, address fertility for density.

Overseeding with NZLA Dark Perennial Rye or RSR Ryegrass in autumn rebuilds density faster than waiting for tillers from existing grass.
