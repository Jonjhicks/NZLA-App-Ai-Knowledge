# My lawn has yellow patches - what's the cause?

Work through the possibilities in this order, most common first.

Round or irregular yellow patches that appeared in a humid spell: fusarium patch or another fungal disease. Look for the patch edge being defined and a small ring of darker grass around it. NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro is the treatment side, plus reducing the conditions that brought it on (less irrigation, less nitrogen if applied recently).

Yellow that's the whole lawn, not patches: nitrogen deficiency or iron deficiency. A general yellow-pale colour across the lawn is fixed by applying nitrogen (NZLA Liquid N, Liquid Boost or a granular feed). If the lawn's growing fine but just pale, iron is more likely the issue and NZLA Iron+ does the job in a few days.

Distinct circular yellow patches with a dead spot in the middle: dog urine if pets use the area, or a single fertiliser granule that landed concentrated.

Yellow stripes or bands: uneven fertiliser application. Spreader pattern overlapped in some lines, missed in others. Fix with another application over the gaps or wait it out, the lawn evens up in a few weeks.

Yellow patches where you didn't expect them, particularly in winter or shoulder seasons: red thread or rust. Look for the pink-red threads on red thread, or orange dust on your shoes for rust.

Yellow in heavy shade or under trees: not enough light. Chemistry won't fix it, the lawn needs more sun or you need shade-tolerant species (NZLA Fine Fescue).

If the pattern doesn't fit any of these, post a photo on the NZLA app forum.
