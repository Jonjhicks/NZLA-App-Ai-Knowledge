# Where do I start when something's wrong with my lawn?

Three things to look at before anything else.

What's it look like and where. Patches in shaded spots tell a different story to patches in full sun. Pet pathways tell a different story to garden edges. Take a couple of photos in daylight, including a wide shot of the whole lawn and a close-up of the worst patch.

When did it start. A problem that appeared after a hot dry week is likely heat stress or disease. A problem that's been creeping in over months is more likely a fertility or compaction issue. Customers often forget the timeline and reach for the chemistry first.

What's changed recently. New chemistry applied. Pet added. Tree removed (more sun). Tree planted (more shade). Sprinkler system installed or stopped working. Renovation done. Fertiliser changed. Mowing height changed. All of these change what the lawn is doing.

Once you've got those three things, the problem narrows down fast. Yellow patches in the shape of stripes after spreading fertiliser is uneven application, not disease. Round yellow patches after a humid week is fusarium, not nutrient deficiency. Pale lawn that's been going for weeks with no obvious trigger is most often nitrogen deficiency, not anything exotic.

If you're still stuck, post a photo on the NZLA app forum with the three pieces of info. Jon and the regulars can usually call it in a day.
