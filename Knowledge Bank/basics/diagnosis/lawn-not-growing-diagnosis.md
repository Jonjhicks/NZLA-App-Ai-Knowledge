# My lawn isn't growing - what's wrong?

Lawn that's alive but not growing usually points to one of a few causes.

Time of year. Cool-season lawns slow down in cold months, especially in southern NZ. Below soil temps of about 8 °C, ryegrass and fescue barely grow. That's not a problem, it's normal seasonal behaviour. Warm-season lawns (kikuyu, couch) are dormant in winter and won't grow until soil temps come back up to about 14 °C.

Drought stress. The lawn shuts down growth to conserve water. Water deeply (15 to 25 mm) and growth resumes within days.

Heat stress in mid-summer. Cool-season lawns over 28 °C with no irrigation stop growing to survive the heat. Same fix: water deeply and let the lawn recover.

Nutrient deficiency. The lawn doesn't have what it needs to push growth. NZLA Liquid Boost or Liquid N gives a fast response within a week if nitrogen is the issue.

Compaction. Roots can't function in dense soil, top growth slows. Core aeration plus NZLA Aerate through summer.

PGR carry-over. If you've applied a growth regulator (NZLA PGR or Paclo) recently, the lawn is doing what it's supposed to do - growing less. Normal response, comes back at the end of the regulation period.

Disease pressure. Fusarium or other disease has stopped the lawn growing. Treat with NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro, then push recovery with Liquid Boost.

Recently sprayed with herbicide. Selective herbicides slow the lawn for a few weeks. Normal. Resume normal feed once the lawn is releasing.

Sleeping in heavy shade. The lawn isn't getting enough light to photosynthesise meaningfully. Chemistry won't fix the light problem.

Brand new lawn. New seedlings grow slowly for the first 4 to 6 weeks while the root system establishes. That's normal, don't push too hard on a new lawn.
