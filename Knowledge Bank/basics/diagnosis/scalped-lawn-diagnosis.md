# My lawn is scalped in patches - how do I fix it?

Scalping is where the mower has hit a high spot and cut down to soil. Shows as brown or bare spots in roughly the same places after every mow.

Two parts to the fix.

First, the immediate damage. Light scalp recovers on its own in 2 to 4 weeks if you raise the cutting height for a couple of mows and apply NZLA Liquid Boost to push the recovery. Heavy scalp where soil is exposed needs reseeding. Rake the bare area, sprinkle NZLA Starter, sow seed, water lightly until established.

Second, stop it happening again. Scalping is caused by uneven ground - the mower wheels sit lower than the high spot and the blade hits it. Three options to fix the high spot.

Light bumps: raise the mowing height. Bringing the cut from 30 mm to 50 mm avoids most minor unevenness without needing earthworks.

Moderate bumps: top dress over a couple of seasons. Spread sandy loam or screened topsoil to level the dip around the bump rather than removing the bump. Over 2 to 3 seasons of light annual top dressing, the lawn evens out.

Severe bumps: physical levelling. Cut around the bump with a spade, lift the turf, remove or redistribute the soil underneath, replace the turf. Or if the bump is from a buried object (concrete, pipe, root), dig it out.

For lawns that scalp consistently in the same patches every mow, the underlying problem is unevenness from settling (over old building debris, drainage trenches, tree roots) or from animal activity (gophers don't exist in NZ, but mole crickets or possums dig in some regions). The ground needs to be properly levelled before the lawn can be mown flat.
