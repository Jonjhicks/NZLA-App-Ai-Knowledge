# Product Safety

Full article on the NZLA blog: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/nzla-product-safety/

## The broad categories

NZLA products fall into two groups from a safety perspective.

**Foliar fertilisers (Charger, Iron+, Liquid Boost, Liquid Starter, Amino+, Root Health, Nurture, Restore, Humic+, Liquid N):** Not classified as dangerous goods. Non-toxic to humans and pets. Keep people and pets off the lawn until the spray has dried — this is to prevent product being tracked inside on feet, and to avoid diluting the product before it's absorbed. Not a safety restriction.

**Herbicides, fungicides, insecticides (BWC, Gold, Halo, Etho, Blockade, Meso, Sedge Pro, Azoxy, Fungus Pro, Posterity, Grub+):** EPA-regulated chemistry. These are stronger products with genuine safety considerations. Follow the label exactly — this is a legal requirement under NZ law, not a suggestion.

General rule for the regulated products: keep children and pets off the treated area for 24 hours after application, unless the product label specifies a different period. When in doubt, wait longer.

## Pet safety

**Dogs eating granular fertiliser:** NZLA granular fertilisers are not toxic, but dogs that tend to eat things off the lawn should be kept off for a few hours after a granular application, until granules are watered in or have dissolved into the soil. If the concern is a dog that consistently eats granular, switch to a foliar programme — Charger and Iron+ deliver similar nutrition without granules present on the surface.

**Cats and dogs after herbicide/fungicide:** Keep off for 24 hours. The main risk is paws picking up product and the animal licking their feet. Once the lawn is dry and 24 hours has passed, risk is very low.

**NZLA Grub+:** Keep off until dry. Chlorantraniliprole has a very low mammalian toxicity profile, but the label instruction is to keep off until dry and it should be followed.

**NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride):** DANGER classification. This is the highest-hazard product in the NZLA range. Causes severe skin burns and serious eye damage. Keep people and pets entirely clear during application and until fully dry. Commercial use requires a qualified handler. This is not a product to apply carelessly.

For any product, Section 11 of the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) covers toxicology — useful for concerned pet owners. SDS documents for all NZLA products are available through the website contact page.

## Children

The same 24-hour rule applies for the regulated products. For foliar fertilisers, until dry is sufficient.

Supervise young children around any product container — keep storage locked or out of reach. Follow label instructions for all products.

## Near vegetable gardens

Take care with spray drift when applying selective herbicides near vegetable or ornamental gardens. Broadleaf selective herbicides (BWC, Gold) will damage or kill broadleaf vegetables, flowers, and ornamental plants. Apply on calm days, use a shield or cardboard to protect garden edges from drift, and don't spray when wind will carry product sideways.

Fungicides (Azoxy, Fungus Pro) and fertilisers generally have lower off-target risk in garden situations, but read the label for any specific restrictions.

## PPE

PPE requirements vary by product. Never assume one product's PPE requirements apply to another. Read the label before each application.

As a general baseline: gloves for all liquid product applications. Eye protection when mixing concentrates or applying overhead. Covered footwear. Long sleeves for higher-hazard products (NZLA Moss, concentrated herbicides).

More detail in the chemistry-safety/ppe article.

## Reading the label

NZ has moved to the Globally Harmonised System (GHS) labelling standard. Look for pictograms:
- Exclamation mark (!) — irritant or less serious hazard
- Health hazard symbol (person with starburst) — more serious health effects possible
- Skull and crossbones — acute toxicity (not on NZLA products at standard rates)
- Environmental hazard (dead fish/tree) — ecotoxic; take care near waterways

The product label is a legal document. Using a product in a way inconsistent with its label is an offence under the HSNO Act.

## Safety Data Sheets

Every NZLA product has an SDS. Available via the website contact/FAQ page. If you need specific toxicology data, exposure limit information, or first aid procedures for a specific product, the SDS is the authoritative document.
