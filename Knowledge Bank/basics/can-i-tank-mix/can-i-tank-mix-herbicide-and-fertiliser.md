# Can I tank mix herbicide and fertiliser?

Sometimes, but be careful and always jar-test first.

The reasons it can work. Combining the two saves a separate spray application, which is convenient. Herbicide uptake can be improved by certain fertilisers (some foliar nutrients act as adjuvants). And for selective broadleaf herbicides on lawns, the lawn benefits from the feed while the herbicide does its job on the weeds.

The reasons it can go wrong. Hormone-type broadleaf herbicides (BWC, Gold) can react with iron in foliar fertilisers (Iron+, Liquid Boost) — at minimum reducing herbicide effectiveness, at worst causing precipitation or sludge in the tank. High-salt fertilisers can affect herbicide uptake. And some adjuvants or stabilisers in fertilisers conflict with herbicide formulations.

Jar test is non-negotiable. Add small amounts of each (at the same ratio you'd use in the tank) to a jar of clean water. Cap, shake, leave for 30 minutes. Watch for separation, precipitation, curdling, or sludge. If the mix stays uniform, you're probably fine in the tank.

Safer combinations from experience:

NZLA Charger (low salt, balanced foliar) tank mixes reasonably well with most selective broadleaf herbicides. Test first.

NZLA Iron+ and Liquid Boost are more reactive — better to spray them separately a few days before or after the herbicide.

What never to do: tank mix Group 3 fungicides (DMIs) with NZLA Paclo (paclobutrazol). Severe over-regulation can result.

When in doubt, apply separately a week apart. Slightly more work, no risk.
