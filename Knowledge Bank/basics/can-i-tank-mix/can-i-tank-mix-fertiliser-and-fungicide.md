# Can I tank mix fertiliser and fungicide?

Sometimes, but always jar-test first.

The jar test is straightforward. Add small amounts of each product (at the same ratio you would use in the tank) to a clean jar of water. Cap it, shake, and let it sit for 30 minutes. Watch for separation, precipitation (chunks settling out), curdling, or sludge formation. If the mix stays homogeneous, you are probably fine. If it separates or precipitates, do not put it in the tank.

Some general rules from experience:

Low salt index foliar fertilisers (NZLA Charger is the obvious one) generally tank-mix well with most fungicides. The non-corrosive, low-burn-risk chemistry suits combining with other actives.

High-iron products (Iron+, Liquid Boost) can react with some fungicide chemistries. Iron is reactive and not always a friendly tank partner. Jar-test carefully.

Group 3 fungicides (DMIs like propiconazole, tebuconazole, triadimenol, triticonazole) should NOT be mixed with NZLA Paclo (paclobutrazol). Severe over-regulation can result. This is the most important hard restriction on PGR tank mixing.

Read both labels carefully — some products specifically list incompatible partners.

When in doubt, apply separately a week apart. Slightly more work but no risk of either product being compromised or causing damage.
