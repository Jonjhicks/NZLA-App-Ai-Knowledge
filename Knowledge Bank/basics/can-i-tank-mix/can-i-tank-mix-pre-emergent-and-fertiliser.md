# Can I tank mix pre-emergent and fertiliser?

Generally yes for granular pre-em with liquid foliar fertiliser applied separately, but tank mixing liquid pre-em with liquid fertiliser has some specifics to watch.

The practical situation:

NZLA Blockade is a liquid suspension concentrate. Tank-mixing it with a foliar fertiliser like Charger or Iron+ is possible but adds complexity. Blockade needs to bind into the top centimetre of soil, which requires the spray to land on the soil surface (not just the leaf). Foliar fertilisers are designed to coat and be absorbed by the leaf. The two delivery requirements pull in different directions.

If you tank mix, you compromise both — neither the pre-em gets ideal soil contact nor the foliar gets ideal leaf coverage. Better practice is to apply them as separate operations.

The other issue is the post-application requirement. Blockade needs 6 mm of irrigation within 7 days to activate (water moves it into the soil). Foliar fertilisers specifically don't want to be watered in immediately (you want the leaf to absorb the spray). Same timing of water-in suits Blockade but ruins the foliar.

The practical workflow most lawn care managers use:

Apply the foliar fertiliser. Wait 24 hours for the leaf to absorb. Then apply the pre-em separately. Water in the pre-em with 6 mm of irrigation.

Or apply pre-em first, water in to activate, wait a day or two, then apply foliar.

Granular fertiliser plus liquid pre-em as separate operations is the easiest pattern. Apply All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular, water in normally, then a few days later apply Blockade as the targeted operation.

When in doubt, apply separately. The extra time is small; the risk of doing one badly is real.
