# Premium Showcase Lawn Programme

This is the high-input programme. It's not for everyone — it takes more time, more product, and more consistent attention. But for people who want the best result possible from their lawn, this is how you get there.

Think of it as the difference between maintaining a car and building a show car. Both are cars. The inputs are different.

---

## What makes a premium lawn different

Three things separate a showcase lawn from a well-maintained one.

**Density.** More tillers per square metre. Achieved through consistent nutrition, close mowing, and PGR use.

**Colour.** Deep green that holds through dry periods and seasonal stress. Achieved through a foliar iron and nitrogen programme that runs year-round.

**Uniformity.** No coarse stems, no seed heads, no uneven growth. PGR suppresses vertical growth and seed head formation, redirecting energy into lateral tiller development.

---

## Plant growth regulators — what they do

PGRs (specifically trinexapac-ethyl in NZLA Primo) slow vertical stem elongation by reducing a naturally occurring growth hormone (gibberellin). The grass doesn't grow less — it grows differently. Energy that would have gone into stem extension goes into lateral growth, tiller density, and root development.

The result: finer texture, denser canopy, reduced mowing frequency, suppressed seed head formation.

**What PGR is not:** It's not a fertiliser. It's not a pesticide. It won't fix nutrition problems — a nitrogen-deficient lawn that gets PGR applied just turns yellow and grows slowly. The prerequisite for PGR is a well-fed lawn.

---

## Prerequisites before starting PGR

Don't start Primo unless the following are in place:

1. **Adequate nutrition.** The lawn must be on a consistent fertiliser programme. Minimum: Charger + Iron+ fortnightly, All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks.
2. **No active stress.** No drought stress, or recovery from renovation. PGR on a stressed lawn compounds the stress.
3. **Established turf.** New lawns need one full growing season before PGR. Seedlings under six months should not receive PGR.
4. **Correct mowing height.** At or below 40 mm for ryegrass. Fine fescue: 20 to 35 mm. Tall fescue: 40 to 55 mm.

---

## NZLA Primo — rate and timing

**Rate:** 30 to 50 mL/100m² per application. Start at 30 mL for the first two applications and assess response before increasing.

**Interval:** Every 3 to 5 weeks during active growth. In summer heat or if growth slows noticeably, extend to 5 to 6 weeks. Don't apply to dormant or very slow-growing grass — you'll delay spring green-up.

**Timing window:** September to April for cool-season turf. Avoid applications within 6 weeks of expected first frost for Otago/Southland.

**Don't tank-mix Primo with Azoxy.** Apply separately. Primo and herbicides: apply Primo at least 48 hours before or after herbicide applications.

**Wetter:** Always apply Primo with Wetter 3W at 30 to 50 mL/100m². Even coverage matters — any misses show in the sward.

---

## Weekly rhythm during peak season (Oct to Mar)

A showcase programme in peak season looks something like this:

**Week 1:** Charger 100 to 150 mL/100m² + Iron+ 100 mL/100m² + Wetter 3W 50 mL/100m²
**Week 3:** Charger 100 to 150 mL/100m² + Iron+ 100 mL/100m² + Wetter 3W 50 mL/100m²
**Week 4-5:** Primo 30 to 50 mL/100m² + Wetter 3W 50 mL/100m² (separate spray to week 3 if possible — at least 24-48 hours apart)

**Monthly:** All Seasons granular 2 to 2.5 kg/100m²

**Seasonal:** Azoxy preventative 50 mL/100m² in high--risk periods (late summer/autumn for dollar spot, cool wet periods for fusarium)

---

## Iron programme

Iron is where the colour comes from. In a standard programme, fortnightly Iron+ is adequate. In a premium programme, Iron+ every 7 to 10 days produces a noticeably deeper colour.

Don't exceed the label rate — excess iron causes a dark bleaching effect rather than deeper colour. 100 mL/100m² per application is the sweet spot.

If the lawn starts looking very dark grey-green rather than rich dark green, you're over-applying iron. Pull back to fortnightly.

---

## Mowing for premium results

**Frequency.** Mow as often as needed to never remove more than 25% of the leaf at once. During rapid spring growth this could be twice weekly. During PGR activity this typically drops to once weekly or less.

**Height.** For ryegrass on a PGR programme: 25 to 35 mm. For fine fescue or Midnight: 15 to 25 mm. Don't try to get a premium result from grass cut at 50 mm — the sward architecture doesn't support it.

**Blades.** Sharp blades. Torn leaf tips turn white within a day on a high-nitrogen, closely mown lawn. Sharpen or replace blades regularly.

**Clippings.** In a high-input programme, bag clippings. At mowing intervals of 3 to 4 days in peak growth, clippings can smother fine turf.

---

## Autumn — the most important season for premium lawns

Most premium lawns are made or broken in autumn. The March to June period is when density improvements from a summer programme become visible, and when the lawn needs to build the carbohydrate reserves and root mass to survive winter with colour intact.

**March:** Liquid Boost application if lawn needs a push heading into autumn. Azoxy preventative application — dollar spot is still active into March in warm areas.
**April:** Ease off PGR. One final Primo application no later than mid-April for most NZ regions.
**April through June:** Shift to foliar-dominant programme (Charger + Iron+ every 10-14 days). NZLA Green from April for cool-season colour.
**Aeration:** Deep-tine aeration in April or May. Critical for premium lawns — high-traffic, densely thatched premium turf compacts faster than a standard lawn.

## Disease management on premium programmes

High-input lawns are higher  risk. Dense sward, high nitrogen, frequent irrigation-general — all favour . An unmanaged premium programme gets sick.

**Preventative Azoxy** (Group 11 fungicide, azoxystrobin) at 50 mL/100m² every 4 to 6 weeks during high-risk periods. Don't wait for  to appear — by then you're dealing with damage, not prevention.

**Posterity** (Group 7, fluopyram) for managing dollar spot where resistance is suspected. Rotate Azoxy with Posterity — don't run two consecutive Azoxy applications before switching. More detail: azoxy-vs-fungus-pro article management articles.

**Signs of pressure:** small tan circular spots 20 to 50 mm (dollar spot), water-soaked patches that turn bronze (fusarium in cool wet weather), irregular dark patches (brown patch in summer). Respond immediately.

## What to expect — realistic timeline

**Month 1 to 2:** Improved colour response. PGR reduces mowing interval.

**Month 2 to 3:** Visible increase in density. Finer texture starting to show. Leaf width reduces slightly.

**Month 4 to 6:** First full season results. Lawn looks materially different from a standard programme.

**12 months:** A lawn that has had a full year on a premium programme is substantially denser, finer, and more uniform than when it started — assuming the nutrition was right throughout.

PGR won't rescue a poor lawn quickly. It amplifies what's already there. If the lawn is thin and yellow, start with the nutrition and get it healthy first, then layer PGR on top.
