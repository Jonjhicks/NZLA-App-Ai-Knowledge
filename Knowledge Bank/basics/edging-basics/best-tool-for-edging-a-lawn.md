# What is the best tool for edging a lawn?

Depends on the job — three different tools for three different stages.

**Weekly maintenance: line trimmer (whipper snipper).** The standard home tool. Petrol or electric, with a rotating head holding nylon line that cuts grass. Quick to use along the lawn edge each week after mowing. Cordless electric models are increasingly capable for most home lawns and quieter than petrol.

**Defining or restoring the edge line: half-moon edger.** A curved blade on a long handle that you push vertically into the lawn along the edge line. Creates a clean defined boundary that stops grass creeping into garden beds. Done twice a year (spring and autumn) it keeps the edge sharp. A sharp flat spade does the same job if you don't want a dedicated tool.

**Frequent precision edging: dedicated lawn edger machine.** Petrol or electric tool with a vertical blade specifically designed for cutting clean edges. Faster and more precise than a line trimmer for premium lawns where the edge appearance matters week-to-week. More expensive than a basic line trimmer and only worth it on lawns where the edge is a feature.

For most home users, a line trimmer plus a half-moon edger covers all the bases. About $200 to $400 combined.

Maintenance notes:

Line trimmer line wears fast. Replace when it's down to 20 mm exposed length. Use thicker line (2.4 to 3 mm) for tougher grass and lighter line (1.6 to 2 mm) for fine work.

Keep the half-moon edger sharp. Bench grinder once a season.

Mow before edging. The mower defines the surface; the edge defines the boundary.
