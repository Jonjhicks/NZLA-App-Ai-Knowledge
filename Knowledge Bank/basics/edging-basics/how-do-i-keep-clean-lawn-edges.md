# How do I keep clean lawn edges?

Three approaches that work, in order of effort and result.

**Regular trimming with a line trimmer.** The most common home approach. After mowing, run a line trimmer around the perimeter to take out the grass that grew over the edge during the week. Quick, no special equipment beyond the trimmer, and good enough for most home lawns. Trade-off: the edge tends to creep outward over time as the grass plant pushes the boundary.

**Half-moon edger or sharp spade twice a year.** A vertical cut through the lawn surface that defines the edge cleanly. Done in spring and again in autumn, it restores the original edge line and stops the gradual creep. The half-moon edger is a specific tool — a curved blade on a long handle — but a sharp flat spade works too. Push it vertically into the lawn along the desired edge line, lift the soil/turf cap on the bed side, and you have a clean defined edge. Combined with weekly trimming, this gives a properly maintained line.

**Hard edging material installed.** Concrete, brick, paver or steel edging installed permanently along the lawn boundary. The mower can cut right up to the edge without overhang. No trimming needed. Significant upfront cost and installation but minimal ongoing maintenance. Standard option for premium home lawns and commercial landscaping.

Three habits that improve any of these:

Mow first, then trim/edge. Cleaner finish.

Walk a consistent line each week. Inconsistent edges drift over months.

Sharpen the trimmer line head and replace line regularly. Worn line tears rather than cuts.
