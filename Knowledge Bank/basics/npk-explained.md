# Understanding NPK

Every fertiliser label shows three numbers: N-P-K. These are the percentages by weight of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium in the product. NZLA All Seasons is 21-1-16, meaning 21% nitrogen, 1% phosphorus, 16% potassium.

Full explanation: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/understanding-lawn-npk/

---

## The three macronutrients

**Nitrogen (N)** drives chlorophyll production (colour), leaf and stem development, and overall growth rate. It's the primary determinant of how green a lawn is and how fast it grows. Deficiency: yellow, thin, slow-growing turf. Excess: rapid soft growth, increased  susceptibility.

**Phosphorus (P)** drives root development and energy transfer through photosynthesis. Moves slowly through the soil; present in low amounts in most professional turf fertilisers (including NZLA All Seasons at 1%) because established turf has low ongoing phosphorus demand. High-phosphorus products (like NZLA Starter at high P) are used at establishment when root system development is the priority.

**Potassium (K)** regulates water movement within the plant and builds stress tolerance — heat, drought, and  resistance. Helps the plant use nitrogen efficiently. Deficiency leads to weaker plants that are more susceptible to pressure. Charger is 7-0-7, giving a balanced N:K ratio for colour maintenance without heavy nitrogen push.

## What the numbers look like in practice

**NZLA All Seasons 21-1-16+5%Fe:** Balanced maintenance. High N for growth, high K for stress tolerance, iron for colour.

**NZLA All Seasons N+ 32-2-10+2%Fe:** Growth push. Significantly higher N, lower K ratio. For spring or event preparation, not year-round.

**NZLA Starter 8-10-8:** New lawn establishment. High phosphorus for root development.

**NZLA Charger 7-0-7+4%Fe:** Colour maintenance foliar. Low N, equal K, high iron. For regular foliar applications without pushing rapid growth.

**NZLA Liquid Boost 15-0-0+6%Fe:** Nitrogen and iron push, no P or K. For rapid colour correction and recovery situations.

## Micronutrients

**Iron (Fe):** Chlorophyll production and cellular function. The primary driver of deep green colour beyond what nitrogen alone provides. Iron+ is a standalone chelated iron product — it delivers colour without growth stimulus.

**Calcium:** Stem and root cell wall development, metabolic processes.

**Manganese:** Chlorophyll formation. Deficiency shows as grey or tan spots. Excess causes brown spotting.

**Zinc:** Metabolic regulation and chlorophyll production.

## Reading the label

A label showing 21-1-16 tells you the ratio but not the total amount applied. The amount is determined by application rate (kg/100m²). 2 kg of All Seasons per 100m² delivers 420g of nitrogen, 20g of phosphorus, and 320g of potassium to that area. That's why application rate matters as much as the NPK numbers.

High NPK numbers aren't automatically better. A 30-0-0 product delivers a lot of nitrogen and nothing else. A balanced 21-1-16 supports the whole plant system. Match the product to what the lawn needs at that time of year.
