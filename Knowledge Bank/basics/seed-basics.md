# Grass Seed Basics

## Choosing the right seed

Match species to situation. The detail is in the lawn-choices article. The short version:

**NZLA Perennial Rye:** standard choice for most of NZ. Reliable, winter-active, fast establishment.

**Midnight Dark Rye / Dark Perennial Rye:** premium darker colour and finer texture for showcase lawns.

**NZLA RSR Ryegrass:** stoloniferous, self-repairing variety for high-traffic lawns. Fills wear damage laterally.

**NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue / Elfin Tall Fescue:** drought tolerance, heat tolerance, partial shade. Best for Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, Marlborough and upper NI hot dry sites.

**NZLA Fine Fescue:** shade tolerance. Low fertility, low traffic applications.

**NZLA Kikuyu:** warm-season for upper NI. Drought-tolerant, wear-tolerant.

**NZLA Couch:** warm-season fine-textured premium option for upper NI.

For seeding-and-over-seeding an existing lawn: match the dominant species, or accept a gradual species shift over 2 to 3 seasons as the new cultivar establishes.

## Germination times

All species need consistent surface moisture from sowing through to seedling establishment. Dry-out at day 7 on tall fescue kills seedlings before they're up.

**Perennial Ryegrass:** 5 to 10 days at 15 to 20°C soil temperature. 10 to 14 days at 10 to 15°C. Below 10°C, germination slows significantly. Below 5°C, essentially stops.

**Tall Fescue (Ultimate Tall Fescue, Elfin):** 10 to 14 days at warm temperatures, 14 to 21 days at cool. Needs consistent moisture through the longer window.

**Fine Fescue:** 10 to 14 days at optimal temperatures.

**Kikuyu:** 14 to 21 days, needs soil temperatures above 15°C, ideally 18°C+. Don't sow in cool soil — seed sits and rots.

**Couch:** similar to kikuyu, 15°C minimum, ideally 18°C+.

After visible germination, the seedling is fragile for another 4 to 6 weeks. Continue light frequent watering and don't rush the first mow.

Best sowing windows: spring (soil temperatures climbing through the optimal range) and late summer / early autumn (for cool-season species — soil still warm, rains arriving, temperatures dropping away from heat stress).

## Sowing rates

Too little seed means a thin patchy lawn that  move into. Too much means crowded seedlings that compete and thin out anyway. Each species has an optimum range.

**Perennial Rye, MDR, Dark Perennial Rye, RSR Ryegrass:** new lawn 3 to 4 kg per 100m². Overseeding 2 kg per 100m².

**Tall Fescue (UTF, Elfin):** new lawn 3 to 5 kg per 100m². Overseeding 2.5 kg per 100m².

**Fine Fescue:** new lawn 2.5 to 4 kg per 100m².

**Kikuyu:** refer to label — kikuyu seed is much finer. Rate by weight is lower than ryegrass.

**Calculating for your lawn:**

Measure area in square metres. Divide by 100. Multiply by the sowing rate.

Example: 350m² lawn, NZLA Perennial Rye at 4 kg per 100m²:
350 ÷ 100 = 3.5 units × 4 kg = 14 kg total. NZLA Perennial Rye is sold in 5 kg bags — that's 3 bags with a small reserve for patching.

Same lawn seeding-and-over-seeding at 2 kg per 100m²: 3.5 × 2 = 7 kg.

## Sowing technique

Apply at half the total rate in one direction, then the other half at right angles. Cross-pattern sowing reduces misses.

Lightly rake to mix seed into the top 5 to 10 mm of soil. Seed sitting on top of dry surface doesn't germinate — it needs soil contact and consistent moisture.

Roll after sowing if a roller is available. Improves seed-to-soil contact without burying the seed.

Water lightly and frequently for the first 2 weeks — keep the surface consistently moist. Transition to less frequent deeper watering as seedlings establish.

Apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² at sowing. Water in with the first irrigation-general.

First mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height, taking only the top third off. Sharp blade — tearing leaf at the first mow on fragile seedlings does extended damage.
