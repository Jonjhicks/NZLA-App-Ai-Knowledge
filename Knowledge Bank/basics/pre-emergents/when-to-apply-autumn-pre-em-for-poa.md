# When should I apply pre-em for poa annua in autumn?

Late February through early April depending on region, before soil temperatures drop into the 18 to 22°C range where poa annua germinates.

Application 2 to 3 weeks before that gives the chemical barrier time to form in the soil. The general regional pattern:

- Auckland and upper North Island: mid to late March
- Central North Island: late March
- Canterbury and southern South Island: late March into early April

Watch soil temperatures at 50 mm depth, not the calendar. NIWA has soil temperature data for most regions, or use a soil thermometer if you've got one.

Activate with at least 6 mm of rain or irrigation within 7 days. Without that the chemical doesn't bind into the top centimetre of soil properly and the barrier never forms.

NZLA Blockade (prodiamine 476.2 g/L) at 10 to 40 mL per 100m² for poa annua control. Note the NZ label restriction — Blockade is registered for warm-season turf only here (kikuyu, couch), despite the chemistry being safe and widely used on cool-season turf internationally. Off-label use can't be officially recommended.

A second application 90 to 120 days later carries the barrier through the peak poa germination window.

Cultural control alongside — dense healthy desirable grass at the right mowing height outcompetes poa establishment. Pre-em alone never fully fixes a poa problem; it slows the seed bank while the cultural fix builds.
