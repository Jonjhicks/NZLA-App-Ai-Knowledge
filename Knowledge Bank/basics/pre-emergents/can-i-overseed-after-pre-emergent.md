# Can I overseed after applying pre-emergent?

Not within the residual period — typically 4 to 6 months after a Blockade application.

The reason: prodiamine doesn't distinguish between weed seed and the grass seed you've put down. Both germinating seeds hit the chemical barrier in the soil and die at the root tip. Pre-em that's still active kills your overseeding just as effectively as it kills the target weeds.

Specific NZLA Blockade timeline:

Residual lasts 4 to 6 months under typical conditions (full strength for the first 3 months, declining through months 4 to 6).

Plan overseeding to land at least 4 months after the most recent Blockade application, ideally 6 months.

Practical implications:

**Spring Blockade application (August-October).** Targeting summer grass germination. Residual carries through summer. Earliest overseeding window is autumn (March onwards). Won't affect normal autumn overseeding for cool-season lawns.

**Autumn Blockade application (March-April).** Targeting poa annua germination. Residual carries through winter. Earliest overseeding window is mid to late spring (October onwards). Affects any winter overseeding work.

If you need to overseed and you've recently applied Blockade:

Wait the full residual period. Period.

Don't try to overseed at half-life thinking the residual has weakened enough. Some seedlings might come up but most will fail, and the patchy result is worse than waiting.

If urgent overseeding is needed (severe damage, dog spots, etc.), seeded areas treated with Blockade in the last 3 months may need topsoil replacement to bypass the residual layer. Practical for small patches, not for large areas.

If you're planning a renovation involving overseeding, time the pre-em programme to land outside the renovation window. Skip a pre-em cycle rather than try to work around residual.

Pre-em and seeding are deliberately on different schedules for this reason. Most NZ year-round programmes alternate them by season — pre-em on top of established lawns; seeding work in the gaps when pre-em has cleared.
