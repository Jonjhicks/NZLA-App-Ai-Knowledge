# When should I put down pre-emergent in spring?

Timing is the whole game with pre-emergent. Get it down before soil temperatures at 50 mm depth hit 12°C and stay there. That is the germination trigger for summer grass and crabgrass. Application 2 to 3 weeks ahead of that gives the chemical barrier time to form properly.

Auckland and the upper North Island, that means late August. Central North Island, mid-September. Canterbury and the southern South Island, late September into early October. Watch soil temperatures, not the calendar.

Activate it with 6 mm of rain or irrigation within 7 days of application. Without that the chemical does not bind into the soil properly and the barrier never forms. Do not apply if heavy rain is forecast within 48 hours, you will wash it off-target.

For NZLA Blockade, 10 to 30 mL per 100m². Note the NZ label restriction — Blockade is registered for warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) only here, even though the chemistry is safe on cool-season grass and widely used on it overseas. Off-label use on cool-season cannot be officially recommended.

A second application 90 to 120 days later carries the barrier through peak summer grass germination.
