# Choosing the Right Lawn

## Grass species by region and use

Match the species to what your lawn actually deals with. The NZLA range covers every NZ situation.

**Upper North Island — Auckland, Northland, Bay of Plenty, Waikato.**
Warm humid summers, mild winters. Standard ryegrass struggles in the heat.
- Cool-season option: NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue or Elfin Tall Fescue — handles heat and drought better than ryegrass.
- Warm-season option: NZLA Kikuyu for drought tolerance, wear tolerance, low input through summer. NZLA Couch for premium fine-textured finish.

**Wellington, central NI, lower South Island coast.**
Mild temperate, cool damp winters. Ryegrass thrives.
- NZLA Perennial Rye or Dark Perennial Rye for general use.
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass for high-traffic family lawns.
- Midnight Dark Rye for premium dark colour.

**Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, Marlborough.**
Hot dry summers, cool winters.
- NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue or Elfin for drought tolerance with reliable colour.
- Ryegrass also works with consistent irrigation-general.

**Otago, Southland, West Coast.**
Cool summers, cold winters, West Coast wet.
- NZLA Perennial Rye or Dark Perennial Rye thrives.
- Fine Fescue for shaded sites.

**By use:**

Heavy-traffic family lawn: NZLA RSR Ryegrass. The stoloniferous habit spreads laterally and actively repairs wear damage — standard bunch-type ryegrass can't do this. Also suppresses poa annua through dense canopy coverage.

Premium showcase: Midnight Dark Rye (MDR) for the darkest colour and finest texture in the range. Or NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue for premium drought-tolerant aesthetics.

Sports use: NZLA RSR Ryegrass for wear recovery. Tall fescue blend on dry sites.

Heavy shade: NZLA Fine Fescue — most shade-tolerant cool-season option. Handles 3 to 4 hours direct sun plus filtered light. Lower fertility requirement, fine texture, not for heavy traffic.

Mixed property with sunny and shaded areas: 70% perennial rye + 30% Fine Fescue blend covers both.

For seeding-and-over-seeding existing lawns: match the dominant species present, or accept a gradual shift over 2 to 3 seasons as overseeded cultivars establish.

## Low maintenance lawns

A real grass lawn doesn't have to be high effort. Four products cut most of the work out:

**NZLA PGR (trinexapac):** reduces vertical growth by 30 to 50% for 4 to 6 weeks per application. Two applications a year (late spring, late summer) cuts mowing frequency by a third to a half through the main growth period. Biggest single time-saver in the range.

**NZLA All Seasons slow-release granular:** 8 to 10 week release. Three applications a year covers the full fertility programme.

**NZLA Wetter 3W:** 12-week residual wetting agent. One application in late spring covers summer dry patch.

**Species selection:** NZLA Kikuyu in upper NI runs on minimal input through summer. Established kikuyu lawns are drought-tolerant and wear-tolerant with far less intervention than cool-season turf. Tall fescue (Ultimate or Elfin) handles drought without irrigation-general in the right climate.

A low-maintenance programme using these tools needs roughly 7 to 10 product applications across a full year — each one a short job. That's a well-maintained lawn without weekly intervention.

## Real grass vs artificial turf

For almost every home lawn situation, real grass comes out ahead.

Cost: artificial turf $80 to $200+ per m² installed, replacement every 10 to 15 years. A seeded NZLA lawn $3 to $5 per m². Real grass wins on cost across any realistic time horizon.

Heat: artificial turf reaches 60 to 80°C in direct summer sun — burns bare feet, heats nearby buildings. Real grass cools the surrounding air by 5 to 10°C.

Pets: dog urine on artificial turf doesn't drain or break down biologically. Smell builds over years. Real lawn with RSR Ryegrass handles wear well and urine spots reseed in autumn.

Environment: artificial turf sheds microplastics into stormwater, sequesters no carbon, supports no soil biology. Real lawn processes rainwater, supports worms and insects, and cools the property.

People choose artificial turf when they've had bad results with real grass — but that's usually a species or programme problem, not a real-grass problem. The right species choice plus a sensible fertility programme delivers a lawn that outperforms artificial on every measure.

Artificial turf has a narrow place for genuinely unsuitable spots: deep shade where no grass grows, small awkward courtyards without soil. For a typical home lawn, real grass is the right answer.

## Clover in the lawn

Better to control it. The argument for leaving clover (that it fixes nitrogen) doesn't hold up in practice on a home lawn.

What clover actually does: forms distinct patches that grow and look different to the surrounding grass, flowers attract bees (stings, problems for bare feet and children), goes slippery when wet, recovers from wear differently so traffic patterns show up clearly, and competes with grass for space over time.

Control: NZLA BWC at label rate handles white clover well. Apply spring or autumn when clover is actively growing. Follow up with All Seasons granular to thicken the lawn into the gaps.

Steady fertility from the NZLA granular range delivers consistent, even nitrogen across the whole lawn without the patchy fixation you get from isolated clover. A dense well-fed lawn is also less likely to be reinvaded by clover after control.
