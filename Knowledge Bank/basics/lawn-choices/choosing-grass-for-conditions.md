# How do I choose the right NZLA grass for my conditions?

Match the species to what your lawn actually deals with - climate, sun, traffic, soil, and what you want it to look like. The NZLA range covers every NZ situation.

By region:

Auckland, Northland, Bay of Plenty, Waikato (upper NI):
- Warm humid summers, mild winters.
- Cool-season option: tall fescue blend (NZLA Ultimate or Elfin) handles heat better than ryegrass.
- Warm-season option: NZLA Kikuyu (drought-tolerant, wear-tolerant, low-input through summer) or NZLA Couch (finer texture, better for premium aesthetic).

Wellington, central NI, lower South Island coast:
- Mild temperate climate, cool damp winters.
- NZLA Perennial Rye or DPR for general use.
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass for high-wear family lawns.
- Midnight Dark Ryegrass for premium colour.

Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, Marlborough:
- Hot dry summers, cool winters.
- NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue or Elfin Tall Fescue for drought tolerance.
- Or ryegrass blend with reliable irrigation.

Otago, Southland, West Coast:
- Cold winters, mild summers (West Coast wet).
- Cool-season ryegrass (NZLA Perennial Rye, DPR) thrives.
- Fine fescue in shaded sites.

By use:

High-traffic family lawn with kids and dogs:
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing) is the standout choice.
- Recovers from wear faster than standard ryegrass.
- Suppresses annual poa through dense growth.

Premium showcase lawn (aesthetics first):
- Midnight Dark Ryegrass (MDR) for the darkest colour and finest texture.
- Or NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue for premium drought-tolerant choice.

Sports use (rugby, soccer, cricket):
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass for wear recovery.
- Tall fescue blend on dry sites.

By condition:

Heavy shade:
- NZLA Fine Fescue is the most shade-tolerant cool-season option in the range.
- Handles 3 to 4 hours direct sun plus filtered light.

Drought-prone with no irrigation:
- Tall fescue (Ultimate or Elfin) for cool-season regions.
- NZLA Kikuyu or Couch for upper NI.

New build over disturbed soil:
- Start with NZLA Starter granular plus your chosen species.
- Tall fescue blend builds deeper roots in difficult soils.

Mixed property (sunny areas plus shaded patches):
- 70% NZLA Perennial Rye or DPR + 30% Fine Fescue blend covers both situations.

For overseeding existing lawn:
- Match the species to what's already there (or accept the gradual species shift over multiple seasons).
- DPR or MDR overseed gradually shifts the lawn to darker colour over 2 to 3 seasons.
- RSR Ryegrass overseed gradually adds self-repair capability.

Sowing rates and timing covered in seed-basics. NZLA Starter granular at establishment is standard for all species.

If you're unsure which species suits your situation, post conditions and goals on the NZLA app forum and the team can point you to the right choice.
