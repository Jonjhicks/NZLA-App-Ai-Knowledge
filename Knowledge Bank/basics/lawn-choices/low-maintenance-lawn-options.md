# Can I have a low-maintenance lawn?

Yes. The right NZLA species choice plus a small set of products cuts most of the work out of lawn care without giving up appearance.

The big four levers for less work:

NZLA PGR (trinexapac):
- Reduces vertical growth by 30 to 50% for 4 to 6 weeks per application.
- Cuts mowing frequency by a third to a half through summer.
- Two applications a year (late spring, late summer) covers the high-growth months.
- Single biggest time-saver available.

NZLA All Seasons (granular slow-release):
- 8 to 10 week release per application.
- Three applications a year covers the whole fertility programme.
- Replaces the need for monthly feeding.

NZLA Wetter 3W:
- 12-week residual wetting agent.
- One application in late spring covers the worst of summer dry patch.
- Replaces monthly wetter applications and reduces irrigation needs.

Species choice that suits your conditions:

For lifestyle blocks and rural-fringe lawns with mixed conditions:
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass for self-repair from wear. The stoloniferous habit fills in damaged areas without needing reseed.
- Or a tall fescue blend (NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue) for drought tolerance.

For upper NI warm regions with dry summers:
- NZLA Kikuyu thrives with much less input than cool-season turf in the heat. Wear-tolerant, drought-tolerant, established kikuyu lawns can run on minimal fertiliser through summer.

For dry sites where you don't want to irrigate:
- Tall fescue blend (NZLA Ultimate or Elfin) handles drought through deep roots. Lawn browns off in worst summer weeks but recovers in autumn.

For shaded sites where you don't want to keep reseeding thinning patches:
- NZLA Fine Fescue tolerates lower light, lower fertility, lower maintenance.

The programme that delivers a low-maintenance lawn:
- Sow or overseed with NZLA species suited to your conditions.
- NZLA All Seasons three times a year (March, September, November).
- NZLA PGR in November and February.
- NZLA Wetter 3W in November.
- BWC herbicide once a year if weeds creep in.
- Mow with a sharp blade at 40 to 50 mm.

That's seven product applications a year on a typical home lawn for a lawn that looks well-maintained without the weekly mowing and constant attention. NZLA PGR is the single biggest game-changer for time savings.

For genuinely lower input than that:
- Accept some weed presence (BWC once a year handles the worst).
- Skip the wetter agent if your soil isn't prone to hydrophobic conditions.
- Pick a more drought-tolerant species so irrigation isn't critical.

Real-grass low-maintenance is achievable. Artificial turf isn't the answer - it has its own time cost (cleaning, infill maintenance, replacement) and the heat, smell, and aesthetic compromises that come with plastic.
