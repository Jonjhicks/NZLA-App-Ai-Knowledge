# Is artificial turf better than a real grass lawn?

For almost every home lawn situation, real grass wins. Customers sometimes look at artificial turf because they've been told a real lawn is too much work for their conditions - but that's usually a species and programme problem, not a real-grass problem.

The match-up:

Cost:
- Artificial turf $80 to $200+ per m² installed. Replacement every 10 to 15 years adds up.
- A new NZLA grass seed lawn $3 to $5 per m² delivered.
- Real grass wins on cost across any sensible time horizon.

Heat:
- Artificial turf hits 60 to 80 °C in direct summer sun. Burns bare feet. Heats nearby buildings.
- Real grass cools the surrounding air by 5 to 10 °C.

Pets:
- Dog urine on artificial turf doesn't drain or break down biologically. Smell builds up over years.
- Real lawn with NZLA RSR Ryegrass handles wear well. Urine spots reseed in autumn.

Environment:
- Artificial turf is plastic. Microplastic shedding into stormwater, no carbon sequestration, no habitat for insects or worms.
- Real lawn supports soil ecology, cools the property, processes rainwater.

The reason people sometimes give up on real grass is usually the wrong species for their conditions or a thin programme. Both are fixable:

For high-traffic family lawns where wear is the issue:
- NZLA RSR Ryegrass (self-repairing stoloniferous cultivar) handles wear and fills back in faster than standard ryegrass.

For shaded areas where standard ryegrass thins out:
- NZLA Fine Fescue handles 3 to 4 hours direct sun plus filtered light.

For hot dry sites where ryegrass struggles in summer:
- NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue or Elfin Tall Fescue for cool-season drought tolerance.
- NZLA Kikuyu or NZLA Couch for warm-season options in upper NI.

For premium aesthetic lawns:
- NZLA Midnight Dark Ryegrass or NZLA Dark Perennial Rye for darker colour.

With the right species and a sensible NZLA fertility programme (granular base three times a year plus occasional foliar work), most NZ home properties get a strong-looking real lawn that beats artificial turf on every measure including cost.

Artificial turf has a narrow place for unusable spots (deep shade where no grass grows, small awkward courtyards), but for a typical home lawn the answer is real grass with the right NZLA species choice.
