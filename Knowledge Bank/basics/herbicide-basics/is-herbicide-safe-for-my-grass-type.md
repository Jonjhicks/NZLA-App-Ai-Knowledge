# How do I know if a herbicide is safe for my grass type?

Read the label specifically for which grass species are listed as safe.

Selective herbicides are safe on specific species and damaging or lethal on others. Same product, opposite outcomes depending on the grass in your lawn. The label tells you which.

Key NZLA selectives and their grass species restrictions:

**NZLA BWC (broadleaf control)** — safe on established cool-season turf (perennial ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop, fine fescue). Always check label for kikuyu/couch use.

**NZLA Gold (heavy broadleaf)** — same general restrictions as BWC.

**NZLA Halo (selective grass kill)** — registered for fine fescue specifically with a hard 15 mL/100m² rate cap. **WILL KILL perennial ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop, kikuyu, couch.** This catches people out because they assume "selective" means safe for all turf.

**NZLA Etho (poa control)** — registered for perennial ryegrass and tall fescue. **DO NOT use on fine fescue.** Will damage tall fescue if not actively growing.

**NZLA Blockade (pre-em)** — NZ label restricts to warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch). Off-label on cool-season here.

**NZLA Meso (broadleaf and grass weeds)** — cool-season turf only. Damages warm-season grasses (kikuyu, couch).

**NZLA Sedge Pro (sedge control)** — broad turf safety on established lawns.

How to be sure:

Step 1: Identify what grass species you actually have. If you don't know, find out before spraying. A misidentified grass plus a selective herbicide can take out a whole lawn.

Step 2: Read the herbicide label for "Registered grass species" or equivalent.

Step 3: Look for any rate caps specific to your grass species.

Step 4: If unsure, spot-test on a small inconspicuous area first and wait 2 to 3 weeks for damage to show.

When in doubt, ask in the NZLA forum or via the website contact form before spraying. Killing a lawn with the wrong herbicide is recoverable but expensive.
