# How much fertiliser do I put in my knapsack sprayer for 100m²?

Depends on the product and the rate, but here is how the maths actually works.

Foliar rates are usually given as millilitres of product per 100m² of lawn. For NZLA Charger that is 200 to 500 mL per 100m². For Liquid Boost, 200 to 400 mL per 100m². For Iron+, 200 to 400 mL per 100m².

The water you put it in is separate. Most foliars want 4 to 15 litres of water per 100m² for good coverage. So if you are spraying 100m² with Charger at 250 mL, you would mix 250 mL of product into roughly 5 to 10 litres of water in the sprayer, then walk it out evenly across the 100m².

For a typical 15 L knapsack, that is enough to cover 100 to 300m² depending on the product rate and how heavy you are walking the spray on.

Practical tip — measure the lawn properly once. Pacing it out under-estimates by 20 or 30 percent in most cases. Once you know the area, the product and water maths is straight off the label.

Always add the product to roughly two-thirds full of water, agitate, then top up to the final volume.
