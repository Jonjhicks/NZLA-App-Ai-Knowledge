# What does kg/ha mean and how do I convert it?

kg/ha means kilograms per hectare. A hectare is 10,000m² (a square 100m by 100m, or roughly 2.5 acres in the old imperial units).

For home lawn use, convert kg/ha to kg per 100m² by dividing by 100.

Example: NZLA All Seasons is rated at 200 kg/ha. Divide by 100 = 2 kg per 100m². For a 350m² lawn: 350 ÷ 100 = 3.5, then 3.5 × 2 kg = 7 kg of All Seasons total.

Same calculation for liquids in L/ha. Divide by 100 to get L per 100m². NZLA Blockade at 2.3 L/ha = 23 mL per 100m².

Why labels use kg/ha. Most professional turf and agricultural product labels were developed for commercial sports fields, farms or sports turf where hectares are the standard unit. The home lawn industry uses the same labels.

A useful reference for visualising: a typical small home lawn (urban backyard) is 100 to 250m². A larger suburban lawn is 300 to 600m². A lifestyle block lawn might be 1,000 to 5,000m². Sports fields are usually a couple of thousand square metres each.

If you're new to lawn product calculations, do the conversion once on paper, write down the result for your lawn area, and reference that note rather than recalculating every time.
