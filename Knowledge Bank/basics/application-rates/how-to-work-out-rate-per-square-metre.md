# How do I work out application rate per square metre?

Most NZLA product labels give rates in millilitres per 100m² (foliar liquids) or kilograms per 100m² (granular). Some labels also include rates per hectare or per litre of water.

Per 100m² rates are the easiest for home users. Measure your lawn area in square metres, divide by 100, and multiply by the label rate.

Worked example. For NZLA Charger at 250 mL per 100m², on a 350m² lawn: 350 ÷ 100 = 3.5, then 3.5 × 250 = 875 mL of Charger total.

The water you add to the sprayer is separate. Most foliars need 4 to 15 litres of water per 100m² for good coverage. For the same example: 3.5 × 8 L = 28 L of water total, mixed across multiple sprayer tanks.

Per litre rates (for fungicides like Fungus Pro) need a different calculation. The label says "10 mL per L of water, 1 L spray per 5m²." That means you mix 10 mL of product into 1 L of water, and spray that 1 L over 5m². For 100m² you'd use 20 L of water with 200 mL of product.

Measure your lawn properly once and write the area down. Pacing it out under-estimates by 20 to 30 percent in most cases. Once you know the area, every label calculation is straight maths.
