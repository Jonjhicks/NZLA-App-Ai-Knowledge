# What's Wrong With My Lawn?

Before reaching for a product, work out what you're actually dealing with. Treating  with pesticide, or  with fungicide, achieves nothing. The four main causes of lawn problems are environmental/physical damage, and nutrition. They look similar from a distance and respond to completely different treatments.

The NZLA blog has a guide to distinguishing fungal  from other causes: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/lawn--nz-is-it-really-fungus-or-something-else/

---

## Step 1 — Describe the problem precisely

Start here. Don't just say "my lawn looks bad." Get specific.

**Where is it?** Patches in shade tell a different story to patches in full sun. All over the lawn is different to edges only. Near tree roots, near the fence, in the middle — each narrows the possibilities.

**What shape?** Circular or near-circular patches suggest  or pest. Irregular shapes suggest environmental (drought, waterlogging, chemical). Stripy patterns (following mowing lines) suggest mowing-related. Random scattered patches suggest  or .

**How fast did it appear?** Overnight or within 24 hours: almost always Fusarium patch in cool wet conditions, or acute chemical damage. Over days to weeks:  progressing, or  building. Over months: nutrition, core-aeration, or slow environmental decline.

**What colour?** Yellow, pale, or bleached: often nutrition deficiency, drought, or some diseases. Brown and dead: advanced or physical/chemical burn. Yellow ring with green centre: Fairy ring or yellow patch . Brown tips on blades only: blunt mower blades.

**Can you pull the turf up?** Roots intact — not pest damage. Turf lifts like carpet with no root resistance — grass grub or black beetle. Roots present but blackened and rotted — root  (take-all patch, pythium).

## Step 2 — Apply the decision tree

### Problem: circular patches, any size, with defined edges

**Cool season (autumn, winter, spring), appeared overnight, water-soaked edge:** Fusarium patch. Apply NZLA Fungus Pro or Azoxy. See fusarium-patch article.

**Warm season (December–March), dark border, smoke ring in morning humidity:** Brown patch. Apply NZLA Azoxy. See brown-patch article.

**Small patches 20–80 mm, bleached straw colour, active in summer:** Dollar spot. Apply NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro. See dollar-spot article.

**Circular patches with dark green outer ring, grass inside normal or elevated, no obvious dead centre:** Fairy ring. See fairy-ring article.

**Yellow to pale rings, green centre, cool shoulder seasons:** Yellow patch. Azoxy curative. See yellow-patch article.

**Patches in shade with white powdery coating on blades:** Powdery mildew. NZLA Fungus Pro. See powdery-mildew article.

---

### Problem: yellow or pale lawn, no clear patch pattern

**All over, slow decline, especially winter or after long dry period:** Low fertility. Apply NZLA Charger + Iron+ as foliar. Base feed with All Seasons.

**All over, fast yellowing after fertiliser application:** Fertiliser burn. Water heavily immediately. See fertiliser-burn article.

**Yellow with reddish or orange tinge on blade tips, dusty coating:** Rust . Improves with nutrition. Iron+ and Charger as foliar. NZLA Fungus Pro if severe. See rust article.

**Pale, lacking colour, reasonable growth:** Iron deficiency likely. NZLA Iron+ at 200–400 mL/100m².

**Yellowing in patches under trees, heavy shade:** Shade stress plus possible powdery mildew. See shade article.

---

### Problem: patches of dead or dying turf, lawn feels spongy or loose

**Turf pulls up easily with no root resistance, C-shaped white grubs visible in soil:** Grass grubs. NZLA Grub+. See grass-grubs article.

**Turf looks chewed or stripped overnight, moth activity at dusk, upper North Island Nov–Apr:** Fall armyworm. NZLA Grub+ evening application. See fall-armyworm article.

**Roots blackened and rotted when pulled up, returns same spots year after year, browntop species:** Take-all patch. See take-all-patch article.

**Couch or kikuyu lawn, patches not greening up in spring, roots blackened:** Spring dead spot. NZLA Posterity preventative next autumn. See spring-dead-spot article.

---

### Problem: patches following a clear pattern

**Stripes following mowing lines, consistent width:** Mowing issue — scalping, uneven deck, or mower tracking. Check mowing height and blade evenness.

**Dead patches exactly following a spray path:** Herbicide damage. Check what was sprayed, whether the correct product was used for the grass species. See individual herbicide articles.

**Circular dead patches near trees, with weed/mushroom activity:** Fairy ring. See fairy-ring article.

**Patches where dog spends time, circular burn with green outer ring:** Dog urine nitrogen burn. See dog-urine article.

---

### Problem:  taking over

**Yellow flowers, deeply lobed leaves, puffball seed heads:** Dandelion. NZLA BWC or Gold.

**Flat rosettes, hairy leaves, multiple small flowers per stem:** Catsear (false dandelion). NZLA BWC + Sticker.

**Clover (white or red flowers, three-leaf):** NZLA BWC. Also indicates low fertility — increase feeding.

**Light green paler than surrounding grass, seed heads in cool months:** Poa annua. NZLA Etho (ryegrass/tall fescue only). Blockade as pre-emergent (warm-season turf label).

**Finger-like seed heads in summer, wide spreading low habit:** Summer grass/crabgrass. NZLA Blockade as pre-emergent before germination.

**Small prickly rosettes, low to ground:** Onehunga weed. NZLA BWC. Timing is critical — treat in autumn before seed sets.

**Creeping oxalis (small heart-shaped leaves, yellow flowers):** Persistent. NZLA Gold for best results. Multiple treatments needed. See oxalis article.

---

### Problem: lawn thin and patchy overall, no specific  pattern

**Thin, compacted, poor drainage-and-soil-moisture:** Aeration needed. NZLA Aerate + Penetrate to improve water movement. Core aerate annually.

**Thin in shade:** Shade stress. Increase mowing height, reduce fertility slightly, consider NZLA Fine Fescue oversowing.

**Thin after summer, generally worn:** Standard autumn renovation — aerate, top dress, overseed with NZLA Starter and grass seed.

**Thin overall, slow recovery after mowing, all year:** Low fertility, possibly also low pH. Soil test. Start consistent All Seasons programme.

## Step 3 — When you're still not sure

Take photos: a wide shot of the whole lawn and a close-up of the worst-affected area. The close-up should include the blade tips, the crown at soil level, and ideally the root system (pull up a small piece).

Post in the NZLA Forum in the app. Jon and other experienced users can identify most problems from a good photo set within a day. This is faster and more reliable than guessing and buying the wrong product.

The app forum is at newzealandlawnaddicts.com — download the NZLA app from the App Store or Google Play.
