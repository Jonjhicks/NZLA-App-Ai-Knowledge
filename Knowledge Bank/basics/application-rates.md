# Application Rates

## How to work out how much product to use

Most NZLA product labels give rates in millilitres per 100m² (foliar liquids) or kilograms per 100m² (granular). Some labels also include rates per hectare or per litre of water.

Per 100m² rates are the most practical. Measure the lawn area in square metres, divide by 100, and multiply by the label rate.

**Worked example.** NZLA Charger at 250 mL per 100m², on a 350m² lawn:

350 ÷ 100 = 3.5 units  
3.5 × 250 = 875 mL of Charger total

The water volume is separate — most foliars need 4 to 15 litres of water per 100m² for good coverage. For the same lawn at 8 L water per 100m²: 3.5 × 8 = 28 L total water. That 28 L goes across multiple sprayer tanks, with the product split proportionally between them.

For products labelled per litre of water (some fungicides like Fungus Pro), the calculation runs differently. If the label says "10 mL per L of water, 1 L spray per 5m²," you mix 10 mL into 1 L of water and apply that 1 L over 5m². For 100m², that's 20 L of water with 200 mL of product.

Measure the lawn properly once and write it down. Pacing under-estimates by 20 to 30 percent in most cases. Once the area is confirmed, every calculation is straight arithmetic.

## Converting kg/ha to home lawn rates

kg/ha means kilograms per hectare. One hectare is 10,000m². To convert to kg per 100m², divide by 100.

**Example.** All Seasons at 200 kg/ha: 200 ÷ 100 = 2 kg per 100m². For a 350m² lawn: 3.5 × 2 = 7 kg total.

The same conversion works for liquids in L/ha. NZLA Blockade at 2.3 L/ha = 23 mL per 100m².

Why labels use kg/ha: most commercial product labels were developed for sports fields and agriculture where hectares are the standard unit. The same labels apply to home lawns.

Reference points for context: typical suburban backyard is 100 to 250m². A larger suburban section is 300 to 600m². A lifestyle block lawn might be 1,000 to 5,000m². Sports fields are typically a few thousand square metres each.

## Knapsack calculations

Foliar rates are millilitres of product per 100m². The water is separate. A typical 15 L knapsack sprayer covers 100 to 300m² depending on the product rate and walking speed.

**Worked example.** Charger at 250 mL per 100m², spraying a 200m² lawn with a sprayer calibrated to apply 8 L per 100m²:

Product: 2 × 250 = 500 mL of Charger  
Water: 2 × 8 = 16 L  
Total: 500 mL product in 16 L water (just over one tank)

The product always goes into two-thirds of the water first, agitate, then top up to the final volume. Never add product to an empty dry tank.

Calibrate the sprayer to know its actual output rate (see the sprayer calibration article). Applying at the wrong water volume doesn't change the product rate, but it does affect coverage uniformity.
