# Annual Programme — North Island Cool-Season Lawn

This programme covers ryegrass, tall fescue, and blended cool-season lawns across the North Island (Auckland, Waikato, Bay of Plenty, Northland, Hawke's Bay, Manawatu, Wellington region).

Adjust timing based on your specific location — Auckland runs 2 to 3 weeks ahead of Wellington for soil temperatures.

---

## January — peak summer

**Priority:** keep the lawn alive in heat and  pressure.

Mow at 45 to 50 mm. Don't drop height in heat — the leaf area is needed.

**Fertiliser:** hold off heavy nitrogen if temperatures are above 25°C and the lawn is heat-stressed. NZLA Iron+ at 200 mL/100m² maintains colour without pushing growth. If the lawn is actively growing and irrigated, Charger at 200 mL/100m² is fine.

**Disease:** brown patch and dollar spot pressure. Apply NZLA Azoxy (75 mL/100m²) preventatively on any lawn with a  history, or at first sign of circular patches with dark borders. Don't wait until the outbreak is established.

**Irrigation:** deep watering twice weekly. Wetter 3W or Penetrate if dry patch is developing (water beading, dry spots persisting after irrigation-general).

**Pests:** Fall armyworm (upper NI) — watch for ragged overnight damage, moth activity at dusk. NZLA Grub+ evening application.

---

## February — still summer, renovation prep begins

The soil is still warm but autumn is approaching. The most important month for thinking ahead.

**Fertiliser:** same as January — Iron+ and moderate Charger. Still too hot for heavy nitrogen push.

**Renovation prep:** Start planning the autumn renovation. Decide which areas need seeding-and-over-seeding, whether scarifiers is needed, order seed.

**Grub+:** Apply NZLA Grub+ in late September/early October before egg hatch (first application), then again in March (second application, six months later). Water in 6 mm immediately after each application.

---

## March — most important month of the year

The ground is still warm (soil temps 16 to 20°C) but air is cooling. Ideal conditions for growth and renovation. Miss March and you've lost the best window.

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m². The autumn push — biggest granular application of the year.

**Foliar:** Liquid Boost at 200 mL/100m² 10 days after granular for a combined push.

**Renovation:** Scarify if thatch is excessive. Core aerate (2 passes). Topdress. Oversow thin or bare areas with NZLA Perennial Rye or appropriate species at 25 to 30 g/m² (renovation rate). Apply NZLA Starter at 1.5 kg/100m² before seeding.

**Herbicide:** NZLA Etho now begins the poa annua control programme — water in the first application for pre-emergent effect against germinating poa.

**Disease:** Fusarium pressure begins as nights cool. Watch for small circular patches with water-soaked edges after wet cool nights. NZLA Fungus Pro or Azoxy at first sign.

---

## April — establishment window management

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons at 2.5 to 3 kg/100m². Last solid granular of the season.

**Seeding:** Last good window for ryegrass seeding-and-over-seeding before soil temps drop below reliable germination threshold (14°C). Soil temp check before sowing — at or above 14°C, proceed.

**Pre-emergent:** NZLA Blockade if managing annual grass in warm-season turf (kikuyu component). For cool-season only lawns, Etho second application for poa control.

**Weed control:** NZLA BWC at 50 mL/100m² for broadleaf  while still actively growing.

**Disease:** Fusarium preventative — NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro on any lawn with previous  history. The investment here prevents a damaging winter outbreak.

---

## May — final prep before winter

**Fertiliser:** Optional final application of All Seasons at 2 kg/100m² on northern NI lawns (Auckland, Northland) where active growth continues. Central and lower NI: skip or apply minimal foliar only.

**Foliars begin:** NZLA Charger every 2 weeks from now through winter. NZLA Liquid N for any growth stimulus needed during mild winter spells (check: do not apply above 26°C — not an issue in May).

**Iron+:** Every 2 to 3 weeks. Winter colour comes from iron, not nitrogen.

**Mowing:** Reduce frequency as growth slows. Stop scalping height adjustments — hold at the maintenance height.

---

## June, July — winter minimum

**Fertiliser:** Charger every 2 weeks + Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks. No granular. Soil temps too low for meaningful granular response.

**Disease:** Fusarium most active in cold wet conditions. NZLA Fungus Pro curative at 800 mL/100m² if active patches develop. Preventative Azoxy if conditions are consistently cool and wet.

**Mowing:** Every 2 to 3 weeks or as growth dictates. Keep blades sharp — blunt blades on a winter lawn cause tip dieback that compounds with .

**Equipment:** Service mower, check spreader, clean sprayer. Order spring products now.

---

## August — pre-spring

Soil temperatures beginning to lift. The lawn is preparing to grow.

**Pre-emergent:** Spring window opens for Blockade on warm-season turf components (kikuyu). Check soil temps at 50 mm — target application when approaching 10 to 12°C and rising.

**Wetting agents:** NZLA Wetter 3W spring application. Apply now while soil still has some moisture from winter rain — better penetration than waiting until soil is dry.

**Continue foliars:** Charger + Iron+.

---

## September — spring begins

The year restarts. Soil temps rising, growth accelerating.

**Granular restart:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m². First granular of the season — push the spring flush.

**Foliars:** Continue Charger. Add Liquid Boost 10 days after granular for a combined spring push.

**Renovation:** Any spring renovation work — aeration, seeding-and-over-seeding (secondary autumn is better, but spring works).

**Weed control:** BWC for broadleaf . Etho for poa annua if not treated in autumn.

**Disease:** Disease pressure easing. Continue Azoxy or Fungus Pro if fusarium has been active through winter.

---

## October — full spring

**Fertiliser:** All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks now through summer.

**PGR:** If running a premium programme, NZLA PGR (trinexapac) application as soil temps stabilise above 14°C and growth is consistent. GDD tracking in the NZLA app for reapplication timing.

**Disease:** Shift  management to summer mode — watch for dollar spot and brown patch rather than fusarium.

**Mowing:** Frequency increasing. Ensure 1/3 rule is maintained — raise cutting frequency before raising the blade.

---

## November, December — early summer transition

Return to January programme. Shift focus from growth to heat management. Pre-event Liquid Boost application 7 to 10 days before any event or inspection.
