# How do I prevent fungal issues in my lawn?

Fungal pressure on lawns is mostly about conditions — moisture, fertility balance, airflow, plant health. Get those right and you avoid most disease without ever spraying a fungicide.

Top of the list: reduce leaf moisture duration.

Knock the morning dew off — drag a hose, rope or brush across the lawn each morning. The dew is gone in minutes and the leaf dries hours earlier than it would otherwise. Canopy moisture duration is the single biggest driver of fungal disease pressure.

Water in the morning, not evening. Evening watering extends the overnight wet window. Morning watering lets the lawn dry through the day.

Improve airflow where you can. Trim hedges or branches that block air movement across the lawn.

Sort out drainage issues. NZLA Penetrate moves surface moisture down through the profile, drying the canopy faster after rain.

Second priority: balanced fertility, no spikes.

High nitrogen feeds disease — particularly Fusarium and brown patch. Use balanced slow-release feeds (All Seasons, All Seasons) rather than fast-release nitrogen spikes. Through autumn into winter, ease off the nitrogen further.

Iron and potassium harden the plant against disease. NZLA Iron+ as a foliar through the cool months. All Seasons' 16% potassium does the same job through the granular base.

Third priority: cultural basics.

Sharp mower blades — torn leaf tips are infection entry points.

Right mowing height — scalping stresses the plant and opens it to disease.

Healthy seed rate and species choice — dense well-bred turf resists disease better than thin patchy lawn.

Fungicide as backup, not the foundation. NZLA Fungus Pro as a protectant through the high-pressure months (autumn into winter). NZLA Azoxy as systemic knockdown if disease shows up despite prevention.

The lawns that get hit are usually the ones doing the opposite — fast-release fertiliser, evening watering, no dew removal, blunt blades, scalped mowing height.
