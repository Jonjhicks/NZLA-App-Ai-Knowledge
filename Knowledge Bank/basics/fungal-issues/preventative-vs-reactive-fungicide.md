# Should I use fungicide as a preventative or only when I see disease?

Depends on the lawn's value and the disease pressure history.

For most home lawns, reactive treatment is fine. Wait until you see disease, then treat aggressively with both cultural fixes (dew removal, fertility adjustment, airflow) and chemistry (Fungus Pro plus Azoxy in rotation). The cost of routine preventative fungicide on a home lawn that may never see disease isn't justified for most users.

For premium or high-pressure lawns, preventative is the better approach. The triggers:

**The lawn has had disease in the same conditions before.** If you got Fusarium last autumn, the spore load is still in the soil and conditions for re-infection are similar. Preventative Fungus Pro through autumn into winter is cheaper and more effective than chasing disease when it appears.

**The lawn is high-value or high-visibility.** Showcase lawns, sports turf, commercial properties where disease damage carries reputation cost.

**Conditions favour disease.** Heading into known high-pressure periods (autumn-winter for Fusarium, summer for brown patch on warm-season turf), preventative makes sense even on a clean lawn.

**Cultural fixes aren't enough on their own.** If the lawn has structural issues (shade, drainage, compaction) that can't be fully resolved, fungicide carries the protection cultural can't.

The standard preventative pattern:

Fungus Pro (thiram contact) on a routine 4-week pattern through the high-pressure season (every 2 weeks in high humidity). Multi-site chemistry with essentially no resistance risk.

Azoxy (systemic) only when active disease shows up, with strict 2 consecutive applications maximum before rotating to Fungus Pro.

Always rotate the two if both are in use. Single-site systemic chemistry without rotation invites resistance.

For most home lawns, the cost-benefit of preventative isn't there. Reactive treatment with strong cultural control is plenty.
