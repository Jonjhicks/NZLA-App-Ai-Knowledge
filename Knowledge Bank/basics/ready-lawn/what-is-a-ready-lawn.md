# What is a ready lawn?

A pre-grown lawn delivered as rolls or slabs, ready to lay over prepared soil. Also called instant lawn or turf rolls.

The lawn is grown for 12 to 24 months at a turf farm, then cut as slabs or rolls about 30 mm thick (the grass plus a layer of root mat and soil). Delivered fresh, laid like carpet over prepared ground, and you've got an instant lawn the same day.

The visual is immediate. The establishment care is not. Ready lawn needs the same attention to soil prep, watering and early management as a seeded lawn for the first 4 to 6 weeks — keep traffic off, water consistently to keep the root mat damp, hold off heavy mowing until the roots have anchored down into the soil below.

Pros and cons versus seeded:

**Ready lawn pros:** instant result, no establishment window where the lawn looks rough, can be laid year-round (subject to weather), works on slopes where seed would wash away.

**Ready lawn cons:** much more expensive per square metre (typically 5 to 10 times the cost of seed), variety limited to what the turf farm grew, transition lines between slabs can show for months.

**Seeded pros:** cost effective for large areas, full choice of grass species and cultivars, no transition lines.

**Seeded cons:** establishment window of 6 to 12 weeks where the lawn is fragile, weather-dependent timing, more weed pressure during establishment.

Choose ready lawn for fast result on small areas (entrance lawns, small backyards). Seed for cost-effective coverage of larger areas where you can wait out the establishment window.
