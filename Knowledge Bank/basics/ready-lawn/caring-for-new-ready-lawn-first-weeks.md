# How do I care for a new ready lawn in the first few weeks?

Three priorities for the first 4 to 6 weeks: water, traffic management, and patient feeding.

**Watering (the biggest job).** The grass came off the turf farm with a thin root mat. It needs to grow new roots down into your soil before it's properly established. Until that happens, the root mat dries out fast and the lawn dies.

First 2 weeks: water twice a day in warm conditions, once a day in cool. Keep the root mat consistently moist. 5 to 10 mm of water at each application — enough to soak the root mat and the top 20 mm of soil below it.

Weeks 2 to 4: as roots establish into the soil below, back off to once a day or every other day with deeper watering.

Weeks 4 to 6: transition to twice-weekly deep watering as the lawn becomes established.

Watch the lawn. If edges or corners are wilting, increase watering. If you can lift the turf cap and it lifts easily without resistance, roots haven't yet established into the soil — keep watering consistently.

**Traffic.** Keep off the new lawn for the first 4 weeks if at all possible. Foot traffic on un-rooted turf shifts the slabs out of position and damages the root mat. Mark routes around the lawn for the first month.

If you have dogs, keep them off for the first 4 to 6 weeks. Pet urine on establishing grass kills patches.

**Feeding.** Don't overdo it early.

NZLA Starter granular gets watered in at installation by the contractor (or DIY at 1.5 kg per 100m²). That feeds the lawn through establishment.

At 4 to 5 weeks, NZLA Liquid Starter as a foliar supports the early root development. 100 to 200 mL per 100m² in 5 L water.

Transition to NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ as the maintenance base at 8 to 12 weeks once the lawn is fully established.

First mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height with a sharp blade, taking only the top third off.
