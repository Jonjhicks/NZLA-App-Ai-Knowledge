# How long after foliar fertiliser application can I mow?

Standard rule: 24 hours minimum.

The reason. Foliar fertilisers are absorbed through the leaf — the active needs to sit on the leaf surface long enough for the plant to take it up. Mowing within hours of spraying removes most of the spray with the clippings before the leaf has absorbed it, which means most of the product is wasted.

24 hours is the practical minimum that lets the leaf absorb the bulk of the spray. Specific NZLA labels:

NZLA Charger — avoid mowing for 24 hours.

NZLA Iron+ — avoid mowing for 24 hours. Iron uptake is fast (visible green-up within hours) but giving it the full day before mowing maximises the effect.

NZLA Liquid Boost — avoid mowing for 24 hours.

NZLA Liquid N — avoid mowing for 24 hours.

NZLA Liquid Starter — avoid mowing for 24 hours.

If 24 hours doesn't suit your schedule, 48 hours is better — gives the leaf more time and protects against rain washing the spray off before absorption completes.

What to do if you mowed by mistake: wait until the lawn has had a couple of mowing cycles of recovery, then reapply at a lighter rate. Not a disaster, just less effective.

When NOT to apply foliar fertiliser:

The day you plan to mow.

If rain is forecast within 2 to 4 hours of application.

On lawns under heat stress over 25°C (Iron+, Liquid N have specific temperature ceilings).

In hot midday sun — apply in cool conditions (early morning or evening) for best absorption.
