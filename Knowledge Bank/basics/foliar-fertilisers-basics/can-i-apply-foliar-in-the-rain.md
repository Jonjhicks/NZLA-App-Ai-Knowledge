# Can I apply foliar fertiliser in the rain?

No. The rain washes the spray off the leaf before the plant can absorb it.

Foliar uptake takes hours, not minutes. The leaf has to take the nutrients in through stomata and the cuticle. Rain hitting the spray within an hour or two of application washes most of it down to the soil — where you do get some root uptake, but at much lower efficiency than the foliar delivery you paid for.

The standard rule: dry foliage at application, no rain forecast for at least 2 to 4 hours after.

A few NZLA products have more specific rainfast guidance:

NZLA Iron+ — let the spray dry on the leaf (typically 30 to 60 minutes in normal conditions), then it's relatively rainfast. Heavy rain within 2 hours still reduces effectiveness.

NZLA Sticker (when used as an adjuvant) — rainfast 30 minutes on dry plants. Useful when you've got a tight weather window.

If unexpected rain happens within an hour of application, you've effectively lost the spray. Wait 2 to 3 weeks and reapply.

What about a light shower or mist? Light rain within an hour is still a problem. Heavy dew already on the leaf at application also reduces effectiveness — the spray gets diluted before it can absorb properly.

Best application windows:

Cool morning after dew has lifted but before midday heat.

Cool evening 2 to 3 hours before sunset (gives time to dry before night dew).

Avoid midday sun in summer (heat stress on the leaf), evening with rain forecast overnight, or any time when wind is high enough to cause drift.

Check the weather forecast for the 4 to 6 hours after the planned application before you mix the spray.
