# Sprayer and Spreader Calibration

## Knapsack sprayer calibration

Fill the sprayer with clean water. Mark out a known area — 10m² (2 m by 5 m) is easy to pace out.

Walk the test area at your normal application pace, spraying evenly as you go. Same nozzle, same pressure, same walking pace you'd use for a real application.

Refill the sprayer to the original level and measure how much water you used. That's your output for 10m². Multiply by 10 to get the carrier water rate per 100m².

Once you know the carrier rate, the product calculation is simple. If the sprayer applies 8 L per 100m² and the label says 250 mL of product per 100m², mix 250 mL into 8 L for each 100m² being sprayed. For 300m²: 750 mL of product in 24 L of water across three tanks.

Re-calibrate whenever you change the nozzle, adjust pressure, or change walking pace. The same procedure, a couple of minutes of setup.

Walk at a steady pace — speeding up at the end of a tank throws the rate off. Keep pressure consistent. Most knapsack sprayers, properly calibrated, are accurate to within 10 percent.

## Spreader calibration

Mark out 10m². Weigh out the amount of granular product the label specifies for that area. For All Seasons at 2 kg per 100m², that's 200 g for the 10m² test.

Set the spreader to a middle setting. Walk the test area at normal pace, covering it in two directions (north-south at half rate, east-west at half rate) for even coverage.

Check the result. Fertiliser remaining means the setting is too low. Ran out before finishing means too high. Adjust one notch and retest on a fresh patch.

Write down the correct setting for each product you use. Different prill sizes flow differently — All Seasons and Mini Prill need separate calibrations. Same walking pace every time.

Calibrate on a still day. Wind throws the spread pattern off.

## Nozzle selection

Match the nozzle to the job. Three things matter: droplet size, spray pattern, and flow rate.

**Foliar fertiliser (Charger, Iron+, Liquid Boost).** Fine to medium droplets for good leaf coverage. Flat fan nozzle (LF or LFR series) at 200 to 300 micron droplet size. 4 to 15 L water per 100m².

**Selective broadleaf herbicide (BWC, Gold).** Medium droplets, balancing leaf coverage with drift control. Air induction or medium flat fan nozzle. 8 to 15 L water per 100m² for thorough coverage.

**Pre-emergent herbicide (Blockade).** Coarser droplets — the active needs to land on the soil surface and bind into the top centimetre, not coat the leaf. Air induction nozzle at coarser droplet sizes. 6 to 10 L water per 100m².

**Contact fungicide (Fungus Pro).** Fine to medium droplets. High water volume essential — 20 L per 100m² for thorough canopy contact. Thiram only works where the spray physically lands.

**Systemic fungicide (Azoxy).** Medium droplets. Lower water volume than contact fungicides — 4 to 8 L per 100m². The active moves inside the plant after uptake.

**Total kill (glyphosate).** Coarser droplets, 4 to 8 L per 100m². Glyphosate uptake is concentration-driven and high water volume dilutes effectiveness.

**Practical notes:**

Worn nozzles increase the orifice size over time, raising flow rate by 10 to 30 percent and throwing calibration off. Replace after about 100 hours of use or whenever a nozzle gauge shows wear.

Don't mix nozzle types on the same boom — inconsistent output.

For backpack sprayers, standard 02 or 03 flat fan nozzles cover most home lawn applications.

Calibrate every time you change nozzle or adjust pressure. The spray rate changes with both.
