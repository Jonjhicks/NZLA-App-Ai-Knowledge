# Lawn Presentation

Getting a lawn looking its best for a specific occasion — photos, open homes, events, or just to impress. Most of the impact comes from three things: mowing technique, colour, and edges.

---

## The timeline

**4 to 6 weeks out:** Apply All Seasons granular if due. Start a Charger + Iron+ foliar programme fortnightly. Address any major weed issues with Gold —  die and disappear within 3 to 4 weeks.

**2 weeks out:** Second Charger + Iron+ application if on a fortnightly programme. Oversow any bare patches (ryegrass shows cover within 10 to 14 days in warm conditions).

**3 to 5 days out:** Apply Iron+ foliar (100 mL/100m²). Colour response shows within 48 to 72 hours. Don't apply on a day above 28°C.

**Day before (or morning of):** Mow. Edge. Blow or sweep paths, edges, and hard surfaces. Clear clippings. The final mow is the single highest-impact presentation action.

---

## Mowing for appearance

**Fresh mow always wins.** A lawn mown the morning of an open home or event looks materially better than one mown three days ago. Grass stands up, cut is clean, colour is consistent.

**Stripe it if you can.** Alternating mowing direction creates light/dark stripes. Even without a dedicated roller, mowing in parallel straight passes in opposite directions gives a noticeable stripe effect. See the lawn-striping article for technique.

**Height:** A shorter-than-normal cut (within the species' safe range — never below the minimum) looks neater in photos. Drop the height one notch for the presentation mow, then return to normal maintenance height the next mow.

**Sharp blade.** Torn leaf tips go white within 24 hours on a healthy, well-fed lawn. A sharp blade makes a visible difference. If the blade is due for sharpening, do it now.

---

## Colour

Iron is the fastest cosmetic tool available. NZLA Iron+ at 100 mL/100m² deepens colour within 48 to 72 hours without driving rapid growth (which would require another mow).

Charger adds both colour (via iron) and a slight nitrogen push — use this if the lawn looks both pale and thin.

Liquid Boost (high N) for rapid colour recovery on a genuinely depleted lawn — only if there's enough time for it to green up properly before the event (allow 5 to 7 days).

---

## Edges

Crisp edges transform how a lawn looks. An edged lawn with mediocre grass outperforms an unedged lawn with good grass, in terms of visual impression.

Edge the boundary with the driveway, path, and garden beds the day before or on the day. Use a half-moon edger or powered edger for a clean vertical cut. Blow or sweep the debris off the hard surfaces.

---

## Photography specifically

**Morning or late afternoon.** Midday sun flattens the appearance of a lawn and washes out colour. The low-angle light of early morning or late afternoon creates texture, depth, and makes stripes dramatic. Shoots done at noon in summer look flat.

**Water the lawn the day before.** A well-hydrated lawn stands up straight and reflects light evenly. A dry lawn looks stressed and limp.

**Remove distraction.** Hoses, garden tools, pet toys, any debris visible from the camera angle. The eye goes to imperfections.

**Camera angle matters.** Shooting from low, along the length of stripes, maximises their visual impact. A standard standing-height photo loses the pattern. Get low.

---

## What doesn't work in a hurry

Green spray paint and lawn dye exist. They don't look good in person and produce odd saturation in photos that experienced eyes notice. Don't bother.

Reviving a severely drought-stressed, brown lawn within a week isn't realistic. Heavy irrigation-general will green up the edges and partly recover ryegrass, but a properly dormant or dead lawn won't recover in a week. Start earlier.
