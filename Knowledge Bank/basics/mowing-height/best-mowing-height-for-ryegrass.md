# What is the best mowing height for ryegrass?

Depends on the time of year and what you want from the lawn.

**Summer:** 25 to 50 mm typical for standard perennial rye. Lift higher (40 to 50 mm) in the worst of summer to shade the soil and keep moisture in. Lower (25 to 30 mm) suits closer-mown showcase lawns under active management.

**Winter:** 40 to 60 mm. More leaf area for low-light photosynthesis, more carbohydrate storage for cold tolerance, and a tighter canopy that shades out moss.

**Premium dark cultivars** (Midnight Dark Rye, Dark Perennial Rye) can be held closer than standard rye thanks to the finer leaf and higher tiller density. 20 to 40 mm with active management for showcase use.

**RSR Ryegrass** sits well at 20 to 50 mm. The stoloniferous habit means the canopy fills in laterally rather than vertically, so it tolerates closer mowing without thinning out.

The single biggest rule across all of them: never take more than a third of the leaf off in one mow. If the lawn is 60 mm, do not drop it below 40 mm in a single cut. Bring the height down gradually over multiple mows if you want to go shorter. Scalping stresses the plant and opens it up to disease.

Sharp blade. Always.
