# Should I leave the clippings on the lawn or catch them?

Generally leave them. Mulch mowing returns the nutrients in the clippings back to the soil, which reduces your fertiliser need by 20 to 30 percent over a season.

Nitrogen is the big one. Grass clippings are roughly 4% nitrogen. Returning them feeds the lawn the same nitrogen it just took out of the soil to grow, which on a healthy regularly-mowed lawn is a meaningful chunk of the year's fertiliser requirement.

Three exceptions where catching makes sense.

**Disease.** If the lawn has Fusarium, dollar spot, red thread, rust, or any other foliar disease, catch the clippings to take the infected leaf off the lawn. Leaving infected clippings on the surface spreads the disease back through the canopy.

**Long grass after a missed cut.** If you've been a long time between mows and have a thick mat of clippings, that smothers the lawn rather than mulching into it. Catch this time, then return to mulching once you're back on a regular schedule.

**Wet or seed-heavy mowing.** If the lawn is wet enough that clippings clump, or the lawn has gone to seed (poa, browntop seed heads), catch rather than spread the mess.

Otherwise mulch and let the soil do its job. The clippings break down within days in active growing conditions and you save money on fertiliser at the same time.
