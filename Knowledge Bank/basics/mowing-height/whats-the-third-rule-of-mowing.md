# What is the third rule of mowing?

Never take more than a third of the leaf off in a single mow.

If the lawn is 60 mm and you want to bring it down to 40 mm, that's a 33% cut — at the limit but acceptable. If you want to go from 60 mm to 25 mm in one mow (a 58% cut), the lawn gets scalped and stressed badly.

Why it matters.

The third you take off is photosynthetic area. The plant produces energy by photosynthesis in the green leaf tissue. Lose a third and the plant adapts within days — the remaining leaf area is plenty to keep the plant fed. Lose more than half and the plant goes into severe energy debt — yellowing, browning, growth stalls, susceptibility to disease and pests climbs.

Practical implication for mowing frequency.

If you want to maintain the lawn at 40 mm, mow when it reaches about 60 mm (third rule taking 20 mm off back to 40 mm).

For a faster-growing lawn in spring or summer, that's every 5 to 7 days. For winter or slow growth, every 2 to 4 weeks is fine.

For a lawn maintained at 25 mm (premium cut), mow when it reaches 37 mm. Faster growth period needs every 3 to 5 days for a tight programme.

When the lawn gets ahead of you (holiday, illness, weather delay, etc.):

If the lawn is 80 mm and target is 30 mm, don't drop straight to 30. Take it to 55 mm (about a third off), wait 3 to 5 days, mow to 40 mm, wait, then to 30 mm. The HOC reset pattern brings it back without scalping stress.

Sharp blade and proper mowing frequency together are the two biggest lifts on lawn quality. The third rule is non-negotiable across both.
