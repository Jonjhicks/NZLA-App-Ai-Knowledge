
## Tank mixing order

The sequence you add products to the tank matters. Adding in the wrong order can cause compatibility failures that a jar test wouldn't predict, because in the jar test you typically add everything at once.

The standard industry sequence, adapted for typical NZLA mixes:

1. **Half fill the tank with water first** — never add concentrate to an empty or nearly empty tank.
2. **Wettable powders or dispersible granules** (if any) — these need the most agitation to go into suspension.
3. **Flowable or suspension concentrates** (like Azoxy).
4. **Emulsifiable concentrates** (EC formulations).
5. **Water-soluble liquids and chelated products** (Charger, Iron+, Liquid Boost).
6. **Adjuvants and wetting agents** (NZLA Sticker, Wetter) — add last, they can cause foaming if added too early.
7. **Top up to final volume with water.**

Agitate gently throughout. If the mix shows any signs of curdling, layering, or precipitation during adding: stop, identify the problem, don't apply.

For most NZLA combinations — say Azoxy + Charger + Wetter 3W — the practical order is water first, Azoxy, then Charger, then Wetter last. Jar test first regardless.
