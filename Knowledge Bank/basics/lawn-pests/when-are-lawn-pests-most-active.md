# What time of year are lawn pests most active?

NZ pest activity follows fairly predictable seasonal patterns. The key windows:

**Grass grub (Costelytra zealandica) — Spring through early summer (October to December).** Larvae feed on grass roots from late spring as soil warms. Most damage shows up October to December. Adults emerge as beetles in December-January, fly, mate, lay eggs for next year's generation.

**African Black Beetle (Heteronychus arator) — Spring through summer (October to February).** Similar pattern to grass grub. Larvae do the damage feeding on roots in spring. Adult beetles active flying and mating through summer.

**Argentine Stem Weevil (Listronotus bonariensis) — Spring through early summer.** Larvae feed inside grass stems and at the base. Damage tends to be subtle until cumulative.

**Porina (Wiseana spp.) — Autumn through winter.** Caterpillars build vertical burrows in soil from late summer, feed at night through autumn into winter. Heaviest damage September to November coming out of winter when grass growth is slow.

**Sod webworms — Late summer through autumn.** Small caterpillars in silk-lined surface tunnels. Damage builds through summer dry periods on stressed lawns.

**Lawn armyworm — Summer through early autumn.** Larger caterpillars feeding in groups. Fast damage when populations spike.

**Billbugs — Spring through summer.** Adults visible walking on lawn in spring; larvae feed at crown and root through summer.

For treatment planning:

NZLA Grub+ (chlorantraniliprole) covers all of these. Maximum 1 application per year — so timing matters. The spring window (October-December) catches grass grub, black beetle larvae, stem weevil and the start of caterpillar pressure. That's the standard application timing for most NZ home lawns.

For porina specifically (which peaks autumn-winter), autumn application is more effective on the active larval stage.

Monitor before treating. Many lawns have low pest pressure and don't need intervention. The square spade test in spring tells you whether grub counts are above damage threshold.
