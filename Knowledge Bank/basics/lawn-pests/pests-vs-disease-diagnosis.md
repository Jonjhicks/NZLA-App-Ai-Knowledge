# How do I tell if my lawn problem is pests or disease?

A few diagnostic checks point you in the right direction.

**The lift test for pests.** Grab a handful of grass in the affected area and pull up firmly. If the grass lifts away from the soil like a carpet — roots have been chewed through — you've got a soil-dwelling pest (grass grub, black beetle larvae, porina, billbugs). Disease patches keep their roots, so the grass won't lift.

**The cobweb test for disease.** Walk the lawn first thing in the morning while the dew is still on. Fungal mycelium shows up as white cottony webbing on the leaf surface that doesn't disappear as the dew dries. Bare droplets of dew lift as the sun gets on them. Persistent webbing = fungus.

**The pattern.** Disease patches usually have defined edges, often roughly circular, sometimes with a darker outer ring. Pest damage is more irregular — patches that follow no pattern, with bird damage extending the visible area as they hunt for grubs.

**The season.** Soil-dwelling pests peak spring through summer (grass grub October-December, black beetle similar). Caterpillars (sod webworms, armyworms, porina) peak late summer through autumn. Most fungal disease peaks in cool damp conditions (autumn-winter for Fusarium, summer for brown patch).

**The soapy water flush.** For surface-feeding caterpillars (sod webworm, armyworm, porina), pour a watering can of dish soap and water over a suspected damaged area. Caterpillars come to the surface within minutes. Diseases don't.

If the lift test is positive (lawn comes up like carpet), treat with NZLA Grub+. If mycelium is present, treat with Fungus Pro plus Azoxy.

Sometimes you have both — pests stressing the lawn, disease taking advantage. Treat both if the diagnostics show both.
