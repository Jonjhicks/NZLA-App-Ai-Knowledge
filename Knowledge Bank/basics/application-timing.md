# Application Timing

Getting the chemistry right is half the job. Applying in the wrong conditions wastes the product and sometimes damages the lawn. These rules apply across the NZLA range.

---

## Rain

**Granular fertiliser:** Don't apply to wet foliage. Granules stuck on wet leaf surface release salts directly onto the blade and cause scorch. Light drizzle is not enough — you need consistent soaking rain (at least 6 to 10 mm) to wash granules into the soil before they cause damage. Best practice: apply when foliage is dry, with rain forecast 12 to 24 hours later. Or apply dry and irrigate immediately.

Never apply granular fertiliser to waterlogged or flooded soil — it leaches past the root zone before the grass can use it.

See the NZLA blog: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/should-you-apply-slow-release-fertiliser-in-the-rain/

**Liquid fertilisers and foliars:** Apply to dry leaf. Foliar nutrients absorb through the leaf surface — if rain washes the liquid off before absorption occurs (within about 30 minutes to 1 hour), uptake is reduced. Apply when no rain is forecast for at least 1 hour.

**Herbicides:** Rain before the product is rainfast washes it off before translocation. Most contact and selective herbicides need 30 minutes to 2 hours of dry conditions after application. NZLA Sticker (organosilicone adjuvant) improves rainfastness significantly — worth using on herbicide applications when rain timing is uncertain.

**Fungicides (Azoxy, Fungus Pro):** Apply to dry foliage. Azoxy is systemic and needs time to penetrate the leaf. Fungus Pro is contact-acting — rain removes it from the surface. Aim for 2 hours of dry conditions minimum.

**Wetting agents:** The exception. NZLA Wetter, Wetter 3W, and Penetrate can be applied just before rain — this is actually ideal for wetting agents, as the rain helps move the product into the soil profile. Don't leave wetting agents sitting on hard surfaces (may stain), but applying to the lawn before forecast rain is fine.

**Pre-emergent herbicides (Blockade):** Must be activated with 6 mm of irrigation-general or rain within 7 days of application. The active needs to move into the top centimetre of soil to form the germination barrier.

---

## Wind

**Herbicides:** Don't spray in wind above about 15 km/h. Drift onto garden beds, vegetables, neighbours' plants, or water features is a real risk. Selective herbicides are selective only for grass species — they'll damage or kill broadleaf garden plants. Apply on calm mornings.

**Liquid fertilisers and fungicides:** Wind reduces even coverage. Application in still conditions produces better results. Not safety-critical, but coverage quality drops with increasing wind.

---

## Heat

**Liquid fertilisers (especially nitrogen-rich products):** Don't apply above 25 to 28°C. High nitrogen on heat-stressed grass causes scorching — the grass is already under stress and concentrated salts compound the damage. Apply in early morning or evening when temperatures are below 25°C.

**NZLA Liquid N:** Hard limit of 26°C. Ammonium-based chemistry at high temperatures can produce ammonia-related damage. Do not apply above 26°C under any circumstances.

**Herbicides:** Don't spray selective herbicides in hot conditions. Above 28°C the product moves through the plant faster than it can be metabolised safely, increasing the risk of off-target injury to the desired species. Apply in the cooler parts of the day.

**Grub+:** Apply in the evening on hot days — this also coincides with when surface-feeding larvae and armyworm are most active.

---

## Frost

**Don't apply anything to frozen grass.** Frozen cell walls are compromised and the plant can't process nutrients, herbicides, or fungicides normally. Fertiliser applied to frosted grass risks scorch as the frost lifts. Herbicides applied to frosted grass give poor results.

Apply once the frost has fully lifted and the grass is no longer frozen. Usually mid-morning on a frost morning.

**Pre-emergent herbicides:** Don't apply when soil temperatures are below 5°C. The chemistry is less active at low soil temps and germination isn't occurring anyway, so timing is wasted.

---

## Mowing before and after applications

**Herbicides:** Mow 1 to 2 days before applying selective herbicides. More leaf area = more uptake surface. Don't mow for 2 to 3 days after applying herbicide — you want maximum leaf area present for translocation. After that, wait the full label withholding period before the first mow.

**Fertiliser:** No strict mowing requirement, but mowing immediately before a granular application gives the granules a better chance of reaching the soil surface rather than sitting on dense foliage.

**Fungicides:** Apply as soon as symptoms appear — don't delay to fit a mowing schedule. If mowing is needed, mow first, then apply fungicide. Mowing after application removes treated leaf material.

**Pre-emergent:** Once applied and activated, avoid deep coring or heavy scarifiers that would break the chemical barrier in the soil.

---

## Time of day

**Foliar fertilisers and fungicides:** Early morning is ideal — cooler temperatures, stomata (leaf pores) are open and absorbing, no heat scorch risk, product dries before the day heats up.

**Herbicides:** Morning on a calm mild day. Not midday in heat, not late evening in damp conditions.

**Wetting agents:** Any time of day works. Morning preferred to allow product to move into soil before evening.

**Insecticides (Grub+, armyworm treatment):** Late afternoon or evening. Target larvae are most active and closer to the surface at this time.

---

## Re-entry intervals (people and pets)

**Liquid fertilisers (Charger, Iron+, Liquid Boost, etc.):** Not classified as hazardous. Keep off the lawn until the spray has dried — not for safety reasons, but to avoid tracking product onto hard surfaces and to let the product absorb undisturbed.

**Herbicides, fungicides, insecticides:** More regulated. General rule: keep children and pets off for 24 hours after application unless the label specifies otherwise. Read the specific label for each product — re-entry periods vary.

**NZLA Grub+:** Keep people and pets off until dry.

**NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride, DANGER classification):** Follow label strictly. High-hazard product — keep people and pets clear until fully dry and any risk of contact removed.

For specific safety data on any NZLA product, Safety Data Sheets are available — contact NZLA via the website. Section 11 of any SDS covers toxicology detail.
