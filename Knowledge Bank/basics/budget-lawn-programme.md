# Budget Lawn Programme

Not everyone can or wants to run a full programme. If you're working with a limited budget, this is the order to prioritise spending for maximum return.

## The non-negotiables

Two things make the biggest difference to lawn quality, and both are relatively low cost.

**1. Granular fertiliser.** NZLA All Seasons 3 times a year — September, March, and one more in summer — is the single most impactful thing you can do for a lawn. A healthy, well-fed lawn is naturally more -resistant, weed-competitive, and wear-tolerant. Skipping fertiliser is the most common reason lawns look poor. Budget roughly $30 to $60 per 100m² per year for this.

**2. Sharp mower blade and correct mowing height.** Free, but important. A blunt blade tears the leaf and creates entry points for . Mowing too short stresses the plant and reduces  and weed resistance. Neither costs anything to fix.

## Second tier — if budget allows

**Iron+ or Charger through winter.** When granular stops working in cold soil, a basic foliar keeps the lawn presentable for very low cost. Iron+ at 200 mL per 100m² costs around $5 to $8 per application. Doing this monthly from June to August makes a real visible difference.

**BWC or Gold for broadleaf  — twice a year.** Weeds in a well-fed lawn are mostly manageable without herbicide. But if clover, dandelion, or plantain is taking over, one spring and one autumn application brings them under control quickly. Budget $20 to $40 per 100m² per year.

## When to spend more

**Grub+ once per year (February to March):** Essential if you've had grub damage before or you're in a region with consistent grass grub pressure. One application per year, $15 per 100m² at the 250 mL size. One treatment often justifies itself by preventing the need for full renovation of grub-damaged areas.

**Fungicide (Fungus Pro) for fusarium-prone lawns:** If your lawn gets fusarium patch every winter, one preventative Fungus Pro application in April costs less than recovering from a bad outbreak. Not needed on every lawn — only if you've had fusarium damage.

## What to skip on a tight budget

**Pre-emergent herbicide** — unless poa annua or summer grass is a major problem. Prevention is more efficient than cure, but if the weed pressure is light, the money is better spent on fertiliser.

**PGR** — requires a full nutrition programme to give the results it promises. Without the base programme, PGR is a wasted investment.

**Wetting agents** — useful but not urgent unless you have persistent dry patch problems.

**Specialty products** — Amino+, Root Health, Nurture, Restore are all good products but they're refinements on top of a working programme. Get the basics right first.

## The minimum effective programme

4 products, about $120 to $200 per year for a 100m² lawn:

1. All Seasons granular × 3 applications (Sep, Jan/Feb, Mar)
2. Iron+ monthly June to August
3. BWC or Gold × 2 applications (Sep, Apr)
4. Grub+ × 1 application (Feb/Mar) — only if grubs are a known issue

This programme, consistently followed, produces a noticeably better result than doing nothing and isn't a large investment. The September and March granular applications are the most important. If you can only afford two applications: do March (the single most important application of the year) and September.
