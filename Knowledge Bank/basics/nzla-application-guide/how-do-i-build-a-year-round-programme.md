# How do I build a year-round NZLA programme?

Five components form the framework. Adapt each to your lawn's specific situation.

**1. Granular base feed.** The foundation of the programme. NZLA All Seasons (21-1-16 + 5% Fe) or All Seasons N+ for spring push every 6 to 8 weeks October through May. That's roughly 5 to 6 applications across the warmer months. All Seasons N+ provides extra nitrogen for the spring growth push.

**2. Foliar supplements.** Between granular applications. NZLA Charger every 2 to 3 weeks for steady maintenance feeding and lateral density. NZLA Iron+ for colour pickup. NZLA Liquid Boost when the lawn needs a push (post-stress, pre-event). The foliar role takes over completely from May through August when granular response slows.

**3. Pre-emergent.** NZLA Blockade twice a year at the germination windows — spring for summer grass and crabgrass (late August Auckland to early October Canterbury), autumn for poa annua (March to early April). Note the NZ label warm-season turf only restriction.

**4. Disease management.** NZLA Fungus Pro (thiram contact protectant) on a routine 4-week pattern through the high-pressure months (autumn into winter). NZLA Azoxy (systemic) as backup if disease shows up despite preventative chemistry — strict 2-application maximum then rotate to Fungus Pro.

**5. Establishment and recovery.** NZLA Starter granular and Liquid Starter for any new lawn work, overseeding or post-renovation recovery. These come in as needed rather than calendar-driven.

A typical home lawn year:

September: All Seasons N+ (spring push) + Blockade pre-em.
October: Charger foliar between granulars.
November: All Seasons (mid-spring base feed).
December: Iron+ foliar; Wetter monthly.
January: All Seasons (summer base feed).
February: Iron+ + Charger between.
March: All Seasons (autumn feed) + Blockade pre-em autumn + Fungus Pro starts.
April: Fungus Pro continues; Iron+ for colour.
May: Switch to foliar — Charger + Iron+.
June-August: Charger + Iron+ every 2 weeks; Fungus Pro routine.

About 15 to 20 applications across a year, each one a quick job. The total cost is far less than chasing problems with rescue products on a hungry, disease-prone lawn.
