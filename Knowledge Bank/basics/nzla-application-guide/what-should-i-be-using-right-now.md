# What products should I be using right now?

Depends on what month you're in and where you are in NZ. Quick guide:

**September (early spring).** All Seasons or All Seasons N+ for the spring kick-start as growth fires up. Charger as a foliar follow-up. Pre-em Blockade in late August / early September for upper North Island; late September for Canterbury — before soil temps hit 12°C and summer grass germinates.

**October-November (mid-spring).** Push period. All Seasons N+ or Mini Prill for active growth. Iron+ for colour. Spot treat broadleaf weeds (BWC) before they set seed. NZLA Starter for any seeding work.

**December-January (early-mid summer).** Slow-release base every 6 to 8 weeks (All Seasons or All Seasons N+). Watch for dry patch — Wetter monthly. Iron+ between granulars. Hold off high-N pushes in heat-stressed lawns.

**February (late summer).** Often the recovery window after summer stress. Light feed with All Seasons or All Seasons N+ to push back into autumn. Watch for brown patch in humid inland areas.

**March-April (autumn).** Hardening feed — All Seasons (21-1-16) every 6 weeks. Pre-em Blockade for poa annua before soil temps drop into the germination range. Start Fungus Pro protectant as Fusarium pressure builds.

**May-June (late autumn / early winter).** Switch from granular to foliar. Charger every 2 weeks. Iron+ for colour. Fungus Pro on a 4-week pattern. Don't apply high-N now — feeds Fusarium.

**July-August (deep winter).** Foliar only. Charger, Iron+. Mow less. Watch for disease. Treat Fusarium with Azoxy + Fungus Pro rotation if it appears.

If you can tell us your region (Auckland, Wellington, Canterbury, etc.) and your specific lawn situation (grass type, recent issues), we can tighten the recommendation. The NZLA website programme builder also tailors recommendations to your lawn details.
