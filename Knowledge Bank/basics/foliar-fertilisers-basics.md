# Foliar Fertilising

## What it is and when to use it

Foliar fertilising means applying nutrients as a liquid spray the leaf absorbs directly, rather than through the soil via root uptake. Response is faster than granular — colour and growth visible within 3 to 5 days versus 7 to 14 days for granular as soil biology breaks it down.

Four main situations where foliar is the right choice:

**Cool conditions.** Soil temperatures below about 10°C slow granular response dramatically. Soil biology and root activity have wound back. Foliar uptake bypasses the soil — the leaf absorbs the nutrient directly — so foliar keeps the lawn fed through winter when granular can't deliver.

**Between granular applications.** The granular base feeds every 6 to 8 weeks. Foliar fills the gaps with a more responsive top-up.

**Colour without growth.** NZLA Iron+ (pure chelated iron, no nitrogen) lifts colour without forcing leaf growth. Useful heading into cold conditions or pre-event when you want the appearance without the nitrogen.

**Establishment and recovery.** NZLA Liquid Starter (8-10-5 plus kelp) supports new lawns through early establishment. NZLA Amino+ and Restore for post-stress recovery.

## Rain after application

No. Rain within 2 to 4 hours washes the spray off the leaf before the plant can absorb it. Foliar uptake takes hours through stomata and the cuticle — it's not instant. Heavy rain within that window means the product has gone to the soil, not the plant.

Standard rule: dry foliage at application, no rain forecast for at least 2 to 4 hours after.

NZLA Iron+ is relatively rainfast once the spray has dried on the leaf (30 to 60 minutes in normal conditions), but heavy rain within 2 hours still reduces effectiveness. If unexpected rain hits within an hour of application, the spray is largely lost. Wait 2 to 3 weeks and reapply.

Light mist and heavy dew on the leaf at application also reduce effectiveness — the spray gets diluted before it absorbs.

Best windows: cool morning after dew has lifted but before midday heat, or cool evening 2 to 3 hours before sunset.

## Mowing after application

24 hours minimum before mowing. Mowing sooner removes the spray with the clippings before the leaf has absorbed it.

All the main NZLA foliars have this same 24-hour guidance: Charger, Iron+, Liquid Boost, Liquid N, Liquid Starter. 48 hours is better where schedule allows — more time maximises absorption.

Don't plan foliar application on the day of mowing. Apply the day after mowing, then mow 24 to 48 hours later in the next cycle.

## What NOT to apply foliar fertiliser in

Midday summer heat over 28°C — the leaf stomata close in heat stress and uptake drops. Some products (Liquid N, Iron+) have specific temperature ceilings. Apply in cool conditions.

Evening before forecast rain. Apply morning or midday in a weather window instead.

On a lawn under severe heat or drought stress. The leaf isn't functioning normally. Wait for conditions to improve.
