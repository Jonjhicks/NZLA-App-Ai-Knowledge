# Should I mow my lawn in winter?

Yes, just less often. Lawns still grow through winter, especially up north, just slowly. Skipping it entirely means it gets away on you, then you take too much off in one go come spring and the lawn doesn't like that.

How often depends on where you are. Cool-season ryegrass and fescue in most of the South Island might only need a cut every 3 to 4 weeks through the depths of winter. Auckland and the upper North Island, more like every 2 to 3 weeks. Watch the lawn, not the calendar.

Lift the height up. 40 to 60 mm for ryegrass over winter. Longer grass photosynthesises better in low light, holds carbohydrate reserves better, and shades out moss.

Don't mow when there's a frost on the grass, you'll bruise the blades and leave footprints in the lawn for weeks. And don't mow when it's wet enough that the mower's making tracks. Sharp blades matter more in winter than summer too, a tear is a doorway for fungal pathogens in cool damp conditions.
