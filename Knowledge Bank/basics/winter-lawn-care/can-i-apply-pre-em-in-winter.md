# Can I apply pre-emergent in winter?

You can apply Blockade in winter, but the question is whether it's the right window for the weeds you're targeting.

Pre-em works on weed seeds at germination. The barrier needs to be in place before the target weeds germinate, not after. Late application catches some weeds but misses others that have already germinated.

By winter, the autumn poa annua germination window is largely past. Most poa that's going to come up has already done so. A winter Blockade application:

**Catches some late poa germination.** Provides some ongoing barrier for the autumn-into-winter window where soil temperatures sit in the poa-favourable range (18 to 22°C and falling).

**Doesn't fix the autumn miss.** Once poa is up, you need post-emergent control (Etho on ryegrass and tall fescue lawns specifically) or to accept the established population for the season.

**Provides residual into spring.** Blockade residual lasts 4 to 6 months, so a winter application carries the barrier through into early summer — but most of the value is autumn-into-winter coverage you missed.

For summer grass and crabgrass pre-em:

Winter is too early. The summer grass germination window is spring (soil temps hitting 12°C and rising). A winter Blockade application has decayed significantly by the time summer grass germinates. Better to apply spring at the proper timing.

For broadleaf pre-em coverage:

Diflufenican (in BWC) has some pre-emergent activity on early-stage broadleaf seedlings, but applying it specifically as a pre-em in winter is unusual. Post-em on established broadleaf weeds is more typical.

The practical answer:

Get the autumn pre-em timing right (March-April for poa annua before soil temps fall). Get the spring pre-em timing right (late August Auckland to early October Canterbury for summer grass). Don't try to fix missed timing with mid-winter applications — different windows entirely.

Note: NZLA Blockade is registered for warm-season turf only in NZ. Off-label use on cool-season can't be officially recommended whatever the season.
