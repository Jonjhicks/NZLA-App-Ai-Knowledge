# What should I do for my lawn in deep winter?

Less than you'd think, and different to what you'd do other seasons.

Granular fertiliser is largely off the menu. Soil temperatures below 10°C mean granular response slows dramatically. The lawn just isn't metabolising the nutrients fast enough to make granular worthwhile. Switch to foliar feeding.

**The deep winter foliar programme.**

NZLA Charger every 2 weeks. Maintenance feeding without forcing soft growth. Holds colour and lateral density through the cool months. Particularly suits warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) in cooler regions where they slow but don't fully stop.

NZLA Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks. Colour without nitrogen. Iron drives chlorophyll directly and hardens the plant against cold and disease. The lawn stays presentably green without forcing soft growth that disease would feed on.

NZLA Liquid N as a sustained slow-release N option if the lawn needs more than Charger gives. Temperature ceiling 26°C — fine in winter conditions.

**Disease management.**

Fusarium pressure peaks in winter. NZLA Fungus Pro on a routine 4-week pattern through the wet months (every 2 weeks in high humidity). Protectant contact chemistry with almost no resistance risk — preventative coverage.

Watch for active disease. If you see Fusarium showing up despite preventative Fungus Pro, NZLA Azoxy as systemic knockdown. Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating to Fungus Pro.

**Cultural basics that matter more in winter.**

Knock the morning dew off. Most useful single thing you can do for winter disease prevention.

Don't water unless the lawn genuinely needs it (rare in winter). Most NZ regions get enough rainfall through winter.

Mow less. Every 2 to 4 weeks at lifted height (40 to 60 mm for ryegrass). Sharp blade.

Keep traffic off frosty or waterlogged ground.

**What NOT to do in winter.**

Don't apply high-nitrogen granular. Feeds Fusarium, lawn can't use it.

Don't core aerate. Save for spring or autumn.

Don't push aggressive renovation. Wait for spring.

Don't expect dead disease patches to fill in. They'll regrow in spring.

Deep winter is the lawn's quiet period. Steady foliar maintenance, preventative disease cover, and patience. Spring isn't far away.
