# How long after applying granular fertiliser should I water?

Within 24 hours, ideally same day. Water it in lightly with 5 to 10 mm of irrigation, or apply just before forecast rain.

Why timing matters:

**Activation.** Slow-release coated fertilisers (All Seasons, All Seasons, Mini Prill) need moisture to start releasing the nitrogen. Polymer or sulphur coatings only break down in damp conditions. Dry granular sitting on dry soil does nothing until it gets wet.

**Burn prevention.** High-nitrogen fertilisers (Mini Prill, All Seasons N+) can burn the lawn if the granules sit on damp foliage in hot conditions. Watering in moves the nitrogen down into the soil and off the leaf surface.

**Even distribution.** Light irrigation washes the granules off the leaf canopy down to soil level where they belong. Granules sitting on top of the grass don't feed the lawn; they need to be at the soil surface to dissolve and feed the roots.

Practical pattern:

Apply granular onto dry foliage (granules bounce off and reach the soil cleanly).

Water in with 5 to 10 mm of irrigation within 24 hours. Light rain works too.

Don't over-water — saturating the lawn doesn't help the fertiliser and creates short-term anaerobic conditions in the soil. 5 to 10 mm is plenty.

If rain is forecast within 24 hours of application, that's ideal — the natural rain saves the irrigation step.

If you can't water in within 24 hours, you'll still get response once it does rain or you do water. The fertiliser won't go off — it sits as inert granules until moisture arrives.

What NOT to do: apply granular onto wet foliage (granules stick to wet leaves and burn them), or apply just before a heavy rain forecast over 25 mm (washes granules off-target).
