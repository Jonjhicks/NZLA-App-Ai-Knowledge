# Can I apply granular fertiliser in summer?

Yes, with the right product, but ease back on rate and frequency through the worst of summer heat.

Slow-release granular (All Seasons, All Seasons) suits summer fine. The metered release means no nitrogen spike, the potassium and iron support heat tolerance, and the lawn gets steady feeding through warm conditions when active growth needs it.

What to avoid in summer:

**High-nitrogen push fertilisers (All Seasons N+, Mini Prill) in heat stress.** Forcing growth on a heat-stressed lawn makes the stress worse. The lawn can't use the nitrogen efficiently and you risk brown patch and other heat-related disease. Save the push fertilisers for spring and early autumn.

**Apply onto wet foliage in hot weather.** Granules stuck to wet leaves in 28°C+ heat can cause leaf burn — visible brown patches where the granule sat. Apply onto dry foliage and water in.

**Heavy application before forecast hot dry weather.** Concentrated nitrogen release into a stressed lawn over a few weeks of heat is asking for trouble. Spread applications across the season.

Practical summer programme:

All Seasons or All Seasons N+ every 6 to 8 weeks at standard rate (2 to 2.5 kg per 100m²). The 6% iron and 16% potassium suit heat conditions better than higher-N alternatives.

Iron+ foliar every 2 to 3 weeks for colour pickup without forcing growth.

Charger foliar between granular applications if you want maintenance feeding without the spike of a high-N hit.

Water in granular applications same day. Hot dry surfaces leave granules sitting longer than they should.

When NOT to fertilise:

Lawns visibly heat-stressed (wilted, dry, browning). Wait for cooler weather or irrigation to revive the lawn first.

In the leadup to forecast extreme heat (35°C+ days). Hold the application for a milder window.

On dormant warm-season turf (kikuyu in mid-summer occasionally goes semi-dormant in dry inland areas).

For most NZ home lawns at typical summer temperatures (22 to 28°C), routine slow-release granular application works fine.
