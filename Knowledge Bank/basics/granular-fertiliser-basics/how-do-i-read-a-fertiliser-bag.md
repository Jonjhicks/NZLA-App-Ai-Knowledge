# How do I read a fertiliser bag?

Three numbers tell you most of the story: **N-P-K**. Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, as percentages.

The first number is nitrogen (N). Drives leaf growth and colour. High first number (25%+) means a push feed.

The middle number is phosphorus (P). Drives root development. High middle (10%+) usually means a starter or establishment fertiliser. Low middle (under 3%) means maintenance.

The third number is potassium (K). Drives plant strength, cold tolerance, disease resistance. Matched to nitrogen (for example 21-1-16) means balanced maintenance.

Common NZLA examples that show the pattern:

- All Seasons 21-1-16 + 5% Fe — balanced maintenance, year-round base
- All Seasons N+ 32-1.3-8.3 + 2% Fe — high N push for recovery and spring
- All Seasons 20-0-16 + 6% Fe — similar to All Seasons at sharper price
- NZLA Starter 19-10-4 + trace — high P for new lawns

Look for slow-release vs fast-release. Premium products will say "polymer coated", "PCSCU", or "controlled release". Cheap products are fast-release urea — quick green-up that fades fast.

Iron (Fe) and other trace elements are usually listed after the NPK. Iron at 4 to 6 percent is meaningful for colour.

Rate is given as kg/ha or kg per 100m². Convert: 1 kg/100m² = 100 kg/ha. For All Seasons at 2 kg/100m² (200 kg/ha), a 10 kg bag covers 500m².
