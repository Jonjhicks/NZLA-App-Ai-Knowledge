# When should I apply autumn fertiliser?

Late February through April depending on region. Soil temperatures are still warm enough for the lawn to use the feed, and you are setting it up for winter resilience.

All Seasons (21-1-16 + 5% Fe) is the autumn pick. The 16% potassium and 5% iron harden the plant against cold and disease. The 21% nitrogen is enough to keep growth steady without forcing soft growth that Fusarium will feed on.

Avoid the high-nitrogen feeds (All Seasons N+, Liquid Boost) heading into autumn. Same reason — soft growth into cool damp conditions sets up disease pressure. Use them in spring and through summer for push, not for autumn hardening.

For Auckland and the upper North Island, late February into March. Central North Island, mid to late March. Canterbury and the South Island, late March into early April.

Pair it with a pre-em application if poa annua is a problem — Blockade goes down at roughly the same window to catch poa germination before it fires.

Iron+ as a foliar top-up between the autumn granular and the cooler months. Adds colour and hardens the plant further without pushing growth. Then transition to a foliar-only programme (Charger, Iron+, Liquid N) for the depths of winter when granular response slows.
