# How often should I fertilise my lawn?

Depends on the season and the product type.

Slow-release granular base feeds (All Seasons, All Seasons) typically go down every 6 to 8 weeks through the growing season — late September through April for most of the country. That gives steady feeding without the spike-and-trough cycle that cheap fast-release fertilisers cause.

Foliar top-ups (Iron+, Charger, Liquid Boost) sit on top of the granular, every 2 to 4 weeks depending on what you want from them. Iron+ for colour pickup, Charger for maintenance, Liquid Boost when you want a real push.

In winter, granular response slows right down as soil temperatures drop. The lawn just is not metabolising the nutrients fast enough to make granular worthwhile. That is when foliar takes over — Charger every 2 weeks holds colour and growth steady, Iron+ for additional green-up, Liquid N for steady nitrogen without the spike.

Summer needs less frequent feeding if the lawn is heat-stressed. Pushing N on a struggling lawn in 30°C heat is counterproductive.

The standard year-round NZLA programme is All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks spring through autumn, switching to foliars (Charger, Liquid Boost, Iron+) through the cooler months when granular slows down.
