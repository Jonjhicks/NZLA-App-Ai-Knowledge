# Annual Programme — South Island Cool-Season Lawn

Covers Canterbury, Otago, Southland, West Coast, Marlborough, and Nelson. The South Island calendar runs 2 to 4 weeks behind the North Island depending on region. Soil temperatures are the guide, not the calendar dates.

Key differences from the NI programme: shorter growing season, colder winters, frost risk from April through to October in colder regions, longer  window for fusarium and snow mould in the south, less summer heat stress (South Island dry heat is different to NI humidity).

---

## October — the South Island spring start

Soil temperatures only now reliably above 10°C in most South Island regions. This is when the NI would be into its second month of the season.

**First granular:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m². The spring push.

**Wetting agents:** NZLA Wetter 3W now — before the Canterbury nor'wester season begins. The dry foehn winds dry soil rapidly and hydrophobic conditions develop fast.

**Weed control:** BWC for broadleaf  once temperatures are consistently above 12°C.

**Pre-emergent:** Blockade is label-registered for warm-season turf only — less relevant for most South Island cool-season lawns. Etho for poa annua post-emergent control on ryegrass lawns.

---

## November — growing season underway

**Fertiliser:** Charger fortnightly foliar on top of the October granular.

**Disease:** Dollar spot and brown patch less prevalent than NI due to lower humidity. Fusarium risk drops in summer. Monitor but  pressure is lower overall.

**Mowing:** Frequency increasing. Maintain 1/3 rule.

---

## December, January — summer

**Canterbury:** Nor'wester winds and hot dry periods. Irrigation becomes critical, especially in sheltered inland sites. Deep watering twice weekly minimum in hot spells. Penetrate for surface dry patch.

**Otago/Southland:** Milder summer, lower irrigation-general demand. Still need fertiliser continuity.

**Fertiliser:** All Seasons at 2.5 to 3 kg/100m² in December.

**Foliars:** Charger + Iron+ fortnightly. Liquid Boost pre-event if needed.

---

## February — the most important month in the SI calendar

The South Island autumn opportunity is shorter than the North Island's — soil stays warm enough for good establishment through February and into March, but the window closes earlier.

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m². This is the South Island's most important application of the year — do not miss it.

**Renovation:** Core aeration, oversowing thin areas. Soil temps 14 to 18°C, still warm enough for reliable ryegrass germination.

**Grub+:** Apply NZLA Grub+ in late September/early October (first application, before egg hatch), then again in March (second application). Water in 6 mm immediately.

---

## March — last renovation window prevention

**Fertiliser:** All Seasons at 2 to 2.5 kg/100m² for the final push before winter.

**Last seeding:** Ryegrass can still establish if soil temps stay above 14°C. In Canterbury, March sowing is often reliable. In Otago/Southland, March is borderline — check soil temps before sowing.

**Disease prevention:** The most important fusarium prevention window for South Island lawns. Apply NZLA Azoxy or Fungus Pro in late March as a preventative before conditions deteriorate. South Island winters can be severe enough for extended fusarium pressure — prevention here is worth the investment.

**Weed control:** BWC or Gold for autumn weed control.

---

## April — frost risk begins in cold regions

**Granular:** Final optional application of All Seasons for mid-Canterbury and above. Below Canterbury (Otago, Southland), switch to foliars from April.

**Foliars begin:** NZLA Charger every 2 weeks. Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks for colour through winter.

**Disease:** Fusarium and snow mould prevention on susceptible sites (lower South Island, shaded, poorly draining). NZLA Fungus Pro + Azoxy rotation preventative.

**Frost:** Stop applying to frozen grass. Morning applications only on frost days — wait until the frost has fully lifted.

---

## May, June, July — South Island winter

More consistent frost, shorter days, slower growth. The South Island winter programme is similar to the NI but with longer duration and colder temperatures.

**Foliars only:** Charger fortnightly + Iron+ 2 to 3 weekly. No granular.

**Liquid N:** For any mild spell where visible growth stimulus is wanted — only apply when temperatures are consistently above 8°C. Do not apply in frost conditions.

**Disease:** Fusarium patches most common in cold wet periods. NZLA Fungus Pro curative at first sign. On lower South Island and alpine sites, snow mould (same pathogen as fusarium, same treatment) is the relevant concern.

**Mowing:** Monthly or less in deep winter. Keep blades sharp.

---

## August — South Island pre-spring

Soil temperatures beginning to lift, particularly in coastal Canterbury and Nelson.

**Wetter 3W:** Apply now to prepare soil ahead of the growing season.

**Continue foliars:** Charger + Iron+.

---

## September — the South Island is just warming up

While the NI is in full swing, the South Island spring is just beginning.

**First granular:** All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m² when soil temps consistently above 10°C (mid to late September in Canterbury, October in Otago/Southland).

**Weed control:** BWC spring application.

## Canterbury-specific notes

Nor'wester wind events cause rapid moisture stress. Wetter 3W before summer is essential. Canterbury soil dries fast during these events and turf on free-draining soils can go from healthy to heat-stressed within 24 hours.

Soil temperature fluctuations are more extreme than northern NZ — cold nights and warm days in spring/autumn mean soil temp checks are more valuable than calendar timing.
