# Annual Programme — Warm-Season Lawn (Kikuyu and Couch)

This programme covers kikuyu and couch lawns in the upper North Island (Auckland, Northland, Bay of Plenty, Waikato). These warm-season grasses have a fundamentally different annual pattern to cool-season turf — active in summer, semi-dormant in winter.

---

## January — peak performance window

The warm-season lawn is at its best. Soil temperatures 22 to 28°C, active growth, good colour.

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons at 2.5 to 3 kg/100m². Midsummer maintenance feed. Don't push excessive nitrogen in peak summer — moderate, steady feeding produces better results than high-N surges.

**Foliars:** Charger every 2 to 3 weeks for maintenance colour. Iron+ every 3 to 4 weeks.

**Mowing:** Kikuyu at 20 to 40 mm, couch at 15 to 25 mm. Mow frequently enough that you're not removing more than a third of the leaf at each cut.

**Edge management:** Kikuyu edging is a weekly job in summer. Stolons advance rapidly. Stay ahead of it or it gets into garden beds and paths very quickly.

**Irrigation:** 1.5 to 2.5 cm per week, deep and infrequent. Wetter 3W or Penetrate for any hydrophobic dry patches.

**Fall armyworm:** Upper NI, peak risk December to March. Check for ragged overnight damage and moth activity at dusk. NZLA Grub+ evening application.

---

## February — most important application month

February to March is the single most valuable fertiliser window for warm-season turf.

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m². The warm-season autumn push. The lawn is still actively growing and will carry this into winter.

**Grub+:** Apply NZLA Grub+ in late September/early October before egg hatch (first application), then again in March (second application, six months later). Water in 6 mm immediately after each application.

**Spring dead spot prevention:** Apply NZLA Posterity as preventative treatment before soil temperatures drop below 15°C. On any lawn with spring dead spot history, this is the most valuable single application of the year.

---

## March — begin transition management

Soil temperatures starting to ease. Growth rate slowing.

**Fertiliser:** Last solid granular of the active season — All Seasons at 2.5 kg/100m² if not done in February.

**Pre-emergent:** NZLA Blockade on kikuyu and couch lawns for annual grass suppression. Apply as soil temperatures approach the target weed germination threshold.

**Broadleaf :** NZLA Gold for broadleaf weed control. Warm-season lawns actively growing in March — good uptake window.

**Posterity:** Second Posterity application if using a two-spray programme for spring dead spot prevention.

---

## April — slow down

Growth is slowing noticeably. Mowing frequency drops.

**Foliars:** Switch to maintaining colour with Iron+ and light Charger. No more granular.

**NZLA Green:** Optional — kikuyu and couch begin to lose some colour intensity. NZLA Green at 12 mL/100m² for colour presentation if the lawn is used for events or needs to maintain appearance going into winter.

## May, June — winter holding

Warm-season lawns go semi-dormant. Growth essentially stops in cooler regions of the upper NI.

**No granular fertiliser.** The lawn can't use it.

**NZLA Green:** For cosmetic purposes — the lawn may brown off in the cooler months. NZLA Green maintains appearance without growth requirement. 11 to 15 mL/100m², lasts 4 to 8 weeks.

**Minimal mowing.** Monthly or as growth dictates. Don't disturb the lawn unnecessarily during dormancy.

**Disease:** Spring dead spot developing underground through winter. Nothing to see yet, but management decisions made in autumn (Posterity, soil pH) determine spring outcomes.

---

## July, August — pre-spring

The lawn is dormant but about to wake up.

**Wetting agents:** NZLA Wetter 3W spring application in August before the lawn breaks dormancy. Getting the soil conditioned before summer helps.

**Scalp and topdress:** Late August is the window for the spring scalp on kikuyu (if practised). Closely mow, remove debris, apply NZLA All Seasons N+ at 2 kg/100m² to drive spring green-up. This promotes finer, leafier summer growth and reduces the need for verticutting.

**Couch:** Do not scalp without confirmed soil temps above 15°C. Too early scalping of couch causes stress.

---

## September — spring green-up

Soil temperatures recovering. The lawn starts moving again.

**Fertiliser:** NZLA All Seasons N+ at 3 to 4 kg/100m² if not done as part of the August scalp programme. The spring push.

**Spring dead spot assessment:** Patches that didn't green up with the rest of the lawn are spring dead spot. Plan Posterity programme for next autumn. Overseed bare areas with same species once soil temps are above 18°C.

**Mowing:** Resume regular mowing as growth picks up.

---

## October, November, December — full growing season resumes

Return to January programme. Edge management becomes critical again with kikuyu. Monitor for fall armyworm from November.
