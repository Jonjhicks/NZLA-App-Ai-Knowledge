# Lawn Edging

## Tools

Three different tools for three different jobs.

**Line trimmer (whipper snipper)** for weekly maintenance. The standard home tool. Petrol or electric, rotating nylon line cuts grass quickly along the edge after mowing. Cordless electric models are capable for most home lawns and quieter. This is the tool most people reach for.

**Half-moon edger** for restoring or defining the edge line. A curved blade on a long handle that you push vertically into the lawn along the boundary. Creates a clean, defined cut that stops grass creeping into garden beds. Done twice a year (spring and autumn), it keeps the edge sharp. A sharp flat spade does the same job if you don't want a dedicated tool.

**Dedicated lawn edger machine** for regular precision edging on premium lawns. Petrol or electric with a vertical blade specifically designed for edge work. Faster and cleaner than a line trimmer for lawns where the edge is a visual feature. More expensive and only worth it if weekly edge appearance really matters.

For most home users, a line trimmer plus a half-moon edger covers all bases. About $200 to $400 combined.

## Keeping edges sharp

Three approaches, in order of result and effort.

**Regular line trimmer.** The most common home approach. Run around the perimeter after mowing. Quick, no special skill, adequate for most home lawns. Trade-off: the edge tends to creep outward over time as the grass plant pushes the boundary.

**Half-moon edger or sharp spade twice a year.** A vertical cut that restores the original edge line and stops gradual creep. Push it vertically into the lawn along the desired boundary, lift the turf cap on the garden bed side, and the clean defined edge is back. Combined with weekly trimming, this keeps a properly maintained line.

**Permanent edging material.** Concrete, brick, paver, or steel edging installed along the boundary. The mower cuts right up to it, minimal trimming needed. High upfront cost, minimal ongoing maintenance. Standard for premium home lawns and commercial landscaping.

Three habits that improve any approach: mow first then trim, walk a consistent line each week, and replace line trimmer nylon regularly. Worn nylon tears rather than cuts.
