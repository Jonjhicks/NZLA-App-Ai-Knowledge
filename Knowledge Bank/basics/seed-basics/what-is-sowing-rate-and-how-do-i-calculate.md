# What is sowing rate and how do I calculate it?

The amount of seed to put down per unit area. Usually given as kilograms per 100m² or per hectare.

Why it matters:

Too little seed and the lawn establishes thin and patchy — weeds find gaps, the lawn never becomes dense. Too much seed and the seedlings compete with each other for light and nutrients, producing a weak crowded lawn that thins out anyway.

Each species has an established optimum range based on its growth habit, leaf size and tillering pattern.

Typical NZLA sowing rates:

**NZLA Perennial Rye:** new lawn 3 to 4 kg per 100m². Oversowing 2 kg per 100m².

**MDR, Dark Perennial Rye, RSR Ryegrass:** new lawn 3 to 4 kg per 100m². Oversowing 2 kg per 100m².

**Tall Fescue (UTF, Elfin):** new lawn 3 to 5 kg per 100m². Oversowing 2.5 kg per 100m².

**Fine Fescue:** new lawn 2.5 to 4 kg per 100m². Slightly lower than ryegrass.

**Kikuyu:** refer to label — kikuyu seed is much finer and smaller, so much less weight covers the same area.

Calculating for your lawn:

Measure the lawn area in square metres. Divide by 100 to get the number of "100m²" units. Multiply by the sowing rate.

Example. 350m² lawn being established with NZLA Perennial Rye at 4 kg per 100m²:

350 ÷ 100 = 3.5

3.5 × 4 kg = 14 kg of seed total

NZLA Perennial Rye is sold in 5 kg bags, so that's 3 bags (15 kg) with a small reserve for patching.

For overseeding the same lawn at 2 kg per 100m²:

3.5 × 2 = 7 kg total.

Distribute the seed evenly across the lawn. Walk a half-rate pass in one direction, then a half-rate pass at right angles to catch any misses. Calibrate a spreader for accurate distribution on larger areas; hand sow on small patches.

After sowing, lightly rake to mix the seed into the top 5 to 10 mm of soil. Roll if available for soil contact.
