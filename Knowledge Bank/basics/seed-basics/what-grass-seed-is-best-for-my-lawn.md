# What grass seed is best for my lawn?

Depends on your climate, lawn use, shade level and aesthetic preference. Match the species to the situation.

**General home lawn, cool-season climate (most of NZ).** NZLA Perennial Rye is the standard choice. Hard-wearing, winter active, fast establishment, reliable performance. Suits backyards with normal kid-and-dog use.

**Premium home lawn, dark green aesthetic.** NZLA Dark Perennial Rye for noticeably darker colour and finer texture than standard rye. Step up to Midnight Dark Rye (MDR) for the premium showcase finish — among the darkest ryegrass cultivars available in NZ, professional density.

**Heavy use, sports field or family backyard with wear damage.** NZLA RSR Ryegrass. Stoloniferous habit spreads laterally and repairs damage actively, which standard bunch-type ryegrass can't do. Also makes Poa annua establishment harder.

**Drought tolerance, partial shade, low input.** NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue. Deep root system gives drought tolerance, partial shade tolerance, holds colour through summer when ryegrass struggles. Slower to establish than ryegrass — needs irrigation through establishment.

**Self-repairing tall fescue.** Elfin Tall Fescue (rhizomatous cultivar) for tall fescue's toughness plus active spread to fill wear damage.

**Heavy shade, low input, low maintenance.** NZLA Fine Fescue. Best shade tolerance of the cool-season options. Low fertility requirement, fine texture. Won't tolerate heavy traffic — different positioning to ryegrass.

**Warm climate, upper North Island (Waikato and north).** NZLA Kikuyu (raw seed) for general home and sports use. Aggressive coverage, drought tolerance, winter colour retention better than other warm-season options.

**Premium warm-season aesthetic, fine texture.** NZLA Couch. Bermudagrass for showcase warm-climate lawns. Finer texture than kikuyu, premium appearance.

If you're not sure, NZLA Perennial Rye is the safe choice for most of NZ. The NZLA website has a seed selection guide that takes you through specific situations.

Sowing rates are typically 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for new lawns, 2 kg per 100m² for overseeding.
