# How long does grass seed take to germinate?

Depends on the species and soil temperature.

**Perennial Ryegrass.** The fastest of the common turf species. 5 to 10 days at warm soil temperatures (15 to 20°C). 10 to 14 days at cool but adequate temperatures (10 to 15°C). Slower below 10°C. Doesn't germinate below 5°C.

**Tall Fescue.** Slower than ryegrass. 10 to 14 days at warm temperatures, 14 to 21 days at cooler temperatures. Tall fescue particularly needs consistent moisture through the longer germination window — dry-out at day 7 kills seedlings before they're up.

**Fine Fescue.** Similar to tall fescue, 10 to 14 days at warm temperatures.

**Kikuyu.** 14 to 21 days at warm soil temperatures above 15°C. Slower or fails completely below 15°C. Don't sow kikuyu in cool soil — the seed sits and rots rather than germinates.

**Couch (Bermudagrass).** Similar to kikuyu, needs 15°C minimum and ideally 18°C+. 14 to 21 days at suitable temperatures.

Why temperature matters:

Germination is a metabolic process. The seed needs warmth to power the biochemistry that breaks down the seed coat, mobilises stored carbohydrates, and pushes the new root and shoot out. Cool conditions slow that down dramatically; cold conditions stop it entirely.

After visible germination, the seedling is fragile for another 4 to 6 weeks until the root system develops. Continue light frequent watering through that window. First mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

Tips for faster, more consistent germination:

Sow when soil temperatures are climbing through the optimal range (spring or late summer/early autumn for cool-season species).

Keep the surface consistently moist for the first 2 weeks — the seed needs continuous moisture, not deep occasional watering.

Press the seed into soil contact (light rake, roll if available). Seed sitting on top of dry surface doesn't germinate.

Don't apply pre-emergent within the residual window. Don't sow into recently-applied pre-em.

Patience. New lawns take 6 to 12 weeks from sowing to looking like a lawn.
