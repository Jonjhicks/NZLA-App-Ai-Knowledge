# How do I calibrate my knapsack sprayer?

Fill the sprayer with clean water. Pace out a known area — 10m² (2 m by 5 m) is the standard.

Walk the area at your normal walking pace, spraying evenly as you go. Use the same nozzle, same pressure, same walking pace you'd use for a real application.

When you finish the test patch, refill the sprayer to the original level and measure how much water you used. That's your carrier water rate per 10m². Multiply by 10 to get per 100m².

Once you know your carrier water rate, the product calculation is straightforward. For example, if your sprayer applies 8 L of water per 100m², and the product label says 250 mL per 100m², you mix 250 mL of product into 8 L of water in the tank for each 100m² you're spraying. For 300m² of lawn, that's 750 mL of product in 24 L of water across three sprayer tanks.

Re-calibrate if you change anything — new nozzle, different walking pace, different pressure setting. Same applies if you switch between fine spray (foliar fertilisers) and coarse spray (pre-em or selective herbicides).

A couple of practical notes. Walk at a steady pace — speeding up at the end of a tank throws the rate off. Keep the pressure consistent (pump regularly to maintain it). Use clean water for calibration — actual product application matches calibration only if conditions match.

Most knapsack sprayers, properly calibrated, are accurate to within 10 percent.
