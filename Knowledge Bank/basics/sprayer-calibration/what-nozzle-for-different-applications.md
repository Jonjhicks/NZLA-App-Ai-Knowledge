# What nozzle should I use for different applications?

Match the nozzle to the job. Three considerations: droplet size, spray pattern, and flow rate.

**Foliar fertiliser application.** Fine to medium droplets give good leaf coverage. A flat fan nozzle (LF or LFR series — Lecler / Teejet equivalents) at 200 to 300 micron droplet size produces good coverage without excessive drift. Walking pace at moderate pressure. Apply at 4 to 15 L water per 100m².

**Selective broadleaf herbicide.** Medium droplets to balance leaf coverage with drift control. Air induction or medium flat fan nozzles. Higher water volume (8 to 15 L per 100m²) for thorough leaf contact.

**Pre-emergent herbicide (Blockade).** Coarser droplets to land on soil rather than canopy. The pre-em needs to bind into the top centimetre of soil, not coat the leaf. Air induction nozzles at coarser droplet sizes work well. 6 to 10 L water per 100m² is plenty.

**Contact fungicide (Fungus Pro).** Fine to medium droplets for thorough leaf canopy coverage. High water volume essential — 20 L per 100m² for proper contact coverage. The chemistry only works where the spray lands.

**Systemic fungicide (Azoxy).** Medium droplets, 4 to 8 L water per 100m². Less water volume than contact fungicides because the active moves into the leaf.

**Glyphosate or total kill.** Coarser droplets, lower water volume (4 to 8 L per 100m²). Glyphosate uptake is concentration-driven and high water volume reduces effectiveness.

Practical notes:

Use a nozzle gauge or replace nozzles when they wear (typically every 100 hours of use). Worn nozzles enlarge the orifice and increase flow rate by 10 to 30 percent, throwing calibration off.

Use a single nozzle type per tank. Don't mix nozzle types on the same boom.

Calibrate every time you change nozzle or pressure setting. Walking pace and spraying pressure both affect actual rate.

For backpack sprayers, the standard 02 or 03 flat fan nozzles cover most home lawn applications adequately.
