# How do I calibrate my spreader?

Mark out a known area — 10 m² (2 m by 5 m is easy to pace out) is the standard test patch.

Weigh out the exact amount of fertiliser the label says for that area. For All Seasons at 2 kg per 100 m², that is 200 g for the 10 m² test patch. Use a kitchen scale.

Set the spreader to a middle setting on its scale. Walk the test patch at your normal walking pace, covering it evenly. Walk it in two directions (e.g. north-south, then east-west at half rate each) for proper even coverage.

Check the result. If you have leftover fertiliser when you finish the patch, the spreader setting is too low. If you ran out before finishing, the setting is too high. Adjust the setting one notch and try again on a fresh patch.

Once dialled in, write the correct setting down so you do not have to redo the whole exercise each time.

A couple of practical notes. Use the same product you calibrated for — different prill sizes (e.g. All Seasons vs Mini Prill, which has SGN 150 small prills) flow differently and need separate calibrations. Walk at the same pace every time. And calibrate on a still day; wind throws the spread pattern off and gives you a misleading result.

Most home rotary spreaders are accurate to within 10 percent once calibrated.
