# When can I first mow grass seedlings?

When the seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm height. Typically 4 to 6 weeks after sowing, depending on species and conditions.

How to tell when:

**Height check.** Measure the tallest seedlings. If they're consistently 60 mm or above across the lawn, the lawn is ready for first mow.

**Visual density check.** The lawn should look like grass rather than scattered green fuzz. The canopy should be reasonably continuous, not bare soil with isolated tall stems.

**The pull test.** Gently grip a tuft of seedlings and pull. If they pull out of the soil easily, roots haven't established enough. If they tear at the leaf level but stay rooted, you can mow.

Don't mow earlier even if you're impatient. Mowing seedlings under 60 mm:

Damages the developing crown. The crown (growing point at the base of the plant) is still soft and underdeveloped. Cutting at this stage stresses the plant severely.

Reduces leaf area to dangerous levels. Seedlings need most of their leaf area for photosynthesis to drive root development. Cutting too early starves the plant of growth resources.

Can pull seedlings out of the soil. The mower wheels and blade action can dislodge un-rooted seedlings before they've anchored properly.

First mow specifics:

**Set the mower at the highest cut setting** that takes meaningful material off. If the seedlings are 80 mm, target a cut height of 55 mm (a third off).

**Sharp blade. Always.** Tearing leaf during the first mow on new seedlings does extended damage that takes weeks to recover.

**Catch the clippings on the first mow.** Don't mulch on new seedlings — the clippings can smother the still-young grass.

**Mow when the lawn is dry.** Wet ground plus a heavy mower equals tracking marks and root mat damage on un-rooted seedlings.

**Mow slowly.** Don't rush the mower across the new lawn. The wheels can shift seedlings that aren't fully rooted yet.

After the first mow:

The lawn looks rough for a week — uneven height, clipping debris, some seedlings showing stress. Normal. The lawn recovers within 7 to 10 days.

Wait another 10 to 14 days before the second mow. Apply the third rule — third of the leaf off each time.

By the third or fourth mow (8 to 12 weeks post-sowing), the lawn handles normal mowing on a regular cycle.

When NOT to mow:

If significant areas of the lawn are still under 50 mm. Wait longer.

If the lawn pulls out easily when tested. Roots aren't anchored — wait for more establishment.

In wet conditions where the mower will track or scalp.

In hot stressed conditions where adding mowing stress could damage establishment.

Gradual height reduction:

If you want to bring the lawn to a final target height of 25 to 30 mm but seedlings are 80 mm:

First mow: 55 mm.

Mow at progressively lower settings across 2 to 3 mows over 4 to 6 weeks. Third rule each time.

Don't try to drop straight to 30 mm from 80 mm. Scalping new seedlings damages them severely.

By 10 to 12 weeks post-sowing, the lawn handles full normal maintenance.
