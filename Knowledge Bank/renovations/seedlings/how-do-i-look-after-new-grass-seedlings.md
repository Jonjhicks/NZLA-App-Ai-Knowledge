# How do I look after new grass seedlings?

Three priorities for the first 4 to 6 weeks: water consistently, keep traffic off, support gently.

**Watering — the most important thing.**

New seedlings have tiny root systems and dry out fast. Until roots establish into the soil (typically 3 to 4 weeks), the seedlings need consistent surface moisture.

First 2 weeks: light frequent watering. 2 to 3 times a day in warm conditions, once a day in cool weather. Enough to wet the top 5 to 10 mm of soil without pooling.

Weeks 2 to 4: back off frequency, increase depth. Twice a day in warm conditions, once a day in cool. Going deeper as roots develop.

Weeks 4 to 6: transition to twice-weekly deep watering as the lawn becomes properly established.

Watch the seedlings. If wilting in afternoons or edges drying, increase watering. If waterlogged or yellowing, back off.

The number one cause of new lawn failure is inconsistent watering during establishment. The seedling dries out and dies within hours. Once the seedling is dead, it doesn't come back.

**Traffic management.**

Keep off the new lawn for the first 4 to 6 weeks. Foot traffic on seedlings damages the developing roots and crushes the plants before they're robust enough to handle it.

Mark routes around the lawn if possible. If you have dogs, keep them off for the first 6 weeks.

For lawns where some traffic is unavoidable (entrance to the house, etc.), use temporary stepping stones or boards to distribute load.

**Feeding gently.**

NZLA Starter granular at sowing or laying provides the establishment phosphorus and trace elements. Don't apply more Starter on top of new seedlings — risk of burn.

At 4 to 5 weeks, NZLA Liquid Starter as foliar at 100 to 200 mL per 100m² supports the seedlings into maturity. Gentle compared to maintenance fertilisers.

Don't apply high-N fertilisers on new seedlings. Even maintenance NPK can burn seedlings under 6 weeks old.

**Mowing — wait until ready.**

First mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height. Set the mower at the highest cut setting. Take only the top third off. Sharp blade. Mow when the lawn is dry.

Don't mow earlier even if you're impatient. Mowing seedlings under 60 mm tall damages the developing crowns.

**Watch for issues.**

Birds eating seed or seedlings — cover with hessian or netting if a problem.

Disease in young seedlings (damping off) — usually water-related (over-watering creates conditions for fungal disease). Reduce watering frequency, increase airflow.

Weeds emerging alongside seedlings — generally don't treat with selective herbicide on new seedlings under 8 weeks. Hand-pull obvious weeds, accept some weed pressure until the lawn matures.

Pets, kids, or grub damage breaking up the lawn — temporary fencing if needed.

**Patience.**

New lawns don't look like established lawns until 6 to 12 months in. The plant population is younger, less developed, less dense. That's normal.

Don't compare a 6-week-old lawn to a 6-year-old lawn. Don't push it with heavy mowing, heavy feeding, or normal use until 6 to 8 weeks in.

Establishment is mostly about patience and consistent gentle care.
