# How long do I leave frost cloth on the lawn?

For overnight protection: put it on in the evening before the frost, remove in the morning once the frost has lifted from surrounding areas. Usually 12 to 16 hours of coverage.

For extended cold spells: leave the cloth in place for the duration of the cold spell rather than removing and replacing daily. Up to a week is typically fine.

For new lawn establishment in winter: 2 to 4 weeks of intermittent coverage during heavy frost periods. Remove on mild days to allow direct light to reach the lawn.

What NOT to do:

**Don't leave frost cloth on permanently.** Continuous coverage for weeks blocks light reaching the lawn. The grass weakens from light deprivation faster than it would have suffered from intermittent frost exposure. Light is more important than frost protection for establishing grass.

**Don't leave it on during the day in warm conditions.** Trapped heat under the cloth on sunny days can scorch the lawn. Remove during daylight hours when the temperature climbs above about 15°C.

**Don't leave it on wet for extended periods.** Damp cloth held against the lawn surface creates ideal conditions for fungal disease. Remove and let the lawn dry between uses.

When to remove for good:

Once the lawn is robust enough to handle frost on its own — generally after the first 2 to 3 mows for new lawns from seed, or after 6 to 8 weeks for ready lawn.

For seasonal protection of established lawns (rare situation), remove once the frost season ends — typically late September in colder regions, earlier in milder areas.

Practical decision rules:

**Light frost forecast on an established lawn:** don't bother with cloth. The mature lawn handles it.

**Heavy frost (below -4°C) on an established lawn:** still usually fine without cloth. Some discolouration possible but recovery is normal.

**Light frost on a new lawn under 6 weeks old:** cover for the night. New seedlings are vulnerable.

**Heavy frost on a new lawn:** cover and consider extending coverage for several days if the cold spell persists.

**Heavy frost on premium showcase turf (golf greens, bowling greens):** professional management decides based on specific factors. Often combination of cover plus irrigation for protective effect.

Storage between uses:

Fold the cloth dry and store somewhere protected from UV when not in use. Polypropylene frost cloth typically lasts 3 to 5 seasons before UV damage starts to degrade it. Treated cloth lasts longer.

Don't store wet or damp — degrades the material faster and can develop mildew issues that transfer to the lawn next time it's used.

For most NZ home lawns:

Frost cloth is occasional use, not routine. Mature established lawns rarely need it. New lawns started in cool conditions benefit during the first few weeks. Beyond that, the lawn manages frost on its own.
