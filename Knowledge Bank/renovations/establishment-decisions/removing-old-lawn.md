# How do I remove an existing lawn?

Three main methods, each suited to different situations.

Method 1: Glyphosate kill (the standard approach)

Process:
1. Mow the existing lawn short and water it well 2 to 3 days before application.
2. Apply glyphosate (Roundup or similar) at label rate to actively growing turf.
3. Wait 10 to 14 days for full kill.
4. Apply a second application if any green is showing.
5. Rake out the dead material.
6. Cultivate (rotary hoe, dig, till) to break up the surface.
7. Soil prep for new lawn establishment.

Pros: thorough, kills weed seeds in the surface, prepares the area properly.
Cons: 2 to 3 week timeline, glyphosate use restrictions in some councils, chemical exposure considerations.

Method 2: Sod cutting (physical removal)

Process:
1. Hire a sod cutter (a wheeled machine that slices under the turf at root depth).
2. Cut the lawn into manageable strips and roll them up.
3. Remove the rolled sod (compost it or send to green waste).
4. Cultivate the exposed soil.
5. Soil prep for new lawn establishment.

Pros: faster than chemical kill, no waiting for kill period, no chemistry.
Cons: physical labour or hireage cost, leaves behind weed seeds in soil, removes some topsoil with the sod.

Method 3: Smothering (slow but chemistry-free)

Process:
1. Cover the existing lawn with cardboard or thick black plastic.
2. Weigh down with soil, mulch, or stones.
3. Wait 6 to 12 weeks for the lawn underneath to die from light starvation.
4. Remove the cover, cultivate, prep for new lawn.

Pros: no chemistry, no labour-intensive removal.
Cons: long timeline, doesn't kill deep-rooted weeds, leaves dead material underneath.

Which to choose:
- Small lawn (under 100 m²): sod cutter or smothering both work.
- Large lawn: glyphosate kill most cost-effective.
- No chemistry preference: smothering or sod cutter.
- Time pressure: sod cutter.
- Heavy weed contamination: glyphosate kill (handles weeds and lawn together).

Glyphosate considerations in NZ:
- Currently registered for home use.
- Some councils have restrictions on use in residential areas - check yours.
- Read label, wear appropriate PPE.
- Wait the full kill period before cultivating.

After removal:
- Soil prep is the next stage (see soil-prep Q&As).
- New lawn establishment via seed or ready lawn (see seed-vs-ready-lawn Q&A).
- Timing affects success - autumn establishment after summer removal is the usual programme.
