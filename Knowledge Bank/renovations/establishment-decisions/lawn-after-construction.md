# How do I establish a lawn after a new build?

Post-construction sites have specific challenges that affect lawn establishment. Worth knowing before you start.

Common issues:
- Topsoil stripped during build, lawn going onto subsoil.
- Severe compaction from building traffic.
- Buried building debris (concrete, brick, timber off-cuts).
- Contamination (oil from machinery, plaster, cement washings).
- Variable soil depth across the site.

Assessment:
- Dig a test hole 300 mm deep in several spots.
- What's the topsoil depth?
- Is the subsoil heavily compacted?
- Any debris below the surface?
- Any obvious contamination?

For sites with stripped topsoil:
- Import topsoil to bring depth to at least 150 mm.
- Quality matters - get screened, weed-free topsoil from a reputable supplier.
- Estimate cost: $30 to $60 per cubic metre delivered, plus spreading labour.
- Don't skimp - thin topsoil means a thin lawn forever.

For sites with severe compaction:
- Cultivate or rotary hoe to 150 mm depth before adding topsoil.
- Without breaking up the compacted layer, water won't drain and roots won't develop.
- Major projects may need ripping with a tractor implement.

For sites with buried debris:
- Excavate and remove debris before laying topsoil.
- Concrete chunks, brick, plaster all interfere with rooting and drainage.
- Time-consuming but pays off long-term.

For contaminated sites:
- Oil spots: excavate and dispose of contaminated soil through licensed disposal.
- Plaster and cement runoff: heavy lime in the soil, may need additional sulphur to correct pH.
- Test if unsure of contamination level or type.

Soil preparation:
1. Address subsoil compaction with cultivation or ripping.
2. Import and spread good quality topsoil to at least 150 mm depth.
3. Cultivate the surface 50 mm for seedbed.
4. Apply NZLA Starter granular and incorporate.
5. Final level with rake or harrow.
6. Apply NZLA Aerate if soil seems borderline on drainage.

Establishment:
- Seed at standard rate for chosen species.
- Or lay ready lawn after preparation.
- NZLA Liquid Starter through the establishment phase.
- NZLA Humic+ supports developing soil biology (the soil from the supplier may have low biological activity).

Common mistakes:
- Inadequate topsoil depth (lawn grows for a year then declines).
- Skipping cultivation of the subsoil (water can't get past the compaction layer).
- Sowing too early (before the site has settled and finished off any post-build movement).

Timing:
- Better to delay lawn establishment until the site is properly prepared than rush a poor result.
- Most new builds in NZ end up with lawn 3 to 12 months after move-in.
- Coordinate timing with seasons for best establishment.

For new home owners, budget the lawn establishment properly. Trying to establish a lawn on a poorly-prepared post-build site is a recipe for years of frustration. Spend the money on proper soil prep before sowing.
