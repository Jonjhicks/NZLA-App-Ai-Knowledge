# Should I sow seed or lay ready lawn?

Different trade-offs across cost, time, and result.

Seed:
- Cost: low to moderate. Premium NZLA cultivars cost more per kg but cover large areas.
- Time to lawn: 8 to 12 weeks from sowing to first use.
- Species choice: full range available (premium cultivars, blends, mixes).
- Skill required: moderate. Soil prep matters, watering through establishment matters.
- Risk: weather and bird damage during establishment.
- Best for: medium to large areas, owners who can manage establishment care, full species choice.

Ready lawn:
- Cost: high. Roughly 5 to 10x the cost of seed per square metre.
- Time to lawn: 2 to 6 weeks from laying to first use.
- Species choice: limited to what the grower stocks (usually couch or kikuyu in upper NI, ryegrass blends elsewhere).
- Skill required: lower. Lay and water. Less variable than seed.
- Risk: lower establishment risk if installed properly.
- Best for: small areas, immediate result needed, less establishment skill or time available.

Decision tree:
- Less than 50 m² and want a result fast: ready lawn.
- 50 to 200 m² and can wait 8 weeks: seed often more cost-effective.
- Over 200 m²: seed by a wide margin on cost.
- Sale of house in 6 weeks: ready lawn for the certain timeline.
- Specific premium species (MDR, RSR, fine fescue): seed - ready lawn growers don't carry these.

Cost comparison (approximate, varies by region and supplier):
- Seed at $50-80 per kg, 25-35 g/m² sowing rate, $1.50 to $2.80 per m².
- Plus NZLA Starter and Liquid Starter establishment package, add $1 to $2 per m².
- Plus soil prep cost varies.
- Ready lawn at $10 to $18 per m² installed.

Total seed-route cost typically $3 to $5 per m² of lawn delivered. Ready lawn $10 to $18 per m².

Other considerations:
- Ready lawn comes with whatever soil is under it, which may or may not be ideal for your site.
- Seeding lets you prep the soil exactly how you want it.
- Ready lawn is heavy and hard to install on steep slopes.
- Both need watering through establishment - the difference is how long.

For most NZ home lawn establishment projects, seed gives better value at acceptable timeline if the owner can manage the 6 to 8 week establishment period properly. Ready lawn is the right choice when time or skill is limited.
