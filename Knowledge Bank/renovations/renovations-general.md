# Lawn Renovation — Overview

## Light renovation vs full renovation

These are different levels of intervention with different triggers, timelines, and costs.

Light renovation covers the ongoing maintenance work that keeps an established lawn in good shape: scarifiers or aerating (once a year or every two years), seeding-and-over-seeding thin areas, topdressing for levelling and soil amendment, and recovery support with NZLA Starter. The lawn stays in place throughout. This isn't renovation in the heavy sense — it's active maintenance.

Full renovation is a complete rebuild. Kill the existing grass, fix the soil, re-establish from seed or ready lawn. It's the right answer when the existing lawn has problems that can't be addressed within its current state: over 30 to 40 percent weed cover, wrong species for the site, severe thatch over 25 mm, significant drainage-and-soil-moisture or levelling issues, or chronic  and pest problems with underlying causes.

## How often does each type happen?

Most lawns with decent management need light renovation work every year or two. Annual or biennial scarifiers and aerating, annual seeding-and-over-seeding of thin areas if needed, periodic topdressing. This is normal maintenance, not a sign of a failing lawn.

Full renovation on a well-managed home lawn happens once every 8 to 15 years, if at all. It's triggered by a specific problem accumulating past the point of gradual management, not by scheduled timing.

The realistic annual cycle on a well-maintained home lawn looks like this:

Spring (September through November): light scarify or aerate as needed, pre-em Blockade on warm-season turf, overseed thin spots with NZLA Perennial Rye, All Seasons N+ for the spring push, Iron+ between granulars.

Summer (December through February): All Seasons base feed every 6 to 8 weeks, foliar top-ups with Charger and Iron+, Wetter 3W if dry patch is an issue, standard mowing programme.

Autumn (March through May): All Seasons autumn feed, pre-em Blockade for poa annua, light scarify or aerate as needed, topdress if levelling work is on the list, Fungus Pro as preventative as conditions cool.

Winter (June through August): foliar programme with Charger and Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks, Fungus Pro routine, reduced mowing.

## Full renovation process

Week 0: Apply glyphosate (with NZLA Sticker as adjuvant) to the whole area with active growth. Most grass dies within 7 to 14 days.

Week 2: Second glyphosate pass to catch survivors and new germinations.

Week 4: Remove dead vegetation. Rotary hoe or fork the top 100 to 150 mm. Remove rocks and debris. Amend the soil (sand for clay drainage-and-soil-moisture, compost for low-organic soils). Level the surface.

Week 5: Apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m², rake in. Sow seed or lay ready lawn.

Weeks 6 to 12: Consistent watering, no traffic, NZLA Liquid Starter foliar at 4 to 5 weeks.

Month 3 onward: transition to All Seasons granular, standard maintenance.

Total timeline: roughly 12 to 16 weeks from start to an established mature-looking lawn.

## Cost

Light renovation DIY: $100 to $400 for a typical home lawn. Equipment hire (scarifier $80 to $150 per day, aerator $80 to $120 per day), topdressing material, and NZLA Starter make up most of that. Professional: $500 to $1,500.

Full renovation DIY (200 to 400 m² home lawn): $300 to $800 in product and materials. Glyphosate, rotary hoe hire, soil amendments, NZLA Starter, seed or ready lawn, Liquid Starter. Professional full renovation: $3,000 to $8,000. Ready lawn installation adds significantly to that — the material alone runs $15 to $25 per m² for standard varieties.

The size of the lawn, the existing condition, the species choice, and how much soil work is needed all affect the final number.

## The honest answer for most home owners

Light work every one to two years, plus a major intervention every 5 to 10 years when something specific has accumulated past the point of maintenance management. Don't renovate a lawn that's working well.
