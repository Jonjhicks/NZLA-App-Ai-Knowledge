# How do I prepare for overseeding?

Five-step preparation. Each step makes the difference between seed that takes and seed that wastes.

**1. Mow short.** The day before overseeding, mow the existing lawn down to 20 mm. Short grass exposes soil between plants and gives new seedlings light to germinate. Long grass shades out new seedlings before they establish.

If the lawn is 50 mm or higher, don't drop straight to 20 mm in one cut (violates the third rule and stresses the existing lawn). Bring it down over 2 or 3 mows in the week before overseeding.

**2. Open the soil surface.** New seed needs contact with soil to germinate. Seed sitting on top of thatch or established grass doesn't germinate.

Light scarifying with a powered scarifier set to shallow depth. Or rake hard with a metal-tined rake. The goal is to break up the surface and expose small patches of soil between existing plants without destroying the existing lawn.

For larger overseeding jobs, a slit seeder cuts shallow slots and drops seed into them in one operation — combines surface preparation and seeding.

**3. Apply NZLA Starter granular** at 1.5 kg per 100m². The phosphorus drives root development in the new seedlings, the trace elements support seedling vigour. Rake lightly into the surface.

This is the same Starter product you'd use for a new lawn — don't substitute standard maintenance granular here.

**4. Sow the seed at the right rate.** 2 kg per 100m² for ryegrass varieties for overseeding (vs 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for new lawn establishment). Match the species to the existing lawn.

Distribute evenly. Walk a half-rate pass in one direction, then a half-rate pass at right angles to catch any misses.

**5. Light rake.** Cover the seed in the top 5 to 10 mm of soil. Seed exposed on the surface dries out and birds eat it. Lightly raked in, it stays in contact with moist soil and germinates.

Roll the area if you have a small lawn roller. Improves seed-to-soil contact further.

What NOT to do:

Don't apply pre-emergent within 4 to 6 months of overseeding. Pre-em kills new seed.

Don't apply heavy nitrogen feed at the time of sowing. The existing lawn responds first and out-competes the new seedlings.

Don't overseed onto compacted ground without addressing the compaction first. Compacted patches kill new seedlings.

Don't skip the watering plan. New seedlings need consistent moisture for 10 to 14 days.

After sowing:

Water consistently for 10 to 14 days until seedlings establish. Light frequent watering rather than deep occasional watering.

Hold off mowing for 4 to 6 weeks until new seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm.

Liquid Starter foliar at 4 to 5 weeks supports establishment.

Resume normal maintenance once the lawn has had 2 to 3 mows post-overseeding.

Timing:

Autumn (March through April) is the best window. Spring (September through October) is second best.

A well-prepared overseeding produces visible thickening within 4 to 6 weeks and full integration within 8 to 12 weeks.
