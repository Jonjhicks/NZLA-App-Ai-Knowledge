# How do I overseed an existing lawn?

Five steps that make the difference between seed that takes and seed that wastes.

**Time it right.** Autumn (late February through April) is the prime window — soil temperatures still warm, less weed pressure, lawn establishing before winter. Spring (September through October) is the second window. Don't overseed in summer (heat stress kills seedlings) or winter (no growth).

**Prep the surface.** Mow short (down to 20 mm) the day before overseeding. Rake hard or lightly scarify to break the surface and expose soil between existing plants. New seed needs soil contact to germinate — it won't germinate sitting on top of a thatch layer.

**Apply NZLA Starter granular** at 1.5 kg per 100m² before sowing. Phosphorus drives root development in the new seedlings, trace elements support seedling vigour. Rake it in lightly.

**Sow the seed.** For ryegrass varieties, 2 kg per 100m² is the standard oversowing rate (less than new lawn rate of 3 to 4 kg because you're filling gaps not starting from scratch). Choose seed appropriate to the existing lawn — match the species. Rake the seed in lightly so it sits in the top 5 to 10 mm of soil.

**Water consistently.** Keep the surface moist for 10 to 14 days until seedlings establish. Light frequent watering through this window. After seedlings are up, back off to deep less frequent watering.

Hold off mowing until new seedlings are 60 to 80 mm. First mow at the higher setting with sharp blades.

Liquid Starter as a foliar 4 to 5 weeks after sowing supports the new seedlings into maturity.

Don't apply pre-emergent within 4 to 6 weeks of overseeding — it will kill the new seed.
