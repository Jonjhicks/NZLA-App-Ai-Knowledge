# New Lawn — First Year Programme

Establishing a new lawn from seed. This covers the complete first-year programme from soil preparation through to a mature, self-maintaining turf.

Detailed renovation step-by-step: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/lawn-renovation-guide/

---

## Before sowing — soil preparation

This is where most new lawns succeed or fail. Seed in poorly prepared soil produces poor results that take years to correct.

**Soil test.** Test before doing anything else. Hills Labs or similar NZ soil testing service. A basic test ($40 to $60) tells you pH, phosphorus, potassium, and organic matter. You'll know what you're working with rather than guessing. Get results 3 to 4 weeks before planned sowing.

**Address pH.** Target pH 6.0 to 6.5. Below 5.8: apply agricultural lime at 50 to 100 g/m² worked into the soil. Above 7.0: sulphur at 20 to 30 g/m². Both take time to change pH — work into the profile and allow at least 2 weeks before sowing.

**Cultivate.** Rotary hoe or fork to 100 to 150 mm. Break up core-aeration, remove large stones and debris. Good cultivation is the foundation for root penetration.

**Starter fertiliser.** NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg/100m² raked into the top 50 mm of the prepared soil. High phosphorus drives root development from day one. This is not optional — new seedlings need this nutrient availability immediately.

**Level and roll.** Rake to a fine level tilth, remove high spots and fill low spots. Light rolling compresses the seed bed — seed-to-soil contact is critical for germination. After rolling, footprints should only compress the surface 10 to 15 mm.

---

## Sowing day

**Species choice.** Match species to your region and use. Full guide: basics/lawn-choices article. For most of NZ: NZLA Perennial Rye or appropriate fescue blend.

**Rate.** 25 to 35 g/m² for a new lawn on prepared soil. Divide seed into two equal lots, sow at right angles to each other. Cross-pattern sowing ensures even coverage.

**Pre-emergent (optional but recommended for ryegrass/tall fescue).** NZLA Etho and/or Meso on day of sowing, watered in. Creates a chemical barrier that targets poa annua and some broadleaf  before they can establish with the new lawn. DO NOT use on fine fescue at sowing — Meso damages fine fescue seedlings.

**Roll again.** Light roll after sowing to push seed into soil contact.

**First irrigation-general.** Water to 5 mm depth on sowing day. Don't create puddles or runoff — just saturate the surface.

---

## Weeks 1 to 3 — germination

**Irrigation is everything.** Keep the top 10 to 15 mm of soil consistently moist. Two light irrigations per day in warm, dry conditions — morning and afternoon. Germination fails if the seedbed dries out even briefly. One dry day can kill the whole batch.

In mild overcast conditions, once daily may be sufficient. In wind or heat, three times daily. Watch the soil surface — if it looks dry, water.

**Germination timeline.** NZLA Perennial Rye: 7 to 14 days. Tall fescue: 10 to 14 days. Fine fescue: 14 to 21 days. Warm soil speeds this; cool soil slows it.

**Don't walk on it.** Stay off until the seedlings are established.

---

## Weeks 3 to 6 — seedling establishment

Seedlings visible and growing. Shift irrigation-general from twice daily to once daily deep watering — start training roots to grow down.

**First mow: when seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm.** Don't mow earlier — the root system isn't established enough to handle the stress. Set mower high — just tip the tops at 50 to 60 mm, removing no more than 20 mm. Don't bag the first mow if clippings are light — leave on lawn for moisture retention. Remove if heavy.

**Light irrigation-general after first mow** to reduce shock.

**Week 4 to 5 — Liquid Starter.** Now the seedlings have enough leaf area for foliar uptake. Apply NZLA Liquid Starter at 100 to 200 mL/100m². The phosphite phosphorus, slow-release N, and kelp drive root development at the exact stage it matters most.

---

## Months 2 to 3 — active establishment

The lawn is growing, mowing regularly, starting to look like a lawn.

**Mowing frequency:** as needed to maintain 1/3 rule. For actively growing ryegrass in warm conditions, this could be weekly.

**6 weeks after sowing:** NZLA All Seasons at 2 to 2.5 kg/100m². The first granular application. The Starter nutrition is beginning to deplete and the plants need a broader feed.

**Continue Liquid Starter:** Second application 8 to 10 weeks after sowing at 200 mL/100m².

**Weeds:** New lawns get . Don't panic. Wait until the grass has had at least 2 mows before applying any herbicide. On ryegrass/tall fescue lawns: Gold for broadleaf  after the 2-mow minimum. Meso post-emergent for browntop if it appears.

---

## Months 4 to 6 — approaching maturity

The lawn is beginning to look established. Tiller density increasing, coverage filling in.

**Fertiliser programme:** Shift to standard programme — All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks, Charger + Iron+ fortnightly foliar. See the appropriate annual programme article for your region and season.

**Wetting agents:** NZLA Wetter 3W if dry patch is developing.

**Bare or thin patches:** Oversow at 15 to 20 g/m². New lawns commonly have thin areas where germination was inconsistent or foot traffic occurred during establishment.

---

## Month 6 onwards

A well-established lawn on a proper programme. The first anniversary of sowing typically marks the transition from "new lawn management" to "mature lawn maintenance."

The autumn following an autumn sowing is when the lawn typically reaches its first genuine quality state — one full growing season behind it, root system established, species density at or near potential.

---

## Common establishment failures

**Drying out during germination.** Single most common cause of poor new lawn establishment. Two days without irrigation-general in warm weather and the germination fails. Plan irrigation-general before sowing.

**Sowing too thin.** Fewer plants means more gaps and more time for  to establish. Use the full recommended rate.

**First mow too early or too low.** Seedlings at 40 mm with a root system 20 mm deep can't handle the stress. Wait for 60 to 80 mm.

**Applying herbicide too early.** Herbicide on seedlings before they've established kills the new grass, not just the . Two mow minimum before any herbicide.

**Sowing in wrong soil temperatures.** Ryegrass germinates reliably between 14 and 25°C soil temperature. Below 12°C: poor and slow germination. Above 30°C: heat stress. Check soil temps before sowing — a basic soil thermometer costs $15.
