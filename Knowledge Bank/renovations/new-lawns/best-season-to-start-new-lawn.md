# What is the best season to start a new lawn?

Autumn (March through April) for most of NZ. Spring (September through October) is the second-best window.

Why autumn:

**Soil temperatures are still warm enough for fast germination** (12 to 18°C through autumn for most of the country). Cool-season grass germinates within 7 to 14 days.

**Air temperatures are mild.** Not too hot, not too cold. Seedlings don't suffer heat stress and have time to establish before winter.

**Rainfall picks up.** Natural rain through autumn supports establishment. Less reliance on irrigation than summer establishment.

**Weed pressure is lower than spring.** Summer-germinating weeds have finished their cycle. Winter weeds haven't fully fired up yet. Less weed competition for new seedlings.

**Winter establishment window.** The lawn has 4 to 6 weeks of active establishment then enters winter with developed root system. Spring brings rapid maturation.

**Less drought stress through summer.** Autumn-sown lawns face their first summer with a year's root development underneath, far better than spring-sown lawns hitting summer at 4 months of age.

Why spring is second-best:

**Reliable warming.** Soil temperatures climbing through 12 to 18°C through September-October. Good germination conditions.

**Long growing season ahead.** Spring-sown lawns have months of active growth to develop before winter slows things down.

**Issues:** Summer heat stress while the lawn is still young (under 4 months). Heavier weed pressure than autumn — summer annual weeds germinate alongside the new lawn.

When to AVOID starting a new lawn:

**Mid-summer (December-February).** Heat stress kills new seedlings. Establishment failure rate is high. Even with irrigation, success is patchy.

**Winter (June-August).** Soil temperatures below 10°C mean slow or no germination. Ready lawn establishes slowly. Frost damage potential on new growth.

**Drought conditions.** If rainfall isn't reliable and you can't commit to consistent irrigation, postpone the establishment to a wetter time.

For warm-season grass (kikuyu, couch):

The timing is shifted toward warmer months. Kikuyu and couch need soil temperatures above 15°C minimum, ideally above 18°C, to germinate. October through early February for most of the country. Don't sow warm-season grass into cool autumn or winter conditions.

Regional adjustments:

**Auckland and upper North Island.** Earlier spring (early September) and later autumn (into May) windows than the rest of the country. Some flexibility for summer establishment with consistent irrigation.

**Wellington, Canterbury and southern regions.** Tighter windows. Spring (late September through October) and autumn (March through early April) only.

**Otago and Southland.** Even tighter windows. Spring and autumn windows are short. Plan establishment carefully.

Practical advice:

Pick your window 1 to 2 months in advance. Order seed and starter products. Plan the soil prep, sowing, and watering schedule. Have everything ready before the right conditions arrive rather than scrambling once you spot them.
