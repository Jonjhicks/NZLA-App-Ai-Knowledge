# What is the best way to start a new lawn from scratch?

Five steps, in order.

**Soil prep first.** Clear existing growth, dig over the top 100 to 150 mm, remove rocks and debris, and roll or rake the surface flat. The single biggest reason new lawns disappoint is rushed soil prep. Time spent here saves years of frustration.

**Pick the right seed for your situation.** NZLA Perennial Rye for general home lawn use, RSR Ryegrass if wear recovery matters, Dark Perennial Rye or MDR for premium aesthetic, Tall Fescue blends for drought tolerance and partial shade, Fine Fescue for shaded low-input situations, Kikuyu for warm climates in the upper North Island.

**Apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² at soil prep, before sowing.** Phosphorus drives early root development, trace elements support seedling vigour. Rake it lightly into the top 5 to 10 mm of soil.

**Sow at 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for ryegrass varieties.** Rake the seed in lightly so it sits in the top 5 to 10 mm of soil. Roll if you have one available — improves seed-to-soil contact for better germination.

**Water consistently for the first 2 to 3 weeks** to keep the surface moist. First mow once seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm height. Set the mower at the higher cut setting, sharp blade, only take the top third off.

Liquid Starter as a foliar at 4 to 5 weeks supports the seedlings through the vulnerable establishment window. Transition to All Seasons granular as the maintenance base after 8 to 12 weeks, once the lawn is established.
