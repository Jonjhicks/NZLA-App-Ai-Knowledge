# Can I lay ready lawn over an existing lawn?

Generally no. The standard professional approach is to kill the existing lawn first, prepare the soil properly, then lay the new lawn over prepared bare ground.

Why laying over existing lawn doesn't work well:

**Air gap and rooting failure.** The new turf cap sits on top of the existing grass canopy with a layer of foliage between the new root mat and the underlying soil. Roots can't reach the soil through that intervening grass layer. The new lawn doesn't anchor properly and dies within weeks.

**Levelling problems.** Existing lawn surface is rarely as level as freshly prepared soil. Laying ready lawn over uneven existing turf produces a bumpy, uneven new lawn that can't be levelled afterward.

**Competition and disease.** Two grass populations competing in the same space causes problems. The buried existing lawn dies but rots underneath, creating disease habitat. The new lawn struggles in the contaminated zone above.

**Long-term failure.** Even where over-laying appears to work initially, the result is typically a thin patchy lawn that fails within months and needs replacing properly. Money wasted on the over-laying attempt.

The right approach:

**Kill the existing lawn first.** Spray glyphosate (typically twice with 2 weeks between to catch regrowth) to ensure complete kill. Wait for the existing grass to fully die before proceeding.

**Remove the dead grass and roots.** Scrape off the dead vegetation, or rotary hoe to break up the existing lawn and soil mix.

**Prepare the soil properly.** Level the surface, remove rocks and debris, work in any needed amendments (sand for clay soils, organic matter for sandy soils). Roll or rake to a smooth firm surface.

**Apply NZLA Starter granular** at 1.5 kg per 100m² before laying.

**Lay the ready lawn over the prepared soil.** Standard ready lawn installation process — slabs butted tight together, no gaps.

**Water and establish properly.** Same care as for any new ready lawn — consistent watering for the first 4 to 6 weeks, no traffic, first mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

When over-laying might appear in some lawn care advice:

Some old or informal advice still suggests over-laying as a shortcut. The advice is wrong. Professional installers, NZLA recommendations, and modern turf practice all agree: kill the existing lawn first.

Exception — patching small bare areas:

For small bare patches in an existing lawn (under 1 m²), small ready lawn squares can be laid over the patches after removing the dead grass and leveling the patch. This is patching, not over-laying.

The patch ready lawn integrates with the surrounding established lawn at the edges and roots down into the prepared soil underneath. Standard patch repair technique.

For complete lawn replacement, do the renovation properly. Over-laying isn't a viable shortcut.
