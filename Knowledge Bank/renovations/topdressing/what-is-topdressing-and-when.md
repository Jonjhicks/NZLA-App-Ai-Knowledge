# What is topdressing and when should I do it?

Topdressing is applying a thin layer of soil or sand over the lawn surface, then working it down between the grass plants. The goal is one or more of: levelling out bumps and dips, amending the soil profile, smothering thatch (lightly), or filling in over a renovation.

The material varies by purpose:

**Pure sand** for sports turf, golf greens, fine cut lawns. Improves drainage, builds a true surface for low cutting heights, dries fast after rain.

**Sandy loam** for home lawn levelling. Sand mixed with a small amount of fine soil. Levels without smothering the existing grass, settles into the surface.

**Compost or rich topsoil** for soils that need organic matter. Useful on sandy or low-organic-matter soils but can introduce weed seeds — buy from a reputable supplier.

When to topdress:

**After core aeration** — fill the holes left by the cores with sand or sandy loam to gradually amend the soil profile.

**For levelling bumps and dips** under 10 mm — gradually fill the dips with several thin topdressings over a season.

**After scarifying** — light topdressing protects the exposed crowns and adds organic matter back if you've removed thatch.

**Pre-overseeding** — thin sandy loam layer gives the new seed soil contact.

How much: never more than 5 mm of topdressing material at a time. More than that smothers the existing grass. Work it down into the canopy with a stiff brush or drag mat.

Time it for active growing season (spring or autumn) so the grass recovers quickly.
