# How often should I topdress my lawn?

Depends on what you're trying to achieve and the lawn's current state.

**For general maintenance on a healthy lawn — every 2 to 3 years.** Light topdressing combined with aeration to refresh the soil surface and address minor unevenness. Not a frequent intervention on lawns that don't need it.

**For drainage improvement on clay lawns — 2 to 3 times per year for a few years.** Aggressive sand amendment to gradually shift the soil profile. Each application 3 to 5 mm. Multi-year programme — meaningful change takes 2 to 3 years of consistent topdressing.

**For levelling minor unevenness — 2 to 3 applications across one or two growing seasons.** Each application fills the dips and evens out the surface progressively. Once the lawn is level, drop back to maintenance frequency.

**For organic matter amendment on sandy soils — annually with compost-rich material.** Builds soil biology and water retention over years.

**For sand-based root zones (golf turf, sports turf) — every 4 to 8 weeks during growing season.** Maintains the sand profile against thatch accumulation and soil structure changes. Professional turf operation pattern.

**For premium home lawns or showcase turf — annually or biannually.** Supports the higher presentation standard and aggressive growth.

**For low-maintenance home lawns — rarely or never.** Many home lawns go years without topdressing and function fine. Topdressing is intervention work, not routine maintenance for every lawn.

What drives the decision:

**Soil type.** Clay lawns benefit from more frequent topdressing for drainage. Loam lawns less. Sandy lawns may need topdressing only for organic matter amendment, not drainage.

**Current lawn condition.** Lawn that's struggling with drainage, unevenness, or compaction benefits from a topdressing programme. Lawn that's thriving doesn't need intervention.

**Use level.** Heavy-use lawns (sports, kids and dogs) benefit from more frequent intervention to address ongoing compaction and wear.

**Presentation standard.** Showcase lawns held to a high standard get more attention. Standard home lawns get less.

**Budget and time.** Topdressing is real work — material cost, delivery, application labour, recovery support. Not free.

**The "I don't need to" rule.**

If the lawn is healthy, drains well, looks the way you want it to, and isn't showing signs of problems — you don't need to topdress. Money and effort better spent elsewhere.

Watch for indicators that topdressing would help:

Water sits on the surface after rain.

Lawn feels spongy underfoot (thatch building).

Minor unevenness developing.

Recent aeration or scarifying has left rough surfaces.

Soil profile visibly poor when you check (e.g. clay subsoil where topsoil has eroded).

Timing:

Spring (September through November) or autumn (March through April). Active growing season supports recovery — the grass grows up through the new material.

Don't topdress in summer heat (lawn stressed) or winter (slow recovery).

Recovery support:

NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² before or during topdressing supports the recovery. Light watering to settle the material. Hold off mowing for a week.

Honest answer for most home lawn owners:

A light topdressing every 2 to 3 years combined with aeration is the typical maintenance pattern. More frequent only if there's a specific problem you're addressing or you're managing a premium lawn to a high standard.
