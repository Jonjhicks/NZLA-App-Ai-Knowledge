# What is the best material for topdressing?

Depends on what you want to achieve. Four materials for four different purposes.

**Sandy loam (most common for general home lawn use).** Mix of sand (typically 60 to 70 percent) and fine loam (30 to 40 percent). Levels well, settles into the canopy without smothering existing grass, gradually improves soil drainage over time on clay soils.

Use for: general topdressing, minor levelling, post-aeration fill, soil amendment on heavy soils.

Cost: $40 to $80 per cubic metre delivered (varies by region and quantity).

**Pure sand (specific situations).** Coarse builder's sand or specialised turf sand. Amends the soil profile toward sand-based drainage. Used heavily on clay soils where drainage improvement is the priority.

Use for: drainage improvement on clay lawns, sand-based root zone maintenance (golf turf, sports turf), aggressive drainage amendment.

Cost: $30 to $60 per cubic metre delivered.

Don't use pure sand on already sandy soils — makes drainage worse not better.

**Compost or organic-rich topsoil (organic matter amendment).** For soils that are low in organic matter — sandy soils, pumice soils, recent fill where topsoil was minimal. Adds soil biology, nutrient cycling capacity, water retention.

Use for: low-organic soils that need biological function and water retention improvement.

Cost: $60 to $120 per cubic metre delivered.

Buy from reputable suppliers — cheap compost often contains weed seeds, pests, or contamination.

**Loam or topsoil (general soil building).** Real soil rather than sand. Levels well, supports grass directly, builds up soil depth over time.

Use for: building up soil depth on lawns where the existing soil profile is shallow, levelling significant dips, general soil amendment.

Cost: $80 to $150 per cubic metre depending on quality and source.

Practical decision guide:

**Average home lawn with minor unevenness:** sandy loam.

**Clay lawn with drainage issues:** sand or sandy loam, depending on how aggressive the amendment needs to be.

**Sandy lawn that dries out fast:** compost or organic-rich topsoil.

**Premium showcase lawn or sport turf:** specific sand specifications based on the lawn's existing profile.

**Lawn over recent construction with thin or poor topsoil:** quality loam topsoil to build the profile.

Quality check before buying:

Ask the supplier for a small sample before committing to bulk delivery. Check for:

**Texture.** Falls apart in hand for sandy loam. Doesn't form a hard ball for sand. Crumbles with organic matter visible for compost-amended material.

**Weed content.** No visible weed seeds, roots or material that suggests contamination.

**Smell.** Fresh earthy smell. Sour, anaerobic, or chemical smell indicates poor quality.

**Wetness.** Workable moisture content, not soaking wet or bone dry.

Don't buy from suppliers who can't or won't provide a sample. Cheap material from unknown sources causes more problems than the cost saving justifies.

Quantity calculation:

For a 5 mm topdressing across 100m²: roughly 0.5 cubic metres. Round up for delivery — most suppliers don't deliver fractions of a cubic metre.

For a typical 300m² home lawn with light topdressing: about 1.5 cubic metres per application.

Application:

Spread evenly. Work down into the canopy with a stiff brush or drag mat. Don't exceed 5 mm per application — more smothers the existing grass.
