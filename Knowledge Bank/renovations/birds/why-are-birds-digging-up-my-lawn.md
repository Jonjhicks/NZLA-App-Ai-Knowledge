# Why are birds digging up my lawn?

They're looking for grubs. Birds tearing up a lawn is one of the most reliable diagnostic signs that you have a soil-dwelling pest problem — grass grub, black beetle larvae, billbugs, porina, or armyworm caterpillars in some cases.

Which birds and how to tell:

**Blackbirds and starlings** work over a lawn methodically, flipping small chunks of turf to expose grubs underneath. They leave a characteristic pattern of small disturbed patches across affected areas. Most active in early morning and late afternoon.

**Magpies** are bigger and do more damage. They'll grab whole sections of turf and flip them over. The damage looks more dramatic and the bare patches can be larger.

**Pukekos** in rural and lifestyle block areas will tear up substantial sections of turf going after pasture pests. More damage than typical garden birds.

What this tells you:

Birds don't randomly attack lawns. They're hunting food. If they're working a specific area of your lawn consistently, there are grubs in the soil there in numbers worth a bird's effort. Bird presence is often the first sign of pest pressure — well before the lawn visibly declines from root damage.

How to confirm:

Lift a 30 cm square of turf in an area the birds have been working. Cream-coloured C-shaped grubs about 15 to 20 mm are typical grass grub or black beetle larvae. Brown segmented caterpillars are porina or armyworm.

More than 15 grubs per 0.1m² (a square the size of a dinner plate) warrants treatment.

Treatment:

NZLA Grub+ (chlorantraniliprole 200 g/L) is on label for grass grub, black beetle, billbugs, porina and caterpillars. Up to 6 months control from a single application. Maximum 1 application per year.

For soil-dwelling targets, irrigate 6 mm immediately after application to move the chemical into the soil where the larvae are.

The bird side:

The birds aren't the problem — they're the messenger. Once the grubs are controlled, the birds move on. Don't try to deter the birds while leaving the underlying pest. The birds are showing you exactly where to apply treatment.

Healthy thick lawns handle minor grub pressure without bird attention. If birds are regularly working your lawn, the pest pressure has reached a threshold worth addressing.
