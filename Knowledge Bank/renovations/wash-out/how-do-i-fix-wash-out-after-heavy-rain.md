# How do I fix wash out after heavy rain?

Depends on what got washed out and how much damage was done.

**For seed wash out (recent sowing affected by heavy rain):**

Wait 7 to 10 days. Check for germination in the original sowing area. Often some seed remained and germinates despite heavy rain.

Assess actual damage. Visible erosion channels, bare patches, or missing seed where you can see it didn't take?

Patch repair the bare areas. Loosen the top 20 mm of soil in affected patches. Apply NZLA Starter at 1.5 kg per 100m². Re-sow at 3 to 4 kg per 100m² with seed appropriate to the lawn type. Rake lightly. Water consistently.

If the entire sowing failed: full re-prep and resowing as for a new lawn establishment.

**For soil wash out (erosion of topsoil or fill material):**

Re-fill the eroded areas with soil matching the surrounding lawn surface. Sandy loam or topsoil for general lawn. Level to surrounding grade.

Apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² to the re-filled areas.

Sow seed to re-establish grass cover on the re-filled patches.

Stabilise the area against future wash out — review whether the original wash out indicates an ongoing drainage or slope issue that needs structural attention.

**For fertiliser wash out (granular application affected by heavy rain shortly after):**

If the wash out happened within 6 to 12 hours of application, much of the fertiliser may have moved into the soil before being washed away. Wait 7 to 10 days to see if the lawn responds normally.

If the lawn shows uneven response (some areas greening up, others showing no response), apply a top-up application at half the original rate to the under-responsive areas.

If the wash out happened immediately after application (within hours), reapply at full rate after the soil drains and surface dries. Don't double-up while the original is still in the soil.

**For established lawn damaged by flooding:**

Most established lawns recover from temporary flooding without long-term damage. The key is letting the soil drain and dry before resuming normal management.

Don't mow the lawn while it's still saturated or muddy. Tracking and damage from mowing wet ground compounds flood damage.

Once the lawn has drained and dried:

Assess for actual damage. Yellowing or dying patches where flood water sat for extended periods (24 hours or more) may need patch repair.

NZLA Liquid Starter foliar at 200 to 300 mL per 100m² supports recovery from stress.

Watch for disease pressure. Saturated conditions favour fungal disease. NZLA Fungus Pro as preventative if you see early signs of Fusarium or other waterlogging-related disease.

Address underlying drainage issues. Repeated flooding indicates a structural drainage problem worth investigating.

**For severely damaged areas (multiple flood events, extensive bare patches):**

Full renovation may be the realistic answer. Re-grade, re-prep, re-establish from seed or ready lawn. Address drainage as part of the renovation.

What NOT to do:

Don't try to walk on or work the lawn while it's still saturated. Compaction damage compounds the original problem.

Don't immediately re-fertilise heavily. Wait 7 to 10 days to see normal recovery before adding more inputs.

Don't ignore the underlying cause. Wash out is often a sign of drainage or slope issues that recur in future heavy rain.

Long-term prevention:

Improve drainage where possible. Address slope angle. Establish robust ground cover. Plan future sowing or major applications around weather forecasts.

For chronic wash-out areas, terracing, drainage interception, or accepting alternative ground cover (mulch, ground cover plants) on the worst-affected areas may be more practical than ongoing repair.
