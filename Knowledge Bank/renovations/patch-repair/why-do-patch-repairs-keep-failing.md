# Why do my patch repairs keep failing?

Five common reasons patch repairs fail. Usually one or two of these is the underlying issue.

**1. Underlying cause not addressed.** Bare patches don't appear by accident. If the cause is still active, repair attempts fail repeatedly.

**Pet urine.** Dogs creating new spots in roughly the same locations. Either move the dog routine, or flush new urine spots with water immediately to dilute the salt and nitrogen concentration.

**Wear.** Foot traffic in the same area. Either route traffic differently with paths or barriers, or accept that the spot needs ongoing renewal.

**Disease damage in a chronically vulnerable spot.** Fix the conditions (shade, drainage, watering) before repairing.

**Grub damage.** Treat with NZLA Grub+ before repair, otherwise the grubs eat the new seedlings' roots.

**Compaction.** Compacted patches won't support healthy growth. Aerate the patch before seeding.

**2. Inadequate soil prep.** Sowing seed onto unprepared bare ground produces patchy weak establishment. Properly preparing the patch — loosening the top 20 mm of soil, removing dead material, adding NZLA Starter at 1.5 kg/100m² — gives the seed proper soil contact.

**3. Inconsistent watering.** Seedlings need consistent surface moisture for 10 to 14 days. Drying out for a single day kills emerging seedlings. Most patch repair failures trace back to watering failures during establishment.

**4. Wrong seed for the situation.** Sowing ryegrass into deep shade where the surrounding lawn is fine fescue — the wrong species can't establish where the rest of the lawn struggled. Match the species to the spot's conditions.

**5. Pre-emergent residual.** If Blockade was applied within the last 4 to 6 months on or near the patch, the residual chemistry kills new seedlings. Wait out the residual before re-attempting.

Diagnostic process for a repair that failed:

**Did the seed germinate at all?** If yes but seedlings died young, watering or pre-em residual is likely. If no, seed contact with soil was wrong, or the seed was killed by some other factor.

**Are surrounding areas thriving while just this spot fails?** Strong signal that the spot itself has an issue (compaction, drainage, shade, chemical residue) the surrounding lawn doesn't face.

**Has the patch repair failed in the same way previously?** Same underlying cause hasn't been addressed.

**Are weeds establishing in the same spot easily?** If yes, the soil supports plant growth — the issue is grass-specific, often species mismatch or chemistry residue.

How to repair successfully:

Identify and address the underlying cause first. Don't just keep sowing seed into a spot where the cause is still active.

Prep the soil properly. Loosen top 20 mm, remove dead material, apply NZLA Starter.

Sow appropriate seed at proper rate. 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for ryegrass varieties.

Water consistently for 10 to 14 days. Cover with hessian if needed to maintain moisture.

Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks as foliar support.

Patience. 8 to 12 weeks for full integration into the surrounding lawn.
