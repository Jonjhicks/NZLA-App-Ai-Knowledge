# Pre-germinating Seed

Pre-germinating means soaking seed in water for 2 to 3 days before sowing to start the germination process before the seed hits the ground.

## What it actually does

Standard ryegrass seed sown dry takes 7 to 14 days to show visible growth. Pre-germinated seed, with the germination process already underway, shows visible growth at 3 to 5 days after sowing — roughly 2 to 5 days faster.

That's the entire benefit. The total establishment timeline to a mature lawn is the same either way. The seedlings are equally fragile and need the same care. The sowing rates are the same.

Pre-germination does nothing to improve poor seed quality. Old, low-vigour, or contaminated seed gives the same poor result whether pre-germinated or not. Start with good certified seed regardless.

## Method

Soak in clean water (a bucket works) at room temperature for 24 to 36 hours. Drain and rinse. Soak again for another 24 to 36 hours, changing the water once.

After soaking, drain thoroughly and spread on a tarp in a cool dry place for a few hours to surface-dry. The seed should be tacky but not wet enough to clump.

Sow within 24 hours of the surface-dry step. Pre-germinated seed that sits for days starts to mould or develops visible shoot growth that makes it hard to broadcast evenly.

## When it's worth the effort

Time-pressured situations with a narrow weather window for establishment. Need to get the lawn to visible cover before forecast bad weather or before a deadline.

Cool conditions where standard germination is slow. Starting the biochemistry in warm water before the seed hits cool soil can meaningfully accelerate establishment in marginal autumn or early spring conditions.

Overseeding into competition with established grass. Pre-germinated seed has a head start that improves the chances of surviving the competition for light and resources.

Sports turf renovation under deadline pressure, where the timeline to cover is the limiting factor.

## When it's not worth the effort

For most home lawn establishment in autumn conditions, standard dry-seed sowing into properly prepped soil with consistent watering works fine. The 2 to 5 day advantage rarely justifies the process.

If the soaking can't be done properly (clean water, temperature control, proper drainage-and-soil-moisture) the risk of mould or rotting in the bag outweighs the benefit.

If you can't guarantee sowing within 24 hours of completing the soak, don't bother — pre-germinated seed can't just sit waiting.

Apply NZLA Starter granular at sowing regardless of method — the phosphorus and trace elements support root establishment in new seedlings whether the seed was pre-germinated or not.
