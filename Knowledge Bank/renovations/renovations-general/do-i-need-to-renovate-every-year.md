# Do I need to renovate my lawn every year?

Depends what you mean by renovate. Light annual maintenance — yes for most lawns. Full renovation — no, only when major issues require it.

**Light annual or biennial renovation (recommended for most lawns):**

Most well-maintained lawns benefit from some renovation work every year or two:

**Annual scarifying or aerating** (autumn or spring) on lawns with normal thatch build-up or moderate compaction. Keeps the lawn ahead of accumulating issues.

**Annual overseeding** if the lawn is thinning in spots. Sowing fresh seed at 1.5 to 2 kg per 100m² over thin areas keeps density high.

**Annual topdressing** to address minor unevenness and gradually amend the soil profile.

**Annual NZLA Starter application** combined with the renovation work supports recovery.

This light annual work is closer to "active maintenance" than "renovation" in the heavy sense. Doesn't require killing the lawn or starting over.

**Full renovation (rebuild from scratch):**

Only needed when major issues can't be addressed within the existing lawn:

Heavy weed infestation (over 30 percent weed cover).

Severe thatch over 25 mm that won't respond to scarifying.

Wrong species for the conditions.

Major drainage or grading issues requiring soil work.

Repeated disease or pest problems indicating fundamental issues with the lawn's situation.

For typical home lawns, full renovation happens once every 8 to 15 years (if at all) — usually triggered by an accumulating problem rather than scheduled timing.

**What an ongoing lawn maintenance year typically involves:**

Spring (Sep-Nov): Light scarify or aeration as needed. Spring pre-em Blockade (where appropriate). Overseed thin areas. All Seasons N+ for spring push. Iron+ between granulars.

Summer (Dec-Feb): All Seasons base feed every 6 to 8 weeks. Foliar top-ups (Charger, Iron+) between. Wetter if dry patch. Standard mowing programme.

Autumn (Mar-May): All Seasons autumn feed. Autumn pre-em Blockade for poa. Light scarify or aerate as needed. Topdress if levelling work needed. Fungus Pro starts as preventative.

Winter (Jun-Aug): Foliar programme (Charger, Iron+). Fungus Pro routine. Reduced mowing.

That's a typical year on a well-managed lawn. No full renovation — just steady maintenance with periodic light interventions.

**When more frequent intervention is needed:**

Sports turf and golf turf run on more aggressive renovation programmes — multiple aerations per year, regular topdressing, sometimes annual overseeding. The use level and presentation standard justify the work.

Heavily-used home lawns (kids and dogs in particular) may need more frequent patch repair and overseeding. Annual instead of biennial.

Premium showcase lawns get more attention generally — scarifying twice a year rather than annually, topdressing across multiple sessions, etc.

**When less is needed:**

Low-maintenance lawns in good condition can go 2 to 3 years between scarifying and aeration if nothing's indicating compaction or thatch buildup. Watch the lawn rather than the calendar.

Honest answer for most home lawn owners: light annual work plus a major intervention every 5 to 10 years is the typical cycle. Don't over-renovate a lawn that's working well.
