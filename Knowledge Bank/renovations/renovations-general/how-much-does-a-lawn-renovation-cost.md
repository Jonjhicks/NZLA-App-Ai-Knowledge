# How much does it cost to renovate a lawn?

Depends heavily on scope (light vs full renovation) and approach (DIY vs professional).

**Light renovation (scarifying, aerating, topdressing on existing lawn).**

DIY: $100 to $400 for a typical home lawn. Hire of scarifier ($80 to $150 per day), aerator ($80 to $120 per day), topdressing material ($150 to $300 for 1 cubic metre of sandy loam). Plus fertiliser support ($50 to $100 for NZLA Starter or All Seasons).

Professional: $500 to $1,500 for the same work on a typical home lawn. Higher for larger lawns or multiple sessions.

**Full renovation (kill and rebuild from seed).**

DIY: $300 to $800 for a typical 200 to 400 m² home lawn. Glyphosate ($50), turf cutter hire if needed ($150 per day), rotary hoe hire ($120 per day), soil amendments ($100 to $300), NZLA Starter ($60), NZLA Perennial Rye seed ($150 to $250 for 14 kg covering 350 m²), Liquid Starter ($40 to $60). Some tools and supplies on top.

Professional: $3,000 to $8,000 for a typical home lawn. Includes all labour, equipment, materials, and warranties on establishment. Higher for premium grass varieties (MDR, Dark Perennial Rye), ready lawn installation (rather than seeding), or larger lawns.

**Full renovation with ready lawn (kill and rebuild with installed ready lawn).**

Ready lawn material itself costs $15 to $25 per square metre for standard varieties, $20 to $35 per square metre for premium varieties. So a 200 m² home lawn is $3,000 to $5,000 in ready lawn material alone.

Plus delivery, plus installation labour, plus soil prep — typical professional ready lawn installation on a home lawn runs $6,000 to $15,000.

DIY ready lawn installation is possible (reduces labour cost) but the material itself is the major expense.

What affects the cost:

**Lawn size.** Larger lawns cost more in proportion. Smaller lawns have minimum-job premiums on professional work.

**Existing lawn condition.** A lawn with heavy weeds, severe thatch, major compaction, or unevenness requires more work than a simple kill-and-replace.

**Species choice.** Premium grass varieties cost more in seed. Ready lawn costs vary by variety.

**Access and complexity.** Lawns with awkward access, slopes, multiple sections, or complex shapes cost more than open simple rectangles.

**Region.** Major centres typically have more competitive professional rates than rural areas (more contractors competing). Material costs are similar nationally.

**Soil amendments.** Heavy clay lawns needing sand amendment, or low-organic soils needing compost, add to the cost.

When DIY makes sense:

Home lawns 100 to 500 m². Owner has time, basic tools, and physical capacity for the work. Wants to learn the process and stay involved. Budget is a key factor.

When professional makes sense:

Larger properties. Showcase or premium lawns where the result must be perfect. Time constraints where the owner can't commit to the work. Complex situations (irrigation systems, drainage work, levelling) where specialist expertise is needed.

For most home lawn renovations, DIY is realistic. The cost is far lower and the learning supports ongoing lawn care.
