# What is a full lawn renovation?

A complete rebuild of the lawn — kill the existing grass, fix the soil, re-establish from seed or ready lawn. Different from light renovation (scarifying, aerating, topdressing on an existing lawn) which keeps the existing grass intact.

When a full renovation is needed:

**The existing lawn is mostly weeds.** Over 30 to 40 percent of the cover is weeds (broadleaf, grass weeds, or both). Selective herbicide can't clean it up. Easier to start fresh than fight the weed population.

**The grass species is wrong for the situation.** Cool-season grass in a hot dry inland site, or ryegrass in deep shade. Changing species requires kill-and-replace.

**Heavy thatch over 25 mm.** Severe thatch can't be removed by scarifying without effectively destroying the lawn anyway. Better to rebuild properly.

**Significant unevenness over 30 mm.** Regrading is needed. Can't be done without removing the existing turf.

**Compaction so severe that aeration can't address it.** Sometimes the soil structure needs more than core holes — needs rotary hoeing or amendment.

**Chronic disease or pest issues.** Year-after-year problems in the same lawn often have underlying causes (soil, drainage, shade) that a renovation gives the chance to address.

**Wrong product history.** A lawn that's had years of cheap fast-release fertiliser, no slow-release base, no pre-em, no disease prevention may be in a state where rebuilding is more effective than gradual rehabilitation.

The renovation process:

**Week 0 — Kill the existing lawn.** Glyphosate (with NZLA Sticker as adjuvant for better uptake) sprayed across the whole area. Apply with active growth. Most grass dies within 7 to 14 days.

**Week 2 — Second glyphosate pass** to catch any survivors or new germinations. Wait another 1 to 2 weeks for full kill.

**Week 4 — Remove dead vegetation.** Scrape off or rake out the dead grass. For heavy turf removal, hire a turf cutter to lift the dead layer cleanly.

**Week 4-5 — Soil prep.** Rotary hoe or fork the top 100 to 150 mm. Remove rocks and debris. Amend the soil — sand for clay drainage, compost for low-organic-matter soils. Level the surface.

**Week 5 — Apply NZLA Starter granular** at 1.5 kg per 100m². Rake in lightly.

**Week 5-6 — Sow seed or lay ready lawn.** NZLA Perennial Rye at 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for general home use. Other species per situation.

**Weeks 6-12 — Establishment care.** Consistent watering, no traffic, light foliar feeding (Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks).

**Month 3+ — Transition to maintenance programme.** All Seasons granular every 6 to 8 weeks. Standard ongoing care.

Total timeline:

Roughly 12 to 16 weeks from start to a fully established mature-looking lawn. Quicker on smaller lawns and ready lawn installations; longer on larger lawns from seed.

Cost:

For a typical home lawn (200 to 400 m²), DIY renovation costs $200 to $800 in product, seed, soil amendments. Professional renovation runs $2,000 to $8,000 depending on size and scope.
