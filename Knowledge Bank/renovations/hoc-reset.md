# HOC Reset

## What it is

HOC stands for Height of Cut. An HOC reset is a controlled process of progressively lowering the mowing height back to a target when a lawn has grown significantly above it.

The problem it solves: standard third-rule mowing (never take more than a third of the leaf in one cut) works for ongoing maintenance, but it's slow when you're 30 to 50 mm above your target height. Waiting for the lawn to come down naturally through normal mowing takes many weeks. An HOC reset accelerates the process by spacing multiple cuts close together, each taking roughly a third, until the target height is reached.

This also applies when transitioning a lawn from a high maintenance height to a lower target — a lawn that's always been mowed at 50 mm that you want to bring down to 25 mm for a finer finish.

## The process

Each step removes roughly a third of the current height. Allow 3 to 5 days between cuts for the plant to recover.

Example — bringing a lawn from 70 mm down to 25 mm target:

Day 0: Mow at 47 mm (about a third of 70 mm). Lawn now at 47 mm.
Day 4: Mow at 35 mm (about a third of 47 mm). Lawn now at 35 mm.
Day 8: Mow at 27 mm (approximately a third of 35 mm). Lawn now at 27 mm.
Day 12: Final mow at 25 mm target.

Roughly 2 weeks, four mows.

Example — bringing a lawn from 100 mm down to 25 mm:

Day 0: 70 mm.
Day 5: 50 mm.
Day 10: 35 mm.
Day 15: 27 mm.
Day 20: 25 mm.

Roughly 3 weeks, five mows.

## Timing

Spring and autumn growing windows are the right time for an HOC reset. The lawn is growing actively and recovers well between cuts.

Don't attempt a reset through summer heat over 28°C. Don't attempt in late autumn heading into winter when growth is slowing. On a stressed or slow-growing lawn, extend the recovery window between cuts to 5 to 7 days rather than 3 to 5.

## Supporting the lawn through the reset

Sharp blade on every cut. Aggressive mowing on a blunt blade scalps and tears rather than cuts cleanly. If the blade needs sharpening, do it before starting.

NZLA Liquid Starter or Liquid Boost as a foliar between mows. Drives recovery between the mowing stress events. NZLA Iron+ maintains colour through the cosmetically rough period.

Water consistently. Don't compound the mechanical stress with drought stress.

Apply NZLA Starter granular or All Seasons before starting a significant reset. A well-fed lawn handles the work better than a hungry one.

## What to expect

The lawn looks rough through the reset period — partial heights, visible mowing lines, some discolouration. This is normal. It's not failing; it's being managed.

Full recovery to a uniform polished look typically happens 1 to 2 weeks after the final cut.

## When not to do it

Beyond about 75 mm of height to remove, consider extending the reset over 4 to 6 weeks rather than compressing it. Continuous aggressive mowing on a plant that's trying to recover compounds the stress.

If the lawn is already diseased or stressed for other reasons, deal with those first. An HOC reset on a struggling lawn risks real damage.
