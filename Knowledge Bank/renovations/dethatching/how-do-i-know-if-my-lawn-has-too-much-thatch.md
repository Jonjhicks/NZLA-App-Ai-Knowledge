# How do I know if my lawn has too much thatch?

Three diagnostic tests, and a few symptoms that show up when thatch is excessive.

**The depth test.** Use a knife or sharp spade to cut a small wedge out of the lawn — about 100 mm wide and 100 mm deep. Look at the side of the cut. You'll see the green grass at the top, then a brown layer between the grass and the soil (thatch), then the soil.

Healthy thatch layer: under 10 mm. The grass is well-anchored, water reaches the soil quickly, fertiliser gets to the roots.

Excessive thatch: over 15 to 20 mm. Restricts water and air movement, holds moisture against the crowns, creates disease habitat.

Severe thatch: 25 mm or more. The lawn is largely growing in thatch rather than soil. Major intervention needed.

**The spongy test.** Walk on the lawn. Does it feel firm underfoot, or springy and bouncy?

Firm = healthy thatch.

Slightly bouncy = some thatch building.

Noticeably spongy = excessive thatch.

Markedly squishy = severe thatch.

**The water absorption test.** Pour a litre of water on the lawn surface.

Absorbs within 30 seconds = thatch is fine.

30 to 60 seconds = some thatch slowing things.

Over a minute = significant thatch issue.

Symptoms when thatch is excessive:

**Water sits on the surface.** After rain or irrigation, the surface stays visibly wet much longer than it should.

**Fertiliser response is sluggish.** Granular fertiliser sits in the thatch layer rather than reaching the roots. The lawn responds weakly to feeds that should produce strong growth.

**Disease pressure climbs.** Thatch holds moisture against the crowns, creating perfect fungal habitat. Persistent Fusarium, moss issues, or other disease often correlates with heavy thatch.

**Lawn is more vulnerable to drought.** Roots growing in thatch rather than soil don't reach deep moisture. The lawn dries out fast in heat.

**Mowing scalps unevenly.** The mower wheels press into the spongy thatch, then the blade scalps the high spots that bounce back. Patchy scalping is a thatch sign.

When to scarify:

Thatch over 15 to 20 mm needs intervention. Spring (September through October) or autumn (March through April) when growth is active enough to recover from the damage scarifying does.

Light scarifying suits 15 to 20 mm thatch. Heavy scarifying or full renovation is needed for severe thatch over 25 mm.

After scarifying, NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² to support recovery. Liquid Starter as a foliar 4 to 5 weeks later.
