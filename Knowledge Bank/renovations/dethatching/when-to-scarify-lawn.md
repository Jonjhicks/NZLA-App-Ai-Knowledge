# When is the best time to scarify my lawn?

Spring or autumn when the grass is growing strongly enough to recover fast from the damage scarifying does.

For cool-season lawns (ryegrass, fescue, browntop), the best windows are late September through October when soil temperatures are climbing and growth is firing, or March through April when temperatures are still warm but the heat stress of summer has gone.

Do not scarify in summer — the lawn is stressed by heat and will not recover from the damage. Do not scarify in winter either — growth is too slow to fill the bare patches the scarifier leaves.

For warm-season lawns (kikuyu, couch), scarify in late spring through summer when those species are growing hardest.

After scarifying, the lawn looks rough — that is normal. Get NZLA Starter granular down to drive root recovery, and oversow any thin areas with seed appropriate to the lawn type. Liquid Starter as a foliar 4 to 5 weeks later supports the establishment.

A heavy scarify is closer to a full renovation than a light maintenance pass. Plan for 4 to 6 weeks of patchy looking lawn before it fills in properly. If timing is tight (you want a presentable lawn for an event), wait for the right window rather than rushing it.
