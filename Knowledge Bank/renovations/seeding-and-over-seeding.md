# Seeding and Over-seeding

## What's the difference?

Seeding is establishing a lawn from scratch over prepared bare soil. No existing grass, no competition. The full plant population comes from the seed.

Overseeding is sowing seed into an existing lawn to thicken it or introduce additional species. The existing lawn stays; new seed fills in gaps and increases density.

Different purposes, different preparation, different sowing rates.

## Seeding — new lawn from scratch

Full soil preparation is necessary. Rotary hoe or fork the top 100 to 150 mm, remove rocks and debris, level, firm, apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² and rake in lightly.

Sow at 3 to 4 kg per 100m² for ryegrass varieties. Rake in to the top 5 to 10 mm. Roll if available for better seed-to-soil contact. Water consistently for the first 2 to 3 weeks.

The establishment environment is favourable — all light, moisture and nutrients available to the new seedlings, no competition. First mow at 60 to 80 mm. Full integration at 8 to 12 weeks.

## Overseeding — thickening an existing lawn

Timing first. Autumn (late February through April) is the prime window. Spring (September through October) is second. Don't overseed in summer heat or winter cold.

Surface preparation is the difference between seed that takes and seed that wastes. New seed needs contact with soil to germinate — it won't germinate sitting on top of a thatch layer.

Mow the existing lawn short (down to 20 mm) the day before sowing. If the lawn is at 50 mm or higher, bring it down over 2 to 3 mows in the week before rather than dropping to 20 mm in one cut.

Lightly scarify or rake hard with a metal-tined rake to break the surface and expose soil between existing plants. For larger areas, a slit seeder cuts shallow slots and drops seed into them simultaneously — combining surface prep and seeding in one pass.

Apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m² before sowing. Rake lightly into the surface.

Sow at 2 kg per 100m² for ryegrass varieties (less than the new lawn rate because you're filling gaps in an existing population, not starting from scratch). Distribute in two half-rate passes at right angles to catch misses. Rake lightly to get seed into the top 5 to 10 mm.

Water consistently for 10 to 14 days until seedlings establish. Light frequent watering rather than deep occasional. After seedlings are up, back off to deeper less-frequent watering.

Don't mow until new seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm. First mow on the high setting, sharp blade.

NZLA Liquid Starter as a foliar 4 to 5 weeks after sowing supports establishment through to maturity.

Visible thickening develops within 4 to 6 weeks. Full integration with the existing lawn at 8 to 12 weeks.

## What not to do

Don't apply pre-emergent within 4 to 6 months of seeding-and-over-seeding — it kills the new seed. If Blockade is on the programme, plan the timing around the seeding-and-over-seeding window.

Don't apply heavy nitrogen feed at sowing time. The existing lawn responds first and out-competes the seedlings. NZLA Starter is phosphorus-focused for this reason.

Don't overseed onto compacted ground without addressing the core-aeration first. Open the soil with aeration or a fork before sowing.

Don't skip the watering programme. New seedlings are the most vulnerable plants in your lawn.

## Species matching

For seeding-and-over-seeding to be effective long-term, the new seed needs to suit the same conditions as the existing lawn. Match the species where possible. Overseeding premium varieties (MDR, Dark Perennial Rye) into an existing cheaper ryegrass lawn gradually shifts the mix over several seasons — a legitimate strategy for upgrading a lawn without full renovation.
