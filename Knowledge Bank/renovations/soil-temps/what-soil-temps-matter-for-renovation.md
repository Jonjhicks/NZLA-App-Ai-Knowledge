# What soil temperatures matter for lawn renovation?

Different jobs need different temperatures. The key numbers:

**Cool-season grass seed germination.** Ryegrass and fescue need soil temperatures of around 10°C minimum to germinate within a reasonable timeframe (10 to 14 days). At 5°C they germinate but very slowly (3 to 4 weeks); below 5°C they sit dormant. Optimal germination is 15 to 20°C.

**Warm-season grass seed germination.** Kikuyu and couch need 15°C minimum, ideally above 18°C. Below that they sit and rot rather than germinate. Sowing warm-season grass in cool soil is wasted seed.

**Summer grass and crabgrass germination trigger.** 12°C at 50 mm depth and rising — the point pre-em needs to be in place. NZ regional timing for this is late August (Auckland) through early October (Canterbury).

**Poa annua germination trigger.** 18 to 22°C and falling — typically autumn. Pre-em timing for poa needs to land before this window.

**Active grass growth threshold.** Cool-season grasses start active growth around 7 to 8°C. Below that they're effectively dormant. Warm-season grasses need 15°C plus for active growth.

**Scarifying recovery.** Cool-season turf recovers from scarifying when soil temps are above 10°C — late September through late April for most of NZ.

How to measure: a soil thermometer pushed to 50 mm depth gives an accurate reading. NIWA has historical and current soil temperature data for most regions if you don't have a thermometer.

The practical rule of thumb: if you can comfortably walk barefoot on the soil, it's warm enough for renovation work. If it's cold underfoot, hold off.

Renovation timing in NZ — late September through October, and again March through April — are the windows when soil temperatures support both establishment and recovery.
