# What is pre-germinating seed and is it worth it?

Pre-germinating means soaking the seed in water for 2 to 4 days before sowing, then drying it slightly and broadcasting it onto the prepared soil. The water absorption starts the germination process before the seed hits the ground.

The point is faster establishment. A pre-germinated ryegrass seed can have visible growth within 3 to 5 days of sowing, where unprimed seed takes 7 to 14 days. That's a meaningful head start in conditions where speed matters — overseeding into thin areas before weed pressure builds, or establishing into a tight weather window.

Method:

Soak the seed in clean water (a bucket works) for 24 to 36 hours at room temperature. Drain and rinse. Soak again for another 24 to 36 hours, changing the water once or twice. Total soaking time roughly 2 to 3 days.

After soaking, drain thoroughly and spread the seed on a tarp or sheet in a cool dry place for a few hours to surface-dry. The seed should be tacky but not wet enough to clump.

Sow within 24 hours of the surface-dry step. Don't let the wet seed sit for days or it starts to mould or grow in the bag.

Is it worth it? For most home lawn overseeding, no. Slightly faster establishment doesn't justify the fiddle of the soaking process. Standard sowing into properly prepped soil with consistent watering works fine.

Where it earns its place: time-pressured situations where you've got a narrow weather window or need fast cover before weed pressure builds. Sports turf renovation is a common use case. Otherwise, sow dry seed and let nature do its job.
