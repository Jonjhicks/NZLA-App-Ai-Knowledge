# What is the difference between pre-germinated and standard seed?

Pre-germinated seed has had the germination process started by soaking the seed in water for 2 to 3 days before sowing. Standard seed is dry and starts germination only after being sown into moist soil.

The functional difference:

**Standard seed timeline.** Sown dry into prepared soil. Absorbs moisture over 24 to 48 hours. Germination process begins. First visible growth (radicle and shoot emerging from seed coat) at 5 to 10 days for ryegrass.

**Pre-germinated seed timeline.** Soaked for 2 to 3 days before sowing — germination is already underway when sown. First visible growth at 3 to 5 days after sowing. Effectively saves 2 to 5 days of establishment time.

What pre-germination doesn't change:

**Total establishment time.** A pre-germinated lawn isn't ready to mow earlier — it just shows green slightly sooner. The full establishment process to mature lawn still takes 6 to 12 weeks.

**Seedling fragility.** Pre-germinated seedlings have the same vulnerable establishment period as standard seedlings. Consistent watering, no traffic, proper care still required.

**Sowing rates.** Same kg/100m² rates for both. Pre-germination doesn't change the seed density needed.

**Seed quality.** Pre-germination doesn't improve poor seed. Old, low-vigour or contaminated seed gives the same poor result whether pre-germinated or not. Always start with good quality certified seed.

When pre-germination is worth doing:

**Time-pressured situations.** Narrow weather window for establishment. Need to get the lawn to visible growth before forecast bad weather.

**Cool conditions where standard germination is slow.** Pre-germination starts the biochemistry in warm controlled conditions before the seed hits the cool soil. Useful in marginal autumn or early spring conditions.

**Sports turf renovation under pressure.** Professional renovation timelines often have specific dates for play resumption. Pre-germination compresses the establishment timeline.

**Overseeding into competition.** Standard seed competing with established grass for resources sometimes loses. Pre-germinated seed has a head start that improves establishment success.

When pre-germination isn't worth the effort:

**Standard home lawn establishment in autumn.** Conditions favour quick germination anyway. The extra few days don't justify the fiddle.

**When the soaking process can't be done properly.** Wet seed sitting too long without proper drainage starts to ferment or mould. Poor pre-germination is worse than no pre-germination.

**When immediate sowing after soaking can't be guaranteed.** Pre-germinated seed needs to be sown within 24 hours of completing the soaking process. If your schedule doesn't allow that, don't pre-germinate.

Practical method:

Soak in clean water for 24 to 36 hours. Drain and rinse. Soak again for another 24 to 36 hours, changing water once. Total 2 to 3 days.

Drain thoroughly. Spread on a tarp in cool dry place for a few hours to surface-dry. Seed should be tacky but not wet enough to clump.

Sow within 24 hours of surface-drying.

For most NZ home lawn establishment, standard sowing into properly prepped soil with consistent watering works fine. Pre-germination is a tool for specific situations rather than a routine practice.
