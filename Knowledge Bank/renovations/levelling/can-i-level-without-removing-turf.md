# Can I level my lawn without removing turf?

Yes, for minor unevenness under about 10 mm. Topdressing across multiple applications gradually fills dips and evens out high spots without removing any turf.

How it works:

Apply 3 to 5 mm of sandy loam (or pure sand for clay soils) across the lawn or just the uneven areas. Work it down into the canopy with a stiff brush or drag mat so it settles between the grass plants rather than smothering them.

The existing grass grows up through the topdressing layer. Dips fill in. High spots get topped up to a more even level. The lawn surface evens out over multiple applications across a season.

What you can level this way:

**Minor dips up to 10 mm.** Easily filled with topdressing across 2 to 3 applications.

**Slight ridges or bumps up to 5 mm.** The topdressing settles around the high spot, blending it into the surrounding lawn level over time.

**Wear patterns from foot traffic.** Subtle uneven surfaces from regular walking routes.

**Settling around tree roots or in compacted areas.** Light topdressing addresses minor surface variations without disturbing the underlying root system or compacted layer.

What you can't level without removing turf:

**Bumps or dips over 15 to 20 mm.** Topdressing 20 mm in one go smothers the existing grass. Removing the turf cap and either filling underneath (for dips) or removing soil beneath (for bumps) is the only realistic approach.

**Significant slopes or unevenness across larger areas.** Multiple square metres of substantial unevenness needs proper regrading — kill the lawn, regrade the soil mechanically, re-establish.

**Drainage-related dips that hold water.** A persistent low spot collecting water is usually a drainage issue, not a levelling issue. Address the drainage rather than just filling the dip.

The multi-application approach:

For a lawn with several minor uneven spots, plan 2 to 3 topdressings across a single growing season — once in spring, once in mid-summer if conditions allow, once in autumn. Each application 3 to 5 mm. The combined 9 to 15 mm of material substantially evens minor variations.

Material:

Sandy loam (typically 70% sand, 30% loam) for general levelling. Pure sand for clay soils where drainage improvement is also a goal. Avoid heavy topsoil — too dense, settles unevenly, can introduce weed seeds.

Buy a small quantity to test before committing to a larger amount for the whole lawn.

Recovery support:

NZLA Starter granular before topdressing supports the recovery. Water lightly to settle the material into the surface. Hold off mowing for a week after topdressing to let the surface stabilise.

Time of year:

Spring or autumn. Active growing season supports the grass growing up through the new layer. Don't topdress in summer heat or winter cold.

For most home lawns with mild unevenness, this approach delivers noticeable improvement within a single growing season. Substantial bumps need different methods.
