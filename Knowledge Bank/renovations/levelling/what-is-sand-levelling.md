# What is sand levelling?

Topdressing the lawn with pure sand (rather than sandy loam or organic material) to gradually amend the soil profile and level the surface at the same time.

Two outcomes from one application:

**Levelling.** Like other topdressing materials, sand fills dips and gradually evens out surface variations across multiple applications.

**Soil profile amendment.** Pure sand moving into the upper soil profile changes the soil composition. On heavy clay or silt soils, repeated sand topdressing over years gradually opens up drainage and root growth potential.

Why pure sand specifically:

Clay and silt soils suffer from compaction, poor drainage and limited root development. Mixing sand into the soil profile would help, but you can't easily mix sand into an established lawn's soil without destroying the lawn.

Sand topdressing achieves the same gradual amendment without disturbing the lawn. Each application introduces sand into the top of the soil profile. Over multiple applications across years, the upper soil layer becomes increasingly sand-amended.

When sand levelling is the right approach:

**Heavy clay lawns** where drainage and root development are limited. Sand amendment improves both gradually.

**Lawns with chronic surface waterlogging** that doesn't drain well. Sand introduces drainage pathways into the upper profile.

**Sports turf and golf turf** where the standard practice is sand-based root zones. Topdressing maintains the sand profile.

**Premium home lawns being upgraded** toward a sand-based profile for better drainage and finer maintenance options.

When NOT to use pure sand:

**Already sandy lawns.** Adding more sand to soil that already drains too fast makes drought stress worse. Use organic-rich topdressing instead.

**Lawns with low organic matter overall.** Adding sand without compensating organic material leaves the soil with poor nutrient cycling and microbial function.

**Single-application levelling on a lawn where you just want to fill dips.** Sand alone settles unevenly compared to sandy loam, and the texture difference between sand and existing soil shows visibly until growth fills in.

What sand to use:

Coarse builder's sand or specialised turf sand. Avoid fine masonry sand (drainage too poor) or marine sand (salt content damages grass).

Wash test: pour a handful into a glass of water. Clean sand settles clear within seconds. Sand with silt or clay contamination leaves a layer of fine particles suspended.

How much per application:

3 to 5 mm at a time, worked down into the canopy. More than that smothers the grass. Multiple light applications across a season compound the effect.

Recovery support:

NZLA Starter granular supports the lawn through topdressing recovery. Water lightly to settle the sand.

Timing:

Spring or autumn when active growth supports the lawn growing up through the new layer.

The long view:

Sand levelling is a multi-year programme on heavy clay lawns. 4 to 6 applications a year across 2 to 3 years substantially shifts the upper soil profile toward better drainage and root development. Don't expect single-application results.
