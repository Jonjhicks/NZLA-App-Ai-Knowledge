# How do I level a bumpy lawn?

Depends how bumpy. Three approaches for three levels of severity.

**Minor bumps and dips under 10 mm.** Topdressing over time. Apply 3 to 5 mm of sandy loam over the whole lawn (or just the bumpy areas), work it down into the canopy with a stiff brush or drag mat, and let the grass grow up through it. Repeat across two or three applications over a season. Existing grass adapts and the levels even out gradually. Apply NZLA Starter before each topdressing to support recovery.

**Moderate undulations 10 to 30 mm.** Combination of cutting high points and filling low points. For high points, scrape soil off with a flat-edged shovel after removing the turf cap, lower the level, lay the turf back down. For low points, lift the turf cap, add sandy loam underneath, replace. More work than topdressing alone but handles bigger variations.

**Significant unevenness over 30 mm or large areas.** Full renovation. Kill the existing lawn, regrade the soil mechanically (rotary hoe and level rake, or use a contractor), then re-establish from seed or ready lawn. This is the only way to deal with serious unevenness — topdressing 30 mm of fill would kill the existing grass.

What NOT to do: don't apply 20 or 30 mm of topdressing in one go expecting it to "smooth things out." The grass beneath suffocates and dies, leaving you with a worse problem than you started with.

For most home lawns with mild undulations, two or three topdressings over a year with consistent fertility and watering produces a noticeable improvement.

Time it for active growing season (spring or autumn) so the lawn recovers between applications.
