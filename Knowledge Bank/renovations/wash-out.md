# Wash Out

## What it is

Wash out is the loss of seed, soil, or fertiliser from a lawn area due to heavy rain or runoff. Three forms: seed wash out (seed carried off a freshly sown surface before germination), soil wash out (topsoil eroding from slopes or newly-prepared ground), and fertiliser wash out (granular product washed off-target shortly after application).

All three create problems that persist. Seed lost to wash out leaves bare patches in the final lawn that need remediation. Eroded soil creates uneven surfaces and exposes subsoil. Fertiliser washed into drains is product wasted and an environmental concern.

## Prevention

**Check the forecast before you sow or apply.** Heavy rain in the next 24 to 48 hours means wait. This applies to seed sowing, topdressing, and granular fertiliser applications.

**Rake seed in after sowing.** Seed in the top 5 to 10 mm of soil is largely protected from wash out. Seed sitting on the surface is vulnerable to even moderate rain. Rolling after sowing presses seed into soil contact and reduces risk further.

**Cover with hessian or jute matting on slopes.** For new lawn establishment on any noticeable slope, hessian or jute matting weighted in place protects seed and soil from rainfall impact. It allows water and light through while breaking the kinetic energy of falling water and preventing material being carried away. Remove once seedlings are established.

**Water granular fertiliser in promptly.** Irrigation within a few hours of application moves the granules down into the soil, reducing the risk if heavy rain follows.

**Don't apply on saturated soil.** Soil that can't absorb water will carry anything on its surface off as runoff. Wait for the profile to dry.

**For chronic slope wash out:** match the species to the site. NZLA RSR Ryegrass has a stoloniferous habit (spreads laterally as well as growing upright) that gives better soil cover and slope stability than standard bunch-type ryegrass. NZLA Penetrate helps water move down through the profile on hydrophobic soils, reducing surface runoff duration.

## Repairing seed wash out

Wait 7 to 10 days and check for germination in the original sowing area. Often some seed survived and is establishing despite the rain event.

For areas that clearly failed: loosen the top 20 mm of soil, apply NZLA Starter granular at 1.5 kg per 100m², re-sow at 3 to 4 kg per 100m², rake in, water consistently. Standard patch repair process.

If the entire sowing failed across a large area, treat it as a new establishment from the prep stage.

## Repairing soil wash out

Refill eroded areas with soil matching the surrounding lawn surface. Sandy loam or topsoil to grade. Apply NZLA Starter to the filled areas before seeding. Sow and establish as for a patch repair.

Review whether the erosion indicates an ongoing drainage-and-soil-moisture or slope issue. If the same channel washes out in every significant rain event, the solution isn't repeated repair — it's addressing the drainage-and-soil-moisture.

## Repairing fertiliser wash out

If the rain happened 6 to 12 hours after application, much of the fertiliser may already have moved into the soil before washing. Wait 7 to 10 days to see if the lawn responds evenly.

If response is uneven (some areas greening, others flat), top up at half rate over the underperforming areas.

If the wash out happened within a couple of hours of application, reapply at full rate once the soil surface has dried. Don't double-dose while the original application might still be in the soil.

## Repairing flood-affected established lawns

Most established lawns recover from temporary flooding without lasting damage. The root system of a well-established lawn handles short-term waterlogging.

Don't mow while the lawn is still saturated. Wheel tracking and root mat damage from mowing wet ground compounds the flood damage significantly.

Once drained and surface-dry, assess for actual damage. Yellowing or dying patches where flood water sat for extended periods (24 hours or more) may need patch repair. NZLA Liquid Starter foliar at 200 to 300 mL per 100m² supports recovery from stress.
