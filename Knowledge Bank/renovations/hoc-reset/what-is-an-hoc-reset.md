# What is an HOC reset?

HOC stands for Height of Cut. An HOC reset is a controlled aggressive cut that takes the lawn down to a much lower height than its normal maintenance level, then progressively brings it back up over several weeks.

The job is restoring a lawn that's been allowed to grow long, or transitioning a lawn from a higher maintenance height to a lower target height. Standard "third rule" mowing (never take more than a third of the leaf in one cut) doesn't work when you're 30 mm over target — it takes weeks of single mows to get back to target. An HOC reset accelerates that.

Typical pattern:

Mow at the highest setting that takes meaningful material off (still respecting roughly the third rule on that cut). Wait 3 to 5 days. Mow again, dropping the setting one level. Wait another 3 to 5 days. Mow again, dropping another level. Continue until you reach the target height.

For a lawn 30 mm over target, that's typically 3 to 4 mows over 2 to 3 weeks to reset cleanly.

Critical support during the reset:

Sharp blade. Every cut. Blunt blades on aggressive mowing scalp and bruise the lawn.

Fertility. Apply NZLA Starter or All Seasons just before starting the reset. Liquid Starter or Liquid Boost as a foliar through the reset period supports recovery between mows.

Water consistently. The aggressive mowing stresses the plant. Don't compound it with drought stress.

When NOT to do an HOC reset:

Going into winter (slow recovery). Through summer heat (stress). On stressed or diseased lawns. Spring and autumn growing windows are the right time.

Don't try to reset 50+ mm in one go. Better to live with the longer height for a season than scalp the lawn into recovery for months.
