# How long does an HOC reset take?

2 to 4 weeks for most resets, depending on how much height you're taking off and how active the growth is.

The pattern is sequential mowing at progressively lower settings, with 3 to 5 days between cuts for recovery.

**Worked example — bringing a lawn from 70 mm down to 25 mm target:**

Day 0: Lawn at 70 mm. Mow at the highest setting that takes meaningful material off — 47 mm cut (a third of 70 mm). Lawn now at 47 mm.

Day 3-5: Mow at 35 mm (a third of 47 mm). Lawn now at 35 mm.

Day 7-10: Mow at 27 mm (close to a third of 35 mm). Lawn now at 27 mm.

Day 11-14: Final mow at 25 mm target.

Total: roughly 2 weeks, four mows, all respecting the third rule.

**Faster scenario — bringing a lawn from 50 mm down to 30 mm:**

Day 0: Mow at 35 mm.

Day 3-5: Mow at 30 mm.

Total: about a week, two mows.

**Larger reset — bringing a lawn from 100 mm down to 25 mm:**

Day 0: 70 mm.

Day 5: 50 mm.

Day 10: 35 mm.

Day 15: 27 mm.

Day 20: 25 mm.

Total: roughly 3 weeks, five mows.

Beyond about 75 mm of height to remove, consider a partial reset over 4 to 6 weeks rather than continuous aggressive mowing.

What affects timing:

**Growth rate during the reset.** Active growing season (spring through autumn) supports faster recovery between mows — closer to 3 days. Slow growth periods (mid-summer heat, late autumn) need longer recovery — closer to 5 to 7 days.

**Species.** Ryegrass recovers fast. Tall fescue moderate. Fine fescue slow. Kikuyu fast in warm conditions, slow in cool. Match the recovery interval to the species.

**Health going in.** A well-fed healthy lawn handles aggressive mowing better than a stressed underfed lawn. Apply NZLA Starter or All Seasons before starting the reset.

**Conditions.** Heat stress, drought, or cold all slow recovery. Avoid HOC reset in summer heat above 28°C or winter cold below 8°C soil temperature.

Support during the reset:

Sharp blade for every cut. Aggressive mowing on blunt blades scalps and tears.

NZLA Liquid Starter or Liquid Boost as foliar between mows. Drives recovery between the mowing stress.

Iron+ for colour during the cosmetically rough period.

Water consistently. Don't compound the mowing stress with drought stress.

When NOT to do an HOC reset:

Going into winter (slow recovery). Through extreme summer heat. On stressed or diseased lawns. Wait for spring or autumn growing windows.

What to expect during the reset:

The lawn looks rough through the reset period — partial heights, visible mowing lines, some discolouration. Normal. Full recovery to a uniform polished look happens 1 to 2 weeks after the final cut.
