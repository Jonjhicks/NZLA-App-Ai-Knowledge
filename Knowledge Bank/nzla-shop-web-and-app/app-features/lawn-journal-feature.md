# How does the lawn journal feature work?

It's a record of what you've done on the lawn and when. You log entries through the app: mowing, fertiliser applications (with product and rate), herbicide or fungicide sprays, weather notes, photos.

Why it matters: a good lawn is about timing and pattern over a season, not single events. Looking back at six months of journal entries tells you a lot. When did the rust show up, what was the lawn doing before, what fertiliser had you used and when. That kind of pattern-spotting is hard from memory but easy from notes.

It's also useful for product timing. You can see exactly how long it's been since the last application of a particular product, which helps avoid double-up and helps you stick to label intervals.

For people running a programme across multiple lawns (lawn care professionals, lifestyle blocks with several distinct areas), the journal handles separate lawns under one account.

Set up takes a few minutes. The payoff is six months in when you can scroll back and see what's actually happened versus what you think happened.
