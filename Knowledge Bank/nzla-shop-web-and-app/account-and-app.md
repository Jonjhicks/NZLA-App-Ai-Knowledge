# Account, App and Forum

## Account

You don't need an account for a single guest checkout order. For everything else, an account is worth having. Saved addresses, faster reordering, access to order history, and entry to the app forum and journal all sit behind a login.

Setup is an email address and a password. The same account works across the website and the app.

If you've forgotten your password, the password reset on the login page handles it. If you've got duplicate accounts from years of ordering, contact NZLA support to merge them.

## The NZLA app

Download from the App Store or Google Play and log in with your NZLA account.

Three main things the app does:

**Shop.** The full product range at the same prices as the website. Browse, search, order, track delivery. Saved payment details make repeat ordering fast.

**Forum.** The community part of the app. Post questions about your lawn, add photos, get answers from Jon and other regular users. Jon spends most of his time in the app forum answering technical questions directly. It's the fastest way to get a straight answer on a specific lawn situation rather than waiting on email.

**Journal.** A running record of what you've done on your lawn and when. Log mowing, fertiliser applications (product, rate, date), herbicide and fungicide sprays, weather notes, photos. Six months of journal entries shows patterns that are impossible to track from memory: when did the rust show up last year, what was the last fertiliser used and how long ago, what was the programme when the lawn looked its best. Useful for professionals managing multiple lawns too — the journal handles separate lawns under one account.

Beyond those three: push notifications for seasonal reminders, the AI Lawn Expert for quick product and timing questions, and direct access to NZLA support.

## Forum

The forum is the best part of the app for most users. Post a question with a photo and you'll often have responses within a day — from Jon and from other regulars who know what they're talking about.

It's particularly useful for identifying  or diseases from a photo, getting a second opinion on a programme, and for questions that need a bit of back and forth rather than a single answer.

Posting is open to all account holders. No charge to participate in the forum beyond having an account.

The Facebook group (facebook.com/groups/nzlawnaddicts) has 20,000+ members and is good for fast, open community discussion. The app forum has a smaller but more focused membership and is where Jon is most active for technical questions.

## App subscription

The app is $40 per year. That covers the forum, journal, AI Lawn Expert and seasonal notification tools. The shop works without a subscription.
