# Is a sprinkler better than hand watering with a hose?

For most lawns, yes. A sprinkler delivers water more evenly and lets you put down a measurable amount. You can stick a couple of straight-sided containers (tuna cans work) on the lawn while it runs and measure how many millimetres of water you're applying per hour. From there you know exactly how long to run it to put down 12 to 15 mm.

Hand watering is fine for spot work, new patches of seed, settling in repairs, or for watering pots and gardens. For a whole lawn it's slow, uneven, and you almost always underestimate how long it takes to wet the soil properly. People hand-watering for ten minutes a night are basically just spraying the leaves.

A decent oscillating or impact sprinkler costs about $50 and pays itself back in the first dry summer. A pop-up irrigation system with a timer is the next step up if you've got the budget and the use case.

Either way, water deeply and less often rather than briefly and daily. The goal is getting moisture into the top 100 to 150 mm of soil, not just wetting the surface.
