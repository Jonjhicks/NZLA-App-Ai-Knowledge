# Should I water my lawn in winter?

Hardly ever, and only with specific reasons.

Most of NZ gets enough winter rainfall that lawns are well watered through winter without irrigation. Adding extra water on top of regular rain extends the wet period, which feeds Fusarium and other cool-season disease. So the default winter setting on most irrigation systems should be OFF.

Exceptions where you might water:

**Long dry winter spells.** Some parts of NZ (Canterbury particularly) can have weeks of dry weather in winter. If the soil has been dry for 3 weeks plus and the lawn is starting to look stressed, a single deep watering once that fortnight is reasonable.

**New lawns under 6 weeks old.** Establishment-stage lawns still need consistent surface moisture. Light watering as required to keep the top of the soil damp, even in winter.

**Warm-season lawns (kikuyu, couch) in the upper North Island.** These keep growing through warmer winters and may need occasional watering. Once a fortnight if it's been dry.

**Frost-prevention irrigation on golf greens or similar.** Specific application, not relevant for home lawns.

What NOT to do: stay on your summer irrigation schedule. Twice-weekly deep watering through winter when the lawn isn't growing puts soft, damp conditions on the lawn for months — perfect for disease.

Same logic applies to autumn — back the irrigation right off through March and April as rainfall picks up.

Watch the lawn, not the calendar. If the soil's wet, it doesn't need more water. If it's been dry for weeks and the lawn's looking thirsty, a single deep watering helps.
