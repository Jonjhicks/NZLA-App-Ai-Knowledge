# How much water does my lawn need per week?

Around 25 mm a week is the working figure for an established cool-season lawn in summer (ryegrass, fescue, browntop). That's including rainfall, so if it's rained 15 mm in the week, you're adding 10 mm.

Warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch, paspalum) needs less, more like 15 to 20 mm a week through summer because they're more drought-tolerant.

A few caveats. The number assumes average summer weather, no wind, mild temperatures. In a heatwave or windy spell, evapotranspiration goes up and you may need 30 to 40 mm a week. In overcast cool weather, much less.

Soil type matters too. Sandy soils need more frequent smaller applications because they don't hold water. Clay soils hold more so you can water less often but heavier.

Better to apply that 25 mm in two deep waterings (12 to 13 mm each) than seven light ones every day. Deep watering encourages roots to follow the water down, giving you a more drought-tolerant lawn.

Skip the calendar approach. Watch the lawn. Footprints staying compressed for more than a few seconds means it's thirsty. Grey-blue cast across the surface is the next stage. If you're seeing either, water.
