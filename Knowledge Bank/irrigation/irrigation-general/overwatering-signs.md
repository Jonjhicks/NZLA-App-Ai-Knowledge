# What are the signs of overwatering my lawn?

A constantly wet surface is the obvious one. If the lawn squelches under foot most days, you're applying too much.

Disease is the bigger indicator. Fusarium patch, red thread, brown patch, dollar spot, all of them favour wet conditions. If you're getting recurring fungal issues, irrigation is often the cause. Cut watering back and frequency of disease drops.

Shallow root systems show up too. Overwatered lawns don't push roots deep because they don't need to, the water's always at the surface. Then come a hot dry spell and the lawn stresses out fast because the roots aren't where they need to be.

Weed pressure goes up. Poa annua, summer grass, paspalum, all prefer wet conditions. A constantly damp lawn invites them in.

Yellowing in patches without obvious cause can also be waterlogging. Roots starved of oxygen don't function properly and the grass goes off-colour.

Fix is straightforward. Cut back watering frequency, water deeper but less often. Let the lawn dry out between waterings. A mild drought stress between irrigations is healthier than constant saturation.
