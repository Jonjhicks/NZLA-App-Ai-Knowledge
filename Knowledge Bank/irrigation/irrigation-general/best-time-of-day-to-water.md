# What is the best time of day to water?

Early morning. Between about 4 am and 8 am is the window.

Three reasons.

**Disease pressure.** Evening watering extends the overnight leaf wetness window by hours. Fungal disease pressure tracks canopy moisture duration — every extra hour the leaf is wet is more opportunity for Fusarium, dollar spot, brown patch and the rest. Morning watering lets the lawn dry off through the day before night.

**Evaporation loss.** Watering in the heat of the day loses a meaningful amount of water to evaporation before it reaches the soil — 20 to 30 percent on a hot windy day. Morning is cool, calm, and the water gets where it should.

**Wind.** Mornings are typically the calmest part of the day. Less drift, more accurate coverage.

If you can only water in the evening (work schedule, rate restrictions, automatic timer that won't change), get it done early enough that the leaf is dry before dark — 4 pm to 6 pm in summer.

What to avoid: late evening and overnight watering. The lawn stays wet from your irrigation through to the morning dew, then through to mid-morning when the dew finally lifts. That's a 10 to 14 hour wet window every day. Disease pressure climbs accordingly.

If your irrigation timer can be set, programme it for 5 am or 6 am. Most modern systems handle multiple zones across a window without issue.
