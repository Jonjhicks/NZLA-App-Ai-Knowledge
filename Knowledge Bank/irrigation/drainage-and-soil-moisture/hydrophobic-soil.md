# What is hydrophobic soil and how do I fix it?

Hydrophobic means water-repellent. The soil particles develop a waxy coating from decomposing organic matter, especially after a hot dry spell, and water won't soak in. Drop water on the surface and it beads up and rolls off rather than penetrating.

Symptoms are dry patches that won't take water no matter how much you irrigate, hard crusty soil surface, grass thinning out in those spots while neighbouring areas stay green.

The fix is a wetting agent. NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W both work, the difference is residual length. Apply at label rate, water in straight after with a decent irrigation (5 to 10 mm), and you should see water moving back into the soil within a day or two.

For prevention, regular wetting agent use through summer keeps soils receptive. A single application in October that lasts through the heat of summer is the cheapest preventative against dry patch. Wetter 3W has a longer residual so one application can carry the whole hot stretch.

Don't use household dish soap as a substitute. It works briefly but kills soil biology and can damage the lawn at the rates you'd need. Use proper turf-grade wetting agents.
