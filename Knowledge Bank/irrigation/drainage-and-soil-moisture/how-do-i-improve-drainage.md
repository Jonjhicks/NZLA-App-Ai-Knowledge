# How do I improve drainage in my lawn?

Three levels depending on how bad the problem is.

Surface level: regular aeration plus a wetting agent. Core or solid tine aeration once or twice a year, ideally followed by a top dressing of sandy loam to fill the holes. NZLA Aerate applied with the irrigation through summer keeps water moving down through compacted profiles. NZLA Wetter on hydrophobic patches gets water soaking in rather than running off.

Soil profile level: top dressing with a sandier mix over a few seasons gradually improves a heavy clay lawn. Coring then top dressing fills the channels with better material. Doing it once doesn't change much, doing it annually for three or four years does noticeably improve a heavy-soil lawn.

Subsoil level: where the issue is genuinely a water table or no exit for water, you need physical drainage. Slotted pipe in gravel trenches running to a soakage or stormwater outlet. That's earthworks territory, not a DIY hour on a Saturday.

Start at the top. Most home-lawn drainage problems improve with consistent aeration and wetting agent use without going underground.
