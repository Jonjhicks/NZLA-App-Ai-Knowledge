# Why does water pool on my lawn after rain?

A few possible causes, often more than one at the same time.

Compacted soil is the most common. Heavy clay, foot traffic, kids playing, mower wheels in the same lines, all of it compresses the soil over time and the water has nowhere to go. Aeration (core or solid tine) opens up the profile and lets water move down. NZLA Aerate (penetrant wetter) helps water break through surface tension into compacted soil.

Hydrophobic soil is the next likely culprit. Sandy or organic-matter-poor soils develop a waxy coating on the particles in dry conditions and water beads off instead of soaking in. NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W applied to the lawn breaks the surface tension and gets water moving back into the soil.

A high water table or genuinely poor subsoil drainage can sit underneath both of the above. If your lawn is at the bottom of a slope and the water has nowhere to escape, surface treatment isn't going to fix it. Subsoil drainage (slotted pipe in gravel) may be needed for serious cases.

Start with aeration and a wetting agent. That solves most home-lawn pooling.
