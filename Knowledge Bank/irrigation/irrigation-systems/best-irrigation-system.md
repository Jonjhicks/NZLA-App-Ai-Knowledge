# What's the best irrigation system for a home lawn?

Depends on the lawn size and what you want to spend.

For a small lawn (under 100 m²), a decent oscillating sprinkler on a hose is genuinely fine. Move it around the lawn over a couple of hours, use containers to check you're putting down 12 to 15 mm. Cheap, low-maintenance, works.

For a medium lawn (100 to 500 m²), a permanent pop-up system is worth the investment. Spray heads on lateral lines, valves on a controller, runs while you sleep. Cost varies a lot depending on layout, water pressure, and whether you DIY or get it installed. Typically $2,000 to $6,000 installed for a medium home lawn.

For larger lawns and lifestyle blocks, rotary heads (Hunter PGP or similar) throw water further so you need fewer heads. Higher pressure required and slightly more expensive per head, but the coverage is better.

Smart controllers (Hunter Hydrawise, Rain Bird ESP) are the upgrade worth paying for if you go with an automated system. They use rainfall data and evapotranspiration calculations to skip waterings when they're not needed. Decent water and money savings over a standard timer.

Whatever you install, design it for full head-to-head coverage so every spot gets the same amount of water. Patchy coverage is the most common installation mistake.
