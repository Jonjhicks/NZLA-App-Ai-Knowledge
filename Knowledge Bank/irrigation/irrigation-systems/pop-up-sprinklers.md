# Are pop-up sprinklers worth installing?

If you've got a decent-sized lawn and you want it consistently good through summer, yes. The water gets put down evenly, on a schedule, regardless of whether you remember to drag the hose out. That's the main argument.

The other argument is consistency. Lawns that get watered properly and regularly through summer are vastly better than lawns that get the hose treatment when someone notices it's looking stressed. Disease pressure is lower because you can water at the right time of day (early morning), drought stress is lower, fertiliser results are better because the lawn isn't constantly stop-starting on water availability.

The downsides are cost (a few thousand dollars installed for a typical home lawn), some maintenance (heads need cleaning, the odd line repair), and the install itself can be disruptive if you're retrofitting into an existing lawn.

If you're laying a new lawn from scratch, install the irrigation before you sow seed or lay turf. Vastly easier than retrofitting later.

For lawns under about 100 m² the math gets thinner. A good hose-end sprinkler may do the job for a fraction of the cost.
