# Do I really need an irrigation system?

Depends what you want out of the lawn.

If you're happy for the lawn to brown off in summer and bounce back in autumn, no. Established cool-season turf in NZ survives most summer dry spells without intervention. It looks rubbish for a few weeks but it'll be there in March.

If you want the lawn looking good year-round through summer, yes. Reliable irrigation is the single biggest factor that separates a good-looking summer lawn from a tired one. You can have the best fertiliser programme and the best mowing in the country, if the lawn's dehydrated it's not going to perform.

The middle ground is hand-watering or a hose-end sprinkler. That works for small lawns and committed owners. It falls apart on bigger areas or when you go away for a week in February.

A few alternatives or partial solutions:
- Choose drought-tolerant grass species at establishment. Tall fescue, warm-season grasses in the upper North Island.
- Improve soil to hold more water. Top dressing with compost, getting organic matter into a sandy soil.
- Use wetting agents through summer (NZLA Wetter, Wetter 3W) so the water you do apply gets used efficiently.

For most quarter-acre lawns where the owner cares about appearance, pop-up irrigation pays itself back in lawn quality within a couple of summers.
