# Are smart irrigation controllers worth it?

Yes, if you've got a multi-zone irrigation system. Hunter Hydrawise and Rain Bird ESP-Me are the most common in NZ. They use local weather data (rainfall, evapotranspiration, forecast) to skip waterings when they're not needed and adjust run times based on actual conditions.

The water savings are real. Most homes see 20 to 40 percent less water use compared to a standard timer that runs the same schedule regardless of weather. Pays itself back over a few seasons on water bills alone in metered areas, and the lawn usually looks better because it's not getting watered through rain or in cool overcast spells when it doesn't need it.

App control is the other big benefit. Adjust schedules from your phone, skip a watering remotely, get alerts if a zone isn't running. Useful when you're away or the weather changes.

Setup is a bit more involved than a basic timer. You need wifi at the controller and a bit of patience to set up zones, plant types, soil types and so on. Once it's running it's largely set-and-forget.

If you've got a simple one-zone hose-end system, a basic battery tap timer for $80 is plenty. Smart controllers earn their keep on permanent multi-zone systems.
