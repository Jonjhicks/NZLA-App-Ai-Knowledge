# What's the best sprinkler for watering a new lawn?

You want a fine even spray rather than big heavy droplets. Heavy droplets dislodge seed and create craters in soft prepared soil.

An oscillating sprinkler with a fine spray setting works well for most home-lawn sizes. Cheap, even coverage, easy to move around. Put it on a low pressure if you can to get a finer spray.

Impact sprinklers (the tick-tick-tick type) throw bigger droplets and aren't ideal for fresh seed. Better suited to established lawns.

For very small new patches (repairs, over-seeding bare spots) a fine-rose watering can or a hose with a fan-spray nozzle on the gentlest setting does the job. Hands-on but precise.

If you've got a permanent pop-up system, the spray heads are fine. Just adjust the run time to short frequent cycles rather than the standard long deep watering you'd use on an established lawn.

Whatever you use, set it up so seed doesn't move and the surface stays evenly moist. Puddles are bad, dry spots are worse.
