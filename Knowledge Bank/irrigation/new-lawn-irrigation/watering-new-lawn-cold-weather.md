# How do I water a new lawn in cooler weather?

Less than you'd think. Cool wet conditions mean evaporation is low and the surface stays moist longer, so light frequent watering is easier to overdo.

For new seed in autumn or spring, the rule is keep the surface moist but not waterlogged from sowing until germination (around 7 to 14 days for ryegrass). Two to three light waterings a day for short bursts is the standard. Each one might only be a couple of minutes if the soil's still damp from the morning's irrigation.

Once seedlings are up and the second leaf is appearing, scale back to one decent watering a day, then to every second day as the roots develop. By six to eight weeks you should be on a normal mature-lawn watering pattern.

If natural rainfall is doing the job, don't add to it. Saturated soil drowns seedlings, encourages damping-off disease, and washes seed around.

Watch the weather. A run of rainy days might mean no irrigation needed for a week. A sudden warm dry spell after a cold start might mean catching up on a missed afternoon.
