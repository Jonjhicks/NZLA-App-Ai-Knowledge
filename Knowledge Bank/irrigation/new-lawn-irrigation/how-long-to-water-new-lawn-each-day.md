# How long should I water my new lawn each day?

Do not think in minutes per day — think in surface moisture. The job during establishment is to keep the top 5 to 10 mm of soil damp until the seedlings are up and rooted.

For the first 2 to 3 weeks after sowing, that usually means light frequent watering — two to three short bursts a day in warm conditions, just enough to wet the surface without pooling. In cooler weather one good morning watering might do the same job.

Once seedlings are up and growing (typically 10 to 14 days), back off the frequency and increase the depth. Two or three good waterings a week, each going deep enough to wet 50 to 75 mm of soil, encourages the roots to chase moisture downwards rather than staying shallow.

By 6 to 8 weeks post-sowing, the lawn should be on roughly weekly deep watering — same as an established lawn.

Watch the actual lawn, not the clock. If the surface is drying out between waterings during establishment, increase frequency. If you are seeing waterlogged patches, back off.

New lawns kill themselves both ways — dried out is most common, drowned is the second. Surface moisture without pooling is the goal.
