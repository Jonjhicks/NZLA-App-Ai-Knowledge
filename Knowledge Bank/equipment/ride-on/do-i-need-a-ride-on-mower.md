# Do I need a ride-on mower?

Depends on lawn size, terrain, and time available for mowing.

When a ride-on makes sense:

**Lawn over 1500m².** Push mowing becomes impractically time-consuming. Ride-on mowers cover the ground 3 to 5 times faster than push mowers.

**Lifestyle blocks and rural properties.** Substantial mowed area, often with rough terrain that suits ride-on capability.

**Time-pressured owners with large properties.** Cutting a 2000m² lawn with a push mower takes 2 to 3 hours. A ride-on does the same in 45 minutes.

**Properties with diverse terrain.** Ride-ons handle slopes (within reason), uneven ground, and longer mowing routes better than push equipment.

**Physical limitations of the user.** Push mowing is physically demanding. Ride-ons remove that demand.

When you don't need a ride-on:

**Lawn under 1000m².** Push mowers handle this size comfortably in 30 to 60 minutes. Ride-on is overkill.

**Lawn under 500m².** A push mower is the right tool. Ride-on doesn't manoeuvre well in small areas.

**Heavily obstructed lawns.** Trees, garden beds, narrow passages — ride-ons struggle with frequent obstacles.

**Steep slopes.** Standard ride-on mowers have safety limits around 15° slope. Steeper terrain needs specialist equipment.

Types of ride-on equipment:

**Lawn tractors.** Standard ride-on mowers with steering wheel, mowing deck underneath, various attachment options (towed sprayers, spreaders, trailers). $3,000 to $10,000+ for quality units. Suit most home and rural properties.

**Zero-turn mowers.** Specialised mowers with separate drive controls for each wheel, enabling pivoting on the spot. Much faster around obstacles than lawn tractors. $5,000 to $20,000+. Common on larger commercial properties.

**Rear-engine ride-ons.** Compact ride-ons with rear-mounted engines. Cheaper than tractors but less capable. Suitable for medium-sized lawns where space is limited.

**Garden tractors.** Larger heavier units suited to substantial mowing plus other property tasks (trailer towing, light tilling). For owners with 5000m²+ of mowed area and other property work.

What to consider:

**Deck size.** Larger decks (38" / 950mm or larger) cover ground faster. Smaller decks (30" / 760mm) navigate obstacles better.

**Engine.** Quality brand (Briggs & Stratton, Kohler, Kawasaki) lasts longer than budget engines. Hp matters for cutting heavy grass and pulling attachments.

**Transmission.** Hydrostatic transmission is the standard for quality ride-ons — smoother operation than gear-driven alternatives.

**Cutting height range.** Standard ride-on heights 25 to 100 mm. Check the range suits your lawn.

**Catcher / mulcher / discharge options.** Different deck configurations for different needs. Most ride-ons offer all three options as standard or optional.

**Width of access.** Measure gates, garage doors, and any narrow passages. Some quality ride-ons are too wide for standard residential access.

**Service support.** Buy from dealers who service the brand. Ride-ons need periodic professional servicing for engine, transmission, deck adjustment.

Costs:

**Entry-level ride-on:** $3,000 to $5,000. Capable for typical home lawn use.

**Mid-range quality:** $5,000 to $10,000. Better engines, transmissions, deck quality.

**Premium home or light commercial:** $10,000 to $20,000+. Professional-grade equipment.

**Annual operating cost:** $200 to $600 for fuel, oil, blades, service. Higher with heavy use.

Hire vs buy:

Ride-on hire is available but expensive — $250 to $500 per day. Realistic only for occasional use on rented or vacation properties.

For owners with ride-on-justifying lawn size, buying is virtually always more economical than hire over the equipment's 10 to 15 year lifespan.

Robot mowers as alternative:

For some properties, robot mowers offer an alternative to ride-ons. Continuous low-impact mowing handles up to several thousand square metres. Significant upfront cost ($2,500 to $8,000) but no ongoing fuel or operator time. Worth considering if the lawn shape suits robot mowing.

For most NZ properties under 1500m²:

Push mower is the right tool. Ride-on is overkill.

For lifestyle blocks, rural properties, and large home lawns:

Ride-on is the practical answer. Choose based on lawn size, terrain, and budget.

Don't underestimate the time difference. A ride-on can transform lawn care from a multi-hour weekend job into a quick weekly routine.
