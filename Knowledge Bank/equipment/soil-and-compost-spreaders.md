# Topdressing Spreaders

Topdressing spreaders apply sand, sandy loam, or compost to a lawn in a controlled even layer. Different from fertiliser spreaders — they're designed for much larger material volumes and the different consistency of soil-based materials.

For most NZ home lawns, topdressing is occasional work rather than a routine. Whether you need specialist equipment depends on how often you do it and how large your lawn is.

## Options

**Manual application (wheelbarrow and rake):** for small to medium lawns with occasional topdressing, this is perfectly adequate. Calculate volume needed (5 mm layer × area = cubic metres), dump small piles across the lawn every 5 to 10 metres, spread and work material into the canopy with a stiff yard broom. Effective and requires no specialist equipment.

For 100 m² at 5 mm, you need 0.5 m³ — roughly 700 kg of sandy loam. Real physical work. Plan accordingly.

**Drag mat:** a mat of metal links or chains towed behind a ride-on mower or quad bike. Dump topdressing material in piles across the lawn first, then drag the mat over to distribute and work it into the canopy. $100 to $400 for home-sized units. The easiest upgrade if you already have a ride-on mower. Effective for lawns up to 1500 m².

**Walk-behind topdressing spreader:** a wheeled hopper designed for soil materials. More precise distribution than a drag mat. $500 to $2,000 for quality home and prosumer units. Worth the investment for owners topdressing 2 to 4 times per year on lawns 500 to 2000 m².

**Tow-behind topdressing spreader:** larger capacity, towed behind ride-on. For large properties and regular programmes. $1,500 to $10,000+. More commonly owned by professionals and sports turf operations.

## Material handling is often the bigger challenge

Topdressing materials are dense — typically 1.3 to 1.5 tonnes per cubic metre of sandy loam. For a 300 m² lawn at 5 mm depth, you need roughly 2 tonnes of material. Getting that delivered to a usable location, moving it by wheelbarrow, and spreading it is significant physical work regardless of the equipment you use. Plan delivery access, wheelbarrow routes, and realistic time before starting.

## Practical decision

**Occasional topdressing, lawn under 200 m²:** manual application. No specialist equipment needed.

**Annual or biennial topdressing, lawn 200 to 1000 m²:** drag mat if you have a ride-on; otherwise manual.

**Regular topdressing programme, lawn 1000 m²+:** walk-behind topdressing spreader justifies the investment.

Hire from equipment hire shops is sometimes available ($80 to $150 per day for walk-behind units). For a one-off project, hiring makes more sense than buying.
