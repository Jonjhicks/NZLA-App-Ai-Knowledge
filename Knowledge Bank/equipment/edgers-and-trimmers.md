# Edgers and Line Trimmers

A line trimmer (or whipper snipper) handles the edges and areas a mower can't reach. Sharp edges make a mowed lawn look tidy rather than just cut. For most NZ home lawns, this is the second most important piece of equipment after the mower.

## Electric vs petrol

**Electric (cordless battery)** suits most home lawns. Quieter, lower maintenance, no fuel mixing. A quality 36V or 40V cordless trimmer handles typical home lawn edges without issue. Runtime on a 4Ah battery is 30 to 60 minutes, which covers most home lawns comfortably. Lighter than petrol equivalents, easier to use for extended sessions. Battery replacement cost ($150 to $300) in 3 to 5 years is worth noting.

**Petrol** suits larger properties and heavier work. More power than equivalent electric, no battery limits, better for long grass and edge clearing. Louder, heavier, requires fuel and periodic maintenance (spark plug, air filter, carburettor). Two-stroke models need fuel mixing.

For most NZ home lawns under 500 m²: cordless electric is the right choice. For lifestyle blocks, larger properties, and heavy use: petrol.

The cordless trimmer market has improved substantially in recent years. A quality modern cordless model from an established brand (Husqvarna, Stihl, Makita, Ryobi) handles home lawn use as well as mid-range petrol.

## Buying a trimmer

Look for: adjustable shaft length (suit different users and terrain), lightweight construction, bump-feed or auto-feed head (easier than fixed-blade refills), quality brand with local service support.

Spend enough to get quality. Cheap trimmers fail fast; a good unit lasts 10+ years with basic maintenance.

## Replacing trimmer line

Most home trimmers use bump-feed heads — a spool of line inside the head that feeds out when you bump the head against the ground.

To replace: stop the trimmer, disconnect power. Press the tabs on the head cover and lift off. Remove the empty spool. Wind 3 to 5 metres of new line onto the spool following the direction arrows on the spool housing (winding against the arrow prevents feed). Leave 150 to 200 mm trailing through the exit hole. Reassemble, ensuring the line passes out through the slot.

Common mistakes: winding the wrong direction, overfilling (spool jams), using the wrong diameter line, not leaving enough trailing.

Line diameter: 1.6 to 2.0 mm for light grass, 2.4 to 3.0 mm for heavier work. Check the trimmer's specified range.

Quality line is worth the extra cost. Cheap line tangles in the head, wears fast, and breaks constantly.

## Dedicated edgers

Some owners prefer a dedicated lawn edger (wheel-guided rotary blade) for defined strip edging along paths and beds, rather than using the trimmer tilted on its side. Dedicated edgers produce very straight clean edges. Worth considering if edge definition matters to you and you edge frequently. Less essential if your trimmer handle allows good control when tilted.

## Maintenance

Clean the head after each use. Remove grass wrapped around the drive shaft. Check for line tangles. For petrol trimmers: annual service (spark plug, air filter, fuel filter). For electric: keep battery contacts clean, store batteries partially charged rather than flat or full.
