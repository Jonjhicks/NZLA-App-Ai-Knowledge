# Reel Mowers

A reel mower (cylinder mower) cuts differently to a rotary. Where a rotary chops with a spinning horizontal blade, a reel uses a cylinder of curved blades rotating against a fixed bedknife — a scissor cut. The grass blade gets sliced cleanly rather than torn. The difference in cut quality is visible, especially at lower heights.

## Why reel mowing matters

The clean scissor cut heals faster than a rotary tear. Less water loss from the cut tip. Less  entry. At heights below 25 mm, a reel mower produces results a rotary can't match — the lawn looks tight and refined rather than just mowed.

Reel mowers also produce better stripe definition. The rear roller bends grass blades in one direction as you mow, creating the visible light-and-dark pattern associated with premium turf. Sports grounds, bowling greens, and showcase home lawns all use reel mowers for this reason.

## The trade-offs

More expensive. More maintenance. The reel-to-bedknife gap needs precise setting — too tight and the blades clash, too loose and the cut is poor. Getting this adjusted correctly (called backlapping) requires either skill or a professional service. Annual professional servicing is realistic for most reel mower owners.

Reel mowers don't handle long grass. Leave the lawn for a fortnight and you'll need a rotary to bring it back down before the reel can work again. Commitment to frequent mowing (once or twice a week in active growth) is part of the reel mowing deal.

They don't handle debris well either. Stones, sticks, and hard objects damage the reel and bedknife. Lawn surface needs to be clean and reasonably smooth.

## Manual vs petrol

**Manual push reel mowers** ($200 to $600) are quiet, emission-free, low maintenance, and work well on lawns up to about 200 m². Practical for owners who mow weekly and enjoy the work. Good exercise.

**Petrol reel mowers** ($2,000 to $10,000+) cover ground efficiently and cut consistently regardless of operator pace. For premium home lawns from 200 to 1500 m² where cut quality is the priority. Significant investment and requires professional servicing.

**Battery reel mowers** exist but are limited in NZ availability currently.

## When reel mowing makes sense

Premium home lawns maintained below 25 mm where cut quality shows. MDR, Dark Perennial Rye, browntop, or bentgrass lawns where the variety deserves to be shown at its best. Owners prepared to mow frequently and maintain the equipment properly.

## When it doesn't

Standard home lawns at 30 to 50 mm. Lawns with rough surfaces, debris, or irregular terrain. Owners mowing less than weekly during active growth.

For most NZ home lawns, a well-maintained rotary mower with sharp blades is entirely sufficient. A reel mower is specialist equipment for owners who care about the next level of cut quality.
