# What is a topdressing spreader?

A topdressing spreader is a tool for applying sand, sandy loam, or compost to a lawn in a controlled even layer. Different from fertiliser spreaders — designed for the much larger material volumes and different consistency of topdressing material.

Types of topdressing spreaders:

**Drag mat or chain harrow.** Simple tool consisting of a mat made of metal links, chains, or stiff bristles, towed behind a ride-on mower or quad bike. Pulled across material spread on the lawn (typically dumped from a wheelbarrow or hopper).

How it works: dump topdressing in piles across the lawn, drag the mat across the lawn surface, the mat distributes the material evenly across the area and works it down into the canopy.

Cost: $100 to $400 for home-sized drag mats.

Effective for home use on lawns up to 1500m². Becomes time-consuming on larger areas.

**Walk-behind topdressing spreader.** Wheeled unit with a hopper that holds and dispenses topdressing material. Wider hopper than a fertiliser spreader, and a different feed mechanism designed for granular soil materials.

Cost: $500 to $2,000 for quality home and prosumer units.

More accurate distribution than drag mats. Suited to regular topdressing programmes on lawns 500 to 2000m².

**Tow-behind topdressing spreader.** Larger hopper capacity, towed behind ride-on mower or tractor. Used on larger properties and sports turf.

Cost: $1,500 to $10,000+ depending on size and quality.

Suited to commercial use or large home properties.

**Manual application.** For smaller lawns, manual application with a wheelbarrow and rake works fine. Time-consuming but no specialist equipment required.

How to topdress with manual application:

Calculate quantity needed (5 mm thickness × area gives volume in cubic metres).

Dump material in small piles across the lawn (every 5 to 10 metres).

Use a stiff yard broom to push and spread the material from each pile.

Work it down into the canopy with the broom or a drag mat.

Result: even 3 to 5 mm layer across the whole lawn.

When you need specialist equipment:

**Regular topdressing programme.** If topdressing 2 to 4 times a year, equipment justifies the investment.

**Larger lawns where manual application becomes impractical.** Once an area takes more than a couple of hours to topdress manually, equipment pays off.

**Premium lawn standards where consistency matters.** Drag mats and specialist spreaders produce more consistent results than manual application.

When manual application is sufficient:

**Occasional topdressing** — once a year or less. Specialist equipment doesn't justify the investment.

**Small lawns** — under 200m² can be done manually in reasonable time.

**Spot topdressing** — targeting specific patches rather than the whole lawn.

Hire considerations:

Drag mats are inexpensive enough to buy if you have a ride-on mower.

Walk-behind topdressing spreaders are sometimes available from equipment hire shops — $80 to $150 per day.

Tow-behind units rarely available for hire — typically owned by professional contractors or sports turf operations.

Material handling:

Topdressing materials are dense (typically 1.3 to 1.5 tonnes per cubic metre for sandy loam). Moving the material to where you need it is often more work than the application itself.

For 5 mm topdressing across 100m²: roughly 0.5 cubic metres = roughly 700 kg of material.

For a typical home lawn project (300m²): 1.5 cubic metres = roughly 2 tonnes of material.

This is real physical work. Plan for delivery to a convenient location, wheelbarrow access, and rest breaks during application.

For lawns with restricted access (steep driveways, narrow paths), the material handling challenge can be the limiting factor.

Practical NZ home lawn approach:

**Small lawn (under 200m²), occasional topdressing:** manual application with wheelbarrow and rake. No specialist equipment needed.

**Medium lawn (200 to 1000m²), annual topdressing:** drag mat behind ride-on mower if you have one. Otherwise manual.

**Large lawn (1000m²+), regular topdressing programme:** consider walk-behind topdressing spreader or tow-behind unit.

For most NZ home lawn owners:

Topdressing is occasional work — not a routine that justifies specialist equipment.

Hire a wheelbarrow and stiff broom if needed, do the work manually, and save the equipment investment for more frequently-used tools.

For owners with larger properties, regular topdressing programmes, or premium lawn standards:

Equipment investment is worthwhile. A drag mat is the easiest entry point if you have a ride-on mower.

Quality matters. Cheap drag mats fall apart quickly; quality units last decades.
