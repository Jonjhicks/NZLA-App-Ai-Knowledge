# What lawn care equipment do I need?

Depends on your lawn size and the level of care you want to achieve. The essential kit, in priority order:

**Tier 1 — Essential for any lawn.**

**Mower.** The fundamental tool. Rotary mower suits most home lawns. Reel mower for premium showcase lawns at low mowing heights.

**Line trimmer.** For edging and trimming around obstacles. Cordless electric works well for most home lawns.

**Garden hose with sprinkler or watering setup.** For irrigation through dry periods. Permanent irrigation system is the upgrade for larger lawns or busy owners.

**Rake.** Hand rake (fan rake or leaf rake) for thatch removal, debris clearing and seed-bed preparation. A metal-tined rake is more versatile than plastic.

**Garden fork.** For manual aeration of small areas, fork through compacted patches, general garden use.

**Tier 2 — Substantially improves lawn care quality.**

**Spreader.** For accurate fertiliser and seed application. Rotary spreader for larger lawns; drop spreader for premium small lawns and precision.

**Knapsack sprayer.** For applying foliar fertiliser, selective herbicide, fungicide. 5 to 15 litre capacity suits most home lawns.

**Half-moon edger or sharp flat spade.** For defining and restoring lawn edges. Done twice a year, makes a real difference to lawn appearance.

**Mower blade sharpening tool.** Bench grinder, sharpening file, or sharpening kit. Sharp blades transform lawn quality.

**Tier 3 — For premium lawn care or larger areas.**

**Aerator (or hire access to one).** Core aerator for annual or biennial compaction relief. Hire rather than own for most home users.

**Scarifier (or hire access).** For thatch removal and lawn renovation. Hire annually.

**Roller.** Light roller for seed bed preparation, smoothing minor unevenness. Manual push roller for home use.

**Soil thermometer.** For pre-em timing, seeding decisions, growth assessment. Optional but useful for keen lawn managers.

**Soil testing kit or lab access.** Annual soil test informs fertility programme.

**Tier 4 — For larger lawns or commercial use.**

Ride-on mower for lawns over 1000m².

Robot mower for owners wanting automated maintenance.

Larger sprayer (10 to 25 litre) for substantial lawn areas.

Topdressing spreader (drag mat or specialty tool) for regular topdressing programmes.

PPE essentials:

Closed-toe shoes (steel-toe better) for any mowing or chemistry work.

Eye protection for chemistry application.

Chemical-resistant gloves for handling herbicide, fungicide, fertiliser.

Mask or respirator for dusty granular application or chemical spraying.

Hearing protection for petrol equipment.

Maintenance tools:

Air pump for mower and equipment tyres.

Spark plug socket and basic tool kit for petrol equipment.

Funnel and measuring cup for fuel mixing (2-stroke equipment).

Cleaning brush and rags for equipment cleanup after use.

Investment levels for a typical NZ home lawn:

**Basic setup (Tier 1 only):** $400 to $1,200. Rotary mower, line trimmer, hose, rake, fork.

**Comfortable lawn care (Tier 1 + 2):** $800 to $2,500. Adds spreader, sprayer, edging tools, sharpening.

**Premium home lawn care (Tier 1 + 2 + 3):** $1,500 to $4,000. Adds aerator (hired), scarifier (hired), roller, soil testing.

**Showcase lawn or larger property:** $3,000 to $10,000+. Adds ride-on or robot mower, larger sprayer, professional-grade tools.

Most home lawn owners can start with Tier 1 essentials, add Tier 2 as needs become clear, and decide on Tier 3+ based on the lawn's scale and the owner's interest level.

Quality matters more than quantity. A few good quality tools outperform a shed full of cheap equipment.
