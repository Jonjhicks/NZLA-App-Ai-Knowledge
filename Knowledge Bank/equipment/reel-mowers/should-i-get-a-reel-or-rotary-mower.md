# Should I get a reel mower or a rotary mower?

Depends on what you want from your lawn.

**Rotary mowers** spin a single blade horizontally and cut by chopping the grass with the leading edge. Cheap to buy, easy to maintain, work at almost any mowing height from 20 mm up to 80 mm, and handle long grass or rough conditions without complaint. The cut quality is acceptable rather than excellent — at higher heights the blade can rip rather than slice, especially when blunt.

**Reel mowers** (cylinder mowers) cut by pressing the grass between a rotating cylinder of blades and a fixed bedknife — a scissor-style cut. Cut quality is dramatically better, particularly at low heights. The cut tip is clean, the lawn looks tighter, and stripe definition is sharper. Trade-offs: more expensive, more maintenance (the cylinder and bedknife need precise adjustment and regular sharpening), don't handle long grass well (will jam), and need a flat lawn — slopes and bumps cause scalping.

For most home lawns, a rotary mower is the right tool. Cheap, reliable, capable.

For showcase or premium lawns mowed below 25 mm — bowling greens, golf greens, premium home turf running MDR or Dark Perennial Rye — a reel mower transforms the appearance. The cut quality is the difference between a tidy lawn and a stunning one.

The bigger lift on a showcase lawn isn't the mower itself, it's mowing more often. Whatever you're mowing with, sharp blades and frequent mowing matter more than the mechanism.
