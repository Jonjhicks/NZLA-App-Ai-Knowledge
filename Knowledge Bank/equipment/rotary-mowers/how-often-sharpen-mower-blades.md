# How often should I sharpen my mower blades?

Roughly every 20 to 25 hours of mowing, or twice a season for most home users — once heading into spring, once before peak summer growth.

The honest answer is to check the cut, not the calendar. Bend down and look at the grass tip a day after mowing. A sharp blade cuts cleanly, the tip stays green and tidy. A blunt blade tears the leaf, leaving a ragged white or brown end. If you are seeing that ragged tip, the blade is overdue.

Blunt blades matter more than people think. The torn leaf end loses water faster, takes longer to heal, and creates an open wound that fungal pathogens use to get into the plant. Sharp blades are a real cultural disease prevention step, especially heading into cool damp conditions where Fusarium and red thread pressure climbs.

Reel mowers need more attention than rotary. A reel cut depends on the blade meeting the bedknife correctly, which drifts out over use. Get them adjusted and sharpened by someone who knows what they are doing if you are not confident.

For rotary blades, a basic file or a bench grinder does the job. Just do not take too much metal off, and balance the blade after sharpening so the mower does not vibrate.
