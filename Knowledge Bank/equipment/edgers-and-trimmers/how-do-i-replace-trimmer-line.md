# How do I replace trimmer line?

Depends on the trimmer head type. Three common systems.

**Bump feed heads.** Most common on home trimmers.

The head contains a spool of line. When you bump the head against the ground while running, a mechanism feeds out additional line and trims it to length against a built-in blade.

To replace:

1. Stop the trimmer, remove battery or disconnect spark plug for safety.
2. Open the head — typically by pressing tabs and lifting the cover, or unscrewing the bottom cap.
3. Remove the empty spool.
4. Cut new line — typically 3 to 5 metres of 2.0 to 2.4 mm diameter line.
5. Insert one end through the central hole in the spool, then wind onto the spool following the direction arrows (winding the wrong direction prevents feed).
6. Leave 150 to 200 mm of line trailing out through the feed hole.
7. Reassemble the head, ensuring the line passes through the exit hole.

Some bump heads have two lines wound separately — load both following the same pattern.

**Fixed-blade heads.** Plastic blades or pre-cut line strips mounted in the head.

To replace:

Simply remove the worn blade or line strip and insert a new one. Follow the manufacturer's specific instructions for your model.

Faster than spool replacement but more expensive over time (consumables cost more).

**Automatic feed heads.** More sophisticated mechanism that feeds line automatically based on length sensors.

To replace:

Similar to bump feed — open the head, remove spool, wind new line on, reassemble. Specifics vary by brand.

Common mistakes:

**Winding the wrong direction.** Each head has a direction marked (usually with arrows on the spool or inside the head). Winding against the arrow prevents the feed from working. Check before winding.

**Loading too much line.** Overfilled spools jam. Stay within the manufacturer's recommended line length.

**Using wrong line diameter.** Each trimmer head is designed for a specific line diameter range. Too thin and the head doesn't feed properly. Too thick and the head can't accept it.

**Tangling the line.** Wind under tension to prevent tangles. Loose winding causes the line to tangle inside the head when it tries to feed.

**Not leaving enough trailing.** The line needs to extend through the exit hole to engage the trimming action when you start. Leave 150 to 200 mm trailing.

Line selection:

Quality of line affects performance significantly. Cheap line wears fast, tangles in the head, breaks frequently.

Quality line ($15 to $30 per spool) lasts much longer and trims more cleanly.

Diameter: 1.6 to 2.0 mm for light home use. 2.4 to 3.0 mm for heavier work. Check your trimmer's specification.

Shape: round line is standard. Twisted, square or star-shaped profiles cut more aggressively but wear faster.

How often:

Replace line as it wears down. The natural wear pattern is that line shortens over time — replace when there's not enough to trim effectively.

For typical home use, a 3 to 5 metre load lasts several sessions. Heavy use might need replacement weekly during active growing season.

When to replace the whole spool:

Standard bump-feed spools are inexpensive ($10 to $30). Replace the whole spool when the line wears out rather than refilling on the same spool. Saves time.

Pre-wound spools (sold ready to fit) cost slightly more but save the loading process.

For routine maintenance, keep a spare loaded spool in your equipment storage so you can swap quickly during a session.
