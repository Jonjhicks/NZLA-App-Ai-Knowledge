# Should I get an electric or petrol trimmer?

Depends on your lawn size, budget and use pattern.

**Electric trimmers** (corded or cordless battery).

Pros:

Quieter operation. Suits early morning or evening use without disturbing neighbours.

Lower vibration. Easier on hands and arms over longer use.

No fuel mixing or carburetor issues. Push button start.

Lower maintenance. No air filters, spark plugs, or seasonal storage prep.

Cordless models give 30 to 60 minutes runtime on a typical 4Ah battery — enough for most home lawns.

Lighter weight than equivalent petrol models.

Cleaner — no exhaust, no fuel smell.

Cons:

Corded models require an extension cord and are limited to lawns near power. Awkward managing the cord.

Cordless models limited by battery life. Larger lawns may need a spare battery.

Less power than petrol equivalents at the higher end. Heavy work (long grass, edge clearing) can stall electric models.

Battery replacement cost ($150 to $300+) when batteries wear out in 3 to 5 years.

**Petrol trimmers.**

Pros:

More power than electric equivalents. Handles heavy work without stalling.

No battery limitations. Run as long as you keep adding fuel.

Generally longer service life than electric equivalents.

Better suited to commercial or heavy use.

Cons:

Louder operation.

More vibration.

Fuel mixing required (2-stroke models). Some users find this off-putting.

Maintenance — air filter, spark plug, carburetor cleaning periodically.

Heavier than electric equivalents.

Exhaust and fuel smell.

How to choose:

**Small to medium home lawn (under 500m²), light use:** electric (cordless battery model preferred). Sufficient power, easier maintenance, quieter operation. NZ home lawns in this size range are well served by quality cordless electric.

**Large home lawn (500m²+), regular use:** depends on the situation. Quality cordless electric with spare battery works for many. Petrol for heavy users wanting consistent power.

**Lifestyle block, larger rural lawn:** petrol typically. Larger area, may include rougher growth that taxes electric models.

**Commercial or contractor use:** petrol generally. Heavy use, long sessions, full power needed.

Specific NZ considerations:

Most NZ home lawns are well-suited to cordless electric. The Australian and NZ brands (Husqvarna, Stihl, Ryobi, Makita, DeWalt) all offer cordless models with adequate power for typical home use.

Cordless lithium-ion technology has improved substantially in recent years — modern cordless trimmers outperform petrol models from a decade ago on most home lawns.

Brand quality matters more than electric vs petrol distinction. A premium electric trimmer outperforms a cheap petrol trimmer.

Line and head considerations:

For both electric and petrol, the cutting head and line choice affect performance more than people realise.

Use the right diameter line for your application — 1.6 to 2.0 mm for light grass trimming, 2.4 to 3.0 mm for heavier work.

Replace worn line regularly. Worn line tears rather than cuts.

Bump-feed heads, automatic feed heads, and fixed-blade heads all have advantages depending on the work.

Cost:

Quality cordless electric: $200 to $500 including battery.

Quality petrol: $300 to $700.

Premium professional models in either category: $500 to $1500+.

For most NZ home lawn use, a good quality cordless electric handles the job well.
