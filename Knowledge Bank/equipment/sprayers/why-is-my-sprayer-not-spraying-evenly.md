# Why is my sprayer not spraying evenly?

Six common causes. Most uneven spraying traces to one or two of these.

**1. Worn or damaged nozzle.** Nozzles wear with use — the precise orifice that creates the spray pattern enlarges over time, changing the spray width and pattern.

How to check: hold nozzle up to light. Compare to a new nozzle. Visible damage or enlarged orifice indicates wear.

Test the spray pattern by spraying water on dry concrete or paving — observe the pattern shape and evenness.

Fix: replace the nozzle. Standard maintenance, typically every 100 hours of use or annually for active users.

**2. Inconsistent pressure.** Pressure during spraying directly affects spray pattern and droplet size. Variable pressure produces variable spray.

How to check: most pressure sprayers and knapsacks have pressure gauges. Watch the gauge while spraying — should remain relatively constant.

For sprayers without gauges, sound and feel during pumping indicates pressure consistency.

Fix: pump continuously during spraying to maintain pressure. For sprayers with pressure regulators, ensure the regulator is functioning. For aging sprayers, the pump may need maintenance or replacement.

**3. Inconsistent walking pace.** The combination of nozzle output and walking pace determines application rate per square metre. Speeding up or slowing down during a spray pass changes application rate.

How to check: observe yourself spraying. Are you maintaining steady pace from start to finish of each pass?

Fix: conscious effort to maintain consistent pace. Use rhythmic counting or steady breathing to maintain timing.

**4. Inappropriate nozzle height above the lawn.** Spray nozzles are designed for specific spray heights. Too close concentrates the spray pattern; too far disperses it.

How to check: most flat fan nozzles work best at 400 to 600 mm above the target. Cone nozzles vary by design.

Fix: maintain consistent nozzle height during spraying. Adjust trigger position to keep height steady.

**5. Wind affecting spray pattern.** Wind blows light spray off the intended target. Even moderate wind significantly affects spray distribution.

How to check: observe wind conditions before and during spraying.

Fix: spray only in calm conditions. Cancel application if wind picks up during the session.

**6. Tank pressure dropping during pass.** Spray pattern changes as tank pressure drops between pumping. Late portions of a pass may apply less product than early portions.

How to check: observe spray pattern at start and end of a pass.

Fix: maintain continuous pumping. For pressure sprayers, pump until full pressure before spraying, then spray briefly before re-pressurising.

For knapsacks with continuous pumping, maintain consistent pump rate.

Other diagnostic considerations:

**Tank not vented properly.** Some sprayers need air inlet for water to flow out. Blocked vents create vacuum that affects flow.

**Filter blockage.** Clogged filters reduce flow and create uneven patterns.

**Chemical mixture viscosity.** Thick spray mixes (with high adjuvant content) spray differently than water. Adjust technique for the specific mixture.

**Temperature affecting viscosity.** Cold spray mix is thicker than warm mix. Spray performance differs.

How to test if your sprayer is applying evenly:

Set out plastic containers (e.g. small yogurt pots) at regular intervals across a test area — every 1 to 2 metres in a grid pattern.

Spray over the area as you normally would.

Measure water collected in each container. The amounts should be similar (within 10 to 15% of each other).

Significant variation indicates application issues — sprayer problems, technique issues, or both.

Application practice:

**Overlap passes by about 50%.** With most flat fan nozzles, the spray pattern is heaviest in the centre and lighter at the edges. Overlapping ensures even coverage when the lighter edges combine.

**Walk in two directions.** First pass north-south, then a second pass east-west at half rate each. Catches any unevenness from single-direction application.

**Adding NZLA Indicate (spray colourant)** lets you see exactly where you've sprayed. Easy to identify uneven coverage or missed areas. Worthwhile for first-time applications of new products.

For sprayers with persistent unevenness:

If routine maintenance (nozzle replacement, filter cleaning, technique improvement) doesn't fix it, the sprayer may need professional repair or replacement.

Quality sprayers maintained properly produce consistent applications for years. Cheap sprayers or neglected equipment produce inconsistent results regardless of operator technique.

For most home users:

Routine nozzle replacement, consistent technique, and pressure maintenance address most unevenness issues.

Adding NZLA Indicate to applications when accuracy matters helps verify coverage and identify any application problems immediately.
