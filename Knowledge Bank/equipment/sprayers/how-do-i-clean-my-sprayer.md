# How do I clean my sprayer?

Critical maintenance — proper cleaning prevents chemical contamination, equipment damage, and unintended damage to plants from residue.

**Routine cleaning (after each use).**

**Empty all remaining spray.** Don't store with chemicals in the tank. Dispose of spray remnants according to label instructions — typically dilute and apply to the same target area, or disposal per local council guidelines.

**Triple rinse the tank with clean water.**

First rinse: fill tank to about 25% with clean water. Pump to pressurise. Spray through the nozzle to flush the hose, valves, and nozzle. Empty.

Second rinse: repeat with another 25% water. Spray through all parts of the system.

Third rinse: final rinse with clean water.

The triple rinse removes essentially all chemical residue from the tank, hoses, and nozzle.

**Wipe down the exterior** with a damp cloth to remove any spilled chemical or splashes.

**Allow to drain and dry completely** before storage. Standing water in the tank can develop bacterial growth or freeze in cold conditions.

This routine takes 10 to 15 minutes after each use. Done consistently, prevents most contamination problems.

**Thorough cleaning (for specific chemistry transitions).**

When switching between certain product types, simple rinsing isn't enough — chemical residue can survive triple rinsing and damage subsequent applications.

**After herbicide use, before fertiliser or fungicide:**

Triple rinse with clean water.

Fill tank with clean water plus 100 mL ammonia per 10 L water.

Pump and spray through all parts of the system to circulate the ammonia solution.

Let stand for 1 to 2 hours.

Drain and triple rinse again with clean water.

Ammonia neutralises many herbicide residues. Particularly important for persistent chemistry like picloram (in NZLA Gold) and sulfonylureas (in NZLA Sedge Pro).

**After Sedge Pro (halosulfuron) specifically:**

Triple rinse with clean water.

Fill with clean water plus 250 mL chlorine bleach per 10 L water.

Pump and spray through all parts.

Let stand for 1 hour.

Drain and triple rinse with clean water.

Sulfonylureas are extraordinarily potent at trace concentrations. The chlorine bleach flush is the standard sprayer cleanout for Sedge Pro and similar chemistry.

**After NZLA Sticker (organosilicone) or other persistent adjuvants:**

Triple rinse with clean water.

Fill with clean water plus 50 mL dish soap per 10 L water.

Pump and spray through all parts.

Drain and triple rinse with clean water.

Why cleaning matters:

**Cross-contamination damage.** Even trace amounts of herbicide residue can damage subsequent applications. A sprayer that's just been used for Gold (picloram + triclopyr) will damage broadleaf garden plants in the next application even if the next product is just water.

**Dedicated sprayer recommendation.** For households with regular herbicide and regular fertiliser/fungicide applications, dedicate sprayers to specific use:

One sprayer for herbicide only (never used for anything else).

Another sprayer for fertiliser and fungicide.

Eliminates cross-contamination risk entirely.

Quality knapsacks are affordable enough that two dedicated units cost only modestly more than one shared unit.

**Equipment damage from chemical residue.**

Acidic or alkaline chemistry in tank residue corrodes metal components.

Salts crystallise in valves and nozzles.

Some chemistry degrades plastic and rubber seals.

Proper cleaning prevents these issues. Neglected sprayers fail mechanically within a few years.

**Storage considerations:**

Don't store sprayer with any chemical inside. Drain and clean.

Store in cool dry location.

Lubricate pump seals before long-term storage.

For winter storage in frost-prone areas, drain completely or store in heated space — frozen water expansion damages components.

Periodic deep maintenance:

Every 6 to 12 months for regular users:

Disassemble nozzles and inspect for wear.

Check pump seals and lubricate.

Inspect hoses for cracks or hardening.

Replace worn components proactively rather than waiting for failure.

PPE during cleaning:

Cleaning is when chemical exposure risk is highest. Concentrated residue in the cleanup water can be more hazardous than dilute spray.

Wear chemical-resistant gloves.

Eye protection.

Avoid splashing.

Wash hands and exposed skin thoroughly after.

For NZLA Sticker specifically:

The organosilicone chemistry is genuinely toxic if inhaled. Cleaning sprayers used with Sticker requires extra care to avoid respiratory exposure.

For typical NZ home lawn user:

Routine triple rinse after each use. Ammonia or bleach cleaning when switching from herbicide to other applications. Dedicated sprayers for high-risk chemistry where practical.

Skipping cleaning leads to expensive lessons — damaged garden plants, ruined equipment, frustrated lawns.
