# My inherited lawn is full of weeds - where do I start?

Identify the dominant weeds first. The fix depends on what you've got.

Common scenarios:

Mostly broadleaf weeds (dandelion, plantain, daisy, clover):
- Standard hormone herbicide (NZLA BWC) at label rate.
- Spring or autumn timing.
- Single application usually handles 80% of broadleaves.
- Follow-up application 6 to 8 weeks later for the survivors.
- Then fertility programme to thicken the lawn against re-invasion.

Annual poa contamination (small grassy weed with seedheads, in patches):
- NZLA Etho if the lawn is ryegrass or tall fescue (check species first - Etho damages fine fescue and browntop).
- Programme of 2 to 3 applications through autumn and winter.
- Cultural improvements: better mowing, reduce poa-favouring conditions.

Sedge or nutgrass (look like grass but triangular stems):
- NZLA Halo at label rate.
- Sedge Pro for heavier infestations.

Wide grass weed contamination (browntop, kikuyu in cool-season lawns):
- Hardest to fix. Selective control is limited.
- Full renovation may be the most practical solution.

Onehunga (prickle weed, with hard seeds in turf):
- Standard chemistry (BWC, Gold) works on regular populations.
- Clopyralid-resistant populations exist in parts of NZ (documented by Massey research). If BWC doesn't control it, that may be why.

Process for a weed-dominant inherited lawn:

Month 1:
- Identify dominant weeds.
- First herbicide application targeting the dominant weed.
- Begin fertility programme (NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+).

Month 2 to 3:
- Second herbicide application for survivors.
- Continued fertility.
- Mow at appropriate height (40 to 50 mm typically).

Month 4 to 6:
- Lawn starting to thicken from fertility programme.
- Spot treat remaining weed patches.
- Begin thinking about autumn renovation if the lawn is still thin.

Months 6 to 12:
- Autumn renovation: scarify, aerate, overseed.
- Continued maintenance programme.
- Weed pressure should be significantly reduced by this point.

Don't:
- Try to kill all weeds in one heavy application. Risk lawn damage and resistance.
- Apply herbicide on stressed lawn (drought, heat). Will damage the lawn worse than the weeds.
- Skip the fertility programme thinking you can just kill the weeds and the lawn will look great. Dense competitive turf is the long-term weed prevention.

For most inherited weedy lawns, the first 6 months of consistent programme makes a dramatic difference. Year 2 onwards is fine-tuning.
