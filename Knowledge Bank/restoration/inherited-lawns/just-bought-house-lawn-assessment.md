# I just bought this house - how do I assess the lawn?

Spend 30 minutes walking the lawn properly before deciding what to do. Most owners overspend on the wrong things in the first 6 months because they didn't read the lawn properly.

Walk through and note:

What species are there?
- Pick a few clumps of grass and identify them.
- Single species lawn (ryegrass, fescue, kikuyu) suggests it was deliberately established.
- Mixed species (ryegrass with browntop, poa, fescue) means it's been overseeded or wild-recruited over the years.
- This affects what chemistry is safe and what programme will work.

What's the density?
- Thick uniform sward = well-managed lawn, probably needs minimal intervention.
- Thin patchy sward = under-fertilised or stressed, needs a building programme.
- Bare patches with weeds = neglected or damaged, needs renovation.

What's the weed pressure?
- Few weeds = lawn was being managed.
- Moderate weeds = standard neglect after 1 to 2 years without herbicide.
- Heavy broadleaves dominant = years of neglect, hormone herbicide programme over the next year.
- Clover or onehunga heavy = specific weed issues to address.

What's the colour?
- Deep green = adequate fertility.
- Pale = needs nitrogen.
- Yellow patches = check for disease or pest damage.
- Specific spots dead = check for dog urine or compaction.

What's the soil like?
- Push a screwdriver in 50 mm easily? Good.
- Hard to push more than 25 mm? Compacted.
- Sandy or clay? Affects programme.

Drainage?
- Walk around after rain. Standing water anywhere?
- Soft squelchy patches?
- These need addressing before the rest.

Equipment available:
- What did the previous owner leave behind?
- Mower condition?

After assessment, you've got a starting point. Most inherited lawns benefit from:
1. Address any obvious immediate issues (dead patches, weed dominance).
2. Soil test (cheap baseline for the programme).
3. Standard fertility programme (NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ three times a year).
4. Renovation in autumn if needed (scarify, aerate, overseed).
5. Patience - inherited lawns take 1 to 2 seasons to fully shift to your programme.

Don't:
- Apply heavy chemistry in the first 2 weeks before you understand what you've got.
- Overseed before identifying the existing species (texture clash).
- Spend big on premium products until you know what the lawn needs.

For typical NZ inherited lawns, 6 to 12 months of consistent management transforms most of them. The investment pays off year on year.
