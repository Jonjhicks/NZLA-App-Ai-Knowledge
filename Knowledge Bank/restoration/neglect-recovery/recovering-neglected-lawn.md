# How do I recover a neglected lawn?

Depends on how long it's been neglected and what state it's in. Two main scenarios:

Light neglect (under 12 months without consistent care):
- Weeds present but lawn density still reasonable.
- Just needs a standard programme to bring it back.
- Spring or autumn standard fertiliser plus herbicide cleanup.
- 6 months of consistent management returns it to standard.

Heavy neglect (2 to 5+ years without care):
- Weeds dominate over grass in patches.
- Thinning lawn, bare ground visible.
- Possibly significant thatch build-up.
- Soil chemistry may be off.
- Needs renovation, not just maintenance.

For heavy neglect, the renovation pathway:

Year 1 - stabilise and assess:
- Soil test for baseline.
- First herbicide application on dominant weeds (NZLA BWC for typical broadleaves).
- Standard fertility programme (All Seasons or All Seasons N+ three times a year).
- Don't overseed yet - need to clear weeds first.
- Address obvious bare patches with NZLA Starter and seed.

Autumn of Year 1 - first renovation:
- Scarify or dethatch to remove built-up thatch.
- Core aerate to address compaction.
- Top dress with sandy loam if soil is poor.
- Overseed with appropriate species.

Year 2 - rebuild:
- Continue fertility programme.
- Address weeds as they emerge through the new turf.
- Monthly foliar feeds (Liquid Boost, Iron+) to push growth.
- Don't expect perfection yet - the lawn is rebuilding.

Year 3 - maintenance phase:
- Standard maintenance programme.
- Annual renovation if needed.
- Lawn should be looking like a well-managed lawn by this point.

What you can do in the short term while the longer programme runs:
- Mow consistently (sharp blade, sensible height) - immediate visible improvement.
- Apply Iron+ for colour boost (quick green response).
- Address visible damage (dead patches, drought stress).
- Don't apply heavy chemistry to a struggling lawn - makes it worse.

Common mistakes in neglected lawn recovery:
- Rushing renovation in the first 6 months - lawn isn't ready.
- Heavy herbicide programmes that stress the already-struggling lawn.
- Trying premium products on a basic lawn that needs the basics first.
- Giving up after 3 months because the lawn doesn't look perfect yet.

Cost of recovery vs starting over:
- For lawns over about 30% bare or weed-dominant, sometimes starting fresh is more cost-effective than recovery.
- Calculate the cost of recovery (renovation, herbicide, multiple seasons of programme) vs full re-establishment.
- For severe cases, glyphosate kill + cultivate + re-establish gives a faster better result.

For typical NZ home lawns inherited in moderate neglect, a 12 to 24 month recovery programme gets them back to good condition. Don't expect overnight transformation, do expect steady visible improvement month over month.
