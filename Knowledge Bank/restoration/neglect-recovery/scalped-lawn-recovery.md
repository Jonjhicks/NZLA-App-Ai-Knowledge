# How do I recover a lawn that's been scalped or over-mowed?

Scalped lawn has been cut down to or near soil level. Possible causes: mower hit a high spot, blade set too low, lawn was let grow too long and then cut hard, contractor mistake.

What scalping does:
- Removes all green leaf tissue.
- Exposes the crown (growing point at soil level).
- Stops photosynthesis temporarily.
- Severe scalping can kill grass plants outright.
- Less severe scalping is recoverable.

Recovery process:

Immediate (day 1 to 7):
- Stop further mowing for a week or two.
- Water deeply to help the lawn recover.
- Avoid traffic on the scalped areas.
- Apply NZLA Restore for tissue recovery support.

Short term (week 2 to 6):
- New leaf growth from the surviving crowns.
- NZLA Liquid Boost at moderate rate to push regrowth.
- Iron+ for colour as new leaves emerge.
- Raise the cutting height for the next several mows (don't return to short cutting immediately).
- Apply standard granular fertiliser if not done recently.

Medium term (week 6 onwards):
- Assess what survived vs what died.
- Dead patches need reseeding with NZLA Starter and seed.
- Continue fertility programme.
- Address the underlying cause of the scalping (uneven ground, mower setup, technique).

What dies vs what survives:
- Crowns that weren't damaged regrow within 2 to 4 weeks.
- Crowns crushed or removed by the scalping won't recover.
- Severity depends on how short the cut was and how stressed the lawn was beforehand.

Preventing recurrence:
- Identify uneven ground that caused the scalping.
- Top dress over low spots and bumps over a couple of seasons.
- Set mower at appropriate height for the grass species and conditions.
- Don't let lawn grow excessively long then cut it short in one pass (the third rule of mowing).
- For ride-on mowers, check deck level and wheel ride height.

Recovery acceleration:
- Foliar nutrition (Liquid Boost, Iron+) gives faster response than granular alone.
- Adequate water (but not waterlogged) supports recovery.
- Reduce other stresses (no herbicide, no fungicide during recovery).
- Avoid heavy traffic.

Realistic timeline:
- Light scalping: 2 to 4 weeks to visible recovery.
- Moderate scalping: 4 to 8 weeks.
- Heavy scalping (dead patches): full reseed needed, 2 to 3 months to look normal.

For lawns that get scalped repeatedly in the same spots, the underlying issue (uneven ground, fixed mower setting) needs solving. Annual cycle of scalping the same spots is exhausting for the lawn and slow to recover.
