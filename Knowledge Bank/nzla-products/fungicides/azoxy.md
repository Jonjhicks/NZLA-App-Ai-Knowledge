# NZLA Azoxy

Azoxystrobin at 250 g/L. A systemic, broad-spectrum fungicide that delivers protectant, curative, and anti-sporulant activity in a single product. FRAC Group 11 strobilurin — globally one of the most-used turf fungicides because the chemistry sits at the right intersection of spectrum, residual, and safety.

## How it works

Azoxystrobin is a QoI (quinone outside inhibitor) fungicide. It binds to a specific site on cytochrome bc1 (Complex III) in fungal mitochondria, blocking ATP biosynthesis. Without ATP, the fungus can't sustain energy-dependent processes — spore germination, hyphal extension, infection establishment. It starves to death.

The mechanism targets early infection stages. Spores germinating on the leaf surface, hyphae trying to penetrate the cuticle, early-stage infection spreading through the leaf — all vulnerable. Once infection is well established and tissue has died, azoxystrobin stops the spread but won't recover what's already gone.

## Three modes of action

**Protectant:** applied to healthy turf, sits on and in the leaf, prevents new infection.

**Curative:** active against early-stage infection already inside the leaf tissue. Hits the fungus before major damage occurs.

**Anti-sporulant:** even on infected tissue, suppresses spore production. Reduces inoculum available for spread to surrounding healthy turf.

Xylem-mobile movement through the leaf after foliar uptake means full leaf coverage from a single spray side. Translaminar movement from the upper surface to lower surface completes the coverage.

## Resistance management — non-negotiable

Strobilurins are at high resistance risk. Single-site binding means a single mutation in the fungus can confer complete resistance. Microdochium patch (winter fusarium) has documented azoxystrobin resistance internationally.

Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating to a different FRAC group. Fungus Pro (thiram, FRAC M3 multi-site) is the standard NZLA rotation partner. Multi-site chemistry is essentially impossible to develop resistance to, which is why it's the rotation backbone of any serious programme.

A sound programme: Azoxy for active  knockdown (2 applications max), Fungus Pro as the protectant between systemic sprays, then back to Azoxy if needed.

## Disease coverage

Label covers: winter fusarium (Microdochium patch), brown patch, red thread, grey leaf spot, anthracnose, Helminthosporium diseases, pythium leaf blight, pythium root rot, seedling damping off, spring dead spot.

Not covered: rust and dollar spot. For rust, Fungus Pro is the NZLA option.

## Rate and application

23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L of water. Apply to dry foliage.

**Do not water in for 24 hours for foliar diseases.** The fungicide needs to stay on and in the leaf where the pathogen is. Irrigating immediately after washes it into the soil, away from where it's needed.

Exception: for soil-based diseases (pythium root rot, spring dead spot), water the product in so it reaches the root zone.

28-day application interval for most diseases, 21 days for Helminthosporium. Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating.

Don't mow within 24 hours either side. Don't spray wet foliage. Don't spray near apple trees — phytotoxic to some varieties.

## When to use

**Preventative:** when weather conditions favour . Warm humid spells (brown patch, pythium), cool damp autumn and winter (fusarium patch, red thread). If you've had recurring  in the same spots in previous years, schedule Azoxy ahead of the onset window rather than waiting for symptoms.

**Curative:** at first sign of infection. Earlier is better — once infection is established and tissue has died, you're managing spread, not reversing damage.

## Tank-mixing

Pairs well with Iron+ for colour support while treating . Also works with Amino+, Humic+, and light liquid nitrogen. Standard tank-mix order: water first, agitate, add Azoxy, then partners one at a time with agitation between.

Don't mix with copper-based products. Don't mix with another Group 11 fungicide — that's the same mode of action with no benefit and increased resistance pressure.

## Azoxy vs Fungus Pro

Azoxy is the primary programme fungicide: systemic, long residual (3 to 4 weeks), protectant plus curative, wide  spectrum, not on rust.

Fungus Pro is the rotation partner and the rust option: contact only (protectant), shorter residual (10 to 14 days), multi-site so no resistance risk, covers rust where Azoxy doesn't.

Most home lawn  situations: Azoxy first, Fungus Pro for rotation and rust.
