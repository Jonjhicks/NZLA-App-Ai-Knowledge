# NZLA Fungus Pro

Thiram at 400 g/L. FRAC Group M3 dithiocarbamate. A contact, multi-site protectant fungicide that's been in the turf trade for decades — still the backbone of resistance management in fungicide programmes because multi-site chemistry essentially cannot develop resistance.

## How it works

Thiram disrupts multiple sulfhydryl-containing enzymes in the fungal cell simultaneously — energy production, cell wall maintenance, cellular regulation. Because it hits multiple sites at once, the fungus would need multiple simultaneous mutations to develop resistance. That's essentially impossible. Decades of agricultural and turf use have produced almost no documented resistance development to thiram.

It's contact-only. Thiram sits on the leaf surface as a chemical barrier the fungus must pass through to infect the plant. It doesn't enter the leaf vascular system, doesn't move to infection already established inside tissue. That's the trade-off for the resistance-proof profile.

## Coverage is the whole game

Because it's a contact, thiram only protects where the spray actually lands. Patchy application equals patchy protection.

The application rates (20 L carrier water per 100m² for prevention) are not excessive — they're what makes the chemistry work across every leaf surface. Cut the water volume to save time and you cut the effectiveness proportionally.

**Prevention rate:** 10 mL per L of water, sprayed at 1 L per 5m² (200 mL product in 20 L water per 100m²).

**Active outbreak:** 40 mL per L (800 mL per 100m² in 20 L water). Treat the affected area plus a metre around it. Repeat after 2 weeks.

Reapply every 4 weeks in normal conditions, every 2 weeks in high humidity or persistent wet weather. Being a contact, it washes off with rain and breaks down with UV — wet or sunny conditions shorten the residual.

Apply to dry foliage. Don't water in. Don't mow within 24 hours either side.

## Rust — Fungus Pro is the NZLA option

Rust is on the Fungus Pro label. Azoxystrobin (Azoxy) doesn't cover rust on its NZ label. So when rust shows up — the orange dust on shoes, yellow-orange pustules on leaf blades — Fungus Pro is what you want.

Standard rust response: Fungus Pro on the foliage plus a light nitrogen application. Rust commonly appears on nitrogen-starved lawns in late summer and autumn. The chemistry takes the rust off but if the lawn stays hungry, it returns quickly. Fungicide plus feed clears it within a couple of weeks.

## Resistance management role

Fungus Pro is the rotation partner for Azoxy. Azoxy (FRAC Group 11, single-site) is at resistance risk if used repeatedly without rotation. Fungus Pro (FRAC Group M3, multi-site) dilutes that selection pressure.

Standard professional programme through a winter  season:
- Azoxy as curative knockdown on active  (maximum 2 consecutive applications)
- Fungus Pro as protectant between systemic sprays
- Never two consecutive applications of the same chemistry without rotation

Used this way, the systemic retains its effectiveness long-term because you're not giving the fungal population a chance to select for resistance.

## Other uses

Seed treatment and root protection at planting — the dithiocarbamate chemistry is fungicidal at the root zone.

Vertebrate repellent for rabbits, hares, and possums on ornamentals (the smell is deterrent to these animals).

## Environmental notes

Very ecotoxic to aquatic life. Keep well away from ponds, streams, drains, and waterways. GHS Cat 1 classification — buffer zones matter.

## Fungus Pro vs Azoxy

Azoxy is the primary systemic: longer residual (3 to 4 weeks), curative plus protectant, wide spectrum, doesn't cover rust.

Fungus Pro is the contact protectant: shorter residual (10 to 14 days), protectant only, covers rust, multi-site so no resistance risk.

Most home lawn programmes use Azoxy as the main tool and Fungus Pro as the rotation partner and rust option. If you're only running one product, Azoxy for a programme with no rust. If rust is recurring, Fungus Pro stays on the shelf alongside it.

## Common mistakes

Expecting it to cure established infection. Fungus Pro is protectant-only — it protects healthy tissue around an outbreak but doesn't clean up what's already inside the leaf.

Cutting the water volume to save time. Contact fungicides only work where the spray lands.

Skipping reapplication during wet weather. Rain washes it off. Wet conditions need more frequent applications, not fewer.

Tank-mixing with copper-based products. Thiram and copper react — don't combine.
