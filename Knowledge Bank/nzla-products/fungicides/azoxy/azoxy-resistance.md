# Will fungus develop resistance to Azoxy?

Yes, if you over-use it. Azoxystrobin is FRAC Group 11 (strobilurin / QoI). Single-site mode of action, which means one specific mutation in the fungus can confer high-level resistance. Internationally, fusarium patch (Microdochium nivale) has documented azoxystrobin resistance, and it's spreading.

The way to keep Azoxy working is to limit how often you use it. Standard FRAC guidance for QoIs:
- Maximum two consecutive applications before rotating to a different FRAC group.
- Don't rely on Azoxy as the only fungicide in your programme.
- Use protectant multi-site partners (Fungus Pro / thiram, FRAC M3) in rotation to dilute selection pressure.

If you're spraying Azoxy every three weeks all autumn and winter and not rotating, you're actively breeding resistant fungal populations on your own lawn. Once that happens, the product stops working and you've lost a tool from the programme.

For home lawns getting a couple of disease incidents a year, this isn't something to worry about as long as you rotate. For lawns with constant disease pressure (heavily shaded, poor air movement, high nitrogen, irrigation overnight) the resistance risk is higher and the rotation discipline matters more.
