# What's the difference between NZLA Azoxy and Fungus Pro?

Different chemistry, different roles in a programme.

Azoxy is azoxystrobin, a systemic strobilurin (FRAC Group 11). Moves into the leaf, has protectant, curative and anti-sporulant activity. Long residual (3 to 4 weeks). Covers a wide range of diseases including brown patch, fusarium patch, red thread, helminthosporium, pythium, anthracnose. Doesn't cover rust or dollar spot.

Fungus Pro is thiram, a multi-site contact fungicide (FRAC Group M3). Stays on the leaf surface, doesn't absorb in. Protectant only. Shorter residual (10 to 14 days). Covers rust, plus most of the diseases Azoxy covers as a contact protectant.

The way they work together is the point. Azoxy as the main programme product (systemic, longer residual, broader curative activity), Fungus Pro as the rotation partner (different mode of action so you don't build resistance) and the option for rust.

Two consecutive applications of Azoxy max, then rotate to Fungus Pro for the next round, then back to Azoxy if needed. That's the resistance management programme.

If you're only buying one and you don't have rust pressure, Azoxy is the better all-rounder. If you've got rust as a recurring issue, Fungus Pro covers it where Azoxy doesn't.
