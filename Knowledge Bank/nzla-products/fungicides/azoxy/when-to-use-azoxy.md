# When should I use NZLA Azoxy?

Either preventatively when disease pressure is building, or at the first sign of infection. Azoxy is both protectant and curative, so it works in either situation, but earlier is better. Once an infection is well established and tissue has died, the cure side gets a lot weaker, you're effectively limited to preventing further spread.

Preventative use makes sense when the weather conditions favour disease. Warm humid spells (brown patch, dollar spot, pythium), cool damp autumn and winter (fusarium patch, red thread), shoulder seasons when the lawn is moving in and out of stress. If you've had recurring disease in the same spots in previous years, schedule Azoxy ahead of the typical onset window rather than waiting.

Curative use is when you see the disease starting. Azoxy stops new infections and pulls the active infections back, but the dead leaf tissue won't recover, the lawn has to grow new leaf to look right again.

One thing Azoxy doesn't cover: rust and dollar spot are off-label. For rust, Fungus Pro is the option. Dollar spot doesn't have an ideal NZLA fit currently, the chemistry that handles it well isn't in the range yet.

Maximum two consecutive applications before rotating to a different mode of action. Resistance is real and the next-best option in the NZLA range is Fungus Pro.
