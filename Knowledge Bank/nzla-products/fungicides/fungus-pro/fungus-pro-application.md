# How do I apply NZLA Fungus Pro?

Foliar spray on dry leaves, label rate, decent water volume for coverage. Because it's a contact product (doesn't move into the leaf), coverage is everything. Patchy spray = patchy protection.

Check the label for the exact rate as it can vary by use. Carrier water in the 5 to 10 L per 100 m² range is typical for good leaf coverage. Higher water rate than systemics like Azoxy because contact products need to physically reach every leaf surface where the fungus might land.

Apply when the lawn is dry and stays dry for several hours afterwards. Rain or irrigation within a few hours of application washes a contact product off. That's the trade-off, faster knockdown of surface spores, but less rainfast than a systemic.

Don't water in. Don't mow within 24 hours either side.

Residual is 10 to 14 days under typical conditions. If disease pressure is heavy, a second application 10 to 14 days later is reasonable. If you're alternating with Azoxy for resistance management, you might go Fungus Pro, then Azoxy 14 days later, then Fungus Pro again, depending on conditions.
