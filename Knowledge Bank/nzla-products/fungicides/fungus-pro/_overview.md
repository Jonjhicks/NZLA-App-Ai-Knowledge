# NZLA Fungus Pro

A contact, multi-site protectant fungicide for turf, ornamentals and root protection. Thiram at 400 g/L. FRAC Group M3 dithiocarbamate. Has been around the trade for decades and remains the go-to protectant chemistry in turf disease programmes because multi-site action means essentially zero resistance risk.

## How it actually works

Thiram is a multi-site fungicide. Rather than binding to a single specific enzyme the way modern single-site fungicides do, thiram disrupts multiple enzymes in the fungal cell at once. It targets sulfhydryl-containing enzymes essential for fungal metabolism — energy production, cell wall maintenance, cellular regulation. The fungus is hit on multiple fronts simultaneously and can't function.

That mechanism is the basis of its resistance-management value. A single-site fungicide can be defeated by a single mutation in the fungus that changes the binding site. A multi-site fungicide would require multiple simultaneous mutations across multiple enzymes — essentially impossible. Over decades of agricultural and turf use, thiram has accumulated almost no documented resistance development. That's the chemistry's greatest strength.

## Why contact only matters

Thiram doesn't move into the plant. It sits on the leaf surface as a chemical barrier the fungus has to physically pass through to infect the plant.

Two practical effects.

**Best used preventatively.** Because thiram doesn't move into the plant, it can't reach infection already established inside leaf tissue. If you spray a lawn with active red thread, the thiram protects healthy surrounding tissue but doesn't cure the existing infection. Use it before disease establishes, or alongside a systemic that handles the curative side.

**Coverage is critical.** As a contact, thiram only protects where the spray actually lands. Patchy coverage equals patchy protection. The high water volume (20 L per 100m²) and the 1 L per 5m² spray pattern are not negotiable — they're what makes the contact chemistry work.

## What it controls

The label covers rust, powdery mildew, leaf spot, blight and dieback.

The important NZ-specific point: **rust is on the Fungus Pro label** but not on the Azoxy label. So when rust shows up in a NZ lawn, Fungus Pro is the NZLA option — Azoxy doesn't have rust on its NZ label.

It also gets used as a root protectant at planting (the dithiocarbamate chemistry is fungicidal at the root zone too) and as an animal repellent for rabbits, hares and possums on ornamentals.

## Rate and application

**Prevention:** 10 mL of product per L of water, sprayed at 1 L per 5m². That works out to 200 mL of product in 20 L of water per 100m².

**Active outbreak:** step the rate up to 40 mL per L (800 mL per 100m² in 20 L water). Treat the affected area plus a metre around it. Repeat after 2 weeks.

Reapply every 4 weeks in normal conditions, every 2 weeks in high humidity. Being a contact, it washes off with rain and breaks down with UV, so wet or sunny conditions burn through it faster than dry overcast conditions.

The high water volume is the whole game with a contact. Cutting carrier water to save time means cutting coverage, which means cutting effectiveness.

## Resistance management role

Fungus Pro is the resistance management backbone of an NZLA fungicide programme. Single-site systemics like Azoxy are at real resistance risk if used repeatedly. Multi-site contacts like thiram aren't. The standard professional pattern through a winter disease season is:

- Azoxy as the curative knockdown when disease is actively running (2 consecutive applications maximum)
- Fungus Pro as the protectant between systemic sprays, holding the line on prevention
- Never running two consecutive applications of the same chemistry without rotation

Used that way, the systemic does its job on active infection without burning out, and the protectant carries the prevention work in between.

## When not to use

Don't expect it to clean up infection already established inside the plant. As a contact protectant, it works on the surface, so anything already inside the leaf or crown rides through.

Keep it well away from ponds, streams and drains. Very ecotoxic to aquatic life.

Don't apply it expecting it to revive turf that's already wiped out. Like all fungicides, Fungus Pro stops disease spreading, doesn't grow dead tissue back.

Don't cut the water volume to save time. The 20 L per 100m² is the chemistry working.

## Common mistakes

Watering it in straight after spraying. The product needs to stay on the leaf where the pathogen is — washing it off into the soil defeats the purpose.

Cutting corners on water volume. Contact fungicides only work where the spray actually lands.

Tank-mixing with foliar feeds without checking compatibility. Thiram doesn't always play nicely with other chemistry — jar-test if you're combining.

Treating an active outbreak with only Fungus Pro. It'll protect the healthy grass around the patch but won't clean up the active infection. Pair with Azoxy for that.

Skipping reapplication during wet weather. Rain washes contact fungicides off. Wet conditions need more frequent application, not less.

## Best paired with

Azoxy in rotation. Azoxy curative knockdown on active disease, Fungus Pro protectant between sprays. Different FRAC groups, different modes of action — the standard professional resistance management pair.

A solid base fertility programme. Disease pressure is much lower on well-fed turf.

Cultural disease management (dew removal, airflow, drainage). Reducing the conditions that drive disease means less work for the fungicide.
