# What is PGR rebound and how do I manage it?

Rebound is the burst of vertical growth that happens when the PGR's effect wears off. The lawn has been holding back on leaf extension for 4 to 6 weeks, the chemistry breaks down, and the gibberellins start flowing again. For about 1 to 2 weeks after that point, the lawn grows faster than usual to catch up.

Two ways to deal with it.

First, stay on programme. Apply the next dose before rebound kicks in, typically 4 to 5 weeks after the previous application. This avoids rebound entirely. Programme applications through a season keep the lawn in regulated mode continuously.

Second, time the single application so rebound lands when extra growth is welcome. A single autumn application that releases just before winter slowdown lines the rebound up with cooling temperatures, so the extra growth is mild and short-lived.

Avoid the worst scenario: PGR applied right before a stress window (heatwave, drought, renovation) so the rebound hits while the lawn is also under stress. That stacking causes problems.

For a typical NZ home lawn, two applications per growing season works well. One in late spring (October to November) for hardening into summer, one in late summer (February) to push density through autumn. Lower commitment than a full season programme, big enough difference in mowing frequency and density to be worth the time.
