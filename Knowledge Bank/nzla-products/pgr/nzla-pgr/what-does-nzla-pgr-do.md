# What does NZLA PGR do?

NZLA PGR (trinexapac-ethyl) regulates above-ground growth. The grass keeps photosynthesising and feeding the roots, but vertical leaf growth slows down for around 4 to 6 weeks per application. The result is denser turf with darker colour and noticeably less mowing through the regulated window.

Mechanism: trinexapac inhibits gibberellin biosynthesis, the hormone responsible for cell elongation. Without it, leaves don't extend as far between cuts. The energy that would have gone into vertical leaf growth goes into root development and tillering, so the lawn fills in laterally instead of pushing up.

What you'll see:
- 30 to 50% less vertical growth for 4 to 6 weeks
- Darker green colour (chlorophyll concentration goes up as leaf surface area stays compressed)
- More tillering and density
- Stronger heat and drought tolerance through the regulated period
- Rebound growth at the end of the regulation period

Good fit for lawns that don't get mown often enough, lawns going into a hot dry summer that you want to harden up first, or anyone who just wants to mow less in summer.

Not a fix for poor mowing technique or starving the lawn. PGRs work with healthy lawns, they make a struggling lawn worse not better.
