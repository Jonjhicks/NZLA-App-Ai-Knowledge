# NZLA PGR

The standard turf plant growth regulator. Trinexapac-ethyl at 116.39 g/L. Globally one of the most-used PGRs on professional turf — clipping reduction, canopy tightness, increased density, and a particularly clean mechanism that doesn't damage the plant the way some older PGR chemistries did.

## How it actually works

Trinexapac-ethyl is a late-stage gibberellic acid biosynthesis inhibitor. Gibberellic acid (GA) is the plant hormone that drives stem and leaf elongation — vertical growth. The plant builds active GA through a multi-step biosynthesis pathway, with the final step being the conversion of GA20 to GA1 (the active form), catalysed by the enzyme 3β-hydroxylase.

Trinexapac binds to 3β-hydroxylase and blocks that final step. The plant keeps making GA precursors but can't convert them to active GA. Without active GA, vertical growth slows dramatically. The plant doesn't die — all other plant functions continue normally. It just can't extend stems and leaves at the rate it would otherwise.

The energy that would have gone into top growth gets redirected into lateral spread, root development, carbohydrate storage and tillering. The lawn ends up denser, with better roots, more tillers per square metre, and you mow less.

## Why the rebound effect happens

Because trinexapac blocks the late step of the GA biosynthesis pathway, GA precursors accumulate in the plant during the regulated period. When the trinexapac wears off (typically 2 to 6 weeks after application), the 3β-hydroxylase enzyme becomes available again and rapidly converts the accumulated precursors to active GA. The plant experiences a sudden surge of GA and grows vigorously for a period.

That's the rebound effect. Most professional users either apply again before rebound hits, or accept a stronger mow when it does. Planning the mowing schedule around the rebound is part of using PGR successfully.

## Rate by grass type

Ryegrass amenity turf: 16 to 30 mL per 100m².

Bent grass golf greens: 4.5 to 10 mL per 100m² (sensitive species, lower rates).

Kikuyu: 16 to 80 mL per 100m² (wider range, kikuyu can take a heavier hit).

Refer to label for the specific situation. Rates vary because trinexapac sensitivity varies by species — the same dose that gives mild regulation on kikuyu can over-regulate bent grass.

## Application notes

Rainfast 1 hour after application. No need to water in.

Foliar uptake primarily. The active enters through the leaf and concentrates in the leaf and tiller tissue where vertical growth happens. Some xylem and phloem movement gets it to growing points elsewhere in the plant.

Temperature drives both effect and rebound timing. Warm conditions metabolise the active faster — shorter effect, earlier rebound. Cool conditions extend the effect.

## Resistance — not really an issue

PGRs aren't at resistance risk in the same way herbicides and fungicides are. The chemistry acts on the plant's own hormone system rather than killing the plant, so there's no selection pressure for resistance development. No documented resistance to trinexapac after decades of use.

That said, repeated heavy use can cause cumulative regulation issues — yellowing, stunting, reduced recovery. Rotating between trinexapac and paclobutrazol (NZLA Paclo) or alternating PGR seasons with non-PGR periods keeps the lawn out of cumulative trouble.

## When to use

Active growing season on lawns where reducing mowing frequency, tightening the canopy and lifting density are the goals.

Poa annua suppression at low rates on bent grass greens (specific use — trinexapac at low rates selectively biases against Poa relative to bent grass).

Pre-show or pre-event use to tighten the canopy and lift colour for a period.

Through summer to reduce mowing pressure on heavily-used home lawns or sports turf.

## When not to use

On turf going into or coming out of dormancy. The plant needs to transition without further interference, and applying near dormancy can cause excessive yellowing.

On stressed, recovering, wear-damaged, waterlogged or diseased turf. PGR slows recovery further.

In high heat or drought. Heat-stressed turf with PGR on board can yellow severely.

In a programme where rapid recovery from damage is expected in the following weeks.

## Common mistakes

Applying too heavy. Trinexapac is potent — over-application causes yellowing and stunting that persists for weeks.

Treating it as a year-round constant. Use it strategically through active growth, off through transitions and dormancy.

Applying to a stressed lawn expecting it to tidy things up. It'll make a struggling lawn worse.

Not planning for the rebound. The lawn is regulated for 2 to 6 weeks, then growth surges. Schedule mowing around the rebound.

Mixing with Group 3 fungicides (DMIs). Severe over-regulation can result.

## Best paired with

Iron+ to lift colour while the PGR holds growth tight. The combination is the standard "show lawn" prep.

A solid base feed programme (All Seasons or All Seasons N+). PGR works better on a fed, healthy lawn than on a hungry one.

Charger as the maintenance liquid feed — Charger drives lateral density, PGR holds vertical growth tight, together they tighten the canopy from both directions.
