# Should I use Paclo or NZLA PGR?

Different tools for different jobs.

NZLA PGR (trinexapac) is the easier general-purpose growth regulator. Fast onset (within a few days), predictable 4 to 6 week regulation period, clean release with manageable rebound. First choice for slowing mowing frequency and improving density on a home lawn. Hard to over-do at label rate.

Paclo (paclobutrazol) is more specialised. Slower onset (7 to 14 days), longer regulation period (8 to 12 weeks at higher rates), more selective effect on poa over ryegrass and fescue. Useful for fine turf management and as a poa suppression tool over a programme.

Decision tree:
- Want less mowing and a denser lawn: NZLA PGR.
- Want to manage annual poa long-term in a ryegrass or fescue lawn: Paclo (programme of multiple applications over a season).
- Fine turf, bowling green, golf green or high-input lawn: either, depending on the specific use case.
- New to PGRs and unsure: NZLA PGR. It's more forgiving.

Don't tank-mix the two. They work on the same pathway and stacking the effect can stunt the lawn longer than you intend.

For most NZ home lawns, NZLA PGR handles the standard growth regulation needs. Paclo is the second-tier tool when poa management is the priority.
