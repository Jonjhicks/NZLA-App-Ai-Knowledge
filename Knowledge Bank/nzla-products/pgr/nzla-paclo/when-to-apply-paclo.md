# When should I apply NZLA Paclo?

Apply when the lawn is healthy and actively growing, soil temps 14 to 24 °C. Spring and autumn are the prime windows for cool-season turf.

For growth regulation: a single application gives 8 to 12 weeks of regulation. Apply in late spring (October to November) for hardening into summer, or in autumn (March to April) to slow growth heading into cooler months.

For poa suppression: programme approach is what works. Multiple low-rate applications across autumn through to early summer. The poa gradually thins while the ryegrass and fescue strengthen. Single applications don't shift the composition meaningfully, this is a season-long approach.

Avoid:
- Drought-stressed or heat-stressed turf
- Diseased turf
- New lawn under 8 weeks
- Lawn going into winter dormancy (apply early enough that the regulation has run its course before dormancy)
- Stacking multiple full-rate applications within a month, the residual can build up and over-regulate

Programme planning matters more with Paclo than with NZLA PGR because the longer residual makes mistakes longer-lasting. If you're new to it, start at the low end of the label rate and see how the lawn responds before scaling up.
