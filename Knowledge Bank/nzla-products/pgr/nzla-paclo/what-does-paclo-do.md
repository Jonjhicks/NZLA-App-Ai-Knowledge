# What does NZLA Paclo do?

Paclo (paclobutrazol) is a different class of growth regulator from NZLA PGR. Where trinexapac (PGR) works on gibberellin biosynthesis through a late-stage enzyme, paclobutrazol works on an earlier stage in the same pathway, plus it has selective activity against annual poa.

Two main uses:

First, slow lawn growth like NZLA PGR. Reduces vertical growth, increases density, improves heat tolerance. Longer regulation period than trinexapac (8 to 12 weeks at higher rates) but slower onset.

Second, suppress annual poa in cool-season turf. Paclobutrazol slows poa more than it slows ryegrass and fescue. Over multiple applications across a season, the desirable grasses outcompete the weakened poa and the turf composition shifts. Useful tool for poa management on bowling greens, golf greens and high-input home lawns.

Long residual is the trade-off. Multiple stacked applications can build up in the soil and cause stunting that doesn't release for months. Easier to over-do than trinexapac.

Best on healthy actively-growing turf, soil temps 14 to 24 °C. Avoid heat-stressed and drought-stressed lawns.

For home lawns where the priority is less mowing and more density, NZLA PGR is the easier first-time choice. Paclo comes in for poa management or for fine turf where the longer regulation suits the use case.
