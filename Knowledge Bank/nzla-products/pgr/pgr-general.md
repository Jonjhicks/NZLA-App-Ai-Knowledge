# Plant Growth Regulators — Overview

PGRs reduce mowing frequency, tighten the canopy, lift density, and improve heat and drought tolerance. Most home lawn owners don't know they're available. The ones who try them on a healthy lawn don't stop.

## What a PGR does

PGRs work by interfering with gibberellic acid (GA), the plant hormone responsible for stem and leaf elongation. The plant keeps photosynthesising and building dry matter, but that energy gets redirected from top growth into lateral spread, denser canopy, deeper roots, and carbohydrate storage.

The practical result: 30 to 50% fewer mows through the regulated window, darker green colour, better density, improved stress tolerance. The lawn grows shorter between cuts, not less healthy. Underneath, it's actually in better shape.

## The two NZLA options

**NZLA PGR (trinexapac-ethyl)** is the general-purpose regulator. Works across all turfgrass species. 20 to 50% clipping reduction for 2 to 6 weeks per application. The standard choice for home lawns and sports fields where the goal is less mowing and a tighter canopy.

**NZLA Paclo (paclobutrazol)** is the Poa annua specialist. Deeper and longer-lasting than trinexapac, with selectivity that hits Poa harder than ryegrass and fescue. Used on cool-season fine turf — greens, sports grounds, high-performance home lawns — where Poa management over multiple seasons is the objective.

## When PGRs make sense

Lawns being mowed more often than the owner wants through summer. Lawns where presentation and density are the goal. Sports fields managing limited maintenance resources. Pre-event preparation where you want the lawn to hold its appearance longer.

## When they don't

Recovery situations. After scarification, renovation, wear damage, drought, or you want the lawn growing hard, not regulated. Heat-stressed turf. Going into or out of dormancy. Newly established lawns under 8 weeks. Any situation where you need the lawn to push growth in the following weeks.

## Common rules for both products

Don't apply above label rate. PGRs are potent, more is not better.

Don't apply to stressed, dormant, or recovering turf.

Don't mix with Group 3 DMI fungicides (propiconazole, tebuconazole, triadimenol, triticonazole) without confirming compatibility. Severe over-regulation can result.

Commercial use requires an approved handler.

Don't tank-mix NZLA PGR and Paclo — they target the same pathway and stacking produces over-regulation.

## For most home lawns

NZLA PGR is the right starting point. One or two applications per season on a healthy, well-fed lawn delivers a real difference in mowing frequency and presentation. See NZLA PGR for specific rates and timing.

Paclo comes in for Poa annua management programmes on fine turf. See NZLA Paclo for specific use.
