# Are PGRs worth using on a home lawn?

If you want a denser lawn and less mowing in summer, yes. PGRs are one of the more underused tools on the home lawn side.

The trade-off is straightforward. You pay for the product and apply it every 4 to 6 weeks. In return:
- Mowing frequency drops by around a third to a half through the regulated period.
- Lawn density improves over a season.
- Colour goes deeper green.
- Heat and drought tolerance improves.
- Disease pressure often drops as the lawn isn't pushing soft growth.

The cost-benefit depends on lawn size. Small lawn (under 100 m²), the savings on mowing time are modest. Larger lawn (300 m² plus) or someone running a busy summer schedule, the time and effort savings add up fast.

What PGRs don't do:
- Fix a struggling or under-fed lawn (work on healthy lawns only)
- Replace mowing entirely (you still mow, just less often)
- Substitute for fertiliser or water management
- Help on warm-season lawns the same way as cool-season (different application rates, less dramatic effect)

For most NZ home lawn owners with a half-decent lawn who'd like less to do in summer, one or two applications of NZLA PGR through the growing season is good value. Try one application as a single hardening dose in late spring and see what you think.
