# Can PGRs damage my lawn?

At label rate on healthy turf, no. The chemistry is well-studied and home lawn use at the right time on the right lawn is safe.

Where damage shows up:
- Over-rate (especially with paclobutrazol). Stacking PGR over PGR or doubling rates causes stunting that takes months to release.
- Applied to a stressed lawn. Heat, drought or disease combined with PGR makes the stress worse. The lawn can't grow out of the problem because growth is being held back.
- Applied to a young lawn under 8 weeks. PGRs slow what they touch, including young establishing grass that you want growing fast.
- Applied to dormant or going-dormant turf in late autumn. The lawn doesn't process the chemistry and you can get extended stunting into spring.

If you've over-applied, the response depends on what you used. NZLA PGR (trinexapac) over-application typically resolves within 6 to 8 weeks. Paclo (paclobutrazol) over-application can last 3 to 6 months.

To recover: water deeply, apply a light dose of Liquid N to support whatever growth the lawn can manage, mow when growth resumes. Don't add more PGR or any other stress chemistry until the lawn is fully releasing.

The simplest rule: stay at label rates, apply to healthy lawns only, leave at least 4 weeks between applications.
