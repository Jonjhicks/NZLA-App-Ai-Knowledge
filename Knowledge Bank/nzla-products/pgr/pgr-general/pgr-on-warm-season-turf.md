# Do PGRs work on warm-season lawns?

Yes, though the response is different from cool-season turf.

Kikuyu, couch and paspalum respond well to trinexapac (NZLA PGR), particularly through summer when they're growing hardest. The effect is most useful for managing aggressive vertical growth (kikuyu in particular can need mowing every 4 days through the peak of summer). PGR cuts that frequency back to once a week or less.

A few practical points specific to warm-season:
- Apply during active warm-season growth, soil temps above 18 °C.
- Don't apply to dormant warm-season turf in winter, the chemistry won't be metabolised.
- Rates may differ from cool-season recommendations. Check the label.
- Effect period can be shorter on warm-season because the grass grows so fast through summer that it processes the chemistry quicker.

Paclobutrazol has less pronounced effect on warm-season turf than on cool-season, and the poa suppression angle (one of paclo's selling points) isn't relevant on a warm-season lawn that's competing with poa during the kikuyu's growing season.

For a kikuyu lawn that's getting away on the homeowner through summer, one or two NZLA PGR applications in December and February is well worth the effort. The lawn looks denser, mowing drops back to manageable, and the seedhead production reduces.
