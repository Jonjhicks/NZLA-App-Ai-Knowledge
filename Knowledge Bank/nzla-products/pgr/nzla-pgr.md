# NZLA PGR

Trinexapac-ethyl at 116.39 g/L. The standard turf growth regulator — reduces mowing frequency, tightens the canopy, lifts density, and improves colour. The most widely used PGR on professional turf globally, and genuinely useful on home lawns for anyone who wants less to do in summer.

## How it works

Trinexapac-ethyl inhibits 3β-hydroxylase, the enzyme responsible for the final step in gibberellic acid (GA) biosynthesis — converting inactive GA20 to active GA1. GA is the hormone that drives stem and leaf elongation. Without it, vertical growth slows dramatically.

The plant keeps photosynthesising and building biomass. That energy, instead of going into top growth, gets redirected into lateral spread, tillering, root development, and carbohydrate storage. The result: denser, more compact turf that grows slower vertically but fills in better laterally.

What you'll see: 30 to 50% less vertical growth for 2 to 6 weeks, darker green colour (chlorophyll concentration increases as leaf surface area stays compressed), more tillers per square metre, and improved heat and drought tolerance through the regulated period.

## The rebound effect

Because trinexapac blocks the late step, GA precursors accumulate during the regulated period. When the chemistry wears off, the enzyme becomes available again and rapidly converts those precursors to active GA. Growth surges for 1 to 2 weeks before settling back to normal. That's the rebound.

Two ways to manage it. Stay on programme — apply the next dose before the rebound hits, around week 4 to 5. Or accept the rebound and time the single application so the growth surge lands when you want it. What you don't want is to have the rebound hit during a stress window (heatwave, renovation) or right before a period where you can't mow.

## When to use

Active growing season on healthy turf — soil temperatures 14 to 28°C. Spring through autumn, October to April for cool-season turf.

Good applications:
- Pre-summer hardening (late October to November). Lawn enters summer denser, with better roots.
- Programme through summer to reduce mowing frequency.
- Pre-show or pre-event to tighten the canopy.
- Sports fields through summer where mowing resources are limited.

Two applications per season (one October-November, one February) is a practical home lawn schedule. Low commitment, real difference in mowing frequency and presentation.

## When not to use

On turf going into or out of dormancy. On heat-stressed, drought-stressed, diseased, waterlogged, wear-damaged, or recently renovated turf. On new lawns under 8 weeks. Above 28°C soil temperature.

The common thread: PGR slows the lawn's ability to do anything, including recover. Apply only when the lawn is healthy and actively growing.

## Rate and application

Ryegrass amenity turf: 16 to 30 mL per 100m². Bent grass golf greens: 4.5 to 10 mL per 100m². Kikuyu: 16 to 80 mL per 100m².

Check the current label for your specific situation — rates vary because trinexapac sensitivity varies by species. What mildly regulates kikuyu can over-regulate bent grass.

Apply in 4 to 8 L water per 100m². Even coverage matters; patchy application produces patchy regulation and an uneven look. Rainfast after 1 hour. No water-in required.

First time on a particular lawn: go to the lower end of the rate range. Assess the response over 4 to 6 weeks, then dial up or down for the next application.

## Can PGR damage the lawn?

At label rate on healthy turf, no. Damage shows up from: over-application, applying to stressed turf, applying to turf going into dormancy, or applying to young lawns under 8 weeks. NZLA PGR over-application typically resolves within 6 to 8 weeks as the chemistry breaks down.

Don't stack with paclobutrazol (NZLA Paclo) or mix with Group 3 DMI fungicides. Both interactions can cause severe over-regulation.

## NZLA PGR vs NZLA Paclo

NZLA PGR is the first-choice general-purpose regulator. Predictable 4 to 6 week window, manageable rebound, works on all turf species. Easier to use and more forgiving for first-timers.

NZLA Paclo (paclobutrazol) is the specialist tool for Poa annua management and longer-duration regulation on fine turf. Deeper suppression, soil persistence, selective activity against Poa. Used when the goal is shifting turf composition as well as regulating growth.

For most NZ home lawns, NZLA PGR is all that's needed.

## Best paired with

Iron+ at application. PGR holds the canopy tight, Iron+ drives the colour — the combination is the standard pre-event prep for a high-presentation lawn.

All Seasons or All Seasons N+ as the base feed. PGR works on healthy well-fed lawns. A hungry lawn regulated with PGR looks stressed, not tight.

Charger as the liquid maintenance feed — Charger drives lateral density, PGR holds vertical growth tight, together they tighten the canopy from both directions.
