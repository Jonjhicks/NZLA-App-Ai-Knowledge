# Can I overseed an existing lawn with Elfin Tall Fescue?

Yes, particularly useful on existing ryegrass lawns in dry regions where you want to gradually shift toward better drought tolerance.

Process:
1. Autumn (March to May), mow the lawn short.
2. Scarify or rake heavily to remove thatch and open soil surface.
3. Spread ETF at 25 to 30 g per square metre with a calibrated spreader.
4. Roll or rake to get seed into soil contact.
5. Apply Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 days, then every 7 to 10 days through first 4 to 6 weeks.
6. Water lightly and frequently until germination, then less often as seedlings establish.
7. First mow once new seedlings are 70 to 80 mm.

The ETF seedlings establish alongside the existing ryegrass. Over 2 to 3 seasons of consistent autumn overseeding, the proportion of tall fescue increases and the lawn shifts toward better drought tolerance. The rhizomatous behaviour develops as the tall fescue matures.

Caveats:
- Tall fescue establishes slower than ryegrass. Don't expect the same speed of fill.
- The lawn will look mixed for the first 2 to 3 seasons (ryegrass texture in some patches, tall fescue texture in others). Over time this evens out.
- Don't overseed with tall fescue if the existing lawn is fine fescue or browntop, the textures clash too much.

Best results paired with annual aeration (autumn before the overseed) and a regular fertiliser programme. Tall fescue establishes faster on well-aerated, well-fed soil.
