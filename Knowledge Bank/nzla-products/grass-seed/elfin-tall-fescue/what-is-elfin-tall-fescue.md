# What is Elfin Tall Fescue?

A rhizomatous tall fescue cultivar with self-repairing ability. The standout feature is that Elfin produces rhizomes (underground stems) that spread laterally and fill in patches, unlike standard tall fescue cultivars that only reproduce through tillering.

What that gives you:
- Self-repair from underground stems. Wear patches fill in from the surrounding lawn.
- Better recovery from damage than standard tall fescue.
- All the tall fescue benefits: drought tolerance, heat tolerance, deep roots.
- Reasonably fine texture for a tall fescue.
- Dark green appearance.

Where it fits:
- High-use family lawns in dry summer regions.
- Sports field surfaces where drought tolerance plus recovery both matter.
- Lawns where wear patches have been a recurring issue.
- Areas with hot dry summers (Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, inland Otago) where the drought tolerance of tall fescue plus self-repair is the right combination.

Compared to standard ryegrass (non-rhizomatous): better drought tolerance and slow recovery.

Compared to RSR Ryegrass (stoloniferous): both self-repair, RSR is above-ground stolons in a ryegrass, ETF is underground rhizomes in a tall fescue. Different species, different climate fits.

Sow at 35 to 45 g per square metre for new lawn. The rhizome benefit develops over 2 to 3 seasons as the lawn matures.

For drought-prone regions with high-use lawns, ETF is one of the strongest cool-season choices in the NZLA range.
