# Should I sow Elfin or Ultimate Tall Fescue?

Both are premium tall fescue options with different selling points.

Elfin Tall Fescue (ETF):
- Rhizomatous, self-repairing through underground stems.
- Reasonably fine texture.
- Dark green appearance.
- Better recovery from wear damage.

Ultimate Tall Fescue (UTF):
- Premium blend selected for very dark colour and fine texture.
- Higher density at maturity.
- Doesn't have the rhizomatous self-repair behaviour.
- Best visual appearance of any tall fescue in the range.

Decision tree:
- High-use lawn in dry region, wear recovery matters: ETF.
- Premium aesthetic lawn in dry region, lower-use: UTF.
- Sports field, drought-prone area: ETF for the recovery.
- Showcase home lawn where look is everything: UTF for the visuals.
- Want both: blend (50/50 ETF and UTF) for combined self-repair and premium appearance.

For most NZ home lawns in dry summer regions, ETF is the more practical choice because the self-repair value compounds over the seasons. UTF earns its keep when the priority is visual quality and the lawn is well-maintained enough that wear patches aren't the main concern.

Both perform best with autumn sowing. Both respond to standard tall fescue establishment programmes (NZLA Starter, Liquid Starter, then maintenance with All Seasons or All Seasons N+).
