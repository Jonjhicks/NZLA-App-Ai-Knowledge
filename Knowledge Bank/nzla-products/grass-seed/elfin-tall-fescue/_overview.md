# Elfin Tall Fescue (ETF)

The self-repairing tall fescue. Rhizomatous cultivar — produces underground stems that allow it to spread laterally and fill in bare patches, which standard bunch-type tall fescue can't do. Combines the drought, heat, shade and wear tolerance of tall fescue with active recovery from damage.

## What it is

Standard tall fescue is a bunch-forming grass. Tough, deep-rooted, drought tolerant, but when damage takes out a patch it doesn't actively recover. Elfin is bred with rhizomes (underground horizontal stems) that spread and produce new plants. Damage areas fill in actively rather than waiting for re-seeding.

The dark green appearance, reasonably fine texture and tall fescue's natural shade and drought tolerance round out the package. Deep root system pulls water from below where shallow-rooted grasses are already brown.

Tall fescue is a slow species to establish from seed compared to ryegrass. Irrigation through the establishment window is essential, ideally on a timer or temporary irrigation if a permanent system isn't installed.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: refer to label, typically in the 3 to 5 kg per 100m² range.

Oversowing rate: typically 2.5 kg per 100m².

Tall fescue germinates more slowly than ryegrass (10 to 14 days at warm soil temperatures, longer cool). The slow establishment means moisture management is critical, dry-out at day 7 kills seedlings before they're established.

NZLA Starter at soil prep, irrigation programme through establishment, first mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

## Maintenance height

30 to 70 mm typical. The deeper root system that gives tall fescue its drought tolerance also means it photosynthesises hard from below the canopy, so a slightly taller mowing height suits the species.

## When to use

Drought-prone sites where summer green retention matters.

Sites with partial shade where ryegrass struggles.

Wear-prone sites where the self-repair from rhizomes adds value over standard tall fescue.

Sand-based soils or low-irrigation situations.

## When not to use

Wet, poorly-drained sites. Tall fescue prefers reasonably well-drained ground.

Heavy frost regions where the species struggles, although Elfin's NZ selection handles most of the South Island fine.

Where ultimate dark colour matters more than drought tolerance. MDR ryegrass is a better aesthetic choice.

Where rapid establishment matters. Tall fescue is slow.

## Common mistakes

Letting the seedling lawn dry out. Slow germination plus dry surface equals failed establishment.

Mowing too short. Tall fescue at 15 to 20 mm looks terrible, sits at 30 to 50+ mm well.

Expecting it to perform like ryegrass. Different species, different establishment timeline, different mowing height, different shade and drought profile.

Skipping the starter fertiliser. New tall fescue needs phosphorus and trace elements.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter granular at soil prep.

Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks.

All Seasons as the maintenance base.

Penetrate or Aerate where the lawn is on clay or compacted ground. Tall fescue's deep roots need access to the profile.
