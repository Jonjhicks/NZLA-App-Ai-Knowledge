# RSR Ryegrass

RSR stands for Recover, Spread, Regenerate. The name describes what this cultivar does differently from standard perennial ryegrass: it produces above-ground stolons that spread laterally, fill gaps, and create a dense mat that standard ryegrass can't replicate.

## What makes RSR different

Standard perennial ryegrass tillers from the crown. RSR does that too, but it also produces stolons that run along the surface and produce new plants at their nodes. This gives RSR two mechanisms for filling bare patches: crown tillering (same as regular rye) plus stolon spread.

The stolon mat that develops over time is the other key trait. Dense stolon coverage creates a physical barrier that Poa annua seed can't penetrate easily. Not a chemical fix, a competitive one.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 25 to 35 g per square metre (3 kg per 100m²).

Oversowing: 15 to 20 g per square metre (2 kg per 100m²).

Autumn is preferred (mid-March to early May) for the same reasons as standard ryegrass: cooler air, warm soil, lower  pressure. Spring sowing works but summer stress can limit establishment before the plant has fully rooted.

Germination is 7 to 14 days. The stolon-spreading habit develops progressively over 2 to 3 seasons.

## Mowing height

20 to 50 mm. Handles down to 15 mm in good conditions.

The most important thing to understand: do not scalp RSR. The stolons run on the surface. Scalping removes them. If you cut too low and strip the stolon mat, you've removed the spreading mechanism. Let the stolons sit just above the surface — they're not thatch, they're the plant doing its job.

## What RSR is for

Sports fields with high wear. The self-repair from stolons means the turf recovers from localised wear without patching.

Family lawns with heavy foot traffic and kids. Same principle.

Poa annua-prone situations where chemical control alone isn't holding the line. RSR won't eliminate Poa but the competitive stolon mat reduces ingress over time.

Any situation where ryegrass wear recovery is too slow and ongoing seeding-and-over-seeding is the current solution.

## Poa suppression

This is a long game. Year one of RSR doesn't look different from standard rye. By years two and three, as the stolon mat thickens, Poa germination is physically suppressed by the dense coverage at soil level.

For best Poa outcomes: sow at the higher end of the rate range, avoid scalping, maintain consistent mowing (regular height removal, not letting it grow long then cutting hard), and use pre-emergent herbicide in late summer/early autumn while the RSR establishes. The combination of competitive density and pre-emergent gives much better results than either alone.

## RSR vs standard perennial ryegrass

For a straightforward lawn that will be maintained without major traffic or wear: standard perennial ryegrass is fine. Simpler, same care, proven.

For wear recovery and Poa suppression: RSR. The stolons are the point of difference. You pay a small premium for the cultivar and you get active spread between sowings.

## Common mistakes

Scalping. Mentioned above, worth mentioning twice.

Expecting instant Poa suppression. The stolon mat takes 2 to 3 seasons to develop fully. Patience is required.

Sowing too thin. At low rates the stolon mat doesn't develop densely enough to suppress Poa or handle hard wear. Stick to the recommended range.

Neglecting irrigation-general during establishment. RSR stolons need consistent moisture to root into the surface. Dry out during the first 6 weeks and you lose the stolon development.

## Establishment programme

Same as any premium ryegrass: NZLA Starter at preparation, Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks, All Seasons as the ongoing maintenance base. Iron+ and Charger help density. Keep mowing height up (30 mm or above) for the first season to give stolons time to establish without being removed.
