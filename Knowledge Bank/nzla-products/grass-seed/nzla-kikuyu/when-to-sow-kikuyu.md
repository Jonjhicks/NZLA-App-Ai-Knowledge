# When should I sow NZLA Kikuyu?

Late spring through early summer. Soil temps 18 to 25 °C. October to December in upper North Island, late November to December in cooler regions where kikuyu is still viable.

Why warm soil:
- Kikuyu is a warm-season grass. Cool soil delays germination and weakens seedlings.
- Optimal germination is at 22 to 25 °C soil temps.
- Sowing too early (cool soil) results in patchy establishment and slow growth.

Process:
1. Soil prep: cultivate, level, remove debris.
2. Apply NZLA Starter at the prep stage.
3. Sow kikuyu at 1 to 2 g per square metre.
4. Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
5. Germination in 14 to 21 days at optimal temps.
6. First mow at 50 to 70 mm leaf height.

The low seed rate is right for raw kikuyu seed. Don't go heavier hoping for faster cover, you'll get crowded weak seedlings that thin out.

Don't sow:
- Cool South Island (mid to lower South Island, kikuyu not really viable here).
- Autumn (lawn won't establish before winter dormancy).
- Winter (soil too cold, no germination).
- Heatwaves above 30 °C without cooling irrigation.

For best results, irrigate consistently through establishment. Kikuyu seedlings handle the heat but need consistent moisture for the first 4 to 6 weeks.

Once established, kikuyu is one of the most drought-tolerant and wear-tolerant lawn grasses in NZ. The trade-off is the aggressive spreading habit (it invades garden beds) and the brown dormancy through cold winters.
