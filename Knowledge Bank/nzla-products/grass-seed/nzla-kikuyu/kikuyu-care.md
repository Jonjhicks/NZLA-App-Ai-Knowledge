# How do I care for a kikuyu lawn?

Different rhythm to cool-season lawn care.

Mowing:
- Cutting height 30 to 50 mm for general home lawn use. Lower for higher input lawns.
- Through summer, mow every 4 to 7 days at peak growth. Skip a week and the lawn gets away on you.
- Through winter (in upper NI), once every 2 to 4 weeks as growth slows.
- NZLA PGR (trinexapac) is hugely valuable on kikuyu - one application can cut mowing frequency by a third to a half through the peak growing season.

Fertiliser:
- All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular through spring, summer and autumn.
- Less fertiliser through winter (kikuyu is semi-dormant and won't take it up).
- Foliar feeds (Charger, Liquid Boost) optional for high-input owners.

Edging:
- Critical for kikuyu. Without regular edging, the lawn invades garden beds, paths and driveways within weeks.
- Use a half-moon edger every 2 to 3 weeks through summer.
- Edging strips or plastic edging help slow the invasion but don't stop it.
- Some owners use a strip of bare or pebbled mulch as a kikuyu barrier.

Herbicides:
- BWC at label rate is safe on kikuyu (not on fine fescue or browntop).
- Halo for sedge control - check label, generally safe.
- NZLA Blockade (warm-season label) is registered for kikuyu.
- Don't use Etho on kikuyu.

Renovation:
- Scarify in late spring as the lawn is starting to push.
- Top dressing with sandy loam helps level the surface and supports the spreading growth habit.
- Core aeration once or twice a year if compaction is a problem.
