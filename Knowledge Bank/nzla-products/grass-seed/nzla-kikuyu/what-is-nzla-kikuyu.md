# What is NZLA Kikuyu seed?

Raw kikuyu seed (Pennisetum clandestinum). Worth knowing this is the actual seed, not the 5% kikuyu blend that's commonly sold in retail packaging where 95% of the bag is ryegrass carrier.

Why raw seed matters:
- Kikuyu establishes from kikuyu seed, not ryegrass carrier. The 5% blends germinate as mostly ryegrass with kikuyu coming through underneath later.
- Raw seed gives a kikuyu lawn from the start.
- Lower total seed weight needed per area (raw seed is more concentrated).

What kikuyu is good for:
- Hot dry summer regions (upper North Island, Northland).
- High-wear family lawns (kikuyu recovers from damage faster than any other species in NZ).
- Heat-stressed sites where cool-season grasses struggle.
- Drought-tolerant lawns once established.

What kikuyu isn't great for:
- Cool regions (South Island, lower North Island) - the lawn goes dormant for longer through winter and looks brown.
- Tidy aesthetic lawns - kikuyu has a coarser texture than ryegrass and tall fescue.
- Owners who don't want to mow often through summer (kikuyu is very fast-growing).
- Garden beds and edges - kikuyu invades aggressively, requires regular edging.

Sows at 1 to 2 g per square metre (much lower rate than cool-season grasses because raw kikuyu seed is potent). Soil temps 18 to 25 °C for germination, so late spring to early summer sowing (October to December in upper North Island).

NZLA Kikuyu continues growing at temperatures as low as 8 °C, which is lower than couch and most other warm-season grasses. Better winter colour retention than typical warm-season options.
