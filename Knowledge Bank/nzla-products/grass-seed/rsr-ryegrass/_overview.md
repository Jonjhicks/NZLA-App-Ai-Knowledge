# NZLA RSR Ryegrass

The wear-recovering ryegrass. RSR stands for "Recover, Spread, Regenerate." Stoloniferous perennial ryegrass that spreads horizontally via stolons, which standard bunch-type ryegrass can't do. The choice for lawns getting hammered by kids, dogs, sports use, or anywhere damage to bare patches happens regularly.

## What it is

Standard perennial ryegrass is a bunch-forming grass. Each plant tillers from a single crown and the lawn fills in by tillers being close enough that the canopy joins. When wear damage tears out a patch, the only way the lawn recovers is by adjacent plants growing into the bare ground — slow and patchy.

RSR is bred to throw determinate stolons, horizontal runners on the surface that put down new roots and produce new plants. When wear damage takes out a patch, the surrounding RSR plants send stolons into the bare area and fill it in actively. The lawn self-repairs.

The other advantage is canopy density. The stolons create an extremely tight surface mat, which makes it hard for Poa annua and other invading grasses to establish.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: 3 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: 2 kg per 100m².

Same establishment programme as other ryegrasses. NZLA Starter at soil prep, consistent moisture, first mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

## Maintenance height

20 to 50 mm typical. Handles down to 15 mm with active management.

The lateral growth habit means the lawn can carry slightly more height without looking shaggy because the canopy density is high.

## When to use

Sports fields, parks, public spaces.

Heavy-use family backyards where kids and dogs are tearing patches out regularly.

Anywhere wear damage is a recurring issue and you want the lawn to actively repair itself.

Lawns under Poa annua pressure where the dense stolon mat helps competitively exclude poa.

Overseeding into existing wear-damaged lawns to bring in the spreading habit.

## When not to use

Heavy shade. Ryegrass shade limitations still apply.

Where ultimate dark colour or fine texture is the top priority. MDR or Dark Perennial Rye are better aesthetic choices.

Warm subtropical sites where kikuyu suits better.

## Common mistakes

Sowing too thin expecting the spread to compensate. Stolons need a starting plant population, sow at the rate.

Mowing too short. The stolons run on the surface, scalp them with a low cut and you remove the spreading mechanism.

Treating it as a self-maintaining lawn. RSR repairs wear better than other ryegrasses but it still needs the same fertility, water and mowing programme.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter at soil prep, Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks.

All Seasons as the maintenance base.

For Poa pressure: NZLA Paclo or Blockade in addition to the competitive density RSR provides.

Iron+ for colour through cooler months, since the high density tends to show colour issues a bit more visibly than thinner lawns.
