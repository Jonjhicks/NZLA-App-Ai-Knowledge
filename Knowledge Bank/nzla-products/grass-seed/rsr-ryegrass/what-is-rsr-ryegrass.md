# What is NZLA RSR Ryegrass?

A stoloniferous perennial ryegrass cultivar. The standout feature is that it spreads horizontally through above-ground stolons (runners) rather than just relying on seed and tillering. RSR stands for Recover, Spread, Regenerate.

What that gives you:
- Self-repairing turf. Wear patches fill in from the surrounding lawn rather than needing reseed.
- Dense sward that makes it hard for annual poa to establish.
- Better wear tolerance than standard non-spreading ryegrass.
- Recovery from damage faster than standard ryegrass.
- All the standard ryegrass benefits (fast establishment, decent colour, cool-season activity).

Where it fits:
- High-use family lawns where wear is a recurring issue.
- Sports field surfaces where recovery between use matters.
- Areas prone to poa establishment, where the dense self-repairing growth suppresses poa.
- Patch repair situations where the surrounding lawn is RSR (the new stolons will spread into the patch).

Trade-offs:
- Higher cost than standard Perennial Rye.
- Not as dark as DPR or MDR (RSR is selected for behaviour, not colour).
- Stoloniferous behaviour is new for most NZ lawn owners, takes a season to see the effect.

Sow at standard ryegrass establishment rates. The stolons start showing after the first season as the lawn establishes. The full self-repairing benefit develops over 2 to 3 seasons.

For lawns with active kids, dogs, and high traffic, RSR is one of the strongest options in the NZLA range.
