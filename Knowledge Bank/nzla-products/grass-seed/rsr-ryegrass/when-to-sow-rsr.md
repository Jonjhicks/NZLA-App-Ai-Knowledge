# When should I sow RSR Ryegrass?

Same as standard perennial ryegrass. Soil temps 12 to 18 °C. Autumn (mid-March to early May) or spring (October to early December) for most regions.

Autumn is the better window:
- Lawn establishes through cool moist conditions.
- Stolons start forming in spring of the following year when the lawn is mature enough.
- All winter to build root depth.

Spring sowing:
- Works but lawn has to establish through summer.
- Stolon formation is delayed until the lawn has matured (typically 4 to 6 months from sowing).

New lawn establishment:
- Soil prep, apply NZLA Starter granular, incorporate.
- Sow RSR at 25 to 35 g per square metre.
- Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
- First mow at 70 to 80 mm leaf height.

Overseeding existing lawn:
- Autumn, mow short, scarify or rake heavily.
- Sow at 15 to 20 g per square metre.
- The new stolons add to the existing lawn over time.
- After 2 to 3 seasons of consistent overseeding, the lawn shifts toward RSR's stoloniferous behaviour.

Mixed cultivar blends:
- Can mix RSR with DPR or MDR for combined stoloniferous behaviour and dark colour.
- Typical blend would be 50% RSR for the self-repair, 50% DPR or MDR for the colour upgrade.
- Sow at standard total rate (25 to 35 g per square metre).
