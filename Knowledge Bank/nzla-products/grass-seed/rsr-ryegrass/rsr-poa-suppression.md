# Does RSR Ryegrass suppress annual poa?

Yes, indirectly through density. The stoloniferous spreading habit creates a denser sward than standard ryegrass, and poa annua struggles to establish in dense turf. The seedlings don't get the space and light they need.

How it works:
- Standard ryegrass relies on tillering alone, which creates a sward with small gaps at the soil surface between tillers.
- RSR's stolons spread horizontally, filling those gaps and closing the canopy at the soil level.
- Poa seed lands on the surface but can't establish because there's no exposed soil and no light reaching the surface.

This isn't a substitute for selective herbicide control of poa (NZLA Etho) or cultural management. It works alongside those tools, the dense sward gives less room for poa to start, the herbicide handles poa that does establish, the cultural side prevents the conditions that favour poa.

For a lawn with persistent annual poa problems:
- Establish or overseed with RSR for the dense competitive sward.
- Selective herbicide (NZLA Etho) on actively growing poa through autumn and winter.
- Cultural fixes (don't overwater, fertilise well, mow at sensible height) to keep the lawn competitive.

Over 2 to 3 seasons, the combination noticeably reduces poa populations. RSR is one of the better cool-season options for lawns where poa has been an ongoing issue.

Same dense behaviour helps with weed pressure generally, not just poa. Annual weeds (chickweed, dandelion seedlings, others) struggle to establish in a dense RSR sward.
