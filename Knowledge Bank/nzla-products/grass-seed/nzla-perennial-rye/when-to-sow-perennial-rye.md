# When should I sow NZLA Perennial Rye?

Soil temps 12 to 18 °C. Autumn (mid-March to early May) and spring (October to early December) for most regions.

Autumn is the better window:
- Soil still warm from summer, fast germination (7 to 14 days).
- Cool moist conditions through autumn favour establishment.
- Winter to build root depth before the next summer.
- Lower weed pressure than spring.

Spring is acceptable for new lawn establishment if autumn timing was missed:
- Germination is fast in warming spring soil.
- Higher weed pressure (tank-mix NZLA Meso at sowing).
- Lawn establishes through summer, vulnerable to heat stress in first season.

New lawn establishment:
- Soil prep: cultivate, level, remove rocks and debris.
- Apply NZLA Starter at the prep stage, incorporate into the top 25 to 50 mm.
- Sow seed at 25 to 35 g per square metre.
- Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
- First mow at 70 to 80 mm leaf height.

Patch repair:
- Rake the bare patch to break up the surface.
- Apply a small amount of NZLA Starter.
- Sow seed at 30 to 40 g per square metre (slightly heavier than full establishment for fast cover).
- Cover lightly with topsoil or compost.
- Water lightly and frequently until germination.

Larger areas (lifestyle blocks, sports fields): same approach but using a seed drill or spreader for application rather than hand broadcasting.
