# Should I use NZLA Perennial Rye or DPR?

Same species (Lolium perenne), different selection. DPR is a darker premium cultivar at a higher price. Perennial Rye is the standard quality at lower cost.

Decision tree:
- Standard family home lawn, want a functional lawn at low cost: Perennial Rye.
- Want a noticeably darker lawn without paying premium cultivar pricing: DPR.
- Overseeding existing standard ryegrass to improve colour over time: DPR.
- Large area (lifestyle block, big sports field): Perennial Rye for cost-effectiveness.
- Patch repair where colour match matters: same cultivar as the existing lawn (Perennial Rye if standard, DPR if darker).

The two seeds can be blended for partial colour upgrade. 30 to 50% DPR in a Perennial Rye base gives noticeably better colour at much less than full DPR cost.

For wear tolerance, both perform similarly. For drought tolerance, both perform similarly. For establishment speed, both perform similarly. The meaningful difference is colour, density and price.

For most NZ family lawns where the priority is a hard-wearing functional lawn rather than maximum visual quality, Perennial Rye does the job. DPR earns its keep on home lawns where the owner notices and values the deeper colour.
