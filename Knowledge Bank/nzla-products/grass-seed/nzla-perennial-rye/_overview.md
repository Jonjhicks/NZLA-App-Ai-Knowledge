# NZLA Perennial Rye

The standard NZLA perennial ryegrass. Hard-wearing, winter active, drought-tolerant, fast to establish. The reliable choice for most NZ lawn situations where the standard ryegrass profile suits.

## What it is

Lolium perenne is the workhorse cool-season grass for NZ. Establishes fast from seed, ties together into a dense sward, handles wear, and stays green through winter in most of the country. NZLA Perennial Rye uses certified seed selected for the standard NZ lawn use case.

The "winter active" trait is the one that matters in NZ. Standard ryegrass varieties slow or stop growth as soil temperatures drop. Winter-active selections keep growing through cool months, which reduces disease pressure (constant turnover of leaf area) and keeps the lawn presentable through autumn and winter.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: typically 3 to 4 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: typically 2 kg per 100m².

Apply NZLA Starter at soil preparation before sowing. Water to keep the surface moist during germination (7 to 10 days for ryegrass at warm soil temperatures, longer in cool). First mow once seedlings reach roughly 60 to 80 mm.

## Maintenance height

Mowable down to 15 mm if you want a tight cut. Most home users hold 30 to 50 mm for a balance of appearance and durability. The lower you mow the more often you'll need to cut and the more attention to fertility, watering and disease the lawn needs.

## When to use

Standard home lawns where reliable performance matters more than ultimate aesthetic premium.

Lawns expecting wear from kids, dogs, sports.

Quick establishment situations (real estate prep, post-renovation).

Cool-season climate, which is essentially all of NZ for ryegrass selection purposes.

## When not to use

Heavy shade. Ryegrass is light-hungry, performance suffers below about 4 to 5 hours of direct sun a day.

Where ultimate dark colour or fine texture matters. NZLA Dark Perennial Rye or MDR are the upgrade options.

Warm subtropical sites in upper Northland where kikuyu may suit better.

## Common mistakes

Sowing too thin. Below 3 kg per 100m² and you get a thin patchy lawn that takes far longer to fill in.

Letting the seedling lawn dry out during establishment. Once the seed has imbibed water, it has to stay damp until the root system is established. A dry-out at day 5 kills the seedlings.

Mowing too short too early. Wait until the first leaves are 60 to 80 mm before the first cut.

Skipping the starter fertiliser. New ryegrass needs phosphorus for root drive and trace elements for vigour.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter granular at soil prep.

Liquid Starter as a foliar at 4 to 5 weeks once seedlings can take it.

All Seasons or All Seasons N+ as the maintenance base once established (8 to 12 weeks post-sowing).
