# What chemicals can't I use on Fine Fescue?

Fine fescue is sensitive to a number of common turf chemicals. Get this wrong and you damage or kill the lawn.

Not safe on fine fescue:
- NZLA Etho (ethofumesate) - will damage or kill fine fescue. Designed for ryegrass and tall fescue only.
- NZLA Gold (picloram + triclopyr) - damages fine fescue at label rate. Use only on ryegrass and tall fescue.
- NZLA Blockade (prodiamine) - NZ label restricts to warm-season turf, off-label on fine fescue (and all cool-season here).
- NZLA Meso at full mature lawn rate - read label, may need lower rate on fine fescue.

Generally safe at label rate:
- NZLA BWC (clopyralid + MCPA + diflufenican) - usually safe but test a small area first.
- NZLA Halo (halosulfuron) - check label, generally safe at label rate.
- NZLA fertilisers and biostimulants - all generally safe.
- NZLA Azoxy and Fungus Pro fungicides - safe at label rate.
- NZLA Grub+ insecticide - safe.
- NZLA wetting agents - safe.

If you've got a mixed lawn (fine fescue patches mixed with ryegrass or tall fescue), avoid the fine-fescue-unsafe products entirely. The ryegrass and tall fescue will be fine but the fine fescue patches will be damaged.

When in doubt, spot-test on a small area (1 to 2 m²) and wait 4 to 6 weeks before treating the whole lawn. Cheaper than killing the lawn off entirely.

For poa control in fine fescue, the chemistry options are limited. Cultural control through dense competitive turf is the main approach.
