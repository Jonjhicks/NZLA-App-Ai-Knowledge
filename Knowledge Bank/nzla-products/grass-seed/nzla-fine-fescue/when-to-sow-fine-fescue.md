# When should I sow Fine Fescue?

Autumn is the right window. Soil temps 12 to 18 °C. Mid-March to mid-May depending on region.

Why autumn:
- Fine fescue establishes slowly. Spring sowing puts seedlings straight into summer stress, autumn sowing gives them through winter to mature.
- Cool moist autumn conditions favour establishment.
- Shade tolerance develops as the lawn matures, autumn-sown lawns are ready to handle the next year's summer.

Process:
1. Soil prep: cultivate, level, remove debris (fine fescue tolerates lower-quality prep than premium lawns require, but good prep still pays off).
2. Apply NZLA Starter granular at the prep stage.
3. Sow fine fescue at 30 to 40 g per square metre.
4. Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
5. Don't mow until seedlings are 70 to 80 mm (fine fescue is slow to recover from early mowing).

Spring sowing:
- Possible but not ideal.
- Lawn has to establish through first summer.
- In dry inland regions (Canterbury, Otago), spring sowing fine fescue often results in significant summer loss.
- Coastal cooler regions (Wellington, Auckland west coast) can manage spring sowing more reliably.

Overseeding existing lawn:
- Autumn timing.
- Mow short, rake the surface.
- Sow at 20 to 25 g per square metre.
- The fine fescue establishes alongside the existing lawn.
- Particularly useful in shaded patches where other grasses are thinning.

Don't mix fine fescue with tall fescue or ryegrass at sowing in patches where you want uniform appearance. The species have different textures and growth habits, the lawn ends up uneven.
