# What is NZLA Fine Fescue?

A blend of fine-leaf fescue species (creeping red fescue and chewings fescue). Very fine leaf texture, exceptional drought tolerance, and the best shade tolerance of any cool-season turf species available in NZ.

What you get:
- Very fine leaf texture (the finest of any NZLA seed option).
- Exceptional drought tolerance.
- Strong shade tolerance (the best cool-season option for shade).
- Low fertility requirements.
- Slow growth (less mowing).
- Cool-season behaviour (winter active, summer slower in heat).

Where it fits:
- Shaded lawns under trees where ryegrass and tall fescue struggle.
- Low-input lawns where the owner wants minimal fertiliser and mowing.
- Premium fine-textured aesthetic lawns.
- Difficult dry shady spots that other species can't handle.

Important restrictions:
- Does not tolerate Etho (poa selective herbicide) - will be damaged or killed.
- Does not tolerate Gold (picloram + triclopyr) - damaged.
- BWC at label rate is generally safe, but always test a small area first.
- Most warm-season herbicides will damage fine fescue.
- Low wear tolerance - not suited to high-traffic family lawns.

Sow at 30 to 40 g per square metre for new lawn. Establishment is slow (10 to 21 days germination). Don't mow until seedlings are 70 to 80 mm.

For shaded difficult areas where no other lawn grass works, NZLA Fine Fescue is the right choice. For high-traffic family lawns, look at ryegrass or RSR instead.
