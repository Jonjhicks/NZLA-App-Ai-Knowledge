# NZLA Fine Fescue

The shade-tolerant fine-leaf fescue blend. Creeping red fescue plus chewing fescue, both fine-textured cool-season grasses with strong shade tolerance and very low fertility requirement. Different use case to ryegrass and tall fescue, this is the answer for shaded, low-input situations where the other species struggle.

## What it is

Fine fescues are the cool-season grass family adapted to lower light, lower fertility, drier sites. The leaf is very fine (much finer than ryegrass or tall fescue), the colour is a softer green, and the maintenance requirements are genuinely lower than ryegrass.

The NZLA blend uses creeping red fescue (Festuca rubra, which spreads slowly via rhizomes) plus chewing fescue (Festuca rubra var. commutata, bunch-forming). Together they produce a fine, dense, low-maintenance sward.

The trade-off is wear tolerance. Fine fescue doesn't handle the heavy use that ryegrass or tall fescue does. It's the choice for lawns where appearance and survival under shade matter more than how many kids and dogs can hammer it.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: typically 2.5 to 4 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: typically 2 kg per 100m².

Germinates in 10 to 14 days at warm soil temperatures, longer in cool. NZLA Starter at soil prep.

## Critical herbicide restriction

DO NOT use NZLA Etho on fine fescue. It will damage or kill it.

NZLA Halo (haloxyfop-P-methyl) is registered for fine fescue but with a hard rate cap of 15 mL per 100m². Do not exceed.

Other selective herbicides need label-checking before use on fine fescue. Always verify the species is listed.

## Maintenance height

25 to 60 mm typical. Fine fescue tolerates a wider mowing height range than its appearance suggests.

For premium fine-fescue lawns, 25 to 40 mm with reel mower cuts gets the most out of the texture.

## When to use

Shaded lawns where ryegrass and tall fescue thin out.

Low-input, low-maintenance sites where the owner doesn't want a high-fertility, frequent-mowing programme.

Drought-prone exposed sites where reduced summer water use matters.

Fine-aesthetic lawns where leaf texture is part of the look.

Renovations of shaded areas where the existing rye has struggled.

## When not to use

Heavy-wear sites. Fine fescue is not for the kids-and-dogs lawn.

Lawns wanting the highest fertility programme. Over-feeding fine fescue causes problems, not benefits.

Areas where Etho or similar non-fine-fescue-safe herbicides will be needed.

Where rapid wear recovery matters.

## Common mistakes

Treating it like ryegrass on the fertility programme. Over-feeding causes thatch, soft growth and disease.

Spraying Etho on a fine fescue lawn. Kills the lawn you wanted to keep.

Exceeding the 15 mL/100m² Halo rate cap.

Heavy-use management on a species not suited to it.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter at soil prep (lighter rate than for ryegrass/tall fescue).

All Seasons at reduced frequency. Fine fescue doesn't want the same fertility level as ryegrass.

NZLA Halo within the 15 mL/100m² cap for selective grass weed control.
