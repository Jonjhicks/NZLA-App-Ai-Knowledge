# NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue (UTF)

The premium tall fescue blend. Selected for very dark colour, fine texture (for a tall fescue), and high density. Combines the visual quality of a premium ryegrass lawn with tall fescue's inherent drought, heat, and shade tolerance that ryegrass can't match.

## What it is

UTF uses selected premium cultivars rather than commodity tall fescue seed. The colour is dark, the leaf is finer than typical coarse-leaf fescue, and the sward density is high. The appearance is closer to a quality ryegrass lawn than to what most people picture when they think "tall fescue."

It's a bunch-forming grass — unlike Elfin Tall Fescue, which is the rhizomatous self-spreading sibling. UTF doesn't actively repair wear patches the way Elfin does. The trade-off is denser, more visually uniform appearance.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 35 to 45 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 25 to 30 g per square metre.

Autumn is the right window (mid-March to early May). Soil temps 14 to 22°C — tall fescue prefers slightly warmer soil for germination than ryegrass. Germination in 10 to 14 days.

Irrigation through establishment is essential. Tall fescue establishment is slower than ryegrass and a dry-out is harder to recover from. Plan for 8 to 12 weeks to "ready for normal use" rather than 6 to 8 weeks for ryegrass.

## Maintenance height

50 to 150 mm. Tall fescue handles a wide mowing range because the deep root system feeds the plant from well below the surface.

Most home users hold 50 to 70 mm. Sports or feature lawns with active management can go closer.

## UTF vs Ryegrass

The comparison that drives most decisions.

Ryegrass: faster establishment, more aggressive tillering, better cold-weather activity, less drought tolerant, less heat tolerant.

UTF: slower establishment, very high drought tolerance (deep roots), strong heat tolerance, wider shade tolerance, slower wear recovery.

For cool moist regions with heavy use: ryegrass. For hot dry summer regions wanting premium aesthetic and summer green retention: UTF.

A 50/50 blend of ryegrass and tall fescue at sowing works well for sites wanting both characteristics — fast establishment and fill from ryegrass, long-term drought tolerance building from the tall fescue.

## UTF vs Elfin Tall Fescue

Elfin: rhizomatous spreading, active self-repair from wear, reasonably fine texture.

UTF: premium cultivar blend, darker and finer than Elfin, non-rhizomatous.

For high-wear sites where self-repair matters: Elfin. For showcase lawns where appearance is the priority: UTF. Blend both for combined self-repair and premium appearance.

## When UTF is the right choice

Drought-prone sites (Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, inland Otago) where summer green retention matters.

Premium home lawns in warm dry summer regions.

Partial shade sites where ryegrass thins.

New lawn installation where drought tolerance is a key requirement.

## Common mistakes

Letting seedlings dry out during slow establishment. Irrigation is essential, not optional.

Mowing too short. Tall fescue scalped at 20 to 25 mm stresses the plant.

Expecting Elfin-style self-repair from UTF. If a wear patch develops, oversow it.

## Establishment programme

NZLA Starter at soil prep. Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks. All Seasons as maintenance base. Iron+ periodically. Aerate or Penetrate on heavy soils to open the profile for the deep root system.
