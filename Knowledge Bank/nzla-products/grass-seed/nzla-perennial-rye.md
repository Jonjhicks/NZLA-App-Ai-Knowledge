# NZLA Perennial Rye

The standard NZLA perennial ryegrass. Hard-wearing, winter active, drought-tolerant by ryegrass standards, fast to establish. The reliable choice for most NZ lawn situations.

## What it is

Lolium perenne is the workhorse cool-season grass for NZ. Establishes fast from seed (7 to 14 days at warm soil temperatures), ties together into a dense sward, handles wear, and stays green through winter in most of the country.

The "winter active" trait is the key one for NZ conditions. Many standard ryegrass varieties slow or stop growth as soil temperatures drop. Winter-active selections keep growing through cool months, which maintains turf cover and reduces  pressure from stagnant canopy.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 25 to 35 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 15 to 20 g per square metre.

Patch repair: 30 to 40 g per square metre (heavier rate for faster cover).

Autumn (mid-March to early May) is the better sowing window. Spring works but comes with higher weed pressure — tank-mix NZLA Meso at sowing if spring establishment is necessary.

## Establishment programme

NZLA Starter granular at soil prep, incorporated into the top 25 to 50 mm. Sow, light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination. First mow at 60 to 80 mm. Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks. Transition to All Seasons at 8 to 12 weeks.

Moisture during establishment: once the seed has imbibed water, it must stay damp until the root system is functional. Dry-out at day 5 kills the seedlings.

## Maintenance height

15 mm minimum mowing height. Most home users hold 30 to 50 mm.

## Perennial Rye vs DPR

Same species, different selection. DPR is a darker cultivar at a higher price. Perennial Rye is standard quality at lower cost.

Standard home lawn, functional and hard-wearing, cost-effective for larger areas: Perennial Rye.

Noticeably darker lawn, home user who values the colour upgrade: DPR.

The two can be blended (30 to 50% DPR in a Perennial Rye base) for partial colour upgrade at a moderate cost premium.

## When Perennial Rye is the right choice

Standard home lawn establishment and repair.

Larger areas (lifestyle blocks, sports fields) where cost per area matters.

Quick repairs where the goal is fast functional cover.

## When to use something else

Heavy shade. Ryegrass is light-hungry below about 4 to 5 hours of direct sun per day.

Dry summer regions where drought tolerance matters more than fast establishment. Tall fescue suits better.

When ultimate colour and density are the priority. DPR or MDR.

## Common mistakes

Sowing too thin. Below 3 kg per 100m² on a new lawn and you get a patchy result that takes much longer to fill in.

Letting the seedling lawn dry out during establishment.

Mowing too short too early. Wait for 60 to 80 mm height before the first cut.

Skipping starter fertiliser. New ryegrass needs phosphorus for root drive.
