# Can I overseed an existing lawn with DPR?

Yes, this is one of DPR's main home use cases. Overseeding an existing standard ryegrass lawn with DPR over two or three seasons gradually shifts the lawn toward darker colour and better density.

Process:
1. Autumn (March to May), mow the lawn short (down to 25 mm if possible).
2. Scarify or rake heavily to remove thatch and open the soil surface.
3. Spread DPR at 15 to 20 g per square metre with a calibrated spreader.
4. Roll or rake to get seed into soil contact.
5. Apply Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 days, then at 7 to 10 day intervals through first 4 to 6 weeks.
6. Water lightly and frequently until germination, then less often as seedlings establish.
7. First mow at 70 to 80 mm leaf height of the new seedlings.

The new DPR seedlings establish alongside the existing lawn. Over the following 2 to 3 seasons, repeating the overseeding annually progressively increases the proportion of DPR in the lawn. After 3 to 4 years of consistent overseeding, the lawn is largely DPR and noticeably darker than it was.

This approach is cheaper and lower-effort than a full lawn renovation. The trade-off is the gradual timeline, you won't see a dramatic colour change after one year, but you'll see steady improvement.

Best results when paired with annual aeration (autumn before the overseed) and a regular fertiliser programme (granular base of All Seasons or All Seasons N+ plus foliar applications). DPR establishes faster and looks darker on a well-fed, well-aerated lawn than on a struggling one.
