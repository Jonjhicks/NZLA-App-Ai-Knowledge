# NZLA Dark Perennial Ryegrass

The dark green premium ryegrass. Selected cultivar with deeper colour, denser growth and finer texture than standard NZLA Perennial Rye, plus stronger performance through warmer months. The step up for home users who want a more premium-looking lawn without going as far as Midnight Dark Rye (MDR).

## What it is

Standard perennial ryegrass cultivars sit in a medium-green range and have a medium leaf width. Dark Perennial Rye is bred for a deeper green appearance and finer leaf, which translates to a more uniform, denser-looking sward.

The other notable difference is summer performance. Standard rye tends to slow and lose colour in warmer months. Dark Perennial Rye holds its colour and growth better through summer, which extends the visual peak of the lawn through the year.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: typically 3 to 4 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: typically 2 kg per 100m².

Same establishment programme as standard ryegrass, NZLA Starter at soil prep, consistent moisture through germination, first mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

## Maintenance height

Mowable down to 12 mm for a tight presentation cut. Most home users hold 25 to 50 mm. The finer texture handles closer mowing better than standard ryegrass.

## When to use

Premium home lawns where colour and density matter more than budget.

Show lawns or feature lawns where the visual finish carries weight.

Sports turf where presentation and performance both matter.

Replacement or overseeding in standard rye lawns to lift colour and density over time.

## When not to use

Heavy shade. Same ryegrass shade limitation as the standard rye.

Budget-conscious establishment of large areas. The premium cultivar carries a premium price.

Warm subtropical Northland where kikuyu suits better.

## Common mistakes

Sowing too thin. Premium seed thinly sown gives a premium-looking but patchy lawn.

Mowing too short too early. Let the seedlings establish before tight cuts.

Treating it as low-maintenance. Premium cultivars reward attention to fertility, mowing height and water management more than the standard equivalents.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter granular at soil prep.

Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks.

All Seasons as the maintenance base, with Iron+ for colour boost periodically.

NZLA PGR through active growing season to tighten the canopy further.
