# When should I sow NZLA Dark Perennial Rye?

Soil temps 12 to 18 °C. Autumn (mid-March to early May) and spring (October to early December) for most regions.

Autumn is the better window:
- Soil still warm from summer, fast germination.
- Cool moist conditions favour establishment.
- All winter to build root depth before next summer.
- Lower weed pressure than spring.

Spring works but isn't ideal. Higher weed pressure, lawn has to handle summer establishment stress. If you're spring sowing, consider tank-mixing NZLA Meso at sowing to manage weed seed bank germination.

For overseeding existing lawns:
- Autumn is the right time.
- Mow short, scarify or rake the surface to open the canopy.
- Sow at 15 to 20 g per square metre over existing turf.
- Roll or rake to get seed into soil contact.
- Apply Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 days post-germination, then every 7 to 10 days through first 4 to 6 weeks.

For new lawn establishment:
- Prepare soil, apply NZLA Starter granular and incorporate.
- Sow DPR at 25 to 35 g per square metre.
- Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
- First mow at 70 to 80 mm leaf height (drop to 40 to 50 mm over the next 2 mows).

Avoid mid-summer or mid-winter sowing.
