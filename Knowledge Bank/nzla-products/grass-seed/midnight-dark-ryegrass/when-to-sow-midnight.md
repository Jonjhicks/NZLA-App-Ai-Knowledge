# When should I sow Midnight Dark Ryegrass?

Soil temps 12 to 18 °C is the sweet spot for ryegrass germination. In NZ that means autumn (mid-March to early May) or spring (October to early December) for most regions.

Autumn sowing is the better choice in most cases:
- Soil is still warm from summer, fast germination (7 to 14 days).
- Cool moist conditions through autumn favour establishment without heat stress.
- Lawn has all of winter to build root depth before the next summer.
- Weed pressure is lower than spring.

Spring sowing:
- Works but less ideal than autumn.
- Lawn has to establish through the first summer, vulnerable to heat stress.
- Higher weed pressure in spring (annual weeds germinate alongside the grass).
- Consider tank-mixing NZLA Meso at sowing to handle the weed seed bank.

Avoid:
- Mid-summer (lawn won't establish in heat).
- Mid-winter (soil temps too cold for reliable germination).

For overseeding existing lawns to improve colour and density:
- Autumn is the right time (lawn density is lower coming out of summer, seedlings have room).
- Mow short before overseeding to open the canopy.
- Sow at lighter rate than new establishment (around 15 to 20 g per square metre).
- Lawn density and colour improve over the following 2 to 3 seasons as the premium seed establishes.
