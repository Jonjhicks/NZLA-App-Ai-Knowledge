# Midnight Dark Ryegrass (MDR)

The premium dark cultivar. Among the darkest perennial ryegrass varieties available in NZ. Used in leading stadiums in Australia and the US. The choice when the lawn is going to be a showcase and the owner is willing to put the maintenance behind it.

## What it is

MDR sits at the top of the perennial ryegrass colour and density range. Deep dark green, fine leaf texture, very high tiller density. Visually it looks like a different species to standard ryegrass when both are healthy side by side.

The cultivar's used in international stadium turf, which is the most demanding cool-season turf use case in the world. That gives a real-world reference for what it can do under heavy management.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: typically 3 to 4 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: typically 2 kg per 100m².

Same general establishment as other ryegrasses but the visible payoff is heavily dependent on the maintenance programme, fertility, mowing height, water, and PGR use. MDR sown and then ignored doesn't look dramatically different to standard rye sown and ignored. Sown and managed properly, the difference is real and visible.

## Maintenance height

Mowable down to 12 mm. The cultivar handles close mowing better than standard rye thanks to the fine texture and high tiller density.

For showcase use, 20 to 40 mm with frequent mowing (every 4 to 7 days during active growth) gets the most out of the cultivar's appearance.

## When to use

Showcase home lawns, premium feature lawns, photography lawns.

Professional turf situations where stripe definition and appearance matter.

Renovations or oversowing of premium lawns where the existing rye is older or lighter.

Where the owner or manager is committed to active maintenance, fertility, mowing height, water management and PGR use.

## When not to use

Set-and-forget lawns. The cultivar's potential is wasted without management.

Heavy shade. Ryegrass shade limitations apply.

Budget-constrained installs where the premium seed cost can't be justified.

Warm-season climates where kikuyu or couch suit better.

## Common mistakes

Buying the premium seed and skipping the maintenance. The cultivar is a tool; without the programme behind it, you've just bought expensive standard-looking grass.

Mowing too infrequently. MDR shows off through frequent close mowing, not weekly home rotary cuts.

Skipping PGR. NZLA PGR holds the canopy tight between mows and keeps the stripe definition that's a big part of the look.

Underfeeding. MDR's appearance peaks with steady feed and won't shine on a hungry lawn.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter at soil prep, Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks.

All Seasons or All Seasons N+ as the maintenance base, Iron+ regularly for colour.

NZLA PGR through active growing season for canopy tightness and stripe definition.

Reel mower for the closest, cleanest cut. Rotary mowers work but reel cutting suits the cultivar best.
