# Should I sow Midnight Dark Ryegrass or NZLA Dark Perennial Rye?

Both are dark cultivar perennial ryegrasses, MDR is the higher-end premium choice.

MDR (Midnight Dark Ryegrass):
- Among the darkest perennial ryegrass cultivars available.
- Very fine leaf texture.
- Exceptional density.
- Higher cost per kg.
- Premium home lawns and sports field surfaces.

NZLA DPR (Dark Perennial Ryegrass):
- Dark green but not as dark as MDR.
- Standard ryegrass leaf texture.
- Good density.
- Lower cost per kg.
- More active in warmer months than standard rye.
- Standard home lawn use.

Decision tree:
- Top-end home lawn, want the darkest cleanest look, budget allows: MDR.
- Standard home lawn, want a dark cultivar without the premium price: DPR.
- Overseeding existing lawn to improve colour gradually: DPR is more cost-effective.
- New lawn establishment from scratch, want the best result: MDR.
- Sports field or commercial situation: depends on budget and use.

The two seed mixes can be combined if you want some MDR for darkness mixed with DPR for cost-effectiveness across a large area. Standard mix would be 30 to 50% MDR for the visible colour benefit with DPR carrying the bulk of the establishment.

Both perform best with autumn sowing in NZ conditions. Both respond to standard ryegrass establishment programmes (NZLA Starter, Liquid Starter, then maintenance with All Seasons or All Seasons N+).
