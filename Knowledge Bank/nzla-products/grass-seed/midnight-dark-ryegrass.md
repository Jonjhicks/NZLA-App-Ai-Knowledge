# Midnight Dark Ryegrass (MDR)

The premium dark perennial ryegrass. Among the darkest cultivars available in NZ. Used in leading stadiums in Australia and the US. The choice when the lawn is going to be a showcase and the management commitment is there to back it up.

## What it is

MDR sits at the top of the ryegrass colour and density range. Deep dark green, fine leaf texture, very high tiller density. Against standard ryegrass at the same fertility and mowing, the visual difference is real.

The cultivar's use in international stadium turf is a genuine reference point. That's the most demanding cool-season turf situation in the world.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 30 to 40 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 15 to 20 g per square metre.

Autumn sowing preferred (mid-March to early May). Soil temps 12 to 18°C. Germination in 7 to 14 days. Standard ryegrass establishment programme: NZLA Starter at soil prep, Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks, transition to All Seasons at 8 to 12 weeks.

## Maintenance height

Mowable down to 12 mm with the right management. Most home users who select MDR are holding 20 to 40 mm and mowing frequently — every 4 to 7 days during active growth.

The look MDR delivers depends heavily on frequent mowing. Occasional mowing with a rotary and MDR doesn't look dramatically different to standard rye.

## MDR vs Dark Perennial Rye (DPR)

MDR is the premium end. Darker, finer texture, higher density, higher cost per kg.

DPR sits in the middle of the range — noticeably darker than standard ryegrass but not as extreme as MDR, at a lower price.

For a showcase home lawn: MDR. For a good-looking lawn at a lower cost: DPR.

The two can be blended (30 to 50% MDR in a DPR base) for a cost compromise that still delivers noticeable colour benefit.

## When MDR delivers

The cultivar's potential is tightly linked to the programme behind it. Steady fertility (All Seasons or All Seasons N+), regular iron (Iron+), active PGR use (NZLA PGR for canopy tightness), frequent close mowing. Those inputs make MDR look exceptional.

Without that programme, the visual difference over DPR narrows substantially.

## When to use something else

Set-and-forget lawns. The premium seed cost isn't justified without the management.

Heavy shade. Ryegrass shade limitations apply.

Warm subtropical sites. Kikuyu or couch are better suited.

Large areas on a budget. DPR delivers 80% of the visual benefit at significantly lower cost per hectare.

## Common mistakes

Buying MDR and leaving the mowing schedule and fertility unchanged. The cultivar can't do the work alone.

Mowing infrequently. Stripe definition and canopy tightness come from frequent close mowing.

Skipping PGR. NZLA PGR holds the canopy between mows and is part of what makes the dark fine appearance of MDR look its best.
