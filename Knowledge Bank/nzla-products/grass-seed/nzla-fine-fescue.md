# NZLA Fine Fescue

The shade-tolerant fine-leaf fescue blend. Creeping red fescue plus chewings fescue — the answer for shaded, low-input, difficult sites where ryegrass and tall fescue struggle. Very fine leaf texture, strong drought tolerance, and genuinely low maintenance requirements.

## What it is

Fine fescues are the cool-season family adapted to lower light, lower fertility, and drier conditions than ryegrass tolerates. The NZLA blend uses creeping red fescue (slow rhizomatous spread) and chewings fescue (bunch-forming). Together they produce a fine, dense, low-maintenance sward.

The shade tolerance is the standout trait. Fine fescue handles sites with 3 to 4 hours of direct sun where ryegrass would be too thin to be called a lawn. In NZ gardens where trees, fences, and buildings create shade, it fills a genuine gap.

Trade-off: low wear tolerance. Fine fescue doesn't handle the heavy use that ryegrass or tall fescue does. It's for lawns where survival under shade matters more than how many kids and dogs can work it over.

## Critical herbicide restrictions

**DO NOT use NZLA Etho on fine fescue.** Ethofumesate damages or kills fine fescue. The label is for ryegrass and tall fescue only.

**DO NOT use NZLA Gold on fine fescue.** Picloram + triclopyr at label rate damages fine fescue.

NZLA Halo (haloxyfop-P-methyl) is generally safe on fine fescue but has a hard cap of **15 mL per 100m²**. Do not exceed.

BWC at label rate is usually safe but test a small area (1 to 2 m²) and wait 4 to 6 weeks before treating the whole lawn.

If the lawn is mixed fine fescue with ryegrass or tall fescue, avoid the fine-fescue-sensitive products entirely. The ryegrass patches will be fine; the fine fescue patches won't.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 30 to 40 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 20 to 25 g per square metre.

Autumn sowing is strongly preferred (mid-March to mid-May). Spring sowing risks seedling loss through the first summer, especially in dry inland regions. Germination 10 to 14 days at warm soil temperatures.

First mow at 70 to 80 mm. Fine fescue takes longer to recover from low early mowing than ryegrass, so let it establish properly before cutting.

For shaded patches under trees: autumn oversowing at 20 to 25 g per square metre into raked-up existing lawn is the practical approach.

## Maintenance height

25 to 60 mm typical. Fine fescue tolerates a wider mowing height range than its delicate appearance suggests.

## Fertility — less is more

Fine fescue has genuinely low fertility requirements. Over-feeding causes thatch, soft growth, and . A lighter All Seasons programme (2 applications a year rather than 3 to 4) is appropriate. Don't apply it on the same fertility schedule as ryegrass.

## When fine fescue is the right choice

Shaded lawns under trees where ryegrass and tall fescue thin out.

Low-input, low-maintenance sites.

Drought-prone exposed sites where reduced summer water use matters.

Premium fine-textured aesthetic lawns.

## When to use something else

Heavy-traffic family lawns. Fine fescue isn't for the kids-and-dogs area.

Anywhere Etho or Gold will be needed for weed control.

Alongside tall fescue or ryegrass where you want uniform appearance — the textures clash.

## Common mistakes

Spraying Etho on a fine fescue lawn.

Exceeding the 15 mL/100m² Halo cap.

Over-feeding with the same fertiliser programme as ryegrass.

Heavy-traffic use on a species that doesn't suit it.
