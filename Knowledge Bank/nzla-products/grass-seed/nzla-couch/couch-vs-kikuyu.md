# Should I sow couch or kikuyu?

Both warm-season grasses with different characteristics.

NZLA Kikuyu:
- Coarser leaf texture.
- Faster establishment.
- More aggressive growth, more mowing.
- Better cold tolerance (continues growing at 8 °C).
- Stronger wear recovery.
- Aggressively invades garden beds.
- Lower seed cost.

NZLA Couch:
- Finer leaf texture.
- Slower establishment.
- Lower-growing habit, less aggressive mowing schedule.
- Less cold tolerant (longer winter dormancy).
- Better drought tolerance.
- Less invasive growth.
- Higher seed cost.

Decision tree:
- High-use family lawn in upper NI, want wear recovery: kikuyu.
- Premium aesthetic lawn in upper NI, want finer texture: couch.
- Sports field surface (cricket, golf): couch.
- Garden surrounded by beds and paths: couch (less invasive).
- Garden where you don't mind aggressive grass: kikuyu (faster establishment).
- Lower budget on a larger area: kikuyu.

For both species, viability is largely limited to the upper North Island in NZ. In cooler regions (southern North Island, South Island), neither couch nor kikuyu is the right choice, cool-season grasses (ryegrass, tall fescue, fine fescue) work better.

In the right region, both grasses give a strong warm-season lawn with good drought tolerance. The choice between them depends on aesthetic preference and management style more than performance.
