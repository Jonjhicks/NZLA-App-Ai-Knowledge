# NZLA Couch (Bermudagrass)

The premium warm-season alternative to kikuyu. Cynodon dactylon — bermudagrass internationally, couch in NZ. Fine-textured, high-density warm-season turf with the cold-tolerance selected for NZ conditions, where standard bermudas would struggle in cooler weather.

## What it is

Couch is the warm-season grass family used in fine-cut turf globally — golf greens, tees and fairways in subtropical climates, premium sports fields, and increasingly home lawns in NZ's warmer regions. The leaf is much finer than kikuyu, the canopy density is high, and the visual finish is closer to a premium cool-season lawn than to standard warm-season grasses.

NZLA Couch is selected for cold tolerance beyond standard bermuda, which extends its viable range further south than typical varieties. It's still a warm-season grass and not suited to heavy-frost regions, but it handles the northern South Island and most of the North Island fine.

## Compared to kikuyu

Both are warm-season, both spread via stolons and rhizomes, both like full sun. The differences are in texture and cold response.

Kikuyu is coarser-leafed, more aggressive lateral spread, and recently selected for better cold tolerance. Couch is finer-leafed, denser canopy, more aesthetic but slightly less cold-hardy. Couch handles closer mowing.

For premium aesthetic in warm climates, couch wins. For maximum cold tolerance in warm-season turf, NZLA Kikuyu has the edge.

## Maintenance height

10 to 30 mm typical. Couch responds well to close mowing, that's what makes it the global choice for fine-cut warm-season turf. Most home users hold 15 to 25 mm.

## When to use

Premium warm-season lawns where appearance matters.

Sports fields and high-performance turf in warm-climate regions.

Replacement of struggling kikuyu where finer aesthetic is wanted.

## When not to use

Heavy frost regions. Cold tolerance is improved over standard bermuda but it's still a warm-season grass.

South Island except possibly the warmest northern fringes.

Heavy shade. Full-sun species.

Mixed with cool-season grasses. Couch will spread aggressively and dominate.

## Common mistakes

Trying to grow it in unsuitable cold climates. Even cold-tolerant selections have limits.

Mowing too high. Couch at 50 mm looks shaggy and loses density. Stay tight.

Letting it invade cool-season lawns. Once established, couch is hard to control in a mixed sward.

Underwatering during establishment. Warm-season germination needs consistent moisture even though the mature lawn is drought tolerant.
