# When should I sow NZLA Couch?

Summer is the right window. Soil temps 20 to 28 °C. November to February in upper North Island, December to January elsewhere where couch is viable.

Why warm soil:
- Couch is a warm-season grass with strict temperature requirements.
- Optimal germination is at 25 to 28 °C soil temp.
- Cool soil delays germination significantly, weakens seedlings, increases failure risk.

Process:
1. Soil prep: cultivate, level, remove debris.
2. Apply NZLA Starter at the prep stage.
3. Sow couch at 1 to 2 g per square metre (similar to kikuyu, couch seed is small and potent).
4. Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
5. Germination in 10 to 21 days at optimal temps.
6. First mow at 30 to 50 mm leaf height.

The low seed rate reflects that couch seed is small and dense. Don't overseed thinking heavier is better.

Don't sow:
- Outside the November to February window (soil too cold).
- In regions where couch isn't viable (most of South Island, southern North Island).
- During cool wet spells where soil temps drop below 18 °C.
- Without irrigation - couch needs consistent moisture for the first 4 to 6 weeks of establishment.

Once established, couch develops drought tolerance through deep roots and is largely self-sufficient through summer dry spells. The dormant winter is the trade-off.

Mowing through the growing season is required regularly (weekly through peak summer). Couch grows hard and seedheads quickly if not mown often enough. NZLA PGR (trinexapac) is useful for cutting mowing frequency on managed couch lawns.
