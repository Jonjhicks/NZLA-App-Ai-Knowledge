# What is NZLA Couch?

A cold-tolerant couch (bermuda grass) variety. Cynodon dactylon, selected for better quality and cold tolerance than standard bermuda options.

What you get:
- Warm-season grass behaviour (warm season active, winter dormant).
- Better cold tolerance than typical bermuda (handles colder NZ winters than standard varieties).
- Fine leaf texture (finer than kikuyu, comparable to fine-textured warm-season options).
- High wear tolerance.
- Strong drought tolerance once established.
- Good colour through the warm months.
- Lower-growing habit than kikuyu (less mowing pressure through summer).

Where it fits:
- Sports field surfaces in warm regions (golf course tees, fairways, cricket grounds).
- Premium warm-season home lawns where finer texture than kikuyu matters.
- Coastal regions where drought and salt tolerance both matter.
- Lawns where kikuyu would be too coarse or too invasive.

Trade-offs:
- Goes dormant in winter (brown lawn through cold months).
- Slower establishment than kikuyu.
- Requires more careful management to look its best.
- Not as wear-recoverable as kikuyu (recovers but more slowly).

Soil temps 20 to 28 °C for germination. Sowing window is summer (November to February in upper North Island).

NZLA's cold-tolerant variety pushes the viable region for couch further south than standard bermuda, but it's still primarily an upper North Island option in NZ. South Island couch lawns are rare and require very sheltered northern aspects.
