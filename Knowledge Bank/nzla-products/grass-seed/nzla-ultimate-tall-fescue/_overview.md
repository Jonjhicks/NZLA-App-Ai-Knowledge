# NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue (UTF)

The premium tall fescue blend. Dark green, fine-textured, dense. Combines the aesthetic of a top-tier turf with tall fescue's underlying toughness — drought tolerance from a deep root system, heat tolerance, moderate shade tolerance, wear handling.

## What it is

UTF uses selected premium tall fescue cultivars chosen for colour, texture and density. The appearance is comparable to a quality ryegrass lawn but with the tall fescue's natural drought and shade tolerance behind it, which ryegrass can't match.

It's a bunch-forming grass (unlike Elfin Tall Fescue, which is the self-spreading sibling), so recovery from heavy wear damage is slower. The trade-off is a denser, more uniform appearance from a non-rhizomatous variety.

## Establishment

Sowing rate for new lawns: 3 to 5 kg per 100m².

Oversowing rate: 2.5 kg per 100m².

Tall fescue establishment is slower than ryegrass. Allow 10 to 14 days for germination at warm soil temperatures, longer in cool. Irrigation through the establishment window is essential.

NZLA Starter at soil prep, consistent moisture, first mow at 60 to 80 mm seedling height.

## Maintenance height

50 to 150 mm. Tall fescue handles a wider mowing height range than ryegrass thanks to the deep root system feeding the plant from below the canopy.

Most home users hold 50 to 70 mm. Sports turf or feature lawns can go closer with active management.

## When to use

Drought-prone sites where you want premium aesthetic and summer green retention.

Sites with partial shade (4 to 6 hours of direct sun) where ryegrass would thin.

Premium home lawns where the owner is looking for tall fescue's tolerance profile with a more premium look than standard.

Hot inland sites where ryegrass struggles in mid-summer.

## When not to use

Heavy wear sites where active self-repair matters more than aesthetic. Elfin Tall Fescue is the spreading alternative.

Cold high-frost sites where the species struggles.

Wet, poorly drained ground.

Where ultimate dark stripe-definition aesthetic is the priority. MDR ryegrass is the colour benchmark.

## Common mistakes

Letting the seedling lawn dry out during slow establishment.

Mowing too short. Tall fescue scalped at 15 to 25 mm looks rough and stresses the plant.

Expecting it to spread and repair like Elfin. UTF is bunch-forming, oversow wear damage rather than expecting active recovery.

Treating it like ryegrass on the maintenance schedule. Tall fescue needs less frequent mowing and copes with periods of stress better but also doesn't recover from damage as quickly.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter at soil prep, Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks.

All Seasons as the maintenance base, with Iron+ for colour.

Aerate or Penetrate on heavy soils, tall fescue's deep root system rewards a well-structured profile.
