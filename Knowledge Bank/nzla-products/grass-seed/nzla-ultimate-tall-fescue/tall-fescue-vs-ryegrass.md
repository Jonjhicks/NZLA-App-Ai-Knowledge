# Should I sow tall fescue or ryegrass?

Different species with different strengths.

Ryegrass:
- Fast establishment (7 to 14 days germination).
- Aggressive tillering, density develops quickly.
- Better cold weather activity (winter active).
- Less drought tolerant.
- Less heat tolerant.

Tall fescue:
- Slower establishment (10 to 21 days germination).
- Develops density more slowly.
- Strong drought tolerance (deep roots).
- Strong heat tolerance.
- Slower to recover from wear damage.
- Coarser leaf texture (depending on cultivar).

Decision tree:
- Cool moist region (Wellington, lower South Island), wear tolerance the priority: ryegrass.
- Hot dry summer region (Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, inland Otago), drought tolerance the priority: tall fescue.
- Heavy summer use with kids and dogs: ryegrass (better wear recovery).
- Lower-use lawn with summer dry stress: tall fescue (better through dry conditions).
- Want both characteristics: blend (50/50 ryegrass and tall fescue at sowing).

For most NZ home lawns, a ryegrass-dominated blend with some tall fescue mixed in (60/40 ryegrass/tall fescue) covers both bases reasonably well. Pure tall fescue lawns make sense in drier regions, pure ryegrass lawns in cooler wetter regions.

Don't mix fine fescue with tall fescue at sowing (different species behaviour, the lawn ends up patchy). Each works better as a primary species or in a blend with similar species.
