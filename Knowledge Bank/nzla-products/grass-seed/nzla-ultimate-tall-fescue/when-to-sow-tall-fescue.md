# When should I sow tall fescue?

Autumn is the right window. Soil temps 14 to 22 °C, mid-March to early May depending on region. Tall fescue prefers slightly warmer soil for germination than ryegrass.

Why autumn over spring:
- Lawn establishes through cool moist conditions.
- Tall fescue's slow establishment doesn't have to compete with summer heat.
- Roots develop deep through winter, setting up for the following summer's drought tolerance.
- Spring sowing puts the establishing lawn straight into summer, which tall fescue handles better than ryegrass but still struggles with as a seedling.

Process:
1. Soil prep: cultivate, level, remove debris.
2. Apply NZLA Starter granular at the prep stage, incorporate.
3. Sow tall fescue at 35 to 45 g per square metre (heavier than ryegrass because of slower tillering).
4. Light roll, water lightly and frequently until germination.
5. First mow at 80 to 100 mm leaf height (tall fescue takes longer to recover from low first mow than ryegrass).

For overseeding existing lawn:
- Autumn timing.
- Mow short, scarify or rake heavily.
- Sow at 25 to 30 g per square metre.
- Apply Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 day intervals through establishment phase.

Tall fescue takes longer than ryegrass to look finished. Plan for 8 to 12 weeks from sowing to "ready for normal use" rather than the 6 to 8 weeks typical of ryegrass.

Don't sow tall fescue in mid-summer (heat stress kills seedlings) or mid-winter (soil too cold for reliable germination).
