# NZLA Couch (Bermudagrass)

The premium warm-season alternative to kikuyu. Cynodon dactylon — bermudagrass internationally, couch in NZ. Fine-textured, high-density, warm-season turf. The selected variety has better cold tolerance than standard bermuda, extending its viable range further into NZ conditions.

## What it is

Couch is the warm-season grass family used in fine-cut turf globally. Golf courses, sports fields, premium home lawns across subtropical climates. The leaf is much finer than kikuyu, the canopy density is high, and the appearance is closer to a premium cool-season lawn than to typical warm-season grasses.

The cold-tolerance selection in NZLA Couch pushes the viable region further south than standard bermuda, but it's still a warm-season grass. Upper North Island is its territory. South Island is generally not couch territory.

## Couch vs Kikuyu

Both warm-season, both spread via stolons and rhizomes, both need full sun.

Kikuyu: coarser leaf, faster establishment, more aggressive lateral spread and mowing demand, better cold tolerance, more invasive. Lower cost.

Couch: finer leaf, slower establishment, lower-growing habit, finer aesthetic, slightly less cold-hardy, longer winter dormancy, less invasive. Higher cost.

For premium aesthetic in warm climates: couch. For maximum cold tolerance and fast establishment: kikuyu. For garden beds surrounded by invasive risk: couch is less aggressive at the edges.

## Sowing rate and timing

Sow at 1 to 2 g per square metre (small dense seed, lower rate than cool-season grasses).

Summer is the only window. Soil temps 20 to 28°C. November to February in upper North Island, late November to January in cooler regions where couch is still viable.

Consistent irrigation-general through establishment is essential. Full coverage in warmer conditions takes approximately 8 weeks from sowing.

## Maintenance height

10 to 30 mm typical. Most home users hold 15 to 25 mm.

Couch handles close mowing well — that's what makes it the global choice for fine-cut warm-season turf. Letting it grow to 50mm looks shaggy.

Mowing frequency through peak summer: weekly or more. NZLA PGR (trinexapac) helps manage the mowing frequency.

## When to use

Premium warm-season lawns where appearance matters.

Sports fields and high-performance turf in warm-climate regions (golf tees, cricket).

Replacement of coarser warm-season turf where finer aesthetic is wanted.

## When not to use

Heavy frost regions. Cold-tolerant but still warm-season — extended frost kills it.

South Island except possibly warmest northern fringes.

Heavy shade. Full-sun species.

Mixed into cool-season lawns. Couch spreads and dominates.

## Common mistakes

Trying to establish in unsuitable cool climates.

Mowing too high. Couch at 50mm looks rough. Keep it tight.

Underwatering during establishment. The mature lawn is drought-tolerant; the seedlings aren't.

Letting it invade garden beds without regular edge management.

## Establishment programme

NZLA Starter at soil prep.

Charger through cooler months to hold colour as growth slows in autumn.

All Seasons N+ in active growing season.

Sharp blades and frequent mowing through active growth.
