# NZLA Kikuyu

Raw kikuyu seed. Not a 5% blend with 95% ryegrass carrier, which is what most retail "kikuyu" products are. Pennisetum clandestinum at high content, for genuine kikuyu establishment in the upper North Island.

## Why raw seed matters

Most products marketed as kikuyu are blended, often around 5% kikuyu with the rest ryegrass carrier. The ryegrass germinates fast and produces initial cover while the kikuyu slowly establishes underneath. The result is a lawn that starts as mostly ryegrass and shifts toward kikuyu over a season or two — if the conditions suit.

NZLA Kikuyu is the actual seed. Lower total weight per area (kikuyu seed is potent and small) and genuine kikuyu from the start.

## What kikuyu is

The dominant warm-season turf grass in the upper North Island. Aggressive lateral spread via both stolons and rhizomes. Very high wear tolerance — recovers from damage faster than any other species in the NZLA range. Deep root system for drought. Dense canopy that outcompetes most .

The selected variety continues growing at temperatures as low as 8°C, lower than standard kikuyu and well below couch. Better winter colour retention than most warm-season grasses in the NZ market.

## Sowing rates and timing

Rate: 1 to 2 g per square metre. That's the right rate for raw kikuyu seed. Don't go heavier.

Timing: late spring through early summer. Soil temps 18 to 25°C. October to December in upper North Island.

Establishment under optimal conditions: full coverage in approximately 8 weeks.

## Maintenance

Mowing height: 25 to 50 mm typical.

Mowing frequency: every 4 to 7 days at peak growth (summer). Weekly or more through active months. NZLA PGR (trinexapac) cuts mowing frequency significantly and is worth running on kikuyu through the growing season.

Fertiliser: All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular through spring, summer, and autumn. Charger as a liquid maintenance feed through cooler months to hold colour as growth slows.

Edging: critical. Kikuyu invades garden beds, paths, and driveways aggressively. Regular edging is not optional if you want contained lawn edges.

Herbicides safe on kikuyu: BWC at label rate, Blockade (prodiamine) — NZ label includes warm-season turf.

## When kikuyu is the right choice

Upper North Island (Waikato and north) where the climate suits.

High-wear sites in warm climates.

Drought-prone warm environments where cool-season grasses would brown out.

Sandy soils or low-irrigation-general situations where cool-season turf struggles.

## When to use something else

South Island. Wrong climate.

Heavy shade. Full-sun species, won't perform in shade.

Premium aesthetic lawns where fine texture matters. Kikuyu is coarser than ryegrass or fescue.

Mixed cool-season lawns you want to keep. Kikuyu wins.

Anywhere you'd struggle with aggressive lateral spread. Once established, kikuyu is difficult to remove.

## Common mistakes

Buying blended retail "kikuyu" expecting pure kikuyu performance.

Sowing into cold soil below 15°C.

Sowing in the South Island or cooler North Island regions.

Letting it establish without edge management planning.
