# NZLA Dark Perennial Ryegrass (DPR)

The step up from standard NZLA Perennial Rye. Darker colour, denser growth, finer texture, and stronger performance through warmer months. The practical choice for home lawn owners who want a noticeably dark, good-looking lawn without paying premium MDR pricing.

## What it is

Same species as standard ryegrass (Lolium perenne) but a selected cultivar bred for deeper green appearance, finer leaf, and better summer performance. The improvement in colour over standard rye is visible side by side without doing anything else differently.

The warm-month performance is the other practical difference. Standard ryegrass varieties slow and lose colour as summer temperatures rise. DPR holds its colour and growth rate through summer better, which extends the visual peak through the year.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: 25 to 35 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 15 to 20 g per square metre.

Autumn preferred (mid-March to early May). Spring works but is less ideal — higher weed pressure and heat establishment stress. Standard programme: NZLA Starter at soil prep, water consistently through germination (7 to 14 days), first mow at 60 to 80 mm.

## Maintenance height

12 mm minimum mowing height. Most home users hold 25 to 50 mm. The finer texture handles closer mowing better than standard rye.

## DPR vs MDR

MDR is darker, finer, and higher density at a higher cost. Premium showcase lawns.

DPR delivers 80% of the colour benefit of MDR at significantly lower cost. Better value at scale or where the absolute premium look isn't the goal.

Blending both (30 to 50% MDR in a DPR base) is a reasonable middle ground for home lawns.

## Overseeding for colour improvement

One of DPR's most common uses is progressive colour improvement. Oversow existing standard ryegrass each autumn at 15 to 20 g per square metre. After 3 to 4 seasons, the lawn shifts noticeably toward DPR's darker appearance.

Process: mow short, scarify or rake heavily, sow, roll or rake in, water lightly until germination, apply Liquid Starter at 7 to 10 days. Repeat annually.

## When DPR is the right choice

Home lawns wanting a noticeably darker lawn without premium seed cost.

Overseeding existing standard rye for colour improvement.

New lawn establishment at mid-range budget.

Upper North Island lawns where summer performance matters.

## Common mistakes

Sowing too thin. The cultivar's potential requires proper establishment density.

Treating it as low-maintenance. Premium cultivars return the investment when fertility, mowing, and water management are there.

Mowing too short before establishment. Let seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm before the first cut.

## Establishment programme

NZLA Starter at soil prep. Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks. All Seasons as the maintenance base. Iron+ periodically for colour top-up. NZLA PGR through active growing season to tighten the canopy.
