# Elfin Tall Fescue (ETF)

The self-repairing tall fescue. Elfin produces rhizomes (underground horizontal stems) that spread laterally and fill in bare patches — something standard bunch-type tall fescue can't do. Drought tolerance, heat tolerance, shade tolerance, and active self-repair in one cultivar.

## What makes it different

Standard tall fescue is bunch-forming. Each plant stays where it is. When wear damage takes out a patch, the only recovery is adjacent plants growing into the gap. Slow.

Elfin's rhizomes spread underground, putting down new plants and filling the damage. The lawn repairs itself between uses.

The other tall fescue traits are all there: very deep root system for drought tolerance, reasonable shade tolerance (better than ryegrass), heat handling, strong wear tolerance from the dense sward. Dark green, reasonably fine texture for a tall fescue.

## Sowing rates and timing

New lawn: refer to label, typically 35 to 45 g per square metre.

Oversowing: 25 to 30 g per square metre.

Autumn is the right establishment window (mid-March to early May). Tall fescue establishes more slowly than ryegrass — 10 to 14 days germination at warm soil temperatures. The slow start means moisture management is critical. Dry-out at day 7 kills seedlings before they're established. Irrigation through the establishment window is essential.

First mow once seedlings reach 60 to 80 mm.

## Maintenance height

30 to 70 mm typical. The deep root system feeds the plant from well below the canopy, so a slightly taller mowing height suits it.

## When ETF is the right choice

Drought-prone sites where summer green retention matters.

Sites with partial shade (4 to 6 hours direct sun) where ryegrass thins out.

Wear-prone sites where the self-repair from rhizomes adds practical value.

Sand-based soils or low-irrigation-general situations.

## When to use something else

Heavy wear heavy-use sites with kids and dogs: the self-repair helps but wear recovery is still slower than RSR Ryegrass.

Cold high-frost regions: tall fescue handles most of the South Island fine but check for your region.

Wet, poorly drained ground: tall fescue prefers reasonable drainage-and-soil-moisture.

Where rapid establishment is the priority: ryegrass establishes 30 to 50% faster.

## ETF vs Ultimate Tall Fescue

Elfin: rhizomatous, self-repairing, reasonably fine texture. Better for wear recovery.

UTF: premium cultivar blend, very dark, finest texture, non-rhizomatous. Better for visual quality.

For high-use sites: ETF. For showcase home lawns: UTF. Blend both for combined self-repair and premium appearance.

## Establishment programme

NZLA Starter granular at soil prep.

Liquid Starter at 4 to 5 weeks post-germination.

All Seasons as the maintenance base from 8 to 12 weeks.

Penetrate or Aerate on heavy soils — tall fescue's deep roots need access to the profile.

## Common mistakes

Letting seedlings dry out. Slow germination plus dry surface equals failed establishment.

Mowing too short. Tall fescue at 15 to 20 mm looks rough and stresses the plant.

Expecting instant wear recovery from rhizomes. The rhizome behaviour develops over 2 to 3 seasons as the lawn matures.
