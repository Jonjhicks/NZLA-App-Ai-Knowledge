# When should I apply NZLA All Seasons?

Three to four times a year for a typical NZ home lawn. Apply at the start of each main season:

- Early spring (September). Lawn waking up, push the first burst of growth.
- Late spring (November). Carry the lawn through into summer with good density.
- Autumn (March to April). The single most useful fertiliser of the year for a cool-season lawn. Roots are growing through autumn and into winter, and feeding now sets up the lawn for the following spring.
- Late autumn (May, optional). Light application carries the lawn through winter without pushing soft growth.

Skip the height of summer in regions where the lawn is heat-stressed and not actively taking up nutrient. February in the upper North Island, for example, isn't a great time to apply, the lawn just sits on top of the granules.

Apply to a well-watered lawn, water in straight after with 5 to 10 mm of irrigation or rainfall. The 40% immediately available portion needs to dissolve in to start working, the 60% slow-release portion needs to be in contact with moist soil to begin its release.

40 to 50 g per square metre is the standard rate. Even spreading matters, hand-broadcasting on a small lawn or a calibrated spreader on a larger area.
