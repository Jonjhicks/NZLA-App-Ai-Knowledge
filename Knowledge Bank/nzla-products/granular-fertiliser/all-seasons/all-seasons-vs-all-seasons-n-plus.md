# Should I use All Seasons or All Seasons N+?

All Seasons (21-1-16 with 5% iron) is the balanced year-round option. Suits a typical home lawn through the standard application windows. Adequate nitrogen for growth, decent potassium for stress tolerance and root health, iron for colour.

All Seasons N+ (higher nitrogen, lower potassium) is the option when you want more growth and colour push. Useful when:
- Recovery from disease, drought stress, renovation
- Spring push to thicken up a lawn after winter
- Lawn that's been neglected and needs to bulk up fast

Decision tree:
- General year-round programme on a maintained lawn: All Seasons.
- One-off recovery or push application: All Seasons N+.
- Pre-stress hardening (going into summer or winter): All Seasons (potassium content helps stress tolerance).
- Mid-renovation feed: All Seasons N+ for the push, then back to All Seasons for the maintenance cycle.

You can run a programme that's All Seasons for the bulk of applications with one or two All Seasons N+ swap-ins where the lawn needs it. Most home lawns won't need to over-think this, All Seasons covers the standard year-round needs and N+ comes in when something specific calls for it.
