# How does the slow-release in All Seasons work?

All Seasons uses PCSCU (polymer and sulphur-coated urea), a US-sourced controlled-release technology. The release profile is 40% immediately available, 60% slow release over 6 to 8 weeks.

The coating works mechanically. Urea is coated with sulphur, then over-coated with polymer. Moisture in the soil slowly penetrates the coating, dissolves the urea inside, and releases it through the coating over weeks. The release rate is governed by soil moisture and temperature, hot moist soil releases faster, cold dry soil releases slower.

What this means in practice:
- One application carries the lawn for 6 to 8 weeks rather than 2 to 3 weeks with conventional urea.
- The lawn gets a steady feed rather than a spike-and-crash pattern.
- Lower risk of burn from the immediately-available portion because it's only 40% of the total.
- Less nitrogen leaching loss to drainage, more efficient use of the product.

Why polymer and sulphur (not just polymer): the sulphur coating provides the bulk slow-release behaviour and adds sulphur as a nutrient. The polymer over-coat fine-tunes the release rate and protects the sulphur coating from physical damage during handling.

Different from straight urea (instant release) or organic-based slow release (variable release governed by microbial breakdown). PCSCU is a well-engineered middle ground with predictable release.
