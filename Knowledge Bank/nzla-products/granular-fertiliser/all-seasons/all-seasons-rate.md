# What's the application rate for All Seasons?

2 kg per 100 m² (20 g per square metre). One 20 kg bag covers 1000 m². The bag size suits typical NZ home lawn applications well.

A few practical points:
- Use a spreader for anything over about 80 to 100 m². Hand broadcasting at 20 g per m² is tough to do evenly, you'll get strips of dark green and strips of pale green a few weeks later.
- Calibrate your spreader. Different brands and different settings deliver different rates. The marking on the spreader is a starting point, not a guarantee.
- Walk a steady pace and split the rate (half going north-south, half going east-west). Cross-pattern spreading evens out any spreader unevenness.
- Water in straight after application. 5 to 10 mm of irrigation or rainfall within 24 hours starts the chemistry working.

Don't apply at higher than label rate. Slow-release products are designed for the rate they release at, doubling the rate doesn't give you double the result, you just waste fertiliser and risk burn if the slow-release coating fails.

For a lighter feed (recovery from a stress, light maintenance through summer), apply at half rate (1 kg per 100 m²). This still gives reasonable response without overdoing it.
