# NZLA All Seasons

The flagship NZLA granular base feed. Balanced NPK at 21-1-16 with 5% iron, delivered through PCSCU (polymer and sulphur-coated urea) for 6 to 8 weeks of steady feeding without the spikes and troughs you get from cheap fertiliser.

## What it is

All Seasons is the professional-grade base fertiliser the NZLA programme is built around. The blend is 40% immediately available nitrogen for a visible green-up within days, and 60% slow-release for sustained feeding through the following 6 to 8 weeks. The coating technology is US-sourced PCSCU, which gives reliable release rather than the inconsistent breakdown you get from low-grade coatings.

## The numbers

21% nitrogen, 1% phosphorus, 16% potassium, with 5% iron.

The high potassium (close to the nitrogen level) is the part that makes this work across the seasons. Potassium drives plant strength, cold tolerance and disease resistance, so the lawn isn't just growing on nitrogen, it's hardening up as well. The iron lifts colour without forcing extra growth.

Rate is 2 kg per 100m².

## When to use

Across the whole growing season. Apply every 6 to 8 weeks for steady feeding. The balanced profile means it works as well in spring (where you want growth) as it does heading into autumn and winter (where you want strength and colour without forcing soft growth into disease pressure).

Apply to moist soil and water in lightly to activate the coating.

## How it sits next to the others

All Seasons is the steady base. All Seasons N+ is the higher-N push and recovery option (32% N with mostly fast-release). All Seasons is the value-priced alternative to All Seasons with a very similar NPK profile but at a sharper price point. Mini Prill is the high-N push option in a smaller prill for dense canopy.

## When not to use

Don't apply to brand new seedings until after the first mow. Starter is the call for that stage.

Don't apply to dry ground without watering in. The coating needs moisture to start releasing.

Don't use it as a quick rescue feed. The 60% slow-release fraction means most of the feed is metered over weeks. If you need fast recovery, All Seasons N+ or a foliar (Liquid Boost) is the better tool.

## Common mistakes

Skipping the water-in. Without moisture the coating doesn't activate.

Confusing slow response with the product not working. Most users see green by day 5 to 7, but the steady feed continues for 6 to 8 weeks. If you reapply too early you'll overdo it.

Applying with rain forecast within 48 hours. Heavy rain on freshly applied granular can wash prills off-target and waste product.

## Best paired with

Foliar Iron+ or Charger between applications for additional colour without more nitrogen.

NZLA Starter at establishment, then transition to All Seasons after the first mow.

Liquid N or Liquid Boost as foliar top-ups during heavy growth periods.
