# Is All Seasons N+ the right choice for lawn recovery?

Often yes, with some caveats.

N+ pushes the lawn into recovery growth fast because the extra nitrogen drives leaf production. Useful after renovation (cores filling in), after disease damage (lawn growing past the dead patches), after drought stress (greening back up).

When to use N+ for recovery:
- Renovation recovery (post-scarify, post-aeration, post-seed): yes, once new seedlings are 4 to 6 weeks old.
- Disease recovery (after the disease is controlled): yes, push the lawn through.
- Drought recovery: water first to restore hydration, then N+ to push growth.
- Wear and tear recovery (high-traffic patches, dog spots): yes, combined with overseeding if patches are bare.

When N+ isn't the right tool:
- Active disease pressure. Don't push soft growth into a disease window.
- Going into winter. Soft growth in cold conditions invites fusarium.
- Recovery from over-fertilising or fertiliser burn. Lawn needs water, not more N.

For faster recovery on smaller patches, Liquid Boost (NZLA's liquid feed) gives an even faster response because foliar uptake of nitrogen is quicker than granular release. Combine N+ broadcast across the recovery area with Liquid Boost as a foliar spray for accelerated thickening.

One N+ application followed by 2 to 3 Liquid Boost applications over the following 6 weeks is a strong recovery programme.
