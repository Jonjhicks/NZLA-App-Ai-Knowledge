# When should I apply All Seasons N+?

When you want a strong growth response, typically:

- Spring (October to November). Push thickening up after winter, particularly on lawns that came out winter thin or stressed.
- Post-renovation. After scarifying, dethatching, core aeration or seeding once new seedlings are established. The extra nitrogen accelerates fill-in.
- Recovery feeds. After disease damage, drought stress, pest damage. Push the lawn into recovery growth.
- Late summer (March). On warm-season lawns or lawns that have been hammered through summer, a strong autumn push helps the lawn bulk up before the cooler months.

Not the right time for N+:
- Late autumn into winter. Pushing soft growth into cold conditions invites disease (fusarium patch in particular).
- Height of summer in stressed regions. The lawn isn't taking it up, you're wasting product.
- Drought-stressed lawn. Apply water first, restore lawn health, then feed.
- Pre-renovation. Lawn is going to be torn up anyway, save the fertiliser for the recovery side.

For a typical home lawn, one or two N+ applications a year fits well within an All Seasons base programme. The N+ goes in where you need the growth push, All Seasons covers the rest.
