# NZLA All Seasons N+

The push and recovery version of All Seasons. NPK 32-1.3-8.3 with 2% iron. Mostly fast-release nitrogen for a rapid green-up, with a slow-release fraction holding the feed for 6 to 8 weeks.

## What it is

Where All Seasons is the steady balanced base, All Seasons N+ is the tool for when you actually want the lawn to push hard, growing in, recovering from wear, or hitting peak colour through spring and early summer.

The blend is 70% fast-release nitrogen for quick visible response (days, not weeks) and 30% slow-release so the feed isn't completely gone after the initial flush. PCSCU coating on the slow fraction, same supplier and technology as standard All Seasons.

## The numbers

32% nitrogen, 1.3% phosphorus, 8.3% potassium, with 2% iron.

The N to K ratio is much heavier on nitrogen than standard All Seasons (3.8:1 vs 1.3:1). So this is a growth-driving feed, not a balanced hardening feed.

Rate is 2 kg per 100m².

## When to use

Spring green-up and early growth push.

Recovery from wear, renovation, or stress damage (post-scarifying, post-renovation, kid and dog wear).

Filling in a lawn that's gone hungry over winter and needs to fire up fast.

Mid-summer push if the lawn's looking tired.

Apply to moist soil, water in well after application to move the available nitrogen down into the root zone and prevent leaf scorch.

## How it sits next to the others

All Seasons (21-1-16) is the steady balanced base for year-round feeding. All Seasons N+ is the push and recovery alternative when you want the lawn going hard. Most programmes use All Seasons as the base and bring N+ in at 2 to 3 strategic points per year (spring push, post-renovation, summer recovery).

Mini Prill (28-0-12) does a similar push job in a smaller prill for dense canopy.

## When not to use

Heading into winter. The high fast-release nitrogen will force soft growth into the cold and damp, which is exactly what feeds Fusarium and the cool-season patch diseases. Switch to All Seasons or All Seasons N+ for the cool months.

On stressed or heat-damaged turf. Adding a hit of fast-release N to a struggling lawn pushes it harder when it can't recover.

On new seedings before the first mow. Starter is the establishment fertiliser.

## Common mistakes

Using it as a year-round base. N+ is a tool for specific moments, not a constant feed. Sitting on it month after month will overdo the nitrogen and leave the lawn dependent on the next hit.

Applying without watering in. The fast-release component can scorch the leaf if it sits on damp foliage.

Reaching for it in autumn or winter. Wrong tool for the season.

Combining it with a foliar high-N feed (Liquid Boost) in the same window. You'll overdo it and end up with soft, disease-prone growth.

## Best paired with

All Seasons (the steady base) underneath, with N+ brought in for the push moments.

Iron+ as a colour follow-up if you want to extend the green appearance after the N+ flush settles.
