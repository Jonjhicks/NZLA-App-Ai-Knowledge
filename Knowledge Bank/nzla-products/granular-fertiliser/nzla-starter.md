# NZLA Starter

The new lawn fertiliser. 19-10-4 NPK with a trace element pack (Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu), slow-release nitrogen, and higher phosphorus than any other product in the NZLA range. Designed to go into the soil at establishment, before the seed.

## Why establishment needs a different fertiliser

New turf has different requirements than established turf. A germinating seedling needs phosphorus for root development, micronutrients for early-stage health, and steady (not aggressive) nitrogen that won't burn fragile roots. Standard granulars like All Seasons have the wrong P level, no trace elements, and nitrogen release designed for an established plant's root system.

NZLA Starter addresses that. The 10% phosphorus drives early root development — P is the nutrient that builds root mass in the establishment window. The trace element pack covers deficiencies that often hold seedlings back in new or disturbed soil. The slow-release nitrogen (up to 3 months duration) feeds the lawn through the entire establishment period without spiking.

## Rate and application

1.5 kg per 100m². Apply to prepared soil, rake or roll into the top 25 to 50 mm, then sow seed or lay turf on top.

**Apply before sowing, not after.** Phosphorus doesn't move much in soil. It needs to be in the zone where roots will grow, not sitting on the surface hoping roots find it.

For seeding-and-over-seeding or patch repair where full soil prep isn't possible: top-dress at 1.5 kg/100m² and rake in lightly before sowing. Less effective than full incorporation but still better than leaving it out.

Pack sizes: 4 kg covers around 270m², 7.5 kg covers around 500m².

## How long it lasts

Up to 3 months. One application at establishment carries the lawn through germination, early establishment, and first mows. Transition to All Seasons at 8 to 12 weeks post-sowing once the lawn is established and growing regularly.

## Starter vs Liquid Starter

Both, in sequence — they're designed to work together.

NZLA Starter (granular) goes in before sowing: foundation P and micronutrients in the soil profile where roots will grow.

Liquid Starter (8-10-5 plus kelp, foliar/soil drench) applies at and after sowing: faster-acting foliar push for seedling growth.

Standard establishment programme: granular Starter at soil prep, seed, then Liquid Starter applications starting at 7 to 10 days after germination, every week or so for the first 4 to 6 weeks. The combination gives the strongest possible start — the granular covers the soil profile, the liquid lifts the above-ground growth.

If budget only allows one: granular Starter is more important for root development. Liquid Starter accelerates the result on top of that.

## When not to use on established lawns

High phosphorus isn't needed once a root system is established, and over-applying P on established turf can lock up iron and other micronutrients. Don't use Starter as a regular maintenance feed — that's what All Seasons is for. Once the lawn has been mown three times and is clearly established, transition to the standard maintenance programme.

## Common mistakes

Applying after sowing the seed. The phosphorus needs to be in the soil before the roots get there.

Skipping the rake-in step. Surface P is far less available to the new root system than incorporated P.

Continuing to apply Starter every 6 to 8 weeks. One application at establishment is the programme — then switch to All Seasons.

Using it as a substitute for soil preparation. No fertiliser makes up for poor drainage-and-soil-moisture, wrong pH, or inadequate weed control at establishment.
