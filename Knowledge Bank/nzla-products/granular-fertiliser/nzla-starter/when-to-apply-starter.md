# When should I apply NZLA Starter?

Before you sow the seed or lay the turf. NZLA Starter is a new lawn fertiliser, designed to go into the prepared soil at the establishment stage.

Process:
1. Prepare the soil (clear, level, rake out for sowing or laying).
2. Apply NZLA Starter at the label rate, rake or roll it into the top 25 to 50 mm of soil.
3. Sow seed or lay turf on top.
4. Water as normal for establishment.

Why before sowing rather than after: the phosphorus in Starter drives early root development, and phosphorus doesn't move much in soil. Putting it in the root zone before the roots get there is far more effective than top-dressing it later when the roots have to find it.

For overseeded lawns or thin patch repair where you're not preparing fresh soil, top-dress Starter onto the area and rake in lightly before sowing. Less effective than full incorporation but still better than not using it.

One application at establishment is typically all the lawn needs. Switch to All Seasons or All Seasons N+ for the maintenance feeds once the lawn is 6 to 8 weeks old and growing well.
