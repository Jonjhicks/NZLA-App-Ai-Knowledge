# NZLA Starter

The fertiliser for new lawns. Higher phosphorus than the standard granulars to drive root development, plus trace elements (Mn, Zn, Cu, Fe) for seedling health. Designed to go down at soil preparation, before the seed.

## What it is

Establishment is a completely different fertility job to maintenance. A new lawn needs phosphorus for root development, micronutrients for seedling vigour, and a steady nitrogen feed that won't burn fragile seedlings. Standard granular fertilisers don't do this job well.

NZLA Starter is built for that early window. The 19-10-4 NPK has phosphorus in the middle digit (10%) for root drive, lower potassium because it's not the priority for new turf, and slow-release nitrogen so seedlings don't scorch. The trace element pack (Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu) covers the micronutrient gaps that often hold establishment back.

## The numbers

19% nitrogen (slow-release), 10% phosphorus, 4% potassium, plus iron, manganese, zinc and copper.

Rate is 1.5 kg per 100m², applied at soil preparation, before the seed goes down.

Lasts up to 3 months, which carries the lawn through establishment and into early maintenance.

## When to use

New lawn establishment from seed. Apply at soil preparation, rake in lightly, then sow the seed on top.

Major renovation work where the lawn's effectively being re-established (full scarify and reseed).

Patch repair where you've prepped the soil and are oversowing.

Don't reapply for the first 3 months unless growth is clearly slowing, at which point switch to All Seasons.

## How it sits next to the others

Starter is the only NZLA granular built for establishment specifically. The standard All Seasons range is for maintenance on established lawns.

Liquid Starter (8-10-5 plus kelp) is the foliar partner for Starter. Granular at soil prep, foliar at 4 to 5 weeks after sowing once seedlings are robust enough to spray.

The Gateway Starter Package bundles Starter granular with Liquid Starter for new lawn projects.

## When not to use

On established lawns as a regular feed. The high phosphorus isn't needed once the root system is built, and over-applying P on established turf can lock up iron and other micronutrients.

Right next to actively growing seedlings as a top-dress. The phosphorus goes in at soil prep, before the seed. Top-dressing a fragile seedling can burn it.

As a quick recovery feed. Starter is slow-release across 3 months, that's not the right tool for a fast push.

## Common mistakes

Applying after sowing the seed. The whole point is to get the P down into the soil where roots will reach it. Surface-applied phosphorus doesn't move down well.

Skipping the rake-in step. Lightly raking the granular into the top 5 to 10mm of soil mixes the fertiliser into the root zone.

Continuing to apply Starter every 6 to 8 weeks on the new lawn. Once established (typically 8 to 12 weeks post-sowing) transition to All Seasons.

## Best paired with

Liquid Starter (8-10-5 plus kelp) as the foliar follow-up 4 to 5 weeks after sowing.

NZLA Seed (whichever variety suits the lawn) at the recommended sowing rate.

Transition to All Seasons after the first 3 months and the first 2 to 3 mows.
