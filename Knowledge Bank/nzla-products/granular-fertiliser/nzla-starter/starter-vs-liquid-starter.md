# Should I use NZLA Starter or Liquid Starter?

Both, in sequence. Different roles in the same establishment programme.

NZLA Starter (granular): goes into the soil before sowing. Phosphorus and slow-release nitrogen positioned in the root zone for the first 12 weeks of establishment. The foundation feed for the new lawn.

Liquid Starter (foliar/soil drench): used at and after sowing as a foliar and root-zone push. Faster uptake, drives initial seedling growth on top of the granular base. Apply at sowing, then at 7 to 10 day intervals through the first 4 to 6 weeks.

Standard new lawn programme:
- Day 0: NZLA Starter incorporated into prepared soil. Sow seed.
- Day 7 to 10 after germination: Liquid Starter foliar application.
- Day 17 to 20: second Liquid Starter application.
- Week 4 to 6: third Liquid Starter or transition to Liquid Boost.
- Week 6 to 8: first mow.
- Week 8 to 10: first granular maintenance feed (All Seasons).

Some NZLA establishment packages bundle both. The combination is what gives the lawn the strongest possible start, granular for the rooting and slow-release base, liquid for the rapid above-ground push.

If budget only allows one: NZLA Starter (granular) is the more important of the two for root development. Liquid Starter accelerates the result on top of that.
