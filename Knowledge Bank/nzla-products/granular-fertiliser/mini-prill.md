# NZLA Mini Prill

High-nitrogen granular push feed in a tiny SGN 150 prill. 28-0-12 with 3% iron and a 70% immediately-available, 30% controlled-release nitrogen profile. The alternative to All Seasons N+ for dense canopy situations and low cutting heights where standard prills don't reach the soil.

## The prill size is the point

SGN 150 is small enough to drop through a dense turf canopy to soil level. Standard granulars (SGN 200 to 250 range) on a thick, fine lawn — or any lawn mown at below about 25 mm — tend to hang up on the leaf rather than reaching the root zone. Fertiliser on top of the canopy doesn't feed the plant well.

For fine fescue, closely maintained ryegrass under PGR, bowling green surrounds, or any lawn where the canopy is dense or the mowing height is tight, the smaller prill is what makes the fertiliser actually work.

## The numbers

28% nitrogen (70% immediately available, 30% controlled release), 0% phosphorus, 12% potassium, 3% iron.

The fast-release majority means visible green-up within a week of application, with the controlled-release fraction carrying the feed through the following 6 to 8 weeks. Apply every 10 to 12 weeks during the growing season.

## Rate and application

2 kg per 100m². Apply to moist soil, water in with 5 to 10 mm of irrigation-general or rainfall within 24 hours. The 70% immediately-available fraction will scorch the leaf if it sits on dry foliage without being washed in.

Use a spreader calibrated for fine prills. Cheap spinner spreaders can struggle with SGN 150 and produce uneven coverage.

## When to use

Dense or fine canopy lawns (fine fescue, close-mown ryegrass, bowling and golf surrounds).

Lawns maintained at below 25 mm cutting height where standard granulars don't penetrate the surface.

Spring push (September to November) and late summer recovery (February to March).

Post-renovation once seedlings are established.

Not required on standard home lawns at 35 mm plus — All Seasons N+ delivers the same nitrogen hit in a prill size that reaches the soil fine on a typical lawn.

## Mini Prill vs All Seasons N+

Same role (high-N push), different delivery mechanism. All Seasons N+ is the simpler and more cost-effective choice for lawns mown at 35 mm and above where canopy penetration isn't an issue. Mini Prill is for the specific situations where the smaller prill genuinely makes a difference to where the fertiliser ends up.

## When not to use

As a year-round base. Mini Prill is a push tool for specific windows, not a constant feed. Repeated high-N applications without a potassium and iron foundation underneath creates an unbalanced programme.

Going into winter. High immediately-available nitrogen plus cool conditions plus Fusarium pressure is a bad combination.

On stressed or dormant turf.
