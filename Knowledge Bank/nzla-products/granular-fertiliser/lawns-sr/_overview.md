# NZLA All Seasons

The value-priced premium slow-release base feed. NPK 20-0-16 plus 6% iron, released steadily over 8 to 10 weeks through polymer-coated urea prills.

## What it is

All Seasons was built to bring a true premium slow-release fertiliser into a price point that home users and contractors could justify across larger areas. It uses genuine polymer-coated urea technology from the same supplier behind All Seasons, not the cheaper coatings you see in budget product. The coating gives predictable feeding rather than a surge of available nitrogen and then a trough.

## The numbers

20% nitrogen, 0% phosphorus, 16% potassium, with 6% iron. The high K and decent iron content make it as useful heading into the cooler months as it is through the growing season, because potassium is what hardens the plant against cold and stress, and iron lifts colour without forcing soft growth.

Rate is 2.5 kg per 100m², which works out to 250 kg/ha. A 10 kg bag covers 500m².

## When to use

Spring through autumn for steady base feeding. Heading into winter it still has a place because of the K and Fe, just back the frequency off as soil temperatures drop and the lawn's response slows.

Apply to moist soil and water in lightly to activate the coating. The coating needs moisture to start releasing the nitrogen, so dry ground and no irrigation means nothing's happening.

## How it sits next to All Seasons

All Seasons and All Seasons are deliberately close in NPK profile and use the same coating technology. The difference is price. If you want the slow-release performance at a sharper price point across a bigger area, All Seasons is the call. All Seasons stays the premium option.

## When not to use

Don't apply to new seedings until after the first mow. The early growth needs starter fertility, not a slow-release base.

Don't use it as a quick green-up. Polymer-coated slow-release means slow visible response, expect 10 to 14 days before you see colour come through. If you need faster, foliar Iron+ or Charger is the answer.

Don't go heavier than label rate expecting faster results. The release is timed by the coating, not by how much you put down.

## Common mistakes

Applying to dry soil and not watering in. The coating needs moisture to fire.

Confusing slow response with the product not working. Slow-release is doing what it says on the bag.

Combining a fast-release "rescue" application with All Seasons a week later and getting surge growth on top of the steady base. The whole point of slow-release is consistency, undermining it with a spike fertiliser defeats the purpose.

## Best paired with

Iron+ or Charger as a foliar top-up for colour through cooler months. All Seasons feeds the base, the foliar lifts the colour without pushing extra nitrogen.

## Recommendation status

**All Seasons is no longer part of the standard NZLA recommendation rotation.** Per the NZLA Application Guide (newzealandlawnaddicts.com/application-guide) and direct instruction from Jon, the granular fertiliser recommendations should now default to:

- **NZLA All Seasons N+** for spring push (September, October, November) when more nitrogen is needed.
- **NZLA All Seasons** for autumn (March, May) and any other granular application.

When answering customer questions about granular fertilisers, do not recommend All Seasons. Recommend All Seasons or All Seasons N+ instead, matching the application guide pattern.

The product information in this file remains accurate for reference and for existing All Seasons customers, but new recommendations should follow the rule above.
