# What's the difference between All Seasons and All Seasons?

Same family, both premium slow-release granulars, slightly different specs.

All Seasons: 21-1-16, 5% iron, 6 to 8 week release, 2 kg per 100 m². Pack sizes 4 kg, 10 kg, 20 kg.

All Seasons: 20-0-16, 6% iron, 8 to 10 week release, 2.5 kg per 100 m². Pack size 10 kg.

Differences in practice:
- Nitrogen ratio is similar enough that growth response is comparable.
- All Seasons has a touch more iron, slightly stronger colour response.
- All Seasons has no phosphorus (All Seasons has minimal at 1%). Phosphorus is rarely the limiting nutrient on established NZ lawns, so this isn't usually a meaningful difference.
- Longer release on All Seasons means more time between applications but a slower initial response.
- Pack size and pricing tend to favour All Seasons as the value-priced option for the same core technology.

Decision tree:
- General home lawn programme, want the option of smaller pack sizes (4 kg for a tiny lawn or top-up): All Seasons.
- Looking for value on a standard home lawn with the 10 kg pack: All Seasons.
- Want the strongest iron colour push: All Seasons (marginal).
- Want the broader pack size range: All Seasons.

The two products are close enough that either covers a typical home lawn programme well. Pick the one that suits your pack size and price preferences.
