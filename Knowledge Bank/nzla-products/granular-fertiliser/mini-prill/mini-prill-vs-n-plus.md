# Should I use Mini Prill or All Seasons N+?

For most home lawns, All Seasons N+ is the right choice. Standard prill size, predictable spread, easier handling with most spreaders, suits cutting heights of 25 mm and above.

Mini Prill makes sense when:
- Cutting height is below about 25 mm and standard granules sit visible on the surface for too long.
- The canopy is dense enough that standard prills get stuck on the leaves rather than reaching the soil.
- You want the higher 28-0-12 ratio (vs N+'s 21-1-16) for a stronger nitrogen push.
- You're applying through a spreader that handles fine prills well.

The drawbacks of Mini Prill:
- Smaller prill = trickier to spread evenly with cheaper spreaders. Spinner spreaders for SGN 150 need to be set up properly.
- The 70% immediately available portion means stricter watering-in. Skip the irrigation and you risk surface burn.
- More expensive per unit nitrogen than All Seasons N+.

For a regular home lawn at 35 to 50 mm cutting height on standard ryegrass or fescue, All Seasons N+ is the simpler, cheaper, and equally effective choice. Mini Prill is a tool for the situations where prill size genuinely matters, not a general substitute.
