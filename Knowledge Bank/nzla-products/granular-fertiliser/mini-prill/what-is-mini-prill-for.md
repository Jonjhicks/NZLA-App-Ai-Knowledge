# What's NZLA Mini Prill used for?

Two situations.

First, dense or fine canopy lawns where standard-size granulars get hung up on the leaf canopy instead of reaching the soil. Mini Prill's SGN 150 prill size is small enough to drop through a thick turf canopy to soil level, where the fertiliser can actually work.

Second, lawns mown at low cutting heights (under 25 mm) where you want a push feed without the visual disruption of larger granules sitting on the surface. The smaller prill is less visible and clears faster.

Lawns where Mini Prill makes the most sense:
- Fine fescue lawns, especially when maintained at lower heights
- Bowling greens, golf course tees and surrounds (commercial side)
- Densely tillered ryegrass lawns at moderate to low cutting heights
- Lawns under PGR programmes (denser canopy holds standard granules at the surface)

For a standard home lawn at 35 to 50 mm cutting height, Mini Prill isn't necessary, All Seasons or All Seasons N+ will reach the soil fine. Mini Prill is a tool for specific situations rather than a general-purpose product.
