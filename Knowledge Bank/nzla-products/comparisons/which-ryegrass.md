# Which Ryegrass?

All four are Lolium perenne (perennial ryegrass). The differences are in colour, texture, spreading habit, and what programme is needed to get the best from each.

## NZLA Perennial Rye

The standard option. Reliable, proven, widely adapted across NZ. Fast establishment (7 to 14 days), strong winter activity, good traffic tolerance, available in larger quantities at accessible price.

Suited for: general home lawns, patch repair, oversowing, any situation where performance is more important than premium appearance.

Rate: 25 to 35 g/m² new lawn, 15 to 20 g/m² oversow, 30 to 40 g/m² patch repair.

## NZLA Dark Perennial Ryegrass

Selected dark-coloured cultivar. Same species as standard rye, better colour genetics. Noticeably darker in the canopy than commodity ryegrass. Finer leaf texture, moderate improvement in summer performance.

The colour improvement shows progressively with annual seeding-and-over-seeding — the first year on an existing lawn is a modest improvement; by year three of consistent oversowing, the difference is significant.

Suited for: showcase home lawns wanting improved colour without stepping up to a premium programme. A straightforward upgrade from standard rye.

Rate: 25 to 35 g/m² new lawn, 15 to 20 g/m² oversow.

## Midnight Dark Ryegrass

Premium cultivar. The darkest, finest-textured ryegrass in the range. The colour difference versus standard ryegrass is striking when the programme supports it.

Critical caveat: Midnight's colour potential is entirely programme-dependent. Without regular iron (NZLA Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks), a PGR programme, and frequent close mowing, Midnight looks like a slightly nicer standard rye. The genetics are there — but only the programme brings them out.

For customers asking "what's the best looking lawn?" the answer involves Midnight as the seed plus the management programme. Without the programme, the extra cost of Midnight over DPR is not justified.

Rate: 30 to 40 g/m² new lawn, 15 to 20 g/m² oversow. Mow down to 12 mm on a full programme.

Suited for: high-input showcase lawns, customers already running a consistent fertiliser and iron programme who want to maximise visual result.

## RSR Ryegrass (Recover, Spread, Regenerate)

Stoloniferous Lolium perenne. Produces above-ground stolons that spread laterally and create new plants — unlike standard rye which only tillers from the crown. The stolon mat fills wear damage passively and suppresses Poa annua through competitive density over 2 to 3 seasons.

The trade-off for the spreading habit: do not scalp RSR. The stolons run on the surface — cutting too low removes the spreading mechanism. Mow at 20 to 50 mm, never below 15 mm.

Suited for: sports fields, high-wear family lawns, lawns with persistent Poa annua pressure, any situation where wear recovery and self-repair are the priority.

Rate: 25 to 35 g/m² new lawn, 15 to 20 g/m² oversow.

## Choosing

**Standard home lawn, budget-conscious, no premium aspirations:** NZLA Perennial Rye.

**Home lawn wanting noticeably better colour without major programme change:** Dark Perennial Rye.

**Showcase lawn, high-input programme, premium appearance as the goal:** Midnight + regular iron + PGR + frequent close mowing.

**Kids, dogs, heavy traffic, wear repair, or Poa annua problem:** RSR.

**Blending:** RSR + Midnight is a strong combination for lawns wanting both self-repair and premium colour. Sow at 50/50 and run the full programme.
