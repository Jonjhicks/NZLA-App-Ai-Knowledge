# Which Wetting Agent?

NZLA has four wetting agent products. They're not interchangeable — each is optimised for a specific soil situation. Understanding what your lawn actually needs tells you which one to reach for.

Full background on wetting agents: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/wetting-agent-for-lawns-liquid-vs-granular/

## The three surfactant types

Wetting agents work through different mechanisms:

**Penetrant** — breaks surface tension and hydrophobic coatings to get water moving into dry or repellent soil. The fix for water beading off or puddling rather than soaking in.

**Retainer** — slows the movement of water through the soil profile, holding moisture in the root zone longer. The fix for sandy soils that drain too freely.

**Spreader** — promotes even distribution of water horizontally through the soil, reducing dry patches and hot spots. Works alongside the other two.

## The four NZLA products

**NZLA Wetter** — primarily a retainer. Best for sandy soils, coastal lawns, and any lawn that dries out too quickly after irrigation-general or rain. Holds moisture in the root zone where roots can access it.

**NZLA Wetter 3W** — three-way product containing penetrant, retainer, and spreader. The best all-round option for most lawns. Covers all three mechanisms in a single application. Use this when you're not sure which mechanism the lawn needs, or when the soil has combined issues.

**NZLA Penetrate** — pure penetrant. The specific tool for very dry, hardened, or hydrophobic soils where water is running off or sitting on the surface instead of soaking in. Also the right product for clay soils or compacted profiles where surface penetration is the bottleneck. Post-flood application to reopen surface penetration.

**NZLA Wetter GR** — granular formulation of the wetting agent actives. Suits situations where liquid application isn't practical (no sprayer, very small area, broadcast with spreader). Less efficient than liquid at the same active rate — granular requires irrigation-general to activate the actives before they can work. Liquid wetting agents start working immediately on application.

## Choosing

**Sandy soil, dries too fast:** Wetter (retainer).

**Clay, compacted, water not soaking in, puddling after rain:** Penetrate (penetrant).

**Most home lawns, general maintenance:** Wetter 3W (three-way).

**Severe hydrophobic soil, water beading on surface:** Penetrate first, then Wetter 3W for ongoing maintenance.

**No sprayer available, spreading with granular spreader:** Wetter GR.

## Application

All NZLA liquid wetting agents: apply and water in with 6 to 10 mm irrigation-general immediately after. The product needs to move from the leaf surface into the soil to do its job.

Apply monthly from early September through summer. September application is particularly important — you're loading the soil before the dry season begins, when the soil still has enough moisture to carry the product deep into the profile.

Wetting agents can be applied just before rain — this is actually ideal timing. The rain activates the product and moves it into the soil.

## Can you tank-mix Aerate with wetting agents?

Yes. NZLA Aerate (soil conditioner that works on clay particle structure) can be tank-mixed with Penetrate. On soils with both clay composition and water repellency, the combination addresses both issues simultaneously.
