# Elfin vs Ultimate Tall Fescue

Both are Festuca arundinacea (tall fescue). Both are drought-tolerant, heat-tolerant, and shade-tolerant compared to ryegrass. The critical difference is one spreads and one doesn't.

## Elfin Tall Fescue — self-repairing

Elfin is rhizomatous. It produces underground rhizomes that spread laterally, producing new plants and actively filling in bare or worn areas. The self-repair develops over 2 to 3 seasons as the rhizome network establishes.

This is the defining reason to choose Elfin over UTF. On lawns that take regular wear (children, dogs, foot traffic) where patches need to fill, Elfin closes them from its own spread rather than requiring seeding-and-over-seeding.

The appearance is good — reasonably fine texture for a tall fescue, darker than standard commodity fescue, but not as dark or fine as UTF.

Rate: 35 to 45 g/m² new lawn, 25 to 30 g/m² oversow. Mow at 30 to 70 mm.

## NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue (UTF) — premium appearance

UTF is bunch-forming, not rhizomatous. It doesn't spread. Patches don't self-repair — they need to be overseeded.

What UTF trades away in self-repair, it gains in appearance. Very dark green, finer-textured than Elfin, higher density. The visual result from UTF is closer to a quality ryegrass lawn than to what most people picture when they think "tall fescue."

The correct comparison is UTF vs premium ryegrass, not UTF vs Elfin. UTF wins on drought tolerance, heat tolerance, and wider shade tolerance. Premium ryegrass wins on self-repair from dense tillering and better cold-weather activity.

Rate: 35 to 45 g/m² new lawn, 25 to 30 g/m² oversow. Mow at 50 to 150 mm.

## Choosing

**High-wear, kids and dogs, needs to self-repair:** Elfin.

**Premium appearance, drought-prone site, hot dry summers:** UTF.

**Both:** blend them. Elfin + UTF at 50/50 gives combined self-repair from Elfin's rhizomes and improved visual quality from UTF's genetics. Strong option for warm, dry regions wanting both.

## Tall fescue vs ryegrass

The broader choice for NZ lawns.

Ryegrass: faster establishment, better cold-weather active growth, easier to maintain at lower heights, less drought tolerant.

Tall fescue (either Elfin or UTF): slower establishment, far better summer and drought performance, wider mowing height range (can hold at 50 to 70 mm easily), more tolerant of partial shade and heat.

For Canterbury, Hawke's Bay, inland Waikato, and upper North Island coastal sites: tall fescue is often the better long-term choice even though it takes a season longer to establish to full density.

For high-rainfall regions where summer heat isn't extreme and close-mowing is preferred: ryegrass is generally better suited.
