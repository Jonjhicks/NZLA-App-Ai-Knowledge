# BWC vs Gold

Both control broadleaf  in cool-season lawns. BWC is the professional standard product. Gold is the stronger option for tougher  and resistant populations.

## NZLA BWC

Active ingredients: MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican.

The professional-grade broadleaf herbicide. From March 2025, BWC is restricted to PCBU (commercial/professional) use — online purchase requires verification at checkout.

Highly effective on most common NZ broadleaf : clover, dandelion, catsear, plantain, oxalis, daisy, dock, thistles. The clopyralid component is particularly effective on Asteraceae family  (catsear, dandelion) and legumes (clover). Diflufenican has some residual activity against germinating broadleaf weed seed.

Rate: 50 mL per 100m² in 10 L water. Apply to actively growing  in mild conditions.

## NZLA Gold

Active ingredient: picloram + triclopyr.

The heavier option. More aggressive chemistry for harder-to-kill  and situations where BWC hasn't achieved full control. Not PCBU-restricted — available for home use.

Picloram and triclopyr are both auxin-type herbicides, moving through the plant more aggressively than the BWC actives. More effective on established perennial  with strong root systems: dock, onion weed, some oxalis species, bindweed, and broadleaf  that have partially resisted BWC.

Rate: check the current label — Gold has higher activity and rates need careful attention.

Add NZLA Sticker as an adjuvant for both products on waxy or hairy-leafed  (catsear, thistles). The organosilicone in Sticker improves leaf coverage and penetration.

## Which to use

**BWC for:**
- Standard broadleaf weed programmes on commercial or professional properties
- Clover, dandelion, catsear, plantain — the common NZ 
- Where clopyralid's residual activity against germinating seed is useful

**Gold for:**
- Persistent or partially-resistant  after BWC hasn't fully controlled them
- Dock, onion weed, established perennial broadleaf  with strong roots
- Home lawn use where BWC's PCBU restriction applies
- Ongoing maintenance broadleaf control for residential customers

## What they don't control

Neither BWC nor Gold will kill grass  (poa annua, summer grass, paspalum, kikuyu). For grass-in-grass control, see the herbicide-basics article and the comparisons/grass-weed-options article.

Neither should be used on fine fescue — Gold particularly has activity on fescue species. Confirm species before applying.

## Can you use both?

Not simultaneously — no benefit to mixing them. If BWC at the correct rate fails on specific  after a proper application, step up to Gold as the follow-up 3 to 4 weeks later.
