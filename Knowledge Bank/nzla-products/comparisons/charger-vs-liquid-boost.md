# Charger vs Liquid Boost

Both are liquid foliar fertilisers. Both contain nitrogen and iron. They're designed for different jobs and work best when used together in a programme rather than choosing one over the other.

## Charger — the maintenance feed

NZLA Charger is 7-0-7 + 4% Fe + 2% Mn. Equal nitrogen and potassium, with iron and manganese.

The nitrogen in Charger is a blend of urea, nitrate, and SRN-IQ slow-release nitrogen. The potassium is citrate and carbonate form — not chloride or sulphate, which is a relevant difference on sodium-sensitive soils.

Charger is designed for regular application, every 2 to 3 weeks through the growing season. It maintains colour and density without pushing aggressive growth. The equal N:K ratio keeps the plant balanced — feeding leaf and root development at the same time. The lateral density bias of Charger is what makes it a maintenance product: it builds a thicker, tighter sward rather than shooting upward.

Use Charger: between granular applications, through winter when soil temperatures limit granular uptake, as the backbone of a foliar programme.

## Liquid Boost — the push product

NZLA Liquid Boost is 15-0-0 + 6% Fe. High nitrogen, no potassium, high iron.

The nitrogen is ammonium-stabilised plus SRN-IQ slow-release. 33% of the iron is EDTA-chelated for immediate uptake.

Liquid Boost is a targeted response product, not a maintenance feed. Apply it when you want a push: before an event or presentation, during recovery from  or stress, after renovation to drive establishment, when coming out of winter dormancy. The high-N, high-Fe formula produces rapid colour response and leaf growth.

Use Liquid Boost: for pre-event preparation, post-stress recovery, spring green-up push, after renovation. Not as a weekly maintenance product — the high nitrogen alone without potassium balance is not suitable for regular use.

## Using both in a programme

A typical programme looks like this:

Charger every 2 weeks through the season as the maintenance base. Liquid Boost 7 to 10 days before an event, or following any significant stress period (heat, drought recovery, post-post-renovation). Iron+ separately for colour without nitrogen push.

Charger + Iron+ is a common tank mix for regular maintenance — the Iron+ adds extra colour and hardening on top of Charger's balanced feed.

Liquid Boost replaces Charger on any application where a push is wanted rather than maintenance. Don't apply both on the same day — the combined N load is higher than needed.

## The simple version

Charger = weekly maintenance, steady state, 2-weekly application.
Liquid Boost = when you need a result fast or the lawn needs a push.
Iron+ = colour and hardening independently of nitrogen.
