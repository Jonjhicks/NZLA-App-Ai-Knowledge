# Azoxy vs Fungus Pro

Both are NZLA fungicides. They work differently, target diseases differently, and the right choice depends on what you're dealing with and what you've recently applied.

## The key difference

**NZLA Azoxy** (azoxystrobin 250 g/L) is a systemic fungicide. It's absorbed into the plant tissue and moves through the leaf. It works from inside — killing fungi that have already started to penetrate plant cells, and protecting new growth. Group 11 fungicide (FRAC classification).

**NZLA Fungus Pro** (thiram 480 g/L) is a contact protectant. It stays on the leaf surface and kills fungal spores before they can germinate and infect. It doesn't move into plant tissue. Multi-site mode of action.

## When to use Azoxy

Active  outbreaks where the fungus has already penetrated the plant. Fusarium patch, dollar spot, brown patch, yellow patch, rust, red thread, anthracnose. Azoxy's systemic action reaches infection sites that a contact product can't.

Preventative applications on lawns with  history — particularly for premium or showcase lawns going into high-pressure windows (autumn for fusarium, summer for brown patch/dollar spot).

## When to use Fungus Pro

Preventative programmes on healthy lawns where you're stopping infection before it starts. Fungus Pro applied before conditions deteriorate forms a protective barrier on the leaf surface.

Also appropriate for diseases where contact activity is sufficient — powdery mildew, some Rhizoctonia activity.

And critically: as the rotation partner for Azoxy. Never apply Azoxy more than twice consecutively without rotating to Fungus Pro.

## Resistance management — why rotation matters

Azoxy is a Group 11 fungicide (QoI/strobilurin class). Pathogens exposed repeatedly to the same mode of action develop resistance. Fungus Pro has a multi-site mode of action — resistance development is much slower because the fungus can't overcome multiple attack points simultaneously.

The standard rotation: Azoxy for the first application, Fungus Pro for the second, then Azoxy again if a third is needed. Never more than two consecutive applications of Azoxy before rotating.

On lawns that have used azoxystrobin products heavily over multiple seasons without rotation, resistance may already have developed — particularly in dollar spot and brown patch populations. If Azoxy seems to be working less effectively over time, resistance is likely. Rotate to Fungus Pro and allow several seasons before relying heavily on Azoxy again.

## Can you use both at the same time?

They can be tank-mixed but the primary benefit of each is different enough that the combination is more about insurance than necessity. On a severe active outbreak where both preventative and curative action is wanted simultaneously, a tank mix is reasonable. For standard management, sequential application (Azoxy first, Fungus Pro on second pass) is more resource-efficient.

## Rates

**Azoxy:** 50 to 100 mL per 100m² in 5 to 10 L water. Lower rate preventative, higher rate curative or severe infection.

**Fungus Pro:** 200 mL per 100m² preventative (every 4 weeks), 800 mL per 100m² curative.

Apply both to dry leaf in mild conditions. Don't apply in heat (above 28°C) or frost.
