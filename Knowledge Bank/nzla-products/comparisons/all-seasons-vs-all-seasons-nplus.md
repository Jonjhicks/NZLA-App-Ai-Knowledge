# All Seasons vs All Seasons N+

Both are NZLA granular base fertilisers using PCSCU slow-release technology sourced from the US. The difference is the NPK ratio and where each fits in the seasonal programme.

## The numbers

**All Seasons:** 21-1-16 + 5% Fe. Balanced nitrogen and potassium. The steady-state maintenance fertiliser.

**All Seasons N+:** 32-2-10 + 2% Fe. Higher nitrogen, less potassium. The growth and recovery fertiliser.

## When to use All Seasons

The backbone of the year-round programme. Apply every 6 to 8 weeks from October through May on cool-season turf.

The 21-1-16 ratio provides balanced nutrition — enough nitrogen for consistent colour and growth, with potassium to match. Potassium improves cold tolerance resistance, and plant strength. The 5% iron content drives colour without excessive growth stimulus.

All Seasons suits most conditions across most of the season. The balanced N:K ratio is particularly appropriate for:
- Maintenance feeding from late spring through summer
- Autumn hardening feed going into winter
- Any time the lawn is healthy and in maintenance mode rather than recovery mode

## When to step up to All Seasons N+

The 32-2-10 ratio is a growth push. The extra nitrogen drives rapid leaf growth and recovery. Use it when the lawn needs to move fast:

**Spring green-up** — the first fertiliser application of the season (September to October). Coming out of winter, the lawn needs to build leaf mass quickly. The higher N drives that initial push.

**Post-stress recovery** — after drought, heat damage outbreak, or renovation. Getting the lawn growing again fast is the priority.

**Pre-event preparation** — when the lawn needs to look its best quickly before a specific date.

**Slow-establishing lawns** — where growth is disappointing and the lawn needs a push to fill.

## What about the lower iron in N+?

All Seasons has 5% Fe. N+ has 2% Fe. If colour is a priority alongside the growth push, add NZLA Iron+ as a foliar application. The granular iron in All Seasons is slower to show visually than foliar iron. For rapid colour response while also pushing growth, N+ granular + Iron+ foliar is a stronger combination than All Seasons alone.

## The programme

A typical cool-season NI lawn programme:

**September/October:** All Seasons N+ (spring push)
**November/December:** All Seasons (maintenance)
**January/February:** All Seasons (maintenance, possibly skip in heat drought)
**March:** All Seasons N+ (autumn push, most important application of year)
**April/May:** All Seasons (hardening feed)
**Winter:** switch to foliar programme (Charger + Iron+)
