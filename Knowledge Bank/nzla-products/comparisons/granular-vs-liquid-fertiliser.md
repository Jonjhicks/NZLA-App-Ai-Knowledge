# Granular vs Liquid Fertiliser

The question isn't which is better — it's which does what job. A complete fertiliser programme uses both. Understanding what each does helps you know when to reach for which.

## How they work differently

**Granular fertiliser** releases nutrients into the soil, which roots then absorb. NZLA granular products use PCSCU slow-release technology — the coating breaks down gradually over weeks to months, releasing nitrogen at a rate the grass can use. The soil biology processes the nutrients, roots absorb them, and the plant responds over 7 to 14 days. Slower to show visually, longer-lasting.

**Liquid (foliar) fertiliser** is applied as a spray that the leaf absorbs directly. The plant doesn't need root uptake — nutrients enter through the stomata and leaf tissue. Response visible within 3 to 5 days. Shorter duration — no soil reservoir to draw from.

## When to use granular

**The foundation of the programme.** Granular provides the long-duration base nutrition that maintains the plant between applications. Apply every 6 to 8 weeks through the growing season. You can't run a lawn on foliars alone — there's no sustained nutrient release.

**When soil temperatures are above about 12°C.** Root uptake is active, soil biology is working, granular will be processed efficiently.

**For phosphorus and potassium loading.** These nutrients move through the soil more slowly than nitrogen. Granular application puts them in the root zone where they build up and persist.

## When to use liquid

**Winter.** Below about 10°C, soil biology slows, root uptake drops, and granular response is poor. Foliar bypasses this — nutrients absorb through the leaf regardless of soil temperature. NZLA Charger every 2 weeks through winter keeps the lawn fed when granular can't deliver. This is the most important reason to maintain a foliar programme.

**Between granular applications.** Granular feeds every 6 to 8 weeks. Foliar fills the gaps with responsive top-up nutrition. Particularly useful for colour maintenance (Iron+) and steady growth between feeds (Charger).

**For rapid response.** Pre-event push, post-stress recovery, spring green-up when you need results in days not weeks. Liquid Boost delivers a visible response in 3 to 5 days. Granular takes 7 to 14.

**When soil nutrient uptake is impaired.** Compaction, waterlogging, extreme pH, or root damage from  or  all reduce the plant's ability to absorb granular nutrients. Foliar works independently of soil conditions.

## The programme

A complete programme combines both:

**Granular base:** All Seasons or All Seasons N+ every 6 to 8 weeks, October through May.

**Foliar maintenance:** Charger every 2 to 3 weeks, year-round. Iron+ every 2 to 4 weeks for colour.

**Foliar push:** Liquid Boost before events, during recovery periods, or when a rapid response is needed.

**Winter only:** Drop granular by May, continue foliars through June to August, resume granular in September.

## Common mistakes

Using only granular and wondering why the lawn looks flat in winter: soil temps are too low for granular to work — add foliars.

Using only liquid and wondering why the lawn isn't improving long-term: no base nutrient reservoir — add granular.

Applying granular and liquid on the same day at full rates: combined nitrogen load can be high. If using both, either use granular at normal rate and foliar at half rate, or separate the applications by a week.
