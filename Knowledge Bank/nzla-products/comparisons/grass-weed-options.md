# Grass Weed Control Options

Grass  are harder than broadleaf  because you're trying to kill one grass species without harming another. The options are limited and each has strict species restrictions. Using the wrong product on the wrong lawn species is the most common mistake.

Full background: newzealandlawnaddicts.com/lawn-tips/lawn-herbicides-in-new-zealand-choosing-the-right-product-for-your-grass-type/

## Pre-emergent vs post-emergent

**Pre-emergent** stops seeds germinating. It has zero effect on established  already growing. Timing is everything — the barrier must be in place before target seeds germinate.

**Post-emergent** kills  that have already emerged. Some post-emergents also have pre-emergent activity when watered in (Etho, Meso).

## The products

### NZLA Blockade (prodiamine 476.2 g/L) — pre-emergent

Target : annual grasses (summer grass, crabgrass, poa annua), some broadleaf .

**NZ label restriction: registered for warm-season turf only (kikuyu, couch).** Off-label use on cool-season turf is not officially recommended.

Apply 2 to 3 weeks before soil temperatures at 50 mm reach the target weed's germination threshold. Activate with 6 mm of irrigation-general within 7 days.

Summer grass/crabgrass: apply when soil at 50 mm is approaching 12°C and rising (Auckland late August, Central NI mid-September, Canterbury late September to early October).

Poa annua: autumn application before soil temps drop to 18 to 22°C range.

Do not overseed through an active Blockade barrier. The barrier kills all germinating seed — including the grass seed you're trying to establish.

### NZLA Etho (ethofumesate 200 g/L) — primarily post-emergent, some pre-emergent activity

Target : poa annua, some annual grasses, some broadleaf .

**Safe on ryegrass and tall fescue. DO NOT use on fine fescue — will damage or kill it.**

Two-stage poa control: water in first application (kills germinating seed, pre-emergent effect) then apply to leaf surface 14 days later (kills emerged plants). Two applications 14 to 21 days apart for best results.

Safe at ryegrass seeding time as a pre-emergent — apply on day of sowing, water in to create a germination barrier that targets poa while allowing ryegrass to germinate. Wait until seedlings have had 2 mows before applying post-emergent.

Overuse risk: resistance development. Don't rely on Etho season after season without rotating to other modes of action.

### NZLA Meso (mesotrione 480 g/L) — post-emergent with pre-emergent activity

Target : browntop, creeping bentgrass, some broadleaf .

**Safe on ryegrass and tall fescue. Safe on fine fescue as post-emergent only (not at seeding). DO NOT use on fine fescue at seeding.** Safe on kikuyu at label rates.

EPA restriction: maximum 2 applications per 12-month period, minimum 14 days apart.

Can cause temporary bleaching/yellowing of desired species — this is expected and resolves. Apply when turf is actively growing and soil is moist. Particularly useful where browntop has invaded a ryegrass or fescue lawn.

Can tank-mix with Etho for ryegrass lawns — combined broadleaf, browntop, and poa control in one application.

### NZLA Halo (haloxyfop-P-methyl) — grass-in-grass, fine fescue lawns ONLY

Target : warm-season grasses (kikuyu, paspalum, couch, most grass species).

**Only for fine fescue lawns. Hard rate cap: 15 mL per 100m² on fine fescue.** This product WILL kill ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop, kikuyu, and most other grass species. Only fine fescue has tolerance.

Specific use case: kikuyu or paspalum invading a fine fescue lawn. Not for any other situation.

### Spot glyphosate + NZLA Sticker — non-selective

No lawn species tolerance. Kills everything it touches. Use only for isolated patches of problem grass where selective options don't exist, or for complete renovation. Reseed after 7 to 14 days.

Add NZLA Sticker to improve uptake on difficult-to-wet species (kikuyu, paspalum, kikuyu rhizomes in fine fescue).

## Quick reference by lawn species

| Lawn species | Poa annua | Summer grass | Browntop/bent | Kikuyu/warm-season invasion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ryegrass | Etho + Meso | Blockade (off-label) | Meso | Spot glyphosate |
| Tall fescue | Etho + Meso | Blockade (off-label) | Meso | Spot glyphosate |
| Fine fescue | Meso post-em only | — | Meso | Halo (max 15 mL/100m²) |
| Kikuyu | Blockade (on label) | Blockade | — | n/a |
| Couch | Blockade (on label) | Blockade | — | n/a |
