# What's the difference between Penetrate and Wetter?

Opposite jobs.

Wetter pulls water into hydrophobic dry soil. Use it through summer when water is beading off the surface and dry patches develop.

Penetrate pushes water down through wet soil. Use it through autumn and winter when canopy moisture lingers and the surface stays wet.

The two products belong to the same soil-water management toolkit but cover different seasons and different problems.

Decision tree:
- Summer dry patch, water beading off: Wetter.
- Surface waterlogging after rain, slow dry-down: Penetrate.
- Heavy clay with both problems at different times of year: Wetter through summer, Penetrate through autumn and winter.
- Lawn prone to disease in wet conditions: Penetrate as a preventative.
- Sandy soil with dry patch issues: Wetter or Wetter 3W.

Don't tank-mix Wetter and Penetrate. They work toward different outcomes (Wetter holds water in the surface for the lawn to use, Penetrate pushes water down past the surface). Together they cancel each other out.

For a lawn with year-round soil-water issues:
- October to March: Wetter or Wetter 3W programme.
- April to September: Penetrate programme.
- Transition months (March, October): assess what the lawn is doing and adjust based on what you see.
