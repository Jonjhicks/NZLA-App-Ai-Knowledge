# What does NZLA Penetrate do?

Penetrate moves surface water down through the soil profile. Different role to a wetting agent.

A wetting agent (Wetter, Wetter 3W) makes water move into hydrophobic soil that's repelling it. A penetrant (Penetrate) pushes water away from the surface and canopy, down deeper into the profile, accelerating dry-down at the surface.

Why that matters:
- Wet canopy = disease risk. Fusarium patch, dollar spot, brown patch all thrive in extended canopy moisture. Penetrate reduces the moisture sitting in the leaf canopy, lowering disease pressure.
- Surface waterlogging = lawn stress. Saturated surface soil starves roots of oxygen. Penetrate moves water down through the surface, reducing the time the lawn sits waterlogged.
- Soft squelchy ground = damage from foot traffic. Pushing surface water down firms the surface and reduces compaction from use.

Where it fits:
- Lawns prone to wet canopy and recurring disease.
- Heavy clay or silt soils that hold water at the surface.
- After heavy rainfall events to accelerate surface dry-down.
- Lawns over the cooler wet months when canopy moisture lingers.

Used to be called Push, renamed Penetrate in January 2025. Same product. Worth knowing if you've got older communication or labels referencing the previous name.
