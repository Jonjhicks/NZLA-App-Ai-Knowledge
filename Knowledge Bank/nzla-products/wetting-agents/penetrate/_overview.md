# NZLA Penetrate

A soil penetrant that drives water off the turf surface and down through the profile. Speeds soil dry-down, lifts turf firmness, reduces surface moisture. Completely different chemistry and different job to a conventional wetting agent.

## What it does

Some lawns have the opposite problem to a hydrophobic soil. Water enters the surface fine but then sits in the top centimetre of the profile, keeping the leaf canopy and crown damp for longer than it should be. That creates ideal conditions for fungal disease (Microdochium, dollar spot), moss and algae.

Penetrate accelerates the downward movement of water from the surface into the profile. The surface dries out faster, the lawn firms up, and the canopy stays drier between rainfall events.

## The chemistry

Three components do the work.

**Propanediol** (about 35% of the 250 mL formulation) is a glycol-based carrier and mild surfactant. It helps the product spread evenly across the soil surface and carries the active ingredients into position.

**Poly(2-methyloxirane)** (about 20%) is the water-mobility agent. It's a block copolymer that interacts with soil moisture and increases the rate at which water moves downward through the soil profile. This is the active responsible for the penetrant effect.

**Docusate sodium** (5% in the 500 mL pack) is an anionic surfactant that improves wetting and distribution of the formulation through the upper soil layer.

The combination is non-phytotoxic, doesn't change soil pH, and doesn't leave persistent residues. It works on water behaviour in the soil, not on plant tissue.

## Why this matters

Three real benefits.

**Disease pressure management.** Reducing canopy moisture is one of the most useful things you can do to lower Fusarium and related disease pressure heading into winter. Canopy moisture duration is one of the strongest predictors of disease outbreak. Penetrate shortens that window.

**Surface firmness.** On sports turf and high-use home lawns, surface firmness matters for play and for wear resistance. Soft waterlogged turf gets torn up by foot traffic. Drier surface turf holds up.

**Moss and algae reduction.** Both thrive in damp surface conditions. A drier surface reduces their growing environment.

## Rate and application

50 to 100 mL per 100m². Monthly application as needed.

Apply before rainfall to let the rain move the product down, or water in well after application.

## When to use

Lawns where the surface stays wet too long between rain events.

Disease pressure management. Reducing canopy moisture is the most useful cultural step you can take heading into winter to reduce Fusarium pressure.

Surface firmness improvement on sports turf or high-use home lawns.

Heavy clay or silt soils as part of a moisture management programme alongside Aerate.

Moss-prone areas as part of the cultural fix.

## How it sits next to the others

**Aerate** changes soil structure on clay and silt. **Penetrate** moves water away from the surface into the profile. The two pair well on heavy soils.

A conventional wetting agent (**Wetter, Wetter 3W, Wetter GR**) makes water enter hydrophobic soil in the first place. Penetrate is for the opposite situation — water entering fine but not moving down.

## When not to use

On dry, sandy soils where water already moves through fast. No problem to solve.

On lawns where the issue is hydrophobic crust at the surface — that's a wetting agent job.

## Common mistakes

Confusing it with a wetting agent. Penetrate is non-surfactant chemistry — it doesn't change surface tension to help water enter. It works downstream of that, on water already in the top of the profile.

Using it on lawns where the surface is genuinely dry. No water present to move.

Skipping the water-in or pre-rain application. Without water moving through, the product can't migrate down.

## Product history

NZLA Penetrate was previously sold as "Push" until renamed in January 2025. An older product also called "Penetrate" (different formula, 48% surfactant, 10 mL rate) is superseded. Use the current 50 to 100 mL/100m² rate.

## Best paired with

NZLA Aerate for clay and silt soils. Aerate restructures the soil, Penetrate keeps water moving through it.

Disease prevention programme heading into winter. Drier canopy, lower disease pressure.
