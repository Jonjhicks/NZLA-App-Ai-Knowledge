# NZLA Wetter 3W

Greens-grade three-way wetting agent. Penetrant, spreader, and retainer in a single application. Designed for high-performance turf and sand-based profiles where consistency of moisture across the full root zone is the goal.

## Three-way action

Most wetting agents do one job. Mid-range products do two. Premium greens-grade chemistry does all three.

**Penetrant** breaks through water-repellent soil so moisture enters quickly when you irrigate. Without it, water beads on the surface and runs off rather than reaching the roots.

**Spreader** ensures water distributes laterally across and through the soil rather than channelling straight down through whichever patch took water first. On sand-based profiles this is what stops water blowing through the root zone and being lost to the lower profile.

**Retainer** holds moisture around the root zone for longer use by the plant. Less water lost to deep percolation means more efficiency from every irrigation-general event.

## Why sand profiles need greens-grade chemistry

Sand-based root zones drain fast. That's why they're built — to get playability through winter and reduce  pressure from poor drainage-and-soil-moisture. But the same drainage-and-soil-moisture speed that makes them good in winter makes moisture management hard in summer.

Standard wetting agents don't do enough on pure sand profiles. The surface often becomes hydrophobic as thin organic matter builds up, so the penetrant fraction is needed to get water in. The sand offers no natural moisture retention, so the spreader and retainer fractions are what keep moisture distributed in the root zone long enough for the grass to use.

For premium home lawns built on a sand cap, sports turf and golf-related turf, greens-grade is the right specification.

## When to use

Monthly through the growing season as a programme, or at minimum before extended dry periods.

- Sand-based root zones where standard wetting agents aren't solving the problem.
- High-performance turf where presentation and consistency of condition matter.
- Pre-summer setup (October to November) for a single long-residual carry through the dry season.
- Mid-summer top-up (February) if conditions have been harder than usual.

The longer residual means one application in November can carry through January. Two applications (October/November and February) cover most NZ summers on a typical lawn.

## Rate and application

Check the current label for the exact rate. Apply in 4 to 8 L of water per 100m², using an 08 nozzle for even coverage. Apply early in the day in calm conditions so the spray pattern goes where you intend it.

Always water in after application, or apply right before steady rainfall. Don't leave the product sitting on hot foliage without watering — surfactant residue on hot leaves causes burn.

## Wetter 3W vs Wetter

Same non-ionic surfactant family, different residual and specification.

Wetter: standard residual (4 to 6 weeks), cheaper per litre, better for lawns already running a monthly programme.

Wetter 3W: longer residual (around 12 weeks), higher cost per litre but fewer applications per year. Net seasonal cost is comparable.

For most standard home lawns, Wetter does the job. Wetter 3W is the step up for high-performance turf, sand-based profiles, and owners who want to apply once and be done for the bulk of summer.

## Wetter 3W vs Wetter GR

Same three-way chemistry, different delivery. Wetter 3W is a liquid (spray application, requires watering in, lower cost per area). Wetter GR is granular (spreader application, no watering-in required, suited to non-irrigated lawns and larger areas).

For a home lawn with irrigation-general and a sprayer, Wetter 3W is the more cost-effective choice. For a large non-irrigated site or an owner who prefers granular application, Wetter GR suits better.

## Common mistakes

Leaving product on the leaf in hot sun without immediately watering in. Surfactant burn.

Treating as a one-off rescue rather than a programme product. Monthly application is the right pattern.

Using it on lawns where the problem is surface waterlogging rather than hydrophobicity — Wetter 3W holds moisture, it doesn't move it away. That's Penetrate's job.

## Where it sits in the range

Wetter 3W is the premium liquid option when standard Wetter isn't quite cutting it, or when the lawn is high-performance and you need the full three-way action. **Wetter** covers standard maintenance. **Wetter GR** delivers the same three-way chemistry for non-irrigated lawns and spreader application. **Penetrate** handles the opposite problem: surface moisture that won't drain. **Aerate** addresses clay soil structure.
