# NZLA Penetrate

A soil penetrant that moves surface water down through the profile. Speeds dry-down, lifts surface firmness, reduces canopy moisture. The autumn-winter partner in the NZLA soil-water management range — the opposite job to a conventional wetting agent.

*Previously sold as Push. Renamed Penetrate in January 2025. Same product, same rates.*

## What it does

Some lawns have the opposite problem to a hydrophobic soil. Water enters the surface fine but then sits in the top centimetre of the profile, keeping the leaf canopy and crown damp for longer than it should be. That creates ideal conditions for fusarium patch, dollar spot, and brown patch. It also keeps the surface soft and prone to damage underfoot.

Penetrate accelerates downward movement of water from the surface into the profile. The surface dries out faster, the lawn firms up, and the canopy stays drier between rainfall events.

## The chemistry

Propanediol (about 35% of the formulation) is a glycol-based carrier and mild surfactant that helps the product spread evenly across the soil surface and carry the other actives into position.

Poly(2-methyloxirane) — a block copolymer — is the water-mobility agent. It interacts with soil moisture and increases the rate at which water moves downward through the profile. This is the active responsible for the penetrant effect.

Docusate sodium in the 500 mL pack is an anionic surfactant that improves wetting and distribution through the upper soil layer.

None of these components change soil pH, leave persistent residues, or affect plant tissue.

## Why reduced canopy moisture matters

Canopy moisture duration is one of the strongest predictors of fungal  outbreak. Fusarium, red thread, and brown patch all require extended wet conditions on the leaf for infection to establish. Penetrate shortens that window by drying the surface faster after rain.

Running Penetrate through autumn as part of the pre--season programme is one of the most useful cultural steps you can take — it reduces  pressure before a single drop of fungicide is applied.

Secondary benefits: firmer surface on sports turf and high-use home lawns (saturated turf gets torn up by foot traffic), and drier surface conditions that suppress moss and algae.

## When to use

Through the wetter months when canopy moisture lingers. Autumn through winter — April to August — is the standard window.

- Pre- season (April): apply as autumn rains start, reduces canopy moisture going into fusarium patch and dollar spot season.
- After heavy rainfall events: accelerates surface dry-down.
- Monthly through winter on lawns prone to wet canopy and surface waterlogging.
- On sports turf before events where you need a firmer surface.

## Rate and application

50 to 100 mL per 100m² in 4 to 10 L of water, monthly as needed. Check the current label for the exact rate.

Apply before rainfall to let the rain move the product into the profile, or water in well after application. The product needs to reach the soil to do its job — surface-only application won't achieve much.

Tank-mix well with NZLA Aerate on clay and silt soils (Aerate restructures the soil, Penetrate keeps water moving through it). Also pairs well with Fungus Pro or Azoxy in the lead-up to  season.

Do not tank-mix with Wetter or Wetter 3W. They work toward opposite outcomes. Wetter holds moisture in the upper profile; Penetrate pushes it away from the surface. Used together they cancel each other out.

## Penetrate vs Wetter

Opposite jobs, different seasons.

Wetter pulls water into dry hydrophobic soil. Use it October to March through the dry months.

Penetrate pushes surface water down through wet soil. Use it April to September through the wet months.

The two products cover the full year between them: Wetter for summer, Penetrate for winter.

Decision: water beading off a dry surface in summer = Wetter. Surface waterlogging after rain, slow dry-down, wet canopy = Penetrate.

## Product note

If older labels, messages, or records refer to "Push" — that's this product. Renamed Penetrate in January 2025. An older product also called Penetrate (different formula, much lower application rate of around 10 mL/100m²) is superseded. Use the current 50 to 100 mL/100m² rate on current labels.

## Where it sits in the range

Penetrate is the autumn-winter soil-water tool. **Wetter**, **Wetter 3W**, and **Wetter GR** are the summer-dry-patch tools working in the opposite direction. **Aerate** addresses clay soil structure and tank-mixes well with Penetrate on heavy soils.
