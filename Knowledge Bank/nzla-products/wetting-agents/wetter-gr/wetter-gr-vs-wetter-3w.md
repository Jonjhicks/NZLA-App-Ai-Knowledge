# Should I use Wetter GR or Wetter 3W?

Same chemistry, different format. The right choice depends on your setup and the lawn size.

Wetter 3W (liquid):
- Spray application
- Requires watering in
- One application gives 12 weeks of residual
- Lower cost per area
- Best for lawns with irrigation and a sprayer setup

Wetter GR (granular):
- Spreader application
- No watering in required
- Monthly applications through the growing season
- Higher cost per area
- Best for non-irrigated lawns and larger areas

Decision tree:
- Have irrigation, have a sprayer, want low total cost: Wetter 3W.
- No irrigation, only a spreader, want set-and-forget granular: Wetter GR.
- Large area (lifestyle block, sports field): Wetter GR is faster to apply.
- Smaller home lawn with summer dry patch: Wetter 3W is more cost-effective.
- Already applying granular fertiliser monthly and want to combine: Wetter GR fits the workflow.

For a typical NZ home lawn with irrigation and a hose-end or backpack sprayer, Wetter 3W is the cheaper and equally effective choice. Wetter GR is the right pick when granular delivery suits the situation better than spray, particularly on non-irrigated lawns.
