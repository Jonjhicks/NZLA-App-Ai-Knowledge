# NZLA Wetter GR

The granular version of NZLA's premium wetting agent range. Same three-way action as Wetter 3W (penetration, lateral spreading, rehydration of hydrophobic soil) delivered through a granular carrier. The right tool for lawns without irrigation and for users who prefer spreader application.

## What it does

Granular wetting agents work by gradually releasing the surfactant active over weeks as the granule breaks down with moisture. That gives a slower, steadier wetting effect than a liquid hit, which suits routine prevention more than rescue application.

The three actions are the same as Wetter 3W. **Penetrant** for getting water through hydrophobic surfaces. **Spreader** for even distribution across the lawn. **Retainer** for holding moisture around the root zone for longer use by the plant.

## Why granular vs liquid matters

Three real differences.

**No water-in required.** This is the big one. Liquid wetting agents need to be sprayed and then washed into the soil with 15 to 20 mm of irrigation to be effective. On lawns without irrigation systems, that's a problem — you can't reliably apply a wetting agent and then control whether it gets washed in.

Granular Wetter GR sits on the lawn until the next rain. The moisture from rain dissolves the carrier and releases the surfactant gradually. No need to control the irrigation step.

**Slower release.** Liquids hit the soil with the full surfactant load on application, fast effect but less duration. Granular delivers the surfactant over weeks as the granule breaks down, giving longer steady action.

**Easier application on larger areas.** Spreader application of granular product is faster than spraying for large areas. For a contractor or large lawn, that's a real time saving.

## Rate and application

1.5 kg per 100m² every 4 weeks during the growing season.

Irrigation is not strictly required after application. Apply before rain and let the weather do the work.

If irrigation is available, water in with 10 to 15 mm to activate the granule and move the active into the soil faster.

## When to use

Lawns without irrigation, where applying liquid wetting agents and immediately watering them in isn't practical.

Routine through the growing season for preventative moisture management.

Larger lawns where granular spreader application is faster than spraying.

Users who prefer a spreader over a sprayer.

Lawns where the wetting agent application schedule needs to match rainfall patterns rather than irrigation availability.

## How it sits next to the others

**Wetter**: standard non-ionic residual liquid wetting agent. Routine and prevention. Good for most home lawns with irrigation.

**Wetter 3W**: premium liquid three-way for high-performance turf and sand-based profiles.

**Wetter GR**: granular three-way. Same chemistry concept as 3W, delivered on a carrier that doesn't need watering in.

**Penetrate**: different chemistry doing the opposite job — moves water down through the profile.

**Aerate**: soil-structure tool for clay and silt.

## When not to use

For rescue treatment of severely hydrophobic patches. A liquid (Wetter or Wetter 3W) works faster.

On already waterlogged lawns.

Without any moisture (irrigation or rain) at all to activate the granule.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a granular fertiliser. It's a wetting agent, not a feed. Don't substitute it for All Seasons or any nutrient product.

Expecting overnight results. The slow release that makes it useful for routine prevention also means it doesn't hit a problem patch as fast as a liquid.

Skipping reapplication. The 4-week cycle is the right pattern through the growing season.

Comparing the upfront cost against cheaper granular products. The three-way chemistry is doing more work than a single-action granular.

## Best paired with

A routine maintenance programme through summer when water efficiency matters most.

A solid fertility base.

NZLA Penetrate or Aerate where the lawn also has water-movement or soil-structure issues that a wetting agent alone won't solve.
