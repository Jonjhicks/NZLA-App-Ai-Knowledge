# NZLA Wetter

The standard non-ionic residual wetting agent. Heavy molecular weight surfactant chemistry — longer-lasting and gentler on the lawn than the cheaper ionic products you'll find at garden centres.

## What it does

Over time, soil develops hydrophobic patches. Sandy soils that have dried out hard, thatchy lawns with a waxy layer at the surface, soils coming out of a long dry summer where the surface has become water-repellent. The symptom is the same: water sits on top, runs off, and the lawn dries out even when you're irrigating.

Wetter is a non-ionic residual surfactant. Each molecule has a dual function: the hydrophobic end binds to the dry, waxy soil particles that are repelling water. The hydrophilic end then attracts water and distributes it through the soil profile. That conversion from water-repellent to water-absorbing is what fixes the dry patch.

## Why the chemistry matters

Most cheap wetting agents are ionic surfactants. They're more reactive on plant tissues, which means higher burn risk, especially in hot conditions. Wetter is non-ionic, so it's gentler on the lawn at the same rate.

The heavy molecular weight means the molecules don't leach out of the soil as quickly as lighter ones. That's where the residual comes from — fewer applications needed across the season to keep hydrophobicity under control.

After application you'll see: water soaking into previously dry patches within one or two waterings, more even soil moisture across the lawn, and often a colour improvement as moisture-stressed turf rehydrates.

## When to use

Spring through summer and into autumn — October to April for cool-season turf — at four-to-six-week intervals for a programme.

- Pre-summer preventative on lawns prone to dry patch, particularly sandy soils.
- Active treatment as soon as dry patches appear.
- After dethatching or coring to help water move into freshly opened soil.
- Tank-mixed with summer fertiliser applications to help nutrients move down into the root zone.

Don't apply on saturated soil — the product can't establish if there's no room for it to move. Don't apply without a follow-up irrigation-general (see below).

## Rate and application

Check the current label for the exact rate. Apply in 4 to 8 L of water per 100m². Always wash in with 15 to 20 mm of irrigation-general after application — without that water-in, the surfactant sits on the surface and does nothing useful. For severe hydrophobicity, repeat 7 to 10 days later.

Routine maintenance: apply every 30 days through the dry months.

## Wetter vs Wetter 3W

Both are non-ionic wetting agents. The difference is residual length and application frequency.

Wetter has a residual of around 4 to 6 weeks. Best for lawns already running a monthly foliar programme — tank-mix the wetting agent in with regular nitrogen and iron applications and one trip across the lawn covers everything.

Wetter 3W has a longer residual, typically around 12 weeks. One application in late spring carries through the bulk of summer. Better for set-and-forget situations or lawns where monthly applications aren't practical.

For cost: Wetter is cheaper per litre. Wetter 3W has fewer applications per year. Total seasonal cost is usually comparable.

## Common mistakes

Skipping the wash-in. Without 15 to 20 mm of irrigation-general the product sits on the surface. That's the single most common reason applications don't work.

Treating it as a one-off fix. Hydrophobicity rebuilds over time. Monthly application keeps it in check.

Using it on waterlogged lawns. Wetter pulls water in — the opposite of what a waterlogged lawn needs. If water is entering fine but sitting on the surface, you need Penetrate.

## Where it sits in the range

Wetter is the standard maintenance tool. **Wetter 3W** is the longer-residual liquid for set-and-forget applications or greens-grade turf. **Wetter GR** delivers the same surfactant chemistry through a granular carrier for lawns without irrigation-general. **Penetrate** does the opposite job: moves surface water down through the profile, and is the autumn-winter partner in cool wet conditions. **Aerate** works on soil structure rather than surface tension, and is the tool for heavy clay soils.
