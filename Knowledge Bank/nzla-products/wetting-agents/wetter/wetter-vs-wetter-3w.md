# Should I use Wetter or Wetter 3W?

Different residual lengths, different programme commitments.

Wetter: standard residual around 4 to 6 weeks. Requires monthly application through summer for continuous cover. Cheaper per litre.

Wetter 3W: longer residual around 12 weeks. One application carries through the dry season. More expensive per litre but fewer applications needed.

Decision tree:
- One-off treatment for a localised dry patch: Wetter, single application.
- Want to set and forget across summer: Wetter 3W, one application in late spring.
- Running a foliar programme through summer anyway and happy to tank-mix monthly: Wetter (cheaper, integrates with monthly programme).
- Set-and-forget owner who doesn't want to apply anything during summer: Wetter 3W.

Both work the same way (surfactant on soil particles), the only meaningful difference is how long the effect lasts before reapplication.

For a typical NZ home lawn with summer dry patch problems, a single Wetter 3W application in October to November carries through the worst of summer with one trip across the lawn. For lawns that don't have major dry patch issues, monthly Wetter tank-mixed with summer fertiliser applications is more cost-effective.

For commercial use (sports fields, golf courses) the choice often goes to longer-residual products like Wetter 3W for the labour savings.
