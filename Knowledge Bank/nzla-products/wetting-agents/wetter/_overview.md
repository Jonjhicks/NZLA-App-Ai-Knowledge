# NZLA Wetter

The premium standard NZLA liquid wetting agent. Heavy molecular weight, non-ionic, residual surfactant chemistry — longer-lasting and gentler on the lawn than the cheaper ionic wetting agents you'll find in garden centres.

## What it does

A lot of lawns develop hydrophobic patches over time. Sandy soils that have dried out hard, thatchy lawns with a waxy layer at the surface, lawns coming out of a long dry summer where the soil surface has become water-repellent. The result is the same — water sits on top, runs off, doesn't reach the root zone, and the lawn dries out even when you're irrigating.

NZLA Wetter is a non-ionic residual surfactant that breaks the hydrophobicity. The surfactant molecules have a dual-function structure. The hydrophobic end binds to dry, waxy soil particles. The hydrophilic end attracts and distributes water through the soil profile. Together they convert a water-repellent soil surface into one that takes water up evenly.

## Why the chemistry matters

Three characteristics of NZLA Wetter that separate it from cheap alternatives.

**Heavy molecular weight.** Bigger molecules don't leach out of the soil as quickly as small ones. That gives a longer residual effect — fewer applications needed across the season to keep the hydrophobicity under control.

**Non-ionic.** Ionic surfactants carry charge, which makes them more reactive on plant tissues. They can cause more leaf burn, especially in hot conditions. Non-ionic surfactants are gentler — lower burn risk at the same rate.

**EO/PO block copolymer structure.** The ethylene oxide / propylene oxide block copolymer is the specific surfactant family used in professional turf wetting agents because the molecular structure does both jobs (binding hydrophobic soil and attracting water) better than simpler surfactants.

The result is long-term prevention of localised dry spot, with low burn risk and good economy of application.

## Rate and application

Preventative or rescue rate: 250 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L of water. Wash in with 15 to 20 mm of irrigation. Repeat 7 to 10 days later for severe hydrophobicity.

Routine maintenance rate: 260 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water, applied every 30 days through the growing season.

## When to use

Lawns showing dry patch or hydrophobic spots that don't take up irrigation evenly.

Sandy soils after summer dry-down.

Coming into spring after winter dryness on north-facing or exposed lawns.

Routine through the growing season on lawns prone to hydrophobicity.

Around irrigation heads or sprinkler edges where coverage is uneven and dry patches develop.

## How it sits next to the others

**Wetter**: standard non-ionic residual liquid surfactant — long residual, low burn risk, the routine maintenance tool.

**Wetter 3W**: premium greens-grade three-way (penetrant, spreader, retainer) for high-performance turf and sand-based profiles.

**Wetter GR**: granular delivery for non-irrigated lawns and spreader application.

**Penetrate**: different chemistry, different job — moves water down through the profile rather than getting it through the surface in the first place.

**Aerate**: soil-amending polymer for clay and silt structure, different category entirely.

## When not to use

On already waterlogged lawns. If the problem is too much water, not too little entering, a wetting agent isn't the answer.

Mixed with hot tap water. Use clean cool water.

Without a follow-up irrigation. The product needs to be washed into the soil to do its job.

## Common mistakes

Skipping the wash-in irrigation. Without 15 to 20 mm of water moving the product into the soil, it sits on the surface and does nothing useful.

Treating it as a one-off rescue. Hydrophobicity rebuilds over time — monthly routine application keeps it in check.

Using on lawns where the problem isn't hydrophobic. If water enters the soil fine but pools at the surface, you need Penetrate, not Wetter.

Comparing against a cheap ionic wetting agent and expecting the same behaviour. The non-ionic residual chemistry costs more for a reason.

## Best paired with

Regular irrigation programme. The wetting agent works with even water application, not as a substitute for it.

A solid fertility programme. A well-fed dense lawn is less prone to hydrophobic patches than a thin patchy one.

NZLA Penetrate on heavy soils where both surface uptake (Wetter) and downward movement (Penetrate) need addressing.
