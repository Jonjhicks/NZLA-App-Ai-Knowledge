# NZLA Wetter GR

Granular three-way wetting agent. Same penetrant, spreader, and retainer chemistry as Wetter 3W, delivered through a granular carrier that releases as moisture breaks it down. The right tool for non-irrigated lawns and anywhere spreader application makes more sense than spraying.

## Why granular matters

The main reason to choose granular over liquid is irrigation-general. Liquid wetting agents need to be sprayed and then immediately washed in with 15 to 20 mm of irrigation-general to be effective. On lawns without a reliable irrigation-general system, that creates a problem — you apply the product and then have no control over whether it gets washed in.

Wetter GR solves that. Apply it with a spreader and it sits in the turf canopy until the next rainfall or dew. The moisture dissolves the carrier and releases the surfactant gradually into the soil. No need to control the irrigation-general step.

The gradual release also gives a steadier, more sustained wetting effect than a liquid hit. That suits routine prevention more than rescue application.

## Application

1.5 kg per 100m² every 4 weeks during the growing season.

Apply with a calibrated spreader in a cross pattern (half north-south, half east-west) for even coverage.

No irrigation-general required to activate the product. If irrigation-general is available, 10 to 15 mm will accelerate the release and move the active into the soil faster.

**Pack sizes:** 4.5 kg covers 300m². 7.5 kg covers 500m².

## When to use

Spring through autumn on lawns prone to dry patch, at four-week intervals as a programme.

- Non-irrigated lawns where liquid applications can't be reliably watered in.
- Larger lawns where granular spreader application is faster than spraying (lifestyle blocks, sports fields).
- Programmes where you want to combine wetting agent application with a granular fertiliser in a single pass. Co-applying with All Seasons at their respective rates covers nutrient and surfactant in one trip.
- Owners who don't have or don't want a sprayer setup.

The four-week interval is shorter than Wetter 3W's 12-week residual because granular release is slower and the surfactant builds up in the soil more gradually. Monthly applications maintain steady cover.

## Wetter GR vs Wetter 3W

Same three-way chemistry, different format.

- **Wetter 3W**: liquid, spray application, requires water-in, longer residual (12 weeks), lower cost per area.
- **Wetter GR**: granular, spreader application, no water-in required, monthly reapplication, suited for non-irrigated lawns and large areas.

For a home lawn with irrigation-general and a sprayer, Wetter 3W is cheaper and equally effective. Wetter GR is the right pick when granular delivery suits the situation — particularly on non-irrigated sites.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a fertiliser. It's a wetting agent, not a feed. Don't substitute it for All Seasons or any nutrient product.

Expecting immediate rescue results. The slow release makes it excellent for prevention but it won't sort out an active severe dry patch as fast as a liquid wetting agent. Use a liquid (Wetter or Wetter 3W) for urgent rescue.

Skipping the monthly reapplication. Monthly is the right pattern — more frequent than Wetter 3W because the granular release is slower.

## Where it sits in the range

Wetter GR is the spreader-application option in the wetting agent range. **Wetter** and **Wetter 3W** are the liquid equivalents for lawns with irrigation-general. **Penetrate** handles surface waterlogging (opposite problem). **Aerate** is the clay soil structure tool.
