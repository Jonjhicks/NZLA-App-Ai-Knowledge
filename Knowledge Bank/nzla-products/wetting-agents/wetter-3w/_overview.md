# NZLA Wetter 3W

The greens-grade premium wetting agent. Three-way action — penetrant, spreader, retainer — designed for high-performance turf and sand-based profiles where consistency of moisture across the entire root zone matters more than just getting water through the surface.

## What it does

Most wetting agents do one of three jobs: get water through a hydrophobic surface (penetrant), spread water evenly across and through the lawn (spreader), or hold water in the root zone (retainer). Cheap products do one. Mid-range products do two. Premium greens-grade chemistry does all three in a single application.

The **penetrant fraction** breaks through water-repellent soil so moisture can enter quickly when it rains or you irrigate. Without that, water beads up and runs off the surface.

The **spreader fraction** ensures water spreads laterally rather than running straight down through the patches that took it up first. On a sand-based profile or any well-drained soil, this is what stops water moving past the roots and being lost to the lower profile.

The **retainer fraction** holds moisture around the root zone for longer use by the plant. Less moisture lost to deep percolation means more efficient water use from every irrigation event.

The concentrated liquid format works immediately — granular wetting agents rely on carriers to slowly release the active, liquids hit the soil with the full surfactant load on application.

## Why the greens-grade chemistry matters

Sand-based root zones (golf greens, sports turf, premium home lawns built on a sand cap) are difficult for water management. The sand drains too quickly for standard wetting agents to do much, and the lack of organic matter means there's nothing holding moisture in the root zone naturally.

Greens-grade wetting agents are formulated to address all three of those issues at once. The penetrant gets water through the often-hydrophobic surface layer that sand profiles develop. The spreader distributes it laterally so the moisture covers the whole root zone, not just where the water entered. The retainer holds it there long enough for the plant to use.

For high-performance home lawns on sand, premium sports turf, or anywhere consistency of moisture is the goal, greens-grade is the right specification.

## Rate and application

Monthly application during the growing season, or before prolonged dry periods.

For best coverage use an 08 nozzle. Apply early in the day when conditions are calm so the spray pattern stays where you put it.

Water in after application, or apply right before steady rainfall to wash the product into the soil. Don't leave the product sitting on the leaf for extended periods without watering — the surfactant on hot foliage can cause burn.

## When to use

Sand-based root zones (golf greens, sports turf, premium home lawns built on sand).

High-performance turf where consistency of moisture across the lawn matters more than maximum value.

Through summer when water efficiency is at a premium and you want to get the most out of every irrigation event.

Pre-event preparation where the lawn needs to handle stress without browning out.

Hydrophobic lawns where standard wetting agents haven't fully resolved the problem.

## How it sits next to the others

**Wetter**: standard non-ionic residual liquid wetting agent. Routine and prevention. Good for most home lawns.

**Wetter 3W**: greens-grade premium three-way. For high-performance turf, sand-based profiles, and situations where standard wetting agents aren't quite cutting it.

**Wetter GR**: granular three-way for users who prefer spreader application and non-irrigated lawns.

**Penetrate**: different chemistry, different job — moves water down through the profile rather than getting it through the surface.

For most home users, Wetter does the job. Wetter 3W is the step up when the lawn is high-performance and the single-action chemistry isn't quite cutting it.

## When not to use

On already waterlogged lawns. Adding moisture retention to a saturated soil makes the problem worse.

Hot windy conditions where drift and rapid evaporation reduce effectiveness.

Without follow-up watering or rainfall.

## Common mistakes

Leaving the product on the leaf in hot sun without water-in. Surfactant burn is real.

Treating it as a one-off rescue. Monthly application is the right pattern.

Using it on lawns where the problem isn't hydrophobic. Won't help with surface pooling on waterlogged ground (Penetrate), and won't help with clay structure (Aerate).

Using greens-grade chemistry on a standard home lawn where Wetter would do the job at lower cost.

## Best paired with

Consistent irrigation programme through summer.

A solid fertility base. Healthy thick turf retains moisture better than a thin patchy one.

NZLA Penetrate on heavy soils where you want both surface uptake (Wetter 3W) and downward movement (Penetrate).
