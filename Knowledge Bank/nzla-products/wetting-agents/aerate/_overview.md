# NZLA Aerate

A professional-grade soil wetting agent and soil-amending polymer designed for heavy soils — clay and loam — where compaction, poor drainage and surface waterlogging are the real limiting factors. Different category to a standard wetting agent: Aerate works on the structure of the soil itself.

## What it does

Heavy clay and loam soils are difficult because the individual soil particles are so small they pack tightly together, leaving little space for water and air. The result is a soil that's either waterlogged or rock-hard, with very little of the open structure roots actually need to function.

A standard wetting agent doesn't help much on these soils because the problem isn't hydrophobicity at the surface. The problem is the soil itself. Aerate is built for this — it's part wetting agent (for water movement through the profile) and part soil-amending polymer (for binding the small particles into larger aggregates).

The polymer fraction is electrostatically attracted to clay and silt particles. The polymer strings then link individual particles into larger, more stable aggregates. That opens up porosity between the aggregates, gives roots air, lets water move through, and lets excess water actually drain rather than sitting on the surface.

## Why this is different to a wetting agent

A wetting agent makes water enter hydrophobic soil. Surfactant chemistry, surface-level effect.

Aerate changes the structure of the soil itself. The polymer binds particles together into aggregates, which permanently improves the soil's porosity and drainage. The change persists in the soil rather than disappearing once the product washes through.

That makes Aerate a slow-build product. You won't see overnight results because soil structure changes take weeks to months of consistent application to fully manifest. But the changes are durable, and over a season of monthly applications the clay or silt lawn becomes a different soil entirely.

## Rate and application

10 mL per 100m². A single 1 L bottle treats an entire hectare, which makes it economical even on larger areas.

Monthly application during the growing season, or as needed depending on soil condition.

Apply before rainfall, or water in well after application so the polymer moves into the top of the soil profile where the clay and silt particles are.

Can tank mix with NZLA Penetrate on clay and silt soils.

## When to use

Lawns on heavy clay or silt soils that hold water in winter and crack in summer.

Lawns where compaction is an ongoing issue.

Lawns where the surface stays boggy even when you've already addressed irrigation and drainage.

New lawn establishment on heavy soils as part of soil prep.

Long-term soil improvement programmes where mechanical aeration alone isn't sustaining the improvement.

## How it sits next to the others

**Aerate**: soil-structure tool for heavy soils. Polymer chemistry, slow-build, durable.

**Penetrate**: moves water down through the profile. Helps water leave the surface.

**Wetter** and **Wetter 3W**: standard surfactant wetting agents for hydrophobic soils. Surface chemistry, immediate effect.

On a heavy clay lawn, Aerate and Penetrate together is the standard combo — Aerate restructuring the soil, Penetrate moving water through it. They tank mix.

## When not to use

On sandy soils, where the problem is the opposite (water moves through too fast). Aerate is for clay and loam structure issues.

As a quick fix for waterlogging. It works over months of repeated application, not days.

Expecting it to replace mechanical aeration. Aerate complements cores and hollow tining, doesn't replace them.

## Common mistakes

Confusing it with a wetting agent. Different job entirely.

Skipping the water-in. The polymer needs to be in the soil to do its work.

Expecting overnight results. Soil structure changes take weeks to months of consistent application to show.

Using on sandy soils where there's no clay or silt fraction to work with.

## Best paired with

NZLA Penetrate for clay and silt soils. The pair handles structure (Aerate) and water movement (Penetrate) together.

Mechanical aeration to physically open the soil while Aerate works on the chemistry.

A solid base feed programme. A clay soil opened up with Aerate suddenly has much better fertiliser uptake.
