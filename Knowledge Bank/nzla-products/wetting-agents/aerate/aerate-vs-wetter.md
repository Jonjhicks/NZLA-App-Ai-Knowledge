# Should I use Aerate or Wetter?

Different problems, different products.

Wetter (and Wetter 3W) is for hydrophobic soil. Surface tension on dry coated particles repels water, the wetting agent breaks that surface tension and lets water soak in. Typical issue on sandy soils and dry-cycled organic-matter-rich soils through summer.

Aerate is for compacted soil. Dense soil with limited pore space resists water movement. The penetrant chemistry helps water move through whatever pore space exists. Typical issue on heavy clay soils, high-traffic lawns, and surface-sealed soils.

Decision tree:
- Water beads up on the surface and won't soak in: hydrophobic soil, use Wetter.
- Water pools or runs off after rain or irrigation: compacted soil, use Aerate.
- Both problems (common on clay-loam soils that go through dry-wet cycles): tank-mix Aerate and Wetter, or use Aerate as the base and Wetter 3W as a longer-residual partner.
- Sandy soil with dry patch: Wetter or Wetter 3W.
- Heavy clay with pooling: Aerate plus mechanical aeration.

The two products are complementary rather than substitutes. Tank-mixing them addresses both surface tension and compaction at the same time.

For most home lawn situations:
- North Island sandy soils: Wetter or Wetter 3W is the right pick.
- South Island clay soils: Aerate is the right pick.
- Mixed soil types or unsure: tank-mix both at lower rates of each.
