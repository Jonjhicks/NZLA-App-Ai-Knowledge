# What is NZLA Aerate used for?

Aerate is a penetrant wetting agent for compacted soils. Where standard Wetter breaks surface tension on hydrophobic soil particles, Aerate goes further by helping water break through dense compacted soil layers.

Where it fits:
- Heavy clay lawns with surface compaction.
- Lawns suffering from foot traffic compaction (high-use areas).
- Lawns where water pools on the surface after rain or irrigation rather than soaking in.
- Pre-aeration treatment to help mechanical aeration work into the soil.
- Post-aeration treatment to help water and nutrients move into the newly opened cores.

Mechanism: surfactant chemistry that reduces water's surface tension and helps it break through tight pore spaces in compacted soil. Not a chemical that decompacts the soil (no liquid does that), but it makes the most of whatever pore space exists by getting water moving through it.

Doesn't replace mechanical aeration. Compacted soil needs to be opened up physically with core or solid tine aeration. Aerate helps water move through the soil between aeration events but it's not a substitute for the mechanical work.

For typical home lawn pooling issues from clay or compacted soil, a programme of mechanical aeration once or twice a year plus regular Aerate applications through the wet months noticeably improves drainage and reduces surface pooling.
