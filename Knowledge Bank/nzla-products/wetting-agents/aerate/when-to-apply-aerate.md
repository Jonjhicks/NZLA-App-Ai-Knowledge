# When should I apply NZLA Aerate?

Year-round if compaction and drainage are persistent issues, monthly intervals through the active programme.

Specific timing:
- Pre-mechanical aeration: 1 to 2 weeks before scheduled core or solid tine aeration. Helps water soften the surface and reduces pulling damage during aeration.
- Post-mechanical aeration: within 1 to 2 weeks after aeration. Helps water move into the freshly opened cores and supports the drainage benefit.
- Through wet months: monthly applications keep water moving through clay or compacted soils.
- Through dry months: monthly applications combined with regular irrigation help water reach deeper soil layers.

Apply to a dry surface with a calibrated sprayer at label rate in 4 to 10 L water per 100 m². Water in with 5 to 10 mm of irrigation within 24 hours.

Don't apply during heavy rain or on saturated soil. The product needs to establish in the soil profile, not get washed off the surface.

Tank-mix friendly. Common partners:
- Aerate + Wetter or Wetter 3W = compacted hydrophobic situations
- Aerate + Humic+ = combined soil chemistry and structure support
- Aerate + summer fertiliser = nutrient movement through tight soil

For lawns with severe pooling or compaction, mechanical aeration once or twice a year plus monthly Aerate applications usually resolves the surface water issue over a season or two.
