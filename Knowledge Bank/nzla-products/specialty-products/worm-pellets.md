# NZLA Worm Pellets

Granular worm cast deterrent using the same camellia tea seed saponin active as NZLA Tea Saponin liquid. Spreader application, slower release, suits routine prevention on fine cut lawns and larger areas where spraying isn't practical.

## Same caveats as the liquid

Earthworms benefit lawns. They aerate the soil, cycle organic matter, improve drainage-and-soil-moisture, and support soil structure. Worm Pellets are for situations where surface casting is a genuine problem on fine turf. Normal home lawns at 30 to 50mm cutting height don't typically need this product. Rake the occasional cast off if it bothers you.

## How it works

The granular format dissolves with moisture and releases saponins into the soil. Earthworms move deeper to avoid the irritant chemistry, stopping new casts from forming at the surface. Effect starts building as the pellets dissolve and lasts approximately 2 weeks before worm activity resumes.

Water-in is essential. Dry pellets sitting on the surface release the active too slowly and the worms don't move.

## Rate and application

2 to 4 kg per 100m².

Apply with a calibrated spreader. Cross-pattern (half north-south, half east-west) for even coverage — uneven application produces treated and untreated strips which looks worse than no treatment at all.

Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation-general after application.

Programme use on fine turf: 2 kg per 100m² monthly through autumn (March to May) and spring (September to November).

Pre-event use: 3 to 4 kg per 100m² 5 to 7 days before the event. The granular format releases more slowly than liquid Tea Saponin, so more lead time is needed.

## Worm Pellets vs Tea Saponin

Same active, different operational considerations.

Tea Saponin (liquid): faster effect, better for quick pre-event application or targeted patches, requires sprayer.

Worm Pellets (granular): easier spreader application, suits routine programme use and larger areas, slower release.

Some users run Worm Pellets for routine seasonal management and Tea Saponin liquid for specific problem patches or pre-event sharpening.

## Common mistakes

Not watering in. The pellets don't dissolve and the active doesn't move where it needs to.

Applying during heavy rain. The granules can wash off the surface before breaking down properly.

Uneven spreader application. Visible strips.

Treating a normal home lawn that doesn't have a genuine casting problem.
