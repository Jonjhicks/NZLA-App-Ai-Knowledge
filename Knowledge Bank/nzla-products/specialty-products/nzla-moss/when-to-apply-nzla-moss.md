# When should I apply NZLA Moss?

When moss is actively growing. Spring and autumn are ideal (cool wet conditions when moss is at its strongest). Best results under warm, active moss growth.

Process:
1. Wet the affected area first (the chemistry works best on actively growing moss).
2. Apply NZLA Moss at the label rate.
3. Wait 5 to 15 minutes for the moss to brown.
4. Wait 7 to 14 days before scarifying or dethatching to remove the dead moss.
5. Address the underlying conditions (shade, drainage, fertility) to prevent re-establishment.

Standard rate: 200 mL per 10 L water per 100 m².
Severe rate: 500 mL per 10 L water per 100 m² for heavy infestations.

Apply in dry weather. Rain within 30 minutes washes the product off before it can kill the moss.

Avoid:
- Hot days above 28 °C (some leaf scorch risk on the surrounding grass).
- Frosty mornings (chemistry slows in very cold conditions).
- Highly wind drift onto desirable plants in nearby beds.

Don't apply repeatedly to the same area. Once you've killed the moss, the underlying causes need to be addressed. Reapplying NZLA Moss every few weeks without fixing the cause is treating the symptom.

For winter shade problems where you can't reduce the shade, accept moss as part of the lawn or replace that area with shade-tolerant ground cover. Lawn grasses won't compete with moss in heavy shade no matter how much chemistry you throw at it.
