# NZLA Moss

The contact moss killer. Benzalkonium chloride at 500 g/L, a quaternary ammonium compound that disrupts cell membranes on contact. Moss turns black within 5 to 15 minutes of being properly wetted with the product.

## What it does

Benzalkonium chloride is a contact biocide. It punches through the cell membranes of moss, lichen, liverwort and algae and kills them within minutes. The dead moss then turns black, dries out, and can be raked out.

It doesn't fix the underlying conditions that grew the moss in the first place. Damp, shaded, compacted, low-fertility lawns will grow moss back unless the conditions get addressed alongside the moss kill.

## Critical label note

NZLA Moss is registered for **fine turf and cotula only**, not for general lawn use. This is a meaningful restriction. The product is a strong biocide and the label limits intentional use to fine turf situations (greens, bowls).

The DANGER classification on the label is real, severe skin burns and serious eye damage from concentrate contact. PPE is non-negotiable.

## Rate and application

Standard infestation: 200 mL per 10 L of water per 100m².

Severe infestation: 500 mL per 10 L of water per 100m².

Wet the affected area first so the moss is rehydrated and the active makes contact. Spray. Best results under warm, active growth, the moss takes up the active fastest when it's metabolically active.

Don't immediately follow with irrigation, the active needs to stay on the moss.

## Cultural fix is half the job

Killing moss without addressing the underlying conditions just means the moss comes back. Conditions that grow moss:

- Shade (overhanging trees, structures blocking sun)
- Compacted soil (pull cores, open it up)
- Wet soil that won't dry (drainage, Penetrate)
- Low fertility (feed the lawn, give the grass a competitive edge)
- Mowing too short (lift the height, denser canopy, less light to soil surface)

NZLA Moss kills what's there. Fixing the conditions stops it returning.

## When not to use

On general lawns where the label restricts it.

On stressed turf.

On young or establishing turf, the biocide is too aggressive for fragile grass.

Near ponds, drains or waterways, very toxic to aquatic life.

Without PPE. Severe skin and eye damage are not theoretical with this product.

## Common mistakes

Applying without addressing the underlying conditions. Moss comes back within months.

Skipping PPE. The DANGER label is real.

Spraying on dry moss. The product needs to contact actively growing moss for best kill.

Following up with heavy irrigation. Washes the product off before it works.

Using on stressed or young turf.

## Best paired with

NZLA Penetrate to move surface moisture down through the profile, which dries out the surface and reduces moss-friendly conditions.

Cultural fixes: lifting mowing height, opening shade where possible, pulling cores on compacted areas, feeding the lawn so the grass outcompetes the moss.
