# What's the rate and safety information for NZLA Moss?

Rate:
- Standard: 200 mL per 10 L water per 100 m².
- Severe infestation: 500 mL per 10 L water per 100 m².

Safety:
- Benzalkonium chloride is a quaternary ammonium compound. Mildly toxic to aquatic life and skin/eye irritant at concentrate.
- Wear gloves, eye protection, long sleeves when mixing and spraying.
- Keep pets and people off the treated area until it's dried.
- Don't apply near waterways or stormwater drains, the chemistry is toxic to aquatic invertebrates.
- HSNO code HSR000822 (consult the SDS for full hazard information).

Practical application:
- Wet the moss before applying so it's actively growing.
- Spray to thorough coverage of the affected area.
- The moss browns within 5 to 15 minutes.
- Don't reapply within 14 days, the chemistry needs time to work.

Don't tank-mix with foliar fertilisers or other lawn products. The benzalkonium chloride is a strong contact biocide and reacts with most things. Apply NZLA Moss as a standalone treatment, then return to normal foliar programmes 7 to 14 days later.

After moss is killed, scarifying or hand-raking removes the dead material. Reseed any bare patches that result.

Address the underlying cause:
- Shade: trim back overhanging trees, reduce shade where possible.
- Drainage: improve surface drainage, address waterlogged conditions.
- Compaction: aerate to reduce surface compaction.
- Fertility: feed the lawn to outcompete moss return.
