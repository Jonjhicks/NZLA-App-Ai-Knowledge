# NZLA Moss

Contact moss killer. Benzalkonium chloride at 500 g/L. Kills moss, lichen, liverwort, and algae on contact, with visible browning in 5 to 15 minutes. **Registered for fine turf and cotula only** — this is a meaningful label restriction, not a formality.

## What it does

Benzalkonium chloride is a quaternary ammonium compound that punches through cell membranes on contact. Dead moss turns black, dries out, and can be raked out. Fast and effective on actively growing moss.

It doesn't fix what grew the moss. Damp, shaded, compacted, low-fertility lawns will have moss back within weeks unless the underlying conditions get addressed.

## Critical label notes

**Registered for fine turf and cotula only.** Not for general lawn use. Read the current label.

**DANGER classification** — severe skin burns and serious eye damage from concentrate contact. This isn't a caution label, it's a danger label.

**PPE is non-negotiable.** Gloves, sealed safety glasses or goggles, long sleeves. Commercial use requires a qualified handler.

Very toxic to aquatic life. Don't apply near ponds, drains, or waterways.

## Rate and application

Standard infestation: 200 mL per 10 L of water per 100m².

Severe infestation: 500 mL per 10 L of water per 100m².

Wet the affected area first so moss is rehydrated and the active makes contact. Apply to actively growing moss for best kill. Don't irrigate immediately after — the active needs to stay on the moss.

Don't tank-mix with foliar fertilisers or other lawn products. Apply standalone, then return to normal programmes 7 to 14 days later.

## After the kill

Wait 7 to 14 days then scarify or rake out the dead material. Reseed any bare patches.

Address the underlying cause or the moss comes back.

Conditions that grow moss: shade, compacted soil, poor drainage-and-soil-moisture, low fertility, mowing too short. NZLA Moss kills what's there. Cultural correction stops it returning. Combining moss kill with NZLA Penetrate to dry out the surface moisture can help.

## Common mistakes

Treating without addressing the conditions. Moss returns within months.

Skipping PPE. The DANGER classification is accurate.

Spraying on dry dormant moss. The product needs actively growing moss to contact for best kill.

Irrigating immediately after application.

Using on stressed or establishing turf. The biocide is too aggressive.
