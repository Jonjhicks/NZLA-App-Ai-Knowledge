# NZLA Tea Saponin

Liquid worm cast deterrent from camellia tea seed extract. Saponins irritate earthworms and drive them deeper into the soil or off the surface, reducing casting at the turf surface. Used where surface casting is a genuine problem on fine cut lawns.

## Say it first

Earthworms benefit lawns. They aerate the soil, cycle organic matter, improve drainage-and-soil-moisture, and build soil biology. NZLA's own position is that worms should be encouraged in home lawns, not managed out.

Tea Saponin is for the situations where casting on the surface creates a real problem. That's primarily fine cut greens (bowling, golf) and high-quality home lawns where surface casts affect roll, mowing quality, or aesthetics at a level that matters. On a standard home lawn at 30 to 50mm mowing height, worm casts usually aren't worth treating. Rake them off if they bother you.

## How it works

Saponins are natural compounds from Camellia oleifera seed that are surface-active and irritant to worms. Earthworms exposed at the soil surface burrow deeper to get away from it. Result: no new casts forming at the treated surface. The effect lasts around 2 weeks, then worms return to normal activity.

The chemistry is organic and breaks down naturally in soil. Low residual, low environmental risk.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 8 to 15 L of carrier water.

Water in immediately after application with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation-general. The saponins need to move into the soil to reach where the worms are working. Surface application without water-in is largely ineffective.

Repeat at 14 days if casting hasn't reduced satisfactorily.

For pre-event use: apply 3 to 5 days before the event. Peak effect is within a week of treatment.

For programme use on fine turf: every 4 to 6 weeks through autumn and spring (peak casting seasons).

## Tea Saponin vs Worm Pellets

Same active chemistry, different formats.

Tea Saponin (liquid): spray application, faster effect (worms move within hours), suits targeted or pre-event use.

Worm Pellets (granular): spreader application, slower release, suits routine programme use without setting up a sprayer.

Both deliver the same outcome. The choice is operational.

## Common mistakes

Treating a standard home lawn that doesn't actually have a casting problem.

Skipping the water-in. The active needs to move into the soil, not sit on the surface.

Expecting permanent suppression. Worm activity rebuilds after 2 weeks, sustained treatment is the approach.

Applying during heavy rain. Saponins wash off the surface before they can move into the soil at the right concentration.
