# NZLA Sticker

Organosilicone spreader-sticker adjuvant for herbicide tank mixes. Dramatically reduces spray droplet surface tension so the spray flattens and absorbs into waxy or hairy leaves rather than beading off. Used specifically with glyphosate and metsulfuron.

## Why it matters

A lot of herbicide failure isn't the chemistry not working, it's the spray not getting onto the leaf properly. Waxy  (some thistles, plantain, dock, kikuyu, paspalum) and hairy-leafed plants shed spray droplets before the active has time to absorb.

Organosilicone surfactants reduce surface tension an order of magnitude more effectively than standard non-ionic surfactants. The spray droplet collapses flat across the leaf surface, spreads into stomata, and the herbicide gets in. Uptake improves substantially.

Rainfast in around 30 minutes on dry plants, which is unusually fast for an adjuvant.

## Rate and application

10 to 20 mL per 10 L of spray solution.

Add when the tank is 90% full, not earlier. Adding to a less-full tank causes excessive foaming that makes the mix hard to manage.

Sequence: half-fill with water, add herbicide, top up to 90%, add Sticker, top up to full, agitate, spray.

## Safety

This is a concentrated organosilicone. Toxic if inhaled, wear a P2/P3 mask when mixing and spraying. Serious eye irritation risk, wear sealed goggles or safety glasses. Skin irritation possible, wear chemical-resistant gloves and long sleeves. Ecotoxic, don't apply near waterways or drains.

Commercial use requires a qualified handler.

## Where it fits

Glyphosate on tough waxy  (gorse, broom, blackberry, kikuyu, paspalum being killed off pre-renovation, mature thistles).

Metsulfuron on tough perennial broadleaf  where standard coverage isn't delivering.

Pre-renovation lawn kill where you want one application to do the job.

Total weed kill on hard surfaces or edges where maximum uptake matters.

## Where it doesn't fit

With selective broadleaf herbicides (BWC, Gold) at standard rates. The selective chemistry is formulated for adequate uptake without additional penetration enhancement. Adding Sticker can push uptake to the point of turf damage.

With fungicide tank mixes. Fungicide chemistry uses its own surfactant systems.

In windy conditions. The very low surface tension means drift carries further than standard sprays.

## Common mistakes

Using as a general wetting agent. Sticker is an adjuvant for herbicide leaf uptake, not a soil wetter.

Adding too early in the mix. Foaming.

Skipping PPE.

Using with selective lawn herbicides at standard rates without understanding the increased penetration implications.
