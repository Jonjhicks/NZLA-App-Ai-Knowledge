# What are NZLA Worm Pellets used for?

A granular worm cast deterrent. Same camellia tea seed saponin chemistry as Tea Saponin (liquid), delivered through a granular carrier that releases over time as the granules break down with moisture.

Where it fits:
- Fine cut lawns where worm casts disrupt the surface (bowling greens, putting greens, premium home lawns).
- Non-irrigated lawns or larger areas where spray application isn't practical.
- Programme use alongside granular fertiliser applications.

Same caveats as Tea Saponin liquid:
- Earthworms benefit lawns. Use only where surface casting is a genuine problem.
- Doesn't kill the worms, drives them deeper for a few weeks.
- For typical home lawns at 30 to 50 mm cutting height, not necessary.

Apply at 2 to 4 kg per 100 m² with a spreader. Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation. The granules release the saponins as they break down with moisture, driving worms off the surface.

For routine programme use on fine turf, monthly applications at 2 kg per 100 m² through autumn and spring (the worm casting peak seasons) maintain low surface casting. For one-off pre-event use, single application 5 to 7 days before the event.

Don't use as a routine application on a standard home lawn. The worms are doing useful work and the casts can be raked off if they're aesthetic issues.
