# What's the application rate for Worm Pellets?

2 to 4 kg per 100 m² depending on use.

For programme maintenance on fine turf: 2 kg per 100 m² monthly through worm casting seasons (autumn and spring).

For one-off pre-event applications: 3 to 4 kg per 100 m² 5 to 7 days before the event.

Practical points:
- Use a calibrated spreader. Hand broadcasting at this rate is hard to do evenly on anything over about 50 m².
- Cross-pattern application (half north-south, half east-west) for even coverage.
- Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation after application.
- Don't apply during heavy rain (granules can wash off before they break down).

Don't double the rate to extend the interval. Saponin chemistry works at the rate it's designed for, higher rates don't extend the effect meaningfully.

For a 250 m² fine turf area at the programme rate (2 kg per 100 m²), you're using 5 kg per application. Across an autumn and spring programme of 6 applications, that's about 30 kg of product for the season.

Compared to Tea Saponin liquid: more product needed by weight for the same effect, but no sprayer required and no need to water in beyond a single irrigation cycle. The cost difference depends on which packaging and which operational setup suits your situation.
