# When should I apply Worm Pellets?

When worm casts are disrupting the surface and you've decided the cosmetic management is worth the programme. Autumn and spring are peak worm casting in NZ, those are the windows where Worm Pellets earns its keep.

Programme approach for fine turf:
- Monthly applications through autumn (March to May) and spring (September to November) at 2 kg per 100 m².
- Less frequent through summer (worms are deeper in dry conditions) or winter (cold slows casting).

One-off pre-event use:
- Apply 5 to 7 days before the event at 3 to 4 kg per 100 m².
- The slower granular release means more lead time than the liquid Tea Saponin.
- Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation.

Don't apply:
- On newly seeded or freshly established lawn.
- As a routine treatment on a standard home lawn that doesn't need it.
- During heavy rain (granules can wash off the lawn surface before they break down).
- On saturated soil (granules won't release effectively without proper soil contact).

Use a calibrated spreader and split the rate (half north-south, half east-west) for even coverage. Uneven application produces strips where worms continue casting alongside strips where they're driven off, which looks worse than no treatment.

Organic chemistry, low environmental risk. Saponins break down naturally in soil within weeks.
