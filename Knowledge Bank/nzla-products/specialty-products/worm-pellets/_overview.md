# NZLA Worm Pellets

The granular version of NZLA's worm cast deterrent. Same active as Tea Saponin (camellia tea seed extract) just in pellet form. Suits routine surface management on fine turf, and spreader application on larger areas where liquid spraying isn't practical.

## What it does

The saponin active works the same way as the liquid Tea Saponin, irritating earthworms so they burrow deeper or move off the treated area. Granular delivery means the active releases gradually as the pellets break down, which suits sustained low-level suppression more than rescue application.

## Worth knowing first

Earthworms generally benefit lawns. They aerate the soil, cycle organic matter, improve drainage, and build healthy soil structure. NZLA's own position is that worms should be encouraged in home lawns, not controlled.

Worm Pellets are for the situations where casting on the surface is a genuine problem. Fine cut lawns (bowling greens, putting greens) where casts affect roll, mowing or scarifying. On a normal home lawn at 30 to 50 mm mowing height, casts usually aren't a problem worth treating.

## Rate and application

2 to 4 kg per 100m².

Apply via spreader for even coverage.

Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation to start the pellets dissolving and move the active into the soil.

## When to use

Fine cut lawns (bowling, golf, premium home lawns) with casting that affects the surface.

Heavy worm cast periods (autumn through winter on cool damp soils).

Routine prevention on lawns prone to casting issues.

Larger areas where granular spreader application is faster than liquid spraying.

## How it sits next to Tea Saponin

Tea Saponin (liquid) is for quick application and precise targeting. Faster effect, but needs spraying.

Worm Pellets (granular) is for routine prevention and easier application via spreader. Slower release, useful for low-level ongoing suppression.

Some users alternate between the two, granular for routine, liquid for specific problem patches.

## When not to use

On general home lawns where worms aren't causing a real problem.

As a routine "in case" treatment. Wait until casts are visibly affecting the surface.

Without water-in.

## Common mistakes

Treating a normal home lawn that doesn't actually have a worm cast problem.

Skipping the water-in. The pellets need moisture to dissolve and release the active.

Expecting one application to fix it. Worm activity rebuilds over weeks, sustained treatment is the pattern.

## Best paired with

Tea Saponin liquid for quick spot treatment alongside Worm Pellets for routine cover.

Mechanical cast management (brushing) on lawns where casting is severe.
