# When should I use Sticker?

Specific situations where coverage and uptake matter:

- Glyphosate on hard-to-kill weeds (mature gorse, broom, blackberry, mature thistles, established kikuyu or paspalum being killed off pre-renovation).
- Metsulfuron on tough perennial broadleaves.
- Spot spraying with non-selective herbicide where you need maximum penetration into waxy leaves.
- Pre-renovation lawn kill where you want one application to do the job rather than a follow-up.

How to use:
- Add 10 to 20 mL of Sticker per 10 L of spray solution.
- Add when the tank is 90% full (reduces foaming).
- Agitate thoroughly.
- Spray within a few hours of mixing.

Don't use:
- With most selective broadleaf herbicides at label rates (BWC, Gold). The selective chemistry doesn't need the added penetration and Sticker can increase damage risk to the lawn.
- With fungicide tank mixes (fungicide chemistry uses its own surfactant systems).
- With fertiliser-only tank mixes.

For typical home lawn herbicide use, Sticker isn't part of the standard kit. It's a specialised adjuvant for non-selective control and tough weeds. If you're using it, you're usually doing a specific job (clearing a section for re-establishment, killing off existing turf before renovation, knocking back tough edge weeds).
