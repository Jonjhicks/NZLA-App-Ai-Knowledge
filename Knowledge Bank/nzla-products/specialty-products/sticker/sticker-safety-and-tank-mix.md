# What are the safety and tank-mix considerations for Sticker?

Safety:
- Organosilicone surfactant, concentrated form.
- Toxic if inhaled. Wear a respirator or P2/P3 mask when mixing and spraying.
- Serious eye irritation. Wear sealed safety glasses or goggles.
- Skin irritation possible. Wear chemical-resistant gloves and long sleeves.
- Ecotoxic. Don't apply near waterways or stormwater drains.
- HSNO code HSR002503 (consult the SDS for full hazard information).

Tank-mix order:
1. Half-fill tank with water, start agitation.
2. Add herbicide (glyphosate, metsulfuron, etc.).
3. Top up to 90% of full volume.
4. Add Sticker.
5. Top up to full volume.
6. Agitate, spray within a few hours.

Adding Sticker when the tank is 90% full reduces foaming, which is a real issue with organosilicone adjuvants. Adding it earlier or to an empty tank can cause excessive foam that interferes with mixing and application.

Don't combine with:
- Most selective broadleaf herbicides (BWC, Gold, Halo) - the chemistry doesn't need it and increased penetration can damage the lawn.
- Fungicides - they use their own surfactant systems.
- Foliar fertilisers - the strong surfactant can cause issues with nutrient absorption.

If you're using Sticker, you're typically running a focused non-selective herbicide application. Keep the tank mix simple, herbicide plus Sticker, nothing else.
