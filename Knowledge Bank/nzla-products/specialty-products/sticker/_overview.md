# NZLA Sticker

An organosilicone adjuvant for herbicide tank mixes. Dramatically reduces spray droplet surface tension, which gets the active onto and into waxy or hairy leaves that standard sprays bead off. Used with glyphosate and metsulfuron specifically.

## What it does

A lot of weed herbicide failure isn't the chemistry not working, it's the spray not getting onto the leaf surface properly. Waxy weeds (some thistles, plantain, dock) and hairy weeds shed spray droplets before the active has time to absorb.

Organosilicone surfactants are an order of magnitude more effective at reducing surface tension than standard non-ionic surfactants. The spray droplet collapses flat across the leaf surface, spreads into the leaf's stomata, and the herbicide gets in. Uptake goes up dramatically.

This isn't a wetting agent for soil. It's specifically for spray droplet behaviour on plant leaves during herbicide application.

## Rate and application

10 to 20 mL per 10 L of spray solution.

Add to the tank when it's 90% full. Adding earlier or to a less-full tank causes excessive foaming.

Rainfast 30 minutes on dry plants, which is unusually fast. The droplet flattens and starts absorbing immediately.

## When to use

Glyphosate or metsulfuron herbicide applications on tough weeds.

Waxy or hairy-leafed weeds where standard application has failed.

Total weed kill on hard surfaces or in beds where complete uptake matters.

Pre-renovation kill of an existing lawn before reseeding.

## When not to use

In lawn-selective herbicides where penetration is already adequate. Adding Sticker to a BWC application risks accelerating uptake into the desirable lawn too, potentially causing damage.

In non-glyphosate, non-metsulfuron herbicide tanks without checking compatibility.

Without PPE. Eye irritation and inhalation risk are real.

In windy conditions, the very low surface tension means drift carries further than standard sprays.

## Common mistakes

Using it as a general wetting agent. It's an adjuvant for herbicide leaf uptake, not a soil wetting agent.

Adding too early in the mix. Excessive foaming makes the tank hard to manage.

Skipping PPE. Organosilicones are not benign.

Using in lawn-selective herbicides where the standard adjuvant load is already correct.

## Best paired with

Glyphosate for total kill (pre-renovation, hard surface weed control).

Metsulfuron for tough broadleaf control.

Indicate (the colourant) so you can see exactly where the more aggressive spray pattern has gone.
