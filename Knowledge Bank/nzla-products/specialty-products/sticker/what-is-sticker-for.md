# What is NZLA Sticker used for?

A spreader-sticker adjuvant for herbicide tank mixes. Organosilicone-based, dramatically reduces spray droplet surface tension, improves coverage on waxy or hard-to-wet leaves, improves penetration and uptake of glyphosate and metsulfuron.

Where it fits:
- Glyphosate applications where the target weeds have waxy leaves (gorse, blackberry, broom, kikuyu, paspalum, mature thistles).
- Metsulfuron applications on tough weeds.
- Selective herbicide applications where the leaf surface is making coverage hard.

What you'll see:
- Spray droplets spread out across the leaf rather than beading up.
- Stronger and faster herbicide effect at the same label rate.
- Better control on weeds that have been difficult to kill with herbicide alone.

Rainfast in around 30 minutes on dry plants. The chemistry binds the spray droplet to the leaf so rain washes off less product.

Safety: this is a concentrated organosilicone. Toxic if inhaled, ecotoxic, serious eye irritation. Wear PPE (mask, gloves, eye protection, long sleeves). Avoid contact with skin and eyes. Don't apply near waterways.

For most home lawn herbicide applications (selective broadleaf herbicides like BWC, Gold, or sedge herbicides like Halo), Sticker isn't necessary. The standard label rates account for typical coverage requirements. Sticker comes in for tougher non-selective control (glyphosate on weeds in driveways, edges, pre-renovation kill) or where the target has been hard to control with standard chemistry alone.
