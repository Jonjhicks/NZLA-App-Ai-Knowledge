# NZLA Green

A turf pigment that's structurally similar to chlorophyll. Adds dark green colour to the lawn without forcing growth, even on turf that's dormant or too stressed to produce chlorophyll. The chlorophyll-like structure also lifts light absorption, giving a small genuine photosynthetic benefit under stress.

## Two effects

**Aesthetic.** NZLA Green coats the leaf with a green pigment. The colour is visible immediately from the spray. No growth required, no metabolism required. Lasts 4 to 8 weeks depending on mowing frequency.

**Functional.** Because the pigment absorbs light in similar wavelengths to chlorophyll, the leaf gets more photosynthetic light energy than it would without it. This is most useful on dormant or struggling turf where natural chlorophyll levels are low — the pigment helps the plant capture the light it can't fully use through its own chemistry.

## NZLA Green vs Iron+

Iron+ is a nutrient. The plant uses the iron to make chlorophyll, which produces the green colour. The lawn needs to be actively growing and taking up nutrients. Response in 1 to 3 days on an active lawn.

NZLA Green is a pigment. No plant metabolism required. Immediate colour from the spray.

Use Iron+ when the lawn is actively growing and just needs a colour boost. Use NZLA Green when the lawn isn't going to deliver colour through its own chemistry in time — dormant warm-season turf in winter, stressed lawn before an event, recovery situations where the plant can't respond to iron yet.

The two can be tank-mixed. NZLA Green delivers immediate colour, Iron+ builds the chlorophyll response underneath over the following days.

## Rate and application

Initial application: 12 mL per 100m².

Maintenance: 11 to 15 mL per 100m².

Minimum 300 L of water per hectare (3 L per 100m²) for even coverage.

Tank-mix compatible with most products.

Don't mow immediately after application. Most of the pigment is on the leaf surface — mowing removes it into the catcher.

## Key restriction

Avoid overspray onto concrete, masonry, or paving. The pigment stains hard surfaces. Spray on calm days, mask off edges on tight spaces.

## When to use

Warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) going tan over winter dormancy.

Pre-event preparation where you need colour within 24 to 48 hours and Iron+ won't deliver fast enough.

Stressed lawns that can't green up naturally but where appearance matters.

Stripe enhancement for photography.

## What it doesn't do

Fix underlying problems. If the lawn is discoloured because of or nutrient deficiency, NZLA Green will make it look better while the problem continues underneath. Treat the problem, use the pigment for the appearance angle only.
