# Should I use Tea Saponin or Worm Pellets?

Same active (camellia tea seed saponins), different formats.

Tea Saponin: liquid. Spray application. Faster effect (worms move within hours). Requires sprayer setup.

Worm Pellets: granular. Spread with a spreader. Slower release (effect builds over days as the granules break down). No sprayer needed.

Decision tree:
- Quick result needed for an upcoming event: Tea Saponin (liquid).
- Routine programme application without setting up the sprayer: Worm Pellets (granular).
- Non-irrigated lawn or limited water availability: Worm Pellets (less water-dependent than liquid).
- Want to combine with other liquid applications: Tea Saponin tank-mix.
- Have a regular granular fertiliser programme and want to combine: Worm Pellets co-applied.

Both products work toward the same outcome (driving worms deeper, reducing surface casting). The choice is operational rather than effect-based.

For most home lawn use, neither is necessary. The product is for fine turf and event-driven situations where surface casts genuinely matter. If you're not in that situation, leave the worms to do their useful work and rake off any casts that bother you.
