# NZLA Tea Saponin

The organic option for suppressing worm casts on fine turf. Camellia tea seed extract (saponins) irritates earthworms and drives them off the surface, without the broad-spectrum impact of synthetic chemistry. Worth being clear up front, worms are usually good for lawns, this is for the specific cases where casting on the surface causes problems.

## What it does

Saponins are natural compounds found in tea seed (Camellia oleifera and related species). They're surface-active and irritant to worms, so earthworms exposed to saponins at the soil surface burrow deeper or avoid the treated area entirely. Result, no casts pushed up onto the turf.

The chemistry is organic and breaks down naturally. Low residual, low environmental risk compared to synthetic worm controls.

## Worth knowing first

Earthworms generally benefit lawns. They aerate the soil, cycle organic matter, improve drainage, and contribute to healthy soil structure. NZLA's own position is that worms should be encouraged in home lawns, not controlled.

Tea Saponin is for the situations where casting on the surface is a genuine problem. That's primarily fine cut lawns (bowling greens, putting greens) where casts on the surface affect roll, scarify machinery, or mowing quality. On a normal home lawn at 30 to 50mm mowing height, worm casts usually aren't a problem worth treating.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m².

Water in with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation to move the active down to where the worms are.

If worm activity hasn't reduced after 14 days, repeat the application. Sometimes a second hit is needed to drive a population off the surface.

## When to use

Fine cut bowling greens, putting greens, or premium home lawns where casting visibly affects the playing or aesthetic surface.

Heavy worm cast periods (autumn through winter, on cool damp soils).

When mechanical management (brushing casts back into the lawn) isn't keeping up.

## When not to use

On general lawns where worms aren't causing a problem. They're doing more good than harm.

As a routine "in case" treatment. Wait until casts are actually a visible problem.

Without irrigation to move the active in.

## Common mistakes

Treating a normal lawn for worm casts. Most lawns don't need this product.

Skipping the water-in. Surface application without moving the saponins into the soil is largely ineffective.

Expecting a permanent solution. Worms come back, the product reduces casting for a window, then repeat treatment is needed.

## Best paired with

Worm Pellets as a programme option. Liquid Tea Saponin for quick application, granular Worm Pellets for sustained release between treatments.

Mechanical cast management (brushing) on lawns where the chemistry alone isn't fully resolving the problem.
