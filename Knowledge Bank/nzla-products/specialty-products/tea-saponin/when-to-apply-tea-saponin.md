# When should I apply Tea Saponin?

When worm casts are a problem at the surface. Autumn and spring are peak worm casting times in NZ, the surface becomes lumpy with casts in cool moist conditions.

Apply at 200 to 400 mL per 100 m² in 8 to 15 L of carrier water. Water in straight after with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation. The saponins need to move down into the soil to drive the worms off the surface.

Effect lasts around 2 weeks. Repeat application at 14 days if needed to maintain the effect through a critical period.

For event-driven use:
- Apply 3 to 5 days before the event.
- The worms move off the surface and casts stop forming.
- Surface quality stays clean for the event.

For programme use on fine turf:
- Apply at 4 to 6 week intervals through autumn and spring when worm casting is active.
- Less frequent or no application through summer (worms are deeper anyway in dry conditions) or winter (cold slows casting).

Don't apply:
- On newly seeded or freshly established lawn (worms are part of the establishment ecology).
- As a routine treatment on a standard home lawn where worm casts aren't disrupting use.
- Repeatedly without addressing whether the surface management actually needs it.

Organic chemistry, low environmental risk. The saponins break down naturally over weeks. Doesn't kill the worms or harm soil biology.
