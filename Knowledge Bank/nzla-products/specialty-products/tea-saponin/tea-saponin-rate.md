# What's the application rate for Tea Saponin?

200 to 400 mL per 100 m² in 8 to 15 L of water.

Practical points:
- Carrier water is higher than typical foliar applications because the product needs to move into the soil with the irrigation.
- Apply, then water in immediately with 8 to 15 mm of irrigation.
- Repeat at 14 days if effect needs to be maintained.

For event-driven use (one-off): 3 to 5 days before the event at 300 to 400 mL per 100 m².

For programme use through autumn or spring on fine turf: 200 mL per 100 m² at 4 to 6 week intervals.

Don't double the rate to extend the interval. Saponin chemistry works at the rate it's designed for, higher rates can stress nearby plants and don't extend the effect duration meaningfully.

For a 250 m² fine turf area at mid-rate (300 mL per 100 m²), you're using around 750 mL per application. A 5 L bottle covers about 6 to 7 applications.

The chemistry is organic-natured with low environmental risk. The saponins break down naturally in soil over weeks. Doesn't accumulate in soil or move into waterways at significant levels at label rates.
