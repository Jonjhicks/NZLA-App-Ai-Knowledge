# What is NZLA Tea Saponin used for?

A natural worm cast deterrent. Liquid extract from camellia tea seeds, containing saponins that irritate earthworms and drive them deeper into the soil or off the lawn surface. Reduces worm casting at the surface.

Where it fits:
- Fine cut lawns where worm casts disrupt the surface (bowling greens, putting greens, premium home lawns).
- Lawns prepared for events where surface quality matters.
- High-input lawns where casts mow into muddy smears that affect appearance.

Also has some activity against slugs and snails (secondary target).

Important context: earthworms generally benefit lawns. They process organic matter, aerate the soil and improve soil biology. NZLA Tea Saponin reduces surface casting but doesn't kill the worms. It drives them deeper for a few weeks while the saponins are active. The soil ecology benefit continues, just without the surface mess.

For typical home lawns at 30 to 50 mm cutting height, worm casts aren't a real problem and using Tea Saponin isn't necessary. The product is for situations where the surface quality genuinely matters (golf, bowls, premium fine lawns, photo shoots) and the casts are disruptive.

Don't use as a routine application on a standard home lawn. Worm casts can be raked or mowed off without affecting lawn health, and the worms are doing useful work underneath.
