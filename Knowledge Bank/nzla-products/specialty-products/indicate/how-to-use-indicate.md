# How do I use Indicate?

Add to the tank-mix at 8 to 10 mL per litre of spray solution. Add it last, after all other chemicals are mixed in.

For a typical 10 L sprayer load that's 80 to 100 mL of Indicate.

Sequence:
1. Half-fill the tank with water, start agitation.
2. Add herbicide, fungicide, fertiliser or whatever else is going in.
3. Top up the tank to full volume.
4. Add Indicate last.
5. Agitate, spray.

Why add it last: the colourant can react with some chemicals if mixed before they're properly dispersed. Adding last reduces that risk.

What you'll see:
- A green tint to the spray solution.
- Green colouration on the leaf where you've sprayed, visible for a few hours to a couple of days.
- The colour mows off within 2 or 3 cuts.

Don't use on edible crops (vegetables, herbs). The indicator isn't food-safe.

For home lawn use, one application of Indicate in a 5 L sprayer mixed at 50 mL would cover most jobs. A 1 L bottle of Indicate covers many applications, the rate is low because the visible effect is at low concentration.

If you've spilled Indicate on concrete or paving, rinse with water as soon as possible. Set-in colourant can be harder to remove later.
