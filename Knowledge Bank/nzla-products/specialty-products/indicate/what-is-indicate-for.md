# What is NZLA Indicate used for?

A spray pattern indicator. Add it to a tank mix and the spray shows up green on the lawn so you can see exactly where you've sprayed.

Why bother:
- Avoid missing patches. Selective herbicides and fungicides are wasted on areas you skipped over.
- Avoid double-application. Going over the same patch twice burns the lawn or wastes product.
- Verify coverage. You can see the spray pattern of your nozzle, check for blocked nozzles or uneven pressure.
- Useful when learning to spray. New users get a visible record of their stripe pattern.

The colour is temporary. Mows off within 2 or 3 cuts. Doesn't stain skin or shoes if used at label rate.

Mostly used with:
- Selective herbicides where coverage matters and re-treatment is hard.
- Fungicides where missed patches can let disease re-establish.
- Tank-mix applications with multiple components, where missing a patch wastes a lot of product.

Add Indicate last in the tank-mix sequence (after all other chemicals), at 8 to 10 mL per litre of spray solution. Don't use on edible crops.

For most home lawn use, the standard herbicide and fungicide applications don't really need an indicator, the user knows where they've sprayed. For larger areas or commercial applications, the small extra cost is worth the assurance.
