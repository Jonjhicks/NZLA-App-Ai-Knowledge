# NZLA Indicate

A non-staining green colourant added to spray tanks to show exactly where the spray's landed. No active pesticide, no nutrient, just a visual aid. Saves wasted product and uneven application.

## What it does

The single biggest mistake in lawn spraying is uneven coverage. Miss a strip and you've got disease or weeds left untreated. Double over a strip and you've doubled the dose, which can damage the lawn or breach label rates.

Indicate makes the spray visible. As you walk the lawn with the sprayer, you can see exactly where the spray has hit. No missed strips, no overlaps, much more accurate application.

The colourant fades within a few days, so the green tint isn't permanent.

## Rate and application

8 to 10 mL per litre of spray solution. Add LAST after all other chemicals are in the tank. Adding it first or in the middle of the mix can affect how it disperses.

Tank mix compatibility: works with most herbicides, fungicides and fertilisers.

## When to use

Any spray application where coverage matters. Herbicides, fungicides, foliar fertilisers, wetting agents.

Particularly useful on first applications when you're learning the spray pattern of a new sprayer or a new lawn.

Larger lawns where walking lines blur together without a visual indicator.

Showing customers where you've sprayed in a commercial setting.

## When not to use

On edible crops. NOT for use where the colourant could end up on food.

On show lawns immediately before a public event. The green tint lasts a few days, so factor that into your timing.

## Common mistakes

Adding it first to the tank. Always last.

Spraying on concrete and being surprised by the green tint that follows. Indicate doesn't stain permanently, but it'll show on concrete for a few days.

Using on edible crops.

## Best paired with

Any spray application where coverage accuracy matters. Particularly herbicides where missing strips means surviving weeds, and pre-emergents where missed strips mean weeds coming through the gap in the barrier.
