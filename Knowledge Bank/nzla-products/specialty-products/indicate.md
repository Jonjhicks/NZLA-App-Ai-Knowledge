# NZLA Indicate

A non-staining green colourant added to spray tanks to show exactly where the spray has landed. No active pesticide, no nutrients. Just a visual aid that prevents the two most common spray errors: missed patches and double application.

## What it does

Miss a strip on a herbicide application and you've got  left untreated, meaning re-growth and another trip across the lawn. Double over a strip and you've doubled the dose, which can damage turf or breach label rates on the chemical you're using.

Indicate makes the spray pattern visible as you walk. Green on the leaf shows where coverage has hit. No green means you haven't been there. The colour fades within a few days and mows off within two or three cuts.

Useful on any lawn where spray coverage matters: selective herbicides where gaps mean surviving fungicides where missed patches let  re-establish, pre-emergent herbicides where gaps break the barrier.

## Rate and application

8 to 10 mL per litre of spray solution. Add LAST, after all other products are mixed in.

For a 10 L sprayer: 80 to 100 mL.

The sequence matters. Half-fill the tank, add the chemical, top up to full volume, then add Indicate. Adding it early or to a less-full tank affects how it disperses.

Tank-mix compatible with most herbicides, fungicides, and foliar fertilisers.

## What to watch

Not for use on edible crops.

Will leave green tint on concrete and paving for a few days after accidental overspray. Rinse splashes immediately. Won't permanently stain but needs prompt attention on good paving.

The colour on the lawn shows up as green tint for a few days before fading. On a green lawn this is barely noticeable. If you're applying before an event, factor in 2 to 3 days' fading time.
