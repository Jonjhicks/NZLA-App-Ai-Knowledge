# What is NZLA Green used for?

A turf pigment for aesthetic dark green colour, particularly useful on dormant or stressed turf where chlorophyll-based colour (Iron+) won't deliver because the lawn isn't actively growing.

What it does:
- Coats the leaf with a green pigment structurally similar to chlorophyll.
- Immediate dark green appearance from the spray (no growth required to see the colour).
- Lifts light absorption (the leaf takes in more light energy), which can help dormant or semi-dormant turf retain some warmth.
- Lasts 4 to 8 weeks per application depending on mowing frequency and growth rate.

Where it fits:
- Warm-season lawns going dormant in winter (kikuyu, couch, paspalum). Keeps the lawn looking green when it would otherwise brown off.
- Cool-season lawns in temporary stress where colour is wanted before the lawn recovers (event-driven use).
- Pre-event situations where Iron+ won't deliver fast enough or where the lawn isn't actively growing.
- Sports field surfaces where green appearance matters for visual quality.

Not a substitute for nitrogen and iron on actively growing turf. If the lawn is growing and just needs a colour push, Iron+ is the right tool. NZLA Green is for situations where chlorophyll-based response isn't available.

Don't overspray onto concrete, masonry or paving. The pigment stains hard surfaces.
