# When should I apply NZLA Green?

When you need green colour and the lawn isn't going to deliver it through normal nutrition.

Common scenarios:
- Warm-season turf going dormant in autumn or winter (April to August for kikuyu, couch, paspalum).
- Pre-event when there isn't time for nitrogen and iron to drive a chlorophyll response (1 to 2 days before vs 1 to 2 weeks for Iron+).
- Drought-stressed cool-season turf where the lawn won't take up iron fast enough.
- Renovations where the turf is healing and looks rough, pigment helps it look acceptable while the lawn fills in.

Timing within those situations:
- Apply 1 to 3 days before the event for sharp colour.
- For dormant warm-season turf, apply as the lawn starts to lose colour in autumn, repeat every 4 to 8 weeks through the dormant period.

Apply at 11 to 15 mL per 100 m² (initial application 12 mL per 100 m²) in minimum 300 L per hectare of water.

Don't apply:
- In wind that could carry overspray to concrete, paving, walls, garden beds.
- On wet leaves (the pigment won't bind properly).
- During heavy rain forecast within 4 hours.
- On lawns with active disease where you're masking what's actually happening.

Tank-mix friendly. Can be combined with Iron+ for additional colour and chlorophyll response on lawns that can still take up iron.
