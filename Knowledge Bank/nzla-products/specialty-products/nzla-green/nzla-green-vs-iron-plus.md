# What's the difference between NZLA Green and Iron+?

Different ways of getting green colour onto the lawn.

Iron+ is a nutrient. It provides chelated iron to the leaf, the plant uses the iron to make chlorophyll, the chlorophyll gives the deep green colour. The lawn has to be actively growing and taking up nutrients for Iron+ to work. Response within 1 to 3 days.

NZLA Green is a pigment. It coats the leaf with a green colourant. No growth required, no metabolism required, the colour is visible immediately from the spray. Lasts 4 to 8 weeks depending on mowing and growth.

Decision tree:
- Lawn is actively growing, has nitrogen, just needs colour pop: Iron+.
- Lawn is dormant (warm-season in winter) and won't take up iron: NZLA Green.
- Lawn is stressed and won't respond to iron in time: NZLA Green.
- Pre-event with less than 5 days to go: NZLA Green for immediate effect, or tank-mix both for fastest and longest combined response.
- Routine programme colour through summer on an actively growing lawn: Iron+.

The two products can be tank-mixed for combined immediate-and-sustained colour response.

For most home lawn situations through the growing season, Iron+ is the right choice. NZLA Green comes into its own on warm-season lawns through winter dormancy or for pre-event sharpening when chlorophyll response isn't going to deliver in time.

Don't use NZLA Green to mask underlying problems. If the lawn is brown because of disease or pest damage, the pigment hides it but the problem keeps growing underneath.
