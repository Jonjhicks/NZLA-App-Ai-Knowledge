# NZLA Green

A turf pigment that's structurally similar to chlorophyll. Adds dark green colour to the lawn without forcing growth, and the chlorophyll-like structure also lifts light absorption, which provides a small genuine benefit to the plant under stress.

## What it does

Two effects, one aesthetic and one functional.

Aesthetic: NZLA Green is essentially a turf-safe pigment that coats the leaf and gives a dark green appearance. Useful on lawns going into a public event, dormant warm-season turf that's gone tan over winter, or any situation where colour matters and natural green-up isn't possible in the timeframe.

Functional: because the pigment is structurally similar to chlorophyll, it absorbs light in similar wavelengths. The leaf gets more photosynthetic light energy than it would without the pigment, which gives a small genuine boost to plant function. This is most useful on dormant or struggling turf where natural chlorophyll levels are low.

## Rate and application

Initial application: 12 mL per 100m².

Maintenance: 11 to 15 mL per 100m² when topping up.

Minimum 300 L of water per hectare for even coverage.

Tank mixes with most products.

## When to use

Pre-event preparation where a colour boost is wanted that lasts longer than a few hours.

Warm-season turf gone dormant or tan over winter, when you want it green without forcing growth.

Stressed lawns that can't green up naturally but where appearance matters.

Stripe enhancement for show photos.

## When not to use

Right before mowing. Most of the pigment is on the leaf surface, mowing removes it.

On concrete or masonry without protection. Will stain. Mask off edges if needed.

As a substitute for fixing an actual nutrient deficiency. Green doesn't feed the plant. If the lawn is yellow because it's hungry, feed it. Pigment is for situations where you want colour without the growth.

## Common mistakes

Spraying too thinly. Below 11 mL/100m² the effect is patchy.

Spraying on concrete edges without thought. Permanent green stripes you didn't intend.

Mowing too soon afterwards. Lose the colour to the clippings bag.

Treating it as a feed. It's a pigment, doesn't replace nutrition.

## Best paired with

Iron+ for situations where you want natural and pigment colour combined. Iron+ greens the plant from inside, NZLA Green from the outside.

NZLA PGR to hold growth tight so mowing doesn't remove the pigment as quickly.

A solid feed programme for foundation health, with Green for the cosmetic finish.
