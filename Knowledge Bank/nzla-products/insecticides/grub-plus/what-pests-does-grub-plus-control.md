# What pests does Grub+ control?

The main NZ lawn pests:

- Grass grub (Costelytra giveni larvae)
- Black beetle (larvae and adults)
- Porina caterpillars
- Crickets (black field cricket and others)
- Sod webworm caterpillars
- Some surface-feeding insects in turf

The chemistry is broad-spectrum on soil and surface-dwelling lawn pests. One product covers most of what you'll encounter in a NZ home lawn.

Not what Grub+ is for:
- Soil-dwelling beneficial insects and earthworms (treat with restraint, only when needed for actual pest damage)
- Pests of woody plants, vegetable gardens, ornamentals (use specific products for those)
- Aphids, scale, mealybug on garden plants (not a foliar pest product)
- Diseases (fungicide territory, Azoxy and Fungus Pro)

For an integrated lawn pest programme, one well-timed Grub+ application per year covers most situations. Repeated annual applications aren't necessary unless you've got a recurring pest pressure (an established grass grub population in your soil, for example).

Don't use as a routine preventative when no pests are present. Restraint with insecticides preserves soil biology and reduces resistance pressure.
