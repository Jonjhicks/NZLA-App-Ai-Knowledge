# Will Grub+ harm worms and beneficial soil life?

Some impact, yes. Soil-active insecticides can affect non-target invertebrates including earthworms and various beneficials. The label rate balances pest control against this impact, but it's not zero impact on soil life.

What this means in practice:
- Don't use Grub+ as a routine annual preventative when no pest damage is present. Reserve it for actual pest issues.
- One well-timed application per year is far better for soil life than three "just in case" applications.
- Heavy or repeated use over multiple years progressively reduces soil biology, which is the opposite of what a healthy lawn wants.

If you've got a genuine grass grub or porina problem causing visible damage, the trade-off favours treatment. The damage from a heavy grub population is far worse for the lawn than the side-effect on soil life from a single annual application.

If you're applying preventatively with no evidence of pests, you're probably doing more harm than good.

Worms recover over the months following application. Encouraging them back with humic acid (NZLA Humic+), regular grass clipping return through mulch mowing, and a top dressing of compost every couple of years all help.

For most home lawns, one Grub+ application every 1 to 2 years when needed sits well within healthy soil management.
