# When should I apply NZLA Grub+?

Different timings for different pests.

Grass grub: late summer to early autumn (February to March in most regions). Target the small first and second instar larvae when they're feeding shallow and easy to reach. Late autumn applications still work but the damage is already done.

Black beetle: similar timing to grass grub. Late January through March, after peak adult flight, when larvae are small and shallow.

Porina: autumn (March to May). Apply in the evening, the porina caterpillars come up at night to feed.

Crickets: late spring to early summer (November to December), as populations are building, before damage shows.

Sod webworms: when populations and damage are first noticed, evening application.

Common rule across all of them: target the pest when it's small and active near the surface. Waiting until you can see clear damage means the pest is already mature and harder to control.

Apply to a well-watered lawn. For grass grub and black beetle, water in straight after application so the active reaches the soil. For surface-feeding pests (porina, sod webworm, crickets) a light watering rather than a heavy soaking keeps the active where the pest is feeding.
