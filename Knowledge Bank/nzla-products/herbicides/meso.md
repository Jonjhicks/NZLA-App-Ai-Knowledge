# NZLA Meso

Mesotrione at 480 g/L. HRAC Group 27 triketone HPPD inhibitor. Originally launched globally as Tenacity (Syngenta, 2009) and now one of the standard selective herbicides in cool-season turf. Identified by the white bleaching effect on susceptible plants — that visual is the chemistry working, not a sign of failure.

## How it works

Mesotrione inhibits 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase (HPPD), an enzyme essential for carotenoid biosynthesis. Carotenoids protect chlorophyll from light damage. Without carotenoids, existing chlorophyll is destroyed by sunlight (photo-oxidation), the plant bleaches white, photosynthesis stops, and the plant dies over the following 1 to 2 weeks.

The white is the intermediate stage. Bleaching shows 3 to 7 days after application. Necrosis and death follow 7 to 14 days later. Don't reapply when you see the bleaching — that's the chemistry doing exactly what it should. Wait the full 3 to 4 weeks before assessing whether a second application is needed.

## Selectivity

Same mechanism in every plant. What makes mesotrione selective is **metabolism rate** in the treated plant.

Perennial ryegrass and tall fescue rapidly break mesotrione down through efficient metabolic pathways before it accumulates to damaging levels. Browntop (Agrostis capillaris) and creeping bentgrass don't metabolise it efficiently — the chemistry accumulates, carotenoid synthesis is inhibited, and those grasses bleach and die.

That metabolic difference is the basis of using Meso to selectively remove browntop or creeping bentgrass contamination from a ryegrass or tall fescue lawn.

Warm-season grasses (kikuyu, couch) are damaged by Meso. Do not apply to warm-season turf.

## Two capabilities that set it apart

**Safe at lawn establishment.** Mesotrione is one of the very few selective herbicides that can be applied during seeding of perennial ryegrass and tall fescue. The desirable seed germinates and establishes while the Meso barrier suppresses  in the same window. Genuinely useful for new lawns where cultivated ground brings up a weed flush alongside the grass.

**Both pre and post-emergent activity.** Works on seeds germinating in the soil (pre-em) and on young  already emerged (post-em). Wider window than a purely pre-em or post-em product.

## Weed coverage

46+ species on the label. Notable: browntop, creeping bentgrass, annual poa (partial control), crabgrass at seedling stage, chickweed (good control), dandelion, plantain, catsear, clover, oxalis, and many common broadleaves.

## The EPA cap

Maximum 2 applications per area per 12 months. Hard NZ EPA regulatory limit, not a guideline.

The standard browntop control programme uses both applications in one programme: two sprays 14 days apart. After those two applications, no more Meso on that area for 12 months. Plan accordingly.

## Rate and application

Check the current label for rates by target and growth stage. Different rates for establishment use versus mature lawn use — lower rates at seeding, full rate on established turf.

Apply when target  are actively growing in mild conditions. Soil temps 14 to 22°C.

No rain for 4 to 6 hours after application. Mow 3 days before and 3 days after, not within that window.

## When to use

Browntop or creeping bentgrass contamination in a ryegrass or tall fescue lawn — two applications 14 days apart.

New lawn establishment with known weed seed pressure.

Mature lawn broadleaf weed control where Meso is the appropriate chemistry.

## When not to use

On warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch). On lawns where browntop is a desirable species. Beyond the 2-application EPA cap. On heat-stressed lawns.

## Common mistakes

Reapplying after seeing the bleaching. White means it's working. Wait 3 to 4 weeks.

Applying to warm-season turf.

Using both EPA cap applications without a clear plan — if the browntop programme needs two applications 14 days apart, that's the entire year's allowance gone.

Expecting Meso to handle heavy established broadleaves. BWC or Gold handles those better.
