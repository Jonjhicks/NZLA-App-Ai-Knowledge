# What weeds does BWC control?

BWC is a broad-spectrum broadleaf herbicide. The three actives (clopyralid, MCPA, diflufenican) cover most of the common NZ turf broadleaf weeds:

- Plantain (broadleaf and ribwort)
- Dandelion
- Catsear
- Hawksbeard, hawkbit
- Daisy
- Chickweed
- White clover
- Speedwell (some control, can be tougher)
- Cleavers
- Various sow thistles

Caveats. Onehunga (Soliva sessilis) is on the BWC target list, but Harrington's research has documented clopyralid-resistant onehunga in NZ. Resistant biotypes also show cross-resistance to MCPA and dicamba, but not to mecoprop. If BWC has been working for you in the past and suddenly isn't, that may be what's happening. The chemistry that handles resistant onehunga (mecoprop) isn't currently in the NZLA range, so honest answer is the gap is real.

Tougher weeds (oxalis, mallow, hairy bittercress in established stands) often need a follow-up application or a different chemistry. NZLA Gold (picloram + triclopyr) is the stronger broadleaf option for woody and tougher weeds, but it's heavier on the turf and not labelled for fine fescue or browntop.

Read the BWC label for the full controlled weed list and turfgrass restrictions.
