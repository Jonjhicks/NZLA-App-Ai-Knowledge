# When should I apply NZLA BWC?

BWC (clopyralid + MCPA + diflufenican) is a selective broadleaf herbicide for established cool-season turf. The best windows for application are spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) when weeds are actively growing.

Conditions matter more than the calendar. Apply when:
- Lawn temps are 12 to 24 °C. Cold lawns don't take up herbicide well, hot stressed lawns can react badly.
- The lawn isn't drought-stressed. Water the lawn well a day or two before if soil is dry.
- No rain forecast for 6 hours minimum, preferably 24.
- Wind is low. Drift onto neighbouring beds, hedges and trees is a real risk with hormone herbicides.

Established lawn only. Don't spray BWC on a lawn less than about 8 weeks old, the chemistry hammers young seedlings.

Note on onehunga: research (Harrington at Massey, Ghanizadeh et al 2021) confirms clopyralid-resistant onehunga in NZ, with the resistance spreading. If BWC isn't controlling onehunga in your area, that's likely what you're dealing with. The chemistry needed for resistant biotypes is mecoprop, which isn't currently in the NZLA range. Be straight with yourself about that, don't keep doubling the rate hoping it'll work.
