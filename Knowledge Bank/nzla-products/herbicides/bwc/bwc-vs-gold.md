# Should I use BWC or Gold?

Depends on the weeds and the turf.

BWC (clopyralid + MCPA + diflufenican) is the general-purpose broadleaf option. Handles most common turf broadleaves, has some residual cover from the diflufenican, safe on most cool-season lawns at label rates. First-line choice for general broadleaf control on ryegrass, fescue or browntop lawns.

Gold (picloram + triclopyr) is heavier chemistry, for tougher weeds. Woody and persistent weeds, oxalis, hairy bittercress, mallow, gorse seedlings, blackberry seedlings. Picloram has a longer soil residual which can cause issues if clippings end up in compost (don't compost clippings from a Gold-treated lawn). Not safe on fine fescue or browntop, even at label rates.

So decision tree:
- Common broadleaves, cool-season lawn including fine fescue or browntop: BWC.
- Tougher weeds, ryegrass or tall fescue lawn, no fine grass mixed in: Gold.
- Onehunga, suspected clopyralid resistance: neither BWC nor Gold is reliable. Picloram has cross-resistance with clopyralid. Honest answer is mecoprop is needed and isn't in the NZLA range.

For typical NZ home lawns with the standard broadleaf mix, BWC handles 90% of what people are dealing with.
