# Will BWC damage my lawn?

At label rate on healthy established cool-season turf (ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop, fine fescue), BWC is safe. The chemistry is selective, the broadleaf weeds take it up and die, the grass shrugs it off.

Where damage shows up:
- Over-rate. Hormone herbicides at double rate twist and damage the grass. Stick to label.
- Heat stress. Spraying when the lawn is over about 26 °C and water-stressed can cause leaf scorch even at label rate.
- Drought-stressed lawn. Same issue. Water the lawn well a day or two before spraying.
- Very young seedlings (under 8 weeks). The clopyralid component damages young ryegrass and fescue. Wait until the new lawn is mown at least three times before any selective herbicide.
- Tank-mix errors. Combining with another hormone product (Gold, MCPA, dicamba) at full rates of each is over-loading. Don't double up.

Some brief yellowing of the lawn for a few days after spraying is normal and isn't damage. The grass is processing the chemistry. It greens back up quickly.

If you've genuinely damaged the lawn with BWC (visible twisting, dieback), water deeply, hold off on any further chemistry, apply a small dose of Liquid N to support recovery, and wait. Recovery usually takes 2 to 4 weeks.
