# What's the application rate for BWC?

Check the BWC label for the current rate, which sits around 35 to 50 mL per 100 m² depending on the target weeds. Carrier water 4 to 8 L per 100 m².

A few practical points:
- Spray to wet coverage, not run-off. Hormone herbicides need to contact the leaf and absorb. They don't need to soak the lawn.
- Don't mow for at least 3 days before and 3 days after application. The leaf area is what's absorbing the product, mowing removes that area and removes the absorbed herbicide with the clippings.
- Don't water in for 24 hours. This is a foliar product, not soil-applied. Watering in washes it off the leaf before the weed has finished absorbing.

The diflufenican component gives BWC some pre-emergent activity as well as post-emergent, so it'll keep working against fresh germinations for a few weeks after application. Useful in autumn when you want both post-emergent control of established weeds and some residual cover.

If you're spot-spraying rather than blanket-treating, mix at the same concentration as the broadcast rate (a 1% mix is wrong for hand-spraying). Calculate based on the area you're actually treating.
