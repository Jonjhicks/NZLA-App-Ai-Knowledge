# Will Gold damage my lawn?

On the right turf at label rate, no. Gold is safe on established perennial ryegrass and tall fescue.

Where damage shows up:
- Fine fescue (chewings, creeping, hard). Gold will damage or kill it. Don't use on fine fescue lawns or ryegrass-fescue mixes that include fine fescue.
- Browntop (Agrostis). Damaged by Gold. Older NZ lawns often have browntop mixed in.
- Couch, kikuyu, paspalum. Damaged.
- Over-rate. Hormone herbicides at double rate cause twisting and dieback.
- Heat stress and drought stress. Spray on a stressed lawn over 26 °C and you can scorch the leaf even at label rate.
- Very young lawn (under 8 weeks). Young grass seedlings damaged by the picloram component.
- Near tree roots or vegetable gardens. Picloram travels through soil and can reach roots well outside the spray area.

Some yellowing or twisting of the lawn for a week or two after application is normal at label rate and isn't damage. The grass processes the chemistry and returns to normal.

If you're not certain what species your lawn is, spot-treat a small test patch and wait 4 to 6 weeks before treating the whole area.

If you've genuinely damaged the lawn with Gold, water deeply, hold off further chemistry, apply a light dose of Liquid N to support recovery, wait 3 to 6 weeks. If damage is severe (large dead patches) reseeding may be needed.
