# What weeds does Gold control?

Gold is for the tougher end of the broadleaf spectrum. The picloram and triclopyr combination handles weeds that hormone-only products like BWC struggle with:

- Oxalis (creeping oxalis, several species)
- Hairy bittercress
- Mallow
- Buttercup
- Plantain (also covered by BWC, Gold works on heavier infestations)
- Catsear, hawksbeard, hawkbit (also covered by BWC)
- Gorse seedlings
- Blackberry seedlings
- Broom seedlings
- Woody and persistent weeds

Triclopyr is the active that handles a lot of the woodier weeds. Picloram is the auxin component that drives systemic activity through the plant including into the root system.

Important caveat for onehunga: picloram has documented cross-resistance with clopyralid in clopyralid-resistant onehunga biotypes (Harrington's research). If you've got resistant onehunga, Gold isn't going to fix it either. The chemistry that handles resistant onehunga (mecoprop) isn't currently in the NZLA range.

For garden-variety broadleaves on a ryegrass or fescue lawn, BWC is usually enough. Gold comes in for the tougher persistent weeds where BWC isn't getting full control, particularly oxalis, mallow and woody seedlings.
