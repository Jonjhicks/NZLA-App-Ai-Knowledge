# When should I apply NZLA Gold?

Gold (picloram 50 g/L + triclopyr 100 g/L) is for tougher broadleaf weeds that the standard chemistry can't handle. Apply when the target weeds are actively growing, typically spring (October to December) or autumn (March to April).

Soil temps 14 to 22 °C, lawn well-watered (not drought-stressed), no rain forecast for 6 hours minimum, low wind.

The picloram component has a long soil residual. Don't compost the clippings for the next 18 months, they can carry enough picloram to damage vegetable crops and sensitive plants like tomatoes, capsicums, peas and beans. Mulch them back into the lawn or send them to landfill.

Also: don't apply near desirable trees, shrubs or vegetable gardens where the roots may extend under the lawn. Picloram travels with soil moisture and can reach roots well outside the spray area.

Established lawn only. Don't spray on a lawn under 8 weeks old.

Not safe on fine fescue or browntop. Use only on ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. Will damage kikuyu, couch, paspalum.
