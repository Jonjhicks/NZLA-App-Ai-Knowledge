# NZLA Sedge Pro

Halosulfuron-methyl at 750 g/kg. HRAC Group 2 sulfonylurea ALS inhibitor. The selective tool for persistent nutsedge in established turf. Hits purple nutsedge (Cyperus rotundus) and yellow nutsedge (Cyperus esculentus) without taking out the surrounding lawn.

## How it works

Halosulfuron-methyl inhibits acetolactate synthase (ALS), blocking the synthesis of branched-chain amino acids (valine, leucine, isoleucine) that are essential for protein production and cell growth. Susceptible plants can't grow, stop dividing, and die.

Most lawn grasses metabolise halosulfuron before it damages them. Sedges don't — the basis of the selectivity.

It's systemic. The chemistry moves from the leaf down into the underground tuber network where nutsedge stores energy and regenerates from. That's why Sedge Pro is the tool for established nutgrass rather than a contact herbicide: each application hits the above-ground plant and takes out some of the tubers below. Multiple applications progressively reduce the tuber bank.

## Realistic expectations for nutgrass

Nutgrass comes back from underground tubers that can sit in the soil for years. A single application won't clear it. Two applications per year across two or three seasons progressively depletes the tuber bank and brings most home-lawn infestations under control. Complete eradication from a heavy infestation is hard. Manage it as a multi-season thinning exercise, not a one-shot fix.

New or light infestations are more responsive. Heavy or long-established infestations take longer.

## Rate and application

Spot treatment: 1.3 g per 100m² in 10 L water.
Boom: 65 to 130 g/ha in minimum 80 L water per hectare.

Apply to actively growing nutsedge once new leaf growth reaches 5 cm or more. Smaller plants don't have enough leaf area to absorb a useful dose.

Delay mowing 2 days after application — the leaves need time to absorb and translocate the active into the tubers.

No rain for 6 to 8 hours after application. Apply to a moist (not waterlogged) lawn.

## Sprayer cleanout — critical

Sulfonylureas are extraordinarily potent at trace concentrations. Residue in a sprayer that isn't thoroughly cleaned can damage broadleaf crops or sensitive lawns the next time it's used. After using Sedge Pro, flush the sprayer thoroughly with chlorine bleach solution (standard commercial clean-out), then rinse with clean water. Don't skip this.

## When to use

Spring through summer — October to March — when nutsedge is actively growing. Soil temps 18°C or warmer.

First application as nutsedge begins pushing in spring, second application in late summer if needed. This is the standard programme for established infestations.

## When not to use

On newly established or stressed turf. Near broadleaf garden plants — spray drift will damage them. In hot windy conditions where drift is unmanageable.

## Sedge Pro vs Halo

Both control sedge. Halo (halosulfuron-methyl at lower concentration plus some broadleaf activity) is the lighter option for moderate infestations and mixed sedge-plus-broadleaf situations. Sedge Pro is heavier chemistry for established or persistent infestations where Halo hasn't delivered the result.

If Halo has had one or two rounds and the nutgrass is still pushing back, Sedge Pro is the next step.

## Common mistakes

Treating before nutsedge has reached 5 cm new growth — not enough leaf area for useful uptake.

Mowing within 2 days — removes the leaf the active is sitting on.

Expecting one application to clear a heavy infestation. Multi-season programmes are standard.

Skipping the sprayer cleanout after use. Trace sulfonylurea residue can cause damage in the next application.

## Best paired with

Cultural drainage-and-soil-moisture improvements. Nutsedge thrives in wet, poorly-drained soil. Better drainage-and-soil-moisture reduces the conditions that favour it, and the herbicide works more efficiently against a less vigorous infestation.

Repeat applications through the same season (label permitting) and the following season to deplete the tuber bank.
