# Can I use Etho on any lawn?

No, and getting this wrong kills the lawn. Etho is selective in some species and lethal in others.

Safe on:
- Perennial ryegrass
- Tall fescue (knocks it back a bit if it's not actively growing, but recovers)

Not safe on:
- Fine fescues (chewings, creeping, hard, slender)
- Browntop / bentgrass (Agrostis)
- Couch
- Kikuyu
- Paspalum

If you're not sure what your lawn is, find out before spraying. Most NZ home lawns are perennial ryegrass or a ryegrass-tall fescue blend, which means Etho is fine. But if your lawn was seeded as a fine fescue blend or has browntop or kikuyu in it (common in older lawns, particularly Auckland and upper North Island), Etho will damage or kill it.

Mixed lawns are tricky. A ryegrass lawn with patches of browntop or fine fescue mixed through it will get the browntop and fine fescue patches damaged while the ryegrass is unaffected. That can end up looking worse than the original poa problem.

If you can't confirm the species, spot-treat a small test patch first and wait 4 to 6 weeks. If the lawn handles it without damage, you can treat the rest. If anything dies back, you know not to use Etho on it.
