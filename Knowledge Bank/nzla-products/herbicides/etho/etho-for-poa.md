# How well does Etho control annual poa?

In ryegrass and tall fescue lawns through autumn and winter, Etho gives good control of annual poa. Harrington's research at Massey identifies ethofumesate as the selective option for poa in those species, and that aligns with what users see in practice.

The result depends a lot on the application programme. Multiple applications at 4 to 6 week intervals through the growth season outperform a single application. Two or three light hits through autumn and winter knock poa back progressively without smashing the ryegrass.

It's also a thinning-not-killing herbicide on poa, particularly in heavy infestations. You won't see clean poa-free turf overnight. The poa thins, the ryegrass strengthens, and over a season or two the balance shifts. If you're after a one-shot clean-up, this isn't the chemistry to expect that from.

Combine with cultural control for the strongest result. Dense competitive ryegrass turf, sensible mowing height (40 to 50 mm), good fertility through autumn, irrigation only when needed. The poa establishes in thin, weak, over-watered turf. Fix that and the herbicide does its job more easily.

Doesn't work on fine fescue, browntop, couch or kikuyu. On those species poa control is a much tougher problem with much fewer tools.
