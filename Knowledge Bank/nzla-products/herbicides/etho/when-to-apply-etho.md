# When should I apply NZLA Etho?

Etho (ethofumesate 500 g/L) is a selective herbicide for annual poa control in ryegrass and tall fescue. The right window is autumn through winter (April to August), when poa is actively growing and ryegrass is in its strong season.

Why that window: ethofumesate works best on cool-season actively growing poa. Apply too early in summer and the ryegrass is heat-stressed, the poa isn't growing much, and the result is poor. Apply in late winter and you're hitting poa as it sets seed, the timing's too late to stop the seed bank topping up for next year.

Multiple applications work better than a single hammer. Two or three light applications at 4 to 6 week intervals through autumn and winter outperform one heavy application. The poa gets knocked back progressively without the ryegrass taking a big hit.

Important species restriction. Etho is safe on perennial ryegrass and tall fescue. It's not safe on fine fescues (chewings, creeping, hard), couch, kikuyu, or browntop (Agrostis). On those species it'll damage or kill the desirable turf. If your lawn is a ryegrass-fescue mix with browntop or fine fescue in it, Etho is the wrong product.

Soil temps under about 18 °C give the cleanest results. Hot lawns and Etho don't get on.
