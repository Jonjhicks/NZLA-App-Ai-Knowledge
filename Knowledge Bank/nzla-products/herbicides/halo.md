# NZLA Halo

Haloxyfop-P-methyl at 100 g/L. HRAC Group 1 FOP (aryloxyphenoxypropionate). The selective grass-in-grass herbicide for fine fescue lawns. Takes out invading grass species — kikuyu, couch, ryegrass, browntop, summer grass, paspalum — from a fine fescue lawn without killing the fine fescue itself.

## How it works

Haloxyfop-P-methyl inhibits acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACCase), the enzyme that catalyses the first committed step in fatty acid synthesis in grasses. Without ACCase activity, the plant can't build new cell membrane lipids. Cells can't divide and grow. The plant stops growing and dies.

The chemistry targets grasses specifically because broadleaf plants have a different ACCase enzyme form that isn't inhibited by FOP chemistry. So Halo can be applied over broadleaf plants without damaging them.

## Selectivity within grasses

This is the critical part to understand. The "kills grasses" mechanism is broad — most turf grasses are susceptible.

Fine fescue tolerates haloxyfop at restricted rates because its ACCase enzyme has lower affinity for the chemistry. That's the selectivity NZLA Halo provides: selective grass-weed control in fine fescue lawns specifically.

**Susceptible species (will be killed):** perennial ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop (Agrostis), kikuyu, couch, summer grass, paspalum.

If any of these species is the desirable lawn grass, Halo is the wrong product. It will kill the lawn. This is for fine fescue lawns only — where fine fescue is the desirable species and the others listed above are the invaders.

## Rate cap on fine fescue

Fine fescue maximum: 15 mL per 100m². Do not exceed. Going over rate damages even fine fescue. This is the hard limit on the label.

## What it controls

All the susceptible grass species listed above. For fine fescue lawn owners, particularly useful for kikuyu encroachment and couch invasion — situations that would otherwise require full renovation (kill everything, renovate, reseed) to address.

Also covers some common broadleaf  on the label: daisy, plantain, catsear, hawksbeard, chickweed. For a mixed weed scenario (some grass invasion plus some broadleaves), Halo can address both in one application.

## Soil residual and seeding window

Haloxyfop persists in soil for 6 to 8 weeks before degrading to non-toxic levels. Sowing new grass seed within that window will kill the seedlings. Plan any renovation or reseeding around the 6 to 8 week stand-down.

## Rate and application

Check the label for exact rates by application method and target species. Fine fescue cap: 15 mL per 100m².

Apply when target grasses are actively growing. No use in grazed areas.

GHS: eye irritation Cat 2, STOT repeat Cat 2, aquatic chronic Cat 2. Commercial use requires qualified handler (EPA HPC Notice 2017). PPE essential.

## When not to use

On any lawn where ryegrass, tall fescue, browntop, kikuyu, couch, or other susceptible grasses are the desirable species. On fine fescue above the 15 mL/100m² cap. Within 6 to 8 weeks of planned seeding. In grazed areas. Drift onto adjacent grass lawns will damage them.

## Halo vs Sedge Pro

Halo is a grass herbicide that also covers some broadleaves. Sedge Pro targets sedges (nutgrass, kyllinga). For lawns with both a grass weed invasion and broadleaf Halo handles both; Sedge Pro handles the sedge component specifically. They're for different problems, not interchangeable.

## Common mistakes

Applying to a lawn where the desirable grass is ryegrass, tall fescue, or browntop. Halo will kill the lawn. Confirm the species first.

Exceeding 15 mL/100m² on fine fescue.

Drift onto adjacent grass lawns. Will cause damage.

Sowing seed too soon after application.

Confusing "selective grass herbicide" with safe on all lawn species — the selectivity is specifically fine fescue versus the susceptible invaders.
