# When should I apply NZLA Meso?

Meso (mesotrione) works on actively growing weeds in cool-season turf. Spring (October to December) and autumn (March to May) are the prime windows.

Soil temps 14 to 22 °C is the sweet spot. Mesotrione is a bleaching herbicide (HPPD inhibitor) and works through the plant's photosynthesis pathway, so target weeds need to be actively growing and photosynthesising for the chemistry to take effect.

One feature that makes Meso different: it's safe to apply at lawn establishment with new ryegrass and tall fescue seed. Most selective herbicides damage seedlings, mesotrione is one of the few exceptions safe at sowing. That makes it useful when you've sown a new lawn into ground that has a weed seed bank likely to come up at the same time as the grass.

For mature turf, apply when target weeds are visible and actively growing. Allow 2 to 3 weeks for the bleaching effect to show up (weeds turn white as chlorophyll production stops), then another 1 to 2 weeks for collapse.

Established lawn or new seed both work, just keep the rate appropriate for the situation (lower rates at seeding, full rate on established turf).
