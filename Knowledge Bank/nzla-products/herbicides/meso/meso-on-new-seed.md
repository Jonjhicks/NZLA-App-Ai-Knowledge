# Can I use Meso when seeding a new lawn?

Yes, that's one of its key uses. Mesotrione is safe at lawn establishment on ryegrass and tall fescue seed, so it can be applied at sowing or shortly after to keep weed pressure down while the grass is establishing.

How it works at establishment: most lawn-area soils have a weed seed bank that wakes up when you cultivate the ground and water it. Without selective herbicide cover, the weeds come up with (or before) the grass and you end up with a messy lawn full of weed seedlings. Removing them later means either pulling by hand or waiting until the grass is mature enough for normal selective herbicides.

Meso at the right rate at seeding stops most of the broadleaf weeds (chickweed, dandelion, plantain, oxalis seedlings, others) without damaging the germinating ryegrass or tall fescue. Read the label for the establishment rate, which is lower than the mature lawn rate.

Not safe at establishment on:
- Fine fescue
- Browntop
- Bent grass

For mature turf, full label rate applies. For establishment, use the lower rate specified for new seeding.

The other establishment option is to apply Meso about 7 to 14 days before seeding to clear the weed seed bank in the top of the soil, then sow into clean ground. Either approach works.
