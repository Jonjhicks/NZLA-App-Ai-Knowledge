# Why does Meso turn weeds white?

Mesotrione is an HPPD inhibitor. It blocks an enzyme (4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase) that's part of the carotenoid pathway in plants. Without carotenoids, the plant can't protect its chlorophyll from light damage, the chlorophyll degrades, and the leaf bleaches white.

The white isn't the kill, it's the early sign that the chemistry is working. The kill follows over the next 2 to 3 weeks as the plant runs out of energy. Without functional chlorophyll, no photosynthesis. Without photosynthesis, no sugar production. Without sugar, the plant starves and collapses.

The reason ryegrass and tall fescue tolerate it: those species metabolise mesotrione quickly through their own detoxification enzymes, so the chemistry doesn't accumulate to damaging levels. Sensitive species (fine fescue, browntop, many broadleaves) lack the same detoxification capacity, so the chemistry accumulates and they bleach and die.

Cool turf science aside, the practical point is: white isn't dead, it's working. Don't reapply when you see the bleaching. Wait 3 to 4 weeks and see what's left. Reapplication too early just wastes product without adding control.

Same pathway is exploited by some farm crop herbicides (Callisto for corn, others). Mesotrione is a well-studied chemistry, the science is well-understood.
