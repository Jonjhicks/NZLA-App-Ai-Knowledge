# What weeds does Meso control?

Meso covers a useful list of broadleaves plus some grass and sedge species:

- Chickweed (good control)
- Dandelion
- Plantain
- Catsear, hawksbeard
- Yellow nutsedge (partial control)
- Crab grass (summer grass) at seedling stage
- Several common turf broadleaves at early growth stages

The bleaching effect is the giveaway, target weeds turn pale white as chlorophyll production stops, then collapse over the following 2 to 3 weeks. Different look from hormone herbicides like BWC or Gold, which twist and distort the plant.

Where Meso struggles:
- Mature established weeds (over a season old, well-rooted)
- Some grass weeds beyond seedling stage
- Onehunga (not in the meso target spectrum)
- Very tough perennials (mallow, established oxalis)

For tougher weeds, BWC or Gold is a better choice. For new lawn establishment or a clean-up of seedling weeds on a younger lawn, Meso is the right tool.

Combining approaches works well. Meso at establishment to handle seedling pressure, then BWC at 8 to 12 weeks once the lawn is mature, to handle any tougher weeds that have grown through.
