# NZLA Meso

The bleach. Mesotrione at 480 g/L. HRAC Group 27 triketone HPPD inhibitor. Originally launched as Tenacity by Syngenta in 2009 and has been one of the most-used selective turf herbicides globally ever since. The classic identifier is the white bleached appearance on susceptible plants within days of application — diagnostic of HPPD-inhibitor chemistry.

## How it actually works

Mesotrione inhibits 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase (HPPD) — an enzyme essential for the biosynthesis of carotenoids in plant cells. Carotenoids do two jobs in plants: they're accessory pigments for photosynthesis, and (critically here) they protect chlorophyll from light damage.

Block carotenoid synthesis and the plant can't make replacement carotenoids. Existing carotenoids degrade. Without that protection, chlorophyll exposed to sunlight breaks down (photo-oxidation). The plant loses its green pigment. The tissue turns white — the classic bleached visual.

No chlorophyll means no photosynthesis. No photosynthesis means no energy production. The plant dies of carbon starvation within 7 to 14 days of bleaching.

## Why the selectivity works

The chemistry of HPPD inhibition is the same in every plant — turf or weed. What makes mesotrione selective on certain turf species and lethal to certain weed species is **metabolism rate**.

Kentucky bluegrass, perennial ryegrass and tall fescue have efficient metabolism pathways that rapidly break mesotrione down before it can cause damage. The herbicide enters the plant, gets metabolised, and the plant carries on. That's the basis of selectivity in cool-season turf.

Browntop (Agrostis capillaris) and creeping bentgrass don't have the same metabolic efficiency. The mesotrione persists in the plant long enough to inhibit HPPD, the carotenoids deplete, chlorophyll degrades, and the plant bleaches.

That's why Meso can be used to selectively remove browntop or bentgrass contamination from a ryegrass or fescue lawn — same chemistry hits all of them, but only the contaminant grass dies.

## Two unusual capabilities

**Both pre and post-emergent activity.** Most selective herbicides are one or the other. Mesotrione has activity at both stages, which gives a wider application window and lets it catch weeds at different growth stages within one application programme.

**Use during seeding.** Mesotrione is one of the very few selective herbicides that can be applied during seeding of cool-season grass. The desirable seed germinates and establishes while the mesotrione barrier suppresses weeds in the same window. That's a genuinely useful capability when starting a new lawn with known weed pressure.

## The EPA cap

NZ EPA limits mesotrione to maximum 2 applications per area per 12 months. This is a hard regulatory cap, not a guideline.

For browntop control, the recognised programme is two applications 14 days apart — which uses the year's entire allowance in one go. After those two applications, no more mesotrione on that area until 12 months have passed.

International research on creeping bentgrass control shows efficacy varies widely with rate — one application controls 7 to 43% of bentgrass, two applications control 39 to 88% as rates increase from 70 to 1,120 g ai/ha. Mid-range rates in the two-application pattern give reasonable control without maxing out residue.

## Targets

46+ broadleaf and grass weed species on the label. Notable inclusions: browntop, creeping bentgrass, annual poa (partial), crabgrass, plantain, dandelion, clover, oxalis, chickweed.

## Rate and application

Refer to label for specific rates per target. Generally applied to actively growing weeds in mild conditions.

EPA NZ cap: maximum 2 applications per area per 12 months. Hard regulatory limit.

## When to use

New lawn establishment of cool-season grass with weed pressure — sow the seed, apply Meso, weeds in the establishing lawn are suppressed while the desirable grass grows in.

Browntop contamination in a perennial ryegrass or tall fescue lawn — two applications 14 days apart.

Creeping bentgrass contamination in a desirable cool-season lawn.

Established lawns where a specific label target needs hitting and the EPA cap hasn't been used up for the year.

## When not to use

On warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch). Mesotrione will damage warm-season species.

If you want to keep browntop or creeping bentgrass. Meso hits them hard — that's the point.

On stressed lawns.

Beyond the EPA 2-applications-per-year cap.

In a tank that's been used for other herbicides without thorough cleanout.

## Common mistakes

Expecting instant kill. Bleaching is the first stage, full death takes 7 to 14 days. People sometimes assume it didn't work when the weeds are standing but turning white — that's the chemistry doing exactly what it should.

Exceeding 2 applications per year. EPA cap is firm.

Applying to a lawn containing browntop you wanted to keep. Meso doesn't know the difference between target and desirable grass species.

Spraying with rain forecast immediately after — washes product before uptake.

## Best paired with

NZLA Starter at establishment, Meso through the early weeks to keep weeds suppressed while the desirable grass establishes.

Pre-em Blockade ahead of the next germination window once Meso's residual fades and the EPA cap allows.
