# Why would I use Sedge Pro instead of Halo?

Three reasons to step up to Sedge Pro.

First, heavier or older infestations. Halo handles light to moderate sedge populations well. Once a nutgrass colony is established and the tuber bank in the soil is dense, Halo on its own takes a long time to make a dent. Sedge Pro's chemistry is heavier and works harder against established infestations.

Second, where Halo's done a round and the sedge is still pushing back. If you've applied Halo at full rate twice and the nutgrass is still coming through, Sedge Pro is the next step rather than a third application of Halo (which is essentially the same chemistry doing the same thing).

Third, tougher sedge species. Kyllinga and some of the lesser-known nutsedges are tougher than the standard yellow nutsedge. Sedge Pro covers a wider sedge spectrum.

When Halo is the right choice instead:
- Light or new nutgrass infestation
- Mixed sedge + broadleaf where one product handles both
- Smaller budget, sedge problem is moderate, not catastrophic
- New users who aren't sure they need the heavier chemistry yet

Read both labels for registered turfgrass species. Neither product is universally safe on every grass species, and the safe lists differ between the two.
