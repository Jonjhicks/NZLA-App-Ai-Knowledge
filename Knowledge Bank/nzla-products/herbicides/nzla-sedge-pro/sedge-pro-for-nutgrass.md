# Does Sedge Pro kill nutgrass tubers?

Yes, partially, which is what makes Sedge Pro worth using over cheaper contact herbicides. Nutgrass spreads from underground tubers (the "nuts") which can sit dormant in the soil for years and re-sprout long after the visible plants are gone. Contact herbicides only kill the top growth, the tubers send up new shoots weeks later.

Sedge Pro is systemic, the chemistry moves down through the plant into the tubers. Each application takes out some of the tubers in addition to the visible plants. Multiple applications over a couple of seasons progressively reduce the tuber bank in the soil.

Realistic expectations: one application doesn't clear nutgrass. The tubers are deep and numerous in an established infestation. Two applications per year for two or three years brings most home-lawn nutgrass under control. Complete eradication is hard, manage it as a long-term thinning exercise.

Cultural side helps too. Nutgrass thrives in wet poorly-drained soils. Improving drainage and not over-watering reduces the conditions nutgrass prefers, and the herbicide works more efficiently against a less vigorous infestation.

For light or new nutgrass infestations, Halo can be enough. For heavy or long-established infestations, Sedge Pro's systemic activity against the tubers is what's needed.
