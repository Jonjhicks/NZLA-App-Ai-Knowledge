# When should I apply NZLA Blockade?

Blockade is a pre-emergent (prodiamine, HRAC Group 3). It works by sitting in the top layer of soil and stopping weed seeds from establishing as they germinate. Two windows matter for an NZ lawn.

Autumn (March to early May depending on region). Targets winter-germinating annuals, primarily annual poa. Apply once soil temps are dropping below about 18 to 20 °C and before significant rainfall. Watering in afterwards (5 to 10 mm) activates the chemistry into the top of the soil.

Late winter to early spring (August into September depending on region). Targets summer-germinating annuals, primarily summer grass (crab grass) and crowsfoot. Apply before soil temps come up to germination range (around 14 to 18 °C), water in.

Critical NZ restriction: Blockade is registered in NZ for warm-season turf only (kikuyu, couch, buffalo). Off-label on cool-season turf (ryegrass, fescue) here. Don't apply on ryegrass or fescue, even though it's labelled for cool-season turf in other countries. The NZ label is what matters.

For cool-season lawns with annual poa pressure, NZLA Etho is the option to look at (selective post-emergent rather than pre-emergent, but works in ryegrass).
