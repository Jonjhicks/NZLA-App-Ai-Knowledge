# Why is Blockade labelled differently in NZ than overseas?

Labels are registered country by country. The US prodiamine label (Barricade and others) includes cool-season grass species. The NZ EPA / ACVM registration for Blockade specifically restricts use to warm-season turf, primarily kikuyu and couch.

Each country sets its own label based on local trials, regulatory environment, and the registrant's submission. Different climate, different species, different residue tolerances, different labels.

What you legally can and can't use a product on in NZ is what the NZ label says. Not what the US, Australian or any other label says. Off-label use in NZ is the operator's risk, both for damage to the turf and for breach of ACVM regulations.

A few practical implications. If you've read US-based lawn forums or YouTube channels that talk about prodiamine on cool-season ryegrass or fescue, that information doesn't transfer to NZ usage. They're working under a different label. Don't borrow recommendations across countries on registered chemistry.

For cool-season pre-emergent options here, the current NZLA range doesn't have one. Etho (post-emergent) handles annual poa in ryegrass. Cultural control through dense competitive turf is the other half of the answer.
