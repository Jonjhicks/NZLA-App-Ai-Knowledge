# How long does Blockade last in the soil?

Rate-dependent. Prodiamine residual ranges from about 4 months at the low end of the rate to 6 to 8 months at the high end. Most lawn applications target 4 to 6 months, enough to cover the typical winter or summer germination window in one shot.

A few things that affect how long it lasts:
- Higher rate = longer residual, but also more risk to the turf root system.
- Sandy soils break it down faster than heavier soils. Reapply timing may need to be sooner.
- Microbial activity in the soil reduces it over time. Warm wet soils degrade it faster than cool dry soils.
- Cultivation, aeration or top dressing breaks up the chemical layer and effectively ends the residual.

Once the residual ends, weed seeds in the soil can germinate normally. There's no carryover protection beyond the active residual period.

For a standard NZ programme on warm-season turf, one autumn application (March to May) gives 4 to 6 months of winter cover, then one spring application (August to September) gives another 4 to 6 months of summer cover. That's a full year of pre-emergent cover with two well-timed applications.
