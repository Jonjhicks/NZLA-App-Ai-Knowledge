# Can I use Blockade on my ryegrass lawn?

Not in NZ. Blockade is registered here for warm-season turf only, kikuyu, couch and buffalo. The NZ label specifically excludes ryegrass and other cool-season grasses, so applying it to a ryegrass or fescue lawn is off-label use.

Some international labels for prodiamine do include cool-season grasses. That doesn't change the NZ position. The local label is what's legally registered and what NZLA can recommend, and the local label restricts it to warm-season.

For cool-season lawns dealing with annual poa, NZLA Etho is the option. It's a selective post-emergent (ethofumesate) safe on perennial ryegrass and tall fescue but not on fine fescues or browntop. Different chemistry, different approach (kills established poa rather than stopping germination), but it's the in-range NZLA option for poa in ryegrass.

For summer grasses (crab grass, crowsfoot, paspalum) in cool-season lawns, the standard NZ approach is post-emergent products and cultural control. There isn't a clean pre-emergent fit in the current NZLA range for cool-season.
