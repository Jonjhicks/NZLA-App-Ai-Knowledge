# What's the application rate for Blockade?

Check the current label for the exact rate (prodiamine rates are dose-sensitive, label changes get printed for a reason). Working figure for residual control in warm-season turf is in the low-rate range of the prodiamine spectrum, around 1 to 1.5 mL per 100 m² for a 4-month residual, higher for longer.

Carrier water: 4 to 8 L per 100 m². The active needs to be evenly spread across the soil surface to form a uniform pre-emergent barrier. Patchy application = patchy barrier = poa coming through in the gaps.

Water it in after application. 5 to 10 mm of irrigation or rainfall within 7 days activates the product into the top 25 to 50 mm of soil where weed seeds germinate. Without that activation, it's just sitting on the surface waiting.

Don't aerate, dethatch or disturb the soil surface for 4 to 6 months after application. Anything that breaks up the chemical barrier in the soil opens windows for weeds to come through. Plan your renovation before the Blockade application, not after.

Maximum once or twice a year. Multiple stacked applications can build up and damage warm-season turf root systems.
