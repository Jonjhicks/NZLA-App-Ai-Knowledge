# NZLA Etho

Ethofumesate at 500 g/L. HRAC Group 16 benzofuran. The selective option for poa annua control in ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. Pre and early post-emergent activity, and the chemistry that Harrington's research at Massey identifies as the practical option for poa in cool-season NZ turf.

## How it works

Ethofumesate inhibits the synthesis of very long chain fatty acids (VLCFAs) — essential components of plant cell membranes and waxy cuticles. Without VLCFA synthesis, susceptible plants can't build new cell membranes as cells divide, can't maintain the cuticle that prevents water loss, and growth fails.

Works best on actively dividing cells — germinating seedlings and emerging shoots at early growth stages. Once a weed is well-established, effectiveness drops because there's less rapid cell division to target.

## Selectivity — and the hard species restriction

Perennial ryegrass and tall fescue rapidly metabolise ethofumesate through their own detoxification pathways before it can cause damage. Fine fescues, warm-season grasses, and most  don't have the same metabolic capacity.

**Fine fescue cannot be treated with Etho — it will die.** Same for kikuyu and couch. Tall fescue can be knocked back if it's not actively growing.

This is the most important thing to check before applying. If you're unsure what grass is in the lawn, find out first. A "fescue" lawn might be tall fescue (fine for Etho) or fine fescue (will die from Etho). A spot test on a small area and a 4 to 6 week wait is the right approach if species are uncertain.

## Pre-em and post-em in one product

**Pre-emergent phase:** applied before weed seeds germinate, ethofumesate sits in the top centimetre of soil. Germinating shoots absorb it as they push through. Lipid synthesis fails, the seedling dies. Water in with 5 to 7 mm of irrigation-general immediately after to move the active into the soil.

**Post-emergent phase:** applied to early-stage absorbed through foliage and the developing root system. Let it dry on the foliage — don't irrigate immediately after a post-em application.

The two-application pattern (second spray 14 to 21 days after the first) catches staggered germination that escapes the first pass.

## When to use

Autumn through winter — April to August — when poa is actively growing and ryegrass is in its strong season. The window before poa sets seed matters: apply too late and the seed bank tops up for next year.

Multiple lighter applications (three passes at 4 to 6 week intervals through autumn and winter) produce better results than a single heavy application. Progressive thinning of the poa, lower risk to the ryegrass.

Soil temps under 18°C for cleanest results. Hot lawns and Etho don't work well together.

## Rate and application

Check the current label. Carrier water minimum 4 L per 100m². No surfactants. Use water above 5°C. Apply spray solution immediately after mixing — don't store prepared solution.

Grazing withholding period: 28 days.

**Pre-em:** water in with 5 to 7 mm immediately after. **Post-em:** dry on foliage, don't irrigate immediately.

## What to expect

Etho thins poa rather than providing instant knock-down. Over a season or two of consistent application, poa populations decline and ryegrass fills the space. Don't expect a clean result from a single application.

Brief yellowing or paling of the lawn for 1 to 2 weeks after application is normal. The lawn greens back up as it metabolises the chemistry.

## Common mistakes

Not knowing what grass is in the lawn. Fine fescue restriction is absolute.

Adding a surfactant. Label specifically prohibits it.

Mowing within 3 days either side. Removes leaf area the product needs.

Skipping the second application. Staggered germination escapes a single spray.

Watering in immediately after a post-em application. Washes the active off the foliage.

## Best paired with

A dense, well-fed ryegrass or tall fescue sward. Poa establishes in thin, weak, over-watered turf. The herbicide works better alongside cultural improvements. Correct mowing height (40 to 50 mm), adequate autumn fertility, irrigation-general only when needed.
