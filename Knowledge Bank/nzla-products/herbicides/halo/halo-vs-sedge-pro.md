# Should I use Halo or Sedge Pro?

Two options for sedge control with overlapping but slightly different uses.

Halo is for general sedge and broadleaf control with shorter pre-emergent residual. Halosulfuron-methyl base. Good for nutgrass, kyllinga, some broadleaves. Standard option for sedge in most situations.

Sedge Pro is the heavier hitter for tougher sedge problems. Different active mix designed for harder-to-kill species and heavier infestations. Longer residual.

Decision tree:
- Light to moderate nutgrass or kyllinga: Halo.
- Heavy or established sedge infestation: Sedge Pro.
- Mixed sedge + broadleaf in the same area: Halo handles both, single application.
- Sedge in fine turf or sensitive species: read the label carefully on both, neither product is fully safe on every grass.

For most home lawn sedge issues, one or two applications of Halo handles it. Sedge Pro becomes the option when Halo's done a round and the sedge is still pushing back.

Check both labels for the registered turfgrass species before applying. Sedge herbicide chemistry can be species-restrictive.
