# When should I apply NZLA Halo?

Halo is for sedge and broadleaf control in established turf. Apply when target weeds are actively growing, ideally spring and summer (October to March) when sedges are pushing.

Soil temps 16 °C or warmer for the chemistry to move properly through the plant. Cool autumn or winter applications underperform.

Lawn well-watered but not waterlogged. Target weeds well-watered too, drought-stressed sedges don't take up the herbicide.

No rain forecast for 6 to 8 hours after application. The active needs to absorb into the leaf before water can wash it off.

Slow result, expect 3 to 4 weeks for visible knockdown of sedges. They don't drop overnight like a hormone hits a dandelion. A follow-up application may be needed for heavy infestations or for kyllinga, which is tougher than nutgrass.

Established lawn only, not on new seed less than 8 weeks old.

Standard NZ approach is one spring application as sedges start moving, then a follow-up in late summer if needed.
