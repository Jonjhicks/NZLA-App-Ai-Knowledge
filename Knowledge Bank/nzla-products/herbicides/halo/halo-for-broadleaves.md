# Does Halo work on broadleaf weeds too?

Yes, on several common broadleaves. Halo isn't purely a sedge product, it covers a useful list of broadleaf weeds at the same rates used for sedge control. Targets include:

- Daisy
- Plantain (some control)
- Catsear, hawksbeard
- Chickweed
- Some clovers

For a mixed weed scenario (some sedge plus some broadleaves), Halo can do both jobs in one spray.

It's not a full substitute for BWC on broadleaves. The BWC chemistry (clopyralid + MCPA + diflufenican) covers a wider broadleaf spectrum and gets stronger control of woody and persistent broadleaves than Halo does.

The decision depends on what's in the lawn. If you've got nutgrass plus daisies plus chickweed, Halo handles all three. If you've got mostly broadleaves with some persistent species like oxalis or hairy bittercress, BWC or Gold is the better choice.

Don't tank-mix Halo and BWC at full rates. The hormone chemistry in BWC can interact with the halosulfuron and you risk turf damage. If you need both, apply them separately about 10 to 14 days apart.
