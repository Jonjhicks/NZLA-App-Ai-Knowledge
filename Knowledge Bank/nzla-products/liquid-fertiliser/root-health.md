# NZLA Root Health

Liquid kelp biostimulant built around freshly harvested Ecklonia maxima — the South African kelp species with the highest natural auxin content of any commercial kelp extract. Drives root development, lateral root branching, and overall plant resilience under stress.

## Why the kelp species matters

Auxins are the plant hormones that drive root growth, lateral root branching, and root mass. Plants produce their own auxins, but supplemental auxin from kelp extract accelerates root development beyond what the plant does on its own. That's the basis of every commercial root-stimulant biostimulant.

Not all kelp species have the same auxin content. Ecklonia maxima has the highest natural concentration, which is why it's the species used in premium root-stimulant products. Freshly harvested rather than dried-and-rehydrated preserves the hormone activity. Cheaper kelp powders made from other species, or poorly processed, don't deliver the same auxin activity regardless of what the label says.

## Why root mass matters

A lawn's drought tolerance, nutrient uptake efficiency resistance, and recovery from wear all depend heavily on root depth and density.

Shallow weak roots: fast drying, can't access nutrients in the soil profile, slow recovery, susceptible to stress-driven .

Deep dense roots: draws water and nutrients from depth, fast wear recovery, handles drought, withstands  pressure better.

Root Health drives the lawn toward the second outcome. Over a season of regular application, root depth and density build in ways that no amount of nitrogen alone achieves.

## Rate and application

50 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 5 L of water. Apply every 3 to 4 weeks. Check the current label.

Foliar uptake and soil drench both work. Light irrigation-general 4 to 6 hours after application moves the kelp compounds into the soil for additional root-zone activity.

Apply in cool conditions or evening. Heat-damaged product loses potency as the hormones break down. Store the bottle out of direct sun and away from freezing temperatures.

## When to use

4 to 6 weeks before expected summer drought, to build root depth in advance.

New lawn establishment alongside Liquid Starter. Liquid Starter feeds the seedlings; Root Health drives the root system those seedlings are trying to build.

Post-renovation after core aeration, where you want roots filling the cores.

Monthly programme through active growing season on any lawn where root depth and stress tolerance are a priority.

Lawns on sand-based profiles or shallow soils where rooting depth is limited.

Under a PGR programme — PGR redirects energy from leaf growth to root development, and Root Health amplifies the root response.

## What to expect

The effect is below ground. You won't see a dramatic above-ground response from Root Health alone. What shows up over months: the lawn handles drought better, recovers from wear faster, and responds more efficiently to the nutrition it's already getting.

A single application won't transform anything. The benefit builds over a season.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a fertiliser. Root Health doesn't feed the plant directly — it triggers the plant to build a better root system, which then makes nutrition more effective.

Expecting visible above-ground results within days.

Skipping it because of the slow return. The long-term return on root depth is substantial, especially on sandy soils or lawns heading into dry summers.
