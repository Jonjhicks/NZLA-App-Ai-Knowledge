# NZLA Nurture

A combined organic liquid fertiliser and biological inoculant at 10-1-11 NPK. The organic-sourced nutrients and the beneficial bacteria and fungi work together — the biology benefits from the organic matter in the feed, and the feed works better as the biology builds.

## What makes it different

Charger, Liquid N, Liquid Boost, and Iron+ are nutrient products. They feed the plant directly. Nurture feeds the plant and builds the soil community that supports the plant over time.

The biological component includes nitrogen-fixing bacteria (pulling atmospheric nitrogen into plant-available forms), phosphate-solubilising bacteria (releasing locked-up soil P into plant-available phosphate), and beneficial fungi including mycorrhizal-associated species that extend the effective root system. That combination of nutrient cycling and root enhancement compounds over a programme of applications.

## The organic nutrient side

Organic-sourced nutrients release more slowly than synthetic. The nitrogen has to be processed by soil microbes before the plant can use it. That means no spike-and-trough, but it also means the lawn needs warmth and active soil biology to respond well. Organic nitrogen applied in cold conditions doesn't move fast.

The trace elements come naturally with the organic source material — plant-derived inputs carry the micronutrients the source plants needed, in bioavailable forms. Combined with the organic carbon load in the formulation (which feeds the microbes), the biology and the nutrition reinforce each other.

## Rate and application

400 to 600 mL per 100m² in 2 to 5 L of water. Check the current label.

Use the higher rate (600 mL) for the first two applications when building the initial microbial population from a low base. Drop to maintenance rate (400 mL) for ongoing programme use.

Apply in cool conditions or evening — not hot direct sun. The biology needs to survive the application to establish.

Light irrigation-general 2 to 4 hours after application moves the biological component into the soil where it can establish.

Wait 2 to 4 weeks after any broad-spectrum fungicide application before applying Nurture. Fungicide chemistry is hostile to the biology.

## When to use

New lawn establishment, starting 4 to 6 weeks post-germination to build biology from the start.

Lawns recovering from heavy chemical input where soil biology has taken hits.

Sandy or low-organic-matter soils where natural biological function is sparse.

After scarification or major renovation where you want to re-establish the biological community as part of recovery.

Routine maintenance through active growing season on any lawn where long-term soil health is a priority.

## What it's not for

Quick recovery push. At 10-1-11, Nurture's profile isn't high-N for fast response. Use Liquid Boost for that.

Pre-event sharpening. Iron+ responds within hours; Nurture's benefit compounds over months.

Single-application use. The value is in the programme.

## Pairs well with

Granular base feed (All Seasons or All Seasons N+) for foundation NPK. Nurture supports the soil biology, doesn't replace bulk nutrition.

Humic+ for additional nutrient uptake support alongside the biology.

Restore for further soil biology support.

Don't rotate with broad-spectrum fungicides back-to-back. If fungicide is required, time Nurture applications around it, not on top of it.
