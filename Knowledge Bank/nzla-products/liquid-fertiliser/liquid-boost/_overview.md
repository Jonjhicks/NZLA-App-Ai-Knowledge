# NZLA Liquid Boost

The premium push feed. 15% nitrogen with 6% iron and 2% manganese, but the technology underneath those headline numbers is what separates Liquid Boost from a basic high-N liquid. EDTA chelate iron, ammonium stabilised nitrogen, and a slow-release fraction that means the green-up doesn't completely fade within two weeks the way it does on cheap foliar urea.

## What it does

Three things, working together.

The immediate response comes from the soluble nitrogen fraction. Foliar uptake of nitrogen is fast, and the lawn shows a colour and growth response within 3 to 5 days of application.

The sustained response comes from the ammonium stabilised nitrogen and the SRN-IQ slow-release fraction. Both keep nitrogen feeding the plant for weeks after the initial flush, so the lawn doesn't drop back into a hungry trough the way it would on a basic urea foliar.

The colour comes from the iron (driving chlorophyll directly) and the manganese (which supports photosynthesis enzymes). Both held in plant-available form by the EDTA chelate.

## Why the EDTA iron chemistry matters

Iron is a difficult nutrient to deliver foliar. Free iron oxidises rapidly into rust-like compounds that the plant can't use. Iron without proper chelation precipitates out of solution in the tank, blocks nozzles, and gives uneven coverage on the leaf.

EDTA chelation binds the iron in an organic complex that protects it from oxidation, keeps it soluble in the tank, and releases it gradually for plant uptake. 33% of the iron in Liquid Boost is in EDTA chelate form, which is why the iron actually performs rather than just being a number on the label.

## Why the ammonium stabilisation matters

The 15% nitrogen could be delivered as straight urea, in which case it would all flush through and be gone within a couple of weeks. Liquid Boost uses an ammonium stabilised fraction instead, which slows the conversion of ammonium to nitrate in the soil and on the leaf. That extends the feeding window, reduces nitrogen loss to leaching, and keeps the lawn fed longer between applications.

The SRN-IQ slow-release component adds another layer. Methylene urea breakdown by soil microbes provides a steady tail of nitrogen release over weeks after the initial application.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 5 to 15 L of water.

Apply every 14 to 28 days. The slow-release fraction means the longer interval (28 days) still delivers continuous feeding.

Avoid mowing for 24 hours after application so the spray gets absorbed rather than cut off and bagged.

Will stain concrete, paving and stone. Mask off edges or rinse immediately.

Don't use in equipment that's been used for herbicide. Iron and herbicide residue don't mix well.

## When to use

Quick green-up and growth response when you want both.

Push through active growing season (spring, early summer) alongside granular base feed.

Recovery from wear or stress where you need to drive growth fast.

Pre-event or pre-photo polish where colour and density both need a lift.

Cool transition periods where you want some response from a foliar that granular won't provide.

Lawns that need a high-N foliar but where the standard urea-based products are flushing through too fast.

## When not to use

Heading into winter. The high N will force soft growth into disease pressure, slow-release fraction or not.

On stressed turf in heat. Adding push to a heat-stressed lawn makes it worse.

In equipment with herbicide residue.

On concrete or stone without rinse-off planning.

## How it sits next to the others

**Liquid Boost**: 15% N (mostly fast with stabilised plus slow-release tail), 6% Fe (EDTA chelate), 2% Mn. The push tool.

**Liquid N**: 85% slow-release N, no iron or manganese. The steady feed tool.

**Charger**: 7% N, 7% K, 4% Fe, 2% Mn. Half the nitrogen, matched potassium, more about maintenance than push.

**Iron+**: 6% Fe only, no nitrogen. Pure colour without growth.

Choose based on what you actually want the lawn to do. Boost for fast and lasting. Liquid N for steady. Charger for balanced maintenance. Iron+ for colour-only.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon after application. Loses the feed to the catcher.

Overdosing expecting more response. Above 400 mL/100m² you'll see leaf burn rather than more colour.

Spilling on concrete. Iron stains.

Using through winter on cool-season turf. Wrong tool for the season.

Comparing against cheap high-N foliars and expecting the same result. The EDTA iron and stabilised N chemistry are doing real work — without that, you're looking at urea flush and rapid fade.
