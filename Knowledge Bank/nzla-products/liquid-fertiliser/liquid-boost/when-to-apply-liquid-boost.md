# When should I apply Liquid Boost?

Episodically through the growing season, when the lawn needs a push. Not a routine monthly application like Charger.

Specific situations:
- 7 to 10 days after seed germination on a new lawn (light rate, supports establishment alongside Liquid Starter).
- Post-disease recovery (after fungicide application has stopped the infection).
- Post-renovation, once new seedlings are 4 to 6 weeks old.
- Pre-event push (2 to 3 weeks before a date you want the lawn looking sharp).
- Mid-summer recovery on lawns that have been hit by heat stress, once watering is restored.
- Late summer / early autumn to push the lawn into recovery from summer wear.

Soil temps 14 °C or warmer for the lawn to take up and respond.

Not the right time:
- Drought-stressed lawn (water first).
- Heat-stressed lawn over 28 °C with no irrigation.
- Disease pressure still active.
- Going into winter (don't push soft growth into cold conditions).
- Lawn under PGR regulation (the response will be muted).

For a typical home lawn, 2 to 4 Liquid Boost applications a year covers the common push scenarios. More than that and you're probably running a foliar programme, in which case Charger is the better base product.
