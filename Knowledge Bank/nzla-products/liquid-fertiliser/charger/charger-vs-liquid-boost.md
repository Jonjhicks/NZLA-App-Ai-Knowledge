# What's the difference between Charger and Liquid Boost?

Different roles in the foliar nitrogen toolkit.

Charger is slow-release foliar nitrogen. Steady contribution over 3 to 4 weeks per application. Programme base for monthly foliar feeding through the season.

Liquid Boost is faster nitrogen for push and response. Quicker uptake, visible response within days. Lasts 2 to 3 weeks. Used for recovery, push feeds, single-event applications when you want a fast result.

Decision tree:
- Running a foliar programme through the growing season, want steady nitrogen with consistent colour: Charger.
- Need a quick response for recovery or pushing the lawn after stress: Liquid Boost.
- Want to combine both: Charger as the monthly base, Liquid Boost for the occasional push when needed.

Typical home lawn programme using both: monthly Charger for the steady nitrogen base, one or two Liquid Boost applications a year for specific recovery situations or pre-event push (the lawn looking sharp for a wedding, garden party, photo shoot).

For most home lawns running a granular base programme (All Seasons or All Seasons N+), Liquid Boost is the more useful liquid choice because it's used episodically rather than monthly. Charger comes into its own on high-input lawns running foliar-led programmes.
