# What does NZLA Charger do?

Charger is a slow-release foliar nitrogen base for a foliar programme. Think of it as the foliar equivalent of a granular base feed, you put it down regularly and it carries the nitrogen load through the season.

The chemistry is slow-release nitrogen in foliar form. Not a quick spike of fast-release urea (that's Liquid N), more a steady contribution to the nitrogen requirements over 3 to 4 weeks per application.

Where it fits:
- Lawns running a foliar-led programme rather than relying on granular feeds.
- High-input lawns (bowling greens, golf, top-end home lawns) where you want even nitrogen delivery without granular spikes.
- Lawns under PGR programmes where granular nitrogen spikes don't align well with the PGR cycle.
- Recovery from low-fertility situations where you want steady push without burn.

Foliar nitrogen has the advantage of bypassing soil chemistry, which can tie up nutrients on heavy clay or high-pH soils. The leaf absorbs it directly, the plant uses it, no waiting for soil release.

Doesn't replace granular feeds entirely for most home lawns. A typical programme would be a granular base (All Seasons or All Seasons N+) two or three times a year with Charger as a monthly foliar maintenance feed between.
