# NZLA Charger

The maintenance backbone of the foliar range. 7-0-7 plus 4% iron and 2% manganese, but the technology underneath is what makes Charger different to a basic balanced liquid. Dual-form nitrogen, premium citrate/carbonate potassium, low salt index, and a specific bias toward lateral growth and density rather than vertical push.

## What it does

Charger does two jobs that a single-form fertiliser can't easily do at once. It pushes lateral turf growth — the lawn spreads sideways, density goes up, the canopy tightens — without forcing the vertical flush that high-N alone produces. And it holds colour and plant strength steady through periods when growth itself is slow.

The dual-form nitrogen is the foundation. The urea fraction is converted by the soil enzyme urease into ammonium, which gets held on the soil's cation exchange sites for sustained uptake. The nitrate fraction is immediately available for fast response. Together they give both speed and duration without relying purely on either pathway. An SRN-IQ slow-release component extends the feeding window further.

## Why the potassium form matters

The interesting bit of Charger is the chemistry of the potassium. Most liquid potassium products use potassium chloride or potassium sulphate. Both work, both carry a higher salt load and can cause foliar burn at higher rates or in heat. And chloride-based products corrode spray equipment over time.

Charger uses potassium in two non-salt forms: a citrate complex and a carbonate complex. The citrate molecule is already present in every plant cell as part of normal metabolism, so when potassium arrives bound to citrate the plant recognises and assimilates it immediately without first needing to break down a salt. The carbonate form provides additional plant-available potassium that can take up via either leaf or soil routes.

Two practical effects. Foliar burn risk is very low, so you can spray in conditions where a standard K product would scorch. And the spray equipment doesn't corrode the way it does with chloride-based products.

## Why lateral growth matters

A push fertiliser like Liquid Boost drives a vertical growth flush — the lawn grows up, you mow more often, the canopy can thin out as energy goes into top growth at the expense of tillering.

Charger's balanced N+K chemistry pushes lateral growth instead. The plant tillers, spreads sideways, fills in bare spots, builds canopy density. Same growth biomass, redirected to a more useful place. The lawn ends up denser, with better wear tolerance and better aesthetic.

That makes Charger particularly useful on lawns that are thin or filling in, on warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) through cooler months where you want maintained density without forcing flush, and on cool-season lawns through transitions where you want feeding without forcing soft growth that disease can take advantage of.

## Rate and application

200 to 500 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 4 L of water. Light rate for maintenance, heavier rate when you want sustained growth and density.

Foliar application means it goes onto the leaf for direct uptake. Hold off mowing for 24 hours so the spray gets absorbed rather than cut off and bagged.

Repeat every 14 days when you want to maintain steady growth and colour, less often for maintenance only.

## How it sits next to the other liquids

Compared to **Liquid Boost** (15% N, 6% Fe), Charger has half the nitrogen and matched potassium. Liquid Boost is the vertical push tool, Charger is the lateral and maintenance tool.

Compared to **Iron+** (6% Fe only), Charger has the nitrogen and potassium that drive growth. Use Iron+ when you want green without growth, Charger when you want both at a controlled pace with the density angle.

Compared to **Liquid N** (slow-release N), Charger adds the potassium and trace elements. Liquid N is the steady N delivery, Charger is the balanced steady delivery with density bias.

## When to use

Maintenance feeding between granular applications.

Lawns that need density rather than vertical growth.

Warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) through autumn and winter to hold colour and steady growth.

Cool-season lawns through transitions where you want feeding without forcing soft growth.

Pre-event polish that needs to last more than a few days.

Heat conditions where standard liquid K products would burn.

## When not to use

Active recovery from major damage. Charger's nitrogen level isn't high enough for fast recovery — reach for Liquid Boost or All Seasons N+ for that.

Hot heat-stressed turf. Even a low-N foliar can add to heat stress, although Charger's low salt index makes it more tolerant than most.

In equipment used for herbicide without thorough cleanout.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon after application. You'll lose the feed.

Treating it like a push fertiliser and applying it too often. The 14-day interval is there for a reason.

Spilling on concrete. The iron and manganese both stain.

Expecting it to bring a yellow, hungry lawn back to dark green by itself. Charger maintains and densifies. Liquid Boost rescues.

Substituting cheap potassium chloride foliar products and expecting the same performance. The K form and the dual-form N are doing real work.

## Best paired with

Iron+ as an additional colour boost if the lawn needs more green than Charger delivers on its own.

Slow-release granular base (All Seasons or All Seasons N+) for foundation feeding, Charger as the foliar maintenance between granular applications.

NZLA PGR through active growing season for canopy management — Charger drives the lateral density, the PGR holds the vertical growth tight.
