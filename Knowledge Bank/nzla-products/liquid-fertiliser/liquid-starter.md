# NZLA Liquid Starter

Foliar establishment fertiliser at 8-10-5 with kelp. The chemistry is specifically chosen for the seedling stage: phosphite-based phosphorus, kelp biostimulant, and slow-release nitrogen. This combination is what professional turf establishment programmes use, and it's quite different to applying any standard liquid fertiliser onto a new lawn.

## Why standard liquid feeds don't suit establishment

A new seedling is putting down its first root system in soil it hasn't yet built biological relationships with. It has limited leaf area, a fragile root system, and low tolerance for high-N burn. Standard NPK ratios aren't suited to this window. Liquid Starter is.

## The phosphite P story

The "10" in the 8-10-5 is the most important number. Establishment needs phosphorus for root development. Most starter products deliver phosphorus as phosphate (PO4), which is the form plants actually use. The problem: phosphate reacts quickly in soil, binds to calcium, iron, and aluminium, and becomes unavailable within days.

Liquid Starter uses phosphite (PO3) instead. Phosphite converts gradually to plant-available phosphate via microbial and chemical pathways over weeks. The phosphorus stays available through the whole establishment window, not just the first few days. The seedling gets the P it needs to drive root development as roots are actively forming.

## The kelp component

Kelp extract supplies natural plant growth regulators (cytokinins for shoot growth and chlorophyll production, auxins for root growth), amino acids, micronutrients, and stress-tolerance compounds. These work together to improve early seedling vigour — and the auxins specifically drive root growth at a time when root establishment is the whole game.

## Slow-release nitrogen

The 8% nitrogen includes a methylene urea fraction, so the seedling gets sustained feeding rather than a soluble urea hit that could burn fragile new growth. The release rate matches what an early-stage plant can actually use.

## Rate and application

New lawn establishment: 100 to 200 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 5 L of water. Check the current label.

Apply 4 to 5 weeks after sowing, once seedlings have enough leaf area to take up foliar spray. Earlier than that and the leaves are too small to absorb it effectively.

Repeat applications every 7 to 10 days through the establishment phase (typically 4 to 6 weeks post-germination). A light irrigation-general 2 to 4 hours after application is fine.

For post-stress recovery (established lawns): 200 to 300 mL per 100m².

Use a gentle spray pattern. Seedlings are physically fragile. No blasting with a high-pressure sprayer.

## Liquid Starter vs NZLA Starter (granular)

The granular goes into the soil before sowing. It supplies phosphorus to the root zone at the depth where roots will grow, for the first 12 weeks of establishment.

Liquid Starter goes on after germination. It delivers foliar P, kelp, and nitrogen through the leaf once seedlings are actively growing.

The two work together, not as alternatives. Granular Starter first, seed, then Liquid Starter once seedlings emerge. The Gateway Starter Package bundles both.

If only one is possible: Granular Starter is more important because root-zone P at establishment drives root development more effectively than surface foliar P. Liquid Starter accelerates the result on top.

## Transition point

At 8 to 12 weeks once the lawn is mown three or more times and clearly established, switch to All Seasons granular as the maintenance base. Liquid Starter's establishment-specific chemistry isn't needed past that window.

## Common mistakes

Applying too early, before seedlings have enough leaf area. The 4 to 5 week wait is real.

High-pressure spraying. Fragile seedlings get knocked around.

Treating it as a substitute for Granular Starter at soil prep. Different role, different timing, different mechanism.

Using it as a maintenance feed on established lawns that aren't in recovery. Charger or Liquid Boost is better suited for that.
