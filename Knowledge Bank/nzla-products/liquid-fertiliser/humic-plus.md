# NZLA Humic+

Liquid humic and fulvic acid soil amendment at 12% humic and 2% fulvic acid, with an organic catalyst package that drives microbiological activity. This isn't a fertiliser. Humic+ makes other fertilisers work better.

## What it does

Humic and fulvic acids are natural organic compounds that accumulate in soils with high organic matter. They chelate mineral nutrients (especially iron, manganese, and zinc) into plant-available forms and support soil microbial life. In a healthy loam soil they develop over time. In sand-based root zones, new builds, and low-organic-matter soils, they're largely absent.

The result of low humic content: nutrient lockup (where fertiliser applied at correct rates converts to insoluble forms before the plant can use it), weak microbial activity, and poor fertiliser response. Standard granular or liquid feeding doesn't fix this if the uptake chemistry isn't there.

Humic+ restores that chemistry.

## Humic vs fulvic

They're related but do different jobs.

Humic acids are high molecular weight and bind tightly to minerals. They release nutrients slowly over an extended period and build long-term soil organic chemistry. Their effect is durable.

Fulvic acids are lower molecular weight and more biologically active. They move through soil and into the plant more readily, with a faster visible effect on nutrient uptake. They also help carry other nutrients (foliar-applied minerals especially) into the plant.

The 12:2 ratio in Humic+ is standard for professional turf: the humic builds the soil chemistry over time, the fulvic delivers the immediate uptake support.

## The organic catalysts

The catalyst component provides carbon sources and metabolic precursors that activate the soil's existing microbial community. In low-biology soils this provides the kickstart that translates into better nutrient cycling and root zone support. The catalysts and the humic/fulvic work together, which is why buying basic humic powder is a different product.

## Rate and application

50 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 5 L of water. Use more carrier water in hot weather. Check the current label for the exact rate.

Apply every 14 to 28 days through the growing season.

Can be watered in after application, as the benefit is in the soil. 5 to 10 mm of irrigation-general is standard. Heavy irrigation-general (over 20 mm) can dilute the chemistry through the profile too quickly.

## When to use

Sand-based root zones. Low-organic-matter soils. Alkaline soils where iron and trace elements lock up readily. Situations where granular feeds aren't producing the expected response.

After fungicide use to restore some soil biological function.

Routine through the growing season as part of a maintenance programme.

## Common mistakes

Expecting direct visible response. Humic+ improves uptake efficiency; the visible effect shows up as better fertiliser response, not as a direct green-up.

Applying with insufficient carrier water in hot conditions.

Treating it as a replacement for nitrogen. Humic+ supports the chemistry that makes nutrition available, doesn't supply primary nutrients itself.

## Pairs well with

Iron+ in particular: the iron chelation benefit of humic acids is well-established. Humic+ and Iron+ together outperform either alone.

Granular base feeds (All Seasons, All Seasons N+) where Humic+ improves the uptake of the granular nutrients.

Amino+ and Restore as part of a broader biological and amendment programme.
