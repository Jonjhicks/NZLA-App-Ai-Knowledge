# NZLA Root Health

A premium liquid kelp biostimulant built around freshly harvested Ecklonia maxima — the South African kelp species with the highest natural auxin content in any commercial kelp extract. Drives root development, lateral root branching, and overall plant resilience under stress.

## What it does

Kelp extracts are biostimulants — they don't supply primary plant nutrition the way a fertiliser does, but they contain plant growth regulators (specifically auxins and cytokinins) and other natural compounds that drive specific plant responses.

The auxin content is the bit that matters for root development. Auxins are the plant hormones that drive root growth, lateral root branching, and root mass. Plants make their own auxins, but supplemental auxin application accelerates root development beyond what the plant produces on its own. That's the basis of every commercial root-stimulant biostimulant on the market.

Ecklonia maxima has the highest natural auxin content of any commercial kelp species, which is why premium root-stimulant products use it specifically rather than cheaper kelp species. Freshly harvested rather than dried-and-rehydrated maintains the auxin activity.

## Why root mass matters

A lawn's drought tolerance, nutrient uptake efficiency, disease resistance and recovery from wear are all heavily influenced by root mass and root depth.

A lawn with shallow weak roots dries out fast, can't reach nutrients in the soil profile, recovers slowly from damage, and is more susceptible to stress-driven disease.

A lawn with deep dense roots draws water and nutrients from deeper in the profile, recovers fast from wear, tolerates drought, and handles disease pressure better.

Root Health drives the lawn toward the latter. Over a season of regular application, the root system builds depth and density that no amount of fertiliser alone can achieve.

## Rate and application

50 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 5 L of water.

Apply every 3 to 4 weeks during the active growing season.

Foliar uptake and soil drench both work — the kelp compounds move through the plant via both routes.

## When to use

Routine through the growing season as part of a soil-health and root-development programme.

New lawn establishment alongside Liquid Starter — Liquid Starter feeds the seedlings, Root Health drives root development specifically.

Post-stress recovery, particularly after wear damage, drought, or scarifying.

Sand-based root zones where natural biology is sparse and rooting depth is limited.

Lawns going into challenging seasons (summer drought, winter cold) where deeper roots will pay off.

## When not to use

Mixed with broad-spectrum fungicides. The biological inoculants and the kelp compounds can be affected by fungicide tank mixes.

In hot direct sun on dry foliage. Apply in cool conditions or evening.

In equipment with significant herbicide residue.

As a replacement for nutritional feeding. Root Health drives root development, doesn't supply primary nutrients.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a fertiliser. The kelp doesn't feed the plant directly — it triggers the plant to build a better root system, which then makes the nutrition the plant takes up via other products more effective.

Expecting dramatic visible response above ground. The effect is on the root system, which you can't see. The visible response shows up over months as the lawn handles stress better, recovers faster, and uses water and nutrients more efficiently.

Skipping it because it doesn't have an obvious immediate result. The cumulative benefit from a season of regular application is substantial.

Using cheap kelp extracts and expecting the same response. Ecklonia maxima specifically, freshly harvested, is the standard the chemistry is built around. Generic dried kelp powders don't deliver the same auxin activity.
