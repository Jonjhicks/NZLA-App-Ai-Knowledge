# NZLA Liquid N

Premium slow-release liquid nitrogen with 85% of the nitrogen in slowly available forms. Built around two complementary technologies that deliver steady feeding over 4 to 10 weeks, without the spike-and-trough that standard liquid urea produces.

## Why slow-release liquid N matters

Standard liquid nitrogen products use fast-release urea. Quick green-up, growth flush, then taper off in a couple of weeks. The lawn drops back and you're back into the cycle of hungry-and-flush. That cycle also drives soft growth and  pressure.

Liquid N breaks that cycle. The nitrogen releases steadily, so the lawn feeds continuously rather than spiking. For sand-based root zones in particular, where standard urea leaches quickly out of the root zone before the plant can use it, slow-release liquid N is a fundamentally different tool.

## The two technologies

**DMPP nitrification inhibitor.** Slows the conversion of ammonium to nitrate in the soil. Ammonium is more stable, holds on cation exchange sites, and doesn't leach the way nitrate does. By slowing the conversion, DMPP extends the window over which applied nitrogen is available and reduces losses to leaching and volatilisation. This mechanism runs for 4 to 10 weeks.

**Methylene urea.** A polymer-based slow-release nitrogen fraction. Partially soluble on application (immediate response), with the remaining polymer fraction gradually broken down by soil microbes over weeks. The release rate is microbially metered — faster in warm active soil, slower in cool conditions. This adds the sustained tail beyond what DMPP alone covers.

The two stack together. DMPP handles the first window at the chemistry level. Methylene urea handles the longer tail through biology.

## Rate and application

Low rate: 500 mL per 100m² (approx 0.15 kg N/ha).
High rate: up to 1400 mL per 100m² (approx 0.42 kg N/ha).
Carrier water: 8 to 20 L per 100m². Check the current label.

Irrigate 2 to 5 mm within 24 hours of application to establish the slow-release mechanism in the root zone.

No mowing for 24 hours.

**Temperature restriction: do not apply above 26°C.** Ammonium stabilisation chemistry can lose stability at high temperatures and produce ammonia damage. This is a hard limit, not a guideline.

## Liquid N vs Liquid Boost

Liquid Boost (15% N, mostly fast with a stabilised tail): the push tool, quick response for recovery and events.
Liquid N (85% slow-release): the steady feed tool, consistent nitrogen without spike.

Both deliver nitrogen. Completely different time profiles. Use Liquid Boost when speed matters. Use Liquid N when consistency matters, sandy soil holds nutrients poorly, or when you want to avoid nitrogen-driven  pressure.

## Common mistakes

Applying above 26°C. The label cap is there for a reason — ammonia damage can result.

Treating it like Liquid Boost. The point of the chemistry is consistent feeding, not a flush. Expect a steady lawn, not a dramatic green-up.

Skipping the water-in. Moving the active into the root zone is what starts the slow-release process.

Using on a lawn that needs fast recovery. Liquid Boost is the right tool for that.
