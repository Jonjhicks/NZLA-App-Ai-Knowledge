# NZLA Humic+

Liquid humic and fulvic acid soil amendment at 12% humic and 2% fulvic acid, with organic catalysts that drive microbiological activity in the soil. The chemistry chelates mineral nutrients into plant-available forms, particularly useful in sand-based root zones and low-organic-matter soils where natural humic content is low.

## What it does

Humic and fulvic acids are naturally occurring organic compounds derived from decomposed organic matter. In a healthy soil with high organic matter, they accumulate naturally over time and do important work — chelating mineral nutrients (especially iron, manganese and zinc) into plant-available forms, supporting soil microbial life, and improving soil structure.

In sand-based root zones (golf greens, sports turf), low-organic-matter home lawn soils, and many new builds, the natural humic content is low to absent. The result is nutrient lockup (where minerals applied as fertiliser convert to insoluble forms before the plant can use them), poor microbial activity, and inefficient fertiliser response.

Humic+ supplies what's missing. The humic and fulvic acids restore the chelation chemistry. The organic catalysts give the soil microbes a metabolic kickstart.

## Humic vs fulvic — why the combination matters

Humic and fulvic acids are related but do different jobs.

**Humic acids** are high molecular weight organic compounds. They bind tightly to minerals and slowly release them. They sit in the soil profile for extended periods and build long-term soil organic chemistry. Their effect is durable but slow.

**Fulvic acids** are lower molecular weight and more biologically active. They move through the soil and into the plant more readily. They have a faster, more visible effect on nutrient uptake — and they help carry other nutrients (foliar-applied minerals especially) into the plant.

The 12:2 humic-to-fulvic ratio in NZLA Humic+ is the professional turf standard. The humic does the soil-building work, the fulvic does the immediate uptake support.

## Why the organic catalysts matter

The "organic catalyst" component is what drives the microbiological response. Soil microbes break down organic matter, cycle nutrients, support root development, and contribute to disease resistance. In low-biology soils they're sparse. The catalysts provide carbon sources and metabolic precursors that kick the existing microbial community into more active function.

## Rate and application

50 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 5 L of water.

Use more carrier water in hot weather to reduce burn risk.

Every 14 to 28 days through the growing season.

## When to use

Sand-based root zones (golf greens, sports turf, premium home lawns built on sand).

Low-organic-matter soils common in new builds or sandy coastal sites.

Alkaline soils where iron and other trace elements lock up readily. The humic chelation keeps them available.

Situations where granular fertilisers don't seem to be producing the expected response — often a sign of nutrient lockup.

Routine through the growing season as part of a maintenance programme.

After fungicide use to restore some soil biological function.

## When not to use

Hot weather without sufficient carrier water. Humic acids on hot foliage can cause minor burn.

In equipment with fungicide residue without thorough cleanout.

As a replacement for nitrogen-based feeding. Humic+ supports uptake, doesn't supply primary nutrients.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a fertiliser. It's an amendment that makes other fertilisers work better, not a feed in itself.

Applying with insufficient water in hot conditions.

Expecting visible response from Humic+ alone. The effect is on uptake, not on adding nutrients directly. You'll see better fertiliser response, not direct visible response.

Skipping it because it doesn't have an obvious immediate result.

Comparing against cheap humic powders. The chemistry matters — high-quality liquid humic/fulvic with organic catalysts isn't the same product as a feed-grade humic powder.

## Best paired with

Granular base fertiliser (All Seasons, All Seasons, Mini Prill) where Humic+ improves the uptake of the granular nutrients.

Iron+ in particular — the iron chelation effect of humic acids is well-established and the combination performs.

Restore and Amino+ as part of a broader biological and amendment programme.

Foliar fertilisers (Liquid Boost, Charger) for the fulvic-acid-driven uptake enhancement.
