# What does NZLA Humic+ do?

Humic+ is a liquid humic acid soil conditioner. Humic acids are large organic molecules derived from decomposed plant material that play a structural role in soil chemistry and biology. Applying humic acid to a lawn supports a number of soil functions:

- Improves cation exchange capacity (soil's ability to hold and release nutrients)
- Stimulates microbial activity (food source for soil biology)
- Buffers pH (resists rapid changes from rainfall or fertiliser applications)
- Improves soil structure over time (binds soil particles, improves aggregation)
- Chelates micronutrients (keeps iron, manganese, zinc plant-available)

What you'll see on the lawn:
- No immediate visible response (this is a soil product, not a foliar feed)
- Over 2 to 3 months of regular application, improved colour and density as the soil works better
- Over a season or two, noticeably better drought tolerance and disease resistance
- Reduced fertiliser inputs needed for the same lawn response (soil holds nutrients better)

Best on:
- Sandy soils with low organic matter
- Newly established lawns where soil biology is undeveloped
- Lawns under heavy chemical input where soil biology has taken hits
- Heavy clay soils with poor structure

Less impact on:
- Well-developed loamy soils with high organic matter already (smaller incremental benefit)
- Lawns over very shallow soil (limited soil depth limits the benefit)
