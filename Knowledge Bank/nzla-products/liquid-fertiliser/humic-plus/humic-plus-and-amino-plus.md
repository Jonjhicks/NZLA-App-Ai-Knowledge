# Should I use Humic+ and Amino+ together?

Yes, they complement each other well. Different angles on the same outcome (better lawn through better biology).

Humic+ works on the soil side: structural improvement, microbial food source, nutrient holding capacity, pH buffering. Slow build-up of benefits over weeks and months.

Amino+ works on the plant side: amino acid building blocks, stress tolerance, recovery support. Faster individual application response, builds resilience over a programme.

Combined programme:
- Tank-mix monthly Humic+ and Amino+ through the growing season.
- Add Iron+ for colour, Charger or Liquid N for nitrogen.
- One spray covers fertility, colour, soil biology and biostimulation.

The combination is particularly useful on:
- Sandy soils with low organic matter and weak biology
- Newly established lawns developing their first seasons of soil structure
- High-input lawns under PGR and chemical programmes where biology takes hits
- Lawns recovering from disease, drought or renovation stress

For a basic home lawn programme on healthy soil, you can get by with one or the other rather than both. Humic+ alone is the better single choice if soil biology is the priority. Amino+ alone is the better single choice if stress recovery is the priority. Together, they cover both.

Not a substitute for proper fertility. Run Humic+ and Amino+ on top of a granular base programme (All Seasons or All Seasons N+), not instead of it.
