# NZLA Nurture

A premium organic balanced liquid fertiliser with beneficial bacteria and fungi. 10-1-11 NPK profile (balanced low-N, equal K, with the trace element package) plus a biological inoculant component. The combination addresses both immediate nutrition and long-term soil biological function in a single product.

## What it does

Most liquid fertilisers supply nutrients only. Nurture supplies the nutrients (NPK at 10-1-11 plus trace elements) and the biological community that supports soil health over time.

The biological component includes beneficial bacteria and fungi that contribute to nutrient cycling, organic matter breakdown, root zone support, and some disease suppression effects. Over time, repeated application builds the biological diversity of the soil, which has knock-on benefits for fertiliser response, root function, and disease resistance.

## Why organic sources matter

The "organic" label means the nutrients are derived from biological sources (composted plant materials, fish emulsion, kelp, etc.) rather than synthetic chemistry. Several practical effects flow from that.

**The nutrient release is slower and more gradual.** Organic nitrogen has to be processed by soil microbes before it becomes plant-available. That's slower than synthetic urea, which means no spike-and-trough, but it also means the lawn needs warmth and soil microbial activity to use it.

**Trace elements come with the nutrient package naturally.** Plant-derived organic fertilisers carry the trace elements that the source plants needed. They arrive in plant-bioavailable forms.

**The carbon load supports soil biology.** The organic matter in the product feeds the soil microbes, which is why the biological inoculant component complements the nutrition. The two reinforce each other.

## The biology, in more detail

Nurture's biological component includes nitrogen-fixing bacteria (which pull atmospheric nitrogen into plant-available forms in the root zone), phosphate-solubilising bacteria (which release locked-up soil phosphorus into plant-available phosphate), and beneficial fungi (including species associated with mycorrhizal root partnerships, where the fungi extend the effective root system far beyond the plant's own roots).

That combination of nutrient cycling and root enhancement is what makes Nurture's effect compound over time. The biology doesn't just process the fertiliser, it builds the soil's own capacity to support the lawn.

## Rate and application

400 to 600 mL per 100m² in 2 to 5 L of water.

Use the higher rate (600 mL) for the first two applications when starting on a new lawn or a soil where the biology has been knocked back. Drop to maintenance rate (400 mL) thereafter.

## When to use

Lawns where building soil biology is part of the long-term plan.

Organic-leaning programmes where the synthetic high-N liquids don't fit the brief.

Post-stress recovery alongside the biological build.

Established lawns as routine maintenance through active growing season.

After scarification or major renovation, where re-establishing soil biology is part of the recovery.

Sand-based root zones where natural biology is sparse.

## When not to use

Mixed with broad-spectrum fungicide. Defeats the biological purpose.

In equipment with herbicide residue.

In hot direct sun. Apply in cool conditions or evening for biology survival.

As a primary push feed. Nurture's profile is balanced, not high-N — use Liquid Boost for push.

## Common mistakes

Mixing with fungicides without checking timing. Apply Nurture 2 to 4 weeks after any fungicide application so the biology has a chance to establish.

Treating it as a replacement for the granular base. Nurture supports the soil biology, doesn't replace foundation NPK feeding.

Applying too dilute. Stay in the 2 to 5 L water range for the carrier.

Expecting instant visible response. The biological build is gradual — months of regular application before the cumulative benefit shows.

## Best paired with

Granular base feed (All Seasons or All Seasons N+) for foundation nutrition.

Restore for additional biological soil support.

Humic+ to support nutrient uptake alongside the biology.

Avoid alternating with broad-spectrum fungicides — if fungicide is needed, separate the applications by 2 to 4 weeks.
