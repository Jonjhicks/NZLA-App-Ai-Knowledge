# What's the application rate for Nurture?

400 to 600 mL per 100 m² in 2 to 5 L of water. Higher rate end for the first two applications when you're building the initial microbial population, lower rate end for ongoing programme maintenance.

Practical points:
- Lower carrier water volume than typical foliar feeds. The 2 to 5 L per 100 m² range gets the product onto the leaf and surface soil with minimal dilution of the biological component.
- Apply early morning or late afternoon (avoid hot midday).
- Light irrigation 2 to 4 hours after application moves the biology into the soil.
- Tank-mix friendly with Humic+, Amino+, Iron+ at routine rates.
- Don't tank-mix with fungicides, herbicides, or anything that will kill biology.

For a 250 m² home lawn at the higher rate for establishment (600 mL per 100 m²), you're using 1.5 L per application. At the lower programme rate (400 mL per 100 m²), 1 L per application.

The rate is higher than most NZLA liquid products. That's because the biological inoculant component requires meaningful population density to establish effectively. Half-rate applications won't get the biology to function the way the full-rate programme does.

Don't double the rate hoping for faster results. Microbial establishment is rate-limited at the soil-leaf interface. Excess product runs off without adding to the population.
