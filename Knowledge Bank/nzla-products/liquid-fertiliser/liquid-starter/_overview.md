# NZLA Liquid Starter

The foliar partner to NZLA Starter granular. 8-10-5 plus kelp, but the value sits less in those headline numbers and more in the specific chemistry — phosphite-based phosphorus, kelp biostimulant, and slow-release nitrogen. The same combination that goes into professional turf establishment programmes globally.

## What it does

A new lawn has different nutritional needs to an established lawn. The seedling is putting down its first root system, building leaf area, and surviving in soil it hasn't yet developed relationships with biologically. The fertilisation needs to support all three of those simultaneously without burning fragile new growth.

Liquid Starter is built specifically for that window. Three deliberately chosen mechanisms work together.

## The phosphite phosphorus story

The "10" in the 8-10-5 is the most important number on the bag. Establishment needs phosphorus for root development — that's standard knowledge. What's less standard is how that phosphorus is delivered.

Most starter fertilisers use phosphate (PO4), which is the form plants actually use. The problem is phosphate is very reactive in the soil, binds quickly to calcium, iron and aluminium, and becomes unavailable to the plant within days of application. Most of the phosphorus in a standard starter granular is locked up before the seedlings can use it.

Liquid Starter uses phosphite (PO3) instead. Phosphite is gradually converted to plant-usable phosphate in the root zone, by microbial and chemical pathways. That conversion takes weeks rather than days. The phosphorus stays in plant-available form for far longer, which means the seedling gets the P it needs to drive root development through the whole establishment window, not just the first few days.

## The kelp component

Kelp extract is the other key inclusion. Kelp provides natural plant growth regulators (cytokinins and auxins primarily), amino acids, a wide range of micronutrients, and compounds that improve stress tolerance.

The growth regulators specifically support early seedling vigour. Cytokinins drive shoot growth and chlorophyll production, auxins drive root growth. Both are exactly what you want during establishment. The amino acids supply ready-made building blocks the seedling can use without having to synthesise them from nitrogen, which saves the plant energy at a time when it's short of resources.

## The slow-release nitrogen fraction

The 8% nitrogen contains a methylene urea slow-release fraction, so the seedling gets sustained feeding rather than a hit of soluble urea that could burn it. Slow-release nitrogen is gentler on fragile new growth and matches the plant's slow early uptake rate.

## Rate and application

Establishment foliar (new lawns): 100 to 200 mL per 100m². Apply 4 to 5 weeks after sowing once seedlings are robust enough to handle foliar spray.

Foliar maintenance (established post-stress recovery): 200 to 300 mL per 100m².

Minimum 5 L of water per 100m² for even coverage.

## When to use

New lawn establishment, 4 to 5 weeks after sowing once seedlings have enough leaf area to take up foliar spray.

Post-renovation recovery, especially after scarification or core aeration where the lawn needs support to rebuild.

Post-drought or post-wear recovery where the lawn needs gentle balanced feeding rather than a nitrogen push.

Patch repair where seedlings are coming up but need a foliar boost.

## When not to use

On seedlings under 4 weeks old. The foliar is too aggressive for fragile early seedlings and they don't have enough leaf area to use it.

As a substitute for granular Starter at soil prep. Different role — the granular goes in before sowing, the liquid supports the seedlings once they're up.

As a maintenance feed on established lawns that aren't in recovery. Liquid Boost or Charger is better suited.

In hot conditions on stressed seedlings.

## Common mistakes

Applying too early on new seedlings. The 4 to 5 week wait is genuine, earlier spraying can damage fragile leaves and the seedlings can't use the feed anyway.

Substituting it for granular Starter at soil prep. Liquid Starter supports establishment, doesn't replace the soil-level P that goes in before sowing.

Mowing too soon after application.

Applying on hot windy days when the foliar dries before absorbing.

Treating the kelp like just another marketing inclusion. The growth regulators in kelp are doing real work on early seedling vigour — it's not filler.
