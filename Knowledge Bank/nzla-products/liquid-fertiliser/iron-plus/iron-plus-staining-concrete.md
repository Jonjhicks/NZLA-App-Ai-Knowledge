# Will Iron+ stain my paths and concrete?

Yes, if it lands on hard surfaces. Iron in any soluble form stains concrete, paving, paint, vinyl, light-coloured rocks, anything porous. The stain is reddish-brown, sets over a few hours, and is difficult to remove once it's set.

To avoid the staining:
- Spray on a calm day with no wind drift toward paths or buildings.
- Use a sprayer with a controlled spray pattern, not a wide diffuse mist.
- Keep the spray nozzle low to the lawn surface.
- Edge along paths carefully, or use a different product for edge work.
- Don't fill the sprayer over paving (spills stain on contact).

If you do get splash:
- Rinse with water immediately. Don't let it dry.
- A garden hose at high pressure on fresh splash usually clears it.
- If it's dried in, try citric acid (lemon juice) or a proprietary concrete rust remover.
- Set-in stains may not come out fully without acid treatments or grinding.

The risk is highest on:
- Light-coloured concrete (driveways, paths).
- New paving where the surface is still porous.
- Painted timber or fibre cement.
- Brick paths (stains the mortar).

Wear old clothes when spraying, the iron will mark fabric too.

For lawns surrounded by paths or paving on all sides, consider using a controlled-droplet applicator or a backpack sprayer with a tight spray pattern, rather than a hose-end sprayer that throws a wider mist.
