# NZLA Iron+

A straight liquid iron supplement at 6% Fe, with the surfactant and humectant chemistry that actually gets the iron into the plant rather than letting it dry on the leaf surface. Used foliar for fast colour and as a hardening agent against cold and disease pressure, without pushing growth.

## What it does

Three jobs at once, all from one nutrient.

The visible job is colour. Iron drives chlorophyll synthesis, so the lawn goes a deeper green within hours of application. That's the most obvious effect and the one most people are buying iron for.

The less obvious job is plant strength. Iron is part of multiple enzyme systems the plant uses to build cell wall components. Stronger cell walls mean better tolerance to cold, drought, mowing wounds, and lower susceptibility to some foliar pathogens. Iron applications heading into a disease window are part of a sound cultural prevention programme.

The third job is growth without growth. Because Iron+ supplies no nitrogen, you get the deeper green appearance without forcing soft top growth into conditions that wouldn't suit it. In cool weather, going into winter, on warm-season turf that's slowing down — situations where you don't want growth but do want colour — iron is the answer.

## Why the delivery matters

Foliar iron is fussy. Iron oxidises easily, doesn't move through the leaf cuticle well without help, and dries off the leaf surface before the plant can absorb much of it. A cheap iron solution applied without the right adjuvants gives you ten percent of the iron's potential effect.

NZLA Iron+ uses a delivery package built around three things: a surfactant that breaks droplet surface tension so the spray spreads flat across the leaf rather than beading off, a humectant that prolongs drying time so the leaf has longer to absorb, and a carbohydrate base that supplies the plant with energy to actively transport the iron through its tissues. That combination is the difference between iron that goes on and iron that gets into the plant and stays in plant-available form.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m², dissolved in 5 to 15 L of water. Heavier rate for deeper green-up, lighter rate for maintenance colour.

Foliar application works fastest because the leaf takes the iron up directly. Soil drench also works but takes longer and is less efficient.

Avoid mowing for 24 hours after spraying. The iron's still on the leaf surface working its way in.

Repeat every 14 to 28 days depending on how often you want the colour pickup.

Don't apply over 25°C. Heat plus surfactant plus iron on the leaf can cause minor burn.

## When to use

Cool conditions where granular nitrogen won't fire because the soil's too cold to drive uptake.

Pre-event or pre-photo colour boost.

Hardening the plant against fungal pressure heading into a disease window.

Any time you want green without growth — late autumn and winter being the obvious case.

Warm-season turf (kikuyu, couch) in cool months to hold colour as growth slows.

Lawns showing iron deficiency (yellowing on alkaline soils, or on sandy soils where iron leaches out).

## When not to use

If the lawn genuinely needs nitrogen for growth. Iron makes a hungry lawn look slightly less hungry, doesn't actually feed it.

In hot conditions over 25°C. Burn risk goes up.

In a sprayer that's been used for herbicide. Iron is sensitive to herbicide residues in the tank.

Don't store below 5°C. The iron chelate crystallises and the product is effectively ruined.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon after application. The iron's still being absorbed and you've just bagged it.

Overdosing expecting more colour. Past about 400 mL/100m² you don't get more green, you just get more product on the lawn and more burn risk.

Spilling on concrete, paving or stone. Iron stains and it doesn't come out easily.

Storing in an unheated shed in winter. Below 5°C the chelate crystallises.

Comparing the effect against a cheap garden-centre iron solution. Cheap iron without the delivery chemistry will look like it doesn't work as well as Iron+ even if the iron percentage is the same on paper. The delivery is doing real work.
