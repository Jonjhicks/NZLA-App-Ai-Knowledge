# NZLA Restore

A soil biology stimulant with beneficial microbes, plant stimulants, and organic acids. The NPK analysis (1.3-1.5-1) is deliberately low. Restore isn't a fertiliser. It's the infrastructure that makes fertiliser work better.

## What it does

Healthy soil has a thriving microbial community that breaks down organic matter, cycles nutrients, supports root development, and contributes to  resistance. Programmes heavy in synthetic fertiliser, repeated fungicide use, soil core-aeration, and low-organic-matter conditions all impoverish that biology. When soil biology is depleted, fertiliser response drops even when applications are at standard rates.

Three components work together in Restore to rebuild that function.

**Beneficial microbes.** A mix of bacteria and fungi selected for nutrient cycling, root zone support, and stress resilience. Once watered into the soil, they establish in the root zone and start contributing to biological function.

**Organic acids.** These chelate iron and other minerals into plant-available forms, similar to how humic acids work. They prevent mineral lockup in soils where this is a chronic issue.

**Plant stimulants.** Compounds that signal the plant to develop deeper roots, improve stress tolerance, and build resilience. The plant responds physiologically, not just nutritionally.

The three reinforce each other. Biology cycles nutrients more efficiently. Organic acids keep those nutrients in plant-available forms. Plant stimulants drive the plant to make better use of what's available.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 2 to 5 L of water. Check the current label.

Apply as a soil drench. Water in after application to move the biology into the soil where it can establish.

Every 4 to 6 weeks during active growing season for programme use.

Apply in cool conditions or evening, not hot direct sun on dry foliage. The biology dries out.

Wait 2 to 4 weeks after broad-spectrum fungicide before applying. The fungicide has to do its work first, then the biology can rebuild.

## When to use

After heavy fungicide use, to begin rebuilding biological function.

New lawns and sand-based root zones, where soil biology is starting from near zero.

Lawns showing "tired" performance — where standard feeding programmes aren't producing the expected response.

Post-renovation (scarifiers, core aeration) as part of the recovery programme.

Routine soil health maintenance through the growing season.

## What to expect (and what not to expect)

Restore doesn't produce a visible green-up. The effect is on soil biology and mineral availability, not on direct plant feeding. Visible benefit shows as better fertiliser response, better drought tolerance, and faster recovery from stress — over months of regular application, not within days of a single dose.

The biological build compounds. Three to six months of regular application is when the cumulative benefit becomes clearly observable.

## Pairs well with

Granular base feed for primary nutrition. Restore supports the soil, the granular feeds the plant.

Humic+ for additional chelation support alongside the biology.

Nurture or Amino+ for further biological layers.

NZLA Aerate on heavy soils — mechanically opens the soil while Restore works on the biology.
