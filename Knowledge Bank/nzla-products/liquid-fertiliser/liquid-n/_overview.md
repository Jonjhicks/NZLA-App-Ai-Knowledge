# NZLA Liquid N

A premium slow-release liquid nitrogen built around two complementary technologies that work together to deliver steady nitrogen without the spike that standard liquid urea produces. 85% of the nitrogen is in slowly available forms, which is unusual for a liquid product.

## What it does

Standard liquid nitrogen products use urea or other fast-release nitrogen forms. They give a quick green-up and growth flush, then taper off within a couple of weeks. The lawn drops back into a hungry state until the next application, and you're back into the spike-and-trough cycle that drives soft growth and disease pressure.

Liquid N is built specifically to avoid that. Dual slow-release technology delivers nitrogen steadily over 4 to 10 weeks at sustained low rates of release, so the lawn gets continual feeding rather than a flush.

## The dual technology

The first mechanism is ammonium stabilisation. A nitrification inhibitor (DMPP) slows the natural conversion of ammonium to nitrate in the soil. Ammonium is more stable than nitrate, doesn't leach as readily, and stays in plant-available form longer. By slowing the conversion, the inhibitor extends the window over which the applied nitrogen is available, and reduces losses to leaching and volatilisation. That mechanism delivers steady nitrogen over 4 to 10 weeks.

The second mechanism is methylene urea. This is a polymer-based slow-release nitrogen that arrives in the soil as a soluble fraction (immediate growth response) and a polymer fraction that's gradually broken down by soil microbes over weeks. The soluble fraction starts working straight away. The polymer fraction is microbially metered out, so the rate of release matches the soil's biological activity — warmer and more active in good growing conditions, slower in cool.

The two technologies stack. Ammonium stabilisation handles the first 4 to 10 weeks at the chemistry level. Methylene urea handles the longer tail through biological breakdown.

## Why this matters in practice

Three real benefits.

Steady growth. The lawn ticks over rather than flushing and fading.

Lower environmental losses. Nitrate leaches readily, ammonium less so. Slowing the conversion reduces nitrogen lost to the soil profile and to the atmosphere as ammonia.

Particularly strong on sandy soils. Standard urea-based liquids leach quickly through sand, so much of the applied nitrogen is gone before the plant uses it. Liquid N's slow-release stays in the root zone long enough to actually feed the plant.

## Rate and application

Rate varies by target nitrogen output:

Low rate: 500 mL per 100m² (about 0.15 kg N/ha)

High rate: up to 1400 mL per 100m² (about 0.42 kg N/ha)

Carrier water 8 to 20 L per 100m².

Irrigate 2 to 5 mm within 24 hours of application to move the active into the root zone and start the slow-release process.

Avoid mowing for 24 hours.

**Temperature restriction: do not apply when temperature exceeds 26°C.** Stabilised nitrogen products can lose stability at high temperatures and produce ammonia damage.

## When to use

Sustained nitrogen feeding without spike. Particularly through active growing season where you want consistent growth rather than flush-and-fade.

Sand-based root zones (golf greens, sports turf, premium home lawns built on sand) where standard urea leaches.

Lawns on a foliar-only programme where you want the granular role replaced with a steady liquid.

Through cool conditions where soil microbial activity is slower but you still want consistent N delivery.

Sports turf and high-performance situations where consistency matters more than a green-up flush.

## When not to use

In temperatures over 26°C.

When you want a fast push and green-up — use Liquid Boost for that.

Through deep winter when even foliar uptake is slow.

In equipment with herbicide residue.

## How it sits next to the others

Liquid Boost: 70% fast-release N, high-N spike, quick green-up, short duration.

Liquid N: 85% slow-release N, sustained delivery over 4 to 10 weeks.

Same nitrogen output potential delivered over a completely different time profile. Liquid Boost is the push tool. Liquid N is the steady tool.

Use Liquid N when consistency matters more than speed, sandy soil holds nutrients poorly, or when you want to avoid the disease pressure that a nitrogen flush can drive.

## Common mistakes

Applying in hot conditions. The 26°C cap matters — go over it and the ammonium stabilisation chemistry can release ammonia and cause damage.

Treating it like Liquid Boost. Liquid N is the steady tool, not the push tool.

Skipping the water-in. The 2 to 5 mm helps establish the slow-release mechanism in the soil.

Expecting instant green-up. The point of the chemistry is consistent feeding, not a flush.
