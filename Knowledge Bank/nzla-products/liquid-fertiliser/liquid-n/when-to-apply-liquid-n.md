# When should I apply Liquid N?

When the lawn needs a quick nitrogen response. Spring through autumn (October to April for cool-season turf).

Specific use scenarios:
- Pale lawn that needs a colour push within a week
- Rust treatment (tank-mixed with Fungus Pro)
- Between granular feeds, foliar top-up
- Recovery push after wear or stress
- Bowling green or fine turf where granular feeds aren't suitable

Soil and air temps 14 °C or warmer for active uptake. The chemistry works in cooler conditions too but the response is slower.

Avoid:
- Drought-stressed lawn (water first)
- Heat-stressed lawn over 28 °C with no irrigation
- Disease pressure still active and uncontrolled (push the recovery later)
- Going into winter (don't push soft growth into cold conditions)

Apply to a dry lawn with a calibrated sprayer. No watering in for 24 hours. No mowing for 24 hours either side.

Programme use: every 2 to 3 weeks if you're running Liquid N as the main foliar nitrogen. For most home lawns it's used episodically rather than programmed, alongside an All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular base.
