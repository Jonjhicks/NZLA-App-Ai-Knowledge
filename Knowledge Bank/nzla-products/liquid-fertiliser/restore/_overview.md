# NZLA Restore

A soil biology stimulant with beneficial microbes, plant stimulants and organic acids. The NPK analysis (1.3-1.5-1) is deliberately low because this isn't a fertiliser as such — it's a soil builder. The job is to rebuild the soil's biological function so everything else in the programme works better.

## What it does

Healthy soil has a thriving microbial community that breaks down organic matter, cycles nutrients, supports root development, and contributes to disease resistance. Over time, programmes heavy in synthetic fertiliser, repeated fungicide use, soil compaction, or simple low-organic-matter conditions impoverish that biology. The soil's capacity to deliver nutrition to the lawn drops, even when fertiliser is being applied at standard rates.

Restore is built specifically to rebuild that biology and the chemistry that goes with it.

**Beneficial microbes.** A mix of bacteria and fungi selected for nutrient cycling, root zone support, and stress resilience. Once introduced and watered into the soil, they establish in the root zone and start contributing to the soil's biological function.

**Organic acids.** These chelate iron and other mineral nutrients into plant-available forms, very similar to the mechanism humic acids use. They keep minerals from locking up as insoluble compounds, particularly in soils where lockup is a chronic problem.

**Plant stimulants.** Compounds that signal the plant to develop deeper roots, improve stress tolerance, and build resilience. The plant responds physiologically to the stimulants in addition to whatever feeding it's getting.

The three components reinforce each other. The biology cycles nutrients more efficiently. The organic acids keep those nutrients in plant-available forms. The plant stimulants drive the lawn to make better use of what's available.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 2 to 5 L of water.

Apply as a soil drench. Water in after application to move the biology into the soil where it can establish.

Every 4 to 6 weeks during active growing season is the standard programme.

## When to use

Lawns where the soil biology has been knocked back — after heavy fungicide use, over-fertilisation, compaction issues, or just routine programmes on long-established turf.

New lawns to establish a strong biological community from the start.

Sand-based root zones where natural biology is sparse.

Routine soil-health maintenance every 4 to 6 weeks during active growing season.

After fungicide programmes to begin rebuilding the biological community fungicide knocks back.

Lawns showing "tired" performance — where standard feeding programmes aren't producing the response they used to.

## When not to use

Mixed with broad-spectrum fungicides. Wait 2 to 4 weeks after fungicide application before applying Restore.

In hot direct sun on dry foliage. Apply in cool conditions for biology survival.

As a replacement for nutrition. The low NPK isn't enough to feed the lawn — Restore supports the soil, doesn't feed the plant directly.

## Common mistakes

Treating it as a fertiliser. Different role.

Skipping the water-in. The biology dries out and dies on the surface.

Alternating with fungicide without timing buffer. Defeats the purpose.

Expecting visible response from Restore alone. The effect is on soil biology and uptake, not on direct plant feeding.

Stopping the programme too early. The biological build compounds over months — three to six months of regular application is when the cumulative benefit really shows.

## Best paired with

Granular base feed for foundation nutrition.

Humic+ for additional nutrient uptake support via humic acid chelation.

Nurture or Amino+ for further biological support.

NZLA Aerator and mechanical aeration to physically open the soil while Restore works on the biology.
