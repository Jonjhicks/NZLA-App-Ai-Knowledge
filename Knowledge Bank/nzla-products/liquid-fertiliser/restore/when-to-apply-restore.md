# When should I apply Restore?

Within a few days of any stress event, then at 7 to 10 day intervals through the recovery phase.

Common scenarios:
- Day 1 to 7 after fungicide application that stopped a disease outbreak.
- Day 1 to 7 after a heatwave once watering is restored.
- 1 to 4 weeks post-renovation, once seedlings are showing.
- 3 to 7 days after a herbicide application that left the lawn stressed.
- Pre-overseed and post-overseed for thin and damaged areas being repaired.

Soil and air temps 14 °C or warmer for active uptake.

Don't apply:
- During the stress event itself. Water first, restore hydration first, then push recovery.
- On a lawn with active uncontrolled disease (the fungicide has to do its work first).
- On dormant or going-dormant turf in late autumn (the lawn isn't growing, won't respond).

Apply with a calibrated sprayer to dry leaves. No watering in for 24 hours. No mowing for 24 hours either side.

Tank-mix friendly with most NZLA liquid products, particularly Liquid Boost or Liquid N for combined nutrition and recovery support. Don't tank-mix with PGR (you want growth response, PGR works against it).

For a typical recovery situation, 2 to 4 Restore applications over 3 to 4 weeks gets the lawn back to baseline. More applications beyond that aren't adding value, the lawn has either recovered or there's something else going on that's preventing recovery.
