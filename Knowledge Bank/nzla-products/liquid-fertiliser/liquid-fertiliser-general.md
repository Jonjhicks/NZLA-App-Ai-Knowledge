# Liquid Fertilisers — Overview

Granular fertilisers are the base of the programme. They supply steady bulk nutrition over 6 to 10 weeks per application. Liquid fertilisers are the targeted layer on top: faster response, more precise application, and useful in windows where granular won't fire (cold soil, dry surface, specific deficiencies). They also carry the programme through winter when soil temperatures drop and granular nitrogen stops releasing.

## The NZLA liquid range at a glance

**Push feeds and green-up:**
Liquid Boost (15-0-0 + 6% Fe + 2% Mn) — high-N push and quick colour, with EDTA iron and stabilised N for duration.
Liquid N — dual slow-release liquid nitrogen (85% slow-release), steady feeding without spike, suits sand-based profiles.

**Balanced maintenance:**
Charger (7-0-7 + 4% Fe + 2% Mn) — balanced N and K, density and colour without forcing vertical growth.
Liquid Starter (8-10-5 + kelp) — establishment foliar for new lawns, also good post-stress.

**Colour and trace elements:**
Iron+ (6% Fe) — pure chelated iron, deep green colour without nitrogen growth push.

**Biological and soil amendment:**
Amino+ (9-1-1 plus biology) — plant-available amino acids and beneficial biology, biostimulation and stress recovery.
Humic+ (12% humic, 2% fulvic) — soil chelation chemistry and organic catalysts, makes other fertilisers work better.
Nurture (10-1-11 + biology) — organic NPK with beneficial bacteria and fungi, builds soil biology alongside feeding.
Restore — soil biology stimulant, microbes and organic acids, post-stress and post-chemical recovery.
Root Health — Ecklonia maxima kelp biostimulant, drives root development from the hormone level.

## How they fit into a programme

For most home lawns on a granular base (All Seasons or All Seasons N+), three to four granular applications a year plus liquid feeds between handles most situations. The typical liquid layer is Iron+ for colour, Liquid Boost for recovery and push events, and one of the biology products if soil health is a focus.

For higher-input lawns, the foliar programme runs monthly between granular applications. Charger or Liquid N as the nitrogen base, Iron+ for colour, Amino+ and Humic+ for soil biology. One spray application can combine all four.

For fine turf (greens, bowling surfaces) where granulars disturb the playing surface, a foliar-led programme with minimal granular use is often standard.

## Foliar application rules

Apply to dry leaves. Don't mow for 24 hours after application.

Apply in cool conditions, not hot midday.

Don't water in immediately — foliar products need time on the leaf.

Use sufficient carrier water (typically 5 to 15 L per 100m²) for even coverage.

Don't use equipment previously used for herbicide.

## Tank-mix combinations

Standard monthly foliar: Charger + Iron+ + Amino+ + Humic+. One spray covers nitrogen, colour, biology, soil chemistry.

Pre-event push (1 week before): Liquid Boost + Iron+. Fast nitrogen plus strong colour.

Recovery push: Liquid Boost or Liquid N + Restore + Amino+ + Iron+.

Rust treatment: Fungus Pro + Liquid N or Liquid Boost + Iron+. Compatible combination.

Root and soil programme (pre-summer): Root Health + Humic+ + Amino+. Monthly through spring.

## Storage

Cool, dry, out of direct sun, lid sealed. Synthetic foliar feeds (Charger, Liquid N, Liquid Boost, Iron+) store for 3 to 5 years. Biological products (Nurture, Amino+, Restore, Root Health) are more temperature-sensitive — 12 to 24 months once opened, store cool. Don't store any liquid fertiliser below 5°C if it contains chelated iron (chelate crystallises). Don't store mixed tank loads overnight.

## What not to tank-mix

Don't mix two products in the same FRAC fungicide group.
Don't mix PGR with growth-pushing products.
Don't mix biological products (Nurture, Restore, Amino+) with broad-spectrum fungicide.
Anything unfamiliar: jar test first.
