# NZLA Liquid Boost

High-nitrogen foliar push feed at 15-0-0 with 6% iron and 2% manganese. The headline numbers are backed by EDTA chelate iron, ammonium stabilised nitrogen, and a slow-release fraction. The green-up holds longer than a basic fast-release liquid because the chemistry is actually doing something.

## What it does

Fast response is the primary job. Foliar nitrogen uptake is quick, and the lawn shows colour and growth within 3 to 5 days.

The EDTA chelate iron (33% of the iron fraction) drives chlorophyll directly while preventing the iron from oxidising in the tank and on the leaf. Without chelation, foliar iron precipitates out, blocks nozzles, and under-delivers. With it, the colour performance is real.

The sustained feeding comes from the ammonium stabilised nitrogen and the SRN-IQ slow-release fraction. The ammonium stabilisation slows conversion to nitrate, extending the feeding window and reducing leaching. The methylene urea adds a biological release tail via soil microbial breakdown. The combined effect is that Liquid Boost doesn't flash and disappear the way straight urea would — the lawn keeps feeding for weeks after the initial flush.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 5 to 15 L of water. Check the current label for the exact rate.

Apply every 14 to 28 days. The slow-release fraction makes the longer interval workable.

No mowing for 24 hours. Apply to dry leaves.

Will stain concrete, paving, and stone. Mask or rinse immediately.

Don't use in equipment that's been used for herbicide.

## Rust treatment

Rust shows up on nitrogen-starved turf. The standard response: Fungus Pro to knock the active infection, Liquid Boost at the same time (they're compatible in a tank mix) for the nitrogen push. The lawn needs to grow past the damaged tissue. Fungicide alone removes the rust but leaves the lawn in the weak underfed state that brought the rust on. The combination breaks the cycle.

Two applications 7 to 10 days apart can be used for stronger recovery. Beyond that you're double-feeding without benefit.

## How it compares

Liquid Boost: 15% N, EDTA iron, stabilised plus slow-release N. The push tool, 3 to 4 week duration.

Charger: 7% N, matched K, dual-form N. The maintenance and density tool.

Liquid N: 85% slow-release N, no iron. The steady feed tool for consistent nitrogen without spike.

Iron+: 6% Fe only. Colour without growth.

## When not to use

Heading into winter. High N drives soft growth into cold conditions and  pressure.

On heat-stressed turf. Adding push to a stressed plant makes it worse.

Tank-mixed with PGR. The growth response you're buying defeats the PGR.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon. The feed goes in the catcher.

Overdosing past 400 mL/100m². At that point you're getting leaf burn, not more colour.

Using through winter on cool-season turf.

Expecting the same result from cheap high-N foliars. The EDTA chelate and stabilised N are not cosmetic additions — they're the reason the duration and colour performance are what they are.
