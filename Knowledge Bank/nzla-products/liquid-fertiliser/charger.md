# NZLA Charger

Balanced foliar fertiliser at 7-0-7 plus 4% iron and 2% manganese. The technology underneath those numbers is what sets it apart from a basic balanced liquid: dual-form nitrogen, premium citrate/carbonate potassium, very low salt index, and a specific bias toward lateral growth and density rather than vertical flush.

## Why lateral growth matters

A push feed like Liquid Boost drives a vertical growth flush. The lawn grows up fast, mowing frequency goes up, and canopy can thin out as energy goes into top growth at the expense of tillering.

Charger's balanced N+K chemistry pushes lateral growth instead. The plant tillers, spreads sideways, fills in bare spots, and builds canopy density. Same growth biomass, redirected to a more useful outcome. The lawn ends up denser and better able to handle wear.

## The nitrogen side

Dual-form nitrogen: a urea fraction converted to ammonium for sustained uptake on cation exchange sites, plus an immediate nitrate fraction for fast response. An SRN-IQ slow-release component extends the feeding window further. The result is both speed and duration without relying on either pathway alone.

## The potassium side

Most liquid potassium products use chloride or sulphate forms. Both carry a higher salt load and risk foliar burn at high rates or in heat. Chloride-based products also corrode spray equipment.

Charger uses potassium in citrate and carbonate complexes. Citrate is present in every plant cell as part of normal metabolism, so the plant assimilates it immediately. The carbonate form provides additional plant-available K via both leaf and soil. Practical result: the foliar burn risk is very low and the spray equipment doesn't corrode.

## Rate and application

200 to 500 mL per 100m² in a minimum of 4 L of water. Check the current label for the exact rate.

Apply every 14 days when you want sustained growth and colour, less often for maintenance.

Hold off mowing for 24 hours. Apply to a dry leaf surface in cool conditions.

Don't apply to drought-stressed or heat-stressed turf above 28°C with no irrigation-general.

## Where it fits in the range

Liquid Boost (15-0-0, high N) is the vertical push tool. Charger (7-0-7, matched N and K) is the lateral and maintenance tool.

Iron+ (6% Fe only) is pure colour without growth. Charger delivers controlled growth with colour, biased toward density.

Charger as a monthly programme base, Liquid Boost for episodic recovery and push, Iron+ when you want additional colour on top.

For most home lawns on a granular base programme, Liquid Boost will see more use because it's episodic. Charger comes into its own on high-input lawns running foliar-led programmes or on warm-season turf through cooler months where you want maintained density without forcing flush.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon after application. The feed is still on the leaf.

Spilling on concrete. Iron and manganese stain.

Using for active recovery from significant damage. At 7% N, Charger isn't the recovery push tool. That's what Liquid Boost or All Seasons N+ is for.

Running it with PGR at the same time you want density gain. PGR and Charger can be managed together, but the timing and rates need thought.
