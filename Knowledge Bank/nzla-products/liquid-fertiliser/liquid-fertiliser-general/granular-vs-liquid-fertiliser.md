# Should I use granular or liquid fertiliser?

Both, in most cases. They do different jobs.

Granular feeds (All Seasons, All Seasons, Mini Prill, N+, Starter) are the base programme. Slow-release nutrients delivered into the soil over 6 to 10 weeks per application. Three to four applications a year covers the lawn's bulk nutrient requirements with minimal effort.

Liquid feeds (Charger, Liquid Boost, Liquid N, Iron+, others) are the response and refinement layer. Faster uptake through the leaf, smaller doses per application, used episodically or as a monthly tune-up between granular feeds.

Decision framework:
- Standard home lawn: granular base programme (3 to 4 times a year) plus occasional liquid feeds for colour or recovery.
- Higher-input lawn: granular base plus monthly liquid programme tank-mixing Charger or similar with Iron+, Amino+, Humic+.
- Fine turf or bowling green: foliar-led programme with minimal granular use (the granules disturb the playing surface and don't suit low cutting heights).

What each does best:
- Granular: bulk nutrition, slow release, low effort, lower cost per unit nutrient.
- Liquid foliar: fast response, fine control, tank-mix flexibility, suits frequent application.

For most NZ home lawns, three All Seasons or All Seasons N+ applications a year plus a few Liquid Boost or Iron+ applications for events and recovery is the right balance. Daily liquid programmes are overkill for home use.
