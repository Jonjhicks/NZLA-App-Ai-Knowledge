# Liquid Fertiliser General

The liquid fertiliser range covers foliar feeds, soil drenches, and biological soil supports. Each product has a specific role, this is the category-level overview to help work out which is right for which job. For specific product detail, see each individual product page.

## When to use liquids vs granulars

Granular fertilisers are the base of the programme. Steady release over weeks, broad coverage, foundation fertility. Apply every 6 to 8 weeks.

Liquid fertilisers are the targeted top-up between granulars. Faster response, more precise application, useful for situations where granular wouldn't fire (cold soil, dry surface, specific deficiencies). Apply every 14 to 28 days.

Liquids also play through winter when soil temperatures drop and granulars stop responding, because foliar uptake doesn't depend on soil microbial activity.

## NZLA liquid range at a glance

**Push feeds and green-up:**
- Liquid Boost (15-0-0 + 6%Fe + 2%Mn) — high-N push and quick colour
- Liquid N — slow-release liquid nitrogen for steady feeding without spike

**Balanced maintenance:**
- Charger (7-0-7 + 4%Fe + 2%Mn) — low-N, matched K, colour without forcing growth
- Liquid Starter (8-10-5 + kelp) — establishment foliar, also good post-stress

**Colour and trace elements:**
- Iron+ (6% Fe) — pure iron, colour without nitrogen

**Biological and soil amendment:**
- Amino+ (9-1-1 plus biology) — plant-available amino acids and beneficial microbes
- Humic+ (12% humic, 2% fulvic) — humic acid soil amendment
- Restore — soil biology stimulant
- Nurture (10-1-11 plus biology) — organic liquid with beneficial bacteria and fungi
- Root Health — wetting agent and soil ameliorant (PDF), see product page for full detail

## How they sit in a programme

A typical programme runs:
- Granular base feed every 6 to 8 weeks (All Seasons or All Seasons N+)
- Liquid between granular applications every 2 to 4 weeks
- Choice of liquid depends on what the lawn needs that week (push, colour, maintenance, biology, recovery)

Through winter when granular stops responding, the programme shifts to liquids only.

## Foliar uptake basics

Foliar fertilisers are absorbed through the leaf. Application rules:

- Don't mow for 24 hours after application
- Apply in cool conditions, not hot midday
- Don't water in immediately (lets the foliar absorb first)
- Use sufficient water for even coverage (typically 5 to 15 L per 100m²)

Don't apply foliar feeds in equipment that's been used for herbicide.
