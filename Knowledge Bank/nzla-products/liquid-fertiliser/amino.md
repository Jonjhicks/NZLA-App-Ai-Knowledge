# NZLA Amino+

A liquid biostimulant built around plant-available amino acids, Vitamin B1, and beneficial biology. It works by giving the plant the building blocks of growth ready-made, cutting out several energy-expensive metabolic steps the plant would normally do itself.

## What it does

Plants normally take up nitrogen and use it to build amino acids from scratch. That process requires energy and active metabolism. Under stress (cold, heat, drought, wear), the plant's ability to synthesise amino acids slows right down.

Amino+ supplies amino acids in plant-ready form. The plant skips the biosynthesis step, which frees up energy for root growth, photosynthesis, and recovery. The Vitamin B1 (thiamine) component specifically supports root development and helps with cellular repair after stress. The beneficial biology rounds out the programme with microbial inoculant support.

## Why the production method matters

Amino acids are extracted from plant protein by one of two methods. Acid hydrolysis is cheap, uses strong chemicals, and destroys a significant portion of the amino acids in the process. Enzymatic digestion uses biological enzymes that break the protein apart gently, leaving the amino acids intact and functional.

Amino+ uses enzymatic digestion. The amino acids are still in the same form the plant would build them in. Buying on amino acid percentage alone misses this distinction, because the percentage doesn't tell you how many of those amino acids are still biologically active.

## Rate and application

Foliar: 200 mL per 100m² in 5 L of water.

Soil drench: 400 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L of water, watered in immediately so the biology reaches the root zone before drying out.

Check the current label for the exact rate.

Apply every 4 to 6 weeks through the growing season. Don't mow for 24 hours after application.

## When to use

Post-stress recovery, after heat, drought, or wear damage when the plant needs help rebuilding.

Cool conditions where the plant's nitrogen metabolism is running slow but you don't want to force soft growth.

Pre-stress preparation before a known difficult period (heatwave, tournament, wear event).

Establishment phase as part of seedling vigour support.

Monthly programme use through the growing season.

## What to avoid

Don't treat it as a primary nitrogen source. The 9% N is part of the delivery, not the point of the product.

Don't apply in hot conditions above 28°C with no irrigation-general. The biological component is vulnerable to heat.

Don't tank-mix with broad-spectrum fungicides without a timing buffer. Let fungicide do its work, then apply Amino+ 2 to 4 weeks later so the biology can establish.

## Pairs well with

Liquid Boost or Charger for the nutrition side while Amino+ handles the biostimulation.
