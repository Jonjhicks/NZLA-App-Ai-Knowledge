# NZLA Iron+

Liquid chelated iron at 6% Fe, with the surfactant and humectant chemistry that gets the iron into the plant rather than letting it dry on the leaf. Three jobs from one nutrient: deep green colour, plant hardening against cold and and green without pushing growth.

## What it does

The colour job is obvious. Iron drives chlorophyll synthesis, so the lawn goes darker green within hours of application.

The hardening job is less discussed but equally real. Iron is part of multiple enzyme systems used to build cell wall components. Stronger cell walls mean better tolerance to cold, mowing wounds, and lower susceptibility to some foliar pathogens. Applying iron ahead of a  window is sound cultural practice, not just aesthetics.

The growth-neutral angle is what makes iron specifically useful in situations where nitrogen would cause problems. Going into winter, on warm-season turf that's slowing down, in cool transitions where you don't want to force soft growth into  pressure — iron gives you the dark green without the growth spike.

## Why the delivery matters

Foliar iron is awkward chemistry. Iron oxidises easily, doesn't move through the leaf cuticle without help, and dries off the surface before the plant can absorb much of it. Iron applied without proper adjuvants gives a fraction of its potential effect.

Iron+ uses a three-part delivery package: a surfactant to break surface tension so the spray spreads flat rather than beading off, a humectant that slows drying so the leaf has longer to absorb, and a carbohydrate base that supplies the plant with energy to actively transport the iron. This is the difference between iron that goes on and iron that gets in.

## Rate and application

200 to 400 mL per 100m² in 5 to 15 L of water. Check the current label for the exact rate.

Heavier rate for deep green-up, lighter rate for maintenance colour.

Apply every 14 to 28 days depending on how often you want the colour pickup.

Don't apply over 25°C. Heat plus surfactant plus iron on the leaf increases burn risk.

No mowing for 24 hours. Foliar is still being absorbed.

Don't store below 5°C. The iron chelate crystallises and the product is effectively ruined.

## Staining

Iron stains concrete, paving, light-coloured stone, painted surfaces, and fabric. It sets over a few hours and becomes difficult to remove.

Spray on a calm day. Keep the nozzle low. Rinse any splash onto hard surfaces immediately. If it's set in, citric acid or a proprietary rust remover can shift it, but prevention is easier.

## Where it fits

For pre-event sharpening (a week before the date), Iron+ is the single most useful product in the range. Peak colour on the day without the growth flush that would mean extra mowing beforehand.

In the broader liquid programme: Iron+ stacks on top of Charger or Liquid Boost as an additional colour boost. Used alone in cool conditions where granular isn't responding and you want green without growth.

Rust treatment programme: Iron+ tank-mixes with Fungus Pro and Liquid N as the standard rust response combination.

## Common mistakes

Mowing too soon. You've bagged the feed.

Overdosing past 400 mL/100m². No more colour, just more burn risk.

Storing in a cold shed in winter.

Comparing against cheap garden-centre iron solutions. The delivery chemistry — the surfactant, humectant, and carbohydrate — is doing the heavy lifting. Iron percentage alone is not the comparison point.
