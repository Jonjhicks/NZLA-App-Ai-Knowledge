# What is moss telling me about my lawn?

Moss is a symptom, not the problem itself. Wherever moss is establishing, the conditions favour it over grass.

That usually means one or more of: shade, damp, compaction, low fertility, or mowing too short. The moss isn't invading despite the lawn being healthy — it's filling a niche where the lawn is struggling.

So the fix is two-part. Kill the moss that's there, AND fix the underlying conditions. Skip the second half and the moss is back within months.

Killing the moss — NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride 500 g/L). Note the label restricts use to fine turf and cotula, not general lawns. DANGER classification — PPE non-negotiable. Standard rate 200 mL per 10 L water per 100m². Turns moss black within 5 to 15 minutes.

Fixing the conditions:

**Shade.** Cut back overhanging trees or branches where possible. If you can't remove the shade, accept that some shade-tolerant alternatives (fine fescue) suit the spot better than ryegrass.

**Compaction.** Pull cores through the affected area. Open the soil up so water and air can move through it. NZLA Aerator if you've got one, or hire a hollow tine aerator.

**Damp.** NZLA Penetrate moves surface water down through the profile, reducing surface moisture duration. Improve drainage where structural fixes are possible.

**Low fertility.** Get the lawn on a steady fertiliser programme. A well-fed dense lawn outcompetes moss for light and space.

**Mowing height.** Lift it. Short turf thins out and exposes soil — exactly what moss needs to establish.
