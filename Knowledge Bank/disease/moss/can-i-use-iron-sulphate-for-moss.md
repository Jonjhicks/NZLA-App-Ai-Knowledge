# Can I use iron sulphate for moss?

Iron sulphate works on moss but has trade-offs versus NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride) and isn't the NZLA-recommended approach for general lawn moss control.

How iron sulphate works on moss:

Ferrous sulphate (iron sulphate) is applied as a soluble crystal dissolved in water. The iron damages moss cell structure and the moss blackens and dies within hours of application. The dead moss is raked out, the lawn benefits from the iron (greener appearance, hardening effect), and the surrounding grass is largely unaffected at the right rate.

Why NZLA doesn't position iron sulphate as the moss product:

Iron sulphate works but has issues. It stains concrete, paving and clothing extremely aggressively — far worse than NZLA Iron+ (chelated iron). It can over-apply easily and burn grass. The rate window between effective on moss and damaging to grass is narrower than for the dedicated NZLA Moss product. And it's harder to source consistent quality than commercial moss-control products.

NZLA Moss (benzalkonium chloride 500 g/L) is the recommended moss product. Note the label restriction — registered for fine turf and cotula only, not general home lawns. DANGER classification — PPE non-negotiable.

For general home lawns where the label-restricted NZLA Moss isn't strictly applicable:

The cultural fix is the primary lever. Address shade, drainage, compaction, fertility and mowing height. The chemistry options for general home lawn moss within the NZLA range are limited because the dedicated product is fine-turf-only labelled.

If iron sulphate is used as a moss treatment on a general home lawn:

Rate roughly 50 to 100 g per 100m² dissolved in 5 to 10 L of water. Spray onto moss-affected areas, allow to dry. Don't water in for 24 hours.

Apply in cool conditions (under 20°C) to minimise grass burn risk.

PPE — gloves, eye protection.

Avoid concrete, stone or wood surfaces — stains permanently.

The dual benefit: moss dies, lawn greens up from the iron uptake. Two benefits in one application.

Better long-term answer: NZLA Iron+ (chelated iron) at routine rates for the lawn-benefit side, plus addressing the cultural conditions that allowed the moss to establish. Moss-specific chemistry alone never permanently solves a moss problem on a lawn with moss-friendly conditions.
