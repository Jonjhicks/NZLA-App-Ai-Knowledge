# What is the best mowing height for a mossy lawn?

Higher than your normal maintenance height. Lift the mowing height to give the grass a competitive advantage over the moss.

The principle:

Moss needs light at the soil surface to establish and grow. Short mowing exposes the soil to light. Longer mowing creates a denser canopy that shades the soil and starves moss of the light it needs.

Specific heights for cool-season grass with moss issues:

**General ryegrass lawn with moss:** lift to 50 to 60 mm year-round. Higher than the standard 25 to 50 mm range for ryegrass. The thicker canopy shades out moss establishment.

**Heavily shaded areas with moss:** 60 to 70 mm. The extra height gives the grass more leaf area to photosynthesise in low light, and the canopy is dense enough to discourage moss.

**Mixed lawns with mossy patches:** mow the whole lawn at the height the worst patches need. Better to have a slightly longer overall lawn than to keep losing patches to moss.

For warm-season lawns (kikuyu, couch) the moss issue is usually less because the dense lateral spread of these species naturally crowds moss out. Standard mowing heights of 25 to 50 mm are fine if the lawn is healthy.

What NOT to do for moss control:

Don't mow short to "expose the moss to sunlight" or "stress it out." That just exposes the soil and gives moss more space.

Don't scalp the lawn at any point. Even a single scalp in a moss-prone area creates a moss-friendly patch that takes weeks of growth to recover.

Don't fight the height by mowing more often at a higher target. Lift the height and mow the same frequency.

Other mowing-related moss factors:

**Sharp blade.** Tearing leaf creates weak plants and bare patches. Sharp cuts maintain canopy density.

**Catch the clippings if moss is present.** Don't return clippings to the surface where moss is growing — gives the moss organic material to absorb.

**Mow in dry conditions.** Wet mowing damages the lawn more, opens up bare patches.

For lawns where mowing higher isn't aesthetically acceptable:

Accept that moss management will be more chemical than cultural. Routine NZLA Moss applications keep moss back, but moss returns each season because conditions favour it. The trade-off between aesthetic height and moss-resistant height is real.
