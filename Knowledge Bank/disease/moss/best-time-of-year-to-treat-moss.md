# What time of year is best to treat moss?

Autumn or spring for chemical treatment. The cultural fixes (drainage, shade, fertility, mowing height) are year-round work.

**Autumn (March to May) is the primary moss treatment window.** Moss is actively growing through autumn as conditions cool and damp builds. The active growth means the moss takes up the chemistry and dies effectively. Application before the peak autumn-into-winter moss season pre-empts the worst of it.

NZLA Moss product label notes that best results occur under warm, active growth — autumn typically gives that combination of damp conditions (driving moss activity) with mild temperatures (enabling active growth).

**Spring (September to October) is the second window.** Treats moss that came up over winter and is heading into a new season. Cleans up before the lawn fires up for growing season.

Why not winter:

NZLA Moss specifically calls out warm active growth for best results. Deep winter (June to August) the moss is still alive but metabolically slow. The product still kills moss in winter but takes longer to show full results, and the lawn underneath isn't growing to fill in once the moss is removed.

If you have to treat in winter (heavy moss infestation that needs immediate intervention), apply on the warmest day available with good drying conditions. Expect slower kill and plan the cultural fixes for spring when the lawn can respond.

Why not mid-summer:

Hot dry conditions stress the moss into a less active state, and stress the grass too. Application during heat puts the chemistry on stressed plants and reduces effectiveness on the target moss.

Application practical:

Apply on a still mild day with no rain forecast for 24 hours.

Wet the moss area first if it's dry — moss takes up the chemistry more effectively when hydrated.

Standard rate 200 mL per 10 L water per 100m². 500 mL per 10 L for severe infestations.

PPE non-negotiable — DANGER classification on the label.

After application:

Don't water for 24 hours.

Rake out dead moss within 5 to 7 days once it's black.

Apply NZLA Starter granular and overseed bare patches with appropriate grass seed if the moss removal left significant bare areas.

Address the underlying conditions (shade, drainage, fertility, mowing height) to reduce recurrence.

Schedule: spring AND autumn chemical treatment combined with year-round cultural improvement is the typical professional moss management pattern for persistent problems.
