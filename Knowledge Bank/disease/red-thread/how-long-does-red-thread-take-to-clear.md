# How long does red thread take to clear up?

Typically 2 to 4 weeks with cultural intervention (feeding), faster with fungicide added.

The recovery timeline:

**With cultural fix only (light feeding, no chemistry).** Apply Charger foliar or All Seasons granular at the first sign of red thread. The lawn starts growing more vigorously within 5 to 7 days. New growth pushes the disease back and the existing damaged tips get mowed off. Visible recovery within 2 to 3 weeks, full recovery within 4 to 6 weeks.

**With cultural fix plus fungicide.** Add NZLA Azoxy (on label for red thread) at 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. The systemic chemistry stops the disease spreading immediately. Combined with feeding, full clearing within 2 to 3 weeks. Faster than cultural alone but rarely necessary on home lawns.

**Without intervention.** Red thread is mostly self-limiting. It runs for 4 to 8 weeks during the favourable conditions (cool damp weather, low fertility), then fades as conditions change. The lawn often looks rough during this period but generally recovers fully once conditions shift.

What affects the timeline:

**Severity at intervention.** Mild red thread (just appearing) clears faster than established red thread (multiple patches across the lawn). Catch it early for fastest recovery.

**Growing conditions during recovery.** Active growing weather (warm temperatures, adequate moisture) supports faster recovery than cool slow growth. Spring or autumn recovery is faster than mid-winter recovery.

**Underlying fertility.** A chronically underfed lawn keeps producing weak slow growth that the disease can keep up with. A properly-fed lawn outgrows the disease within days of feeding starting.

**Species.** Some grass species recover faster than others. Perennial ryegrass usually clears red thread quickly. Fine fescue, slower to recover from any stress, takes longer.

What to expect during recovery:

Damaged leaf tips fade as new growth comes through. The red thread structures themselves stop appearing once the disease is no longer active. The lawn looks rough during the transition — partly recovered, partly still showing damage — for 1 to 2 weeks before fully clearing.

If recovery seems slow:

Check the fertility — is the lawn actually getting fed at proper rates? Foliar feeding once isn't always enough on a chronically underfed lawn.

Check the species — is it growing actively at the current temperature? Cool-season grass in winter cool conditions grows slowly regardless of fungicide.

Check for compounding factors — drought, shade, compaction, other diseases. A lawn under multiple stressors recovers from any one of them more slowly.

Be patient. Red thread is rarely a long-term problem when fertility is addressed.
