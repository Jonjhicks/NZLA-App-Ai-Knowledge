# Is red thread serious?

Mostly cosmetic. Red thread looks unpleasant but rarely causes lasting damage to the lawn.

The reasons:

**The fungus attacks leaf tips primarily.** It doesn't penetrate deep into the plant or reach the crown. The grass plant itself stays alive — only the leaf tips are damaged.

**The plant grows out of it.** Once growth resumes normally (with feeding), the grass produces new leaves that aren't infected. The damaged tips get mowed off and the lawn looks fine within a few weeks.

**It rarely kills plants.** Even severe red thread leaves the grass alive. The patches look bad but the lawn recovers without overseeding.

When red thread is more of an issue:

**On showcase or premium lawns** where the cosmetic appearance matters. Red thread visibly degrades the look for the weeks it's active.

**On chronically underfed lawns** where the disease keeps recurring. Year after year of red thread indicates a chronic underlying issue (low fertility) that should be addressed regardless of disease.

**Combined with other stresses.** Red thread plus drought plus low fertility on a thin lawn can compound into more noticeable damage. The disease accelerates the existing decline.

**On lawns being managed tight for low input.** Some low-input lawns deliberately keep fertility low to reduce maintenance. Red thread is more common on these lawns and may be accepted as part of the cost.

What red thread tells you:

The lawn is underfed. The disease is signalling that the grass isn't getting enough nitrogen to grow at a healthy rate. The right response is the cultural fix — feed the lawn.

NZLA Charger (7-0-7 + Fe + Mn) foliar gets growth firing fast without forcing soft growth. Iron+ for colour. All Seasons granular for longer-term steady feeding.

The lawn typically clears within 2 to 3 weeks of starting consistent feeding. Chemistry isn't usually necessary.

If you've been feeding properly and red thread persists:

Check for other issues — compacted soil, drainage problems, poor mowing, shade. A consistently underperforming lawn with persistent disease often has multiple compounding factors.

If after cultural intervention red thread is still causing visible damage and the lawn is high-value, NZLA Azoxy is on label for red thread. Systemic knockdown stops the spread. Maximum 2 consecutive applications then rotate to Fungus Pro.

For most home lawns, the cultural fix is plenty. Don't reach for chemistry on a minor cosmetic disease that the lawn will outgrow with proper feeding.
