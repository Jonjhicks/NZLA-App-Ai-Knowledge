# What is red thread and how do I treat it?

Red thread shows up as small irregular patches with distinctive pink to red thread-like fungal growth on leaf tips — the "threads" are 1 to 5 mm long and easy to see in cool damp morning conditions. Patches are 50 to 200 mm across.

Underfed cool-season lawns (ryegrass especially, also fescue) are most susceptible. Same chemistry family as dollar spot and rust — low-N, slow-growth diseases.

Cultural fix is the foundation. A light nitrogen feed gets the lawn growing through it — the lawn outgrows the infected tips faster than the disease can keep up. Charger (7-0-7 with iron and manganese) gives the lift without forcing soft growth that would invite Fusarium. All Seasons granular at 2 kg per 100m² works on a longer timeframe.

Reduce leaf wetness — dew removal, morning irrigation rather than evening, improve airflow. Mow regularly and catch the clippings to take infected leaf off the lawn.

Chemistry as backup. NZLA Azoxy is on label specifically for red thread. Systemic knockdown when the disease is running hard. Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating to a different FRAC group.

Fungus Pro (thiram, contact) is the rotation partner — different chemistry group (M3 multi-site), almost zero resistance risk. Run Azoxy for the active knockdown, Fungus Pro as the protectant between sprays.

Red thread is mostly cosmetic and rarely kills the lawn outright. Most cases sort themselves out within 2 to 4 weeks of feeding and cultural fixes. Chemistry only needed if it's running hard or hitting a showcase lawn.
