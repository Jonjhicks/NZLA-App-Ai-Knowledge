# How do I tell red thread from rust?

Two diseases that both hit underfed lawns but look distinctly different up close.

**Red thread.** Pink to red thread-like fungal structures (called "stromata") visible at the tips of grass blades, 1 to 5 mm long. Look like tiny pink-red whiskers projecting from the leaf tips. Often visible in cool damp morning conditions. The grass underneath is straw-coloured to bleached. Patches are typically 50 to 200 mm across.

**Rust.** Orange or yellow pustules (small raised spots) along the length of the grass blade, not just at the tip. The pustules contain orange-yellow powdery spores that rub off on your fingers, shoes or clothes when you walk through affected grass. Patches are less defined than red thread — often a yellowing across larger areas of the lawn rather than discrete patches.

The diagnostic test: walk through the affected area. If orange dust rubs off onto your shoes or trouser legs, it's rust. If you see distinctive pink-red threads on individual blades, it's red thread.

What they have in common:

Both are "low-nitrogen diseases." Both indicate the lawn is underfed and growing slowly. Both respond to the same cultural fix — a light feed gets the lawn growing through them.

Both are mostly cosmetic and rarely kill the lawn.

Both occur in similar seasonal windows — late summer through autumn primarily, sometimes into winter.

Differences in treatment approach:

**Red thread** — NZLA Azoxy is on label specifically for red thread. Systemic knockdown. Plus cultural — light N feed (Charger foliar), All Seasons granular.

**Rust** — NZLA Fungus Pro is on label for rust. Azoxy is NOT on the NZ Azoxy label for rust. Plus cultural — light N feed, mow regularly to remove infected leaf tips.

For both, the cultural fix often clears the disease without chemistry being strictly necessary. The lawn outgrows the disease once growth is back to normal speed.

If you're seeing both at once:

Common on a chronically underfed lawn. Both diseases are signalling the same underlying issue. Address the fertility (steady balanced feeding programme) and both diseases tend to clear together.

Don't reach for chemistry as the first move on either red thread or rust. The cultural fix (feed it, mow it regularly) usually does the work.
