# How do I treat anthracnose on my lawn?

Chemistry plus cultural correction in the same pass.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Azoxy at label rate, foliar spray.
- Apply at first sign or as a preventative on susceptible lawns going into the summer window.
- Two consecutive applications max, then rotate to Fungus Pro for resistance management.
- Don't water in (foliar disease, the active needs to stay on the leaf).
- Don't mow for 24 hours either side of application.

Cultural fixes alongside the chemistry:
- Lift mowing height. Low mowing on stressed turf is the single biggest trigger for anthracnose. Raise the cut to 40 mm or higher and the disease pressure drops.
- Improve fertility. NZLA Liquid Boost or Liquid N to address nitrogen stress, NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ granular for sustained potassium (which improves anthracnose resistance).
- Address irrigation. Don't let the lawn drought-stress through summer, but don't overwater either. Deep watering 2 to 3 times a week is the right pattern.
- Reduce wear on the affected area while it recovers.

Recovery push:
- After the chemistry has stopped the active disease, push regrowth with Liquid Boost and Iron+.
- New leaf takes 2 to 4 weeks to fully replace damaged tissue.
- Overseed bare areas where the disease has thinned the lawn (autumn timing for ryegrass).

Prevention going forward:
- Maintain higher cutting heights on susceptible lawns.
- Adequate fertiliser programme through summer.
- Consistent irrigation rather than feast-and-famine.
- Soil test if low pH might be contributing - lime to bring pH back into 6.0 to 6.5 if needed.

Anthracnose on a poa-contaminated lawn is partly a symptom of the poa being present. Long-term, reducing the poa proportion (NZLA Etho on ryegrass lawns, cultural management) reduces anthracnose pressure.
