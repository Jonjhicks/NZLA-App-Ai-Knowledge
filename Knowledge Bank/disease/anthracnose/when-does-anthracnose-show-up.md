# When does anthracnose typically show up?

Summer through early autumn. Air temps 25 to 32 °C combined with humidity is the classic anthracnose window.

NZ timing by region:
- Upper North Island (Auckland, Northland, Bay of Plenty): January to March is peak window.
- Central and lower North Island: February to early April.
- South Island: less common, but can show up in Marlborough and Nelson in hot dry summers on stressed turf.

What triggers a flare-up:
- Heatwave following a wet period (lawn stressed by heat while humidity stays up).
- Drought stress without recovery before a humid week.
- Recent low mowing into stressed turf.
- High nitrogen flush followed by drought (pushes soft growth, then collapse).

The pre-disease conditions (June to December) matter more than the moment of outbreak. A lawn that's been stressed all spring is set up for anthracnose by January. A lawn that's been well-managed through spring usually shrugs it off.

Preventative application of NZLA Azoxy in early summer (December) on susceptible lawns (heavy poa contamination, lawns with anthracnose history) is the standard approach for higher-input maintenance.

Curative application once symptoms show works but the damage that's done is done. Faster to spray earlier when you see the conditions building (heat plus humidity) than wait for the visible patches.

Lawns at most risk:
- Poa-contaminated lawns through summer.
- Low-cut lawns (under 30 mm).
- Lawns with marginal fertility.
- Sports field surfaces under wear and stress.

Lawns at low risk:
- Well-maintained home lawns at 40 to 50 mm cutting height with regular fertiliser.
- Tall fescue lawns (less susceptible than poa or ryegrass).
- Lawns without poa contamination.
