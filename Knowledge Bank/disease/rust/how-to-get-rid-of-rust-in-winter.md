# How do I get rid of rust in winter?

Tricky because winter is when the standard cultural fix (feed it and mow it out) doesn't work as well — cool soil temperatures slow growth response and you don't want to force soft N growth into Fusarium pressure.

The winter rust approach is different from the summer/autumn approach.

What works in summer and autumn:

NZLA All Seasons granular gets growth firing fast. Lawn outgrows the rust within 2 to 4 weeks. Standard cultural fix.

What works in winter:

**Foliar feeding instead of granular.** Cool soil temperatures (below 10°C) mean granular nitrogen sits unused. Switch to foliar.

NZLA Charger every 2 weeks. The 7% nitrogen plus 7% potassium pushes growth without the heavy N load that would invite Fusarium. Iron and manganese drive colour without forcing soft growth.

NZLA Iron+ every 2 to 3 weeks. Pure iron — drives chlorophyll and visible green pickup, hardens the plant against multiple disease pressures, no nitrogen to feed Fusarium.

**Catch the clippings.** Every mow removes infected leaf material and orange spores from the lawn. Reduces the spore load building up.

**Mow regularly.** Even in winter when growth is slow, every 2 to 3 weeks at a lifted height (40 to 60 mm for ryegrass). Sharp blades.

**Sort leaf wetness.** Dew removal, no evening watering, improve airflow. Dries the leaf faster between rain events.

**Don't push high N.** This is the bit that catches people out. The instinct on rust is to feed nitrogen — and that works in warmer conditions. In winter, high N invites Fusarium which causes far worse damage than rust does. Stick with the balanced low-N programme.

If you need chemistry:

NZLA Fungus Pro (thiram) is on label for rust. 200 mL per 100m² in 20 L water for prevention, every 4 weeks. 800 mL per 100m² for curative on active outbreaks.

NZLA Azoxy is NOT on the NZ label for rust. Don't reach for Azoxy expecting rust control — different from how it's used overseas.

For most home lawns:

Rust in winter often just persists at low levels until spring brings warmer growing conditions. The lawn doesn't outgrow it fully in winter but the damage stays cosmetic. Once spring fires up growth (October-November), the lawn clears the rust within a few weeks.

If you can accept the cosmetic damage through winter, the cultural management (mow with catching, foliar maintenance) plus patience is enough.

For showcase lawns where appearance matters more, the foliar feeding plus Fungus Pro programme handles it through winter.

Don't push aggressive N feeding in winter. Patience wins.
