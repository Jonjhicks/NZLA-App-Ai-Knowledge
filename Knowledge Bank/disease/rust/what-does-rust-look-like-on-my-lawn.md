# What does rust look like on my lawn?

Orange or yellow pustules along the length of grass blades. The pustules are small raised dots filled with orange-yellow powdery spores. The whole affected area takes on an orange or yellow-orange cast.

The diagnostic test: walk through the affected grass. Orange dust rubs off onto your shoes, socks or trouser legs. That dust is the spores being shaken off the pustules. If you can rub orange off the grass onto something white (a piece of paper, a white sock), it's rust.

Other identifying features:

**Pustules along the blade.** Not just at the tip (that would be red thread) and not as defined spots (that would be leaf spot). The pustules can be on both sides of the leaf and along most of its length.

**Yellow to orange overall colour.** The whole patch or area looks washed-out, yellow-orange rather than green. Sometimes patchy, sometimes spreading across larger areas of the lawn.

**Most active late summer through autumn.** Some rust pressure into early winter too. Less common in spring and early summer.

**Worse on slow-growing underfed lawns.** Rust takes hold when growth is slow — typical of underfed lawns, late summer when nitrogen has been depleted, autumn before the autumn feed.

What rust looks like at different severity:

**Mild.** Scattered yellow-orange patches across the lawn, visible orange dust on contact, the lawn looks unwell rather than dead.

**Moderate.** Larger areas yellow-orange, individual leaves visibly carrying multiple pustules, contact transfers significant orange.

**Severe.** Whole lawn yellow-orange, leaves heavily covered in pustules, walking through the lawn coats shoes in orange spores.

How rust differs from other diseases:

**vs red thread.** Red thread has distinctive pink-red threads at leaf tips. Rust has orange pustules along the blade.

**vs nitrogen deficiency yellowing.** Hungry yellow lawn doesn't transfer orange dust on contact. Rust does.

**vs autumn natural fading.** Cool-season grass slows down in autumn and can yellow somewhat naturally. Doesn't transfer orange on contact.

**vs leaf spot.** Leaf spot has defined dark spots with halos, not orange pustules.

Significance:

Like red thread, rust is mostly cosmetic. It rarely kills the lawn. The disease is a signal that the lawn is underfed and growing slowly.

Treatment: feed the lawn (cultural fix). NZLA Fungus Pro is on label for rust if chemistry is needed (NZLA Azoxy is NOT on label for rust in NZ).
