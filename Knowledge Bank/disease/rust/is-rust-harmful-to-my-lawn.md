# Is rust harmful to my lawn?

Mostly cosmetic. Rust looks unpleasant but rarely causes serious lasting damage.

The reasons:

**The fungus stays on leaf surfaces.** Rust pustules are visible on the leaves but the fungus doesn't penetrate deep into the plant or reach the crown. The plant itself stays alive.

**The lawn grows out of it.** Once feeding gets growth moving again, the new leaves come through clean. The damaged leaves get mowed off and the lawn looks fine within weeks.

**Plant death is rare.** Severe rust thins the lawn over time as the chronic stress weakens individual plants, but it doesn't cause the dramatic patch death you see with Fusarium or brown patch.

When rust matters more:

**Showcase or premium lawns** where the cosmetic appearance carries weight. Rust visibly degrades the look during the months it's active.

**Chronic recurrence year after year.** Persistent rust over multiple seasons indicates chronic underlying issues (low fertility, drought stress, poor cultural management). The lawn gradually weakens over time even though no single year kills it.

**Combined with other stresses.** Rust plus drought plus heat plus low fertility on a thin lawn can compound into more noticeable decline.

**For people allergic to fungal spores or with respiratory sensitivities.** Walking through heavily rusted grass releases visible orange spore clouds. Some people react to this. Worth considering for high-traffic areas if rust pressure is severe.

What rust tells you:

The lawn is underfed and growing slowly. Rust is one of several "low-nitrogen diseases" along with red thread and dollar spot. The right response is the cultural fix — feed the lawn properly.

NZLA Charger (7-0-7 + Fe + Mn) foliar drives growth without forcing soft growth that would invite Fusarium. NZLA All Seasons granular for longer-term steady fertility.

The lawn outgrows the rust within 2 to 4 weeks of consistent feeding starting. New growth pushes through clean.

Cultural intervention beyond feeding:

**Mow regularly and catch the clippings.** Removes infected leaf material and orange spores from the lawn. Reduces the spore load for re-infection.

**Reduce leaf wetness.** Dew removal, morning watering, airflow improvement.

**Don't water in the evening.** Extends overnight leaf wetness that favours rust establishment.

If chemistry is needed (rare on home lawns):

NZLA Fungus Pro (thiram contact protectant) is on label for rust. 200 mL per 100m² in 20 L water for prevention, every 4 weeks. 800 mL per 100m² for curative on active outbreaks.

For most home lawns, cultural fix is plenty. Chemistry only for severe persistent cases.
