# How do I treat take-all patch?

Chemistry plus cultural correction. Take-all is one of the harder lawn diseases to handle and full recovery takes multiple seasons.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Azoxy applied preventatively in autumn (March to May) on susceptible lawns.
- Watered in lightly so the active reaches the root zone (different to most foliar fungicide applications - take-all is a root disease, you need soil contact).
- Two to three applications across autumn at the label interval, then resistance management rotation.
- Effect is suppression, not eradication. The pathogen stays in the soil.

Cultural management is where most of the long-term progress happens:
- Reduce soil pH if it's above 6.5. Sulphur application over multiple seasons brings pH down toward 6.0 where take-all is less aggressive.
- Improve soil biology. NZLA Humic+ and Amino+ to support beneficial microbes that compete with the pathogen.
- Improve drainage. Take-all favours wet conditions.
- Avoid heavy lime applications. They favour the pathogen.
- Manage thatch (don't let it build up, but don't aggressively scarify into the disease window either).

Reseeding affected areas:
- Wait until the chemistry has knocked back the active infection.
- Reseed with tall fescue or ryegrass (less susceptible than browntop).
- For browntop lawns where take-all is a recurring issue, gradual species shift away from browntop is the practical solution.

Realistic expectations:
- Take-all rarely clears completely on a susceptible lawn.
- Management is about reducing damage and slowing expansion.
- Some affected areas may need to be permanently converted to less susceptible species.

For NZ home lawns where take-all has appeared, honest advice is to soil test (confirm pH), apply Azoxy as a holding action, work on cultural improvements over years. Quick fixes don't exist for this disease. The investment in soil chemistry and biology pays off long-term but the lawn won't look perfect overnight.
