Fairy ring is a circular or arc-shaped pattern caused by soil-dwelling basidiomycete fungi growing outward from a central organic food source. There are three types, and which type you have determines what you can actually do about it. Rings expand outward at 100 to 600mm per year and can reach several metres across over time.

## The three types

**Type 1 — Dead ring.** The most damaging. The fungal mycelium creates a hydrophobic (water-repelling) layer in the soil, preventing moisture reaching grass roots above it. The grass dies in a distinct ring or arc. The dead ring can persist for years.

**Type 2 — Dark green ring.** The fungus breaks down organic matter and releases nitrogen — effectively fertilising the grass directly above. Shows as a ring of noticeably darker, faster-growing grass. No damage, purely cosmetic contrast.

**Type 3 — Mushrooms or toadstools.** Fruiting bodies appear in a ring or arc, typically after rain in autumn and spring. The lawn itself may show no other symptoms. Primarily a cosmetic and safety concern.

## What causes it

Fairy ring fungi feed on organic matter in the soil — usually old tree roots, buried wood or construction debris, deep thatch, or any decaying organic material. As the fungus moves outward consuming that matter, it traces the characteristic ring shape. Mature lawns are more prone than new ones because organic matter has accumulated. Sandy soils can be more susceptible to Type 1 (the hydrophobic form).

## Treatment by type

**Type 2 (dark green ring):** Cosmetic only, no damage occurring. Maintain the surrounding lawn at the same colour intensity with NZLA Iron+ and Liquid Boost applied across the full lawn — evening out nutrition levels reduces the visual contrast. The ring fades naturally over time as the underground organic matter is exhausted.

**Type 3 (mushrooms):** Knock them off or rake before they release spores. Don't compost lawn mushrooms — some species are toxic. NZLA Fungus Pro is registered for mushrooms and fairy ring — apply at label rate to the ring area. Keep children and pets away from unidentified mushrooms. If a dog has eaten a mushroom and is unwell, contact a vet.

**Type 1 (dead ring):** Requires the most work. Core aerate through the ring area first, then apply NZLA Wetter or Aerate immediately after. Getting a wetting agent into the aeration channels forces moisture through the hydrophobic layer and back to the root zone.

NZLA Posterity is registered for fairy ring control. Applied with a wetting agent and watered in well after aeration, it suppresses the fungal activity and gives the grass a better chance of recovery.

For severe Type 1 rings where the grass has been dead for an extended period, physical soil removal is the most reliable fix: excavate the affected area to 300mm depth and 500mm beyond the visible ring boundary, replace with fresh uncontaminated soil, and reseed with NZLA RSR Ryegrass or appropriate blend. Significant work, but the most reliable option where other approaches haven't held.

## Mushroom safety

Most lawn mushrooms are cosmetic, but some are toxic to humans and pets. Without expert identification, treat all unknown lawn mushrooms as potentially harmful — rake them off before children or dogs interact with them and dispose in the rubbish, not compost. The underground fungus continues regardless of whether you remove the fruiting bodies.

Repeated mushroom flushes in the same area indicate a buried food source (often a tree stump or large root) that the fungus is working through. Removing the buried organic material removes the food source and the problem resolves over time.

## Long-term management

Remove buried organic matter when encountered during renovation work. Manage thatch through regular light scarification. A lawn with healthy soil biology decomposes organic matter steadily rather than letting it accumulate into the large reserves that sustain fairy rings over years. Once established, fairy rings are persistent — the realistic goal for most home lawns is management and minimising visible impact rather than complete eradication.
