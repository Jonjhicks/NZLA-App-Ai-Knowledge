# What does melting out look like?

The advanced and most damaging stage of Helminthosporium (Drechslera) disease. The fungus has spread from leaf tissue to the crown of the plant, killing entire grass plants in patches.

The progression:

**Stage 1 — leaf spot.** Small dark spots on individual leaves with light centres and yellow halos. Damage is at the leaf level only — the grass plants themselves are fine. Lawn looks "spotty" rather than dead.

**Stage 2 — crown infection.** The fungus moves from leaf tissue down into the crown of the plant (the growing point at the base). The entire grass plant becomes infected and starts dying. From the surface this looks like patches of yellow then brown grass.

**Stage 3 — melting out.** Patches of dead grass appearing as if the lawn has "melted away" in those areas. Where there used to be grass, there's just bare or thinly-vegetated soil. Patches can be irregular shapes from small spots to large areas.

The visual gives the disease its name — sections of the lawn appear to dissolve or melt over the course of weeks.

How to differentiate from other diseases:

**vs Fusarium.** Fusarium patches are typically more uniform in colour (orange-tan) and develop in cool damp conditions. Melting out patches are more variable colour and worst in spring and autumn during temperature transitions.

**vs drought damage.** Drought damage follows topographic patterns (high spots first). Melting out ignores topography.

**vs grub damage.** Grub-damaged turf lifts like a carpet. Melting out lawn stays attached to the soil — the plants are dead but the surface roots may still be present.

**vs heat stress.** Heat damage shows up across the whole lawn uniformly. Melting out appears in distinct patches.

Treatment urgency:

Melting out is the late stage of an infection that should have been caught at leaf spot stage. Once plants are dying, fungicide stops the disease spreading but doesn't bring dead plants back.

NZLA Azoxy is on label specifically for Helminthosporium diseases. 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. 21-day application interval. Maximum 2 consecutive applications then rotate to Fungus Pro.

Plan for overseeding in spring (autumn-occurring melting out) or autumn (spring-occurring) to fill the dead patches.

Prevention is far better than treatment. Mow at higher heights, balanced fertility, reduce stress factors.
