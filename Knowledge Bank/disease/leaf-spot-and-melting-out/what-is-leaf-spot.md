# What is leaf spot and how do I treat it?

Leaf spot shows up as small dark spots on individual grass leaves, often with light centres and a yellow halo. Can progress to "melting out" where the disease spreads to the crown of the plant and kills it outright — entire patches die in severe cases.

Worst on stressed, mowed-too-short lawns in spring and autumn. The Helminthosporium fungus that causes it (technical name Drechslera) takes advantage of any weakness in the plant.

Cultural fix:

Lift the mowing height. Leaf spot hits scalped lawns hardest because there's less photosynthetic area for the plant to recover. 40 to 50 mm for ryegrass during a leaf spot episode rather than the usual 25 to 30 mm.

Reduce stress generally. Avoid the things that compound the problem — drought, over-fertilisation, compacted soil. A relaxed lawn that isn't being pushed handles leaf spot far better than one being managed tight.

Balance fertility. Moderate nitrogen, decent potassium. Avoid high-N pushes during a leaf spot outbreak.

Chemistry:

NZLA Azoxy is on label specifically for Helminthosporium diseases (which includes leaf spot and melting out). 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. Application interval is 21 days for Helminthosporium specifically (shorter than the 28-day standard for other diseases).

Fungus Pro (thiram) covers leaf spot on label as well. Rotation partner with Azoxy — Azoxy for systemic knockdown, Fungus Pro for contact protectant cover.

Maximum 2 consecutive Azoxy applications before rotating.

Catching leaf spot early matters more than with most diseases — once it progresses to melting out the patches that die don't recover without overseeding.
