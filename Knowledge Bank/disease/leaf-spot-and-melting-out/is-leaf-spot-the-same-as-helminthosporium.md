# Is leaf spot the same as Helminthosporium?

Essentially yes. Helminthosporium is the genus of fungus that causes most turf leaf spot diseases. The names are used interchangeably in NZ turf contexts.

The technical position:

Originally, multiple turf diseases were classified under the genus Helminthosporium. Later taxonomic revision moved the turf-relevant species into different genera — primarily Drechslera, Bipolaris and Exserohilum. But the original Helminthosporium name stuck in industry use, so "Helminthosporium leaf spot" or "Helminthosporium disease" still refers to what's now technically Drechslera or related species.

For practical lawn management, the distinction doesn't matter. The same products and cultural practices apply regardless of the precise modern taxonomic name.

What it all means in practice:

The disease in your lawn is leaf spot if it's at the early stage (spots on individual blades) and melting out if it's progressed to plant death in patches.

The causal fungus is Helminthosporium (old name) or Drechslera (modern name) or one of a few related species. NZLA Azoxy's label lists "Helminthosporium diseases" — this covers the various species that cause leaf spot and melting out in NZ turf.

The diseases this category covers:

**Helminthosporium leaf spot** — small dark spots with light centres on individual leaves.

**Melting out** — the advanced stage where the fungus reaches the crown and kills patches of the plant.

**Net blotch** — variant of leaf spot with a net-like pattern of lesions, sometimes called Drechslera net blotch.

**Crown rot** — late-stage damage where the crown is destroyed and plants die.

All caused by similar pathogens, all responding to similar treatment.

Treatment:

NZLA Azoxy is on label for Helminthosporium diseases. 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. Application interval 21 days (shorter than the 28-day standard for other diseases on the Azoxy label). Maximum 2 consecutive applications then rotate to Fungus Pro.

Fungus Pro (thiram contact protectant) for ongoing prevention and rotation partner.

Cultural fix: lift mowing height, balance fertility, reduce stress. The disease takes advantage of stressed weak lawn — keep the lawn strong and it doesn't establish.
