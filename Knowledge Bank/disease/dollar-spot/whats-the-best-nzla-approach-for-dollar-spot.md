# What is the best NZLA approach for dollar spot?


That makes dollar spot management more cultural-focused than for other diseases where NZLA has chemistry that covers them on label.

The NZLA approach is built on the fact that dollar spot is a low-fertility disease. Get the fertility programme right and the disease often doesn't establish, or clears quickly when it does.

**1. Steady balanced fertility.** NZLA All Seasons (21-1-16 + 5% Fe) every 6 to 8 weeks through the growing season. Or All Seasons N+ at the same frequency. The moderate nitrogen with strong potassium and iron is the right profile to keep the lawn growing through dollar spot pressure.

**2. Foliar N support during outbreaks.** When dollar spot appears, NZLA Charger (7-0-7 + Fe + Mn) every 2 weeks delivers the nitrogen the disease is signalling the lawn needs. The lawn grows out of the small patches faster than the disease can spread. Charger's low salt and citrate K chemistry suits warm humid conditions where dollar spot pressure builds.

**3. Iron for hardening.** Iron+ foliar between granular applications. Drives chlorophyll and plant cell wall strength without forcing soft growth.

**4. Cultural moves.** Dew removal, morning watering, improved airflow. Same basics as for other foliar diseases.

**5. Mowing matters.** Sharp blades, regular cuts, catch the clippings during active disease to take infected leaf off the lawn.

The honest customer conversation:


Cultural plus fertility approach handles most home lawn dollar spot effectively. Premium turf operators dealing with persistent severe dollar spot need the chemistry that NZLA doesn't yet offer.
