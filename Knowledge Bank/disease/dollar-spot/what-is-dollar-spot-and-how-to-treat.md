# What is dollar spot and how do I treat it?

Dollar spot shows up as small straw-coloured circular patches about 30 to 50 mm across — roughly the size of a coin, hence the name. On individual leaf blades the lesions are hourglass-shaped, bleached white with tan borders. Often visible in the morning with a faint cobweb-like mycelium across the patches that disappears once the dew has gone.

Worst on lawns with low nitrogen and high humidity. Same disease family as rust and red thread — a low-fertility, slow-growth condition tells dollar spot the lawn is vulnerable.

Cultural fix is the foundation. Feed the lawn (this is a low-N disease — modest nitrogen application gets the lawn growing through it). Reduce leaf wetness — dew removal in the morning, water in the morning not evening, improve airflow. Keep mowing regularly to remove infected leaf tips.

Chemistry is the second tool. NZLA Azoxy is NOT on label for dollar spot — this catches people out because azoxystrobin is widely used on dollar spot overseas, but the NZ label doesn't include it.


If chemistry is needed beyond what NZLA currently offers, the right active will need sourcing through professional channels — flag the gap honestly rather than substituting an unrelated chemistry.
