Leaf spot and melting out are two stages of the same  complex caused by Helminthosporium fungi (modern taxonomy: Drechslera and Bipolaris species). Leaf spot is the early, recoverable stage — spots on individual leaf blades. Melting out is what happens when it's left untreated and the  reaches the crown of the plant, killing entire grass plants in patches.

Catching it at leaf spot stage is the difference between a manageable problem and one that needs seeding-and-over-seeding.

## What drives it

The  takes advantage of stressed, weakened turf. Key triggers are low mowing height, inadequate fertility, and temperature stress during spring and autumn transitions.

**Mowing height** is the biggest single factor. Turf mowed short — particularly under 30mm — has reduced photosynthetic area, shallow roots, and less plant tissue to fight back. Most severe leaf spot outbreaks trace back to a lawn being cut too short. Raising the mowing height is the most impactful single action in treatment and prevention.

**Fertility imbalance.** Underfed lawns growing slowly with weakened cells are vulnerable. Conversely, high-nitrogen pushes that produce soft, sappy growth during temperature transitions are also at risk. Balanced moderate feeding with adequate potassium and iron gives the best resistance.

**Spring and autumn conditions.** Most active during temperature transitions — cool mornings, warm afternoons, high moisture. In NZ: March through May and August through October are the key risk windows.

## Identifying leaf spot

Small dark spots on individual grass blades — tan, brown, or dark reddish-brown with a lighter centre and a clearly defined border. Spots are oval or elongated along the blade. In early morning, look for a yellow halo around the spots.

The lawn at this stage doesn't look dead — it looks "off." The overall colour is less vibrant, the texture looks slightly rough.

## Identifying melting out

When leaf spot progresses to the crown, whole plants die. Damage areas are irregular in shape, not the defined circles of fusarium or dollar spot. Colour in affected areas is tan to grey-brown.

**How to confirm:** Pull a handful of grass from the affected area. In melting out, the plants come up with blackened, rotted crowns — the growing point at soil level is destroyed. This is distinct from drought stress (plants alive but wilted), fusarium (distinctive orange-tan colour), or grub damage (whole turf mat lifts like carpet).

**Progression:** Leaf spot only → fungus reaches crown, plants begin dying → plants dead in patches, recovery requires seeding-and-over-seeding. Catching it at stage one means no dead patches and no seeding-and-over-seeding.

## Treatment

**Lift the mowing height immediately.** Before anything else. If the lawn is at 25-30mm, raise to 40-50mm. The  pressure drops when the plant has more leaf area.

**NZLA Azoxy** is on label for Helminthosporium diseases. Apply at 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 litres of water. For Helminthosporium, reapply every 14 to 21 days — shorter than for some other diseases. Maximum two consecutive applications before rotating.

**NZLA Fungus Pro** covers leaf spot and melting out as a contact protectant. Use as the rotation partner after Azoxy, or as preventative treatment in known problem seasons.

**Fertility correction.** NZLA All Seasons (21-1-16+5%Fe) for balanced feeding. The iron component hardens leaf tissue against infection. Iron+ foliar between granular applications for resilience without nitrogen push.

**What NOT to do:** Don't push high nitrogen during an active outbreak. Soft new growth is more susceptible. Hold off on high-N products until the  is controlled.

## Recovery

Leaf spot (early stage):  stopped by fungicide + mowing height raised + fertility corrected = lawn fills back through new growth. No seeding-and-over-seeding required. Four to six weeks in active growing conditions.

Melting out (advanced): dead patches from crown death don't regrow. Plan for seeding-and-over-seeding. Autumn is the best timing — NZLA RSR Ryegrass or appropriate blend with Liquid Starter and Starter granular for establishment.

## Prevention

Mow at the right height (above 35mm for most home lawns), maintain steady fertility with All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks, use morning irrigation-general, manage thatch, core aerate regularly. A well-maintained lawn at the right mowing height rarely develops significant leaf spot. It's almost always a stressed lawn being pushed too hard.
