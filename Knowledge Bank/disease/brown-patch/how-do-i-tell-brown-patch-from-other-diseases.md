# How do I tell brown patch from other lawn diseases?

Three identifying features for brown patch specifically:

**Size and shape.** Brown patches range from 100 mm to several metres across. Roughly circular or irregular but with reasonably defined edges. Smaller circles join into larger areas as the disease spreads.

**The smoke ring.** In early morning when the disease is active, watch for a darker grey-purple ring around the outer edge of the patch where the fungus is actively advancing into healthy tissue. The smoke ring is the classic brown patch diagnostic — distinct from Fusarium, dollar spot, rust and other diseases.

**Season and conditions.** Brown patch is a warm-weather disease. Most active in hot humid conditions above 25°C with high humidity, typically January through March in NZ. Other diseases peak in cooler conditions (Fusarium is autumn-winter, dollar spot late summer-autumn, rust autumn).

How brown patch differs from other diseases:

**vs Fusarium (Microdochium patch).** Fusarium hits in cool damp conditions (autumn-winter). Brown patch is hot-humid. Fusarium patches usually have a tan to orange-brown colour. Brown patch is brown to grey-brown.

**vs dollar spot.** Dollar spot patches are much smaller — 30 to 50 mm (size of a coin). Brown patch is much larger. Dollar spot has hourglass-shaped lesions on individual blades. Brown patch causes more general blade dieback.

**vs leaf spot / melting out.** Leaf spot shows dark spots on individual blades before any patches develop. Brown patch goes straight to whole-blade dieback in patches.

**vs drought damage.** Drought damage follows topographic patterns (high spots first, where soil dries fastest). Brown patch ignores topography. Drought damage shows uniform yellowing then browning. Brown patch shows the smoke ring as a moving edge.

Treatment if confirmed brown patch:

NZLA Azoxy is on label for brown patch. 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. Systemic knockdown. Maximum 2 consecutive applications then rotate to Fungus Pro.

Cultural fix: back off the nitrogen in summer heat, reduce humidity, improve airflow.
