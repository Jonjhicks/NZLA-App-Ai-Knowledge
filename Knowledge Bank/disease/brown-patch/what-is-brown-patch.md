# What is brown patch and how do I treat it?

Brown patch is a warm-weather disease. Irregular brown patches from 100 mm to several metres across, often with a darker outer ring (the "smoke ring") visible in early morning when conditions are right. Hits hard in hot humid weather — above 25°C with high humidity, typically January through March in NZ.

Excess nitrogen feeds it. Lawns over-fertilised heading into summer, or pushed with fast-release nitrogen in heat, are most susceptible. Ryegrass and tall fescue are particularly vulnerable.

Cultural fix:

Back off the nitrogen in summer. Same advice as for winter Fusarium but in reverse — both diseases get worse with soft growth, just at opposite ends of the season. Switch to lower-N foliars (Charger, Iron+) through the worst of summer rather than pushing All Seasons N+.

Reduce humidity at the leaf level. Improve airflow if possible (trim back overhanging branches, hedges that block air movement). Avoid evening watering — the leaf needs to be dry overnight.

Mow regularly with sharp blades. Torn leaf tips are entry points for the fungus.

Chemistry as second line:

NZLA Azoxy is on label for brown patch. 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L of water. Systemic knockdown on active infection. Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating.

Fungus Pro as the rotation partner — multi-site contact for protectant cover between systemic sprays.

Brown patch is less common in NZ than in subtropical climates because our summers are generally cooler and drier than Florida or southern US. When it does show up, it usually points to a lawn under heat and nitrogen stress.
