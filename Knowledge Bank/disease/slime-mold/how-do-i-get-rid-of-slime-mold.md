# How do I get rid of slime mold?

You usually don't need to. Slime mold disperses naturally within a week and causes no lasting damage. But if you want to remove it for aesthetic reasons, three approaches work.

**1. Hose it off.** A strong jet of water from a garden hose knocks the visible slime mold off the grass blades. The slime mold flushes down into the soil where it continues its natural life cycle without being visible on the lawn surface. Fastest visible result. Take a couple of minutes.

This is the standard professional recommendation for slime mold removal.

**2. Mow it off.** Mowing the affected area removes the leaf material the slime mold is sitting on. The clippings go into the catcher with the slime mold. Bag the clippings rather than mulch them — the slime mold can re-establish from infected clippings if returned to the lawn.

Sharp blade for clean cuts.

**3. Rake it out.** For dried fruiting structures (the crusty or powdery stage after the slimy stage), raking lifts the dried material from the lawn surface. Same principle as raking thatch — light scarifying with a fan rake removes the residue.

**What NOT to do.**

Don't apply fungicide. Slime mold isn't a fungus and fungicides aren't designed for it. The treatment is wasted and the residue may affect beneficial soil biology.

Don't apply moss killer. Wrong product for the organism. Same logic as fungicide.

Don't dig out the soil around the slime mold. Slime mold isn't anchored in the soil the way grass roots are. The soil isn't infected.

Don't worry about the lawn underneath. The grass is fine. Once the slime mold is removed (or disperses naturally), the lawn returns to normal within days.

Preventing recurrence:

Slime mold often appears in damp organic-matter-rich areas. Reducing the conditions reduces recurrence.

**Reduce thatch.** Heavy thatch holds moisture and organic matter at the soil surface — slime mold habitat. Scarify periodically to manage thatch.

**Improve drainage where possible.** Wet conditions favour slime mold.

**Watch overhead watering.** Wet leaf canopy combined with warm conditions favours slime mold.

**Manage mulch carefully near lawn edges.** Garden mulch is a common source of slime mold spores that spread to adjacent lawn.

In practice, slime mold is so transient that prevention isn't worth heavy effort. Hose it off if it appears, accept it as a curiosity, move on. The lawn doesn't suffer.

For 99% of home lawns, slime mold is a one-week event that resolves itself with no intervention.
