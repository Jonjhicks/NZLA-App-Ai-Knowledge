# When does pythium show up in NZ lawns?

Warm humid weather is the trigger. Air temps over 25 °C combined with night temps above 20 °C and high humidity is the danger window.

NZ timing by region:
- Upper North Island (Auckland, Northland, Bay of Plenty): January through March is peak window.
- Central NI: late January to early March in hot wet summers.
- South Island: rare but possible in Marlborough, Nelson, in unusual conditions.

Triggers within the window:
- Heatwave following heavy rain (lawn is wet from rain, then humidity stays high).
- Overnight irrigation during a humid spell.
- Long periods of overcast warm weather where canopy doesn't dry out.
- Recent nitrogen fertilisation pushing soft growth into a vulnerable window.

Damping-off on new seedings:
- Spring and early summer seedings are most vulnerable.
- Particularly bad when soil is warm but rainfall is unpredictable.
- Overwatering is the usual contributor.

Pythium root rot:
- Chronic, can show any time of year on poorly drained stressed lawns.
- Often shows as a gradual decline rather than acute outbreak.
- Cool wet autumn and winter conditions can support pythium root activity.

Preventative chemistry:
- For high-risk lawns (putting greens, bowling greens, high-input home lawns), NZLA Azoxy in late spring before the danger window opens.
- For home lawns, preventative use is overkill in most cases. Curative use at first sign is more common.

Conditions that reduce risk:
- Early morning irrigation rather than evening.
- Adequate drainage and drying out between waterings.
- Moderate nitrogen levels rather than aggressive push.
- Mowing height kept reasonable (40 to 50 mm), not too low.
- Air movement (don't crowd the lawn with overgrown shrubs that block airflow).

For most NZ home lawns, pythium is an occasional issue rather than a chronic one. Lawns in upper NI under aggressive maintenance have more vulnerability than standard suburban lawns elsewhere.
