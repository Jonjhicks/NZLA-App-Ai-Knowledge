# How do I treat pythium?

Speed matters. Pythium foliar blight can destroy a lawn in 24 to 48 hours if conditions are right and chemistry isn't applied fast.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Azoxy at label rate, foliar spray to dry leaves.
- Apply at first sign or preventatively when conditions favour pythium (warm humid spell forecast).
- Don't mow for 24 hours either side of application.

The bigger fix is cultural correction because pythium thrives on the conditions you can change.

Cultural fixes:
- Reduce irrigation immediately. Pythium loves wet conditions, drying the lawn out is the first response.
- Switch overnight irrigation to early morning. Reduces the canopy moisture duration that pythium needs.
- Cut back nitrogen. Soft growth from high nitrogen is more susceptible. Hold off any fertiliser applications during the active infection.
- NZLA Penetrate moves surface water down through the soil profile, reducing canopy moisture duration.
- Improve drainage where possible.

For pythium damping-off on a new seeding:
- Reduce watering frequency. The seedling stage often gets overwatered.
- Improve seedbed preparation - good drainage from the start.
- NZLA Azoxy at label rate at seeding can prevent damping-off but read the label carefully for seedling safety.
- For repeated damping-off failures, reseed when soil temps are slightly cooler (autumn over spring for ryegrass and fescue).

Recovery:
- Bad pythium kills the turf in affected patches. Reseeding is needed once conditions are dry and stable.
- Pythium root rot causes chronic decline rather than acute death. Address the underlying conditions (drainage, irrigation, fertility) and the lawn slowly recovers.

Honest framing: pythium foliar blight is one of the worst lawn diseases to hit. If conditions are favourable and chemistry isn't applied early, the lawn loses out. NZLA Azoxy covers it on the label but more specialised pythium chemistry isn't in the NZLA range. Bowling greens and golf course use heavier pythium chemistry rotations than what's available for home lawn use.
