# How do I treat fairy ring in my lawn?

Depends on the type. For most NZ home lawns, the management is cultural rather than chemical.

Type 2 (dark green ring, no damage):
- Cosmetic only. Skip treatment if you can live with it.
- Mask the ring with a strong nitrogen and iron programme so the whole lawn is dark green and the ring disappears visually.
- NZLA Liquid Boost plus Iron+ across the whole lawn evens out the colour.
- Over years, the ring usually fades as the underlying organic matter is consumed.

Type 3 (mushrooms or toadstools):
- Knock the mushrooms off as they appear (rake or kick them).
- Don't compost - some species are toxic.
- Treat as cosmetic. The underground fungus continues regardless.
- Keep kids and pets away if unsure what species you've got.

Type 1 (dead grass in the ring):
- The harder case. Hydrophobic mycelium in the soil is killing the grass above.
- NZLA Wetter or Aerate applied across the affected area breaks the water-repellent layer and helps water reach the soil.
- Aeration through the affected area opens up the soil for water and chemistry.
- Where possible, dig out the affected soil (down to 200 to 300 mm), replace with fresh soil, reseed. Extreme step but the only reliable way to remove established Type 1 rings.
- Reseeding the dead area with NZLA Starter and Liquid Starter once the soil is treatable.

Long-term prevention:
- Remove old tree roots, buried wood and organic debris when you find them.
- Avoid letting thatch build up (regular scarifying or dethatching).
- Healthy biology overall (NZLA Humic+, Amino+, mulch mowing) tends to crowd out fairy ring fungi.

Chemistry option globally is high-rate fungicide application but it's not a standard NZ home lawn solution and the NZLA range doesn't have a registered fairy ring product. Be honest with customers about the limits here, fairy ring is a tough one.
