# What is fairy ring?

A circular or arc-shaped pattern in the lawn caused by soil-dwelling fungi (various basidiomycete species). The fungus grows outward from a central point underground, creating a ring of fungal activity at the leading edge.

Three types based on appearance:

Type 1: Damaged ring with dead grass in the centre. The mycelium creates a water-repellent layer in the soil that kills the grass above. Most damaging type.

Type 2: Dark green ring with healthy grass. The fungus is releasing nitrogen as it breaks down organic matter, fertilising the grass above. Cosmetic issue only.

Type 3: Ring of mushrooms or toadstools showing on the surface. The fruiting bodies of the underground fungus. Mostly cosmetic, sometimes pet/child safety concern with toxic species.

The rings grow outward at 100 to 600 mm per year and can reach several metres across over time. Once established, they're hard to eradicate.

What drives fairy ring:
- Decaying organic matter in the soil (old tree roots, buried debris, deep thatch).
- Sandy soils more than clay.
- Mature lawns more than young ones.
- Spring and autumn flushes after rain or irrigation.

NZ relevance: common on home lawns, especially older lawns and lawns over previous tree sites. Type 2 (dark green ring) is the most common and the least damaging. Type 1 (dead ring) is the troublesome version.

NZLA range doesn't have a registered fairy ring fungicide. The chemistry that works internationally (some QoI fungicides applied at high rates with wetting agent) isn't a standard NZ home lawn solution. NZLA Wetter or Aerate can help with the water-repellent layer if that's the symptom.

Cultural management is the main approach for home lawns.
