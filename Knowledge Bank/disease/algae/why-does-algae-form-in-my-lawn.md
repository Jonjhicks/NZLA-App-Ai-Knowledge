# Why does algae form in my lawn?

Algae establishes where four conditions combine: constant surface moisture, light reaching the soil, low grass density, and poor drainage.

**Constant surface moisture.** Algae needs water at the soil surface to live and reproduce. Lawns where water sits at the surface for extended periods — after rain, after irrigation, in cool damp conditions — are vulnerable. Heavy clay soils, compacted areas, and spots with poor drainage all create the standing moisture algae needs.

**Light at the soil surface.** Algae photosynthesises (like moss does) and needs light to grow. Where the grass canopy is thin or absent, light reaches the soil surface and algae can grow. Bare patches, thin lawn from wear or disease, mowed-too-short turf, areas where grass has died back from drought or other stress.

**Low grass density.** A healthy thick lawn shades the soil surface and outcompetes algae for light, moisture and nutrients. A thin patchy lawn leaves space for algae to establish.

**Poor drainage.** Surfaces that stay wet between rain events let algae build up. Lawns on heavy clay, lawns with compacted soil, lawns where the surface has become hydrophobic or sealed — all of these encourage algae.

Less obvious contributing factors:

Shade. Even minor shade extends the surface moisture window. North-facing fences and overhanging branches create local algae-friendly conditions.

Over-irrigation. Daily light watering keeps the surface constantly damp. The grass roots stay shallow and the surface conditions favour algae over grass.

Over-fertilisation with high N. Soft lush growth that thins out under disease or stress leaves spaces for algae. Balanced slow-release feeding instead.

Mowing too short. Scalped lawns expose soil to light and shrink the grass canopy.

Cultural prevention:

Improve drainage where possible (core aeration, NZLA Aerate on heavy soils, NZLA Penetrate for surface moisture management). Lift mowing height to develop denser canopy. Sort fertility for a competitive grass canopy. Address shade where you can.

Killing algae chemically without fixing the conditions just buys you weeks before it returns.
