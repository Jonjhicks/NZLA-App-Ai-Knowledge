# How do I tell if my lawn has Fusarium or just morning dew?

Look at the persistence and the pattern.

Dew is droplets. It sits as discrete water beads on the leaf, breaks up if you brush a finger through it, and is gone by mid-morning once the sun is on the lawn. Same look across the whole lawn, no patches.

Fusarium mycelium (the cottony white-ish webbing) sits as a fluffy mass between leaves rather than as droplets. It does not break up like dew does, and it will still be there at 10 or 11 in the morning when the dew has long dried off. There is usually a tan or orange-brown patch underneath where the disease has done damage.

The dead giveaway is the patch itself. Dew is even across the whole lawn. Fusarium picks specific spots — small tan or brown circles a few centimetres across, joining up into larger areas as the disease spreads.

If you are still not sure, photograph it in the morning, then check the same spot two hours later. Dew is gone. Fusarium mycelium is still sitting there.
