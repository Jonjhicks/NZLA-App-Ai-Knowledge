# Will my lawn recover from Fusarium damage?

Yes for mild and moderate damage. Severe damage often needs overseeding to fully recover.

The recovery depends on how much actual plant death the disease caused, versus just leaf damage.

**Mild Fusarium (caught early, small patches).** The fungicide stops the disease before plants die. Once disease is controlled, the lawn fills in over 4 to 8 weeks as surrounding grass grows over the affected areas. No overseeding needed.

**Moderate Fusarium (multiple patches, some plant death).** Fungicide stops the spread, but the dead grass plants don't regrow — those plants are gone. Surrounding grass grows in to cover thin patches but bigger dead areas remain bare. Recovery takes 8 to 12 weeks, and the lawn may benefit from overseeding the bare patches once spring growth returns.

**Severe Fusarium (large dead areas, weeks of untreated progression).** Significant plant death across the affected areas. Overseeding is generally required. Plan for full recovery to take 10 to 16 weeks from spring overseeding.

The winter complication:

Fusarium peaks in autumn through winter when cool-season grass growth is slow. Even if the disease is killed in autumn, the lawn doesn't fill back in until spring temperatures return and growth fires up. So a customer hit by Fusarium in May or June is looking at dead patches all winter even with successful treatment.

That's not the disease failing to be treated — it's the season. Once winter ends and growth resumes in October-November, the dead patches start filling in.

What helps recovery:

**Get the fungicide right immediately.** Don't wait for "how bad it gets" — catch it early.

**Address the underlying causes** (dew, watering timing, soft growth, lack of airflow). Without those fixes, recurrence is likely.

**Don't overload nitrogen during recovery.** Forces soft growth that re-invites disease. Iron+ for colour without growth, All Seasons for steady balanced feeding once weather warms.

**Plan overseeding for spring** if dead patches remain. NZLA Perennial Rye (or whatever species suits the lawn) at 2 kg per 100m² oversow rate, with NZLA Starter and Liquid Starter to support establishment.

**Be patient.** Winter Fusarium recovery doesn't happen in winter. Spring brings the regrowth.
