# What does Fusarium look like as it develops?

Three stages of development worth knowing for diagnosis and timing of treatment.

**Early stage (first signs).** Small irregular patches 50 to 100 mm across appearing as slightly different colour than the surrounding lawn. Tan, yellow-orange, or rust-brown. Often visible after a wet cool night when conditions are favourable. White-pink cottony mycelium may be visible at the patch edges in early morning before the dew has lifted — diagnostic when present.

At this stage, prompt treatment stops the disease before it does major damage. Fungus Pro plus Azoxy at this point typically prevents progression.

**Mid stage (active progression).** Patches enlarge and join up into larger irregular areas. Colour deepens to definite orange-tan or brown. The pink mycelium becomes harder to spot once the disease is established beyond the early infection stage. New patches appear at the edges of existing ones and in fresh spots across the lawn.

This is where most customers notice the disease for the first time. Treatment is still effective but the lawn shows visible damage during recovery.

**Late stage (established damage).** Large irregular dead areas across the lawn, multiple patches joined into bigger sections. Grass plants in the worst patches die outright rather than just showing leaf damage. The patches stay dead through winter — no regrowth until spring temperatures return.

At this stage, fungicide stops the spread but the existing dead areas won't recover until spring growth. Plan for overseeding in spring.

Other features to know:

Patches tend to be larger and rounder on shorter mowed lawns. On longer lawns the patches can be more irregular and harder to identify in the early stages.

Colour can vary from tan to orange-brown to almost grey depending on the lawn species and the temperature when infection peaked.

In wet conditions the patches may have visible mycelium for days. In drier conditions the mycelium dries up overnight and you only see the brown spots.

Treatment urgency: catch it at early to mid stage for best results. Late stage damage takes overseeding to fully recover.
