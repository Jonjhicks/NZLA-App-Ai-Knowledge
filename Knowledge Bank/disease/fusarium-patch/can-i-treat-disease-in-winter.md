# Can I treat disease in winter or should I wait?

Treat as soon as you see it. Fungicides don't care about the season — they work when applied.

What changes in winter is the recovery side, not the treatment side. The fungicide stops the disease spreading and protects healthy tissue. But the dead patches it leaves behind won't regrow until spring temperatures return and growth fires up. So you'll be looking at the patches all winter even after the fungicide has done its job. Accept that and move on.

Get Fungus Pro on as soon as you see disease activity. Contact protectant chemistry that knocks down surface mycelium and protects the healthy tissue around the patch. 200 mL per 100m² in 20 L of water, every 4 weeks (every 2 weeks in high humidity).

Back it up with Azoxy. Systemic knockdown on the infection that has already moved into the plant. 23 mL per 100m² in 4 to 8 L water. Maximum 2 consecutive applications before rotating to Fungus Pro.

What NOT to do in winter:

Don't hit it with high-N fertiliser hoping the lawn will outgrow it. Cool soils mean the lawn can't use the nitrogen anyway, and you're feeding the disease at the same time. Iron+ for colour without growth is the right tool here.

Don't skip the cultural fixes (dew removal, airflow, water timing) just because it's cold. Those reduce the conditions that drive the disease — fungicide alone without cultural fix is short-term.

Don't expect the dead patches to fill in over winter. They won't. Spring overseeding may be needed if the damage is severe.

Catch it early and the winter ride is manageable. Leave it and it spreads through the whole lawn before spring.
