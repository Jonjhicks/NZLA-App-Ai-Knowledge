# How do I prevent winter lawn disease?

The single most useful thing is reducing leaf wetness.

Knock the morning dew off — drag a hose, a brush, or a length of rope across the lawn. The dew is gone in a couple of minutes and the leaf dries hours earlier than it would otherwise. Fungal disease pressure is heavily driven by canopy moisture duration, so cutting that short matters more than most fungicide applications.

Water in the morning rather than the evening. Irrigation overnight extends the wet window by 8 to 12 hours. Morning watering lets the lawn dry off through the day.

Improve airflow if you can — trim back hedges or branches that block air movement across the lawn.

Sort out poorly draining areas. NZLA Penetrate helps move surface water down through the profile.

Fertility going into winter:

Don't hit it with high nitrogen in autumn. Soft growth into cool damp conditions feeds Fusarium and the other cool-season patch diseases. All Seasons (21-1-16 with iron) is the right autumn feed — the potassium and iron harden the plant, the modest nitrogen keeps it ticking without forcing soft growth.

Iron+ as a foliar through the cool months. Greens the lawn and hardens the plant without pushing nitrogen growth.

Fungicide programme as backup. Fungus Pro (thiram, contact protectant) on a routine 4-week pattern through the wet months — preventative rather than waiting for disease to appear. Azoxy as systemic knockdown if disease shows up. Rotate the two.

Disease in winter is mostly preventable with these basics. The customers who get hit are usually the ones doing the opposite — heavy nitrogen, evening watering, no dew removal, no fungicide.
