# Why does Fusarium keep coming back every year?

Because the conditions that drive it haven't been fixed. Fungicide treats the symptom; the underlying conditions are what allowed the disease to establish in the first place.

The reasons Fusarium recurs:

**Continuing soft growth into cool wet conditions.** The single biggest factor. If you're applying nitrogen in autumn (or following a fertiliser programme heavy on N going into winter), you're feeding the disease every year. Switch to a balanced autumn feed (NZLA All Seasons with 16% K and 5% Fe) that hardens the plant rather than pushing soft growth.

**Persistent leaf wetness.** Lawns that stay damp overnight every night are at chronic risk. Evening watering, no dew removal, poor airflow, shade, drainage issues. The disease pressure builds year after year because the conditions are unchanged.

**Spore load in the soil.** Once Fusarium has established in a lawn, the fungal spores persist in the soil for years. Reinfection from the soil spore bank happens whenever conditions favour it. The only way to reduce reinfection is to make conditions unfavourable.

**Drainage and waterlogging.** Lawns where surface water lingers and the canopy stays wet for hours after rain have permanent vulnerability. NZLA Penetrate to move surface water down through the profile. Address structural drainage where possible.

**Compaction.** Compacted soil holds water at the surface, restricts root growth, and keeps the lawn under stress. Core aeration in spring or autumn opens the soil up. Improves overall lawn health and reduces disease pressure.

**Mowing too short heading into winter.** Scalped lawns are weaker and more disease-prone. Lift mowing height to 40 to 60 mm for winter.

**Heavy thatch.** Thatch holds moisture against the crowns and provides habitat for fungal pathogens. Scarify periodically to manage thatch.

The year-on-year fix:

Address each of these systematically across a season. Don't try to do everything at once — pick the biggest two or three causes for your specific lawn and work on those.

Long-term:

Routine preventative Fungus Pro through autumn-winter every year while the underlying causes are being addressed. After 2 to 3 years of cultural improvement, the disease pressure drops measurably and you may be able to reduce preventative spraying.

Disease that "always comes back" usually has 2 or 3 underlying causes that haven't been addressed. Fixing them takes seasons, not weeks.
