# How do I treat yellow patch?

Chemistry plus cultural correction in standard pattern.

Chemistry:
- NZLA Azoxy at label rate, foliar spray to dry leaves.
- Two applications max before rotating to Fungus Pro for resistance management.
- Apply preventatively going into the shoulder season window if you've had yellow patch in previous years.

Cultural fixes:
- Reduce nitrogen if recently applied. Yellow patch loves soft nitrogen-pushed growth.
- Improve canopy drying. NZLA Penetrate moves surface water down through the soil profile, reduces leaf moisture duration.
- Address irrigation timing. Switch to early morning irrigation, not evening, so the lawn dries out during the day.
- Improve air movement. Trim back shrubs and trees that block airflow over the lawn.
- Don't lower mowing height into a disease window.

Recovery:
- Yellow patch often clears as conditions change (warmer drier weather).
- The grass underneath the yellow ring is usually alive and recovers once the disease pressure drops.
- Push recovery with NZLA Liquid Boost and Iron+ once the active infection is controlled.

Preventative approach for susceptible lawns:
- Bowling greens and fine turf with yellow patch history: Azoxy in autumn before the cool wet window.
- Home lawns that have had recurring yellow patch: same approach, single autumn application.
- Most home lawns don't need preventative spraying for yellow patch. Curative use when it shows up is enough.

Distinguishing from brown patch:
- Yellow patch happens in cool weather (10 to 20 °C).
- Brown patch happens in warm weather (over 25 °C).
- Yellow patch centre often stays green, brown patch centre dies.
- Same general chemistry (Azoxy) works on both.

If yellow patch keeps coming back year after year on the same lawn, the underlying cause is usually irrigation timing or excessive nitrogen. Cultural correction is the long-term fix.
