# Can I establish a lawn on a steep slope?

Yes, with extra care during establishment and ongoing management. Several challenges to address.

Erosion during establishment:
- Bare soil on slope washes off in heavy rain.
- Seed gets washed downhill before germination.
- Surface stability is the main challenge.

Solutions:
- Erosion control matting (jute or coir mesh) over the seeded area.
- Hydroseeding (commercial application of seed in a stabilising slurry) for larger slopes.
- Light straw mulch over the seedbed protects from rain impact.
- Establishing in seasons with steady rather than heavy rainfall.

Species selection:
- Self-repairing species (NZLA RSR Ryegrass, Elfin Tall Fescue with rhizomes) fill in better on slopes.
- Tall fescue's deep roots provide better soil stability than ryegrass alone.
- For steep dry slopes, drought-tolerant species are more reliable.

Soil preparation:
- Cultivate across the slope (contour lines) rather than up and down.
- This creates small terraces that hold seed and water in place.
- Apply NZLA Starter at standard rate and incorporate.
- Sow at the upper end of normal seeding rate (heavier seeding for slope security).

Watering during establishment:
- Lighter more frequent applications rather than deep watering during the first 2 weeks.
- Heavy irrigation runs off slope without soaking in.
- Hose-end sprinklers with fine spray work better than impact sprinklers.

Maintenance considerations:
- Mowing safety (see mowing-on-slope Q&A).
- Higher cutting height (50 to 60 mm) for soil protection.
- Fertiliser application is harder on slopes - granular falls downhill, foliar runs off.
- Aeration is harder on slopes - core aerators struggle with stability.

When a lawn isn't the right choice for a slope:
- Slopes over 35° or 1:1 ratio - ground cover plants or terracing more practical.
- Banks where mowing is consistently hazardous.
- Slopes with poor drainage and recurring erosion despite efforts.

Alternative ground covers for slopes:
- Native NZ ground covers (Pittosporum, hebes, kowhai seedlings, others).
- Creeping rosemary, lavender, thyme.
- Selected NZ tussock grasses.

For typical suburban slopes (under 25°), lawn establishment with appropriate species and care works fine. For steeper banks, consider whether lawn is genuinely the right choice or whether alternatives suit the situation better.
