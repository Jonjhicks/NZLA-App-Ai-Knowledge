# How do I mow a sloped lawn?

Safety first, technique second. Slope mowing is one of the higher-risk parts of lawn care.

Slope categories:
- Up to 15° (gentle): standard rotary mower handles it without issue.
- 15° to 25° (moderate): standard mower but needs careful technique.
- 25° to 35° (steep): requires specific approach, push mowers preferable.
- Over 35°: ride-on mowers shouldn't be used. String trimmer or specialised slope mower.

Direction of mowing:

For push and self-propelled rotary mowers: mow across the slope (sideways), not up and down. Going up and down increases the risk of the mower running away from you downhill, or being pulled into your legs if you slip.

For ride-on mowers: mow up and down rather than across the slope. Side-on tipping is the main rollover risk. Going straight up and down keeps the centre of gravity over the wheels.

Why the difference: push mowers can be controlled while you stand on the slope above them. Ride-on mowers concentrate weight high, so cross-slope mowing risks tipping sideways.

Safety practices:
- Don't mow wet slopes - traction failure.
- Wear non-slip footwear.
- Don't pull a mower backward up a slope (drags it onto your feet if you slip).
- Stop the mower before crossing a slope if you need to change direction.
- For ride-ons, never operate without functioning ROPS (rollover protection structure) on slopes over 15°.

Equipment considerations:
- Lighter mowers are easier to control on slopes.
- Self-propelled drive can be either helpful (less effort) or hazardous (mower keeps going if you slip).
- Mulching mowers reduce the need for a catcher (catcher weight on slopes is awkward).

For very steep slopes:
- String trimmer (line trimmer) is safer than any mower on slopes over 35°.
- Specialised slope mowers (remote-controlled or specialised tracked machines) for commercial use.
- Consider terracing or planting alternative ground covers if a slope can't be mown safely.

Maintenance considerations:
- Slopes erode faster than flat ground when mown short. Maintain higher cutting height (50 to 60 mm) for soil protection.
- Bare patches on slopes need quick reseeding to prevent erosion.
- Top dressing on slopes is harder than flat ground (material slides off).

For typical NZ suburban properties with modest slopes, standard rotary mowing across the slope handles most situations. For lifestyle blocks with significant slopes, equipment selection matters.
