# How do I manage a lawn on sandy soil?

Different rhythm to clay or loam lawn care. Sandy soil drains fast, holds less water, leaches nutrients quickly. The programme needs to match.

Water management:
- Sandy soil dries out fast. Deeper less frequent watering than clay (15 mm twice a week through summer).
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W keeps water moving into the soil rather than off the surface during dry hot conditions.
- Hydrophobic dry patch is common in sandy soils - regular wetting agent applications prevent it.

Fertiliser management:
- Nutrients leach quickly through sandy soil. Standard granular intervals may not be enough.
- More frequent applications at moderate rates work better than infrequent heavy applications.
- NZLA Charger or Liquid Boost monthly foliar feeds supplement granular base.
- Slow-release granulars (All Seasons, All Seasons) more efficient than fast-release options on sandy soil.

CEC building over time:
- Sandy soil has low CEC, can't hold many nutrients.
- Organic matter additions (compost top dressing, mulch mowing) improve CEC over years.
- NZLA Humic+ supports the building process.
- Goal is improving the soil over decades, not weeks.

Compaction and structure:
- Sandy soils don't compact as readily as clay.
- Aeration still helpful but less critical.
- Top dressing with loam-rich material over time improves overall soil structure.

Species considerations:
- Sandy soil suits most lawn species, but drought tolerance becomes more important.
- Tall fescue (NZLA Ultimate, Elfin) has deeper roots that reach water further down.
- Warm-season grasses (kikuyu, couch) handle sandy dry conditions well in upper NI.

Sandy soil advantages:
- Fewer drainage problems than clay.
- Less risk of waterlogging.
- Easier to walk on after rain.
- Better surface for sports use (less mud).

Sandy soil disadvantages:
- More frequent management needed.
- Higher cost over time (more fertiliser, more water).
- Lower nutrient holding means standard intervals don't work.

For coastal sandy lawn (sandy plus salt):
- Combine sandy soil management with salt-tolerance considerations.
- See coastal-lawn Q&A for the combined picture.

For inland sandy lawn (sandy without salt):
- Standard programme with adjustments for water and fertiliser frequency.
- Build organic matter as long-term investment.
- Choose drought-tolerant species mix.

NZ regions with prominent sandy soils:
- Coastal areas around most of NZ.
- Volcanic ash soils in Bay of Plenty, central NI.
- Pumice soils in central NI and inland Bay of Plenty.

Each has its own quirks but all share the basic sandy soil management approach.
