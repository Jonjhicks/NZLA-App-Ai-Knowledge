# How do I grow a lawn under trees?

Three challenges combined: shade, root competition for water and nutrients, and leaf litter. Each one needs attention.

Shade management:
- NZLA Fine Fescue for shade tolerance (see shade-tolerant grass Q&A).
- Trim lower tree branches up to 2.5 to 3 metres to admit more light underneath.
- Thin the tree canopy if appropriate (professional arborist for large trees).

Root competition:
- Tree roots near the surface compete with lawn for water and nutrients.
- Extra irrigation and feeding helps the lawn while the trees take their share.
- More frequent feeding at moderate rates (don't try to compete with one big application, the tree gets most of it).
- NZLA Liquid Boost foliar feed bypasses some root competition.

Leaf litter management:
- Leaves smother the lawn if left on through autumn and winter.
- Regular leaf blowing or raking through the leaf fall period.
- Don't let leaves sit for more than a week or two without clearing.

Cultural choices:
- Cutting height 60 to 80 mm (taller for more light capture and root development).
- Mowing direction varies to spread compaction patterns under canopy.
- Reduce traffic where possible.

Species options:
- NZLA Fine Fescue is the primary choice.
- Tall fescue blend for moderate shade with some sun.
- For deep shade, accept thin lawn or convert to non-grass groundcover.

Specific tree types:
- Deciduous trees (oak, plane, maple): summer shade with winter sun. Lawn does better here.
- Evergreen trees (pine, fir, eucalyptus, kauri): year-round dense shade. Hard for any lawn species.
- Trees with shallow surface roots (e.g. some species of maple): worst for lawn establishment because of root competition right at the soil surface.

Pine needles and conifer issues:
- Pine and fir needles add acidity to soil over time.
- Lawn under conifers often suffers from acidic soil compounding the shade problem.
- Soil test for pH if lawn under conifers is struggling.
- Lime application may help.

Realistic expectations:
- Lawn under mature trees will not look the same as lawn in full sun.
- Density and colour will be reduced.
- Goal is acceptable rather than premium.

Alternative options:
- Native ground covers under trees instead of forcing lawn.
- Bark mulch with stepping stones for traffic patterns.
- Garden plantings of shade-tolerant species instead of lawn.

Some sites will never grow good lawn no matter what you do. Accept it and look at alternatives rather than fighting the same losing battle every year.
