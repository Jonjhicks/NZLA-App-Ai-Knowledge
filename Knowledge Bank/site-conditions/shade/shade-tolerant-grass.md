# What grass species handles shade best?

Ranked from most to least shade-tolerant for NZ conditions:

NZLA Fine Fescue (creeping red + chewings fescue): the best shade option in the NZLA range. Tolerates 3 to 4 hours direct sun plus filtered light. Native to forest edge environments in Europe, evolutionarily adapted to lower light.

Tall Fescue (Ultimate, Elfin, or general): handles 4 to 6 hours direct sun. Better than ryegrass but not as good as fine fescue in deep shade. Good fit for partial shade situations.

Ryegrass (Perennial, DPR, Midnight, RSR): needs 6+ hours direct sun for good performance. Thins out quickly in shade. Not the right pick for shade situations.

Warm-season grasses (kikuyu, couch): need full sun. Don't perform in shade.

Why fine fescue handles shade best:
- Evolutionary background in cool moist forest edge environments.
- Lower nitrogen requirements (fits the lower productivity of shaded conditions).
- Slower growth rate (matches limited light availability).
- Better disease resistance under low-light conditions.

Fine fescue trade-offs:
- Lower wear tolerance than ryegrass.
- Slow to establish and slow to recover.
- Limited herbicide options (sensitive to NZLA Etho, Gold, and others - check species restrictions).

For a mixed-sun mixed-shade lawn:
- Establish or overseed with a fescue blend.
- The fescue does the work in shaded areas, ryegrass or tall fescue handles sunny sections.
- Overseed shaded areas separately with NZLA Fine Fescue if the original mix isn't holding up.

For premium shade situations (showcase lawns with some shaded areas):
- 70% NZLA Fine Fescue + 30% Ultimate Tall Fescue gives a fine-textured shade-tolerant lawn that handles moderate sun areas too.

For low-input shade situations (back garden under trees):
- 100% NZLA Fine Fescue at 30 to 40 g per square metre.
- Accept slow establishment and lower density than a sunny lawn.
- Cultural management focused on adequate light (trim lower tree branches), reduced fertility (don't push nitrogen), and shade-appropriate watering.
