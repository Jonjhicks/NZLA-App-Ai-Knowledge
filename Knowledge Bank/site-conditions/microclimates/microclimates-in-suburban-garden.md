# Why does part of my lawn behave differently to the rest?

Suburban lawns have multiple microclimates within one property. Different parts of the lawn experience genuinely different conditions and need different management.

Common microclimate factors:

Sun exposure:
- North-facing areas (more sun in southern hemisphere) are hotter and drier.
- South-facing areas (less sun) are cooler and damper.
- Areas shaded by trees, fences or buildings get less light.

Wind exposure:
- Open areas catch more wind, dry out faster.
- Sheltered corners hold moisture longer.
- Wind from prevailing direction affects one side of the lawn more.

Heat retention:
- Areas near concrete, brick, asphalt are warmer (heat island effect).
- North-facing walls reflect heat onto the lawn.
- Dark mulch or bark in adjacent beds increases nearby temperature.

Water flow:
- Higher ground drains water onto lower ground.
- Roof runoff concentrates water in specific spots.
- Driveway runoff hits the lawn in patterns.

Soil variation:
- Different parts of the lawn often have different soil (from past landscaping, fill, drainage installation, tree removal).
- Some areas have deeper topsoil, others sit on subsoil.
- Patches over old building debris are shallow.

Effects on the lawn:
- One part needs more water than another.
- Different parts respond differently to fertiliser.
- Disease pressure varies across the lawn.
- Some areas need shade-tolerant species, others handle full sun.

Management responses:
- Treat the lawn as multiple zones rather than one uniform area.
- Apply fertiliser at different rates in different zones if the response varies.
- Water different zones for different durations.
- Choose species appropriate for each zone if reseeding (premium ryegrass in full sun, fine fescue in shade).

Common suburban patterns:
- Front lawn (street-facing, often more visible) gets more attention and looks better.
- Side lawns (narrow, between house and fence) often dry out and shade out.
- Back lawn (variable use, often higher traffic) has more wear issues.
- Lawn under washing line gets concentrated foot traffic.
- Lawn around BBQ or outdoor entertainment areas gets concentrated heat and traffic.

For most NZ home lawn owners, recognising that one programme doesn't fit all zones of the lawn is the start of better management. Pay attention to where the lawn is struggling and what's actually different about that zone compared to the parts that are thriving.
