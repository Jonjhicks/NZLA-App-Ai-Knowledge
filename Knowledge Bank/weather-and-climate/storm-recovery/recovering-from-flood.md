# My lawn was flooded - how do I recover it?

Depends on how long the flood lasted and what water came in.

Short flood (under 24 hours, freshwater):
- Most lawns recover fully. Grass is more flood-tolerant than people think.
- Once water drains, assess for silt and debris, remove what's there.
- Resume normal lawn care within a week or two.

Extended flood (24 to 72 hours):
- Some root suffocation likely. Grass may yellow and thin in the worst areas.
- NZLA Aerate after the soil firms up enough to walk on. Helps water and air move through the soil.
- Don't fertilise immediately - the lawn is stressed and the nitrogen will burn.
- Patience and gentle recovery over a few weeks.

Long flood (over 72 hours):
- Significant lawn die-off likely from oxygen starvation.
- Soil may have gone anaerobic (black layer, sulphur smell).
- Aeration urgent once you can walk on the lawn.
- Reseed bare areas with NZLA Starter and seed in autumn.

Saltwater flood:
- More damaging than freshwater. Salt accumulates in soil and damages roots.
- Heavy rain or irrigation needed to leach salt out of the soil profile.
- Recovery slower than freshwater flooding.
- See separate Q&A on saltwater inundation specifically.

Process for typical fresh flood recovery:
1. Wait for water to drain and soil to firm enough to walk on without damaging it.
2. Clear silt and debris.
3. Mechanical aeration to break surface crust and admit air to soil.
4. Apply NZLA Aerate to help water and air movement.
5. NZLA Wetter if the surface has become hydrophobic.
6. NZLA Humic+ to support soil biology recovery.
7. Wait 2 to 3 weeks, then assess.
8. NZLA Liquid Boost foliar feed to push recovery once growth resumes.
9. Reseed bare areas with NZLA Starter and seed.

Don't:
- Walk repeatedly on saturated soil - causes compaction that complicates recovery.
- Apply fertiliser before the soil has firmed and growth has resumed.
- Reseed before the soil is workable and conditions stable.
- Apply herbicide or fungicide on a flood-stressed lawn.

What's normal vs concerning:
- Yellow then green-back within 2 to 3 weeks: normal recovery.
- Slow recovery or persistent bare patches: deeper soil damage, may need physical intervention.
- Black smelly soil: anaerobic, needs aeration and time.
- Mushroom flushes after the flood: natural response to organic matter, ignore.

For typical NZ home lawn freshwater flooding, full recovery within 6 to 8 weeks is reasonable. Saltwater or contaminated water makes recovery longer and more involved.
