# How do I recover my lawn from hail damage?

Hail bruises grass leaves and crowns. Most lawns look terrible for a week or two then recover well.

What hail does:
- Heavy hailstones impact leaves and bruise the tissue.
- Crowns at soil level can be damaged in severe events with large hail.
- Surface ice melts and produces brief but cold conditions.
- Light damage: bruise marks on leaves, recovers in 1 to 2 weeks.
- Heavy damage: brown patches where impact was concentrated, recovery takes 3 to 6 weeks.

Immediate response:
- Wait for the hail and any standing water to clear.
- Don't walk on the lawn until any ice melts and soil is firm.
- Don't mow for at least a week after significant hail.

Recovery push:
- NZLA Liquid Boost or Liquid N at moderate rate once growth resumes.
- Iron+ for colour.
- Restore for tissue recovery support.
- Avoid heavy chemistry (herbicide, fungicide) until the lawn is fully recovered.

For severe damage:
- Patches of bare lawn from impact craters need reseeding in autumn.
- New leaf growth typically replaces damaged leaves within 4 to 6 weeks.
- Crown damage in concentrated areas can lead to permanent thinning if not reseeded.

Hail-affected outdoor objects:
- Inspect for damage to outdoor equipment (sprinkler heads, mower wheels) that may need repair.
- Garden plants and shrubs around the lawn often need their own recovery attention.

Frequency in NZ:
- Hail events are relatively uncommon in most of NZ but do happen.
- Inland Canterbury, Otago, Hawke's Bay see occasional severe hail.
- North Island summer thunderstorms occasionally produce damaging hail.

Insurance:
- Major hail damage may be covered under household insurance for the property.
- Document damage with photos.

For typical NZ home lawn hail damage, the visual impact is worse than the actual long-term effect. Most lawns recover fully within a couple of months with active recovery support.
