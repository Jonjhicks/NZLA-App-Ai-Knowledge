# Can wind damage my lawn?

Yes, in two main ways: physical damage and desiccation (drying out faster than the roots can replace water).

Physical damage:
- Branches and debris falling on the lawn from trees during storms.
- Trampoline or outdoor furniture blown onto the lawn.
- Major cyclones flatten the lawn (recovers within weeks usually).
- Localised damage where heavy objects landed.

Desiccation damage:
- Hot dry wind dries out the lawn faster than passive evaporation alone.
- Lawn browns off even when soil moisture should be adequate.
- Common in inland Canterbury, Otago and Hawke's Bay during nor'wester wind events.
- Coastal lawns dealing with sea spray and constant wind exposure.

How to spot wind desiccation:
- Lawn browns in the windward direction (e.g. western side facing prevailing wind).
- Browning is more uniform than localised disease patches.
- Soil underneath may still be moist - the issue is leaf-level dehydration faster than root replenishment.
- Recovers within days of wind dropping if irrigation is adequate.

Response to wind events:
- Water deeply before forecast wind events to give the lawn moisture reserves.
- NZLA Wetter helps water reach roots during prolonged dry windy spells.
- Don't fertilise during high-stress periods.
- Allow time for recovery after the wind event.

For chronic wind exposure (coastal sites, exposed ridges):
- Windbreaks (hedges, fences) reduce wind speed at lawn level.
- Drought-tolerant species (tall fescue, kikuyu in upper NI) handle wind stress better.
- Higher mowing height (50 to 60 mm) provides some leaf protection.
- Regular wetting agent applications maintain soil moisture availability.

Coastal lawns:
- Salt spray plus wind is a tough combination.
- Salt-tolerant grass species help.
- Regular freshwater irrigation flushes accumulated salt.
- See separate coastal lawn Q&A for ongoing management.

After major wind events:
- Inspect for debris and damage.
- Clear branches and objects from the lawn.
- Check for damaged irrigation infrastructure.
- Apply NZLA Liquid Boost and Iron+ to push recovery.

For most NZ home lawns, wind is a minor and recoverable factor. Coastal and inland-exposed properties deal with more chronic wind stress and benefit from species and cultural choices that match the conditions.
