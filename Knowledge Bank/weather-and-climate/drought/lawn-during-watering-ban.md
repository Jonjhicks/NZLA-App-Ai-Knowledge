# What do I do with my lawn during a watering ban?

The lawn will brown off but most established cool-season lawns recover when rains return. Goal during a ban is to keep the crown alive even if the leaves die back.

What's actually happening:
- Cool-season grass under drought goes into protective dormancy. Leaves brown but the crown (growing point at soil level) stays alive.
- Established lawns can survive 6 to 8 weeks of complete drought without dying, particularly tall fescue and well-rooted ryegrass.
- New lawns (under 12 months) and stressed lawns are more vulnerable.

What to do:
- Raise the cutting height. 50 to 60 mm gives the lawn more leaf area and shading, reducing heat stress.
- Mow less frequently. The lawn isn't growing much, you don't need to.
- Avoid fertilising. Pushing growth into drought conditions just stresses the lawn further.
- Avoid herbicide and fungicide applications - stressed lawn responds badly.
- Reduce traffic. Don't walk repeatedly across drought-stressed turf, the crowns get crushed.

If your ban allows bucket watering or hand-held hose:
- Water key areas (high-value parts of the lawn, new seedings if you have any) rather than trying to cover the whole lawn.
- Water deeply (15 to 20 mm) once a week rather than light daily applications.
- Early morning is best for what little water you can apply.

Pre-drought preparation (if you know dry conditions are coming):
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W applied before the ban gets water moving down into the soil profile. Improves drought tolerance.
- NZLA PGR (trinexapac) before the ban hardens the lawn, reduces growth, improves drought tolerance.
- Deep watering before the ban starts wets the deeper soil profile, giving the lawn moisture reserves to draw on.

After the ban lifts:
- Resume watering gradually rather than dumping huge amounts at once.
- Light irrigation over several days lets the soil rewet without surface runoff.
- Then deep watering programme resumes.

Most cool-season home lawns in NZ recover from drought within 4 to 6 weeks of rain returning. Don't panic during the ban - the lawn is more resilient than it looks.
