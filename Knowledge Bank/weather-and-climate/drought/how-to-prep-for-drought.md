# How do I prepare my lawn for summer drought?

Build drought tolerance before the dry conditions arrive. The investment in spring pays off in summer.

Root depth first. Deep roots reach deeper soil moisture. Build root depth by watering deeply and infrequently from early spring onwards. NZLA Root Health (kelp biostimulant) supports root development through spring.

Soil moisture management:
- NZLA Wetter or Wetter 3W applied in October to November ensures water that does fall (rain or irrigation) actually moves into the soil rather than beading off.
- Wetter 3W gives 12-week residual cover, one application covers the worst of summer.
- For non-irrigated lawns, Wetter GR (granular) is the alternative.

Growth regulation:
- NZLA PGR (trinexapac) applied in late spring hardens the lawn by reducing vertical growth and directing energy to roots.
- A regulated lawn handles heat and drought better than an unregulated one because it's not pushing as much soft growth.

Soil biology and structure:
- NZLA Humic+ and Amino+ through spring support soil biology that contributes to water-holding and stress tolerance.
- A biologically active soil retains more water than a depleted one.

Mowing strategy:
- Raise cutting height in late spring (40 to 50 mm rather than 30 mm). Taller grass shades soil, reduces evaporation, keeps the lawn cooler.
- Sharpen blades. Clean cuts heal faster than torn cuts, less stress for the lawn.

Species considerations:
- Cool-season lawns in drought-prone regions benefit from including tall fescue in the species mix (deeper roots, better drought tolerance).
- NZLA Ultimate Tall Fescue or Elfin Tall Fescue for overseeding in autumn.
- Warm-season lawns (kikuyu, couch in upper NI) are inherently more drought-tolerant.

Fertility:
- Don't go heavy on nitrogen going into summer. Soft pushed growth doesn't handle drought well.
- Adequate potassium improves stress tolerance.
- NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ (both have 16% potassium) supplies that through the spring programme.

A lawn that's been prepared well handles 4 to 6 weeks of drought without serious damage. A lawn going into summer stressed, soft and shallow-rooted struggles after 2 weeks.
