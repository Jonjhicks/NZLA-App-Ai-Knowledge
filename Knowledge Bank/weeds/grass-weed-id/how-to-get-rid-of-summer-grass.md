# How do I get rid of summer grass or crabgrass?

Summer grass and crabgrass (Digitaria sanguinalis and related species) are annual grass weeds that germinate in late spring when soils warm, dominate the lawn through summer, then die off in autumn frosts. Each season starts fresh from seed in the soil — no perennial root system, but the seed bank persists for years.

The whole game is timing pre-emergent before germination.

**Spring pre-em with NZLA Blockade.** 10 to 30 mL per 100m². Apply 2 to 3 weeks before soil temperatures at 50 mm hit 12°C and stay there. Auckland late August. Central NI mid-September. Canterbury late September into early October. Activate with 6 mm of water within 7 days.

A second application 90 to 120 days later carries the barrier through peak germination.

Note the NZ label restriction — Blockade is registered for warm-season turf only here (kikuyu, couch). Off-label use on cool-season turf can't be officially recommended, even though the chemistry is safe and used routinely on cool-season turf overseas.

If you missed the pre-em window and summer grass is already up:

Post-emergent control of established summer grass in cool-season turf is difficult — no fully selective option in the NZLA range. Spot glyphosate (with NZLA Sticker) takes it out along with surrounding lawn; overseed the patches afterward. Or accept the season's damage and rely on pre-em next spring to stop the seeds the current population produces.

Cultural side: summer grass loves stressed thin lawns. A dense well-fed lawn at the right mowing height prevents new seedlings establishing because they can't find light at soil level. Reducing the seed bank long-term requires both pre-em and a healthy competitive grass canopy.

Mow regularly through the season to prevent existing summer grass setting seed, reducing next year's pressure.
