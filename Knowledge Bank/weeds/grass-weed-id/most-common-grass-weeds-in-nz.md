# What are the most common grass weeds in NZ lawns?

Six grass weed species cover most NZ lawn problems. Different control approaches for each.

**1. Poa annua (annual poa).** The most common grass weed in cool-season NZ lawns. Annual lifecycle, germinates in autumn, dominates through winter and spring, dies in summer heat. Light bright green colour distinguishes it from desirable species.

Control: Pre-em Blockade in autumn before germination. Post-em Etho (ethofumesate) for ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. NZLA Paclo (paclobutrazol) for high-performance turf with chronic poa pressure.

**2. Summer grass / Crabgrass (Digitaria species).** Warm-season annual that dominates through summer in cool-season lawns. Wide blue-green leaves, distinctive finger-like seed heads.

Control: Spring pre-em Blockade before soil temperatures reach germination threshold (12°C at 50mm). Post-em options limited in cool-season turf.

**3. Browntop (Agrostis capillaris).** Fine-textured cool-season grass that invades ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. Forms denser finer patches than surrounding grass.

Control: NZLA Meso (mesotrione) — two applications 14 days apart. EPA caps at 2 applications per 12 months.

**4. Kikuyu (Pennisetum clandestinum).** Warm-season grass that's the dominant species in upper North Island but a serious invader in cool-season lawns elsewhere. Spreads aggressively via stolons and rhizomes.

Control: NZLA Halo for fine fescue lawns only (15 mL/100m² rate cap). Spot glyphosate on encroachment patches in other lawn types. Physical barriers along property boundaries.

**5. Paspalum (Paspalum dilatatum).** Coarse warm-season grass that establishes in cool-season home lawns and produces dramatic visual contrast. Wide coarse leaves and distinctive seed heads with 2 to 3 fingers.

Control: NZLA Blockade for pre-em on germinating seed. NZLA Halo on fine fescue. Spot glyphosate on established patches in other lawns.

**6. Creeping bentgrass (Agrostis stolonifera).** Close relative of browntop but spreads more via above-ground stolons. Common in lawns near golf courses or sports turf areas.

Control: NZLA Meso, same as browntop. Two applications 14 days apart.

Less common but worth knowing:

**Yorkshire fog (Holcus lanatus).** Pasture grass that invades lawns. Soft hairy leaves, light grey-green colour.

**Brown sedge (Carex muskingumensis and related).** Sedge family (not strictly a grass). Different chemistry — NZLA Sedge Pro (halosulfuron) for sedge control.

**Smooth witchgrass (Panicum capillare).** Annual warm-season grass that appears in summer lawns. Similar control to summer grass.

The general control hierarchy:

**Pre-emergent prevention** for annual species (summer grass, crabgrass, poa annua) using NZLA Blockade. Timing critical — apply before germination.

**Post-emergent selective control** where chemistry is available — Etho for poa in ryegrass/tall fescue, Meso for browntop and bentgrass in cool-season, Halo for grass-in-grass kill in fine fescue.

**Spot kill with glyphosate** for grass weeds where no selective option exists. Kills surrounding lawn too; reseed afterward.

**Cultural management** as the foundation. A dense well-fed competitive lawn at the right mowing height prevents most grass weed establishment.

For any lawn with multiple grass weed species, prioritise the most damaging ones and address those first. Complete clearance of all grass weeds in one season is rarely achievable.
