# How do I identify browntop in my lawn?

Browntop (Agrostis capillaris, sometimes called bent grass or colonial bent) is a fine-textured grass that often invades cool-season lawns from neighbouring pastures or via wind-borne seed.

Identification:

Very fine narrow leaves, much finer than perennial ryegrass or tall fescue. Leaves typically 1 to 3 mm wide compared to 3 to 6 mm for ryegrass.

Pale to bright green colour. Often looks slightly different shade to surrounding grass species — patches stand out by colour difference.

Spreading habit via short rhizomes. Forms patches that gradually expand outward.

Distinctive seed heads in summer — open branched panicles with a characteristic brownish-purple tint. The colour gives the species its common name.

In mowed lawn, browntop creates noticeably finer, denser patches than surrounding ryegrass or fescue. Looks like a different lawn species coexisting with the main lawn — because it is.

Why it matters:

Browntop in a ryegrass or fescue lawn creates uneven appearance and texture differences. Mowing height adjustment is awkward — what suits the surrounding lawn doesn't always suit the browntop patches.

Browntop is more disease-susceptible than ryegrass — particularly dollar spot. Patches in a ryegrass lawn can become disease entry points.

For golf greens and fine turf where browntop is desirable (it's a common golf green species), the situation is reversed — browntop is the desired species and ryegrass invasion is the problem.

Control:

NZLA Meso (mesotrione 480 g/L) is the NZLA option for browntop control in cool-season turf. The chemistry has selectivity that hits browntop while leaving ryegrass and tall fescue alone.

Two applications 14 days apart provides browntop control. EPA NZ caps mesotrione at 2 applications per area per 12 months — so the browntop programme typically uses both annual applications in one go.

For complete browntop clearance in heavily contaminated lawns, multiple seasons may be needed. The mesotrione 2-application annual cap means each year's progress is limited.

Cultural side:

Browntop establishes in stressed cool-season lawns. Improving lawn fertility and density reduces new browntop establishment but won't clear existing patches.

For large browntop patches:

Spot kill with glyphosate plus NZLA Sticker can be more practical than mesotrione for substantial established patches. Reseeded patches afterward with appropriate ryegrass.

For golf greens or premium fine turf where browntop is the desired species:

Manage the lawn to favour browntop — finer mowing heights, lower fertility, frequent topdressing.

Note: warm-season grass weeds invading cool-season lawns (kikuyu, couch) need different chemistry (Halo on fine fescue only). Browntop is the cool-season grass weed handled by Meso.
