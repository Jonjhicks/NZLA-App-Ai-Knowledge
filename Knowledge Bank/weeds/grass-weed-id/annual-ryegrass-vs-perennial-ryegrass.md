# How do I tell annual ryegrass from perennial ryegrass?

Annual ryegrass (Lolium multiflorum, also called Italian ryegrass) and perennial ryegrass (Lolium perenne) are closely related species often mixed up. Knowing which you have matters because they perform very differently in lawns.

Key differences:

**Lifecycle.** Annual ryegrass completes its lifecycle in one season — germinates, grows, flowers, sets seed, dies. Perennial ryegrass survives for years as a perennial plant, producing seed annually but persisting.

**Growth habit.** Annual ryegrass grows taller and more rapidly than perennial. Often 600 to 1200 mm tall in unmowed conditions, while perennial ryegrass typically reaches 400 to 600 mm.

**Leaf appearance.** Both have similar leaf structure but annual ryegrass leaves are slightly wider and brighter green. Perennial ryegrass tends to have darker green, slightly finer leaves.

**Leaf base (the diagnostic feature).** Roll a young leaf at the base between thumb and forefinger.

Annual ryegrass: the leaf is rolled in the bud (rolled vernation). When you split a young leaf base, you'll see the new leaves emerge rolled.

Perennial ryegrass: the leaf is folded in the bud (folded vernation). Young leaves emerge folded along the midrib.

**Seed heads.** Annual ryegrass has longer awned seed heads — small bristles extending from the seeds. Perennial ryegrass seeds are mostly without long awns.

Why the distinction matters:

**For lawn establishment:** annual ryegrass is sometimes used as a "nurse crop" in mixed seed blends because it germinates fast and provides quick cover while slower perennial species establish. But annual ryegrass dies at the end of its single season, leaving gaps in the lawn unless perennial species have filled in.

**For long-term lawn quality:** perennial ryegrass is the desired species for permanent NZ home lawns. Annual ryegrass provides one season of cover then disappears.

**For seed quality assessment:** cheap or low-quality seed mixes sometimes contain higher proportions of annual ryegrass than premium products. The mix germinates fast and looks good initially but produces a thin lawn after the first season as annual rye dies.
