# How do I identify creeping bentgrass?

Creeping bentgrass (Agrostis stolonifera) is a close relative of browntop, often confused with it. Both are fine-textured cool-season grass species that can become weeds in standard cool-season lawns.

Identification:

Very fine narrow leaves, similar to browntop. 1 to 3 mm wide.

Pale bright green colour, slightly different shade from surrounding grass species.

**Stoloniferous habit** — the defining feature distinguishing it from browntop. Creeping bentgrass spreads via above-ground stolons (horizontal stems that root at nodes). You can see the long horizontal stems running across the soil surface between rooted patches.

Browntop spreads more via rhizomes (underground) and stays in denser patches. Creeping bentgrass spreads more visibly via stolons.

Forms low dense mats over time in suitable conditions.

Where it grows:

Creeping bentgrass is the dominant species on most golf greens (where it's deliberate). In standard home lawns it appears as a contamination, often spreading from neighbouring golf courses, sports turf, or established bentgrass areas.

Common in:
- Lawns near golf courses
- Premium home lawns where original installation included bentgrass mixed in seed
- Lawns where ready lawn or turf was sourced from sports turf operations
- Older lawns that have collected various grass species over time

Why it matters:

Creeping bentgrass in a ryegrass or tall fescue lawn creates uneven texture and colour. Patches stand out by their fine texture and slightly different shade.

Creeping bentgrass is more disease-susceptible than ryegrass (particularly dollar spot). Patches in standard lawn can become disease entry points.

Mowing height suited to ryegrass (25 to 50 mm) is too tall for creeping bentgrass to thrive. Lower mowing heights (5 to 15 mm typical for sports turf) suit it better. So bentgrass in a home lawn often struggles aesthetically.

Control:

NZLA Meso (mesotrione 480 g/L) provides selective control of creeping bentgrass in cool-season turf. Two applications 14 days apart at label rates.

International research (Cambridge University) shows one application controls 7 to 43% of creeping bentgrass; two applications 39 to 88% as rates increase. The variability reflects environmental factors and infestation severity.

EPA cap: 2 applications per 12 months. The full bentgrass programme uses both annual applications.

For lawns with light infestation:

Spot treatment with glyphosate plus NZLA Sticker on individual patches. Reseed with appropriate ryegrass.

For lawns with heavy infestation:

Multi-year mesotrione programme combined with cultural management. Realistic timeline for clearance: 2 to 3 years.

For golf greens or premium fine turf where bentgrass is desirable:

Reverse the control approach — manage to favour bentgrass against invading ryegrass. Different conversation entirely.

Cultural management:

Bentgrass favours close mowing and high fertility — opposite of standard ryegrass lawn management. A standard cool-season lawn maintenance approach (40 to 50 mm mowing height, moderate fertility) suppresses bentgrass naturally.

Adjusting mowing height higher disadvantages bentgrass over ryegrass.

For lawns with persistent creeping bentgrass:

Mesotrione plus cultural management. Manage expectations — complete clearance takes multiple seasons.
