# How do I control yarrow in my lawn?

Yarrow is one of the more persistent broadleaf weeds in NZ lawns — the rhizomatous spread and tolerance of poor conditions make complete eradication difficult.

The chemistry programme:

NZLA BWC (MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican) at 50 mL per 100m². Apply when yarrow is actively growing in mild conditions. Spring (October-November) or autumn (March-April).

NZLA Gold (picloram + triclopyr) for established yarrow patches that BWC has missed or only partially cleared. The triclopyr component handles tough rhizomatous broadleaf weeds better.

Both products applied with NZLA Sticker as adjuvant improves penetration of yarrow's fine feathery leaves.

The rhizome challenge:

Yarrow spreads aggressively underground via creeping rhizomes. A single visible plant can have a rhizome system extending 200 to 500 mm in multiple directions.

Killing the visible above-ground growth with herbicide leaves the rhizome system intact. New shoots emerge from rhizomes over the following 2 to 6 weeks, often in slightly different locations than the original plants.

For complete clearance:

Plan for multiple herbicide applications across a single season — typically 2 to 4 applications at 4 to 6 week intervals through spring-summer-autumn growing season.

Catch new shoots as they emerge from rhizomes. Don't wait for full re-establishment before retreating.

For severely infested lawns (yarrow covering 30+% of the lawn area):

Full renovation may be more efficient than chemistry alone. Kill the lawn with glyphosate (multiple applications across 2 to 4 weeks to catch rhizome regrowth), then re-establish from seed or ready lawn.

For individual yarrow patches:

Spot treatment with glyphosate plus NZLA Sticker kills the patch completely. Reseed with NZLA Perennial Rye or appropriate species.

Cultural improvement:

Yarrow thrives in low-fertility and stressed lawn conditions. A consistent fertility programme with NZLA All Seasons or All Seasons N+ substantially reduces yarrow's competitive advantage.

Lift mowing height to 40 to 50 mm. Yarrow grows lower and can be more competitive at scalped heights.

Address irrigation — yarrow's drought tolerance means it dominates during dry summers. Adequate irrigation removes some of yarrow's advantage.

For lawns with persistent yarrow:

Realistic timeline for substantial reduction: 1 to 2 growing seasons.

Realistic timeline for complete clearance: 2 to 3 growing seasons of consistent intervention.

Acceptance vs eradication:

Some lawn owners eventually accept some yarrow presence rather than continue intensive control. The plant has herbal history, drought tolerance, attracts beneficial insects through its flowers.

For traditional grass-lawn aesthetic, ongoing management is necessary. Complete permanent eradication isn't realistic without renovation.

The combination of chemistry, cultural improvement, and patience is the typical approach. Single-season clearance with single herbicide application is rarely achievable.
