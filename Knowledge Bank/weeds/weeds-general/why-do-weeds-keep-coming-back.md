# Why do weeds keep coming back?

Three reasons, often combined.

**1. The seed bank in your soil.** Every lawn has a soil seed bank — dormant weed seeds accumulated over years. Most weed seeds remain viable for 5 to 20 years. When you kill the visible weeds, the seed bank stays. New weeds germinate from the seed bank each season.

Common lawn weeds and seed bank longevity:

- Dandelion: 5 to 10 years in soil
- Plantain: 10 to 20+ years
- Clover: 10+ years
- Various Asteraceae (catsear, hawkbit, etc.): 5 to 10 years
- Annual grass weeds (summer grass, poa): 3 to 7 years

So even after years of weed control, new germination from the seed bank continues. The cycle only ends when you've killed seedlings consistently for long enough that the seed bank exhausts.

**2. The conditions that favoured them haven't changed.** Weeds establish in lawns where conditions favour them over grass. Thin lawn, low fertility, compaction, shade, wrong species, drought stress, scalped mowing, evening watering — each creates niches for specific weeds.

Address the visible weeds without addressing the conditions and new weeds replace them. The conditions still favour weeds over grass, so something fills the niche.

**3. Continual reintroduction from outside.** Weed seeds blow in from neighbours, parks, pastures, waste areas. Animals (dogs, cats, birds) carry seeds in fur. Garden mulch, soil amendments and seed mixes can introduce new weed seeds.

You'll never eliminate this entirely — but you can reduce it by managing edges, choosing clean soil and mulch, and using certified seed.

The realistic outcome:

For most lawns, complete permanent weed elimination isn't achievable. Realistic goal is suppressing weeds to a low background level where they don't dominate or visually degrade the lawn.

The combination that works:

**Chemistry on visible weeds.** NZLA BWC for broadleaf, appropriate grass weed control as needed. Multi-year programmes for persistent species.

**Pre-emergent prevention.** NZLA Blockade twice a year (spring and autumn) reduces new germination from the seed bank.

**Cultural management.** Steady fertility programme (All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks). Right mowing height (higher than scalped). Proper irrigation. Address compaction.

**Patience.** Combined programme over 2 to 3 years substantially reduces weed populations. Year 1 reduces visible weeds. Years 2 to 3 reduce ongoing germination as the seed bank decreases.

For lawns with chronic weed problems despite intervention:

Check whether the underlying conditions favour weeds. A weak underfed lawn will keep losing to weeds regardless of chemistry. Fixing the conditions is more effective than escalating chemistry.

For lawns with mostly clean appearance and occasional weed appearance:

Spot treat as needed. Routine maintenance keeps the population at acceptable low levels. Don't over-treat a lawn that's mostly clean.

Long-term, weed suppression is part of overall lawn management, not a one-off event.
