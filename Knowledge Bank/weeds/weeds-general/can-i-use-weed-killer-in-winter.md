# Can I use weed killer in winter?

Mostly no for cool-season broadleaf herbicides, but some specific winter applications work.

The general issue:

Selective broadleaf herbicides (BWC, Gold) work by being absorbed and translocated through the target weed. Effective uptake requires the weed to be actively growing.

In winter, weed growth slows dramatically — particularly for warm-season weeds and frost-affected weeds. The herbicide is applied but the weed doesn't take it up effectively. Result: partial kill or failure.

What's typically too slow for winter herbicide:

Most broadleaf weeds in active winter dormancy. Yarrow, established mature broadleaf weeds, anything looking yellow or stressed.

Warm-season grass weeds in any state — kikuyu, couch and similar species are essentially dormant in winter.

What can be sprayed in winter:

**Poa annua control.** Poa annua actively grows through winter in most of NZ. NZLA Etho (ethofumesate) for perennial ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. Two applications 14-21 days apart through autumn-winter window.

**Mild conditions winter days.** Where day temperatures climb into the mid-teens, broadleaf weeds may have enough metabolic activity for selective herbicide uptake. Limited windows but possible.

**Glyphosate spot treatment.** Non-selective glyphosate kills established weeds even in slow growth. Useful for spot kill of persistent weeds in winter. Adding NZLA Sticker as adjuvant improves uptake on slow-growing weeds.

**Onehunga control.** Onehunga is one weed that actively grows through cool months. Late winter application of BWC catches it at the rosette stage before seed heads form (resistance permitting).

What's better waited until spring:

Most general broadleaf weed control. Wait for active spring growth (October-November) for substantially better kill rates.

Pre-emergent application timing — different consideration. NZLA Blockade in autumn before soil temperatures drop into poa germination range works. Spring application before summer grass germination works.

For most home lawn weed problems:

Accept some winter weed presence. Spring weed control programme handles most issues effectively once growth resumes.

Spot treatment of new aggressive weeds (kikuyu encroachment, new infestations) with glyphosate plus NZLA Sticker maintains progress without waiting for spring.

For specific winter weed pressure:

Poa annua: NZLA Etho works through autumn-winter on ryegrass and tall fescue lawns. The chemistry has activity on emerging shoots and roots at the cool-temperature growing window.

Onehunga: late winter applications can be effective if you catch the rosette stage. Apply on mild days.

Cool-season annual weeds: many of these germinate in autumn and remain active through mild winters. Spot treatment can work in mild conditions.

What about pre-emergent in winter:

NZLA Blockade pre-em residual already in place from autumn application is doing its job through winter. New winter applications are unusual — typically you've already applied autumn pre-em before the poa germination window.

For most home lawn owners:

Plan major weed control for spring and autumn. Use winter for monitoring, planning, and minor spot interventions. Don't expect winter herbicide application to deliver the results spring application does.

Sound practice: apply post-em broadleaf control through spring growing window. Apply pre-em in autumn for poa annua. Spot treatment as needed through other seasons.
