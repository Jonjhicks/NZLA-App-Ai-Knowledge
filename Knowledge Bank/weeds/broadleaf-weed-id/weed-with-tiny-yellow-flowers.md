# What is this weed with tiny yellow flowers in clusters?

Hard to call without a photo, but the two most likely candidates this time of year are hop clover and onehunga at the early stage before it sets prickles.

**Hop clover** has soft clover-like leaves (three round leaflets) and small yellow ball-shaped flower heads that look a bit like miniature clover. Generally harmless, easy to control with NZLA BWC.

**Onehunga** has fern-like or parsley-like leaves, low and spreading, with tiny yellow flowers that develop into the prickly seed heads everyone knows by feeling them in bare feet later in the season. Get on top of it before the seed heads form — much harder to control once it has pricked you.

The catch with onehunga in NZ is documented clopyralid resistance, particularly in northern parts of the country, and now spreading. NZLA BWC contains clopyralid, so on resistant populations it underperforms. If it has been working in past years and suddenly is not, suspect resistance.

The proven alternative is mecoprop, which is not currently in the NZLA range. Worth flagging the gap honestly — keep applying more BWC on a resistant population only accelerates the problem.
