# How do I get rid of clover in my lawn?

Two angles — chemistry and conditions.

**Chemistry.** Clover is highly susceptible to most broadleaf herbicides. NZLA BWC (MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican) at 50 mL per 100m² hits it hard — clover usually shows damage within a week and dies within 2 to 3 weeks. NZLA Gold is the stronger alternative for tough clover or lawns where BWC hasn't fully cleared it.

Apply when clover is actively growing (mild conditions, not heat-stressed or cold). Wait 4 weeks before reapplying if any survives.

**Conditions.** Clover thrives where lawns are underfed. Clover fixes its own nitrogen from the atmosphere, so it has a competitive advantage on low-fertility lawns where grass is struggling. If your lawn is full of clover, the underlying signal is that the grass is hungry.

The long-term fix is feeding the lawn properly. A well-fed dense grass canopy outcompetes clover for light and space, and the nitrogen advantage that lets clover dominate disappears when the grass is getting fed externally. NZLA All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks through the growing season, or All Seasons as the value alternative.

Worth knowing: clover is generally beneficial in pastures and farm grass (the nitrogen fixation feeds the surrounding grass), and some people deliberately oversow micro-clover into lawns for that reason. If you're targeting a pure grass lawn for aesthetic reasons, kill the clover. If you don't mind a mixed lawn, leaving the clover and feeding less is a perfectly viable option.

Heavy clover infestation usually clears with one BWC application plus a season of consistent feeding.
