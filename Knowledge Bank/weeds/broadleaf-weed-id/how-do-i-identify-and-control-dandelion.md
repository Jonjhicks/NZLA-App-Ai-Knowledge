# How do I identify and control dandelion?

Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale) is one of the easiest weeds to identify and one of the most common in NZ lawns.

Identification:

Distinctive jagged-edged leaves arranged in a flat rosette at ground level. Leaves are dark green, hairless, with deeply lobed serrations along the edges. Leaves can grow 50 to 250 mm long depending on conditions.

Bright yellow flowers on hollow stems 100 to 300 mm tall. Each flower opens and closes daily, then forms the classic puffball seed head. Each puffball releases hundreds of wind-dispersed seeds.

Deep taproot — sometimes 250 mm or more — that makes hand pulling difficult. Pulled plants regrow from any root left behind.

Where it grows: anywhere in lawns, gardens, paths and waste areas. Adaptable to most soils and conditions.

Control:

NZLA BWC (MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican) at 50 mL per 100m². Dandelion is highly susceptible. Apply when actively growing in mild conditions. Damage visible within a week, full kill within 2 to 3 weeks.

NZLA Gold for tougher mature plants or where BWC hasn't fully cleared dandelion. Stronger chemistry with broader coverage.

Hand pulling works on individual plants if you can extract the full taproot. A weed extractor tool (long thin spade) helps. Any root fragment left will regrow.

Cultural side:

Dandelion thrives in thin underfed lawns where the canopy doesn't shade out germinating seedlings. A dense well-fed lawn at the right mowing height largely prevents dandelion establishing.

Mow regularly to prevent the puffball seed heads forming. Reduces seed bank for next year's pressure.

Pre-em diflufenican has some activity on germinating dandelion seedlings but post-em is the primary control method.

For lawns with established dandelion populations, a complete clean usually takes one season of consistent BWC application plus mowing-out of seed heads.
