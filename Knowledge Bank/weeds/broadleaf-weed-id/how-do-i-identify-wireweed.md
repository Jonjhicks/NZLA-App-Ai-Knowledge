# How do I identify wireweed?

Wireweed (Polygonum aviculare, also called knotweed or prostrate knotweed) is a low-growing summer annual common in compacted areas and lawn edges.

Identification:

Wiry, tough stems that creep along the ground, sometimes mat-forming. Stems are typically 100 to 600 mm long with multiple branches. Each plant can spread 300 to 500 mm across.

Small narrow lance-shaped leaves alternating along the wiry stems. Leaves 5 to 30 mm long, dull blue-green colour. Distinctive small papery sheaths (ochrea) at each node where leaves emerge — the diagnostic feature of the Polygonum family.

Tiny inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the leaf axils through summer.

Where it grows: throughout NZ lawns, particularly in compacted areas, paths, driveways, lawn edges, and worn patches. Tolerates heavy traffic and poor soil better than most broadleaf weeds. Often the first weed to establish in compacted bare patches.

Annual lifecycle: germinates from seed in spring, grows through summer, sets seed, dies in autumn. Each new season starts from seed bank in the soil.

Control:

Wireweed is one of the tougher broadleaf weeds to control with selective chemistry because of the tough wiry stems and small leaves.

NZLA BWC (MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican) at 50 mL per 100m² gives partial control. Young plants respond better than mature plants with extensive wiry stem development.

NZLA Gold (picloram + triclopyr) is more effective on tough wireweed. The triclopyr component handles waxy and tough-stemmed weeds that BWC sometimes leaves.

Apply when wireweed is actively growing — mild conditions in spring or early summer. Catch it early before extensive stem development.

For mature wireweed in compacted areas:

Spot treatment with glyphosate plus NZLA Sticker often works better than selective herbicide. Kills the wireweed completely; overseed the bare patches afterward.

Pre-emergent prevention:

NZLA Blockade (prodiamine) provides pre-emergent control of wireweed germination. Application in late winter through early spring before soil temperatures hit germination range for summer weeds. Note the NZ label restriction — Blockade is registered for warm-season turf only here.

Cultural side:

Wireweed's presence in lawn patches strongly indicates compaction. Core aeration to relieve compaction is often more effective long-term than herbicide alone.

A dense well-fed lawn with proper aeration prevents most wireweed establishment.

For lawns with persistent wireweed:

Combine pre-em programme with aeration and improved cultural management. Single-season clearance is realistic with this combined approach. New germinations from seed bank decline over subsequent seasons.

Wireweed is more difficult than dandelion or plantain but yields to consistent integrated management.
