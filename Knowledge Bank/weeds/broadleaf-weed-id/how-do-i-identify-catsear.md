# How do I identify catsear vs dandelion?

Catsear (Hypochaeris radicata, also called false dandelion or flatweed) looks similar to dandelion at a glance but has distinct features. Common confusion because both are yellow-flowered rosette weeds.

Diagnostic differences:

**Catsear leaves are hairy.** Run a finger along the leaf — you'll feel small bristly hairs on both surfaces. Dandelion leaves are hairless and smooth.

**Catsear leaves are less deeply lobed.** Catsear has more rounded shallow lobes. Dandelion has the classic deep jagged lobes that give it the name "lion's tooth."

**Catsear has multiple branching flower stems with smaller flowers.** Often 2 to 4 flowers per stem at various points along the stem. Dandelion has a single flower at the top of a single hollow stem.

**Catsear flower stems are solid and branched.** Dandelion flower stems are hollow and unbranched.

**Catsear flowers slightly smaller than dandelion** — about 20 to 25 mm across versus 25 to 50 mm for dandelion.

Both produce wind-dispersed seed heads but catsear's is fluffier and less defined than the classic dandelion puffball.

Where catsear grows: throughout lawns and pastures, particularly common in low-fertility lawns. More widespread in NZ than dandelion in many regions.

Control:

Same chemistry as dandelion — NZLA BWC (MCPA + clopyralid + diflufenican) at 50 mL per 100m². Catsear is in the daisy family (Asteraceae) and clopyralid is particularly effective on this family.

Apply when actively growing in mild conditions. Catsear can be slightly harder to kill than dandelion because of the hairy leaves — the spray sometimes beads off rather than spreading.

NZLA Gold for tougher infestations.

Adding a spray adjuvant (like NZLA Sticker if doing a glyphosate spot kill) improves coverage on hairy-leafed weeds generally.

Hand pulling: catsear has a shallower root than dandelion, so easier to remove cleanly. Still difficult on established plants — better as a touch-up after chemistry.

Cultural side:

Catsear indicates underfed lawns. Steady fertility programme reduces catsear pressure significantly. NZLA All Seasons every 6 to 8 weeks through the growing season.

Catsear is more difficult to permanently eliminate than dandelion in NZ — wider establishment, larger seed bank in most lawns. Expect to retreat over multiple seasons.
