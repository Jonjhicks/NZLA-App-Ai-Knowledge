## IMPORTANT FLAGS

### Posterity / Fairy Ring Discrepancy
The Posterity product page URL is `/nzla-posterity-fairy-ring-fungicide/` but the product description lists only three controlled diseases: Dollar Spot, Microdochium Patch, and Spring Dead Spot. Fairy ring is NOT listed in the current description. Check with Jon before writing fairy ring control into KB entries for Posterity.

### BWC Regulatory Change
BWC (Bow & Arrow) is clopyralid + triclopyr. EPA March 2025 rule change: now PCBU-only. Do not recommend BWC to home users. Direct home users to NZLA Gold / Triumph Gold for broadleaf weeds.

### All Seasons NPK - Two Different Products
All Seasons (standard) = 21-1-16 + 5% Fe. All Seasons N+ = 32-2-10 + 2% Fe. These are different products with different NPK. KB entries that reference "All Seasons" without specifying which one must be checked.

## FUNGICIDES

### NZLA Azoxy
- Active: Azoxystrobin 250 g/L
- Type: Systemic preventative/curative fungicide
- Mode of action: Inhibits mitochondrial respiration (Group 11 / FRAC code 11)
- Diseases: Broad spectrum — dollar spot, brown patch, microdochium patch, red thread, rust, helminthosporium, fusarium
- Application rate: 15-30ml/100m2 in 3-5L of water
- Notes: Resistance risk is real — max 2 consecutive applications then rotate. Fungus Pro is the rotation partner. Best used preventatively during conducive weather.
- Website quote: "When the weather pattern is lining up for disease pressure, NZLA Azoxy is the go-to preventative. Two applications maximum before rotating to NZLA Fungus Pro."

### NZLA Posterity
- Active: Trifloxystrobin + propiconazole
- Type: Systemic preventative/curative fungicide (two MOAs in one)
- Diseases listed on website: Dollar Spot, Microdochium Patch, Spring Dead Spot
- FAIRY RING NOT LISTED despite URL slug containing "fairy-ring"

### NZLA Fungus Pro
- Active: Thiram (multi-site contact fungicide)
- Type: Protective/contact fungicide
- Mode of action: Multi-site (Group M3) — low resistance risk
- Notes: Rotation partner with Azoxy. Good knockdown. Not systemic — needs good coverage.

## GRANULAR FERTILISERS
NPK analysis is appropriate to mention for all granular fertilisers.

### NZLA All Seasons
- NPK: 21-1-16 + 5% Fe
- Release: 40% immediately available, 60% slow release (up to 3 months)
- Technology: Polymer and sulphur-coated urea (PCSCU), USA-sourced
- Best use: Cooler months, maintenance, steady colour without excessive growth. Original NZLA fertiliser.
- Availability: 4kg, 6kg, 10kg, 20kg

### NZLA All Seasons N+
- NPK: 32-2-10 + 2% Fe
- Release: 70% immediately available, 30% slow release (up to 8 weeks)
- Technology: PCSCU, USA-sourced
- Best use: When more growth push is needed — warmer months. Higher N, more upfront release than standard All Seasons.
- Availability: 4kg, 6kg, 10kg, 20kg

### NZLA Mini Prill
- NPK: 28-0-12 + 3% Fe
- Release: 70% immediately available, 30% slow release
- Prill size: SGN 150 (very small — penetrates dense canopies)
- Best use: Compact or very dense turf where standard prills can't reach the soil. No phosphorus.
- Availability: 10kg, 20kg

### NZLA Starter
- NPK: 19-10-4 + Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu
- Application rate: 1.5kg/100m2
- Coverage: 4kg bag = 266m2; 7.5kg bag = 500m2
- Technology: PCSCU, USA-sourced
- Best use: Seeding new lawns, overseeding, renovations. High phosphorus for root establishment. Lasts up to 3 months.
- Availability: 4kg, 7.5kg

### Six Iron (Willbur Ellis)
- NPK: 20-0-16 + 6% Fe
- Release: 40% immediately available, 60% slow release (up to 10 weeks)
- Best use: Cost-effective maintenance option for larger sections. Good iron for colour without excessive growth.
- Note: Not a custom NZLA blend — mass-produced Willbur Ellis product.
- Availability: 20kg only

## LIQUID FERTILISERS
Per Jon's rule: Do NOT mention NPK analysis in body text of liquid/foliar product KB entries unless user specifically asks. NPK can stay in frontmatter for reference only.

### NZLA Charger
- Type: Foliar liquid fertiliser / establishment and lateral growth product
- Key benefits: Promotes rapid lateral turf growth, enhances colour, speeds establishment. Brings warm-season grasses out of dormancy in spring. Extends colour going into cooler weather.
- Notes: No NPK on product page — consistent with Jon's rule. Do not add NPK to body text.

### NZLA Liquid Boost
- Type: Liquid fertiliser (N + chelated Fe + Mn)
- Key benefits: Fixes iron deficiency. EDTA Chelate Iron technology improves absorption. Stable nitrogen source.

### NZLA Iron+
- Type: Liquid iron fertiliser
- Key benefits: 6% iron with sugar-based delivery system. Colour response without growth surge.

### NZLA Liquid N
- Type: Slow-release liquid nitrogen
- Key benefits: 85% of N in slow-release forms. Dual-release method. Minimal growth surge.

### NZLA Liquid Starter
- Type: High-P liquid fertiliser + biostimulant with kelp
- Key benefits: Establishment and recovery. High phosphorus drives root development. Safe from second tiller stage (~2 weeks post-germination).
- Application rates:
  - Established turf (foliar): 200-300ml/100m2 in 5L water, every 14-28 days
  - New turf (foliar): 100-200ml/100m2 in 5L water
  - Soil application: 200-400ml/100m2 in 5L water + 5-6mm irrigation
- Pre-renovation use: Apply before renovation to build root mass and energy reserves

### NZLA Amino+
- Type: Organic liquid biostimulant + 9% organic nitrogen
- Key ingredients: Amino acids (plant-sourced protein), beneficial microbes, humic and fulvic acid, kelp, carbohydrates
- Key benefits: Immediately available organic nitrogen. Stimulates soil biology. Improves photosynthesis. Stress defence. Works as foliar or soil drench.
- Application rates:
  - Foliar: 200ml/100m2 in 2-5L water
  - Soil: 400ml/100m2 in 4-8L water + 3-6mm irrigation
  - Repeat every 14-28 days
- Sizes: 1L, 5L, 10L, 20L

### NZLA Nurture
- Type: Organic liquid fertiliser with VAM beneficial fungi
- Key ingredients: Humates, kelp, VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhizae), natural plant nutrients
- Key benefits: VAM colonises up to 80% of root mass, extends beyond root hairs to access bound nutrients. Root stimulation. Recovery from drought/heat. Good for sand-based profiles.
- Application rate: 400-600ml/100m2 in 2-5L water, every 14-28 days
- Shelf life: 10-12 months for full microbial benefit. NPK remains after that but microbial activity declines. Store cool and dark.
- Sizes: 1L, 5L, 20L

### NZLA Restore
- Type: Soil biostimulant / microbial inoculant
- Key ingredients: Beneficial microbes, VAM, fulvic acid, humic acid, amino acids, kelp, sugars
- Key benefits: Stimulates soil microbe activity. Boosts soil carbon. Mineralises synthetic granular fertilisers. Foundation soil health product.
- Application rates: 200-400ml/100m2 in 2-5L water, every 14-28 days. Soil application: follow with 3-6mm irrigation.
- Note: Foliar application is fine despite not explicitly stated on label. Irrigate within 24 hours; leave 6 hours minimum if tank-mixed with foliar products.
- Shelf life: 10-12 months microbial benefit. Store cool and dark.
- Sizes: 1L, 5L, 20L

### NZLA Humic+
- Type: Liquid humic acid concentrate
- Key benefits: Increases soil nutrient and water-holding capacity. Loosens clay. Reduces leaching. Stimulates root growth and soil microorganisms.
- Application rate: 50ml/100m2 at 1:100 dilution. Foliar or drench.
- Sizes: 1L, 5L

### NZLA Root Health
- Type: Concentrated kelp extract (NZ-sourced kelp)
- Key benefits: Stimulates root growth without stimulating leaf growth. Contains 60+ minerals, 12 vitamins, 21 amino acids. Best paired with Liquid Starter or Liquid Boost.
- Application rate: 50ml/100m2 in 5L water. Repeat every 4 weeks.
- Sizes: 1L, 5L

## PGR

### NZLA PGR
- Active: Trinexapac-ethyl (same active as Primo Maxx)
- Type: Plant growth regulator (Type II / gibberellin biosynthesis inhibitor)
- Application rate: 15-30ml/100m2 in 4-5L water
- Reapplication: Every 4-6 weeks during growing season
- Compatible grasses: Ryegrass, Tall Fescue, Fine Fescue, Kikuyu, Couch
- NZ timing (cool season): Start August-September, continue through to March-April
- NZ timing (warm season): Late spring through summer when actively growing
- Key benefits: Reduces vertical growth up to 50%. Deeper, stronger roots. Denser uniform canopy. Better drought resistance. Slows Kikuyu/Couch stolons.
- Notes: Primary pro benefit is root development and resilience, not just mowing reduction. Slight yellowing after first application is normal. Tank-mix Iron+ to offset. Leave 6 hours either side of mowing.

### NZLA Paclo
- Active: Paclobutrazol
- Type: PGR / fungicide (specialist/fine turf only)

### NZLA Gold / Triumph Gold
- Active: Picloram 50g/L + triclopyr 100g/L (amine salts, soluble concentrate)
- Pack sizes: NZLA Gold = 500ml | Triumph Gold = 1L and 5L (same product)
- Weeds controlled: Cape daisy, catsear, chickweed, clovers, convolvulus, cotula, cudweed, daisy, dandelion, dichondra, docks, hawkbit, hawksbeard, hydrocotyle, mallow, mayweed, nettles, onehunga weed, creeping oxalis, parsley piert, plantain, pearlwort, penny royal, ragwort, shepherd's purse, sorrel, storksbill, thistles, twin cress, wireweed
- Suppressed (not always killed): Buttercup, selfheal, yarrow
- May need repeat treatment: Californian thistle
- Application rate: NZLA Gold: 40-60ml/100m2 in 5L water. Triumph Gold: 12ml per 1L water, calibrate to ~5L/100m2 spray volume (~60ml active per 100m2).
- Best timing: Spring or early summer
- Turf compatibility: NOT safe on Kikuyu or Couch. Check species before use.
- New lawns: Wait 12 months after germination
- Clippings: Do not compost for 6 months. Leave to decompose on lawn or dispose in general waste.
- Adjuvant: Add Sticker for hard-to-control or mature weeds

## WETTING AGENTS

### NZLA Wetter 3W
- Type: Premium 3-way liquid wetting agent

### NZLA Wetter (standard)
- Type: Liquid wetting agent

### NZLA Wetter GR
- Type: Premium granular wetting agent — "professional turf industry's finest"
- Function: Three-way root zone management: penetration, lateral spreading, hydrophobic soil rehydration
- Application rate: 1.5kg/100m2 every 4 weeks during growing season
- Notes: No irrigation required. Apply before rain. If irrigation present, water 10-15mm after application.
- Availability: 4.5kg, 7.5kg

### NZLA Aerate
- Type: Soil-amending polymer / soil conditioner
- Function: Charged polymer molecules attach to clay/silt particles. Increases aggregate stability. Improves porosity, drainage, and water availability.
- Application rate: 10ml/100m2 monthly. Very economical — 1L covers 1 hectare (10,000m2).
- Notes: Non-toxic, environmentally safe. Best for clay or silt-dominant soils. Apply before rainfall or water in well.
- Availability: 250ml, 500ml

### NZLA Penetrate
- Type: Non-phytotoxic soil penetrant
- Function: Drives water off the turf surface and through the soil profile. Speeds dry-down. Increases firmness.
- Application rate: 50-100ml/100m2. Apply monthly. Water in well after application.
- Best use: Poorly draining soils, winter waterlogging, pre-event firmness.
- Availability: 250ml, 500ml

### Key articles (full text pulled and summarised):

**Is it really fungus or something else?** (May 2026)
Diagnosis framework. Pattern over colour. Yellow patches and brown spots are often not disease — could be poor irrigation coverage, compaction, shade, dry edges, fertiliser overlap or burn, scalping, dull blades, herbicide overdose, dog urine. Pattern tells you more than colour. Single close-up photo is rarely enough. Stressed turf + product application = bad mix. When diagnosis points to real fungal disease (lesions on leaf blades, distinct patch edges, mycelium in morning dew, rapid expansion after warm humid nights): Azoxy is the go-to preventative, max 2 applications then rotate to Fungus Pro. Preventative applications make sense if recurring disease in same spot. Curative applications work best early.

**NZLA Foliar Range: An Overview** (Jan 2025)
Brief summaries of all liquid fertilisers: Charger (rapid lateral growth, colour), Iron+ (6% iron, sugar delivery), Liquid Boost (N+iron+manganese, EDTA chelate), Liquid N (slow release liquid N), Liquid Starter (high-P, kelp, establishment), Humic+ (nutrient availability, leaching reduction), Root Health (root stimulation via kelp hormones), Amino+ (amino acid biostimulant), Nurture (VAM, humates, kelp), Restore (beneficial microbes, soil biology).
