## HERBICIDES

### NZLA Etho
**Active:** Ethofumesate 500g/L
**Application rate:** 40ml/100m² in 4–5L water
**Use:** Pre- and post-emergent herbicide. Suitable for Perennial Ryegrass and Tall Fescue ONLY — NOT safe for Fine Fescue. Controls Poa annua (winter grass), Summer Grass, chickweed, cleavers, thistle, spurrey, and other broadleaf weeds. Do not mix with PGR products.

---

### NZLA Meso
**Active:** Mesotrione 480g/L
**Application rate:** 3.6–5.6ml/100m² (rate varies by turf type — always check label)
**Use:** Pre- and post-emergent herbicide. Can be tank-mixed with Etho for pre-emergent programs. Bleaching effect visible as part of mode of action. Maximum 2 applications per year per EPA restrictions. Controls 46+ weed species.

---

### NZLA BWC (Bow & Arrow Herbicide)
**Active:** 20g/L Clopyralid + 15g/L Diflufenican + 300g/L MCPA
**Application rate:** 50ml/100m²
**Use:** Broadleaf weed control. PCBU-only product as of March 2025 EPA regulation — home users must be redirected to NZLA Gold at checkout. Effective against a wide range of broadleaf weeds in turf.

---

### NZLA Gold
**Active:** 50g/L Picloram + 100g/L Triclopyr
**Application rate:** 40–60ml/100m²
**Use:** Broad-spectrum broadleaf weed control. NOT safe for Kikuyu or Couch lawns. Suitable for ryegrass and fescue lawns. Effective against difficult perennial weeds.

---

### Triumph Gold
**Active:** 50g/L Picloram + 100g/L Triclopyr (same actives as NZLA Gold)
**Application rate:** 60ml/100m² (12ml per 1L water)
**Sizes:** 1L and 5L
**Use:** Broadleaf weed control. Same mode of action as NZLA Gold. Available in larger sizes suited to trade and contractors.

---

### NZLA Blockade
**Active:** Prodiamine 480g/L
**Application rate:** 10–30ml/100m² (turf); 40–80ml/100m² (garden beds)
**Use:** Pre-emergent herbicide only — no post-emergent activity. Must be activated with 6mm irrigation after application. Up to 6 months residual control. Controls summer grass, poa annua, and other annual weed grasses before they germinate.

---

### NZLA Halo
**Active:** Haloxyfop-P-methyl 100g/L (also known in the trade as "Ignite")
**Application rate:** 15ml/100m² (Poa annua); 12.5ml/100m² (other grasses)
**Use:** Selective grass herbicide. For use on FINE FESCUE ONLY — lawn must be 100% pure Fine Fescue and at least 6 months old. Removes unwanted grass species from fine fescue lawns. NOT safe for ryegrass, couch, kikuyu, or mixed sward.

---

### MSF600
**Active:** Metsulfuron-methyl 600g/kg
**Application rate:** Low rate — check label (dilute product, dose by area)
**Use:** Grass-friendly broadleaf herbicide. Used in Kikuyu lawns to selectively remove ryegrass. Also effective on gorse, scrub, oxalis, and Guildford grass. Suitable where a broad-spectrum non-selective is not desired.

---

### Sedge Pro
**Active:** Halosulfuron-methyl 750g/kg
**Application rate:** 1.3g/100m²
**Use:** Post-emergent sedge control. Targets nutgrass (nutsedge) and other sedge species. MUST be used with NZLA Sticker adjuvant for effective absorption. Slow to show results — allow several weeks for full effect.

---

### Glyphosate
**Active:** Glyphosate 540g/L
**Application rate:** Per label (varies by use case)
**Use:** Non-selective herbicide. Kills all actively growing vegetation. Does not persist in soil — breaks down relatively quickly. Used for lawn kill-off before renovation or for spot treatment of weeds in non-turf areas.

## FUNGICIDES

### NZLA Azoxy
**Active:** Azoxystrobin
**Sizes:** 250ml, 500ml
**Application rate:** 23ml/100m²
**Use:** Systemic fungicide (FRAC Group 11 — strobilurin). Xylem-systemic and translaminar movement. Provides up to 28 days of disease protection. Works best as a preventative treatment. When disease is already visible, tank-mix with a fungicide from a different FRAC group (e.g. Fungus Pro) to improve control and reduce resistance risk.

**Diseases controlled:** Brown Patch (Rhizoctonia), Anthracnose, Winter Fusarium (Fusarium Patch), Grey Leaf Spot, Helminthosporium (Leaf Spot Complex), Pythium (Blight and Root Rot), Red Thread.

**Resistance note:** Rotate with Fungus Pro or other non-Group 11 fungicides. Avoid repeated back-to-back strobilurin applications. Group 11 products can develop resistance over time.

**Application notes:** For newly established lawns, wait until grass is well rooted and mown at least twice before applying. Allow spray to dry before resuming traffic. A light irrigation after application can help movement into leaf tissue — avoid heavy watering.

---

### NZLA Fungus Pro
**Active:** Thiram (dithiocarbamate / thiuram class)
**Sizes:** 250ml, 500ml, 2L, 10L
**Application rate:** 50ml in 5L water per 25m² (early signs of disease). Can be increased to 200ml in 5L per 25m² for more widespread/advanced disease. No resistance risk with this product — starting low and increasing if needed is acceptable.
**Use:** Contact/protectant fungicide (not systemic). Forms a barrier on the leaf surface — thorough coverage is essential. Effective for both preventative and curative use. Reapply every 2–4 weeks under high disease pressure. No resistance concern — this is an advantage over systemic fungicides like Azoxy.

**Diseases controlled:** Red Thread, Fusarium Patch, Brown Patch, Melting Out/Leaf Spot complexes, Greasy Spot, Fairy Rings and fungal collars, Mushrooms and fruiting bodies.

**Application notes:** Apply to dry lawn when fungal conditions are likely (warm, damp, humid). Avoid mowing 1–2 days before and after application. Use a calibrated sprayer for adequate coverage. Keep off treated areas until dry. Aquatic toxicity — avoid spraying near waterways or drains.

**Difference from Azoxy:** Fungus Pro is a contact protectant that stays on the surface. Azoxy is systemic and moves inside the plant. Using both types in rotation gives broader and longer-lasting disease control.

---

### Posterity
**Active:** (Synthetic — proprietary formulation; FRAC Group 7 — SDHI)
**Size:** 500ml
**Application rate:** 5–10ml/100m² (lower rate for prevention; higher rate for active disease)
**Use:** Preventative and curative fungicide for high-value turf disease situations. Provides up to 4 weeks of protection per application. Repeat at 14–28 day intervals under high disease pressure.

**Diseases controlled:** Dollar Spot, Microdochium Patch (Fusarium Patch), Spring Dead Spot.
**NOTE:** Do NOT include fairy ring as a controlled disease despite the product URL slug — the product description does not list it.

**Application notes:** Apply to actively growing lawns. Avoid drought-stressed or waterlogged areas. Do not mow 1–2 days before or after application. Apply in calm, dry conditions. Compatible with most lawn care products — jar test before tank mixing. Keep pets and children off treated areas until dry.

## PLANT GROWTH REGULATORS

### NZLA PGR
**Active:** Trinexapac-ethyl
**Sizes:** 250ml, 500ml
**Application rate:** 15–30ml per 4–5L water per 100m²
**Use:** Reduces vertical turf growth by up to 50% for up to 4 weeks. Reduces mowing frequency. Improves turf density and colour. Does not kill grass — suppresses growth only. Can be used on most cool-season and warm-season turf varieties.

---

### Paclo
**Active:** Paclobutrazol 250g/L
**Size:** 500ml
**Application rate:** 7.5–15ml/100m²
**Use:** Turf growth regulator specifically for suppression and long-term control of Annual Poa (winter grass) in cool-season turf. Also reduces vertical height and encourages desirable turf species to dominate. Used in bentgrass, perennial ryegrass, Kentucky bluegrass, fine fescue, and tall fescue situations.

**Key use notes:**
- Water in immediately with 4–6mm irrigation after application
- Apply 1–2 weeks before Annual Poa seed-head emergence for best effect
- Poa growth slows within 1–2 weeks; higher rates may cause temporary yellowing/browning of Poa
- Wait 14 days after application before overseeding; delay Paclo use on seedlings until turf is 6+ weeks established
- Do NOT mix with Group 3 fungicides (DMIs such as propiconazole, tebuconazole, triadimenol, triticonazole)
- Do not apply when daytime temps consistently exceed 32°C or when turf is under heat, cold, drought, or saturation stress

## INSECTICIDES

### NZLA Grub+
**Active:** Chlorantraniliprole 200g/L (same active as Acelepryn)
**Sizes:** 100ml, 250ml, 1L, 5L
**Application rate:** 15ml/100m² in minimum 4L water (full rate, up to 6 months control). Half rate 7.5ml/100m² gives approximately 3 months control.
**Use:** Long-lasting insecticide for grass grubs, caterpillars, and other soil-dwelling and surface-feeding pests. Up to 6 months residual control from one application. Exempt from ACVM Act. Do not use on food crops, animal feed, or grazing areas.

**Pests controlled:** Grass grubs (including African black beetle larvae, Argentinian scarab), billbugs, surface-feeding caterpillars (cutworm, porina, sod webworm, lawn armyworm).

**Application notes:**
- For grass grubs and root-feeding pests: irrigate with 3–6mm immediately after application
- For surface-feeding caterpillars (e.g. porina): delay irrigation 24 hours so product sits on surface
- Safe to apply before, during, or after lawn renovations
- Best preventative timing: October and March (peak NZ grub/caterpillar activity)
- Safe for pets and people once watered in and lawn is dry — targets specific insect muscle receptors not shared by mammals

**Coverage:** 100ml = 666m²; 250ml = 1,660m²; 1L = 6,000m²+; 5L = 3+ hectares

## LIQUID FERTILISERS

*All liquid fertilisers: foliar application. Do not water for minimum 6 hours after application. Avoid mowing for 24 hours after application. Avoid applying to freshly mown lawns (wait at least 4 hours to allow blade tips to heal). Can be applied from the second tillering stage (~2 weeks after germination). Apply every 14–28 days or as required. Keep warm over winter to prevent crystallisation.*

---

### NZLA Charger
**Liquid Fertiliser Rating System:** Growth 80%, Green Up 100%, Visual Impact 100%, Soil Health 20%, Organic Content 20%, Compatibility 60%
**Application rate:** 200ml/100m² in 4–10L water. Maximum 500ml/100m² if required. (Follow NZLA application guide: 200ml in 4–10L per 100m²)
**Use:** Foliar fertiliser that enhances colour, promotes lateral growth, and contains macro + micro nutrients plus plant hormone growth promoters. Potassium helps harden turf against stress (disease, wear). Features in the NZLA Winter Application Guide. Year-round product, top-seller in all seasons. Also helps correct iron deficiency — can be tank-mixed with NZLA Iron+ when extra iron is needed.

### NZLA Liquid N
**Liquid Fertiliser Rating System:** Growth 100%, Green Up 60%, Visual Impact 80%, Compatibility 100%, Soil Health 20%, Organic Content 20%

**Use:** Premium slow-release liquid nitrogen fertiliser for sustained growth and green colour. For all turf types. Apply every 14–28 days or as required.

### NZLA Nurture
**Liquid Fertiliser Rating System:** Growth 80%, Soil Health 100%, Organic Content 100%, Compatibility 100%, Green Up 40%, Visual Impact 40%
**Application rate:** 400–600ml/100m² in 2–5L water per 100m². Apply every 14–28 days.
**Technology:** VM3 Organics blend including humates, kelp, beneficial fungi, and natural plant nutrients. Contains VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhizae) — beneficial symbiotic fungi that can colonise up to 80% of the root mass. Especially useful in sand-based profiles. Can be applied via garden sprayer, fertigation unit, or fertispray system. Suited to turf establishment and renovation.
**Storage/shelf life:** Contains living microbes — use within 10–12 months for maximum benefit. After that, NPK remains but microbial activity declines. Store in cool, dark location.

## WETTING AGENTS & MOISTURE MANAGEMENT

### NZLA Wetter
**Sizes:** 1L, 5L, 20L
**Application rate:** 250ml/100m² in 5–10L water. Wash into root zone with 15–20mm irrigation within 24 hours. Repeat every 7–14 days or as required.
**Use:** Premium liquid wetting agent for moisture retention. Retains up to 5x more moisture in root zone versus untreated soil (per independent research). Reduces irrigation volume required. Prevents and treats dry patch. Improves water penetration and creates stronger root systems. Best applied morning or late afternoon in cooler temperatures. Use coarse spray pattern for uniform coverage. Avoid applying before heavy rain.
**Ideal for:** Homeowners, lawn contractors, golf courses, sports fields, anyone managing dry/hydrophobic soils or under water restrictions.

---

### Wetter 3W
**Sizes:** 1L, 5L, 20L
**Application rate:** 250ml/100m² in 5–10L water (label rate 100–300ml; 250ml is a good general rate). Wash in with 15–20mm irrigation within 24 hours. Repeat every 7–14 days or as required.
**Technology:** Three-way liquid surfactant system — penetrant (breaks through water-repellent soils for rapid moisture entry) + lateral spreading agent + rehydration component. Works immediately on application — no waiting for granules to dissolve.
**Use:** Premium liquid wetting agent specifically designed for hydrophobic (water-repelling) soils. Fixes dry soils that repel irrigation, causing runoff and patchy dry areas. Restores even water movement through the soil profile. Enhances fertiliser uptake and root health. Suitable for all NZ lawn types, new and established.
**Advantage over granular:** Works immediately on contact with soil versus granular products (like Wetter GR) which must first dissolve before becoming effective.

---

### NZLA Wetter GR
**Sizes:** 4.5kg, 7.5kg
**Application rate:** 1.5kg/100m² every 4 weeks during growing season.
**Technology:** Three-way root zone water management — penetration, lateral spreading, and rehydration of hydrophobic soils.
**Use:** Granular wetting agent. Professional turf industry standard. Irrigation is NOT required after application (suitable for sections without irrigation) — ideally apply before rain. If irrigation is available, apply 10–15mm after application. Three-way action makes it one of the most effective granular wetting agents available.
