# Trusted Sources for Turf Research

A curated list of sources to prioritise when researching turf topics for technical answers. Search these before generic web results. Update as new ones surface or unreliable ones get ruled out.

## NZ-specific (start here for anything NZ-relevant)

**New Zealand Sports Turf Institute (NZ STI)**
- nzsti.co.nz | nzsti.org.nz
- NZ's standard-setting body for sports turf since 1949
- Publishes the Turf Management Journal quarterly (paywalled)
- Public technical guides accessible: Bowls Chemical Guide, natural bowling greens establishment, training material
- The most authoritative NZ-specific turf source

**Massey University**
- massey.ac.nz
- School of Agriculture and Environment
- NZ turfgrass research, soil and plant sciences
- Home of Kerry Harrington's NZ Weeds Database (see harrington-research.md)

**Lincoln University**
- lincoln.ac.nz
- NZ turf, pasture and agronomy research
- Some sports turf and amenity work

**AgResearch**
- agresearch.co.nz
- NZ Crown Research Institute
- Pasture-focused but directly relevant for grass species behaviour, disease pathology, soil fertility

**NIWA**
- niwa.co.nz
- NZ climate data (soil temps, frost dates, rainfall, regional comparisons)
- Best source for verifying regional NZ conditions when seasonal advice depends on it

**NZ Plant Protection Society**
- nzpps.org
- NZ-specific weed, pest and disease research
- Free access to many of their proceedings papers
- Maintains the resistance.nzpps.org register (where the onehunga clopyralid resistance work is documented)

**NZ EPA (HSNO register) — primary regulator for NZLA's range**
- epa.govt.nz
- HSNO Act (Hazardous Substances and New Organisms) covers hazardous substance products. Approval is by HSR number (e.g., HSR101240 for Blockade, HSR000822 for Moss).
- This is the relevant register for NZLA's turf products. The range is turf-only and not registered for use on food or feed crops, which sits it outside ACVM and inside HSNO.
- WorkSafe NZ enforces the workplace use side (qualified handlers, records of use).
- Use this for verifying what's actually approved before recommending any product, and for confirming hazard classifications and label conditions.

**MPI ACVM — not the relevant register for NZLA**
- mpi.govt.nz/agriculture/agricultural-compounds-and-veterinary-medicines
- The ACVM Act regulates agricultural compounds used in food or feed crop production.
- NZLA's range is amenity turf only and sits outside ACVM. Product labels carry the "not registered for use on food or feed crops" statement, which is the ACVM exemption pathway. Don't describe NZLA products as ACVM-registered, they aren't and don't need to be.
- For products where the NZ label differs from international sources (Blockade on cool-season turf being the obvious example), always check the actual NZ label conditions rather than US sources.

## Australia (warm-season turf and upper North Island climate)

**Nuturf Australia**
- nuturf.com.au
- FoliMAX range of foliar fertilisers — useful technical depth on slow-release N chemistry, EDTA chelate iron, citrate/carbonate potassium, phosphite-P, kelp biostimulants, etc.
- Use as research source for chemistry, never name in customer-facing content
- Direct equivalents to NZLA range: Charger/Charger, NFE/Liquid Boost, N-Forcer-N/Liquid N, Launcher/Liquid Starter, Iron+/Iron+, Amino+/Amino+, Humic+/Humic+, Stamina 90/Wetter, Stamina Balance/Wetter 3W, Stamina Relief/Aerate, KelMAX/Root Health

**AgriFutures Australia**
- agrifutures.com.au

**University of Queensland**
- uq.edu.au — warm-season turf research

**Australian Sports Turf Managers Association**
- astma.com.au

## UK (closest climate match to most of NZ)

**STRI (Sports Turf Research Institute)**
- stri.co.uk
- UK turf research institute, similar role to NZ STI
- Cool-season research directly transferable to most of NZ

**BIGGA (British and International Golf Greenkeepers Association)**
- bigga.org.uk
- Practical greenkeeping content

## US (high-volume free research, adjust for climate)

**Turfgrass Information Center (Michigan State University)**
- tic.msu.edu
- World's largest turf research database, aggregates international content

**Penn State Extension**
- extension.psu.edu
- Strong cool-season turf information, very practical

**Rutgers Center for Turfgrass Science**
- turf.rutgers.edu
- Research-heavy, cultivar performance trials

**Cornell University Weed and Turf Science**
- cals.cornell.edu/weed-science
- Strong on cool-season disease and weed management

**USGA Green Section**
- usga.org/course-care
- Golf-focused but broad turf science applicable beyond golf
- PPA-1 "Chemical Control of Turfgrass Diseases" reference document is particularly useful

**University of Tennessee Turfgrass**
- turf.tennessee.edu
- Transition zone research, useful for upper North Island

**University of Florida / IFAS**
- edis.ifas.ufl.edu
- Best for warm-season turf (couch, kikuyu, paspalum, zoysia)

**Michigan State University Turf**
- canr.msu.edu/turf
- Cool-season research and extension (Joe Vargas for disease work)

**University of Georgia turf**
- turf.caes.uga.edu — warm-season disease research

## Active ingredient references (when researching chemistry)

**FRAC (Fungicide Resistance Action Committee)**
- frac.info — current FRAC group classifications and resistance management guidelines

**HRAC (Herbicide Resistance Action Committee)**
- hracglobal.com — HRAC group classifications

**IRAC (Insecticide Resistance Action Committee)**
- irac-online.org — IRAC group classifications

**EPA US active ingredient pages**
- For chemistry depth on individual actives — labels, mode of action, soil persistence, ecotoxicity

## How to use this list

For NZ-specific advice (regional timing, climate, registered chemistry): start with NZ sources first.

For technical and scientific principles (mode of action, plant biology, disease pathology): overseas research transfers well.

For seasonal or temperature-dependent advice: always adjust overseas data for NZ climate. US growing zones don't map cleanly to NZ. Northland is roughly equivalent to mid-Atlantic US, Auckland to Pacific Northwest, lower South Island closer to UK.

For chemical recommendations: NZLA's turf range is regulated under HSNO via the EPA, not ACVM. Verify against the EPA HSNO register before recommending products. Don't assume US labels apply here (Blockade on cool-season turf being the obvious example).

For chemistry depth (mode of action, resistance management): the FRAC/HRAC/IRAC groups plus peer-reviewed journals are the gold standard.

For foliar fertiliser selling angles and chemistry comparisons: Nuturf FoliMAX is useful research material but never name them in customer-facing content.

## Sources to avoid

AI-generated content farms, US-only sources making NZ-specific climate claims, random lawn care blogs without research backing.
