## Active corrections / flags

- **Blockade NZ label restriction: warm-season turf only (kikuyu, couch).** Prodiamine is widely registered for cool-season turf internationally and the chemistry is safe on ryegrass, fescue and browntop, but use on cool-season turf in NZ is off-label. Explain the difference honestly when asked; do not recommend off-label use.
- **Grub+ targets per current website: includes grass grubs and porina alongside black beetle, stem weevil, billbugs, and surface-feeding caterpillars (cutworm, sod webworm, lawn armyworm).** Up to 6 months control from a single application. Maximum 1 application per year.
- **Root Health is a kelp biostimulant (Ecklonia maxima with naturally high auxin content), not just a wetting agent.** Position as root development driver. Earlier "wetting agent/soil ameliorant" framing from older PDFs is superseded.
- **Penetrate** was previously sold as "Push" — renamed January 2025. Current rate is 50-100 mL/100m² monthly. An older different product also called "Penetrate" (48% surfactant, 10 mL rate) is superseded.
- **NZLA Moss** is registered for fine turf and cotula only, not general lawns. DANGER classification — severe skin burns and serious eye damage. PPE non-negotiable.
- **Dew Shield is discontinued.** Do not recommend.
- **Onehunga clopyralid resistance:** documented over 200-fold resistance in NZ populations, cross-resistance to picloram (Gold), some resistance to MCPA and dicamba, NO resistance to mecoprop. Mecoprop-based products not currently in NZLA range — flag this gap honestly when resistance is suspected rather than substituting.

## Outstanding flags

- All Seasons N+: confirmed NPK is 32-1.3-8.3 + 2% Fe (some older listings showed 32-2-10). Use the 32-1.3-8.3 figure.
- Charger trace elements: Aussie equivalent analysis shows 7.2N + 7.2K + 3.8Fe + 2.2Mn + 0.22Zn + 3.6S. NZLA Charger reference is 7-0-7 + 4%Fe + 2%Mn. Whether the NZ formula also includes Zn and S, or just the headline numbers are listed, is worth verifying against the physical label.
